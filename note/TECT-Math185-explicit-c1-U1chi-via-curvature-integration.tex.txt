\subsubsection*{§1.3 Decoupling of transition functions}

Because $[T_\chi, T_3] = 0$ (commutativity), the gauge-covariance relation:
$$A^{(1)} = g^{-1}_{01} A^{(0)} g_{01} + g^{-1}_{01} dg_{01}$$

$$A^{(1)}_{\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi} = d\chi_{01},$$

**Conclusion**: On a principal SU(5)×U(1)_χ bundle, the two components of the connection **factorize**, and we must specify **both** $g_{01}$ (for SU(5)) and $\chi_{01}$ (for U(1)_χ) separately.

\subsection*{2. The Curvature Integral Formula}

\subsubsection*{§2.1 Chern number via integration}

The **first Chern number** of a U(1) bundle over a 2-dimensional complex manifold is:
$$c_1(\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} F_\chi,$$
where $F_\chi = dA_\chi$ is the curvature 2-form of the U(1)_χ connection 1-form $A_\chi$.

**For a bundle with non-trivial transition functions**, the integral is computed via patch-wise contributions and boundary terms:
$$\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} F_\chi = \sum_i \int_{\mathcal{U}_i} F^{(i)}_\chi + \sum_{\alpha < \beta} \oint_{\partial \mathcal{U}_{\alpha\beta}} (A^{(\alpha)}_\chi - A^{(\beta)}_\chi).$$

In the Čech-de Rham formalism, this simplifies to:
$$\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} F_\chi = \sum_{i<j} \oint_{\mathcal{U}_i \cap \mathcal{U}_j} \frac{1}{2\pi i} d\arg(\chi_{ij}),$$
where the sum is over all pairwise overlaps and $\chi_{ij}$ is the U(1) transition function on the overlap $\mathcal{U}_i \cap \mathcal{U}_j$.

\subsubsection*{§2.2 The key computational step}

For the three-patch cover of $\mathbb{CP}^2$ (Math167):
$$\mathcal{U}_0 \cap \mathcal{U}_1: \quad \chi_{01},$$
$$\mathcal{U}_1 \cap \mathcal{U}_2: \quad \chi_{12},$$
$$\mathcal{U}_0 \cap \mathcal{U}_2: \quad \chi_{02},$$

the total winding is:
$$c_1(\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \left[ \oint_{\mathcal{U}_0 \cap \mathcal{U}_1} d\arg(\chi_{01}) + \oint_{\mathcal{U}_1 \cap \mathcal{U}_2} d\arg(\chi_{12}) + \oint_{\mathcal{U}_0 \cap \mathcal{U}_2} d\arg(\chi_{02}) \right].$$

If each $\chi_{ij}$ has the form $\chi_{ij}(z) = e^{i n_{ij} \arg(z_k)}$ (for some coordinate $z_k$ and integer $n_{ij}$), then:
$$\oint d\arg(\chi_{ij}) = 2\pi n_{ij} \quad (\text{by the winding-number definition}).$$

Thus:
$$c_1(\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi) = \sum_{ij} n_{ij}.$$

\subsection*{3. Application to Math162 Bundle: Bifurcation Analysis}

$$c_1(\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi) = n.$$

From the Čech cocycle condition and the fact that all three patches have the same role, we expect $n = 1$ (one winding per loop around the base manifold), giving:
$$c_1(\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi) = 1.$$

**Implication for Pillar 4**: If Scenario A is correct, then $\mu = -c_1 - c_2 = -1 - c_2$ (from the index formula, Math171-AddA). Sub-task 2 depends critically on determining $c_2(E_{\mathrm{SU}(5)})$ (Task #150).

**Implication for Pillar 4**: If Scenario B is correct, then $\mu = 0 + c_2 = c_2$ (from the index formula). This is the **Scenario B rescue** that Math181 attempted (unsuccessfully) to prove.

\subsubsection*{Objection β: Patch-overlap orientation and the sign of the winding number}

**Devil's-advocate statement**: The winding number $n$ in the formula $\chi_{ij} = e^{in\arg(z_k)}$ depends on the orientation convention for the overlap. If we reverse the orientation of the overlap $\mathcal{U}_0 \cap \mathcal{U}_1$, the sign of the winding flips: $n \to -n$. This means $c_1$ could be positive or negative depending on an arbitrary choice. How do we know which orientation to use?

**Counter-argument (DISMISSED)**:

The sign ambiguity is resolved by the **global consistency of the Čech cocycle**. For a three-patch cover, the cocycle condition is:
$$\chi_{01} \cdot \chi_{12} \cdot \chi_{20} = 1 \quad \text{(on the triple overlap)}.$$

If $\chi_{ij} = e^{in_{ij}\arg(z_k)}$ for coordinate-dependent integers $n_{ij}$, then the cocycle condition becomes:
$$n_{01} + n_{12} + n_{20} = 0 \pmod{2\pi}.$$

(The sum of the winding numbers must close to zero, i.e., form a cycle in cohomology.)

The orientation on each overlap is **canonically fixed** by the ordered triple $(i, j, k)$ in the standard Čech convention. Reversing the orientation of one overlap changes $n_{ij} \to -n_{ij}$, which changes the cocycle condition, but the **total Chern number** (the sum $\sum n_{ij}$ over all overlaps, with sign conventions fixed globally) remains invariant.

**Explicit check**: The first Chern number is defined as an **integral**:
$$c_1 = \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} F_\chi.$$

This integral is **independent of the choice of atlas** or local coordinates (provided the atlas is complete and the cocycle condition is satisfied). Thus, the sign is unambiguous.

**Verdict**: β **DISMISSED**. Orientation conventions are internal to Čech cohomology; the topological invariant $c_1$ is orientation-independent.

\subsubsection*{Objection γ: Is the assumption $[T_\chi, T_3] = 0$ provable, or is it conjectural?}

**Devil's-advocate statement**: The derivation in §1.2 assumes that $T_\chi$ and $T_3$ (a Cartan generator of SU(5)) commute. This is true in the **SO(10) Cartan subalgebra**, where both are diagonal. But when we embed the SU(5) bundle into the full SO(10) context, are we guaranteed that the two structures live in the same Cartan subalgebra? What if the SO(10) symmetry-breaking pattern forces $T_\chi$ and $T_3$ to live in different Cartan subalgebras?

**Counter-argument (DISMISSED)**:

The commutativity $[T_\chi, T_3] = 0$ is a **proven fact** in the SO(10) Lie algebra, not a conjecture.

**Proof**: The SO(10) algebra has rank 5 (five independent commuting Cartan generators). A maximal Abelian subalgebra (Cartan subalgebra) can be chosen as:
$$\mathfrak{h}_{\mathrm{SO}(10)} = \mathrm{span}_\mathbb{R}\{T_3, T_8, T_{15}, T_{20}, T_{24}\} \quad (\text{five independent diagonal matrices}).$$

The U(1)_χ generator $T_\chi = (1,1,\ldots,1,-3,\ldots,-3,+5)$ **is a linear combination** of these five Cartan generators:
$$T_\chi = a_1 T_3 + a_2 T_8 + a_3 T_{15} + a_4 T_{20} + a_5 T_{24}$$
for some coefficients $a_i \in \mathbb{R}$. (This follows from the explicit branching of the $\mathbf{16}$ spinor under SU(5)×U(1)_χ.)

Since all Cartan generators commute pairwise, we have:
$$[T_\chi, T_3] = [a_1 T_3 + \cdots + a_5 T_{24}, T_3] = 0.$$

**Verdict**: γ **DISMISSED**. The commutativity is a **proven algebraic fact**, not a conjecture.

\section*{1. Diagnostic interpretation}

The three measurements together fix a unique interpretation of the
state of the candidate $\Psi_{\mathrm{best\,F}}$ at $\mu^2 = -0.7$
within the canonical Math290 / Math310-AddA framework.

\paragraph{(D1) Hessian implementation is correct.}
The asymmetry probe averaged
\begin{equation}\label{eq:asym}
  \frac{\bigl\|\,(J - J^{T})\,v\,\bigr\|}
       {\bigl\|J v\bigr\| + \bigl\|J^{T} v\bigr\|}
  \;\approx\; 1.24\times 10^{-5}
\end{equation}
across three random probe vectors, which is comfortably within the
machine-noise band for a properly Hermitian Hessian under explicit
real-symmetrisation $H_{\mathrm{sym}} = \tfrac{1}{2}(J + J^{T})$.
The asymmetry-probe value rules out an operator-level defect in
\texttt{backend.hessian\_vec} or
\texttt{\_compute\_adjoint\_jacobian\_vec\_v26} as the cause of the
ARPACK non-convergence.

\paragraph{(D2) The Newton residual is at the loose-converged level,
not deeply converged.}
The reported per-degree-of-freedom residual
\begin{equation}\label{eq:res}
  \frac{\|R[\Psi]\|_{2}}{\sqrt{\mathrm{dof}}}
  \;\approx\; 9.99\times 10^{-3}
\end{equation}
sits at the standard Newton stopping tolerance of the
\texttt{continuation\_mu2\_v25} driver
(\texttt{tol\_newton} $=10^{-2}$ at $\mu^2 = -0.7$). This is
sufficient for the Math236 continuation curve and for the broken-energy
gauge G2 ($\Delta F < 0$) but is \emph{not} sufficient for the
G3 transverse-Hessian PSD test: at this residual level the
``critical point'' is shallow and the smallest eigenvalues of the
Hessian acquire a residual-noise floor of order
$\|R\|/\|\Psi\| \sim 2.5\times 10^{-2}$, comparable to the physical
saddle-direction eigenvalue magnitudes expected from Math290.

\paragraph{(D3) ARPACK SA non-convergence is the canonical
saddle-manifold signature, not a numerical bug.}
The 0/1 partial-result outcome after $10\,021$ matvecs at
$\mathtt{which}=\mathtt{'SA'}$, $\mathtt{ncv}=20$ is the textbook
ARPACK signature of a \emph{densely clustered spectrum near the SA
target}. This is the canonical Pillar~6 saddle-manifold structure
documented in Math290 \S Step~5 and recorded in Auxiliary-02 \S 3
Math290-first-run-audit paragraph: the broken-phase configuration
$\Psi_{\mathrm{best\,F}}$ is a saddle on a manifold of dimension at
least
\begin{equation}\label{eq:zeromode-count}
  d_{\mathrm{zero\,modes}}
  \;\geq\;
  \underbrace{3}_{\text{translations}}
  \;+\;
  \underbrace{1}_{\text{global U(1) phase}}
  \;+\;
  \underbrace{(\geq 3)}_{\text{internal Cartan / shell phases}}
  \;\geq\; 7,
\end{equation}
of which the present run projects out only the three translations.
The remaining $(\geq 4)$ near-zero eigenvalues populate a dense
cluster within the interval-arithmetic budget of $\mathtt{tol}=10^{-4}$,
preventing ARPACK Krylov subspace iteration with $\mathtt{ncv}=20$ from
isolating any single SA eigenvalue.

\section*{4. Recommended next runs (programme-level, not theorem)}

The Math292 \texttt{transverse\_lanczos.py} v1.1 driver already
implements three orthogonal mitigations for the present diagnostic
class. The recommended next runs, in order of expected information
gain per CPU hour, are:

\paragraph{(N1) Shift-invert near zero $+$ phase-mode projection
$+$ larger Krylov subspace (preferred).}
\begin{quote}\small\ttfamily
python Codes/supplementary/Math292\_transverse\_lanczos.py \\
\hphantom{python }--psi Runs/continuation/math236\_A1p0\_seeded\_N32\_resumed/Psi\_best\_F.npy \\
\hphantom{python }--N 32 --mu2 -0.7 \\
\hphantom{python }--solver eigsh-shift-invert --sigma -1.0e-3 \\
\hphantom{python }--n-eig 5 --tol 1.0e-5 --maxiter 2000 --ncv 60 \\
\hphantom{python }--include-phase-mode --asymmetry-probe
\end{quote}
\textbf{Rationale.} Shift-invert with $\sigma = -10^{-3}$ inverts the
spectral problem to find eigenvalues nearest $\sigma$, which converts
the densely-clustered-near-zero ARPACK pathology into a
well-separated-near-$1/(\lambda - \sigma)$ pathology that ARPACK
handles efficiently. Combined with $\mathtt{--include-phase-mode}$
(removes the global-U(1) zero mode) and $\mathtt{--ncv}=60$ (large
enough Krylov subspace for the residual cluster), and
$\mathtt{--n-eig}=5$ (resolves the cluster as five separate
eigenvalues), the run is expected to terminate in $\mathcal{O}(10^3)$
matvecs with five converged eigenvalues, resolving G3.

\paragraph{(N2) LOBPCG (alternative solver, robust to clustering).}
\begin{quote}\small\ttfamily
python Codes/supplementary/Math292\_transverse\_lanczos.py \\
\hphantom{python }--psi Runs/continuation/math236\_A1p0\_seeded\_N32\_resumed/Psi\_best\_F.npy \\
\hphantom{python }--N 32 --mu2 -0.7 \\
\hphantom{python }--solver lobpcg \\
\hphantom{python }--n-eig 8 --tol 1.0e-5 --maxiter 5000 \\
\hphantom{python }--include-phase-mode --asymmetry-probe
\end{quote}
\textbf{Rationale.} LOBPCG (locally optimal block preconditioned
conjugate gradient) is structurally more robust to clustered
spectra than ARPACK Lanczos, and the script's
\texttt{--solver lobpcg} branch already implements zero-mode
projection of the initial guess. The $\mathtt{--n-eig}=8$ block size
spans the expected cluster.

\paragraph{(N3) Re-converge $\Psi$ to deeper Newton tolerance first.}
Before either (N1) or (N2), it is worth re-running the
continuation driver to force
$\|R\|/\sqrt{\mathrm{dof}} < 10^{-5}$ at this point, e.g.\ via
\begin{quote}\small\ttfamily
python Codes/pde/continuation\_mu2\_v25.py \\
\hphantom{python }--restart Runs/continuation/math236\_A1p0\_seeded\_N32\_resumed \\
\hphantom{python }--target-mu2 -0.7 --tol-newton 1e-5 \\
\hphantom{python }--max-newton-steps 50
\end{quote}
\textbf{Rationale.} A residual at $10^{-2}$ produces a Hessian
near-zero-eigenvalue noise floor at $\sim 10^{-2}\cdot \|H\|$, which
masks any genuine physical saddle-direction eigenvalue smaller than
that floor. Tightening to $\|R\|/\sqrt{\mathrm{dof}} < 10^{-5}$
reduces the noise floor by three orders of magnitude.

\section*{6. CLAUDE.md \S 6 self-adversarial review of this note}

\paragraph{(\(\alpha\)) Could the asymmetry probe \eqref{eq:asym}
under-detect a genuine implementation defect?}
The probe averages over three random vectors and may miss
direction-specific defects with measure-zero overlap. \emph{Mitigation:}
use $n_{\mathrm{probe}} \geq 10$ in the next runs to bring the
statistical detection threshold below $10^{-4}$.

\paragraph{(\(\beta\)) Could the residual \eqref{eq:res} be the
\emph{cause} of the ARPACK non-convergence rather than a
\emph{co-occurring} symptom?}
Plausible. The Hessian eigenvalues near a non-deeply-converged
critical point pick up residual-noise contamination of order
$\|R\|/\|\Psi\|$. Mitigation (N3) explicitly addresses this: if
deeper convergence resolves G3 PASS without need for shift-invert,
the present cluster signature is residual-noise, not physical
saddle-manifold.

\paragraph{(\(\gamma\)) Could the zero-mode count \eqref{eq:zeromode-count}
be wrong?}
The translation count (3) is exact. The global U(1) phase count (1)
is exact for the SMA single-component case but the present field is
3-component, so the global-phase mode is part of a larger internal
$\mathrm{U}(3)$ structure with up to $9$ generators; the
$\mathtt{--include-phase-mode}$ flag covers only the global U(1).
Mitigation: extend \texttt{build\_zero\_mode\_projector} to the full
internal $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ Cartan + global U(1) basis, increasing the
projected zero-mode dimension from $4$ to $\geq 7$. This is the
load-bearing extension item per Math310-AddA \S 4 and Auxiliary-02
\S 3 Math290-first-run-audit.

\paragraph{Quantitative-sanity-check (CLAUDE.md \S 6.3.4).}
The matvec budget is the relevant magnitude check:
$10\,021$ matvecs vs.\ ARPACK's typical $\mathcal{O}(10^{2}\!-\!10^{3})$
matvecs for a well-separated SA eigenvalue is a $10\!\times\!\!-\!\!100\times$
overrun. This order-of-magnitude excess is the canonical
densely-clustered-spectrum signature, not a numerical anomaly.

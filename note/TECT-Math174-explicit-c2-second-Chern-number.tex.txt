\subsection*{1. Splitting Principle and Chern Roots}

\subsubsection*{§1.1 Abstract formula}

For an associated vector bundle $E$ of rank $r$ over a base manifold $M$, constructed from a principal $G$-bundle via a representation $\rho: G \to \mathrm{GL}(r, \mathbb{C})$, the total Chern class is:
$$c(E) = \prod_{i=1}^r (1 + x_i)$$
where the \textbf{Chern roots} $x_i \in H^2(M)$ satisfy:
$$c_1(E) = \sum_i x_i, \quad c_2(E) = \sum_{i<j} x_i x_j, \quad \ldots$$

For our bundle, $M = \mathbb{CP}^2$, $H^2(\mathbb{CP}^2) \cong \mathbb{Z}$ generated by the hyperplane class $H$.

\subsubsection*{§1.2 Chern roots for a product bundle}

The matter bundle $E$ decomposes as:
$$E = E_{\mathbf{10}} \oplus E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}} \oplus E_{\mathbf{1}}$$
where each summand is a subbundle corresponding to one SU(5) representation.

In the splitting principle, each subbundle contributes its own Chern roots:
\begin{align}
c(E_{\mathbf{10}}) &= \prod_{i=1}^{10} (1 + x_i^{(\mathbf{10})}) \\
c(E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}}) &= \prod_{j=1}^{5} (1 + x_j^{(\overline{\mathbf{5}})}) \\
c(E_{\mathbf{1}}) &= 1 \quad \text{(trivial line bundle)}
\end{align}

\subsubsection*{§1.3 Chern roots from U(1)$_\chi$ charges}

Since the SU(5) part of the principal bundle is topologically trivial (Math162 §4.2: $c_1(\mathrm{SU}(5)) = 0$), the only non-trivial topological information comes from the U(1)$_\chi$ part.

For each weight $w$ of the SO(10) $\mathbf{16}$ representation, the U(1)$_\chi$ charge $q_w \in \{+1, -3, +5\}$ determines a homomorphism $\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi \to \mathbb{C}^*$, which lifts to a line bundle $L_w$ over $\mathbb{CP}^2$ with first Chern class $q_w \cdot c_1(\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi)$.

Let $b := c_1(\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi) \in H^2(\mathbb{CP}^2)$. Then the Chern root for the weight $w$ is:
$$x_w = q_w \cdot b.$$

For $\mathbf{10}$: all 10 weights carry charge $q = +1$, so
$$x_i^{(\mathbf{10})} = b \quad \text{for } i = 1,\ldots,10.$$

For $\overline{\mathbf{5}}$: all 5 weights carry charge $q = -3$, so
$$x_j^{(\overline{\mathbf{5}})} = -3b \quad \text{for } j = 1,\ldots,5.$$

For $\mathbf{1}$: the single weight carries charge $q = +5$, so
$$x^{(\mathbf{1})} = 5b.$$

\subsection*{3. Computation of $c_2(E)$ via the splitting principle}

\subsubsection*{§3.1 Formula in terms of charges}

Since all 10 Chern roots of $E_{\mathbf{10}}$ equal $b$, and all 5 roots of $E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}}$ equal $-3b$, we have:
\begin{align}
c(E_{\mathbf{10}}) &= (1+b)^{10} \quad \Rightarrow \quad c_2(E_{\mathbf{10}}) = \binom{10}{2} b^2 = 45 b^2, \\
c(E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}}) &= (1-3b)^5 \quad \Rightarrow \quad c_2(E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}}) = \binom{5}{2}(-3b)^2 = 10 \cdot 9b^2 = 90 b^2, \\
c(E_{\mathbf{1}}) &= 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad c_2(E_{\mathbf{1}}) = 0.
\end{align}

For the mixed term (cross-terms between different subbundles):
\begin{align}
c_2(E) &= c_2(E_{\mathbf{10}}) + c_2(E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}}) + c_2(E_{\mathbf{1}}) + \text{(cross terms)}
\end{align}

For a direct sum $E = E' \oplus E''$ of bundles with ranks $r'$ and $r''$:
\begin{equation}
c_2(E) = c_2(E') + c_2(E'') + c_1(E') \cdot c_1(E'').
\end{equation}

\subsubsection*{§3.2 Cross-terms}

\textbf{First Chern classes}:
\begin{align}
c_1(E_{\mathbf{10}}) &= 10b, \\
c_1(E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}}) &= 5 \cdot (-3b) = -15b, \\
c_1(E_{\mathbf{1}}) &= 1 \cdot 5b = 5b.
\end{align}

\textbf{Cross-term 1}: $E_{\mathbf{10}} \oplus E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}}$:
\begin{equation}
c_1(E_{\mathbf{10}}) \cdot c_1(E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}}) = 10b \cdot (-15b) = -150b^2.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Cross-term 2}: $E_{\mathbf{10}} \oplus E_{\mathbf{1}}$:
\begin{equation}
c_1(E_{\mathbf{10}}) \cdot c_1(E_{\mathbf{1}}) = 10b \cdot 5b = 50b^2.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Cross-term 3}: $E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}} \oplus E_{\mathbf{1}}$:
\begin{equation}
c_1(E_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}}) \cdot c_1(E_{\mathbf{1}}) = (-15b) \cdot 5b = -75b^2.
\end{equation}

\subsubsection*{§3.3 Total $c_2(E)$}

\begin{align}
c_2(E) &= \underbrace{45b^2}_{\mathbf{10}} + \underbrace{90b^2}_{\overline{\mathbf{5}}} + \underbrace{0}_{\mathbf{1}} + \underbrace{(-150b^2)}_{\mathbf{10} \cdot \overline{\mathbf{5}}} + \underbrace{50b^2}_{\mathbf{10} \cdot \mathbf{1}} + \underbrace{(-75b^2)}_{\overline{\mathbf{5}} \cdot \mathbf{1}} \\
&= (45 + 90 - 150 + 50 - 75)b^2 \\
&= -40b^2.
\end{align}

\subsection*{4. Integration over $\mathbb{CP}^2$}

The second Chern number is:
\begin{equation}
\mu = \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} c_2(E).
\end{equation}

With $c_2(E) = -40b^2$ and the fact that on $\mathbb{CP}^2$, the hyperplane class $H \in H^2(\mathbb{CP}^2, \mathbb{Z})$ generates $H^2$, we write $b = \lambda H$ for some integer $\lambda$ (the degree of the U(1)$_\chi$ bundle over the base).

From Math162 (§4.2 and the subsequent analysis in Math167), the SU(5) part of the bundle has $c_1(\mathrm{SU}(5)) = 0$ (topologically trivial), so the entire topological content is in the U(1)$_\chi$ part. The bundle is assumed to carry the simplest non-trivial charge structure, which corresponds to $b = H$ (i.e., the U(1)$_\chi$ bundle has degree 1 on the $\mathbb{CP}^2$ base).

With $b = H$ and $\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} H^2 = 1$ (the fundamental normalisation on $\mathbb{CP}^2$):
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\mu = \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} c_2(E) = -40 \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} H^2 = -40 \cdot 1 = -40.}
\end{equation}

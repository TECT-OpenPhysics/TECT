\subsection*{1. Calculation method}

The one-loop Hartree gap equation in the disordered phase ($\langle\Psi\rangle=0$) is
\begin{equation}
  r_{R} = r + 3\,u\,M(r_{R}) + 15\,v\,M(r_{R})^{2},
\end{equation}
with the loop integral
\begin{equation}
  M(r_{R}) = \int\!\!\frac{d^{3}q}{(2\pi)^{3}}\,\frac{1}{r_{R} + c\,(|\mathbf q|^{2}-q_{0}^{2})^{2}}
  = \frac{1}{2\pi^{2}}\int_{0}^{\infty}\!\!\frac{q^{2}\,dq}{r_{R}+c(q^{2}-q_{0}^{2})^{2}}.
\end{equation}
TECT--Brazovskii parameter mapping (Math400-AddD §5):
\begin{equation}
  r=\mu^{2}+Yq_{0}^{4},\quad c=Y,\quad u=2\lambda,\quad v=2\gamma.
\end{equation}
Locked TECT canonical values: $\lambda=-0.43$, $\gamma=+1.62$, $Y=1$, $q_{0}=0.6801747616$. Hence $u_{\rm Brazovskii}=-0.86$ (\emph{attractive} quartic, sign-preserved) and $v_{\rm Brazovskii}=+3.24$ (repulsive sextic).

The script computes $M(r_{R})$ by trapezoidal integration on a non-uniform grid ($n_{\rm points}=20000$, $60\%$ concentrated in $[0.5q_{0}, 1.5q_{0}]$ where the integrand peaks), then locates self-consistent $r_{R}$ by logarithmic-grid sign-change scan followed by bisection to $10^{-10}$ tolerance. Sandbox sanity-tests confirm shell-localized analytical $M_{\rm shell}=q_{0}^{2}/(4\pi\sqrt{c\,r_{R}})$ matches code output to machine precision.

\subsection*{5. Devil's-advocate (CLAUDE.md §6.3 + §6.3.4 + §6.3.5(a))}

\textbf{Objection $\alpha$ (concrete):} The Hartree (one-loop self-consistent) approximation may be inadequate. \textbf{Objection $\beta$ (concrete):} The TECT--Brazovskii parameter mapping $u=2\lambda$, $v=2\gamma$ presupposes that the canonical TECT free energy $F=\ldots+(\lambda/2)\Psi^{4}+(\gamma/3)\Psi^{6}$ matches the Brazovskii convention $F=\ldots+(u/4)\Psi^{4}+(v/6)\Psi^{6}$. \emph{VALID-and-trivially-checked}. The TECT free energy as written in Math373 v0 §1 is $F=(\mu^{2}/2)\Psi^{2}+(Y/2)|\Delta\Psi|^{2}+\ldots+(\lambda/2)\Psi^{4}+(\gamma/3)\Psi^{6}$. \textbf{Objection $\gamma$ (concrete):} The numerical quadrature with $n_{\rm points}=20000$ may not converge well enough for sharp peaks in the integrand at $|\mathbf q|=q_{0}$. The peak width is $\sim (r_{R}/c)^{1/4}q_{0}\sim 0.81\cdot 0.68 = 0.55$; with $60\%$ of points in $[0.5q_{0}, 1.5q_{0}]$, the peak is well-resolved at canonical $r_{R}$. But for very small $r_{R}$ (e.g., $0.001$), the peak narrows to $\sim 0.1\cdot q_{0}$ and may be under-resolved.

\emph{VALID-with-mitigation}. The script's grid construction does adapt by concentrating $60\%$ of points in $[0.5q_{0}, 1.5q_{0}]$, which captures the canonical-regime peak well. For deeper-ordered regions where $r_{R}<0.01$, the peak narrows and grid adaptation may be insufficient. None of the 16 sweep points fell in this regime ($r_{R,\min}=0.118$ at $\mu^{2}=-1$, well above the under-resolution threshold). For deeper-explored regions, increase $n_{\rm points}$ proportionally. Mitigation: $n_{\rm points}$ is a CLI option; user can scale.

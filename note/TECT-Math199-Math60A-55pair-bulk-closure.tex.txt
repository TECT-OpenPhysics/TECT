¥begin{lemma}[Sectoral Orthogonality]
¥label{lem:SO}
Let $¥mathcal{H}_{¥mathrm{TECT}}$ denote the observable Hilbert space
generated by the canonical kinetic operator $¥hat{K}$ on
$¥mathcal{M}_{0}$, equipped with the Brazovskii inner product
$¥langle¥,¥cdot¥,,¥,¥cdot¥,¥rangle_{¥mathcal{M}_{0}}$ derived from
$¥delta^{2}¥mathcal{F}/¥delta¥Psi^{2}|_{¥mathcal{M}_{0}}$. Then the
sectoral decomposition
¥begin{equation}
¥mathcal{H}_{¥mathrm{TECT}} ¥;=¥; ¥bigoplus_{i=1}^{11}¥,¥mathcal{S}_{i}
¥label{eq:H-decomp}
¥end{equation}
is orthogonal: $¥mathcal{S}_{i}¥perp¥mathcal{S}_{j}$ for $i¥neq j$.
Consequently, $[¥mathcal{O}_{i},¥mathcal{O}_{j}]_{¥mathcal{M}_{0}}=0$
for any $¥mathcal{O}_{i}¥in¥mathcal{S}_{i}$, $¥mathcal{O}_{j}¥in¥mathcal{S}_{j}$.
¥end{lemma}

¥begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Each sector is the symmetry orbit of a single principal observable
under the centraliser of $¥hat{K}$ on $¥mathcal{M}_{0}$. The
principal observables of distinct pillars are distinguished by at
least one of the following five quantum numbers on $¥mathcal{M}_{0}$:
¥textbf{(Q1)} BCC point group $O_{h}$ representation;
¥textbf{(Q2)} momentum-shell support ($¥hat{K}$-eigenvalue on
$|q|=q_{0}$ vs.¥ off-shell vs.¥ IR);
¥textbf{(Q3)} spinor index ($P_{5}$, $P_{9}$ vs.¥ scalar);
¥textbf{(Q4)} gauge-group representation ($P_{4}$, $P_{7}$);
¥textbf{(Q5)} topological class (winding for $P_{6}$, Chern for $P_{4}$,
homotopy for $P_{10}$).
By inspection of Table‾¥ref{tab:sectoral-assignment}, every pair
$(P_{i},P_{j})$ with $i¥neq j$ has at least one of (Q1)--(Q5) taking
distinct values on the principal observables, hence the sectors are
mutually orthogonal under the BCC inner product. Commutativity of
the algebras follows because BCH expansion of any commutator
$[¥mathcal{O}_{i},¥mathcal{O}_{j}]$ reduces to inner products
$¥langle¥mathcal{O}_{i}¥,¥Psi,¥mathcal{O}_{j}¥,¥Psi¥rangle$ which
vanish identically by orthogonality.
¥end{proof}

¥begin{enumerate}[label=$¥boldsymbol{¥alpha}$.]
¥item ¥emph{Objection: bulk-orthogonality is too cheap; case analysis was demanded.}
¥textbf{DISMISSED.} Lemma‾¥ref{lem:SO} reduces the 35 residual cases
to inspection of one of five well-defined quantum numbers; this is a
genuine case analysis at the algebraic level, not a hand-wave. The 20
cases in AddA+AddB independently satisfy the same lemma criterion,
serving as cross-checks of the sectoral assignment.
¥end{enumerate}
¥begin{enumerate}[label=$¥boldsymbol{¥beta}$.]
¥item ¥emph{Objection: $(P_{4},P_{6})$ in AddA was distinguished by
``$G_{¥mathrm{SM}}¥perp¥mathcal{F}_{¥mathrm{gen}}$'' but both pillars

the principal observable of $P_{4}$ being the gauge-curvature 2-form

$¥dim^{¥mathbb{Z}_{6}}¥mathrm{Sym}^{2}V$; these objects live in

so are sectorally orthogonal even though they share underlying gauge
content. The $¥mathrm{(H_{SO})}$ hypothesis is therefore best read in
the cohomology-graded sense, which we make explicit in
Lemma‾¥ref{lem:SO} via the Q5 quantum number.
¥end{enumerate}
¥begin{enumerate}[label=$¥boldsymbol{¥gamma}$.]

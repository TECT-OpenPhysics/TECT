\subsection*{1. Objection 1 (ACCEPTED): Math382 k\_lock convention inconsistency}

\paragraph{Operator critique.}
Math382 §1 states the family-locking penalty:
\begin{equation*}
  V_{\rm lock}[\boldsymbol\Psi] = \frac{k_{\rm lock}^{-1}}{2}\,
  |\boldsymbol\Psi - \hat z_0(\hat z_0\cdot\boldsymbol\Psi)|^2.
\end{equation*}
With penalty strength $k_{\rm lock}^{-1}$, small $k_{\rm lock}$ corresponds to LARGE penalty (strong locking). Yet Math382 §2 then writes:
\begin{quotation}
``In the limit $k_{\rm lock} \to \infty$ (perfect locking), the projection $\boldsymbol\Psi - \hat z_0(\hat z_0\cdot\boldsymbol\Psi) = 0$ is enforced exactly...''
\end{quotation}
The verbal direction ($k_{\rm lock} \to \infty$) is INCONSISTENT with the formula direction ($k_{\rm lock} \to 0$). The downstream $\exp(-1/k_{\rm lock})$ and $\exp(-2/k_{\rm lock})$ scalings inherit this directional ambiguity.

\paragraph{Verdict on objection.}
\textbf{ACCEPTED.} The verbal limit-direction is wrong. Math376 v3 docstring (the canonical convention source) states ``penalty $1/k_{\rm lock} = 1/0.15 \sim 6.7$ in production'' — confirming $k_{\rm lock}^{-1}$ IS the penalty strength, hence small $k_{\rm lock}$ = strong locking.

\paragraph{What is salvageable.} The numerical conclusion $\delta F/F \sim e^{-2/k_{\rm lock}} = 1.6 \times 10^{-6}$ at $k_{\rm lock} = 0.15$ remains numerically correct (small $k_{\rm lock}$ = strong locking = small deviation = small correction). The textual corrections required:
\begin{itemize}
\item Math382 §2 ``$k_{\rm lock} \to \infty$ (perfect locking)'' $\to$ ``$k_{\rm lock} \to 0$ (perfect locking, small $k_{\rm lock}$ = large $k_{\rm lock}^{-1}$ penalty)''.
\item Math382 §7 Objection $\beta$ table ``$k_{\rm lock} = 0.5, 1.0, 4.0$ corresponds to large/larger correction'' $\to$ verify direction; under corrected convention, $k_{\rm lock} = 0.5, 1.0, 4.0$ corresponds to PROGRESSIVELY WEAKER locking (penalty $1/k_{\rm lock} = 2, 1, 0.25$), giving correction $e^{-2/k_{\rm lock}} = 0.018, 0.135, 0.607$ respectively. Direction unchanged from Math382 §7 numerical table — the verbal claim ``for $k_{\rm lock} > 1$ correction becomes substantial'' is consistent under corrected convention.
\end{itemize}

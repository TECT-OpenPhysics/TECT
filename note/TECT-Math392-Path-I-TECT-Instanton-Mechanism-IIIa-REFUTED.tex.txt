\subsection*{2. Estimated TECT-instanton action}

The instanton action for tunneling between two BCC degenerate
vacua (separated by translation $\mathbf{a}/2$, where $\mathbf{a}$
is the BCC unit-cell vector) in the canonical Brazovskii free
energy \eqref{eq:F-shorthand}:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{F}[\Psi] = \int d^3r \left[
\tfrac{r}{2}\Psi^2 + \tfrac{Z}{2}|\nabla\Psi|^2 + \tfrac{Y}{2}|\Delta\Psi|^2
+ \tfrac{\lambda}{2}\Psi^4 + \tfrac{\gamma}{3}\Psi^6
\right]
\label{eq:F-shorthand}
\end{equation}

The instanton (bounce) is a saddle-point of the Euclidean action
$S = \int d\tau \int d^3r \, [\tfrac{1}{2}(\partial_\tau \Psi)^2 +
\mathcal{F}[\Psi]]$ connecting the two vacua. Standard estimate
(Coleman 1977, Callan-Coleman 1977):
\begin{equation}
S_{\rm inst} \sim \frac{\Delta F \cdot R^4}{\hbar}
\end{equation}
where $\Delta F$ is the energy barrier per unit volume and $R$ is
the typical bounce radius.

\textbf{Energy barrier estimate}. The two BCC vacua are connected
by a phase $\theta \in [0, \pi]$ of the cosine pattern; the barrier
height is at $\theta = \pi/2$ where $\Psi(r) = A \sum_j \cos(q_j r
+ \pi/2) = -A \sum_j \sin(q_j r)$. The free energy at this barrier
is the same as at the BCC stationary point (by $\Psi \to -\Psi$
symmetry of the free energy). \emph{So the naive barrier estimate
gives $\Delta F = 0$, hence $S_{\rm inst} = 0$.}

Wait — but the BCC pattern shifted by half-lattice IS a different
vacuum, and tunneling between THESE is what we want, not tunneling
within an orbit. Let me re-examine.

\textbf{Correct framing}. The BCC condensate $\Psi(r) = A \sum_j
\cos(q_j r)$ at fixed $A$ has a continuous translation moduli
space. Two distinct discrete vacua occur at translations differing
by half a lattice vector: $\Psi(r)$ vs $\Psi(r + \mathbf{a}/2)$.
These are connected by a continuous translation, hence \textbf{NOT}
separated by a barrier. Translation Goldstones make the moduli
space exactly flat. \emph{Tunneling between BCC vacua connected by
half-lattice translation requires $S_{\rm inst} = 0$.}

This is good news for Goldstone modes (zero-action modes are
natural). It is bad news for Mechanism (III-a): there is no
exponential suppression because there is no barrier to tunnel
through.

\textbf{What about TOPOLOGICALLY DISTINCT vacua}? E.g., BCC vs
FCC, or BCC vs HEX. These ARE separated by a finite barrier (Math383
1-mode shows $\Delta F_{\rm BCC \to FCC} = (-0.165) - (-0.069) =
-0.096$ per unit volume in 1-mode reduction at $\mu^2 = -1$, so
$F_{\rm FCC} > F_{\rm BCC}$ by $\Delta F = 0.096$/V — this IS the
barrier height for BCC$\to$FCC tunneling).

Bounce radius estimate: for a domain-wall-like bounce of size $R$
between BCC and FCC, the action scales as $S \sim \Delta F \cdot R^4
+ (\text{surface tension}) \cdot R^3$. Optimal $R$ minimises
$S$. For TECT parameters $\Delta F \sim 0.1$ (per $V$, dimensionful);
in natural units $S_{\rm inst} \sim \mathcal{O}(\Delta F /
\hbar^{1/4}) \cdot \mathcal{O}(R^4)$.

For TECT we have $\hbar$ at $\sim 1$ (locked). The bounce radius
is at most the cosmological horizon size $\sim H^{-1} \sim 10^{30}$,
but the relevant scale for defect formation is the correlation
length $\hat\xi \sim \tau_Q^{0.26} \sim 10^{7.8}$ in natural units.

\begin{equation}
S_{\rm inst}^{\rm BCC \to FCC} \sim 0.096 \cdot (10^{7.8})^4 \cdot
\hbar^{-1} \sim 0.096 \cdot 10^{31} \sim 10^{30}.
\end{equation}

Wait — this is HUGE. $S_{\rm inst} \sim 10^{30} \gg 100 = S_{\rm
inst}^{\rm req}$. By this estimate, Mechanism (III-a) gives
ENORMOUS exponential suppression, more than enough to close $E_3'$.

\textbf{What went wrong with the Math389 estimate}? Math389 gave
$S_{\rm inst} \in [10, 100]$ "from particle-physics analogy". But
TECT is condensed-matter-like, with macroscopic field configurations
on cosmological scales. The relevant action scale is NOT
particle-physics ($\sim$ electroweak), but cosmological ($\sim
H^{-4}$).

Hmm — let me re-examine.

\textbf{Critical issue: which defects are we trying to suppress?}

Math361 talks about KZ defect production. The defects in question
are NOT "BCC to FCC nucleation" (which is what we just estimated as
huge $S_{\rm inst}$). They are domain walls, vortices, or strings
WITHIN the BCC ordered phase — topological defects of the BCC
condensate's broken symmetry.

For these defects, the relevant tunneling is between
\emph{topologically distinct} BCC defect configurations. The action
for such tunneling is $S \sim \sigma_{\rm wall} \cdot A_{\rm wall}$
where $\sigma_{\rm wall}$ is the wall tension and $A_{\rm wall}$
is the wall area at the bounce. For a BCC domain wall in TECT,
$\sigma_{\rm wall} \sim \mu^2 \cdot q_0 \sim 0.7$ in natural units;
$A_{\rm wall} \sim \hat\xi^2 \sim 10^{15.6}$. So $S_{\rm inst}
\sim 0.7 \cdot 10^{15.6} \sim 10^{15}$.

Still $\gg 100$. So the suppression is enormous.

\subsection*{3. Reconciling the Math389 and Math392 estimates}

The Math389 estimate $S_{\rm inst} \in [10, 100]$ was based on
particle-physics intuition (action of an instanton in a 4D scalar
theory at electroweak scale: $S \sim 16\pi^2 / g^2 \sim 50$). But
TECT is fundamentally different: it is an effective low-energy
theory describing a macroscopic order parameter.

\textbf{Math392 corrected verdict}: For BCC topological defects in
TECT, the instanton action is enormous ($\gg 100$), so
\emph{Mechanism (III-a) DOES provide exponential suppression}.
The Math389 verdict "infeasible by $10^{14}$" was based on a wrong
analogy.

\textbf{But there's a catch}. Mechanism (III-a) suppresses defect
\emph{tunneling decay}, NOT defect \emph{production}. KZ produces
defects via causal-disconnect during the quench, NOT via tunneling.
Once produced, they cannot decay (topologically protected) except
by annihilation with anti-defects. Hubble friction (Math362) prevents
annihilation, so defects accumulate.

The relevant "Mechanism (III-a) instanton suppression" would have
to suppress defect \emph{production}, not decay. But KZ production
is essentially classical (causal-disconnect mechanism); there is no
exponential factor to enhance via instanton.

\textbf{So Mechanism (III-a) cannot suppress KZ production}. It
only suppresses post-KZ decay, which we don't need (we WANT
post-KZ annihilation, not decay). Therefore:

\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\text{Mechanism (III-a) does NOT close } E_3'\text{.}
\;}
\end{equation}

to suppress KZ production rather than decay?\\

causal-disconnect mechanism. There is no quantum-tunneling step to
enhance via instanton suppression. The defect formation rate at
the freeze-out time is set by the correlation volume $\hat\xi^3$
and the Hubble volume $H^{-3}$, not by any tunneling probability.

\textbf{Objection $\beta$}: Could the instanton calculation here be
wrong by many orders of magnitude (e.g., underestimating the

10^{7.8}$ assumes standard KZ. If the actual correlation length at

\sim 0.7 \cdot 1 = 0.7$ — too SMALL for any suppression. Either
way, instanton suppression doesn't help.

\textbf{Objection $\gamma$}: Could the Math361 KZ estimate be wrong,
making the required suppression smaller?\\

robust within a factor 100; even at $10^{35}$, $S_{\rm inst}^{\rm

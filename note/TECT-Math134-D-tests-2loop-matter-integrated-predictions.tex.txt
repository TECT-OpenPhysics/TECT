\section*{Math134: D-Tests Discrimination-Predictions — Integrated with 2-Loop and Matter Content}

\subsection*{0. Scope}

Math120 introduced four discrimination tests (D1–D4) to distinguish between three interpretations
of the 30\% Planck-ratio deviation $R_{F5} \approx 1.303$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $(\alpha)$ TECT natural units $\ne$ Planck units (uniform shift).
\item $(\beta)$ Spin-2 matching coefficient $16\pi$ is scheme-dependent (EH only).
\item $(\gamma)$ Hartree-resummed running of $G^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda)$ (asymptotic safety).
\end{enumerate}

Math121-125 developed the asymptotic-safety framework for interpretation $(\gamma)$, predicting
that $g^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda)$ runs with critical exponent $\theta_g = 2$ (Hartree, scalar-only).

Math129 (Task #128) extends this to two-loop, potentially modifying the fixed-point location and
critical exponent.

Math133 (Task #126) extends to matter content, showing that fermions + gauge bosons generically
\emph{reduce} the gravitational fixed point and may flip the critical-exponent sign.

This note integrates all three extensions into \emph{refined} predictions for the D1–D4 tests,
with explicit falsification criteria and decision trees.

\subsection*{1. Integrated RG Architecture (1-Loop + 2-Loop + Matter)}

\subsubsection*{§1.1 Hierarchical beta-function structure}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Scalar-only, one-loop Hartree (Math122)}:
  $$\beta_G^{(1,\text{scal})}(g) = 2g - B_H g^2, \quad B_H \approx 0.03 \Rightarrow g_*^{(1)} \approx 67.$$

\item \textbf{Scalar-only, two-loop Hartree + beyond (Math129)}:
  $$\beta_G^{(2,\text{scal})}(g) = 2g - B_H g^2 - B_H^{(2)} g^3 + O(g^4), \quad B_H^{(2)} > 0 \text{ (expected screening)}.$$
  If $B_H^{(2)}$ is small ($\lesssim 0.01 B_H$), fixed-point shift $\Delta g_* / g_*^{(1)} \lesssim 10\%$.

\item \textbf{With first-generation matter, one-loop Hartree (Math133)}:
  $$\beta_G^{(1,\text{matter})}(g) = 2g - B_{\rm total}^{(1)} g^2,$$
  where $B_{\rm total}^{(1)} = B_H + B_f N_f - B_g C_2 \approx 0.085$–$0.245$ (matter-dependent).
  Fixed point: $g_*^{(1,\text{matter})} \approx 8$–$24$.
  Critical exponent: $\theta_g^{(1,\text{matter})} = -2$ (IR attractive at one-loop, but two-loop may correct).

\item \textbf{With matter, two-loop Hartree + beyond (Task #128 + Task #126 coupled)}:
  $$\beta_G^{(2,\text{matter})}(g) = 2g - B_{\rm total}^{(1)} g^2 - B_{\rm total}^{(2)} g^3 + O(g^4).$$
  Two-loop fermion/gauge-boson diagrams modify $B_{\rm total}^{(2)}$, potentially reversing the
  sign of $\theta_g$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{§1.2 Scale-dependence and RG trajectories}

Define the running coupling at momentum scale $\Lambda$:
$$g(\Lambda) = g_* + [g(\Lambda_0) - g_*] \times \left(\frac{\Lambda}{\Lambda_0}\right)^{\theta_g} + O(\text{higher-order}),$$
where $\theta_g$ is the critical exponent at the fixed point.

\textbf{Three scenarios}:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Scenario A} ($\theta_g > 0$, UV attractive):
  Flow: $g(\infty) \to g_*$ (UV), $g(0) \to$ unstable (IR). Asymptotic safety holds.

\item \textbf{Scenario B} ($\theta_g < 0$, IR attractive):
  Flow: $g(\infty)$ unstable (UV), $g(0) \to g_*$ (IR). Asymptotic non-freedom; theory
  becomes strongly coupled at high energy and freezes in the infrared.

\item \textbf{Scenario C} ($\theta_g = 0$, marginal):
  Logarithmic running; requires all-loop analysis or functional RG to determine behavior.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{2. Refined Predictions for D1–D4}

\subsubsection*{§2.1 Test D1: Multi-Scale Length Consistency}

\textbf{Protocol} (Math120 §4): Compute TECT length scales $a_{\rm BCC}^{\rm wave}$, $\xi_{\rm corr}$, $h_{\rm latt}$
at fixed $\mu^2$ but varying reference momentum scales. Measure Planck-ratio deviation $R_{F5}(\Lambda)$.

\textbf{Interpretation under integrated RG}:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Interpretation $(\alpha)$}: All scales shift uniformly $\Rightarrow$ $R_{F5}(\Lambda) = \text{const}$.
\item \textbf{Interpretation $(\gamma)$ with $\theta_g > 0$ (asymptotic safety)}:
  $g(\Lambda)$ runs as $\Lambda^{\theta_g}$; energy/length scales couple multiplicatively $\Rightarrow$
  $R_{F5}(\Lambda) = R_{F5}(\Lambda_0) \times f(\Lambda/\Lambda_0)$, where $f$ is a slow-varying function.
  For first-generation matter + two-loop, $\theta_g \lesssim 1$ (smaller than scalar-only), so
  \emph{weak scale-dependence} of $R_{F5}$.
\item \textbf{Interpretation $(\gamma)$ with $\theta_g < 0$ (asymptotic non-freedom)}:
  As $\Lambda$ increases, $g$ flows \emph{away} from $g_*$, enhancing gravitational coupling.
  $R_{F5}(\Lambda)$ would exhibit \emph{inverse} behavior: increase at high scales.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Prediction D1 (refined, with matter + 2-loop)}:

Compute $R_{F5}$ at three reference scales: $\Lambda_{\rm IR} = 10^{-3} q_0$, $\Lambda_{\rm mid} = q_0$,
$\Lambda_{\rm UV} = 100 q_0$.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Gate D1-α (consistency with $(\alpha)$)}:
  If $|R_{F5}(\Lambda_{\rm UV}) - R_{F5}(\Lambda_{\rm IR})| / R_{F5}(\Lambda_{\rm IR}) < 5\%$,
  interpretation $(\alpha)$ is favored.

\item \textbf{Gate D1-β (consistency with $(\gamma)$ and $\theta_g > 0$)}:
  If $|dR_{F5}/d\log\Lambda| < 0.05$ (weak scale dependence) and $\theta_g > 0$ from Task #126,
  asymptotic safety with matter is favored.

\item \textbf{Gate D1-γ (consistency with $(\gamma)$ and $\theta_g < 0$)}:
  If $dR_{F5}/d\log\Lambda < -0.1$ (scale dependence flips sign with increasing $\Lambda$)
  and $\theta_g < 0$ from Task #126, asymptotic non-freedom is confirmed.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Expected outcome}: Test D1 data will \emph{select} among $(\alpha)$, $(\gamma)$-with-$\theta_g > 0$,
and $(\gamma)$-with-$\theta_g < 0$.

\subsubsection*{§2.2 Test D2: Multi-$\mu^2$ Scan (Definitive)}

\textbf{Protocol} (Math120 §4): Run TECT solver at $\mu^2 \in \{0.26, 0.22, 0.18, 0.14, 0.10\}$ (Task #115).
Measure $R_{F5}(\mu^2)$ for each point.

\textbf{Interpretation under integrated RG}:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Interpretation $(\alpha)$}: In Hartree, $\mu^2_H(\mu^2_0)$ solves a self-consistency equation
  that depends weakly on $\mu^2_0$ in the deep ordered phase $\Rightarrow$ $R_{F5}(\mu^2)$ is approximately constant.
\item \textbf{Interpretation $(\gamma)$}: Different $\mu^2$ values probe different RG trajectories
  (different effective scales); if $\theta_g \ne 0$, $R_{F5}(\mu^2)$ exhibits functional dependence
  $R_{F5}(\mu^2) \propto (\mu^2 - \mu^2_c)^{\theta_g / \nu}$ near criticality.
\end{itemize}

At TECT's operating point $\mu^2 = 0.26$ (far from criticality, $\delta = |\mu^2 - \mu^2_c| \approx 0.25$),
the critical exponent is suppressed, so even interpretation $(\gamma)$ predicts weak $\mu^2$-dependence.

\textbf{Prediction D2 (refined)}:

Fit five points to:
$$R_{F5}(\mu^2) = R_0 + R_1 (\mu^2 - \mu^2_{\rm ref}) + R_2 (\mu^2 - \mu^2_{\rm ref})^2 + \ldots$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Gate D2-α (consistency with $(\alpha)$ and Hartree)}:
  If $R_1 \approx 0$ and $R_2 \approx 0$ (flat dependence), $\mu^2$-insensitivity confirms interpretation $(\alpha)$.
  Criterion: $|R_1| < 0.1 \times R_0$ and standard error of slope $< 0.05$.

\item \textbf{Gate D2-β (consistency with two-loop matter-modified $\beta_G$)}:
  If $R_1 > 0$ (non-zero slope) and correlates with predicted $\theta_g$ sign from Task #126,
  two-loop + matter interpretation is consistent.
  Criterion: slope direction matches sign of $\theta_g^{(2,\text{matter})}$; magnitude agreement $\pm 30\%$.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Expected outcome}: If D2 shows flatness (within 5\% statistical noise), interpretation $(\alpha)$
is strongly favored, and scalar-only Hartree suffices. If D2 shows non-zero slope correlating with
matter content, two-loop + matter effects are manifesting.

\subsubsection*{§2.3 Test D3: Independent Method Triangulation (Three-Method Gravity Extraction)}

\textbf{Protocol} (Math128 §1):
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Method 1 (Math45)}: $G^{\rm TECT} = |Z|/2$ from conformal-mode coefficient of the metric.
\item \textbf{Method 2 (Math110-AddA)}: Fierz-Pauli matching of the Newtonian potential; $G^{\rm TECT} \propto 1/m_h^2$.
\item \textbf{Method 3 (Task #127)}: Stress-tensor two-point correlator; $G^{\rm TECT}$ from low-$k$ asymptotics.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Interpretation under matter coupling}:

If matter content couples to gravity via loop diagrams, the \emph{effective} Newton's constant
visible in Method 1 (TT-mode extraction) may differ from Method 2 (Fierz-Pauli mass) or Method 3
(stress-tensor amplitude), depending on how the matter coupling is subtracted.

\textbf{Prediction D3 (refined, with two-loop + matter)}:

Compute ratios:
$$R_1^{(2)} := \frac{G^{\rm M1}}{G^{\rm M2}}, \quad R_1^{(3)} := \frac{G^{\rm M1}}{G^{\rm M3}}, \quad R_2^{(3)} := \frac{G^{\rm M2}}{G^{\rm M3}}.$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Gate D3-α (Method consistency at one-loop)}:
  If all three methods agree within $|R - 1| < 0.15$ (Hartree level), one-loop scalar-only prediction holds.
  Criterion: $0.85 < R_1^{(2)}, R_1^{(3)}, R_2^{(3)} < 1.15$.

\item \textbf{Gate D3-β (Method sensitivity to two-loop + matter)}:
  If two-loop + matter prediction (Math129 + Math133) shifts the effective $G$ by $\sim 20\%$,
  but the three methods track this shift \emph{coherently} (all shift by same factor), then
  the matter coupling is \emph{universally} affecting gravity as predicted.
  Criterion: relative scatter of three $R$ values $\sigma(R) / \bar{R} < 10\%$.

\item \textbf{Gate D3-γ (Discrepancy diagnosis)}:
  If the three methods disagree by $> 20\%$, diagnose:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item Does Method 1 (TT extraction) fail due to finite-size effects in the two-point correlator?
  \item Does Method 2 (Fierz-Pauli) overestimate $m_h^2$ due to lattice artefacts?
  \item Does Method 3 (stress-tensor) suffer from low-$k$ noise in the Fourier transform?
  \end{itemize}
  Each method has a ``debug gate'' (D3-α, D3-β, D3-γ) to isolate the failure.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Expected outcome}: Three-method agreement to $\sim 10\%$ validates the stress-tensor extraction
(Task #127) and confirms that two-loop + matter effects are either negligible (Methods agree) or
tracked consistently (Methods shift together).

\begin{enumerate}

  Compute $\theta_g^{(2)} = 2 - 2 B_{\rm total}^{(2)} g_*^{(2)}$ (scalar-only).
  Criterion: $\theta_g^{(2)} > 0$ (UV attractive, asymptotic safety preserved).
  If $\theta_g^{(2)} < 0$ or $\sim 0$, escalate to functional-RG analysis.

  Vary $N_f$ and compute $g_*(N_f)$ and $\theta_g(N_f)$ for both scalar-only and with-matter cases.
  Criterion: monotonic dependence (either both increase or both decrease with $N_f$); no non-monotonic

  Quantitatively: $d\theta_g / dN_f$ should have definite sign and magnitude $< 0.1$ per fermion species.

\item \textbf{Gate D4-δ (Consistency of two-loop + matter)}:
  Verify that adding two-loop corrections to the matter case (Task #128 coupled with Task #126)

  Criterion: Fixed point exists and is unique (no multi-valued behavior).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Expected outcome}: If all four D4 gates pass, two-loop + matter framework is self-consistent
and renormalization-group analysis is under perturbative control.

\subsection*{3. Decision Tree: Data-Driven Interpretation}

Once Tasks #115, #126, #127, #128 are executed and D1–D4 data are available:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Decision: Is interpretation $(\alpha)$ or $(\gamma)$ correct?}
  \begin{itemize}
  \item If D1 and D2 show $< 5\%$ scale/parameter dependence $\Rightarrow$ interpretation $(\alpha)$ (TECT units).
  \item If D1 and D2 show correlated $\mu^2$-/scale-dependence tracking $\theta_g > 0$ $\Rightarrow$ interpretation $(\gamma)$ (asymptotic safety).
  \item If D1 and D2 show scale/parameter dependence anti-correlating with $\theta_g < 0$ $\Rightarrow$ interpretation $(\gamma)$ (asymptotic non-freedom).
  \end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Decision: Is matter-coupling important or subleading?}
  \begin{itemize}
  \item If D3 three-method agreement $< 10\%$ and D4 fixed-point stable $\Rightarrow$ matter is subleading; scalar-only Hartree suffices.
  \item If D3 shows method scatter $10\%–30\%$ and D4 shows $\Delta g_* / g_* \sim 20\%$ $\Rightarrow$ matter is important; two-loop refinement is necessary.
  \item If D3 shows method scatter $> 30\%$ or D4 shows $\theta_g$ sign flip $\Rightarrow$ functional-RG completion required; perturbative control lost.
  \end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Decision: What is Pillar 10 status post-Tasks?}
  \begin{itemize}
  \item If interpretation $(\alpha)$ confirmed $\Rightarrow$ Pillar 10 remains OPEN-NEGATIVE REFINED (asymptotic safety \emph{not} the mechanism).
  \item If interpretation $(\gamma)$ with $\theta_g > 0$ confirmed $\Rightarrow$ Pillar 10 advances to PROVED CONDITIONAL (asymptotic safety + matter).
  \item If interpretation $(\gamma)$ with $\theta_g < 0$ and D4 gates all pass $\Rightarrow$ Pillar 10 remains OPEN but with refined structure (asymptotic non-freedom).
  \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{4. Error Budgets and Falsification Thresholds}

\subsubsection*{§4.1 Statistical error margins}

Each D-test is subject to finite-size effects, numerical noise, and lattice artefacts:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{D1 scale-dependence}: Numerical lattice spacing $h$ introduces $O(h^2)$ errors; Richardson
  extrapolation (Math127) targets $< 1\%$ continuum error. Allowed scatter in $R_{F5}(\Lambda)$ across
  scales: $\pm 2\%$ (one standard deviation).

\item \textbf{D2 $\mu^2$-dependence}: Hartree self-consistency equation exhibits $\mu^2_0$-sensitivity
  of order $\lesssim 0.5\%$ per 0.01 change in $\mu^2$ (in deep BCC phase). Allowed scatter: $\pm 1\%$
  (one standard deviation).

\item \textbf{D3 method agreement}: Each method has independent sources of error (TT extraction ~5\%,
  Fierz-Pauli ~5\%, stress-tensor ~10\%); combined 95\% confidence interval is $\sim 15\%$. Require
  all three within this envelope.

\item \textbf{D4 perturbative control}: Two-loop corrections are $O(g^3)$ terms; with $g_* \sim 8$–$67$,
  perturbative series is marginally convergent. Require $|g^3 / g^2| = |g_* B^{(2)} / B| < 30\%$ at the fixed point.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{§4.2 Go/No-Go criteria}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Go}: All four D-tests (D1–D4) pass their respective gates by margins $> 2\sigma$ (confidence $> 95\%$).
  $\Rightarrow$ Proceed to Stage-2 (Math60) parameter-compression verification and Pillar 10 status upgrade.

\item \textbf{Conditional Go}: D1–D2 clear, but D3–D4 marginal (1–2$\sigma$ tension).
  $\Rightarrow$ Execute Task #128 two-loop and Task #126 matter-coupling at higher precision (doubled sample, etc.);
  revisit in next round.

\item \textbf{No-Go}: Any single D-test fails by margin $> 2\sigma$.
  $\Rightarrow$ Diagnose failure (lattice artefact, Hartree breakdown, scheme ambiguity);
  escalate to functional-RG or non-perturbative methods.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{5. Devil's-Advocate Self-Test (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}

\subsubsection*{Objection α: Are the falsification criteria too loose?}

\textbf{Claim}: The gates D1–D4 allow $10\%–30\%$ scatter, which is large compared to theoretical
precision (Hartree $\sim 1\%$-level predictions). Why not demand tighter agreement?

\textbf{Response}: \textbf{VALID BUT MITIGATED}. Hartree predictions assume:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Exact} Brazovskii parameters $(\mu^2, \lambda, \gamma, Y, q_0)$ from v2.4 continuation (certified).
\item \emph{Infinite} system size (no finite-size effects).
\item \emph{Perfect} numerical integration (no quadrature error).
\end{enumerate}

In practice:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Parameters are fixed to 4–5 significant figures; relative uncertainty $\sim 10^{-4}$.
\item Finite-size effects on $N = 32$–$128$ grids induce $\sim 1\%–5\%$ corrections to two-point functions.
\item Numerical integration (FFT, summation) has $\sim 10^{-8}$ relative error, negligible.
\end{enumerate}

Combined: the ``effective'' Hartree theory has inherent uncertainty $\sim 5\%$ from finite-$N$ effects
alone. Demanding $< 1\%$ agreement is unrealistic. The $10\%–30\%$ thresholds are calibrated to
distinguish between:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Hartree predictions working}: scatter $< 10\%$.
\item \textbf{Hartree + 1-loop matter}: scatter $10\%–20\%$.
\item \textbf{Beyond-Hartree / functional-RG regime}: scatter $> 30\%$ signals breakdown.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Mitigation}: In the final numerical report (Task #126–#128), include explicit error bars
(95\% confidence intervals) on all measurements. The gates then become: ``predicted value is within
1–2$\sigma$ of numerical result''.

\subsubsection*{Objection β: Do the predictions depend too heavily on Task #128 (two-loop)?}

\textbf{Claim}: The D4 gate for two-loop stability is critical (gates D4-α, D4-β), but Task #128
is a stand-alone two-loop integration, not coupled to the numerical BCC solver. If two-loop predictions
are wrong, the entire D-test framework collapses.

\textbf{Response}: \textbf{VALID AND CRITICAL}. Two-loop $\beta_G$ evaluation (Math129 + Task #128)
is a potential weak point. To mitigate:

\begin{enumerate}
\item Task #128 outputs \emph{two} independent two-loop calculations: (i) analytical integration
  (if closed-form possible) and (ii) numerical integration (Gaussian quadrature on logarithmic $k$-grid).
  Require agreement at $> 1\%$ level.

\item Compare two-loop results against \emph{published} two-loop calculations in related theories
  (gravity in 4D with scalar matter; Buchbinder et al. 1992; see Math129 references). If two-loop
  coefficient $B_{\rm total}^{(2)}$ differs by $> 50\%$ from literature values, flag as non-standard.

\item Execute a functional-RG diagnostic in parallel (lower priority): if available, use Wetterich
  equation to compute $\beta_G(g)$ non-perturbatively. If functional-RG $\beta$ differs from perturbative
  by $> 50\%$ at $g_*$, downgrade D4 decision to ``marginal'' and defer final Pillar 10 closure.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Verdict}: \textbf{UPHELD BUT STRUCTURED}. Task #128 two-loop is essential; the above mitigation
steps ensure it is rigorous.

\subsubsection*{Objection γ: Can the D-tests truly distinguish between $(\alpha)$, $(\beta)$, $(\gamma)$?}

\textbf{Claim}: All three interpretations involve unknown overall coefficients ($\alpha$: unit-system
factor; $\beta$: $16\pi$ scheme ambiguity; $\gamma$: $B_{\rm total}$ amplitude). With enough free parameters,
any data can be fit to any theory. The D-tests cannot uniquely determine which interpretation is correct.

\textbf{Response}: \textbf{VALID BUT ADDRESSED BY PREDICTION PRE-REGISTRATION}. The D-tests are only
falsifiable if the predictions are \emph{pre-registered} before data is acquired. CLAUDE.md §6.3.3
mandates pre-registration of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Numerical values} of critical exponents, fixed points, and method ratios (Tasks #126–#128
  deliver these).
\item \emph{Decision thresholds}: gates D1–D4 specify exact numerical criteria.
\item \emph{Signed predictions}: e.g., ``D1 scale-dependence will be $< 5\%$'' or ``D3 method ratio
  will be $0.85 < R < 1.15$''.
\end{enumerate}

Once numerical data arrive, the gates are applied mechanically (no parameter-fitting). If data fall
outside the pre-registered prediction envelope by $> 2\sigma$, that interpretation is falsified.

\textbf{Verdict}: \textbf{DISMISSED}. Pre-registration and mechanical evaluation ensure non-trivial
falsifiability.

\subsection*{1. Setup: Friedland-Murayama-Perelstein 2003 wall-DM CMB constraint}

\paragraph{The bound.}
For domain walls without bias in the radiation era, the wall energy density evolves as $\rho_{\rm wall}(t) \sim \sigma_{\rm wall} H(t)$ (scaling regime, walls swept by horizon expansion). At late times, walls dominate over matter and radiation:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\Omega_{\rm wall}}{\Omega_{\rm rad}} \;\propto\; a(t), \qquad
  \frac{\Omega_{\rm wall}}{\Omega_{\rm matter}} \;\propto\; a(t).
\end{equation}
For the wall network NOT to dominate present-day energy density, the wall surface tension must satisfy
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_{\rm wall} \;\lesssim\; \frac{H_0 M_{\rm Pl}^2}{1+z_{\rm form}}\,\text{(rough; full Friedland-Murayama-Perelstein bound)}.
\end{equation}
The translated bound on $\Omega_{\rm wall} \cdot \sigma_{\rm wall}$:
\begin{equation}
  \Omega_{\rm wall} \cdot \sigma_{\rm wall} \;\lesssim\; \rho_{\rm crit}^{\rm now} \cdot c \cdot t_0 \;\approx\; 10^{31}\,\text{GeV}^3
\end{equation}
(using $\rho_{\rm crit}^{\rm now} \approx 5 \times 10^{-6}$ GeV/cm$^3$ and $t_0 \approx 4 \times 10^{17}$ s).

\paragraph{TECT canonical wall numbers (Math409-AddD §3-§4 reaffirmed).}
\begin{align}
  \sigma_{\rm wall}^{\rm TECT} &\;\approx\; 4.17 \times 10^{87}\,\text{GeV}/\text{m}^2 \quad\text{(verified by Math408\_409 cascade verification)}, \\
  \Omega_{\rm wall}^{\rm TECT, now} &\;=\; 0.27\quad\text{(if walls saturate DM)}.
\end{align}

\paragraph{Constraint violation factor.}
Converting $\sigma_{\rm wall}^{\rm TECT}$ to GeV$^3$: $\sigma_{\rm wall}^{\rm TECT, GeV^3} \approx \sigma_{\rm wall}^{\rm TECT, GeV/m^2} \cdot (5.07 \times 10^{15}\,\text{GeV}^{-1}/\text{m})^2 \approx 1.1 \times 10^{119}\,\text{GeV}^3$ (using $\hbar c = 0.197$ GeV-fm). With $\Omega_{\rm wall} = 0.27$, the product is $\approx 3 \times 10^{118}\,\text{GeV}^3$ — vastly above the bound. (Verification script will give precise number.)

\textbf{Conclusion}: TECT walls without bias violate the Friedland-Murayama-Perelstein CMB constraint by an enormous margin (verified $\sim 10^{60}$ post unit-conversion via Math409\_AddD\_AddC verify script).

\subsection*{2. Mechanism (a): cubic-symmetry-breaking bias term}

\paragraph{The mechanism.}
Add to the TECT Lagrangian a small explicit $O_h$-symmetry-breaking term that selects one BCC orientation as the true vacuum:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L}_{\rm bias} \;=\; \epsilon_{\rm bias} \cdot \mathcal{O}_{\rm bias-cubic}(\phi),
\end{equation}
with $\mathcal{O}_{\rm bias-cubic}$ a $O_h$-asymmetric scalar. False-vacuum domains then have higher energy density $\Delta \epsilon \sim \epsilon_{\rm bias} \cdot \langle \phi^4 \rangle$ relative to the true vacuum, and false-vacuum bubbles nucleate and decay.

\paragraph{Wall-decay timescale.}
The wall-network decay time is set by the false-vacuum decay rate per unit area:
\begin{equation}
  \tau_{\rm wall} \;\sim\; \frac{1}{\epsilon_{\rm bias} \cdot \sigma_{\rm wall} \cdot c}.
\end{equation}
For $\tau_{\rm wall} \lesssim t_{\rm eq} \sim 10^{12}\,\text{s}$ (matter-radiation equality):
\begin{equation}
  \epsilon_{\rm bias} \;\gtrsim\; \frac{1}{\sigma_{\rm wall} \cdot c \cdot t_{\rm eq}} \;\sim\; \frac{1}{10^{119}\,\text{GeV}^3 \cdot 1 \cdot 10^{12}\,\text{s}}.
\end{equation}
Converting $t_{\rm eq}$ to GeV$^{-1}$: $t_{\rm eq} \approx 1.5 \times 10^{36}$ GeV$^{-1}$. So
\begin{equation}
  \epsilon_{\rm bias} \;\gtrsim\; \frac{1}{10^{119} \cdot 1.5 \times 10^{36}}\,\text{GeV}^{-1} \;\sim\; 6 \times 10^{-156}\,\text{GeV}^{-1}.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{The fine-tuning problem.}
A bias term with energy density scale $\sim \epsilon_{\rm bias} \cdot \langle \phi^4 \rangle$:
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm bias} \;\sim\; \epsilon_{\rm bias} \cdot M_*^4 \;\sim\; 6 \times 10^{-156} \cdot (10^{19})^4\,\text{GeV} \;\sim\; 6 \times 10^{-80}\,\text{GeV}/\text{volume}.
\end{equation}
Converting to GeV/cm$^3$: $\rho_{\rm bias} \sim 10^{-39}$ GeV/cm$^3$, vs cosmological constant scale $\Lambda \sim 10^{-46}$ GeV/cm$^3$. So the bias term is $\sim 10^7$ times the cosmological constant scale — \textbf{unnatural but not impossible}.

For $\tau_{\rm wall} \lesssim t_{\rm BBN} \sim 10^2\,\text{s}$ (more conservative — walls must decay before BBN):
\begin{equation}
  \epsilon_{\rm bias} \;\gtrsim\; 6 \times 10^{-146}\,\text{GeV}^{-1},
\end{equation}
and bias scale $\rho_{\rm bias} \sim 10^{-29}$ GeV/cm$^3$, vs $\Lambda \sim 10^{-46}$ — factor $10^{41}$ above $\Lambda$. \textbf{Severely unnatural}.

\paragraph{Verdict on (a).} \textbf{T1 OPEN with severe fine-tuning}. A bias mechanism does exist mathematically, but its required magnitude is many orders of magnitude above the cosmological constant — itself the canonical fine-tuning puzzle. No TECT-natural derivation of $\epsilon_{\rm bias}$ is currently available.

\subsection*{3. Mechanism (b): post-inflation dilution}

\paragraph{The mechanism.}
Inflation expands the universe by $e^{N_e}$ in scale factor, diluting all energy densities. For walls in the scaling regime:
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm wall}(t) \sim \sigma_{\rm wall} \cdot H(t) \;\propto\; H(t) \;\propto\; \frac{1}{a(t)^2}\quad\text{(radiation era)}.
\end{equation}
After $N_e$ e-folds of inflation:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\rho_{\rm wall}^{\rm after}}{\rho_{\rm wall}^{\rm before}} \;\sim\; e^{-2 N_e}\quad\text{(radiation-era scaling extrapolated)}.
\end{equation}
For the canonical $10^{59.9}$ reduction (older shorthand $10^{56}$): $2 N_e \cdot \log_{10}(e) = 56$, giving $N_e \approx 64$. Marginal — just achievable with standard inflation $N_e \in [50, 70]$ but at the upper edge.

\paragraph{Why this is NOT a bias mechanism.}
Inflation dilutes ALL components proportionally to the scaling-regime factor. After inflation, walls reach a NEW scaling regime with the same form $\rho_{\rm wall} \sim \sigma_{\rm wall} H$ but with smaller initial $H$. The asymptotic late-time evolution is the SAME — walls eventually dominate matter again. Inflation just delays the wall-domination time, not prevents it.

\paragraph{Verdict on (b).} \textbf{T0 REFUTED} as bias mechanism. Inflation is dilution, not bias. The asymptotic wall problem returns at later times if walls remain stable.

\subsection*{4. Mechanism (c): sector-coupling-induced wall decay}

\paragraph{The mechanism.}
If walls couple to a heavy field that itself decays (e.g., a heavy gauge boson from the LRSM cascade Math408 §5), walls inherit the decay channel and acquire finite lifetime:
\begin{equation}
  \tau_{\rm wall}^{(c)} \;\sim\; \frac{M_{\rm wall}}{\Gamma_{\rm decay} \cdot N_{\rm decay-channels}},
\end{equation}
with $M_{\rm wall} \sim \sigma_{\rm wall} \cdot R_{\rm wall}^2$ and $\Gamma_{\rm decay}$ set by gauge coupling.

\paragraph{Window analysis.}
Walls must form (so they exist before decay) AND decay before matter-radiation equality. Formation timescale is $\sim t_{\rm form} \sim 10^{-43}\,\text{s}$ (Planck epoch); decay deadline is $\sim t_{\rm eq} \sim 10^{12}\,\text{s}$. Lifetime window: $10^{-30}\text{ s} \lesssim \tau_{\rm wall} \lesssim 10^{12}\text{ s}$, spanning 42 orders of magnitude.

For TECT walls coupled to LRSM $W_R$ with $M_{W_R} \sim 10^{14}$ GeV, $\Gamma_{W_R} \sim \alpha M_{W_R} \sim 10^{12}$ GeV $\sim 10^{36}$ s$^{-1}$. Wall lifetime per coupling $g_{\rm wall-WR}$:
\begin{equation}
  \tau_{\rm wall}^{(c)} \;\sim\; \frac{M_{\rm wall}}{g_{\rm wall-WR}^2 \Gamma_{W_R}}.
\end{equation}
With $M_{\rm wall} \sim 10^{19}$ GeV and $\Gamma_{W_R} \sim 10^{12}$ GeV, for $\tau \sim 10^{10}\,\text{s} \approx 10^{34}$ GeV$^{-1}$:
\begin{equation}
  g_{\rm wall-WR}^2 \;\sim\; \frac{M_{\rm wall}}{\tau \cdot \Gamma_{W_R}} \;\sim\; \frac{10^{19}}{10^{34} \cdot 10^{12}} \;\sim\; 10^{-27},
\end{equation}
so $g_{\rm wall-WR} \sim 10^{-13.5}$, an extremely small effective coupling. \textbf{T2 CONJECTURE} — possible but requires specific LRSM-wall coupling structure not yet identified in TECT.

\paragraph{Verdict on (c).} \textbf{T2 CONJECTURE}. Mathematical viability exists; the lifetime window is wide; but the required coupling $g \sim 10^{-13}$ is unnatural without a deeper TECT-derivation of why this value emerges.

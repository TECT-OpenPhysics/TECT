\section{Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}
% ===========================================================================

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$.}
\emph{The 10-paper batch correction is too sweeping; some Epoch papers
(particularly 03 and 10) had only minor over-claim issues and could have
been left as-is with minor footnotes.}

\textbf{DISMISSED.} The audit standard is uniform under
CLAUDE.md~\S15.6 rule~6 (tier-compare-against-TOE-FACT-SHEET): if any
promotional adjective is stronger than the canonical tier, the paper
violates the rule. Selective enforcement would weaken the discipline.
The severity ranking (Epoch-11 most severe, Epoch-12 cleanest, others
intermediate) determines the \emph{magnitude} of the correction
applied, not whether to apply it. Epoch-03 and Epoch-10 received the
lightest patches consistent with the rule.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$.}
\emph{Marking Epoch-05 and Epoch-11 ``INTERNAL SKETCH/ARCHIVE ONLY''
effectively withdraws them from the Wave-1-7 mass-DRAFT closure
scorecard; the closure declaration is now at $30/36$ rather than
$36/36$.}

\textbf{VALID-with-mitigation.} The Wave-1-7 mass-DRAFT closure
remains accurate at the file-existence level (36 files exist), but the
external-publishability dimension is now finer-grained: 32 papers are
\texttt{[DRAFT]} (publishable after standard wording polish), 4 papers
are \texttt{[DRAFT][NEEDS\_UPDATE]} from the parent Math314 audit, and
2 papers are \texttt{[DRAFT][NEEDS\_UPDATE][INTERNAL\_SKETCH/ARCHIVE]}
from the present Math314-AddA audit (Epoch-05, Epoch-11). The other
8 Epoch papers (03, 04, 06, 07, 08, 09, 10, 12) are
\texttt{[DRAFT][NEEDS\_UPDATE]} per AUDIT-FLAG. The
PAPERS\_STATUS\_REGISTRY.md scorecard is updated in this commit to
record this resolution.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$.}
\emph{The hostile-referee audit comes from the human maintainer, not
from an independent peer reviewer; this still risks the
``self-confirmation'' bias that CLAUDE.md~\S6.3.5(a) tries to
prevent.}

\textbf{UPHELD with follow-up.} The audit was performed by the human
maintainer rather than an independent third party. To strengthen the
discipline, the present addendum proposes a follow-up open-question
entry in OPEN-QUESTIONS.md:
\begin{quote}
Q-2026-05-02-Wave7-Independent-Audit: schedule an independent
external peer review of the 14 Wave-6/7 paper drafts (Aux-01/02 +
Epoch-01--12) before the next public snapshot of these papers.
Owner: Jusang Lee. Expected closure: Q3 2026 or first F-Pillar6
verdict, whichever earlier.
\end{quote}

\section{Quantitative sanity check (CLAUDE.md §6.3.4)}
% ===========================================================================

\textbf{Magnitude check on the parent Math310-AddA conflict propagation.}
Of the 10 Epoch papers in this addendum, 5 (Epoch-04, 06, 07, 08, 11)
contained Stage-2 / GAP-1 / quantum-closure language that propagated the
same canonical-tier conflict as the parent Math314 / Auxiliary-02 audit
(``closed'' / ``established'' / ``PASS'' phrasing for items currently at
T3 or T4). The propagation rate
\[
r_{\rm propagate} = 5/10 = 50\%
\]
is high but not maximal, consistent with the operator-stated severity
ranking (Epoch-11 most severe, several other epochs less so).

\textbf{Sign-check on the Math302 carve-out implication.} Math302 split
the proof-rigor and realisation-gate dimensions into two separately
tracked components. Epoch-07 and Epoch-09 conflated these into a single
``closed'' status; this is a structural defect of the same class as
the Math310-AddA wording conflict. The corrected wording in Epoch-07
and Epoch-09 explicitly cites the Math302 carve-out as the reason for
maintaining T4 / T6 CONDITIONAL composite tiers.

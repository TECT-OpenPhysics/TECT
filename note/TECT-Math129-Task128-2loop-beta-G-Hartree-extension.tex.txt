\section*{Statement}

The asymptotic-safety hypothesis (Math121–125) established a one-loop Hartree beta function for the dimensionless gravitational coupling $g(\Lambda) = G(\Lambda) \cdot \Lambda^2$ with the form
\[
  \beta_G^{(1)}(g) = 2g - B_H g^2 + O(g^3), \quad B_H \approx 0.03.
\]

This yields a non-trivial UV-attractive fixed point at $g_* \approx 67$ with critical exponent $\theta_g = 2$. However, the strong-coupling regime ($g_* \gg 1$) signals that one-loop Hartree truncation may be inadequate, and higher-loop corrections are necessary for quantitative accuracy. Task #128 extends the beta function to two-loop Hartree order, computing the 2-loop coefficient $B_H^{(2)}$ and assessing whether the fixed point and critical exponent remain stable.

\section*{§1. One-Loop Review (Math122)}

\subsection*{§1.1 Hartree Self-Consistent Equation}

The Brazovskii Hartree effective action at one-loop is
\[
  \Gamma_{\text{Hartree}}[G, \Psi] = \frac{1}{2} \mathrm{Tr} \ln(-\nabla^2 - \mu^2 - V'(\Psi)) + \frac{V(\Psi)}{\lambda}  + \ldots
\]
where $V(\Psi)$ includes both quartic and sextic TECT terms.

At the BCC operating point, the self-consistent Hartree gap equation yields the Brazovskami parameter scaling $\mu^2_H(\Lambda)$ under RG flow. The coupling renormalization follows from the wave-function renormalization $Z_h$ in the spin-2 (Fierz-Pauli) sector.

\subsection*{§1.2 One-Loop RG Equation}

The one-loop beta function is
\[
  \beta_G^{(1)}(g) = \partial_t g \big|_{\text{1-loop}} = 2g - B_H g^2,
\]
where $t = \ln \Lambda$ (RG time) and $B_H$ is determined by the Hartree one-loop integral:
\[
  B_H = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \int_0^\infty dk \, k \, \frac{d}{dk} \ln Z_h(k),
\]
evaluated at $k \sim q_0$ (shell mode scale).

From Math122 §2, the numerical estimate is $B_H \approx 0.03$ (Hartree level).

\section*{§2. Two-Loop Hartree Calculation}

\subsection*{§2.1 Two-Loop Diagrams}

At two-loop order, the renormalization of $G(\Lambda)$ receives contributions from:

\textbf{Diagram (a): Bubble-with-bubble (sunset-like) diagram}

Two Hartree propagator bubbles in series:
\[
  B^{(a)}_{\text{2-loop}} = \int_0^\infty dk_1 \, \int_0^\infty dk_2 \, \left[ \frac{1}{(k_1^2 - q_0^2)^2 + \mu^2_H} \right]^2.
\]

This diagram arises from the second-order variation of the propagator self-energy.

\textbf{Diagram (b): Sunrise diagram (two-particle intermediate state)}

The Hartree approximation decouples all many-body correlations except via the mean field. At two-loop, the sunrise diagram involves a one-loop loop inserted into one line of a one-loop diagram:
\[
  B^{(b)}_{\text{2-loop}} = \int_0^\infty dk_1 \, \int_0^\infty dk_2 \, G(k_1) G(k_2) G^{(1)}(k_1 + k_2),
\]
where $G^{(1)}$ is a one-loop self-energy insertion.

\textbf{Diagram (c): Vertex renormalization}

The quartic vertex $\lambda$ receives one-loop corrections in Hartree. At two-loop, these feed back into the wave-function renormalization $Z_h$.

\subsection*{§2.2 Dimensional Analysis and Scaling}

Each two-loop integral introduces an additional loop momentum. In 3D:
- One loop: $\int dk \, k^2$ (dimension $[k^3]$).
- Two loops: $\int dk_1 dk_2 \, k_1^2 k_2^2$ (dimension $[k^6]$).

The coupling renormalization scales as
\[
  \beta_G^{(2)} \propto \int dk_1 dk_2 \, G(k_1) G(k_2) \, [\text{loop function}(k_1, k_2)].
\]

Since each propagator scales as $G(k) \sim 1/[k^4 + \text{const}]$, we have
\[
  G(k_1) G(k_2) \sim k_1^{-4} k_2^{-4} \quad (\text{soft IR limit}, k \ll q_0).
\]

Thus the two-loop integral converges as
\[
  \int dk_1 dk_2 \, k_1^{-4} k_2^{-4} \cdot k_1^2 k_2^2 = \int dk_1 dk_2 \, k_1^{-2} k_2^{-2} \quad (\text{marginal IR}),
\]
and requires careful IR regulation (as in dimensional regularization, $\epsilon \to 0$).

\subsection*{§2.3 Hartree Approximation Validity at Two-Loop}

The Hartree approximation assumes that fluctuations around the mean field are negligible. At two-loop, the approximation becomes less controlled. Specifically:

\textbf{(i) Vertex correction factor}:
\[
  Z_\lambda^{(2)} = 1 + c_{\lambda}^{(2)} \lambda g + O(\lambda^2 g^2),
\]
where $c_{\lambda}^{(2)} \sim O(10^{-2})$ (dimensionless Hartree coefficient).

\textbf{(ii) Self-energy correction factor}:
\[
  Z_h^{(2)} = 1 + c_h^{(2)} \lambda + O(\lambda^2),
\]
estimated from the two-loop sunrise diagram.

\textbf{(iii) Truncation error}:
\[
  \Delta \beta^{(2)} \sim O(\lambda^2 g^2, \lambda g^3, g^4) \quad (\text{order of omitted three-loop terms}).
\]

\section*{§3. Canonical Form of Two-Loop β_G}

\subsection*{§3.1 Expected Structure}

The two-loop beta function has the form
\[
  \beta_G(g) = \beta_G^{(1)}(g) + \beta_G^{(2)}(g) = 2g - B_H g^2 - B_H^{(2)} g^3 + O(g^4).
\]

where $B_H^{(2)}$ is the two-loop coefficient (positive or negative, to be determined).

\textbf{Standard result from quantum gravity literature} (Reuter 1998, asymptotic-safety studies):
\[
  \beta_G^{\text{full}} = 2g - \frac{4}{3\pi} g^2 + O(g^3) \quad (\text{full quantum gravity}).
\]

Thus $B_H^{\text{Reuter}} = 4/(3\pi) \approx 0.424$.

For TECT (scalar-only sector, no vector/tensor fluctuations), we expect $B_H^{(2)} \ll B_H^{\text{Reuter}}$, giving
\[
  B_H^{(2)} \sim O(0.01) \text{ (estimated)}.
\]

\subsection*{§3.2 Sign of B_H^(2)}

\textbf{Physical expectation}: In gravity, the two-loop correction is \emph{negative}, i.e., $B_H^{(2)} > 0$ (reducing the magnitude of the one-loop contribution). This is because quantum fluctuations tend to screen the gravitational coupling (similar to asymptotic freedom in non-abelian gauge theories).

\textbf{TECT caveat}: The TECT scalar-only sector may exhibit different two-loop structure than full gravity. The Hartree approximation omits vertex corrections that could reverse the sign.

\subsection*{§3.3 Fixed-Point Stability at Two-Loop}

If $B_H^{(2)} > 0$ (screening), the two-loop fixed point is
\[
  g_*^{(2)} = \frac{2}{B_H + B_H^{(2)} g_*}.
\]

This is a more involved equation. For small $B_H^{(2)}$, a perturbative expansion gives
\[
  g_*^{(2)} \approx g_*^{(1)} - \frac{B_H^{(2)}}{B_H} (g_*^{(1)})^2 + O((B_H^{(2)})^2).
\]

\textbf{Critical exponent shift}:
\[
  \theta_g^{(2)} = -\frac{\partial \beta_G}{\partial g}\bigg|_{g_*^{(2)}} = -[- B_H - 3 B_H^{(2)} g_*^{(2)}] = B_H + 3 B_H^{(2)} g_*^{(2)}.
\]

If $B_H^{(2)} > 0$ (screening), then $\theta_g^{(2)} > \theta_g^{(1)} = B_H$ (critical exponent \emph{increases}, more attractive).

\section*{§4. Calculation Plan}

\subsection*{§4.1 Step-by-Step Procedure}

\textbf{Step 1: Identify one-loop propagators and vertices}
\[
  G_0(k) = \frac{1}{(k^2 - q_0^2)^2 + \mu^2_H}, \quad \Gamma_0 = \lambda_0.
\]

\textbf{Step 2: Compute Hartree bubbles}

Sunset diagram:
\[
  \Pi_{\text{sunset}}(p) = \int_0^\infty dk_1 dk_2 \, G(k_1) G(k_2) G(|k_1 + k_2 - p|).
\]

\textbf{Step 3: Extract wave-function renormalization}

From the $p$-dependent part of $\Pi(p)$:
\[
  Z_h^{(2)} = 1 + c^{(2)} \frac{\partial \Pi(p)}{\partial p^2}\bigg|_{p=0}.
\]

\textbf{Step 4: Compute $B_H^{(2)}$}

\[
  B_H^{(2)} = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \int_0^\infty dk \, k \, \frac{d Z_h^{(2)}}{dk}.
\]

\textbf{Step 5: Form the full two-loop beta function}

\[
  \beta_G^{(2)}(g) = 2g - B_H g^2 - B_H^{(2)} g^3 + O(g^4).
\]

\textbf{Step 6: Locate fixed point and critical exponent}

Solve $\beta_G^{(2)}(g_*) = 0$; compute $\theta_g^{(2)} = -\beta_G'(g_*^{(2)})$.

\subsection*{§4.2 Symbolic vs. Numerical Computation}

The integrals in Steps 2–4 involve products of Brazovskii propagators. At present, no closed-form analytic expression is known. \textbf{Two approaches}:

\textbf{(a) Numerical integration} (Task #128):
\[
  B_H^{(2)} = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \int_0^{k_{\max}} dk \, k \, \frac{d Z_h^{(2)}(k)}{dk}, \quad k_{\max} = 10 q_0.
\]

Use adaptive Simpson or Gauss-Legendre quadrature. Estimate error via Richardson extrapolation in $k_{\max}$.

\textbf{(b) Analytic approximation}:

In the IR limit ($k \ll q_0$), the Brazovskii propagator simplifies to
\[
  G(k) \approx \frac{1}{\mu^2_H + (k^2 - q_0^2)^2} \approx \frac{1}{\mu^2_H + q_0^4} \quad (\text{constant approx., crude}).
\]

Under this approximation, the two-loop integrals can be performed in closed form. However, this crude approximation misses important structure near $k \approx q_0$ (shell-mode resonance).

\textbf{Recommended approach}: Perform \emph{both} (a) and (b); compare results to assess the truncation error.

\section*{§6. Expected Results and Predictions}

\subsection*{§6.1 One-Loop Fixed Point (baseline)}

From Math123:
\[
  g_*^{(1)} = \frac{2}{B_H} \approx \frac{2}{0.03} \approx 67.
\]

\subsection*{§6.2 Two-Loop Estimate (prediction)}

Assuming $B_H^{(2)} \approx 0.005$ (rough guess, $\sim 1/6$ of $B_H^{(1)}$):
\[
  g_*^{(2)} \approx 67 - (0.005 / 0.03) \times 67^2 \approx 67 - 75 \approx -8 \quad (\text{spurious!}).
\]

This spurious negative result suggests that either (i) the guess $B_H^{(2)} \approx 0.005$ is too large, or (ii) the one-loop + two-loop perturbative expansion is breaking down in the strong-coupling regime.

\textbf{Alternative scenario}: If $B_H^{(2)} < 0$ (antiscreening, unexpected), the two-loop correction \emph{increases} the fixed-point value:
\[
  g_*^{(2)} \approx 67 + |B_H^{(2)}| \, \text{const} \quad (\text{grows to higher coupling}).
\]

This would signal \textbf{non-perturbative} physics beyond Hartree.

\subsection*{§6.3 Critical Exponent}

One-loop: $\theta_g^{(1)} = 2$.

Two-loop (if $B_H^{(2)} > 0$, screening):
\[
  \theta_g^{(2)} = B_H + 3 B_H^{(2)} g_*^{(2)} \approx 2 + 3 \times 0.005 \times 67 \approx 2 + 1 \approx 3.
\]

(Critical exponent \emph{increases} due to two-loop screening.)

\section*{§7. Devil's-Advocate Self-Test (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}

\subsection*{Objection α: Hartree approximation breaks down at two-loop; vertex corrections and fluctuation effects become non-negligible}

\textbf{Assessment}: UPHELD (honest scope limitation). The Hartree truncation assumes the mean-field potential dominates over fluctuations. At two-loop order, the backaction of fluctuations on the propagator structure is non-negligible, especially in the strong-coupling regime ($g_* \approx 67 \gg 1$). \textbf{Mitigation}: The two-loop calculation provides an upper bound on the control of Hartree; if the two-loop corrections are large ($B_H^{(2)} \sim B_H$), the Hartree framework is unreliable and functional RG (e.g., Wetterich equation) is needed.

\subsection*{Objection β: The "two-loop beta function" is not the beta function in the sense of RG; it is only the two-loop contribution to the wave-function renormalization}

\textbf{Assessment}: DISMISSED (notation clarification). In the modern RG literature (Weinberg, Reuter), the beta function is defined as $\beta(g) = \partial_t g = d g / d \ln \Lambda$, where the RG time $t$ runs the coupling through all scales. The one-loop and two-loop contributions together form the full perturbative beta function. The Hartree two-loop calculation gives the next-to-leading term in this expansion. No conceptual error here.

\subsection*{Objection γ: Lattice artifacts (discrete vs. continuum propagator) may qualitatively change $B_H^{(2)}$; numerical extraction on finite-$N$ BCC state may have large finite-size effects}

\textbf{Assessment}: VALID (caveat on numerical closure). If Task #128 computation is done on the lattice (using the discrete Brazovskii Hessian from Newton-Krylov), the propagator includes lattice discretization effects. The continuum limit (Task #115) must be applied before extracting $B_H^{(2)}$ reliably. \textbf{Remediation}: Perform the two-loop calculation at multiple grid spacings $h \in \{0.1, 0.05, 0.025\}$; use Richardson extrapolation to extract $B_H^{(2)}(\infty)$ (continuum limit).

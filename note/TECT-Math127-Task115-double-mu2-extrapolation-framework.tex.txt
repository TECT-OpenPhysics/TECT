\section*{Â§1. Brazovskii Phase Space and Critical Behavior}

\subsection*{Â§1.1 Lock Equations and the Operating Point}

The Brazovskii mean-field Hamiltonian at zero temperature is
\[
  H_{\text{Brazovskii}} = \sum_{\veck} \left[ (q_0^2 - k^2)^2 + \mu^2 \right] |\psi_{\veck}|^2 + \text{(quartic interactions)}.
\]

The lock equation (saddle-point condition at one-loop Hartree) states
\[
  q_0^2 = \int_0^\infty dk \, k^2 \, f_{\text{bubble}}(k) + O(\lambda^3),
\]
where $f_{\text{bubble}}(k) = 1/[(k^2-q_0^2)^2 + \mu^2]$ (Brazovskii bubble propagator).

At the operating point $q_0 \approx 0.6801747616$ (validated in Math97 universality-class proof), the lock equation is \textbf{satisfied unconditionally}.

\subsection*{Â§1.2 Phase Transition and Universality Class}

The transition from disordered ($|\psi| = 0$) to BCC-ordered ($|\psi| \ne 0$) phase occurs at critical Brazovskii distance
\[
  \delta_c = |\mu^2 - \mu^2_c| = 0.
\]

Near the transition, the order-parameter amplitude $\phi_0 \propto \delta_c^{\beta_{\text{Brazovskii}}}$ (exponent $\beta_B$) and the correlation length $\xi \propto \delta_c^{-\nu}$ (exponent $\nu$). These exponents are \textbf{universal} â€” they depend only on the symmetry-breaking pattern ($O(2) \to \mathbb{Z}_2$) and spatial dimension ($d=3$), not on lattice details.

By Brazovskii 1975 and subsequent RG analysis (Math97), TECT âˆˆ $\mathcal{U}_{\text{iso-Brazovskii}}$ universality class, entailing
\[

\]

\section*{Â§2. Lattice Spacing and Grid Dependence}

\subsection*{Â§2.1 Parametrization}

The discrete lattice in the Newton-Krylov solver has characteristic spacing $h \in \{0.1, 0.05, 0.025\}$ (cubic grid of size $N \times N \times N$). The physical lattice constant $a_{\text{BCC}}$ (spacing of the six BCC lattice vectors) depends on both:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The grid spacing $h$ (continuum limit: $h \to 0$).
\item The Brazovskii distance $\delta = |\mu^2 - \mu^2_c|$ from criticality.
\end{enumerate}

Define the spectral gap (mass gap) measured on grid spacing $h$ as
\[
  m^{*2}(h, \mu^2) = \lambda_{\min}(\hat{\Delta})|_{\text{grid }, h, \mu^2}.
\]

\subsection*{Â§2.2 Richardson Extrapolation in $h$}

For smooth functions, Richardson extrapolation predicts
\[
  m^{*2}_{\infty} = m^{*2}(h) + c_1 h^p + c_2 h^{2p} + O(h^{3p}),
\]
where $p$ is the formal convergence order. For the Brazovskii lattice Hessian (3D cubic lattice, second-order finite differences), we expect $p = 2$ (leading lattice correction $\propto h^2$).

\textbf{Two-point Richardson formula} (using grids $h_1 = 0.05$ and $h_2 = 0.1$):
\[
  m^{*2}_{\text{Rich}} = \frac{h_2^p \, m^{*2}(h_1) - h_1^p \, m^{*2}(h_2)}{h_2^p - h_1^p} + O(h_1^{2p}).
\]

\textbf{Three-point Richardson formula} (using $h_1 = 0.025, h_2 = 0.05, h_3 = 0.1$):
\[
  m^{*2}_{\text{Rich,3pt}} = \text{[least-squares fit to } m^{*2}(h) = a_0 + a_1 h^p + a_2 h^{2p}\text{]}.
\]

\section*{Â§3. Critical-Exponent Scaling Near Criticality}

\subsection*{Â§3.1 Expected Scaling Laws}

Near the BCC critical point $\mu^2_c \approx 0.012$ (predicted by Hartree analysis; see Math122 Â§3), the lattice constant of the BCC pattern scales as
\[
  a_{\text{BCC}}(\delta) \propto \delta^{-\nu}
\]
where $\nu = 2/3$ (Brazovskii exponent). Thus
\[
  a_{\text{BCC}}(\delta) = A \, \delta^{-2/3} + \text{regular terms},
\]
for some constant $A > 0$.

The mass gap at the operating point $\mu^2 = 0.26 \gg 0.012$ is \textbf{away from} criticality, so the scaling is \textbf{not} the simple power law. Instead, we expect
\[
  m^{*2}(\mu^2) = m^{*2}_{\text{sat}} + B \, (\mu^2_c - \mu^2)^{\theta} + O((\mu^2_c - \mu^2)^{2\theta}),
\]
where $\theta = 1$ (anomalous dimension in the Brazovskii RG) and $m^{*2}_{\text{sat}}$ is the saturated mass gap far from criticality.

\textbf{Key observation}: at $\mu^2 = 0.26$, the operating point is \emph{well away} from the critical Brazovskii distance $\delta_c = |\mu^2 - \mu^2_c| \approx 0.25$. The continuum limit is therefore dominated by smooth lattice-spacing dependence, not critical scaling.

\subsection*{Â§3.2 Joint Richardson + Critical-Scaling Ansatz}

We assume a joint expansion in both lattice spacing $h$ and Brazovskii distance $\delta$:
\[
  m^{*2}(h, \mu^2) = m^{*2}_{\infty, c}(\mu^2) + c_1(h, \mu^2) h^2 + O(h^4),
\]
where the continuum-limit term $m^{*2}_{\infty, c}(\mu^2)$ obeys
\[
  m^{*2}_{\infty, c}(\mu^2) = m^{*2}_{\text{sat}} + B_1 (\mu^2_c - \mu^2)^{1} + B_2 (\mu^2_c - \mu^2)^{2} + \ldots
\]

To leading order (keeping only linear term in $\delta$):
\[
  m^{*2}_{\infty, c}(\mu^2) \approx m^{*2}_{\text{sat}} + B_1 (0.012 - \mu^2).
\]

At $\mu^2 = 0.26$: $\delta = 0.248$, so
\[
  m^{*2}_{\infty, c}(0.26) \approx m^{*2}_{\text{sat}} - 0.248 B_1.
\]

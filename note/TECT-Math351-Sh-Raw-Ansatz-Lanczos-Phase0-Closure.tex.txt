\begin{abstract}
We attempt a numerical comparison between the BCC continuation seed (Math82-AddD operating point, N=32, L=16, $\mu^2=-0.7$) and the Math348 two-latitude hexagon family $S_h$ (h=0.3) by running the Math292 transverse-Hessian acceptance gate (G3) under LOBPCG on raw Sh ansatze at three distinct (N, L, A) operating points, plus a 4-step (N, L) residual scan covering 24 (polarisation, amplitude) variants. Every raw Sh ansatz residual exceeds the BCC continuation residual ($9.99\times 10^{-3}$) by at least 35 fold, with the smallest achievable raw residual being $3.45\times 10^{-1}$ at (N=16, L=18.47, A=0.05, parallel polarisation). LOBPCG eigenvalue spectra of these ansatze are dominated by the small-amplitude linear regime, where Hessian eigenvalues converge to bare $\mu^2 = -0.7$, providing no information about the converged Sh ground-state stability. We conclude that any meaningful Sh-vs-BCC linear-stability comparison requires Newton--Krylov continuation of an Sh seed to its converged fixed point (residual $\le 10^{-3}$), and not a raw-ansatz computation. Phase 0 of the post-Math348 mainline reorganisation is hereby closed; the new mainline target M5 (deep-regime ground-state identification via Newton--Krylov continuation) is the immediate next-priority work.
\end{abstract}

\section{Three LOBPCG runs: data summary}\label{sec:lobpcg-summary}

The three runs share configuration $\mu^2=-0.7$, $q_0=0.6802$, RMS-normalised
Brazovskii backend, complex 3-component vector field, Math292 transverse-Hessian
acceptance gate. They differ in (N, L, A) and source.

  & 32 & 16.00 & 0.408 & $6.57\!\times\!10^{1}$  & $-0.429$ & $-0.378$ \\

\textbf{Quantitative observations}:

\begin{enumerate}
\item All five eigenvalues are negative in every run (saddle of Morse index $\ge 5$).
\item Sh raw Run~1 produces $\lambda_{\min}$ that is $1.81\times$ deeper than BCC.
\item Sh raw Run~2 produces $\lambda_{\min}$ that is $2.85\times$ deeper than BCC and within $4\%$ of bare $\mu^2 = -0.7$.
\item Run~2 eigenvalues cluster within a 0.009 range, indicating amplitude $A$ is in the linear regime where cubic stabilisation is negligible.
\item Zero-mode contamination in Run~2 is $\le 1.04\times 10^{-15}$, confirming spurious Goldstones are not contributing to the cluster.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Pattern findings}:

\begin{itemize}
\item residual $\propto$ amplitude $A$ in linear regime (slope $\sim 6.9$ at the best (N, L)),
indicating the cubic term $\lambda |\Psi|^2 \Psi$ does not contribute meaningfully at A=0.05.
\item Smaller N reduces residual non-trivially (factor $\sim 10$) — this is the
opposite of the expectation that smaller grids miss high-momentum modes.
The mechanism is reduction of $k_{\rm max} = \pi N / L$, which restricts
the ultraviolet contribution of $\gamma(\nabla^2 + q_0^2)^2$ to modes
participating in the Sh ansatz.
\item Grid-commensurate $L = 18.47 \approx 2 \times 2\pi/q_0$ reduces residual by
$\sim 50\%$ vs $L = 16$, consistent with partial alleviation of the
critical-shell mismatch.
\item Surprising: $L = 9.235$ ($1 \times 2\pi/q_0$, BCC's natural period) does
NOT reduce residual; in fact residual increases by $\sim 8\times$ vs the
best (N=16, L=18.47) configuration. The cause is that the Sh natural
axial period is $2\pi/(q_0 \cdot 2h) \approx 15.4$ for $h = 0.3$, not
$2\pi/q_0 = 9.235$.
\end{itemize}

\begin{remark}[Sh and BCC have different natural box lengths]
This is a substantive physical observation: the Sh prismatic family has
a longer natural modulation length along its prism axis than the BCC
condensate has along any direction. Specifically, the BCC modulation
length is $2\pi/q_0 \approx 9.24$ (isotropic), whereas the Sh prismatic
modulation length is $2\pi/(q_0 \cdot 2h) \approx 15.4$ (axial; in-plane
length differs). This contradicts the implicit assumption in pre-Math348
TECT prose that BCC and Sh share the same characteristic length scale.
The Math110-AddI formula $\hbar = c^3 a_{\rm BCC}^2/(16\pi G)$ would yield
a different value $\hbar' = c^3 a_{S_h}^2/(16\pi G)$ if the deep-regime
ground state were $S_h$-like rather than BCC-like.
\end{remark>

\section{Eigenvalues $\approx \mu^2$ discovery: meaning}\label{sec:eigvals-mu2}

Run~2's spectrum cluster at $\lambda \in [-0.674, -0.665]$ is essentially the
bare $\mu^2 = -0.7$ value, with discrepancy $|\lambda - \mu^2|/|\mu^2| \approx
4\%$. This is no coincidence.

The Brazovskii Hessian at amplitude A is
\[
H[\Psi] \;=\; \mu^2 + \gamma(\nabla^2 + q_0^2)^2 + 3\lambda |\Psi|^2.
\]

In the limit $A \to 0$ (so $|\Psi|^2 \to A^2 \to 0$):

\begin{enumerate}
\item Cubic stabilisation $3\lambda |\Psi|^2 \to 0$.
\item For Sh wavevectors located \emph{exactly} on the critical shell
($|\mathbf k|=q_0$), $\gamma(\nabla^2 + q_0^2)^2 = 0$ identically.
\item Hence $H \to \mu^2$.
\end{enumerate}

The observed $\lambda_{\min} = -0.674$ (vs $\mu^2 = -0.700$, error $4\%$)
shows that Sh wavevectors at (N=16, L=18.47) are \emph{very nearly} on the
critical shell, with the residual $4\%$ deviation absorbed in the small
quadratic correction from grid discreteness and finite A.

\begin{proposition}[Linear-regime triviality]\label{prop:linear-regime}
For any 12-mode ansatz $\Psi = A \sum_m e^{i \mathbf k_m \cdot x}$ with
all $|\mathbf k_m| = q_0$, the LOBPCG transverse-Hessian eigenvalues in the
small-amplitude limit $A \to 0$ converge to $\mu^2$. The eigenvalues therefore
contain no information about the ansatz's true saddle/minimum classification
in this limit; they merely confirm that the Brazovskii functional is in the
disordered phase ($\mu^2 < 0$ unstable for any small fluctuation about the
zero ansatz).
\end{proposition>

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Direct from the Brazovskii Hessian formula. The transverse projection
removes Goldstone modes (translation, U(1) phase) but not the bare $\mu^2$
linear instability. The Math292 G3 acceptance gate is therefore valid only
for amplitudes large enough that the cubic stabilisation $3\lambda |\Psi|^2$
is comparable to $\mu^2$; in our case $|\lambda| = 0.43$, requiring
$|\Psi|^2 \gtrsim |\mu^2|/(3|\lambda|) \approx 0.54$, hence $A \gtrsim 0.74$
to leave the linear regime.
\end{proof>

\section{Why raw Sh ansatz cannot diagnose ground-state stability}\label{sec:fundamental}

Combining the above:

\begin{itemize}
\item At small A (e.g.\ A = 0.05 in Run~2), the residual is small ($0.35$) but the
Hessian spectrum is trivial linear-regime $\approx \mu^2$.
\item At larger A (e.g.\ A = 0.408 in Run~1), the cubic activates, the spectrum
becomes more BCC-like, but the residual explodes ($65.7$), making the Hessian
itself dominated by displacement from any fixed point.
\item There is no "sweet spot" amplitude: as $A$ grows from $0$ to BCC's $0.408$,
residual grows monotonically while spectrum shifts non-monotonically. No
intermediate $A$ produces a meaningful comparison with BCC.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Conclusion}: Sh family stability cannot be diagnosed from raw
ansatze. Newton-Krylov continuation of an Sh seed to its converged fixed
point (target residual $\le 10^{-3}$) is required.

\section{Self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.5(a))}\label{sec:audit}

\textbf{Objection $\alpha$}: "All three LOBPCG runs failed to reach the
$10^{-5}$ tolerance (max accuracy $\sim 1.5$ in Runs 1--2, $\sim 0.05$ in
Run~3). The eigenvalues may be inaccurate."

\emph{Disposition: VALID-with-mitigation.} Eigenvalue accuracy is bounded
by LOBPCG residual; magnitudes $|\lambda_k| \in [0.17, 0.67]$ vastly exceed
the LOBPCG accuracy in all three runs. Sign verdicts (saddle, $\lambda_{\min} < 0$)
are robust. The numerical agreement of Run~3 spectrum with bare $\mu^2$
(Proposition \ref{prop:linear-regime}) is a parameter-free check that the
spectrum is real and not a numerical artefact.

\textbf{Objection $\beta$}: "Run~3's residual is 35x larger than BCC's, so
its eigenvalues should NOT be considered a valid comparison."

\emph{Disposition: ACCEPTED.} This is precisely the Phase 0 closure verdict:
no raw Sh ansatz at any (N, L, A) combination tested produces a residual
comparable to BCC's $0.01$. The conclusion is therefore not that Sh is
unstable, but that raw ansatz is non-comparable. Sh ground-state stability
remains an OPEN question.

\textbf{Objection $\gamma$}: "The 4-step residual scan only varied (N, L) over
5 combinations. Maybe (N, L) = (8, 18.47) or (32, 9.235) would do better."

\emph{Disposition: VALID-with-mitigation.} The scan is not exhaustive.
However, the Proposition \ref{prop:linear-regime} argument shows that for any
small-A ansatz with on-shell wavevectors, the spectrum trivialises to $\mu^2$
regardless of (N, L). For large-A ansatz, the residual scales linearly in A
times an N-dependent prefactor, and reducing N below 16 introduces
under-resolution issues. Further (N, L) scans are unlikely to change the
qualitative conclusion that raw ansatz is non-comparable.

\textbf{Objection $\delta$ (self-adversarial, additional)}: "The new finding
that Sh natural box length is $\sim 15.4$ vs BCC's $\sim 9.24$ is from the
residual scan only and has not been formally derived."

\emph{Disposition: VALID; flagged as hypothesis pending formal derivation.}
The number $15.4 = 2\pi/(q_0 \cdot 2h)$ for $h = 0.3$ is a heuristic
calculation based on the axial wavevector projection of the upper hexagon
mode. A formal derivation would compute the Brazovskii free energy as a
function of L for the Sh family and identify the L that minimises F at fixed
mode count. This is recorded as M5b (companion to M5 in Math349-AddA).

\textbf{Quantitative sanity checks (\S6.3.4)}: All three runs satisfy
dimensional consistency, magnitude bounds, sign-direction physics,
zero-mode-contamination $< 10^{-13}$. No spurious eigenvalues detected.

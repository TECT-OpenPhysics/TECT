\subsection*{2. Identification with physical Planck's constant}

\subsubsection*{2.1 Definition of the Planck length}

The Planck length in natural units ($\hbar = 1, c = 1$) is:
\begin{equation}
\ell_P = \sqrt{G}.
\label{eq:lp-natural}
\end{equation}

In conventional units (restoring $\hbar$ and $c$ explicitly):
\begin{equation}
\ell_P = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^3}}.
\label{eq:lp-conventional}
\end{equation}

\subsubsection*{2.2 Rearrangement for $a_{\rm BCC}$ in Planck units}

From Eq.~(\ref{eq:hbar-intermediate}), rearrange to express $a_{\rm BCC}$ in terms
of Planck-scale quantities. Multiply both sides by $16\pi G$:
\begin{equation}
16\pi G\,\hbar_{\rm eff} = c^5\,a_{\rm BCC}.
\end{equation}

Divide by $c^3$:
\begin{equation}
\frac{16\pi G\,\hbar_{\rm eff}}{c^3} = c^2\,a_{\rm BCC}.
\end{equation}

Rearrange:
\begin{equation}
a_{\rm BCC} = \frac{16\pi G\,\hbar_{\rm eff}}{c^5}.
\label{eq:abcc-hbar}
\end{equation}

Now, identify $\hbar_{\rm eff} = \hbar$ (the physical Planck constant). Then:
\begin{equation}
a_{\rm BCC} = \frac{16\pi G\,\hbar}{c^5}.
\label{eq:abcc-hbar-phys}
\end{equation}

Express in terms of the Planck length (Eq.~\ref{eq:lp-conventional}):
\begin{equation}
a_{\rm BCC} = 16\pi\left(\sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^3}}\right)^2 = 16\pi\,\ell_P^2.
\label{eq:abcc-lp}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Devil's-advocate (§6.3.1, sub-α):}
\textit{Is the identification $\hbar_{\rm eff} = \hbar$ justified, or is $\hbar_{\rm eff}$
a separate coupling constant?}

\textbf{DISMISSED.} The Math98 derivation shows that $\hbar_{\rm eff}$ is the
effective action scale that emerges during the TECT phase transition. In the
standard quantum-field-theory framework, this action scale is precisely $\hbar$
(Planck's reduced constant). There is no separate parameter; the identification
is a consequence of the canonical quantization of the condensate.

\subsubsection*{2.3 Alternative form: $a_{\rm BCC} = 4\sqrt{\pi}\,\ell_P$}

From Eq.~(\ref{eq:abcc-lp}), we can write:
\begin{equation}
a_{\rm BCC}^2 = 16\pi\,\ell_P^2 \implies a_{\rm BCC} = 4\sqrt{\pi}\,\ell_P.
\label{eq:abcc-simplified}
\end{equation}

This is the central result of the Math110 chain: the BCC lattice spacing is
precisely 4√π times the Planck length.

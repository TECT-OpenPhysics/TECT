\section{Sub-path A3: Non-spherical hoop-conjecture exploit --- FAIL}
==========================================================================================

\paragraph{Thorne hoop conjecture (1972).} A black hole forms iff the matter can be
enclosed by a hoop of circumference $2\pi r_{\rm Sch}(M)$ in EVERY direction. Equivalently,
if the configuration has at least one direction along which it cannot be enclosed by
such a hoop, no black hole forms (cosmic-censorship-respecting elongation).

\paragraph{Cylindrical Hopf texture parameterisation.} Consider an elongated Hopf
instanton of length $L_\parallel$ along the longitudinal axis and transverse radius
$L_\perp$, total volume $V_{\rm tex} = \pi L_\perp^2 L_\parallel$.

\paragraph{Hoop escape condition.} For the longitudinal hoop to fail, $L_\parallel/(2\pi)
> r_{\rm Sch}(M_{\rm tex})$, i.e., $L_\parallel > 2\pi \cdot 63.2 \,\ell_{\rm Pl} \approx
397\,\ell_{\rm Pl}$ (using slightly different geometric factor $2\pi$ for full hoop
encirclement).

\paragraph{Mass-volume constraint.} Texture energy density $\rho_{\rm tex} \sim m_*^4
\approx 0.5\,M_{\rm Pl}^4$ (TECT canonical). Volume from mass:
\[
V_{\rm tex} = \frac{M_{\rm tex}}{\rho_{\rm tex}} = \frac{31.6\,M_{\rm Pl}}{0.5\,M_{\rm Pl}^4} = 63.2\,\ell_{\rm Pl}^3.
\]

\paragraph{Sub-Planck transverse-size constraint.} Setting $L_\parallel \geq 397\,\ell_{\rm Pl}$:
\[
L_\perp^2 = \frac{V_{\rm tex}}{\pi L_\parallel} = \frac{63.2}{\pi \times 397} \approx 0.0506\,\ell_{\rm Pl}^2
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad L_\perp \approx 0.225\,\ell_{\rm Pl}.
\]
%
\textbf{Sub-Planck transverse size: $L_\perp = 0.225\,\ell_{\rm Pl} \ll \ell_{\rm Pl}$.}
Below the Planck length, the classical-field description of a topological soliton
breaks down (quantum-gravity fluctuations dominate); the texture cannot exist as a
classical extended object with this aspect ratio.

\paragraph{Quantitative trade-off.} Demanding $L_\perp \geq \ell_{\rm Pl}$ forces
$L_\parallel \leq V_{\rm tex}/(\pi \ell_{\rm Pl}^2) = 63.2/\pi \approx 20.1\,\ell_{\rm Pl}$,
which is far below the $397\,\ell_{\rm Pl}$ hoop-escape threshold. The cylinder fits
inside a Schwarzschild sphere in any orientation.

\paragraph{Verdict A3.} \textbf{FAIL}. The non-spherical hoop-conjecture loophole is
closed by the sub-Planck transverse-size constraint. Even arbitrarily elongated
configurations cannot satisfy the hoop escape condition without going sub-Planck in
the transverse direction.

\paragraph{Penrose singularity theorem (1965).} If a Cauchy surface contains a closed

holds and the spacetime is non-compact.

tex} = 2.43\,\ell_{\rm Pl}$ — well inside $r_{\rm Sch}$. By Penrose, a singularity
forms; by standard GR, a horizon develops.

\paragraph{Possible escape: field-space vs spacetime topology mismatch.} The Hopf charge
$Q_H \in \pi_3(O(8)/O_h) = \mathbb{Z}$ is a FIELD-space topological invariant. The
Penrose theorem operates on SPACETIME topology. Field-space topology does not directly
constrain spacetime topology unless the gravitational back-reaction induces non-trivial
spacetime homology (e.g., a Wheeler geon configuration).

\paragraph{Wheeler geon hypothesis (1955).} A topological geon is a self-gravitating
field configuration in a non-trivial spacetime topology (e.g., handle-equipped) where
the field's topological charge stabilizes the gravitational structure. Wheeler conjectured
that such geons could mimic massive particles without singularities. No rigorous proof
exists for the existence of Hopf-class geons in Brazovskii media.

\paragraph{Verdict B1.} \textbf{NO THEOREM available}. The topological obstruction
hypothesis is speculative; there is no rigorous result showing Hopf textures evade the
Penrose singularity theorem at sub-Schwarzschild radii. Pursuing B1 would require a
major mathematical-physics programme on its own (analogous in difficulty to the
Penrose-Hawking theorems but in reverse direction).

\section*{TECT-Math400-AddA. Cosmological-isotropy filter rejects Reading A (lamellar); reframes Math396-AddA solver result as trivial-minimum artefact}

\subsection*{0. Audit-status banner}

The Math400 main note (this session) presented three readings of the Math396-AddA on-PC numerical result (lamellar deepest at $\mu^{2}=-1$; all lattices trivially zero at $\mu^{2}=+0.005$). Reading A proposed lamellar as a candidate replacement for the BCC vacuum. The operator (2026-05-11) pointed out that Reading A is physically inappropriate for the TECT cosmological context on two distinct grounds: (i) lamellar breaks $\mathrm{SO}(3)\to \mathrm{SO}(2)$ at the order parameter level, designating a single direction as cosmologically special, in conflict with the observed isotropy of the CMB and large-scale structure; (ii) in soft-matter physics where lamellar phases are observed (block copolymers, lyotropic surfactants), lamellar is a phase-separation structure of two distinct materials A--B--A--B alternating layers, not a single-order-parameter condensation. Since TECT condenses a single $\Psi$ field, the lamellar wave $\Psi(\mathbf r)=A\cos(q_{0}z)$ does not represent a phase separation between two materials, and its identification as a ``vacuum'' is geometric rather than physical.

This Math400-AddA accepts the operator's filter, withdraws Reading A from the candidate-vacuum register, and reorganises the Math400 reading framework accordingly. The Math396-AddA collapse-to-lamellar finding is reinterpreted: it is a \emph{solver-trivial-minimum artefact}, not a demonstration that the TECT vacuum is lamellar.

\subsection*{1. Cosmological isotropy filter (binding constraint)}

The TECT framework is constructed to model the high-energy origin of spacetime structure. Any candidate vacuum must therefore satisfy the cosmological-isotropy constraint at the macroscopic averaging scale: the order parameter on which spacetime structure is built must be statistically isotropic, $\mathrm{SO}(3)$-invariant in the macroscopic average. Concretely:

\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;\text{TECT-vacuum candidate }\Psi_{0}(\mathbf r)\;\Rightarrow\;
    \big\langle\Psi_{0}(\mathbf r)\Psi_{0}(\mathbf r+\delta\mathbf r)\big\rangle_{\mathrm{macro}}\;\text{depends only on }|\delta\mathbf r|.\;}
\end{equation}

The macroscopic average is over volumes much larger than the intrinsic period $2\pi/q_{0}\sim 9.24$ but much smaller than cosmological scales ($\sim$ Hubble volume).

\paragraph{Lamellar.} $\Psi_{\rm lam}(\mathbf r)=A\cos(q_{0}z)$.
\begin{align}
  \langle\Psi_{\rm lam}(\mathbf r)\Psi_{\rm lam}(\mathbf r+\delta\mathbf r)\rangle
  &= \tfrac{A^{2}}{2}\cos(q_{0}\,\delta z)
\end{align}
which depends \emph{only on $\delta z$}, not on $|\delta\mathbf r|$. The two-point function is anisotropic. Lamellar fails the cosmological isotropy filter.

Furthermore, the Goldstone breaking pattern $\mathrm{SO}(3)\to\mathrm{SO}(2)$ generates 2 broken-rotation Goldstones $+$ 1 broken-translation Goldstone (along $\hat z$); in cosmological context these would manifest as anisotropies at the order $\Delta T/T \sim (\mathcal{F}_{\rm lam}/V)/\rho_{\rm crit}$ which is unobservably large for any $\mu^{2}$ giving nontrivial condensation. The CMB isotropy $\Delta T/T \lesssim 10^{-5}$ is incompatible with a lamellar condensate at any TECT-relevant amplitude.

\paragraph{HEX.} $\Psi_{\rm HEX}(\mathbf r)=A[\cos(q_{0}\hat x\cdot \mathbf r)+\cos(q_{0}\hat n_{2}\cdot \mathbf r)+\cos(q_{0}\hat n_{3}\cdot \mathbf r)]$ with the three modes at $120^{\circ}$ in the $xy$-plane. The two-point function depends on both $|\delta\mathbf r|_{xy}$ and $\delta z$ separately — anisotropic. HEX also fails the cosmological isotropy filter for the same reason. (Both lamellar and HEX are uniaxial.)

\paragraph{BCC.} 6 modes at $|\mathbf q|=q_{0}$ in directions $\frac{1}{\sqrt 2}(\pm 1,\pm 1, 0)$ permutations with one zero coordinate. The point group is cubic $O_{h}$. The two-point function in directions related by cubic symmetry is identical; in directions not so related (e.g., $(1,1,1)$ vs $(1,0,0)$) it differs at $O(1)$ level but the anisotropy is bounded by the cubic harmonic expansion at order $\ell=4$ (the lowest cubic harmonic above $\ell=0$).

\paragraph{FCC.} 4 modes at $|\mathbf q|=q_{0}$ in directions $\frac{1}{\sqrt 3}(\pm 1,\pm 1,\pm 1)$ with appropriate parity. Similar cubic point group $O_{h}$.

So the cosmological-isotropy filter strongly favours BCC or FCC (cubic point groups: anisotropy bounded by $\ell\geq 4$ harmonic) over HEX or lamellar (uniaxial: anisotropy at $\ell=2$).

\subsection*{2. Reading A withdrawn}

Math400 §4.2 proposed Reading A: ``the TECT canonical condensate, if it exists, is lamellar.'' This reading is hereby withdrawn because:

\begin{enumerate}
\item Lamellar fails the cosmological-isotropy filter (§1 above). Any condensation that selects a single direction $\hat z$ is incompatible with CMB observations at $\Delta T/T\lesssim 10^{-5}$.
\item Lamellar in soft-matter physics is a phase-separation structure of two distinct materials (block copolymer A-B-A-B). TECT has a single $\Psi$ field; the lamellar wave $A\cos(q_{0}z)$ is not a phase separation but a 1D modulation of a single field. The ``layers'' in TECT-lamellar would be regions of high-$|\Psi|$ alternating with regions of low-$|\Psi|$ along a single axis — a 1D crystallisation, not a 2-component phase. The geometric structure exists, but it lacks the physical content (two separable materials) that gives lamellar its meaning in soft-matter contexts.
\item The Math396-AddA collapse to lamellar (HEX collapse to single-mode lamellar; FCC and BCC failure to converge symmetric configurations) is consistent with the L-BFGS-B descent finding the simplest single-mode minimum that exists in the discrete-grid Brazovskii functional. This is a \emph{solver-trivial-minimum artefact}: the single-mode configuration is the lowest-dimensional irreducible representation of the lattice manifold, and the L-BFGS-B in the unconstrained mode-amplitude space naturally descends to it.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{5. Devil's-advocate (CLAUDE.md §6.3 + §6.3.5(a))}

\textbf{Objection $\alpha$ (concrete):} The cosmological-isotropy filter is too restrictive. Cosmological inflation can homogenise a locally-anisotropic vacuum to macroscopic isotropy by stretching one direction much more than others, leaving an effective post-inflation vacuum that appears isotropic at observable scales. Lamellar vacuum at the pre-inflation scale could be inflated to apparent isotropy.

\emph{DISMISSED}. Inflation stretches scales but does not change the local symmetry of the order parameter. A lamellar vacuum stretched by inflation remains a 1D modulation in the asymptotic post-inflation comoving frame; only its wavelength gets stretched. The order-parameter direction does not become statistically isotropic merely because of inflation. The CMB isotropy at $\Delta T/T \lesssim 10^{-5}$ constrains the order-parameter symmetry, not the wavelength.

\textbf{Objection $\beta$ (concrete):} Reading D (extended-lattice search) is an open-ended programme; the candidate set $\{$A15, $\sigma$, gyroid, diamond$\}$ is itself not exhaustive. There may be a quasi-crystal or aperiodic structure that wins.

\emph{VALID-with-mitigation}. The candidate set should be expanded systematically by the criterion ``cosmologically-isotropy-compatible'': any cubic-point-group lattice (or non-crystalline isotropic structure such as a glass / disordered correlated state). The mitigation is to use a symmetry-classification approach: enumerate lattice candidates by point-group order and test in order. The exhaustiveness question is open; for now, $\{$A15, $\sigma$, gyroid, diamond$\}$ extended to cubic structures known from soft-matter literature is the immediate scope.

\textbf{Objection $\gamma$ (concrete):} Reading B (frozen-in non-equilibrium) requires an additional assumption: the relaxation timescale of the BCC saddle exceeds the cosmological age. This timescale should be computable from Brazovskii Model A dynamics (Math385). If the relaxation timescale is short, the BCC saddle would have decayed already, and Reading B fails.

\emph{VALID-with-mitigation}. The Brazovskii Model A relaxation time at the saddle is $\tau_{\rm relax} \sim 1/(\Gamma_{\rm A} \cdot |\lambda_{\min}|)$ where $\lambda_{\min}$ is the most negative Hessian eigenvalue at the BCC saddle (Math383 §6: $\sim -3.7\times 10^{-4}$ at $\mu^{2}=+0.005$). With $\Gamma_{\rm A}\sim 1$ in natural units, $\tau_{\rm relax}\sim 10^{4}$ in TECT lattice time units. This is negligibly short compared to cosmological time scales (Hubble $\sim 10^{60}$ in Planck units). \textbf{Reading B is in serious tension with this calculation}: the BCC saddle cannot survive cosmological time. Either the relaxation is suppressed by additional physics (tunneling? Math392 instanton calculation gave $S_{\rm inst}>100$ giving exponential suppression — but Math392 was REFUTED for Mechanism III-a), or Reading B's frozen-in interpretation needs refinement. Mitigation: Math402 must address this timescale tension explicitly.

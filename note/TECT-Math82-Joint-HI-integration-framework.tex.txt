\subsection*{2. Numerical thresholds for each case}

All thresholds below are **pre-registered falsification criteria** per CLAUDE.md §6.3.3. They are extracted from Math82-H, Math82-I, and Math82-I-AddA §1.3.

\subsubsection*{2.1 GROUND\_STATE confirmation criteria (necessary condition for Case 1 or 2)}

A solution $\Psi^*$ from Math82-H is classified as GROUND\_STATE if:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 1}] $\Delta F(\Psi^*) < 0$ at $\geq 5$ of the 7 $\mu^2$ points.
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 2}] $\lambda_{\min}(\mathcal{H})(\Psi^*) > 0$ at $\geq 5$ of the 7 $\mu^2$ points.
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 3}] $\|\Psi^*\|/\sqrt{N} > 10^{-3}$ at all 7 points.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{Rationale.}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Criterion 1 (Brazovskii free energy $\Delta F < 0$): ensures the solution is energetically favorable relative to the vacuum.
  \item Criterion 2 (positive spectral gap $\lambda_{\min} > 0$): ensures the solution is a local minimum, not a saddle or higher-order critical point.
  \item Criterion 3 (non-zero amplitude): rules out numerical-vacuum artefacts (which have $\|\Psi\| \sim 10^{-5}$ to $10^{-6}$).
\end{itemize}

If any of these three criteria fails, classify as **SADDLE** or **NUMERICAL\_ARTEFACT** and proceed to Case 3 or Case 4 logic.

\subsubsection*{2.2 NON\_TRIVIAL\_BRANCH\_EXISTS confirmation criteria (necessary condition for Case 1 or 3)}

An outcome from Math82-I is classified as NON\_TRIVIAL\_BRANCH\_EXISTS if:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 1}] At least $2$ of the $5$ replicas per $\mu^2$ value converge to non-trivial equilibria (Definition~\ref{def:nontrivial-equilibrium}, Math82-I-AddA §1.3).
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 2}] The two or more non-trivial equilibria satisfy $\|\Psi^*\|/\sqrt{N} > 10^{-3}$ AND $\Delta F < 0$.
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 3}] At least one of the 7 $\mu^2$ values exhibits NON\_TRIVIAL\_BRANCH\_EXISTS (not all 7 return VACUUM\_COLLAPSE or NEWTON\_FAILURE).
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{Rationale.}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Criterion 1 ($\geq 2$ replicas): Theorem~\ref{thm:branch-existence-decision-rule} (Math82-I-AddA §3) guarantees that with $K=5$ i.i.d.\ random-phase seeds, the probability of missing a generic non-trivial basin is $\ll 1$ if any two seeds converge independently to that basin. A single convergence is ambiguous (local basin); two or more is robust evidence.
  \item Criterion 2 (energy and amplitude checks): confirms the converged state is not a numerical artefact or spurious local minimum above the true ground state.
  \item Criterion 3 (existence at $\geq 1$ $\mu^2$ value): establishes that NON\_TRIVIAL\_BRANCH\_EXISTS is not a stray isolated event but a systematic feature of the phase diagram.
\end{itemize}

If any of these three criteria fails, classify as **VACUUM\_COLLAPSE** and proceed to Case 2 or Case 4 logic.

\subsubsection*{2.3 VACUUM\_COLLAPSE confirmation criteria (necessary condition for Case 2 or 4)}

An outcome from Math82-I is classified as VACUUM\_COLLAPSE if:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 1}] All 5 replicas at all 7 $\mu^2$ values converge to numerical vacuum ($\|\Psi^*\|/\sqrt{N} < 10^{-3}$ or $\Delta F > 0$).
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 2}] At least $4$ of the $5$ replicas per $\mu^2$ value terminate with $\mathrm{converged} = \mathrm{true}$ within 15 Newton iterations (i.e., solver converges; does not stall).
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{Rationale.}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Criterion 1: no non-trivial equilibrium detected across independent random-phase seeds.
  \item Criterion 2: solver convergence is reliable; the outcome is not due to truncated iteration budgets.
\end{itemize}

If Newton solver fails to converge on $> 1$ replica per $\mu^2$, classify instead as **NEWTON\_FAILURE** and flag the result as inconclusive.

\subsubsection*{2.4 SADDLE confirmation criteria (necessary condition for Case 3 or 4)}

A solution $\Psi^*$ from Math82-H is classified as SADDLE if:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 1}] $\lambda_{\min}(\mathcal{H})(\Psi^*) < 0$ at any of the 7 $\mu^2$ points.
  \item[\textbf{Criterion 2}] The negative eigenvalue is reproducible: Phase-2 Lanczos mini-spectrum (32-vector basis) reports $\min_j \theta_j < 0$ with relative tolerance $\lesssim 1\%$.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph*{Rationale.}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Criterion 1: immediate indicator of instability.
  \item Criterion 2 (reproducibility check): guards against isolated Lanczos noise; repeated estimates with different random projections must agree.
\end{itemize}

If $\lambda_{\min} > 0$ at all 7 points, confirm GROUND\_STATE instead.

\subsubsection*{3.2 Math82-H classification}

\begin{verbatim}
# Step 1: Classify H outcome
def classify_math82h(results):
    # Criterion 1: Delta F < 0 at >= 5 of 7 points
    n_favorable = np.sum(results.delta_f < 0.0)
    criterion_1 = n_favorable >= THRESHOLD_GROUND_STATE_POINTS

    # Criterion 2: lambda_min > 0 at >= 5 of 7 points
    n_minimum = np.sum(results.lambda_min > 0.0)
    criterion_2 = n_minimum >= THRESHOLD_GROUND_STATE_POINTS

    # Criterion 3: ||Psi|| / sqrt(N) > 1e-3 at all 7 points
    n_nontrivial = np.sum((results.norms / np.sqrt(32)) > THRESHOLD_NONTRIVIAL_NORM)
    criterion_3 = (n_nontrivial == 7)

    if criterion_1 and criterion_2 and criterion_3:
        h_status = "GS-CURVE"
        h_verdict = "GROUND_STATE confirmed"
    elif np.any(results.lambda_min < 0.0):
        h_status = "SADDLE"
        h_verdict = "Saddle point detected"
    else:
        h_status = "NUMERICAL_ARTEFACT"
        h_verdict = "Convergence to trivial state"

    return h_status, h_verdict

h_status, h_verdict = classify_math82h(math82h_results)
print(f"Math82-H: {h_status} ({h_verdict})")
\end{verbatim}

\subsection*{4. Theorem `thm:joint-HI-classification-completeness`}

\begin{theorem}[Joint H+I classification is exhaustive]
\label{thm:joint-HI-classification-completeness}

The four-case matrix in §1 is exhaustive and mutually exclusive over all possible joint outcomes of Math82-H and Math82-I, provided that:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(A)] Math82-H termination is either convergence to a stationary point (minimum or saddle) or numerical failure (flagged separately).
  \item[(B)] Math82-I Newton solver converges to a stationary point or flags a failure.
  \item[(C)] Classification criteria in §2 are applied consistently.
\end{enumerate}

Then every (H, I) pair outcome is assigned to exactly one of the four cases via the decision tree.

\paragraph*{Proof sketch.}

The classification depends on two binary questions:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] Is Math82-H a ground-state curve (GS-CURVE) or a saddle (SADDLE)?
  \item[(ii)] Does Math82-I reveal a non-trivial branch (NON\_TRIVIAL\_BRANCH) or vacuum collapse (VACUUM\_COLLAPSE)?
\end{enumerate}

These two questions are answered independently using the thresholds in §2. The answers partition the outcome space into $2 \times 2 = 4$ disjoint regions corresponding to Cases 1–4. Every outcome falls into exactly one region.

Ambiguous cases (e.g., H inconclusive, I NEWTON\_FAILURE) are explicitly routed to audit-flag status rather than forced into a case.

\end{theorem}

\subsection*{5. Devil's-advocate (CLAUDE.md §6.3)}

Three concrete objections to the joint-classification framework are examined below.

\subsubsection*{Objection α: The 4-case matrix may have unexamined edge cases}

\paragraph*{Claim.}

The matrix assumes that Math82-H outcomes are binary (GS-CURVE or SADDLE) and Math82-I outcomes are binary (NON\_TRIVIAL\_BRANCH or VACUUM\_COLLAPSE). But what if partial convergence occurs, e.g., H succeeds at 4 of 7 points and fails at 3? Or I returns MIXED\_BIMODAL at some $\mu^2$ values and NON\_TRIVIAL\_BRANCH at others?

\paragraph*{Resolution (DISMISSED).}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Partial H convergence}: If Criterion 1 or Criterion 2 (§2.1) is not satisfied, H is classified as SADDLE or NUMERICAL\_ARTEFACT, not GS-CURVE. The threshold $\geq 5$ of 7 points is stratified; outcomes with 4/7 or 3/7 favorable points are explicitly not classified as a ground-state curve and are handled by Case 3 (SADDLE) or Case 4 (VACUUM) logic.

  \item \textbf{Mixed I classification across $\mu^2$ values}: I outcomes are stratified per $\mu^2$ (see §2.2, §2.3). The overall I status is determined by the existence rule: if \emph{any} $\mu^2$ value shows NON\_TRIVIAL\_BRANCH, the overall verdict is NON\_TRIVIAL\_BRANCH. If \emph{all} $\mu^2$ values show VACUUM\_COLLAPSE (or NEWTON\_FAILURE, MIXED\_BIMODAL), the verdict is VACUUM\_COLLAPSE. This rule is exhaustive over the per-$\mu^2$ outcomes.

  \item \textbf{Conclusion}: the 4-case matrix is exhaustive because every outcome is mapped to at least one of the four cases via the stratification rules.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{Objection β: The ground-state curve from Case 1 may be on a non-physical SCAFFOLD branch}

\paragraph*{Claim.}

Criterion 2 (§2.1) requires $\lambda_{\min} > 0$ at $\geq 5$ of 7 points. But what if $\lambda_{\min}$ is computed incorrectly due to Lanczos truncation? Or what if the curve lies on a higher-dimensional manifold of pseudo-minima that is actually a saddle in the full 12-mode space?

\paragraph*{Resolution (DISMISSED).}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Lanczos reliability}: Criterion 2 in §2.4 explicitly requires a reproducibility check: Lanczos mini-spectrum at two different random projections must agree to within 1\% relative tolerance. If $\lambda_{\min}$ is spurious, this reproducibility test will catch it. (See Math82-AddG2 §3 for the full Lanczos-probe validation.)

  \item \textbf{Manifold topology}: If the 12-mode ground-state curve lies on a higher-dimensional manifold of pseudo-minima (e.g., a lower-dimensional saddle surface), the Hessian along the curve would have zero eigenvalues, not positive ones. The criterion $\lambda_{\min} > 0$ strictly rules out manifolds and enforces strict local minimality.

  \item \textbf{Pillar 8 cross-check (Lorentz bounds)}: Any non-physical curve would violate Lorentz-invariant bounds from Pillar 8 (Math82 Addendum-I-AddA §5, Pillar 8 coupling verification). Case 1 outcomes will be validated against Pillar 8 thresholds as a secondary audit gate.

  \item \textbf{Conclusion}: the ground-state curve from Case 1 is physically realized and locally minimal.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{Objection γ: Case 4 (VACUUM\_DOMINATED) would invalidate years of TECT work}

\paragraph*{Claim.}

If Case 4 occurs, the Brazovskii functional supports only the trivial vacuum. This contradicts Pillar 1 (mass gap, ground-state energy), Pillar 7 (energy-momentum tensor), and much of the Stage-2 programme. Have we set ourselves up for a catastrophic failure?

\paragraph*{Resolution (UPHELD, with documented scope).}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Case 4 is theoretically possible but inconsistent with prior evidence}: Math82-AddG2 (2026-04-24) already confirmed that a non-trivial branch exists at $\mu^2 = +5e-3$ (Point 1 of the Phase Z continuation: converged solution with $\|\Psi^*\|/\sqrt{N} = 0.31$ and $\Delta F = -0.0031 < 0$). Case 4 would require this evidence to be retracted or reinterpreted as an artefact.

  \item \textbf{Contingency plan}: If Case 4 occurs, the sequence of actions is:
  \begin{enumerate}

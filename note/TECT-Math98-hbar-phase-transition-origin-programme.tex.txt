\section*{Math98: Phase-Transition Origin of the Quantum of Action --- a Research Programme}

\subsection*{0. Front-matter -- relationship to Pillar 10 classical no-go}

This note opens a \emph{research programme}, not a theorem.  Its purpose
is to delimit precisely how Pillar 10 may be extended \emph{beyond}
the Math79-AddB classical no-go without contradicting it.  The classical
no-go states:
\begin{quote}\itshape
No dimensionless combination of the post-transition classical Brazovskii
parameters $\mathcal{P}_{\rm class}=\{\lambda,\gamma,\mu^{2},Y,a_{\rm BCC},c_{T},\varepsilon\}$
yields $\hbar$ without an external quantisation axiom.
\end{quote}
The present programme proposes that $\hbar$ originates from the
\emph{non-equilibrium condensation transition} preceding the post-transition
PDE, in particular via a frozen-in adiabatic action invariant. The premise
set
$$
\mathcal{P}_{\rm PT}\;:=\;\mathcal{P}_{\rm class}\;\cup\;\{\rho_{\rm cond}^{\rm pre\text{-}PT},\;\tau_{\rm PT},\;\eta_{\rm top}\}
$$
strictly enlarges $\mathcal{P}_{\rm class}$ (the new symbols are not
expressible in $\mathcal{P}_{\rm class}$ without a kinetic coefficient
$\Gamma$, which is absent from the static Brazovskii free energy).
Therefore Math79-AddB does \emph{not} apply to this enlarged premise set,
and a derivation of $\hbar$ from $\mathcal{P}_{\rm PT}$ does not
contradict the classical no-go.

\subsection*{1. Central conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Math98-Main: $\hbar$ as frozen-in condensation action]
\label{conj:Math98-main}
Let the pre-condensation phase be an isotropic high-energy fluid state
with action density $\mathcal{S}_{\rm pre}[\Psi]$ and let the post-transition
phase be the rigid BCC topological condensate of TECT.  Then there exist
three intrinsic transition scales
$$
E_{\rm PT},\qquad \tau_{\rm PT},\qquad \ell_{\rm PT}\sim a_{\rm BCC},
$$
such that the IR effective theory satisfies
$$
[\hat{\Psi}(\mathbf{x},t),\hat{\Pi}_{\Psi}(\mathbf{x}',t)]
\;=\;i\,\hbar\,\delta^{3}(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}'),\qquad
\hbar
\;=\;
\eta_{\rm top}\,\rho_{\rm cond}\,a_{\rm BCC}^{3}\,\tau_{\rm PT},
$$
where $\eta_{\rm top}$ is a dimensionless topological invariant of the
BCC lattice and $\rho_{\rm cond}$ is the condensation energy density per
unit volume.  Here $\hbar$ is not derived inside the post-transition
classical PDE; rather it is the frozen-in adiabatic invariant of the
non-equilibrium condensation event.
\end{conjecture}

The conjecture decomposes into three sub-claims.

\begin{conjecture}[C98-1: Pre-transition action scale]
\label{conj:C98-1}
The pre-condensation phase admits a finite coherence action scale
$S_{\rm PT}=E_{\rm PT}\tau_{\rm PT}$ associated with one BCC unit cell
undergoing the topological rearrangement.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[C98-2: Adiabatic invariance of condensation action]
\label{conj:C98-2}
Under slow quench of $\mu^{2}$ across the Brazovskii critical point
$\mu^{2}\to R_{\rm C}^{\rm global}$, $S_{\rm PT}$ is conserved as an
adiabatic invariant and is frozen into the IR effective theory as a
universal IR constant.
\end{conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[C98-3: Commutator recovery in the IR]
\label{conj:C98-3}
The linearised fluctuation spectrum of the post-BCC condensate satisfies
$$
[\hat{\Psi}(\mathbf{x},t),\hat{\Pi}_{\Psi}(\mathbf{x}',t)]
\;=\;
i\,S_{\rm PT}\,\eta_{\rm norm}\,\delta^{3}(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}')
\;\equiv\;
i\,\hbar\,\delta^{3}(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}'),
$$
with $\eta_{\rm norm}$ determined by BCC shell-mode normalisation
(Math57-AddA) and $\hbar:=S_{\rm PT}\,\eta_{\rm norm}$.
\end{conjecture}

\subsection*{4. Resolving the universality of $\hbar$}

A standard objection: $\hbar$ is empirically the same constant across
all physical systems (atoms, photons, gravitons, neutrinos).  If $\hbar$
originated from a specific BCC condensation, why is it universal across
all subsystems?

\paragraph{Resolution within TECT.}
The TECT framework postulates that the universe \emph{is} a single
cosmic BCC condensate; every particle and field is a phonon, defect, or
sub-mode of this single BCC.  Consequently:
$$
\hbar_{\rm atom}
\;=\;\hbar_{\rm photon}
\;=\;\hbar_{\rm graviton}
\;=\;\hbar_{\rm neutrino}
\;=\;\hbar_{\rm BCC},
$$
because every subsystem inherits its commutation relations from the
single underlying BCC commutator.  Universality is then a structural
consequence of cosmic uniqueness, not a separate empirical accident.
This is the strongest predictive content of the conjecture: \emph{the
universality of $\hbar$ is equivalent to the uniqueness of the cosmic
BCC condensation event.}

\subsection*{5. Four mathematical approach paths}

\paragraph{Path (a) -- Kibble--Zurek scaling for $\tau_{\rm PT}$.}
The Brazovskii class has correlation-length exponent $\nu=1/2$ (mean
field) and dynamical exponent $z=2$ (Model A).  For a quench rate
$\tau_{Q}$ across the critical point, the Kibble--Zurek freeze-out time
scales as
$$
\tau_{\rm PT}\;\sim\;\tau_{Q}^{\,z\nu/(1+z\nu)}\;=\;\tau_{Q}^{\,1/2}.
$$
If $\tau_{Q}\sim (a_{\rm BCC}/c_{T})^{2}\,|\mu^{2}|^{-1}$, then
$\tau_{\rm PT}$ becomes derivable purely from $\mathcal{P}_{\rm class}$
parameters, eliminating it as a free input.  This path is the most
promising and is the subject of the planned Math98-AddA.

\paragraph{Path (b) -- Volovik analogy ($^{3}$He-B).}
Volovik (2003) showed that superfluid $^{3}$He-B exhibits an emergent
Lorentz metric and an effective $\hbar$ tied to the order-parameter
fluctuation spectrum near the topological gap nodes.  TECT's BCC
condensate has the same structural ingredients (order parameter $\Psi$,
emergent Lorentz from Pillar 2 / Math97, shell isotropy from
Math57-AddA).  Translating Volovik's argument from $^{3}$He-B to
TECT-BCC yields a candidate explicit formula for
$\eta_{\rm norm}$ in terms of the BCC shell density of states. Subject
of planned Math98-AddB.

\paragraph{Path (c) -- Berry phase / topological winding.}
The 6 BCC primitive wave-vectors $\{\mathbf{Q}_{i}\}_{i=1}^{6}$ define a
non-trivially fibred phase space; topological defects (vortices, screw
dislocations, disclinations) carry quantised winding numbers.  The
sum of defect winding densities provides an explicit formula for
$\eta_{\rm top}$ as the topological invariant
$$
\eta_{\rm top}
\;=\;
\frac{1}{(2\pi)^{3}}\int_{\rm BZ}\,\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathcal{F}\wedge\mathcal{A}
\;\sim\;
\mathcal{O}(1),
$$
where $\mathcal{F}$ is the Berry curvature of the BCC valence bundle.
Subject of planned Math98-AddC.

\paragraph{Path (d) -- Onsager--Machlup adiabatic invariant.}
For Langevin dynamics
$\partial_{t}\Psi=-\Gamma\,\delta\mathcal{F}/\delta\Psi+\xi$ with white
noise $\xi$, the path-integral weight is $e^{-S_{\rm OM}/\eta}$ where
$\eta$ has dimensions of action.  If $\eta$ originates from
\emph{topological rigidity} of the BCC condensate (rather than thermal
fluctuation), this $\eta$ is the natural candidate for $\hbar$.
Subject of planned Math98-AddD.

\subsection*{7. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md sec 6.3.1)}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$: ``Pre-transition axiom doubles the TECT
axiom set, violating minimalism.''}

\paragraph{Response.}
\textbf{Verdict: VALID with mitigation via Path (a).}
The minimal extra axiom required is the existence of a kinetic
coefficient $\Gamma$ in $\partial_{t}\Psi=-\Gamma\,\delta\mathcal{F}/\delta\Psi+\xi$.
This is a standard thermodynamic/kinetic upgrade of any static free
energy and is not a doubling of the axiom set. If Path (a)
(Kibble--Zurek) succeeds in expressing $\tau_{\rm PT}$ purely in
$\mathcal{P}_{\rm class}$, no additional dimensional scales are
introduced beyond $\Gamma$. The mitigation cost is then the single
new parameter $\Gamma$, equivalent to introducing Newton's $G$ to GR or
$\Lambda$ to cosmology.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$: ``$\tau_{\rm PT}$ is circular -- it just
renames $\hbar$.''}

\paragraph{Response.}
\textbf{Verdict: VALID with falsification gate F2.}
If $\tau_{\rm PT}$ cannot be derived from $\mathcal{P}_{\rm class}\cup\{\Gamma\}$
via the Kibble--Zurek relation, the proposal is indeed circular and
must be retracted. Gate F2 is the explicit falsification criterion.
The non-circularity hinges on Path (a)'s success.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$: ``Universality of $\hbar$ is empirical
and the uniqueness-of-cosmic-BCC argument is unprovable from inside
TECT.''}

\paragraph{Response.}
\textbf{Verdict: VALID with structural-but-not-rigorous defence.}
The cosmic-uniqueness argument is part of TECT's foundational ansatz
(CLAUDE.md sec 8 and TOE-FACT-SHEET Premise: ``a primordial 3D BCC
topological condensate'').  Within TECT, this is a definitional axiom,
not a theorem.  However, this is no worse than the GR axiom of a
single spacetime manifold or the QFT axiom of a single Hilbert space:
it is the same kind of foundational uniqueness assumption that all
fundamental physics must take.  The programme inherits this baseline
and does not require an independent proof of cosmic uniqueness.

\paragraph{Objection $\delta$: ``Even if all four Paths succeed, the
programme only matches ONE empirical constant ($\hbar$). Is this a
genuine prediction or curve-fitting?''}

\paragraph{Response.}
\textbf{Verdict: VALID with sharper test required.}
A successful programme matching only $\hbar$ is suspicious.  The
genuine predictive content emerges if the same framework
\emph{simultaneously} predicts:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\hbar$ to within Gate F3 precision ($\le 10\%$).
\item Universality of $\hbar$ across all sub-modes (free consequence).
\item A finite, calculable correction to $\hbar$ from the next-order
      adiabatic invariant ($\hbar\to\hbar+\delta\hbar$ in higher-order
      Kibble--Zurek expansion). If $\delta\hbar/\hbar\sim 10^{-N}$ for
      some computable $N$, this becomes an experimentally testable
      prediction beyond the leading-order match.
\item No additional empirical inputs beyond $\mathcal{P}_{\rm class}\cup\{\Gamma\}$.
\end{enumerate}
If the programme produces only (1) without (3), the programme is
classified as STRUCTURAL EXPLANATION rather than predictive theorem;
nonetheless it removes Pillar 10 from the OPEN-CONJECTURE list and
classifies $\hbar$'s origin to the same status as Newton's $G$
(structural constant of nature, not derived from microphysics but
explicitly tied to the cosmological initial condition).

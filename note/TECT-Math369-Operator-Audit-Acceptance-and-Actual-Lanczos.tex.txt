§ 4. INTERPRETATION OF NUMERICAL RESULT
==================================================================

This result has three possible interpretations:

(I) **The simple model is wrong**: The 12-mode equal-amplitude Hessian
    with the locked parameter values $(\mu^2, \gamma, \lambda) =
    (-0.7, 1.62, -0.43)$ does NOT represent the true Brazovskii-BCC
    physics. Possible issues:
      - Sign convention on $\lambda$: standard Brazovskii needs
        $\lambda > 0$ for boundedness; $\lambda = -0.43$ indicates
        the operator's parameters describe a first-order transition
        regime where BCC is metastable (free-energy second derivative
        can be negative for fluctuations toward disorder).
      - $c_{4,\rm BCC} = 11/8$ may not be the correct coupling for the
        12-mode (vs 6-mode complex-amplitude) basis.
      - 12-mode equal-amplitude ansatz omits Goldstone modes that
        regularise the spectrum.

(II) **The model is right and BCC is metastable**: Brazovskii's
    weak-crystallisation transition is genuinely first-order; below
    the transition, BCC is the global minimum, but above it (or near
    the spinodal), BCC is a saddle. The chosen $\mu^2 = -0.7$ may
    be near or above the spinodal limit.

(III) **Math358's "expected" verdict was wrong**: The agent
    asserted "BCC = local minimum, all positive eigenvalues except
    6 Goldstone zeros" without actually computing. This simple
    computation FAILS to reproduce that verdict. The agent's claim
    requires the full nonlinear analysis (multi-mode coupling +
    Goldstone subtraction + parameter regime verification), not
    the simple 12x12 matrix.

REGARDLESS OF WHICH INTERPRETATION HOLDS, the conclusion is:

  $\boxed{\text{Math358's claim of "BCC is local minimum, verified" is NOT supported}}$
  $\boxed{\text{by the simplest direct Hessian computation in this sandbox.}}$

This further confirms the operator's audit. The Pillar 4 closure path

\subsection*{4. Devil's-advocate self-test on this analysis}

over-claim (if the difficulty of phenomenological closure is ignored)
or under-claim (if the achievability of theoretical closure is missed).

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$.} ``Scope expansion (DM, BH, CMB) is
unbounded; how does TECT decide where to stop?''

\textbf{Verdict: VALID, mitigated.} The TOE-qualification predicate
$S_1 \wedge S_2 \wedge S_3$ in Math60 specifies the boundary. Scope
expansion is bounded by what is required for $S_3$ to seal; once a
prediction is registered for the corresponding observable (DM density,
BH entropy, CMB structure), the scope item is ``in scope'' for TECT.
Items beyond that --- e.g., particular TOE-candidate features like
SUSY --- are out of scope by the axiom-minimal philosophy.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$.} ``The 30-turn audit-discipline track
record (5 catches in 6 rounds) is not a guarantee of future correctness.
Why should the next 30 turns also catch?''

\textbf{Verdict: VALID, MITIGATION REQUIRED.} The track record is
empirical, not deductive. The mitigation is the layered-review
discipline: agent-level audit + dispatcher-level commit-review. The
Math173 miss → Math171-AddA catch demonstrates the layered structure
working. The discipline is robust, not infallible. Future-round
protocol amendment (Math190 §6) requires verbatim hand-derivation
comparison in audits.

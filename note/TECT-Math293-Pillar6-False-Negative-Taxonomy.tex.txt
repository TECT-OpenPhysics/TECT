\section{Setting and partition definition}
\label{sec:setting}

Recall the four primitive gauges of Math292 §\ref{sec:setting}:
$(G1)\;\Delta F$,\,$(G2)\;f$,\,$(G3)\;\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}$,
$(G4)\;\mathtt{extraction\_status}$. A Phase-2 computation fails
the Math292 acceptance criterion iff at least one of the four
predicates is false. A failure pattern is the indicator vector
$\mathbf{F}\in\{0,1\}^{4}$ where $F_k=0$ means gauge $k$ is
satisfied and $F_k=1$ means gauge $k$ is violated, with
$\mathbf{F}\neq\mathbf{0}$ excluded (the all-zero pattern is the
acceptance class, not a failure class). There are $2^{4}-1=15$
non-trivial failure patterns.

\begin{definition}[Three failure classes]
\label{def:classes}
For a failure pattern $\mathbf{F}\in\{0,1\}^{4}\setminus\{\mathbf{0}\}$:
\begin{description}
\item[$\mathcal{C}_1$ — extraction structural failure:]
$F_4=1$ (i.e.\ $\mathtt{extraction\_status}\neq\mathtt{OK}$),
\emph{or}
$F_4=0$ together with the discrepancy
$f^{\rm reported}=0$ but $f^{\rm patched-extractor}>0$
(detected by re-extraction with the Math236 wrapper v2.0
\texttt{extract\_amplitude} on the persisted $\Psi_{\rm final}.\mathtt{npy}$).
\item[$\mathcal{C}_2$ — trivial-saddle convergence:]
$F_4=0$ AND the patched extractor confirms $f>0$ AND
($F_1=1$ OR $F_3=1$). I.e.\ wrapper integrity is intact and
$f>0$ (so it is not class $\mathcal{C}_1$), but $\Delta F\ge 0$
or $\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}<0$.
\item[$\mathcal{C}_3$ — genuine physical falsification:]
$F_4=0$ AND $F_1=0$ AND $F_3=0$ AND $F_2=1$. I.e.\ wrapper
integrity intact, $\Delta F<0$, $\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}\ge 0$,
but the broken-phase amplitude $f$ is below the pre-registered
threshold $f_{\rm thr}$ of Math292 §\ref{sec:thresholds} \emph{and
the same outcome holds across the full continuum-limit fit window}
$N\in\{16,32,64\}$ at the same $(\mu^2, a)$ scan target.
\end{description}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
The class boundaries are designed so that a single Phase-2 run
falls into exactly one class. The $f^{\rm reported}=0$ vs
$f^{\rm patched}>0$ test in $\mathcal{C}_1$ is the operational
signature of Math290 Bug A; the $\mathtt{extraction\_status}\neq\mathtt{OK}$
condition is the signature of Math290 Bug B (and other
extractor structural defects).
\end{remark}

\section{Theorem statement}
\label{sec:theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Three-class exhaustive disjoint partition]
\label{thm:partition}
The three classes of Definition \ref{def:classes} are pairwise
disjoint and exhaustive on the set of failure patterns
$\{0,1\}^{4}\setminus\{\mathbf{0}\}$ extended with the auxiliary
patched-extractor flag.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textit{Disjointness $\mathcal{C}_1\cap\mathcal{C}_2=\emptyset$:}
$\mathcal{C}_2$ requires $F_4=0$ (wrapper status OK) AND patched
extractor confirms $f>0$. $\mathcal{C}_1$ requires either $F_4=1$
or ($F_4=0$ AND $f^{\rm reported}=0$ but $f^{\rm patched}>0$). The
second sub-case of $\mathcal{C}_1$ requires
$f^{\rm reported}=0$, while $\mathcal{C}_2$ requires
$f^{\rm reported}>0$ (the wrapper is intact, hence
$f^{\rm reported}=f^{\rm patched}>0$). Disjoint.

\textit{Disjointness $\mathcal{C}_1\cap\mathcal{C}_3=\emptyset$:}
$\mathcal{C}_3$ requires $F_4=0$ AND $f$ truthfully extracted (so
$f^{\rm reported}=f^{\rm patched}<f_{\rm thr}$). $\mathcal{C}_1$
requires either $F_4=1$ (excluded) or $f^{\rm reported}=0$ vs.
$f^{\rm patched}>0$ discrepancy (excluded since $f^{\rm reported}=
f^{\rm patched}$ in $\mathcal{C}_3$). Disjoint.

\textit{Disjointness $\mathcal{C}_2\cap\mathcal{C}_3=\emptyset$:}
$\mathcal{C}_2$ requires $F_1=1$ OR $F_3=1$; $\mathcal{C}_3$
requires $F_1=0$ AND $F_3=0$. Disjoint.

\textit{Exhaustiveness:}
Suppose $\mathbf{F}\neq\mathbf{0}$ is a failure pattern. Three cases:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F_4=1$: belongs to $\mathcal{C}_1$ by definition.
\item $F_4=0$, $f^{\rm patched}=0$: $\mathtt{extraction\_status}=\mathtt{OK}$
but no shell modes contributed; this is wrapper-internal logical
inconsistency, classified as $\mathcal{C}_1$ by the second sub-case
($f^{\rm reported}=0$, but here also $f^{\rm patched}=0$ — by
convention, both-zero is treated as $\mathcal{C}_1$ pending
re-extraction with Math292-AddA wrapper v2.1 which adds an
explicit ``no-shell-modes'' status code).
\item $F_4=0$, $f^{\rm patched}>0$: either ($F_1=1$ OR $F_3=1$)
giving $\mathcal{C}_2$, or ($F_1=0$ AND $F_3=0$ AND $F_2=1$)
giving $\mathcal{C}_3$. The remaining sub-case
$F_1=F_2=F_3=F_4=0$ is excluded as the success class.
\end{enumerate}
The 15 non-trivial failure patterns are thereby classified. \qed
\end{proof}

\section{Operational decision tree}
\label{sec:decision}

Given a Phase-2 run output $(\mu^2,N,a,\Psi_{\rm final}.\mathtt{npy},
\mathrm{stdout})$, the wrapper applies the following decision tree:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Read \texttt{extraction\_status} from wrapper diagnostic.
\begin{itemize}
\item if $\neq\mathtt{OK}$ → $\mathcal{C}_1$ (extraction structural).
\textbf{Action:} re-extract with patched extractor; if patched
extractor agrees, escalate as wrapper-internal defect; if patched
extractor disagrees, log Math290-Bug-A-class artefact; \textbf{re-run not required for re-extraction}.
\end{itemize}
\item Run patched extractor on persisted $\Psi_{\rm final}.\mathtt{npy}$.
Compute $f^{\rm patched}$.
\begin{itemize}
\item if $f^{\rm patched}=0$ → $\mathcal{C}_1$ (no shell modes).
\textbf{Action:} re-seed with Math236\_seed\_striped initial condition,
re-run.
\item if $f^{\rm reported}=0$ but $f^{\rm patched}>0$ → $\mathcal{C}_1$
(Bug A class). \textbf{Action:} update wrapper to v2.1, re-run.
\end{itemize}
\item Compute $(\Delta F, \lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse})$ from
solver diagnostic.
\begin{itemize}
\item if $\Delta F\ge \Delta F_{\rm thr}$ OR $\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}<-\epsilon_{\rm Hess}$
→ $\mathcal{C}_2$ (trivial-saddle convergence).
\textbf{Action:} re-seed with deterministic striped seed
$\Psi^{(0)}_{c}(\mathbf{x})=A_{0}\hat e^{c}\cos(q_{0}\hat n\cdot\mathbf{x})$
(Math236\_seed\_striped, post-Math290 helper). If repeat fails
across $\mu^{2}\in\{-0.7, -1.0\}$ at $N=16$, lower $\mu^{2}$ further;
if all of $\mu^{2}\in\{-1.0,-1.5,-2.0\}$ at $N=16$ trivial-saddle,
escalate to Math293-AddA (transition-point recalibration).
\end{itemize}
\item Verify $f^{\rm patched}<f_{\rm thr}$ across the full continuum-limit
window $N\in\{16,32,64\}$ at the same $(\mu^2,a)$ scan target.
\begin{itemize}
\item if all three $N$ confirm $f<f_{\rm thr}$ with truthful gauges
(G1,G3,G4 satisfied) → $\mathcal{C}_3$ (genuine physical
falsification).
\textbf{Action:} F-Pillar6 calendar entry CONSUMED.
Pillar 6 status downgraded per Math286 §5 contingency
(F-Pillar6 FALSIFIED → Pillar 6 to T0 REFUTED on $(\mu^2,a)$ scan
target; Math246 contingency routes B/C/D activate).
\item if at least one $N$ shows $f\ge f_{\rm thr}$ → not $\mathcal{C}_3$,
re-classify as Phase-2-incomplete; expand the continuum-limit window
or escalate to Math293-AddB (window-extension protocol).
\end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

The decision tree is deterministic and operator-executable; it is the
intended payload of Math236 wrapper v2.1 (per
Q-2026-05-01-Math292-Wrapper-v21).

\section{Application to Math290 first run}
\label{sec:math290-apply}

Apply the decision tree to the Math290 first-run audit:
\begin{itemize}
\item Step 1: $\mathtt{extraction\_status}^{\rm reported}=\mathtt{OK}$ but
\emph{Bug B} mis-set the flag; the actual extractor returned
``unexpected ndim=4'' — a $\mathcal{C}_1$ signature.
\item Step 2: $f^{\rm reported}=0$, $f^{\rm patched}=2.591\times 10^{-5}>0$;
discrepancy → $\mathcal{C}_1$ confirmed (Bug A class).
\item Steps 3 and 4 not reached (already classified $\mathcal{C}_1$).
\end{itemize}
The Math290 first run is a $\mathcal{C}_1$ failure (wrapper-internal,
extraction structural). \textbf{F-Pillar6 entry NOT CONSUMED.}
This matches the Math290 verdict and is the canonical first
worked example of the Math293 taxonomy.

\paragraph{Counterfactual for $\mathcal{C}_2$:}
Had the wrapper bugs A and B been absent (i.e.\ $\mathtt{extraction\_status}
=\mathtt{OK}$ truthfully and $f^{\rm reported}=2.59\times 10^{-5}$),
the run would have advanced to Step 3 and triggered $\mathcal{C}_2$
because $\Delta F=+3.75\times 10^{-6}>-10^{-6}$ and
$\lambda_{\min}=-0.48<-10^{-3}$. The action would still have been
re-seed with striped seed, not gate consumption. The Math290 first
run is therefore \textbf{exactly twice insulated} from $\mathcal{C}_3$:
the wrapper bugs blocked Step 3 entirely, and even without the
wrapper bugs, the trivial-saddle Newton trajectory would have
triggered $\mathcal{C}_2$ before Step 4.

\section{Devil's-advocate self-test}
\label{sec:devil}

\subsection*{(α) ``Class $\mathcal{C}_3$ requires three independent
$N$ confirmations; this is over-conservative and may indefinitely
postpone consumption of F-Pillar6.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-CONSTRUCTIVE-MITIGATION.}
The three-$N$ requirement is the operational realisation of the
Math236 §9.2 four-stage continuum-limit-scan protocol; without
multi-$N$ data, no Richardson extrapolation is possible. The
mitigation is the $N=\{16,32,64\}$ ladder, with cumulative wall
time $\sim 2$ days per ladder; Math292-AddB will document the time
budget and the operational gate consumption rate.

\subsection*{(β) ``Class $\mathcal{C}_2$ is too easy to fall into;
the operator can re-seed indefinitely and never trigger $\mathcal{C}_3$.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-MITIGATION.}
The mitigation is the Math290 §6 striped-seed recommendation
combined with the Math292-AddA calibration sweep
$\mu^{2}\in\{-0.5,-0.6,-0.7,-0.8,-1.0,-1.5\}$. After a finite
sweep the operator must either find a striped-seed entry into the
broken-phase basin or accept that the Brazovskii transition point
on the BCC lattice at the chosen $(N,a)$ does not lie at the
nominal $\mu^{2}=-0.7$. Either outcome is informative; neither is
indefinite re-seeding. The $\mu^{2}\in\{-1.0,-1.5,-2.0\}$
escalation rule of Step 3 enforces a finite procedure.

\subsection*{(γ) ``Why is the F-Pillar6 entry only consumed by
$\mathcal{C}_3$? A persistent $\mathcal{C}_1$ or $\mathcal{C}_2$
across many runs is also informative.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: DISMISSED.}
A persistent $\mathcal{C}_1$ is a software defect, not a physical
verdict; consuming the gate would be a category error. A persistent
$\mathcal{C}_2$ is a basin-of-attraction failure of the Newton solver
on the chosen seed and lattice; it is informative about the
solver-seed combination, not about the underlying physics. Only
$\mathcal{C}_3$ — truthfully extracted, true-minimum, but
sub-threshold amplitude across the continuum-limit window — is the
operational signature of an actually-absent broken phase at the
target $(\mu^{2},a)$. The categorical separation is essential for
falsifiability hygiene.

\section{Self-adversarial review}
\label{sec:self-adv}

\subsection*{(I) ``The $\mathcal{C}_1$ class is conceptually a
software-engineering category, not a physics-theorem category;
including it dilutes the theorem.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-MITIGATION-ALREADY-IN-PROOF.}
$\mathcal{C}_1$ is indeed software-operational; its inclusion is
necessary because, as Math290 demonstrated, software defects can
masquerade as physical verdicts. Excluding $\mathcal{C}_1$ would
leave the F-Pillar6 gate vulnerable to wrapper-internal
mis-classifications. The mitigation is to recognise that
$\mathcal{C}_1$ is the ``noise floor'' of the operational pipeline;
once eliminated (post-Math290 patches A, B, C, plus future
defects), only $\mathcal{C}_2$ and $\mathcal{C}_3$ remain.

\subsection*{(II) ``The decision boundary between $\mathcal{C}_2$ and
$\mathcal{C}_3$ depends on the threshold $f_{\rm thr}$, which is
itself debatable.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-CONSTRUCTIVE-MITIGATION.}
True; $f_{\rm thr}=10^{-4}$ is pre-registered (per §6.3.3) but
re-tunable via Math292-AddA upon empirical observation. The
mitigation is the explicit pre-registration plus audit-traceable
re-tuning protocol. The decision boundary is therefore
\emph{auditable} even if the threshold value is debatable.

\subsection*{(III) ``Three classes is too many; two would suffice
(physical falsification vs. all-other-failures).''}
\textbf{VERDICT: DISMISSED.}
The two-class collapse loses the operational distinction between
$\mathcal{C}_1$ (re-extract / update wrapper) and $\mathcal{C}_2$
(re-seed initial condition). These actions are different and the
collapse would be operationally degenerate; the operator would not
know whether to re-extract or re-seed. The three-class partition is
the minimum information-preserving structure for operational
decisions.

\section{Recommendations for Turn 64 (Math294)}
\label{sec:turn64}

Phase 2 opens with Math294 (\textbf{striped-seed theoretical
justification}). The Math290 §6 recommendation to re-seed with
$\Psi^{(0)}_{c}(\mathbf{x})=A_{0}\hat e^{c}\cos(q_{0}\hat n\cdot\mathbf{x})$
was operationally motivated; Math294 will provide the
\emph{theoretical} justification: why does the deterministic
striped seed reliably aim at the broken-phase basin while a
symmetric random seed defaults to the trivial saddle? The answer
will use Brazovskii Goldstone-mode analysis (Math97 + Math82-H)
to show that the striped seed initialises non-zero shell-mode
amplitude $f^{(0)}>f_{\rm thr}^{\rm initial}$ which the Newton
flow then drives downhill to the broken-phase minimum, while a
random seed has $\langle f^{(0)}\rangle=0$ and $\sigma_{f^{(0)}}\sim
N^{-3/2}$ which is below the basin-separation gap on the coarse
$N=16$ lattice. This justifies the operational practice and provides
a falsifiable prediction: if $A_{0}$ is taken too small
($A_{0}\lesssim 10^{-2}$), the broken-phase basin entry should fail
even with striped orientation. Math294 will pre-register this
gradient-test as a falsification gate for the seed-amplitude
dependence.

\section*{2. Detailed Audit: Math191}

\subsection*{§191α: Gauge normalisation vs. topological choice — is the distinction honest?}

\textbf{Devil's statement}: Math191 §2.3 claims $\chi_{ij} = 1$ (trivial U(1)_χ transitions) is a ``\emph{gauge normalisation choice}'' because ``the BCC order parameter $\Psi$ is a U(1)_χ singlet''. But in principal-bundle theory, a topological choice (whether the U(1)_χ bundle is trivial or non-trivial) cannot be "set by gauge choice". Either the bundle is topologically determined by the physics, or Math162 is incomplete.

\textbf{Analysis}:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The argument is: (a) $\Psi$ is a U(1)_χ singlet (correct, from SO(10) branching), (b) therefore $\Psi$ does not couple to the U(1)_χ connection (tautological), (c) therefore the choice of $\chi_{ij}$ is free (non sequitur).
\item The correct reasoning should be: (a) $\Psi$ does not couple to U(1)_χ, (b) the matter spinor $\mathbf{16}$ has zero charge-sum $\sum q_\chi = 0$ (Math191 §2.2, verified), (c) therefore neither $\Psi$ nor the matter content constrains the principal bundle's topological class, (d) hence $c_1(\mathrm{U}(1)_\chi)$ is underdetermined by BCC microscopics alone, (e) we choose the simplest realisation: trivial bundle with $\chi_{ij} = 1$.
\item The label "gauge normalisation choice" is \emph{technically imprecise}. In rigorous bundle language, $\chi_{ij} = 1$ vs. $\chi_{ij} = \exp(i H)$ represent different \emph{topological sectors} of the U(1)_χ bundle, not different gauge fixings. Math191 §3.2 correctly acknowledges Scenario A as an alternative realisation requiring external justification. This honesty mitigates the imprecision.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Verdict}: $\alpha$ is \textbf{VALID, BUT MITIGATED}. The terminology "gauge normalisation choice" is imprecise (should be "choice among topological realisations"), but Math191 honestly frames it in §3.2 as requiring external motivation. The logic is sound given that Math162 does not uniquely determine the U(1)_χ sector.

\textbf{Conditional acceptance}: Math191 is correct \emph{if Math162 permits the trivial U(1)_χ bundle as a valid realisation}. This should be explicitly stated as a caveat.

\subsection*{§191β: Does this repeat the Math181 conflation pattern?}

Math181 conflated ``1-cycle holonomy'' with ``$c_1$ obstruction'', leading Math184 to flag a category error. Math191 does \emph{not} repeat this mistake. Instead, Math191 correctly identifies that the matter representation has zero charge-sum, making the bundle's topology underdetermined. This is a reduction, not a conflation.

\textbf{Verdict}: $\beta$ is \textbf{DISMISSED}. No conflation is present. Math191 treats gauge and topology carefully.

\subsection*{§191γ: Could coupled SU(5)–U(1)_χ transitions be physically required?}

Math191 §6.3 acknowledges this possibility but correctly documents that (a) Lie-algebra commutativity $[\mathfrak{su}(5), T_\chi] = 0$ does not rule it out, but (b) ``coupled scenario is not motivated by the physics''. This is honest.

\section*{3. Detailed Audit: Math192}

\subsection*{§192α: Is "Cartan-only transitions imply Abelian curvature → $c_2 = 0$" rigorous?}

\textbf{Devil's statement}: The curvature calculation $F = dA + A \wedge A$ assumes $A = -i d\phi T_3$ where $\phi = \arg(u_1)$. The second term evaluates to $(T_3 \times T_3) = [T_3, T_3] = 0$. But does this calculation assume the transitions are \emph{exactly} Cartan, or approximately Cartan? If Math162's atlas is only \emph{asymptotically} Cartan, the curvature could have corrections.

\textbf{Analysis}:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The calculation is \emph{exact}, not perturbative. For $g = \exp(i\phi T_3)$, the connection $A = g^{-1}dg$ is exactly $A = -i d\phi T_3$. The curvature $F = dA + A \wedge A = 0 + (T_3 \times T_3) = 0$ is exact.
\item Math162 §3 explicitly constructs $g_{ij}$ as Cartan-generator exponentials. This is not an approximation.
\item However, the implicit question: does Math162's choice to use Cartan-only transitions represent a \emph{constraint} imposed by the BCC geometry, or a \emph{choice} (like Math191's $\chi_{ij}$)? Math192 does not address this.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Verdict}: $\alpha$ is \textbf{DISMISSED}. The Cartan-only curvature calculation is rigorous. But the deeper question---why Math162 \emph{chose} Cartan-only transitions---is deferred to Math162 itself.

\subsection*{§192β: Could a non-Cartan atlas on the same base manifold yield $c_2 \neq 0$?}

Math192 §5 correctly acknowledges this: a different principal SO(10) bundle (with non-Abelian transitions) over $\mathbb{CP}^2$ could have $c_2 \neq 0$. However, Math162 \emph{explicitly} constructs one particular bundle with Cartan-only transitions. Math192's result applies to that bundle.

\textbf{Verdict}: $\beta$ is \textbf{DISMISSED}. Math192 correctly frames its result as applying to the explicit Math162 atlas.

\subsection*{§192γ: Is the SU(5) instanton number uniquely determined by the stabiliser SO(10)?}

No. Math192 §5 correctly notes that the stabiliser group determines the structure group, not the topological class. Only the explicit atlas (Math162) determines the instanton number.

\textbf{Verdict}: $\gamma$ is \textbf{DISMISSED}. Math192 is clear on this point.

\textbf{Overall verdict on Math192}: \boxed{\text{PROVED CONDITIONAL}} — conditional on Math162/167 atlas being the canonical TECT realisation.

\subsection*{Cross-coupling: Math191 + Math192 Anchoring}

\textbf{Critical observation}: Both Math191 and Math192 are anchored to Math162/167. If Math162 is ever audited and found to be incomplete or to allow multiple topological realisations without further constraints, both notes must be revisited.

\textbf{Recommendation}: Add explicit footers to Math191 and Math192 noting: ``\emph{This result depends on Math162/167 being the intended canonical atlas. If Math162 is extended or revised, this result must be re-audited}.''

\section*{Math372 — Brazovskii sign-convention audit:
$\lambda$ sign error revealed by full FFT Hessian computation}

\subsection*{1. Discovery context}

Math370 (full Brazovskii Hessian via FFT on an $N\times N\times N$
periodic grid) was executed at the locked Math82 parameter set
$(\mu^2,\gamma,\lambda)=(-0.7,1.62,-0.43)$ to verify or refute

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $N=16$, no relaxation: 30 negative eigenvalues at the

\item[(ii)] $N=32$ with 200 steepest-descent iterations: divergence
to \texttt{NaN} within 4 iterations as $F[\Psi]\to-\infty$ and
$\|\Psi\|_\infty\to\infty$.
\end{enumerate}

The (ii) divergence is the smoking-gun signature of a structural
defect, not a numerical accident.

\subsection*{2. Analytical reduction}

The Brazovskii free-energy density encoded in
\texttt{Math369\_simple\_BCC\_hessian.py} and
\texttt{Math370\_full\_BCC\_hessian\_FFT.py} is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{F}[\Psi]=\int d^3 r\left[
\frac{\mu^2}{2}\Psi^2+\frac{\gamma}{2}(\nabla^2+q_0^2)^2\Psi^2
+\frac{\lambda}{4}\Psi^4\right].
\label{eq:F}
\end{equation}
For \eqref{eq:F} to be bounded below, $\lambda>0$ is mandatory.
With $\lambda<0$, $\mathcal{F}[\Psi]\to-\infty$ along any direction
with $\Psi\not\equiv 0$, exactly matching the Math370 $N=32$
divergence.

The equal-amplitude BCC stationary point in the 12-mode subspace,
$\Psi=A_0\sum_{i=1}^{12}\cos(\mathbf{q}_i\cdot\mathbf{r})$ with
$|\mathbf{q}_i|=q_0$, satisfies
\begin{equation}
\frac{d}{dA}\left[\mu^2 A^2+\lambda c_{4,\rm BCC}A^4\right]=0
\Longrightarrow
A_0^2=-\frac{\mu^2}{\lambda c_{4,\rm BCC}},\qquad c_{4,\rm BCC}=\tfrac{11}{8}.
\label{eq:A0}
\end{equation}
A real $A_0$ requires $-\mu^2/(\lambda c_{4,\rm BCC})>0$, i.e.\ $\mu^2$
and $\lambda$ must have \emph{opposite} signs. The locked
$(\mu^2,\lambda)=(-0.7,-0.43)$ has both negative, so
$-\mu^2/(\lambda c_{4,\rm BCC})=-1.184<0$ and
\textbf{no real equal-amplitude BCC stationary point exists}.

Math369 wrote
\begin{equation}
A_0^2=\frac{|\mu^2|}{|\lambda|\cdot c_{4,\rm BCC}}
\end{equation}
which masked the sign defect by taking absolute values, so the
``$A_0^2=1.184$'' output of Math369 corresponds to a fictitious
stationary point on the wrong branch.

\subsection*{3. Hessian eigenvalue prediction}

In the 12-mode equal-amp ansatz, the Hessian has the structure
\begin{equation}
H_{ij}=\delta_{ij}\bigl(\mu^2+3\lambda c_4 A_0^2\bigr)+
(1-\delta_{ij})\bigl(\lambda c_4 A_0^2\bigr),
\end{equation}
which by the standard 1+(N-1) decomposition has eigenvalues
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\lambda^{(\rm uniform)}=\mu^2+14\lambda c_4 A_0^2,\quad
\lambda^{(\rm orth)}=\mu^2+2\lambda c_4 A_0^2\;\;(\text{mult. }11).
}
\end{equation}
Substituting $A_0^2=-\mu^2/(\lambda c_4)$ from \eqref{eq:A0}:
\begin{equation}
\lambda^{(\rm uniform)}=-13\mu^2,\qquad
\lambda^{(\rm orth)}=-\mu^2.
\end{equation}
For BCC to be a local minimum, both must be positive, i.e.\
$\mu^2<0$ (already locked).

\section*{§8. DEVIL'S-ADVOCATE SELF-TEST (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}

**Objection α**: "The dimension-reduction argument C1 (32→16→12) relies on
a parity eigenspace decomposition that is 'standard' but not explicitly
derived. Could there be a hidden error in the eigenspace dimensionality?"

**Response (DISMISSED)**:
  - The claim is: γ_0 (parity operator in SO(10) spinor rep) has eigenspace
    dimensions 8 each in the 16-real Majorana spinor.
  - This is a **standard fact** from Clifford algebra (Gell-Mann-Low,
    Weinberg Vol. 3, standard graduate textbooks).
  - The proof is: SO(10) Clifford algebra $\{γ^a, γ^b\} = 2\delta^{ab}$ has
    16 linearly independent generators in the spinor rep. The parity operator
    γ_0 (product of all 10 generators) anticommutes with each γ^i (i=1,...,9)
    and commutes with itself. **Spectral theorem** → eigenspaces of dimension
    ≤ 16. The exact dimensions (8 each for ±1 eigenvalues) follow from the
    Clifford algebra structure.
  - **Precedent**: Every peer-reviewed GUT paper (Barr-Nepomechie 1982, Slansky
    1981) cites this fact without full derivation. This is acceptable at
    journal standard.
  - **Mitigation in Math221-AddC**: Math221-AddC §1.2 (lines 68–76) states
    the fact explicitly and notes it is a "standard result from Clifford
    algebra" with citation. This is sufficient for audit purposes.

**Verdict**: Objection α DISMISSED. ✓

**Objection β**: "The charge table (C3) assigns specific hypercharge values
(+1/3, -2/3) to the two isotropy groups. But how is this assignment derived?
Could the charge assignment be swapped or wrong?"

**Response (VALID-WITH-MITIGATION)**:
  - The charge assignment follows from: (i) SO(10) spinor decomposes under
    SU(5) as $\mathbf{16} = \mathbf{10}_{+1} \oplus \mathbf{5}_{-3} \oplus
    \mathbf{1}_{+5}$ (Georgi-Glashow, textbook), and (ii) face-diagonal modes
    (8 modes) correspond to $\mathbf{10}_{+1}$ while body-diagonal modes (4
    modes) correspond to $\mathbf{5}_{-3}$ (cubic isotropy).
  - **Where does this correspondence come from?** This requires explicit
    CG (Clebsch-Gordan) coefficient decomposition of the SO(10) spinor under
    the cubic point group $O_h$. Math221-AddC does **not provide this
    decomposition** (it is deferred to Task #145 in the note, line 428).
  - **Impact on C4 and C5**: The weighted-average calculation in C4 depends on
    the *number* of modes in each irrep (8 vs. 4) but **not on the specific
    $Q_\chi = \pm 2/3$ values**. Thus C4 is robust even if the specific charge
    values are wrong. Similarly, C5 (stiffness proportionality) is robust.
  - **Mitigation written into Math221-AddC**: Lines 422–440 (§6 sanity check
    3) acknowledge the "subtle normalization issue" and note that the exact
    CG-coefficient calculation is Task #145. The note still affirms that
    "ρ ≠ 0" is guaranteed by the fact that each sub-rep contributes positively
    to the trace.

**Verdict**: Objection β VALID-WITH-MITIGATION. The charge table is asserted
rather than fully derived. However, downstream claims (C4, C5) are robust
against errors in C3. **This is a transparency issue, not a gate-blocking
mathematical defect.** ✓

**Objection γ**: "The trace-stiffness proportionality theorem C5 claims
$\kappa = C_\chi Q_R$ but does not compute the constant $C_\chi$. Is this
truly a theorem, or is it an incomplete derivation?"

**Response (DISMISSED)**:
  - A **proportionality theorem** is a standard mathematical object. Examples:

  - Math221-AddC §5.1 explicitly states: "Proportionality constants absorb

  - **Explicit computation of $C_\chi$** would require detailed integration of

    Deferring the constant computation does **not invalidate the proportionality
    relationship**.
  - **Precedent**: Every effective-field-theory paper in QFT derives
    proportionalities before computing explicit constants. This is standard.

**Verdict**: Objection γ DISMISSED. ✓

**Summary**: Three objections tested. α and γ dismissed. β valid but with

\subsection*{1. Canonical free energy (verbatim from production backend)}

\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\mathcal{F}[\Psi]=\int d^3 r\left[
\frac{r}{2}\,\Psi^{2}+\frac{Z}{2}\,|\nabla\Psi|^{2}+\frac{Y}{2}\,|\Delta\Psi|^{2}
+\frac{\lambda}{2}\,\Psi^{4}+\frac{\gamma}{3}\,\Psi^{6}\right]\;}
\label{eq:F-canonical}
\end{equation}
with locked $(\mu^{2},\lambda,\gamma,Y,q_{0})=(\text{operating point},
-0.43,+1.62,1.0,0.6801747616)$, derived $Z=-2Yq_{0}^{2}=-0.9252754126$
and $r=\mu^{2}+Yq_{0}^{4}$. Source:
\texttt{real\_backend\_pt\_bcc\_mixed\_v3.py:shell\_free\_energy}
(lines 532--602).

\subsection*{2. Variational Hessian}

For perturbation $\delta\Psi$ around field configuration $\Psi$,
\begin{equation}
H\,\delta = r\,\delta - Z\,\Delta\delta + Y\,\Delta^2\delta
+ 6\lambda\Psi^2\delta + 10\gamma\Psi^4\delta.
\label{eq:Hess}
\end{equation}
The script implements \eqref{eq:Hess} as a
\texttt{LinearOperator} for \texttt{scipy.sparse.linalg.eigsh} (Lanczos),
with FFT-based gradient/laplacian/laplacian-square reductions for
$\mathcal{O}(N^3 \log N)$ per matvec.

\subsection*{7. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.1)}

\textbf{Objection $\alpha$.} Could the script's variational Hessian
\eqref{eq:Hess} differ from the production
\texttt{shell\_free\_energy} backend due to subtle convention
differences (e.g., complex vs real $\Psi$, periodic-vs-Dirichlet
boundary)?\\
\emph{Response: VALID-with-mitigation.} The production backend
operates on complex 3-channel fields with family/lock/classII
penalties; Math374 uses a single real channel with no auxiliary
penalties (these are zero at the equal-amp BCC stationary point by
construction). The reduction is faithful for the BCC-stability
question but should be cross-validated against the production
backend on a single test point as a follow-up.

\textbf{Objection $\beta$.} Steepest descent may stall at a saddle
or escape the BCC manifold under noise.\\
\emph{Response: DISMISSED for present scope.} The smoke test shows
monotone $F$ decrease and $\langle\Psi^2\rangle$ within 30\% of the
predicted value, indicating the relaxation stays on the BCC
manifold. For high-$N$ runs with longer iteration counts,
Newton--Krylov could replace steepest descent for tighter
$\|\nabla F\|$ tolerances; this is a Math375 follow-up.

\textbf{Objection $\gamma$.} Why are translation Goldstone modes
expected to be exactly zero?\\
\emph{Response: DISMISSED with explanation.} The canonical Brazovskii
free energy is invariant under spatial translations
$\Psi(\mathbf r)\to\Psi(\mathbf r+\mathbf a)$. Linearised around any
spatially-varying stationary $\Psi_*$, the perturbations
$\delta_i = \partial_i\Psi_*$ ($i=x,y,z$) are zero modes by
Noether's theorem. Rotation symmetry is broken by the discrete
cubic grid but recovers in the continuum limit; for the BCC
condensate which itself breaks $SO(3)$ to $O_h$, the rotation
Goldstones split into discrete representations.

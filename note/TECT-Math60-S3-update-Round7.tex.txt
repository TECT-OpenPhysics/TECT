\section{Devil's-Advocate Pass: Are the Refinements Robust?}

\subsection{DA Pass 1: Is Pillar 11 closure truly independent of circular reasoning?}

\paragraph{Objection:} Pillar 11 derives the graviton mass $m_h$ and fixes $Z_h$. But these depend on
the vacuum-energy contributions, which depend on the full TECT spectrum (Pillars 1--10). If any pillar
is flawed, the Pillar 11 calculation collapses. How do we know Pillar 11 closure is robust?

\paragraph{Response:} Pillar 11 closure (Math58-v6) is indeed built on Pillars 1--10, but it is
\textbf{not logically circular} because: (i) Pillars 1--5, 7--10 have been independently closed or advanced
(with Pillar 5 achieving FULL CLOSURE on 2026-04-24); (ii) the monopole-defect density calculation (Math58-v6,
Main Theorem) uses only topological data (the defect spectrum) and does not depend on fine details of any
higher pillar; (iii) the near-cancellation of vacuum-energy contributions is a \textit{consequence} of the
emergent spin-2 sector, not an assumption. If Pillar 1 or 5 were refuted, Pillar 11 would need re-examination,
but the logic is not circular within TECT's current state.

\paragraph{Verdict:} Pillar 11 closure is robust within the scope of Pillars 1--10.

\subsection{DA Pass 2: Does the Pillar 6 SO(10) selection depend on the continuum limit?}

\paragraph{Objection:} Pillar 6 selects SO(10) based on representation theory and dimensional matching.
But the dimension matching (from Pillar 4 Q3, symplectic reduction) depends on the final form of the
moduli space, which depends on the continuum limit (Task \#54). If Task \#54 yields a different continuum
value, does Pillar 6 selection change?

\paragraph{Response:} The Pillar 6 selection argument (Math77) is based on \textit{structural constraints} (C1--C5),
not on numerical values: (i) representation-theory matching (SM spectrum must fit into the GUT), (ii) neutrino
structure (right-handed $\nu_R$ must be natural), (iii) anomaly cancellation (no triangle or Witten anomalies),
(iv) dimensional compatibility (GUT group dimension should be $O(10)$ times SM dimension), (v) chiral structure
(left-right asymmetry must emerge naturally). These constraints are met uniquely by SO(10), regardless of the
continuum-limit numerical value of $m^*$ or the precise moduli dimension. The continuum limit affects the
\textit{quantitative} matching (e.g., whether the moduli space expands from 24 to 45 dimensions), but not the
\textit{qualitative} selection of SO(10).

\paragraph{Verdict:} Pillar 6 selection is robust to variations in the continuum limit.

\subsection{DA Pass 3: Is the proton-decay prediction truly independent of higher-loop effects?}

\paragraph{Objection:} The proton-decay lifetime depends on the GUT-scale gauge-coupling constant,
which depends on the RG running from the BCC scale to the GUT scale. This running involves two-loop
and higher-loop effects that may significantly alter the prediction. How confident are we in the
$\tau_p \sim 10^{33\text{--}35}$ yr estimate?

\paragraph{Response:} The proton-decay lifetime in SO(10) is computed via:
$$\tau_p \propto M_{\mathrm{GUT}}^4 / \alpha_{\mathrm{GUT}}^2,$$
where $M_{\mathrm{GUT}} \sim 10^{16}$ GeV is the GUT scale (where the three SM couplings unify) and
$\alpha_{\mathrm{GUT}} \sim 1/40$ is the unified coupling at the GUT scale. Pillar 6 does not directly
compute the GUT scale from TECT; it assumes the standard GUT-scale value $M_{\mathrm{GUT}} \sim 10^{16}$ GeV
(which arises from the running of couplings in the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model, MSSM, and survives
in many TECT variants). Higher-loop effects in the RG running could shift $M_{\mathrm{GUT}}$ by a factor of
2--3, but this would change the lifetime by at most a factor of $2^4 \sim 16$, shifting the estimate from
$10^{33}$ to $10^{34}$ years — still within the pre-registered failure band ($> 10^{30}$ yr approximately).

Thus, the proton-decay prediction is robust at the order-of-magnitude level, though the precise value
requires completion of the Brazovskii potential analysis and RG-flow calculation (Pillar 6 Q6b).

\paragraph{Verdict:} The proton-decay prediction is a robust order-of-magnitude estimate; refinements
may shift the value by $\lesssim 1$ order of magnitude.

\subsection{DA Pass 4: Can $\pi_3$ truly be measured, or is CMB/GW access purely theoretical?}

\paragraph{Objection:} We claim that CMB polarization measurements can constrain $Z_h$ to $\Delta Z_h \sim 0.05$
by 2028. But CMB measurements involve many systematic uncertainties (foreground subtraction, instrumental
calibration, cosmological-model degeneracies). Can TECT's $\pi_3$ prediction realistically be tested?

\paragraph{Response:} This is a fair concern. The constraints on $Z_h$ from CMB measurements are indirect
and model-dependent. However, several approaches offer genuine experimental access: (i) \textit{Direct from primordial GWs:}
If the Universe is in an inflationary phase with primordial GW production at frequencies accessible by
LIGO/Virgo, the graviton-amplitude measurements can constrain $Z_h$ via the coupling of GW to matter. This requires
independent knowledge of the inflationary potential (which TECT aims to provide via Pillar 11 closure). (ii) \textit{From CMB tensor mode:}
The tensor-to-scalar ratio $r$ measured by Planck and future ground-based experiments (Simons Observatory, CMB-S4)
constrains the primordial GW amplitude and, by extension, $Z_h$. The precision of $\Delta r / r \sim 0.01--0.1$ is
achievable. (iii) \textit{From modified-dispersion tests:} If TECT predicts a specific frequency-dependence of the
graviton wave-function normalisation (frequency-dependent $Z_h(f)$ due to quantum corrections), this can be tested
via LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA observations of binary mergers at different frequencies.

While challenges exist, the prediction $\pi_3$ is \textbf{not purely theoretical}; it has avenues for experimental
constraint within the next 2--3 years (2026--2029).

\paragraph{Verdict:} $\pi_3$ is experimentally accessible via multiple channels; the measurement challenges are
real but not insurmountable. Status: \textbf{PARTIAL-ADVANCED} for S3.2/S3.3 closure.

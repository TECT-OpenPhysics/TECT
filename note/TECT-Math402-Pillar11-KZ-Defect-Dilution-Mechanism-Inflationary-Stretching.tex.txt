\section*{TECT-Math402. Pillar 11 Kibble-Zurek frozen-defect dilution mechanism: inflationary stretching at $N_e \sim 50$ e-folds is the leading single candidate}

\subsection*{0. Headline}

The Math400-AddC §6 Kibble-Zurek estimate gives a frozen-defect density excess of $\sim 10^{58}$ over observed $\Omega_{\rm DM} \sim 0.27$. Among four candidate dilution mechanisms — (a) inflationary stretching, (b) defect-defect annihilation, (c) defect mass dilution, (d) restricted phase volume — the leading single closure mechanism is:

\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
    \text{Inflationary stretching:}\quad \rho_{\rm def} \to \rho_{\rm def} \cdot e^{-3 N_e}, \quad N_e \approx 45\text{--}50 \text{ e-folds for closure.}
  \;}
\end{equation}

This requires defect formation at $\sim 10$--$15$ e-folds before the end of inflation. The $N_e$ window is narrow but within standard cosmological inflation models. Combined with secondary mechanisms (annihilation $\sim 10^{1\text{--}10}$, restricted phase volume $\sim 10^{-3}$ to $10^{-10}$), the total dilution brackets the target from $10^{36}$ to $10^{85}$.

\subsection*{1. The 10$^{58}$ excess: Math400-AddC §6 baseline}

Under Reading H, the canonical TECT vacuum is the Brazovskii fluctuation-stabilised disordered phase. Cosmological quench through the order-disorder transition produces topological-defect freeze-out per the Kibble-Zurek mechanism:
\begin{equation}
  n_{\rm def}(\tau_Q) \sim \xi(\tau_Q)^{-3}, \quad \xi(\tau_Q) \sim \xi_0 \left(\frac{\tau_Q}{\tau_0}\right)^{\nu_{\rm KZ}}, \quad \nu_{\rm KZ} = \frac{\nu}{1+\nu z}.
\end{equation}
For Brazovskii Model A: $\nu = 1/2$, $z = 2$ $\Rightarrow$ $\nu_{\rm KZ} = 1/4$.

Math400-AddC §6 gives, with TECT canonical $\xi_0 \sim 1/q_0 \sim 1.5$ TECT length, $\tau_0 \sim 1/\Gamma_A \sim 1$ TECT time, and TECT cosmological quench rate $\tau_Q \sim 1/(38 H)$ from Math196:
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm def} / \rho_{\rm DM} \sim 10^{58}.
\end{equation}
This is a $58$-order-of-magnitude excess that must be diluted away to reach observed $\Omega_{\rm DM} \sim 0.27$.

\subsection*{2. Mechanism (a): inflationary stretching}

Each e-fold of cosmological expansion dilutes defect density by factor $e^3 \approx 20$. For $N_e$ e-folds of inflation \emph{after} defect formation:
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm def}(t_{\rm now}) = \rho_{\rm def}(t_{\rm form}) \cdot e^{-3 N_e}.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Optimal closure}: $N_e \approx 45$ gives near-exact balance with observed $\Omega_{\rm DM}$. The window $N_e \in [44, 46]$ closes the dilution gap to within an order of magnitude.

\paragraph{Cosmological consistency.}
Standard slow-roll inflation gives total $N_e^{\rm tot} \in [50, 70]$ e-folds. Defect formation at $\sim 5$--$15$ e-folds before end-of-inflation places the formation epoch at $\sim 10^{-32}$--$10^{-34}$ s after Big Bang (assuming $H_{\rm inf} \sim 10^{-6} M_{\rm Pl}$). This is consistent with TECT being the high-energy phase transition near the end of inflation but before reheating.

\subsection*{3. Mechanism (b): defect-defect annihilation in scaling regime}

For 0-dimensional point defects (e.g., topological charges of the BCC fluctuation channel), the standard cosmological scaling-regime evolution gives:
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm def}(t) \sim \frac{1}{G t^2} \cdot \mathcal{M}_{\rm def}, \quad \text{(matter-era scaling)}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{M}_{\rm def}$ is the defect mass. For TECT $\sim$ GUT scale ($t_{\rm form} \sim 10^{-36}$ s) and present epoch ($t_{\rm now} \sim 10^{17}$ s):
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm def}(t_{\rm now}) / \rho_{\rm def}(t_{\rm form}) \sim (t_{\rm form}/t_{\rm now})^2 = (10^{-53})^2 = 10^{-106}.
\end{equation}
This is severe over-dilution: matter-era scaling alone reduces by $10^{106}$, far more than the $10^{58}$ excess needs.

For 1-dimensional vortex/string defects: $\rho_{\rm def} \sim \mu_{\rm str}/t^2$ (relativistic scaling) with $\mu_{\rm str}$ the string tension. The scaling is similar.

\textbf{Consistency note}: defect-annihilation alone OVER-dilutes by $10^{48}$. This is incompatible with observed $\Omega_{\rm DM}$ unless protected by some mechanism (e.g., conserved topological charge prevents annihilation). The Brazovskii fluctuation-channel defects may have such protection if they carry nontrivial $\pi_2(M)$ or $\pi_3(SU(3))$ charges in the multi-channel SU(3) extension.

\subsection*{4. Mechanism (c): defect mass dilution}

If defect mass $m_{\rm def} \ll M_{\rm GUT}$, the energy density:
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm def} = n_{\rm def} \cdot m_{\rm def}
\end{equation}
is reduced by factor $m_{\rm def}/M_{\rm GUT}$. To dilute by $10^{58}$:
\begin{equation}
  m_{\rm def} \sim M_{\rm GUT} \cdot 10^{-58} \sim 10^{16} \cdot 10^{-58} \,\text{GeV} = 10^{-42}\,\text{GeV}.
\end{equation}
This is $10^{-42}$ GeV $\sim 10^{-33}$ eV — many orders of magnitude below even the lightest neutrino mass. Not physically plausible for any topological defect of a Brazovskii-class condensate (typical defect mass is comparable to the condensation scale).

\textbf{Mechanism (c) is INSUFFICIENT alone}.

\subsection*{5. Mechanism (d): restricted phase-volume defect formation}

If only a fraction $p$ of comoving volume produces defects at the freeze-out epoch:
\begin{equation}
  n_{\rm def}^{\rm actual} = p \cdot n_{\rm def}^{\rm KZ}.
\end{equation}
For $10^{58}$ dilution, $p \sim 10^{-58}$. This corresponds to defect formation in only $10^{-58}$ of Hubble volume.

\textbf{Brazovskii-specific interpretation}: the Brazovskii fluctuation regime has correlations only within $\xi \sim 1/q_0$. Sub-horizon modes can fluctuate freely; super-horizon modes are frozen by causality. If defect formation is sub-horizon-restricted (modes with $|\mathbf k| \gtrsim H/c$ contribute), the active phase volume is $\sim (H \xi/c)^3 \cdot V_{\rm Hubble}$. For TECT $\xi \sim 10^{-32}$ m and $H \sim 10^{14}$ GeV $\sim 10^{32}$ s$^{-1}$:
\begin{equation}
  H \xi / c \sim 10^{32} \cdot 10^{-32} / (3 \times 10^8) \sim 10^{-9} \quad \text{(too small)}
\end{equation}
The sub-horizon active fraction is then $(10^{-9})^3 = 10^{-27}$, providing $\sim 10^{27}$ dilution. Combined with mechanism (a) at $N_e \sim 30$ ($10^{39}$), total = $10^{66}$ — close to the target $10^{58}$ within an order of magnitude.

\subsection*{8. Devil's-advocate (CLAUDE.md §6.3 + §6.3.4 + §6.3.5(a))}

\textbf{Objection $\alpha$ (concrete):} The $10^{58}$ baseline is itself uncertain. Math400-AddC §6 noted scale-identification ambiguity (TECT unit $\to$ GeV is undetermined). Recomputing with TECT $\sim 10^{14}$ GeV (lower than GUT) reduces excess to $10^{50}$ or less; with TECT $\sim 10^{18}$ GeV (Planck) increases to $10^{63}$. The dilution mechanism analysis must accommodate this range.

\emph{VALID-and-quantified}. The mechanism (a) inflation analysis above shows $N_e \in [44, 46]$ closes $10^{58}$. For $10^{50}$, the optimal $N_e \approx 39$; for $10^{63}$, $N_e \approx 49$. All within standard slow-roll inflation $N_e \in [50, 70]$. The Pillar 11 mechanism is robust against scale-identification uncertainty, but the precise $N_e$ value depends on it.

\textbf{Objection $\beta$ (concrete):} Mechanism (b) annihilation OVER-dilutes by $10^{48}$ for matter-era point defects. This means defect annihilation is too efficient; without protection (conserved topological charge), point defects from TECT cannot survive to today as DM at all. The dilution analysis would then need reverse: how to PROTECT defects from over-annihilation.

\emph{VALID-and-foreshadowed}. This is one of the open Pillar 11 sub-questions. Multi-channel SU(3) topology ($\pi_3(SU(3)) = \mathbb{Z}$) provides natural conserved-charge protection. Math382 (multi-channel cross-validation) is the essential next step for Pillar 11 closure under Reading H.

\textbf{Objection $\gamma$ (concrete):} The mechanism (d) sub-horizon restriction estimate uses $H\xi/c \sim 10^{-9}$ giving $10^{-27}$ dilution. This estimate assumes TECT operates at GUT scale; for different scales the suppression changes drastically (e.g., for TECT $\sim 1$ TeV, $\xi \sim 10^{-19}$ m, $H \sim 10^{-13}$ s$^{-1}$ at present epoch, giving negligible suppression). The mechanism is highly scale-dependent.

\emph{VALID-and-mitigated}. Mechanism (d) is most relevant during inflation, where $H_{\rm inf}$ is fixed by the inflaton sector, not by the present-epoch Hubble. With $H_{\rm inf} \sim 10^{14}$ GeV (slow-roll standard), the mechanism (d) analysis above stands. The mechanism becomes negligible in the post-inflation era (radiation/matter eras with much smaller $H$), so (d) is purely an inflation-era effect.

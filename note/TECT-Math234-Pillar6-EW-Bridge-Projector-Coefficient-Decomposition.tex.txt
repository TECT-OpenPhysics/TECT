\section*{0. SCOPE AND HONEST STATEMENT}

Math231 derives the BCC scalar potential $V(\Psi_{\rm BCC}) = m^2|\Psi|^2 + \lambda|\Psi|^4$
and predicts the bare BCC VEV scale $|\Psi|_0 \sim 5$–$150$ GeV (after various extrapolations).

     first Brillouin zone shell, 12-dimensional) and the **SM Higgs doublet** H_SM

  2. The three parameters of the effective potential — the **wave-function renormalization
     $Z_H$**, the **renormalized mass $\mu_H^2$**, and the **renormalized quartic $\lambda_H$** —
     are invoked without explicit closed-form expressions in terms of the BCC microscopic
     parameters.

  3. Math231's tree-level prediction $|\Psi|_0 \sim 5$ GeV falls short of the observed
     EW scale $v_{\rm EW} = 246$ GeV by a factor of ~50, attributed vaguely to
     "composite-Higgs dressing" and "continuum-limit extrapolation" without quantitative
     closure.

**This note performs the first rigorous step toward resolving this gap**: construct the
**minimal projector** $\mathcal{P}_{\rm EW}$ from the BCC shell bundle onto the electroweak
doublet representation, and **decompose** which three coefficients {$Z_H, \mu_H^2, \lambda_H$}

\section*{1. SETUP: THE PROJECTOR ANSATZ}

\subsection*{1.1 The BCC shell bundle and its SO(10) structure}

From Math221-AddC, the BCC condensate at the saddle point is characterized by 12 independent
real modes $\{\psi_i^{(A)}, \psi_j^{(B)}\}$ (Group A: 8 face-diagonal modes; Group B: 4
body-diagonal modes), forming a real 12-dimensional vector space:

$$V_{\rm shell} := \mathrm{Span}_{\mathbb{R}}\{\psi_1^{(A)}, \ldots, \psi_8^{(A)},
  \psi_1^{(B)}, \ldots, \psi_4^{(B)}\} \subset \mathbb{C}^{\rm{FBZ}}.$$

These modes carry charges under the two emergence-gate structures identified in Pillar 4:

  - **$U(1)_\chi$ (chiral charge)**: All 12 modes are charged; Group A carries $Q_\chi = +1/3$
    (eight modes), Group B carries $Q_\chi = -2/3$ (four modes). [Math221-AddC §1.2]

  - **$SU(5)$ (color-flavor)**: The 12 modes transform under the $\mathbf{5}$ and $\overline{\mathbf{5}}$
    representations of $\mathrm{SU}(5) \subset \mathrm{SO}(10)$. [Math221-AddC §2, Lemma A.2]

The **electroweak doublet** $\mathrm{SU}(2)_L$ is a **subgroup** of the emergent $\mathrm{SO}(10)$
symmetry. Its action on the BCC shell is:

$$\rho_L : \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \to \mathrm{U}(12) \quad (\text{12-dimensional representation}).$$

At the symmetry-breaking scale, the **$\mathrm{SU}(2)_L$-doublet sector** is spanned by a
**$2$-dimensional subspace** of $V_{\rm shell}$:

$$V_{\rm EW} := \mathrm{Span}_{\mathbb{R}}\{\psi_{\rm EW}^{(1)}, \psi_{\rm EW}^{(2)}\}
  \subset V_{\rm shell}.$$

The **projector** onto this subspace is:

$$\mathcal{P}_{\rm EW} : V_{\rm shell} \to V_{\rm EW}, \quad
  \mathcal{P}_{\rm EW} = \sum_{i=1}^{2} |\psi_{\rm EW}^{(i)}\rangle\langle\psi_{\rm EW}^{(i)}|.$$

\subsection*{1.2 The matching ansatz: SM Higgs as projected BCC order parameter}

The **SM Higgs doublet** $H_{\rm SM} \in \mathbb{C}^2$ (as a column vector under $\mathrm{SU}(2)_L$
gauge transformations) is related to the BCC condensate by:

$$H_{\rm SM} = Z_H^{1/2} \, \mathcal{P}_{\rm EW}[\Psi_{\rm BCC}] + (\text{higher-order terms}).$$

Here:

  - **$\mathcal{P}_{\rm EW}[\Psi_{\rm BCC}]$**: projection of the full 12-dimensional BCC
    order parameter onto the 2-dimensional EW doublet subspace, yielding a 2-component
    complex field.

  - **$Z_H^{1/2}$**: **wave-function renormalization factor**, defined such that the kinetic
    term of $H_{\rm SM}$ is canonically normalized:

    $$S_{\rm kin}[H] = \int d^4x \, (\nabla_\mu H)^\dagger \nabla^\mu H.$$

  - **Higher-order terms**: $O(\mathcal{P}^3)$ and fermion-bilinear mixing (deferred to later turns).

**Matching condition** (residue matching at the EW scale):

$$H_{\rm SM}(x) = Z_H^{1/2} \, \mathcal{P}_{\rm EW}[\Psi_{\rm BCC}(x)] \quad
  \text{at the symmetry-breaking scale } \mu = m_{\rm EW} \sim 100 \text{ GeV}.$$

\subsection*{1.3 The effective potential in the EW basis}

Under this identification, the **effective scalar potential** in terms of $H_{\rm SM}$ reads:

$$V_{\rm eff}[H] := V_{\rm phys}[\Psi_{\rm BCC}] |_{\Psi = Z_H^{-1/2} H / \mathcal{P}_{\rm EW}}$$

can be expanded as:

$$V_{\rm eff}[H] = \mu_H^2 |H|^2 + \frac{\lambda_H}{2!} |H|^4 + (\text{higher terms}).$$

The **three coefficients of interest** are:

  1. **$Z_H$**: dimensionless wave-function renormalization (ratio of kinetic-term normalizations).
  2. **$\mu_H^2$**: mass-squared parameter in the EW effective theory (units: (GeV)²).
  3. **$\lambda_H$**: quartic coupling in the EW effective theory (dimensionless).

\section*{2. DECOMPOSITION OF COEFFICIENTS: WHAT IS COMPUTABLE?}

\subsection*{2.1 Strategy: bottom-up matching from BCC to EW scale}

To determine which coefficients are **COMPUTABLE** (i.e., derivable from BCC microscopic
Lagrangian alone) versus **EXOGENOUS** (requiring input from higher scales or non-perturbative
resummation), we apply the standard **effective-field-theory matching procedure**:

**Step 1 (Microscopic)**: Start with the full BCC Brazovskii Lagrangian at the condensation
scale:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\rm BCC}[\Psi, \psi, A] = \mathcal{L}_0 + \mathcal{L}_{\rm int} + \mathcal{L}_{\rm gauge}.$$

**Step 2 (Integrate out)**: Integrate out all modes with mass $> m_{\rm EW} \sim 100$ GeV
(the BCC off-shell modes, the bulk phonons, etc.), generating the **EW-scale effective Lagrangian**:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\rm EW}[H] = \int \mathcal{D}\Psi_{\rm heavy} e^{-S_{\rm BCC}[\Psi_{\rm heavy}]} \,
  \mathcal{L}_{\rm EW}[H, \Psi_{\rm light}].$$

**Step 3 (Match coefficients)**: Extract the three coefficients from the EW-scale Lagrangian
by computing observables (propagators, effective vertices) in both theories and matching
residues.

**Honest obstacle**: This is a **one-loop calculation in a strongly-coupled condensed-matter system**,
compounded by the need to perform the continuum limit ($a_{\rm BCC} \to 0$) and handle the
composite nature of the projected field.

\subsection*{2.2 Coefficient α: Wave-function renormalization $Z_H$}

**Definition** (canonical normalization): The kinetic term in the EW effective Lagrangian is

$$\mathcal{L}_{\rm kin}[H] = Z_H (\partial_\mu H)^\dagger \partial^\mu H.$$

The **wave-function renormalization factor** is the **ratio of overlaps** of the projected
BCC mode with the canonically-normalized SM Higgs kinetic term.

**Microscopic origin**: At one-loop order, $Z_H$ receives contributions from:

  (a) **Heavy-mode (off-shell) loop corrections**: When integrating out all BCC shell modes
      orthogonal to the EW doublet subspace, the effective kinetic term for the remaining
      light mode is **dressed** by loop diagrams. The **residue** of the light-mode propagator
      (with external kinematics $p^2 \sim m_{\rm EW}^2$) gives $Z_H$.

  (b) **Fermion-loop dressing**: The light fermions coupled to the BCC sector generate
      **one-loop tadpole** and **one-loop box diagrams** that renormalize the Higgs kinetic
      structure. This is analogous to the **W-boson mass** receiving ~20% QED/QCD corrections
      in the SM.

**Formula (provisional)** [OPEN GAP α]:

$$Z_H = 1 + \frac{g^2}{16\pi^2} \times C_Z \times f(N_f, m_{\rm heavy}),$$

where:

  - $g^2 \sim 0.01$–$0.05$ (emergent gauge coupling, Math230).
  - $C_Z \sim O(1)$ (dimensionless coefficient from loop integrals).
  - $f(N_f, m_{\rm heavy})$ (function of fermion count and heavy-mode spectrum).

**Classicfication**:

  $$\boxed{\text{Status of } Z_H : \textbf{EXOGENOUS}}$$

  **Reason**: The coefficient $C_Z$ depends on the **detailed spectrum of off-shell BCC modes**
  and their **coupling to fermions**, information that is **not yet available** from Math221-AddC,
  Math230, or the one-loop results. Computing $C_Z$ requires:

    - Explicit list of all 12 shell-mode masses and form factors [deferred to Task #146].
    - One-loop propagator correction in the presence of the Brazovskii background [deferred to Task #147].
    - All-order resummation of fermion-loop contributions [deferred to composite-Higgs framework, Task #127].

  **Best current estimate**: From condensed-matter composite-Higgs phenomenology (e.g., technicolor
  precedent), $Z_H \sim 10$–$100$ for a strongly-coupled scalar condensate. The exact value is
  **not determined by BCC microscopic data alone**.

\subsection*{2.3 Coefficient β: Mass parameter $\mu_H^2$}

**Definition**: In the EW effective potential, the **renormalized mass parameter** is:

$$V_{\rm eff}[H] \supset \mu_H^2 |H|^2.$$

This is the **curvature at the origin** of the effective potential (at vanishing field value).

**Microscopic origin**: From Math231 §2.1, the **tree-level mass** in the BCC scalar potential is:

$$m_{\rm BCC}^2 := -\frac{\kappa_{\rm eff}}{f_{\rm BCC}^2},$$

where $\kappa_{\rm eff}$ (the **effective stiffness**) is computed from the BCC Brazovskii
free energy and $f_{\rm BCC}$ is the **wave-function amplitude** of the condensate.

Upon projecting onto the EW doublet and renormalizing by $Z_H$, this becomes:

$$\mu_H^2 = Z_H \times m_{\rm BCC}^2 |_{V_{\rm EW}} + \Delta\mu_H^{(1)},$$

where:

  - **$m_{\rm BCC}^2 |_{V_{\rm EW}}$**: the tree-level mass restricted to the EW subspace.
  - **$\Delta\mu_H^{(1)}$**: one-loop quantum correction (from box and tadpole diagrams).

**Formula (partial)** [OPEN GAP β]:

$$\mu_H^2 = Z_H \left[-\frac{\kappa_\chi + \Delta\kappa^{(1)}}{f_{\rm phys}^2}\right],$$

where:

  - **$\kappa_\chi$** is the **chiral stiffness** from Math221-AddC (Lemma A): $\kappa_\chi = C_\chi \times Q_R(\mathbf{16})$,
    where $Q_R$ is the group-theoretic representation constant. [Math221-AddC, PROVED CONDITIONAL]

  - **$\Delta\kappa^{(1)}$**: one-loop correction to the stiffness from fermion loops [deferred to Task #147].

  - **$f_{\rm phys}^2$**: the **physical wave-function amplitude**, related to the continuum-limit extrapolation
    of the bare BCC field amplitude $f_{\rm BCC}$. This is the **single largest source of numerical uncertainty**.

**Classifification**:

  $$\boxed{\text{Status of } \mu_H^2 : \textbf{PARTIALLY COMPUTABLE}}$$

  **Computable part**: The tree-level stiffness $\kappa_\chi$ is **derivable from BCC microscopic parameters**
  (Math221-AddC, Lemma A). Its value is:

  $$\kappa_\chi = C_\chi \times \mathrm{Tr}[T^A T^A] = C_\chi \times \frac{65}{3}, \quad
    C_\chi \sim O(1)$$

  with the trace taken over the 12-dimensional shell representation. [Math221-AddC §5, PROVED CONDITIONAL]

  **Exogenous part**: The relationship between the **bare BCC amplitude** $f_{\rm BCC}$ (from Math82)
  and the **physical EW-scale amplitude** $f_{\rm phys}$ requires:

    (i)   **Continuum-limit extrapolation** $a_{\rm BCC} \to 0$ [Task #115, pending].
    (ii)  **Matching to asymptotic-freedom boundary** at the GUT scale [Task #145, pending].
    (iii) **Composite-Higgs dressing factor** [Task #127, pending].

  All three are **currently UNKNOWN**. Until they are determined, $\mu_H^2$ can be **bounded**
  (from $\kappa_\chi$ alone) but not **determined**.

\subsection*{2.4 Coefficient γ: Quartic coupling $\lambda_H$}

**Definition**: The **renormalized quartic coupling** is the **second functional derivative of the potential**
with respect to $|H|$, evaluated at $|H| = 0$:

$$\lambda_H := \frac{1}{2} \frac{\partial^4 V_{\rm eff}}{\partial |H|^4}\bigg|_{|H|=0}.$$

**Microscopic origin**: From Math231 §2.2, the **tree-level Brazovskii quartic** is:

$$\lambda_{\rm tree} = \frac{\eta_{\rm real}}{f_{\rm BCC}^4},$$

where $\eta$ is the **Brazovskii biquadratic coefficient** (a locked parameter, $\eta = 1.62$ in the
TECT axiom).

Upon projection and one-loop renormalization:

$$\lambda_H = Z_H^2 \times \lambda_{\rm tree} + \Delta\lambda^{(1)},$$

where $\Delta\lambda^{(1)}$ is the one-loop beta-function contribution (from box, triangle, and bubble diagrams).

**Formula (partial)** [OPEN GAP γ]:

$$\lambda_H = Z_H^2 \left[\frac{\eta}{f_{\rm phys}^4}\right] + \frac{g^2}{16\pi^2} \times B_\lambda
  \times \lambda_{\rm tree}.$$

**Classification**:

  $$\boxed{\text{Status of } \lambda_H : \textbf{PARTIALLY COMPUTABLE}}$$

  **Computable part**: The **tree-level Brazovskii quartic** $\eta$ is a **locked axiom parameter**
  in TECT. Its value is $\eta = 1.62$ (from the phase-diagram analysis of Math82). The one-loop beta-function
  coefficient $B_\lambda$ is a **standard QFT calculation** (SM-like Higgs box diagram); see Math230.

  **Exogenous part**: As with $\mu_H^2$, the **physical amplitude** $f_{\rm phys}$ is unknown,
  entering as $f_{\rm phys}^4$ in the denominator. Additionally, the **wave-function renormalization**
  $Z_H$ appears squared; until $Z_H$ is determined (EXOGENOUS, see §2.2), $\lambda_H$ cannot be
  fully computed.

  **Obstruction summary**: Even if $f_{\rm phys}$ were known, the calculation requires:

    (i)   **$Z_H$ coefficient** [EXOGENOUS per §2.2].
    (ii)  **Two-loop matching** if one-loop beta function is to be applied across scales [partially addressed by Math230].
    (iii) **Non-perturbative resummation** if the quartic is large ($\lambda_H \sim O(1)$), which it is observed to be [deferred].

  (Scenario 2) The BCC theory **requires fine-tuning** of the bare parameters to hit the critical point

**Current status**: TECT operates at **fixed lattice spacing** ($a_{\rm BCC}$ = numerical parameter in the solver).

\section*{6. SELF-ADVERSARIAL REVIEW (CLAUDE.md §6.3.5(a))}

  $f_{\rm phys}$, but they are not independent"}

**Claim**: The wave-function renormalization $Z_H$ and the physical amplitude $f_{\rm phys}$ are

**RESPONSE (UPHELD, status downgraded)**:

This is a **valid structural critique**. In the **standard EFT matching procedure**, there is
indeed a redundancy: the wave-function renormalization $Z_H$ **absorbs the effect of rescaling
the field amplitude**. Including both $Z_H$ and a separate $f_{\rm phys}$ factor is redundant.

**Correct reformulation**: Define a **single composite amplitude** $\tilde{f} = \sqrt{Z_H} \times f_{\rm phys}$,
and absorb this into the definition of the **canonical EW-scale field**:

$$H_{\rm SM}^{(\text{can})} := \frac{1}{\tilde{f}} \mathcal{P}_{\rm EW}[\Psi_{\rm BCC}],$$

so that $H_{\rm SM}^{(\text{can})}$ is dimensionless. Then the **physical scale** is:

$$v_{\rm EW} = \tilde{f} \times |\langle H_{\rm SM}^{(\text{can})} \rangle|.$$

**Impact on the note**: The **decomposition in §2 changes** under this restructuring:

  - **Computable**: The **product $Z_H \times f_{\rm phys}^2$** can be determined (partially) from

  - **Exogenous**: The **relative split between $Z_H$ and $f_{\rm phys}$** is a **matching-scheme choice**
    and cannot be uniquely determined from BCC data alone.

**Status change**: This does **NOT invalidate** the note's main conclusion (that coefficients depend on
exogenous data), but it does **soften the precision of the classification**. The note should be
amended to acknowledge that $Z_H$ and $f_{\rm phys}$ are **not independently measurable**.

**Verdict**: UPHELD. Recommend downgrading status from **T3 PROOF SKETCH** to **T3 PROOF SKETCH
(with parametrization caveat)** in the summary. The logic stands; the execution requires refinement.

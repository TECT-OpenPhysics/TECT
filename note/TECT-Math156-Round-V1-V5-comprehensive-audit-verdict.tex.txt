\subsection*{3. Detailed objection record}

\subsubsection*{3.1 GAP 1 --- $\hbar$ matching is not independently verified}

\textbf{Upstream claim} (Math149, Math110-AddI):
\begin{equation}
\hbar_{\rm Fock} \;=\; \hbar_{\rm gravity}
\quad\text{via two ``independent'' routes A and B.}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Objection (UPHELD)}: Routes A and B are not algebraically independent.
Route A computes $\hbar_{\rm Fock}$ via condensate Fock-quantisation:
\begin{equation}
\hbar_{\rm Fock} \;\sim\; \rho_{\rm cond}\, c\, a_{\rm BCC}^{\,3}.
\end{equation}
Route B computes $\hbar_{\rm gravity}$ via the Planck closure
$a_{\rm BCC}^{\,2} = 16\pi G \hbar / c^3$, but $\rho_{\rm cond}$ in route A
is obtained from the same elastic-modulus identification
$\rho_{\rm cond} \sim c^4/(16\pi G a_{\rm BCC}^{\,2})$ that underlies Route B
(Math110-AddG--AddI). Substituting the latter into the former,
\begin{equation}
\hbar_{\rm Fock} \;=\;
\frac{c^4}{16\pi G a_{\rm BCC}^{\,2}}\cdot c\, a_{\rm BCC}^{\,3}
\;=\;
\frac{c^5\, a_{\rm BCC}}{16\pi G},
\end{equation}
which is the \emph{same} relation as Route B once $a_{\rm BCC}$ is
inverted. Hence the two routes share a common structural input, and the
agreement is a tautology rather than a cross-check.

\textbf{Demoted status}: \textbf{PROVED CONDITIONAL (weak)} --- the
identification $\hbar_{\rm Fock} = \hbar_{\rm gravity}$ is established under
the \emph{single} elastic-modulus hypothesis; an independent route is
required to upgrade to PROVED.

\textbf{Required closure work}: derive $\hbar_{\rm Fock}$ from a
\emph{matter-side} (e.g.\ fermion-loop or Onsager-Machlup) computation
that does not feed through $\rho_{\rm cond} \leftrightarrow G$, and verify
numerical agreement with Route B.

\subsubsection*{3.2 GAP 3 --- the anomaly is not closed}

\textbf{Upstream claim} (Math148): the chiral anomaly evaluated by
component-by-component Fujikawa sum gives
\begin{equation}
\mathcal A_{YYY} = -2,\quad
\mathcal A_{YY2} = \tfrac{1}{3},\quad
\mathcal A_{222} = 1,
\end{equation}
non-zero in absolute value but ``cancelled by Higgs'' or ``redeemed by
SO(10) embedding.''

\textbf{Objection (UPHELD)}: scalar Higgs fields cannot cancel chiral
gauge anomalies. The Adler--Bardeen theorem fixes the gauge anomaly as
a one-loop, fermion-only contribution, and the Wess--Zumino consistency
condition forbids scalar contributions. The claim ``Higgs cancels'' is
categorically wrong. The fallback ``SO(10) embedding closes it'' is also
not a proof: SO(10) lacks a totally-symmetric cubic invariant
$d^{abc}$, so its perturbative gauge anomaly vanishes \emph{trivially}
--- but this property of SO(10) does \emph{not} imply that a particular
Y-charge assignment within a low-energy SU(3)$\times$SU(2)$\times$U(1)
theory is anomaly-free. The non-zero numbers above mean the assumed
Y-charge assignment is not the SM assignment, or the chirality counting
is wrong, or the GUT-normalisation $Y_{\rm GUT} = \sqrt{3/5}\,Y_{\rm SM}$
was misapplied.

\textbf{Demoted status}: \textbf{OPEN (critical blocker)}. GAP 3 is the
single critical blocker for Stage-2 (S2) quantum-consistency
qualification. If the SM anomaly cancellation cannot be reproduced from
TECT's emergent gauge structure, the framework is not a quantum field
theory in the usual sense.

\textbf{Required closure work}: rigorous group-theoretic trace-method
calculation of the six SM anomaly coefficients
$\{\mathrm{Tr}\,T_3^a\{T_3^b,T_3^c\},\ \mathrm{Tr}\,T_2^a\{T_2^b,T_2^c\},\
\mathrm{Tr}\,T_3^a T_3^b Y,\ \mathrm{Tr}\,T_2^a T_2^b Y,\
\mathrm{Tr}\,Y^3,\ \mathrm{Tr}\,Y\}$ over a single
$\mathbf{16}$ of SO(10) decomposed via
$\mathrm{SO}(10)\to\mathrm{SU}(5)\to\mathrm{SU}(3)\times\mathrm{SU}(2)\times\mathrm{U}(1)_Y$
with proper $Y_{\rm GUT}$ normalisation. This is the content of
\emph{Math157} (next note).

\subsubsection*{3.3 GAP 2 --- BRST is outline, not proof}

\textbf{Upstream claim} (Math152): BRST gauge-fixing with
Faddeev--Popov ghosts is consistent for the emergent gauge bundle.

\textbf{Objection (VALID)}: standard BRST applies to any compact
Yang--Mills theory; this is not a TECT-specific proof. The TECT-specific
question is whether the BCC condensate's emergent gauge symmetry is
genuinely realised as a local fibre-bundle gauge symmetry rather than
a non-linearly-realised global symmetry, and whether the Faddeev--Popov
determinant is finite for the lattice-regularised TECT functional
integral. Neither is established.

\textbf{Demoted status}: \textbf{OUTLINE}. No structural problem
identified, but no TECT-specific theorem either.

\textbf{Required closure work}: explicit Faddeev--Popov determinant
calculation for the BCC-condensate gauge orbits at one loop, and proof
of finite ghost determinant on a representative volume.

\subsubsection*{3.4 GAP 4 --- observables fail at $5\sigma$}

\textbf{Upstream claim} (Math151): TECT cosmological observables are
``compatible with current Planck data within parametric tension.''

\textbf{Objection (UPHELD)}: the central TECT prediction
$n_s^{\rm TECT} \approx 0.913$ vs the observed $n_s^{\rm obs} = 0.9649
\pm 0.0042$ is a $\sim 13\sigma$ discrepancy by the literal Planck error,
or $\sim 5\sigma$ if the TECT parametric uncertainty band is widened to
$\pm 0.01$. ``Compatible within parametric tension'' is not the right
language; this is a falsification at conventional thresholds for the
present cosmology branch.

\textbf{Demoted status}: \textbf{FAIL (current cosmology branch)}. The
S3 predicate is not satisfied for the inflationary scenario as currently
parametrised.

\textbf{Required closure work}: identify whether the failure is in
(i) the TECT-side derivation (e.g.\ $n_s$ formula assumes incorrect
horizon-crossing condition), (ii) the parameter mapping
($a_{\rm BCC} \to k_*$), or (iii) the underlying scenario (early-universe
TECT phase transition does not produce observable inflation), and
re-derive accordingly.

\subsection*{6. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.1)}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$.}
``The audit is itself over-conservative; the SO(10) trivial-anomaly
property does close GAP 3.''

\textbf{Verdict: DISMISSED.} SO(10) trivial anomaly closes the
\emph{SO(10)-side} consistency, but the empirical SM gauge group is
SU(3)$\times$SU(2)$\times$U(1)$_Y$ at low energy. The SU(2)$\times$U(1)
embedding within SO(10) requires explicit demonstration that the
Y-charge assignment to TECT-emergent fermions reproduces the standard
SM hypercharge pattern (or an equivalent anomaly-free pattern). Math148
did not establish this; the non-zero numbers in Math148 indicate either
mis-assigned charges or mis-counted chiralities. Math157 provides the
required check.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$.}
``Route A and Route B share an input but they still constrain different
observables --- the agreement is non-trivial.''

\textbf{Verdict: VALID, MITIGATED.} The shared-input critique reduces
the epistemic strength of the agreement but does not falsify Route A
itself. The honest demotion is from PROVED to PROVED-CONDITIONAL with a
mitigation requirement: produce a third route from a different starting
point (e.g.\ explicit fermion-loop computation, or matrix-element
saturation of the canonical commutator at the BCC scale). This is
recorded as Q-2026-04-26-GAP1-third-route in OPEN-QUESTIONS.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$.}
``$n_s$ failure may simply reflect incomplete parameter exploration; the
observable space is large.''

\textbf{Verdict: UPHELD.} The failure is at $\geq 5\sigma$ across the
parametric band Math151 explored. Either the formula or the scenario is
wrong. Saving by ``parameter exploration'' is not a defence; it is a
research task. Recorded as Q-2026-04-26-GAP4-ns-failure in
OPEN-QUESTIONS.

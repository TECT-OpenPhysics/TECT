§ 1. COMPUTATION PLAN
==================================================================

**Objective**: Simulate the cosmological phase transition at the TDGL
(time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau) level with Hubble friction included.
Verify whether Kibble-Zurek defects actually form and freeze in.

**Parameters**:
- System size: 32³ lattice (smallest, fastest)
- Temperature: T from 1.2 T_c down to 0.5 T_c (across transition)
- Cooling rate: β = dT/dt = -10^{-2} T_c / τ_relax (realistic quench)
- Hubble friction: $2H(T) \dot{\Psi} = ...$  term in TDGL
- Noise: Thermal random forces at temperature T

**TDGL equation** (with Hubble friction):

$$\frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial t} + 2H(t)\Psi = -\Gamma \frac{\delta \mathcal{F}}{\delta \Psi^*} + \sqrt{2\Gamma T}\, \xi(t,x).$$

**Runtime**: ~100,000 timestepping iterations
**Computational cost**: ~10 hours on GPU cluster (CUDA + PyTorch)

**Metrics to track**:
- Topological charge density $Q(t,x)$ at each time
- Defect count and positions
- Free energy $F(t)$ (compare to analytical predictions)
- Order-parameter gradient field (visualization)

§ 2. EXPECTED OUTCOMES

**Outcome C** (INCONCLUSIVE):

- Indicates that non-linear TDGL dynamics are more complex
- Requires refined numerical methods (adaptive time stepping, etc.)

\section*{Math171-AddA --- Degree-Arithmetic Correction to Math171 §3.3}

\subsection*{1. The error}

Math171 §3.3 computes the Atiyah--Singer integral
\[
\mathrm{ind}(D_E) \;=\; \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2}\hat A(T\mathbb{CP}^2)\wedge\mathrm{ch}(E),
\]
with $\hat A(T\mathbb{CP}^2) = 1 - H^2/8$ and
$\mathrm{ch}(E) = 16 - c_2(E)$ (using $c_1(E)=0$). The wedge product is
\[
\hat A\wedge\mathrm{ch}(E) \;=\; (1-H^2/8)(16-c_2)
\;=\; 16 - c_2 - 2H^2 + (H^2/8)c_2 .
\]
The degree decomposition of this inhomogeneous form on $\mathbb{CP}^2$
(real dimension $4$) is
\[
\underbrace{16}_{\text{deg }0} \;\oplus\; \underbrace{(-c_2 - 2H^2)}_{\text{deg }4} \;\oplus\;
\underbrace{(H^2/8)c_2}_{\text{deg }8 = 0\text{ on }\mathbb{CP}^2}.
\]

Math171 line 177--178 wrote
\[
\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2}[16 - c_2 - 2H^2] \;\stackrel{?}{=}\; \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2}[14H^2 - c_2],
\]
treating the constant $16$ as if it could be rewritten as $16H^2$ before
integration. \textbf{This is incorrect.} The $16$ is a degree-$0$ form;
its contribution to $\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2}$ vanishes because the integral
of a $4$-manifold extracts only the degree-$4$ part of the integrand.

\subsection*{2. Correct evaluation}

The degree-$4$ part of $\hat A\wedge\mathrm{ch}(E)$ is
\[
\big[\hat A\wedge\mathrm{ch}(E)\big]_{(4)} \;=\; -c_2(E) - 2H^2 .
\]
Integrating with $\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} H^2 = 1$ and writing
$c_2(E) = \mu H^2$:
\[
\boxed{\;\mathrm{ind}(D_E) \;=\; -\mu - 2 \;\;\text{(naive Dirac, no spin-c twist).}}
\]

But $\mathbb{CP}^2$ is \textbf{not spin}: $w_2(T\mathbb{CP}^2) = c_1(T\mathbb{CP}^2)\bmod 2 = H \neq 0$. The standard Dirac operator does not exist on $\mathbb{CP}^2$; one must use a \textbf{spin-c} Dirac with twist by a determinant line bundle $L$ satisfying $c_1(L)\equiv H\pmod 2$. The corrected index formula is
\[
\mathrm{ind}(D_E^c) \;=\; \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2}\hat A(T\mathbb{CP}^2)\,e^{c_1(L)/2}\,\mathrm{ch}(E).
\]

\subsection*{3. Canonical complex spin-c structure}

Since $\mathbb{CP}^2$ is Kähler, the natural spin-c is the
\textbf{canonical complex spin-c} with
$c_1(L_{\rm can}) = c_1(T\mathbb{CP}^2) = 3H$.
Then $e^{c_1(L)/2} = e^{3H/2} = 1 + 3H/2 + (3H/2)^2/2 + \cdots
= 1 + 3H/2 + 9H^2/8$ (to degree 4 on $\mathbb{CP}^2$).

The combined factor is
\[
\hat A \cdot e^{c_1/2}
\;=\; (1 - H^2/8)(1 + 3H/2 + 9H^2/8)
\;=\; 1 + 3H/2 + (9/8 - 1/8) H^2
\;=\; 1 + 3H/2 + H^2.
\]
This equals the \textbf{Todd class} $\mathrm{td}(T\mathbb{CP}^2)$
exactly — as expected, the spin-c Dirac index on a complex manifold
with canonical structure is the Hirzebruch--Riemann--Roch holomorphic
Euler characteristic
\[
\chi(E) \;=\; \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2}\mathrm{td}(T\mathbb{CP}^2)\,\mathrm{ch}(E)
\;=\; \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2}\bigl(1 + 3H/2 + H^2\bigr)\bigl(16 - c_2\bigr).
\]
The degree-$4$ part is $16 H^2 - c_2$ (the $3H/2$ term contributes
nothing because $\mathrm{ch}(E)$ has no degree-$2$ component when
$c_1(E)=0$). Integrating:
\[
\boxed{\;\mathrm{ind}(D_E^c) \;=\; 16 - \mu \;\;\text{(canonical complex spin-c).}}
\]
Sanity check: for trivial bundle $E = \mathcal O$ (rank 1, $\mu=0$), this
gives $\chi(\mathcal O_{\mathbb{CP}^2}) = 1$, which is correct (the
holomorphic Euler characteristic of the structure sheaf on
$\mathbb{CP}^2$ is $1$).

\subsection*{4. Revised gate}

Math171's stated formula $\mathrm{ind}(D_E) = 14 - \mu$ is incorrect
under either interpretation. The correct results are:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|}
\hline
Interpretation & Formula & For $\mathrm{ind}=16$ need \\
\hline
Naive Dirac (no spin-c; physically wrong on $\mathbb{CP}^2$) & $-\mu - 2$ & $\mu = -18$ \\
Canonical complex spin-c (Hirzebruch--RR; correct) & $16 - \mu$ & $\mu = 0$ \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

For the canonical complex spin-c on $\mathbb{CP}^2$ (the only physically
admissible Dirac on a Kähler manifold without spin structure), the
target $\mathrm{ind}(D_E)=16$ requires $c_2(E) = 0$, i.e.\ the
\textbf{second Chern class of the matter bundle vanishes}. This is a
much sharper and more falsifiable criterion than Math171's claimed
$\mu = -2$.

\subsection*{5. Physical interpretation}

$c_2(E) = 0$ for the spinor representation associated bundle on the
Math162 $\mathrm{CP}^2$ base would mean the bundle is, in K-theory
terms, a sum of trivial line bundles up to virtual cancellation. This
is highly restrictive. If the Math162 bundle has any non-trivial
SU(5) instanton number, $c_2(E)$ will not vanish and the index will
not equal 16, which would falsify the Pillar 4 sub-task 2 decomposition
$\mathbf{16}=\mathbf{10}\oplus\overline{\mathbf{5}}\oplus\mathbf{1}$.

\subsection*{7. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$.} ``Maybe Math171 used a non-standard
spin-c structure that makes $14 - \mu$ correct.''

\textbf{Verdict: DISMISSED.} For $\hat A \cdot e^{c_1(L)/2}$ to equal
$1 + 7H^2/4$ (which would yield rank-$16$ contribution
$16 \cdot 7H^2/4 = 28 H^2$, then $14H^2$ after halving — but this is
nonsense), one would need $c_1(L)$ chosen so that the degree-$4$ part
of $\hat A_c$ is $7/4$. No clean choice produces this. The error in
Math171 is the degree-arithmetic mistake described in §1, not a
choice of spin-c structure.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$.} ``Math171 might have used a
representation-theoretic identity that magically converts $16$ to
$16 H^2$ before integration.''

\textbf{Verdict: DISMISSED.} No such identity exists. The constant $16$
in $\mathrm{ch}(E)$ is the rank, a degree-$0$ form. It can only
contribute to $\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2}$ when wedged with a degree-$4$ form
from $\hat A$ (or $e^{c_1/2}$). With $\hat A = 1 - H^2/8$, the degree-$4$
contribution is $16 \cdot (-H^2/8) = -2H^2$. Math171 line 177 has this
correctly as ``$-2H^2$'', then erroneously rewrites $16 - c_2 - 2H^2$ as
$14H^2 - c_2$ (the $16$ becomes $16H^2$, an unjustified upgrade).

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$.} ``This correction is itself an audit
catch by the dispatcher; could it also be wrong?''

\textbf{Verdict: VALID, mitigation.} The correction has been sanity-checked against the trivial-bundle case ($E = \mathcal O$, $\mu=0$):
the canonical complex spin-c yields $\chi(\mathcal O_{\mathbb{CP}^2}) = 1$, which agrees with the standard result. The naive (no spin-c) formula yields $\chi = -2$, which is unphysical (negative dimension of Dirac kernel for the trivial bundle), confirming that the canonical spin-c is the correct physical interpretation.

\subsection*{1. Extended lattice / quasicrystal / non-crystalline candidate set}

The candidate set considered so far is restricted to cubic-or-uniaxial single-shell crystals. The complete enumeration of physically observed TECT-relevant condensate structures is much broader.

\paragraph{1.1 Frank-Kasper extended phases.}
The original Frank-Kasper 1958--1959 classification identifies tetrahedrally close-packed structures with 4 fundamental polytopes (CN12 = icosahedron, CN14, CN15, CN16). Beyond the four already considered (BCC: not strictly Frank-Kasper; FCC: $\rm fcc$; HEX: $\rm hcp$/A3; A15: Cr$_3$Si; $\sigma$-phase), known FK structures include:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Z-phase} (Zr$_4$Al$_3$): hexagonal, near-cubic
\item \textbf{$\mu$-phase} (Mo$_6$Co$_7$): rhombohedral
\item \textbf{P-phase} (Mo$_{42}$Cr$_{18}$Ni$_{40}$): orthorhombic
\item \textbf{R-phase} (Mo$_{31}$Co$_{51}$Cr$_{18}$): rhombohedral
\item \textbf{M-phase}, \textbf{X-phase}, \textbf{X'-phase}, \textbf{K-phase}: various lower symmetries
\item \textbf{C14, C15, C36 Laves phases}: hexagonal / cubic variants
\end{itemize}
The cosmological-isotropy filter (Math400-AddA §1) accepts only those with cubic point group; among Frank-Kasper, this is essentially $\{$A15, C15$\}$ plus extended cubic-FK candidates (e.g., Friauf-Laves cubic phases).

\paragraph{1.2 Bicontinuous and triply-periodic minimal surface (TPMS) phases.}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Single-gyroid} (I$4_1$32, Ia$\bar 3$d single-network): bicontinuous, observed in lipid-water, photonic crystals, block copolymer melts
\item \textbf{Double-gyroid} (Ia$\bar 3$d): two interpenetrating gyroid networks
\item \textbf{Diamond (Fd$\bar 3$m / Pn$\bar 3$m)}: bicontinuous
\item \textbf{Schwarz P-surface (Im$\bar 3$m / Pm$\bar 3$m)}: simple-cubic bicontinuous
\item \textbf{F-RD, FC-RD, Y, etc.}: extended TPMS catalogue
\end{itemize}
All cubic point group, all cosmological-isotropy compatible. Particularly notable: \textbf{the gyroid is the structure observed at the order-disorder transition of weak-segregation block copolymer melts} (Schoen 1970; Hajduk et al.\ 1995; Matsen \& Bates 1996), which is the physical context closest to the TECT Brazovskii framework.

\paragraph{1.3 Quasicrystals.}
Quasicrystals are quasi-periodic (long-range order with non-crystallographic rotational symmetry), violating the crystallographic restriction that point groups have only 1, 2, 3, 4, or 6-fold rotational symmetries. Higher rotational symmetries give more isotropic two-point functions.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Icosahedral (5-fold + 3-fold + 2-fold)} quasicrystals: nearly isotropic, $\ell\geq 6$ harmonics for first anisotropy
\item \textbf{Decagonal (10-fold)} quasicrystals: isotropic in plane, periodic in $z$
\item \textbf{Dodecagonal (12-fold)} quasicrystals (DDQC): in-plane isotropic, periodic in $z$
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Key observation}: dodecagonal quasicrystals (DDQC) have been observed thermodynamically stable in self-assembled block copolymer melts (Hayashida, Dotera, Takano \& Matsushita 2007 \emph{Phys.\ Rev.\ Lett.\ 98 195502}; Zeng, Ungar, Liu, Percec \& Dulcey 2004 \emph{Nature 428 157}). These are the exact physical context of the Brazovskii framework.

If TECT vacuum is icosahedral quasicrystalline, the cosmological isotropy is achieved at $\ell\leq 5$ harmonics (first anisotropy at $\ell=6$, much weaker than cubic's $\ell=4$ first anisotropy). This is the most isotropy-friendly candidate of all and warrants serious consideration.

\paragraph{1.4 Skyrmion / hopfion / vortex lattices (multi-$q$ multi-channel).}
In multi-channel order parameters (e.g., 3-channel SU(3) of TECT production), topological textures can form lattices:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Skyrmion crystals}: 2D triangular lattice of skyrmions, observed in chiral magnets MnSi (M\"uhlbauer et al.\ 2009 \emph{Science 323 915}); 3D analogues are hopfion crystals
\item \textbf{Vortex lattices}: e.g., type-II superconductor flux lattice (Abrikosov 1957); can be triangular or square depending on regime
\item \textbf{Hopfion crystals}: linked vortex configurations with non-trivial Hopf invariant
\item \textbf{$\pi_3(\mathrm{SU}(3)) = \mathbb{Z}$ texture lattices}: relevant to TECT 3-channel field
\end{itemize}
These are inaccessible to the single-channel reduction (Math376 v3) and hence to all of Math383 §6 / Math396-AddA. They are accessible only to multi-channel solvers (Math382 PyTorch 3-channel cross-validation, queued).

\paragraph{1.5 HCP and near-cubic uniaxial variants.}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{HCP (A3)}: hexagonal close-packed; uniaxial (rejected by isotropy filter)
\item \textbf{$\beta$-tin (A5)}: tetragonal, near-cubic at $c/a=1$
\end{itemize}
Mostly rejected by isotropy filter, but $\beta$-tin near $c/a=1$ approaches cubic and might be a candidate edge case.

\paragraph{1.6 The systematic upper bound.}
A complete enumeration of point-group-cubic isotropy-compatible lattices in 3D includes the 36 isohedral cubic space groups (including $Pm\bar 3m$, $Im\bar 3m$, $Fm\bar 3m$, $Pn\bar 3m$, $Pa\bar 3$, $Ia\bar 3$, $Ia\bar 3d$, $Im\bar 3$, $Fd\bar 3$, $Fd\bar 3c$, $Fm\bar 3c$, $Pm\bar 3n$, $Pm\bar 3$, $Pn\bar 3$, etc.). Many of these support multiple sublattices and non-trivial wavevector content beyond first-shell. The candidate space, fully enumerated, is in the hundreds of distinct structures, many with different $\{K_{4}, K_{6}, K_{8}, \ldots\}$ structure factors.

The pragmatic next-step extension (Math401) is the union $\{$BCC, FCC, A15, $\sigma$, gyroid, double-gyroid, diamond, Schwarz-P, dodecagonal QC$\}$ — 9 candidates, computationally tractable at $N=64$.

\paragraph{2.2 Brazovskii 1975 fluctuation-stabilized disordered phase. (CRITICAL)}
The original Brazovskii 1975 paper (Brazovskii, Sov.\ Phys.\ JETP 41, 85--89) is widely \emph{misread} as ``Brazovskii predicts BCC condensation in 3D.'' The actual main result is the OPPOSITE: in dimensions where the inverse Brazovskii vertex is sufficiently divergent at the q-shell, mean-field broken-symmetry condensation is DESTABILIZED by infrared fluctuations into a \emph{fluctuation-stabilized disordered phase}, characterised by:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\langle\Psi(\mathbf r)\rangle_{\rm macro} = 0$ (no broken symmetry)
\item $\langle\Psi^{2}\rangle_{\rm macro} \neq 0$ (variance from critical fluctuations)
\item Two-point function $\langle\Psi(\mathbf r)\Psi(\mathbf r+\delta\mathbf r)\rangle$ oscillates with envelope $e^{-\delta r/\xi}$, $\xi\sim 1/q_{0}$ at low $T$
\item Cosmologically isotropic by construction (no preferred direction)
\item Critical opalescence-like phenomenon: long-range correlations in $\Psi^{2}$ but not $\Psi$
\end{itemize}

This is THE canonical Brazovskii prediction, and it is the most physically natural description of a Brazovskii system at the canonical operating point $\mu^{2}>0$ near the order-disorder line.

In the TECT cosmological context:
\begin{itemize}
\item Macroscopic isotropy: AUTOMATIC (filter PASS by construction).
\item Local order: present within $\xi\sim 1/q_{0}\sim 1.5$ TECT length units.
\item Topological structure: defects and textures are FLUCTUATION-INDUCED, not condensate-induced. They appear and disappear stochastically. Lifetime $\sim 1/q_{0}$ in equilibrium; can be FROZEN by cosmological quench.
\item Pillar 11 DM-origin: candidate frozen-fluctuation-defects from the Brazovskii regime quench, with abundance set by quench rate vs $1/q_{0}$ relaxation rate.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Status of Math82-AddH BCC-condensation in this picture}: at the canonical operating point $\mu^{2}=+0.005$, the BCC mean-field configuration is NOT a thermodynamic vacuum but a transient fluctuation correlation structure with lifetime $\sim 1/q_{0}$ (in equilibrium) or $\tau_{Q}$ (under cosmological quench). \paragraph{2.3 Glass / amorphous frozen disorder.}
After cosmological quench through the order-disorder transition, the system can freeze into a glassy state with:
\begin{itemize}
\item No long-range order
\item Local domains of ordered (BCC, FCC, etc.) clusters separated by domain walls
\item Random texture pattern with macroscopic isotropy
\end{itemize}
Cosmologically isotropic, supports topological defects naturally. Distinguished from Brazovskii fluctuation regime by being non-equilibrium and exhibiting ageing dynamics.

\paragraph{2.4 Topological-only condensate (no spatial modulation).}
TECT may condense at the topological / gauge level without spatial modulation of $\Psi$. Examples:
\begin{itemize}
\item Yang-Mills gluon condensate $\langle G_{\mu\nu}^{a} G^{a\mu\nu}\rangle \neq 0$ but $\langle G_{\mu\nu}^{a}(\mathbf r)\rangle = 0$
\item Chiral condensate $\langle \bar\psi\psi\rangle \neq 0$ but $\langle\Psi(\mathbf r)\rangle = 0$
\item Topological term $\int F\wedge F \neq 0$ in some sector
\end{itemize}
For TECT, this would mean the order parameter at the topological / Hopf-invariant level is non-zero, but the spatial $\Psi(\mathbf r)$ vanishes ensemble-averaged. The TECT framework is consistent with this if the Pillars 5/7 (topological / gauge) structure is enriched at the expense of Pillar 4 (geometric / spatial).

\paragraph{2.5 Multi-channel decoupled.}
The 3-channel SU(3) field $\boldsymbol\Psi=(\Psi_{1},\Psi_{2},\Psi_{3})$ may have $\langle\Psi_{1}\rangle=\langle\Psi_{2}\rangle=\langle\Psi_{3}\rangle = 0$ individually, but with non-trivial expectation values of multi-channel composites: $\langle\Psi_{a}^{*}\Psi_{b}\rangle = M_{ab}\neq 0$, where $M$ is an $\mathrm{SU}(3)$-symmetry-breaking $3\times 3$ matrix. This is a chiral-symmetry-breaking condensate at the matrix-of-channels level, with no spatial modulation. The single-channel reduction (Math376 v3) projects $M_{ab}$ onto the family-locked direction $\hat z_{0}$ giving $\langle z_{0}^{a}\Psi_{a}\rangle = 0$ if $M$ is off-diagonal, hence the appearance of single-channel disorder.

\paragraph{2.6 Dynamical attractor without static order.}
In a continuously driven cosmological evolution (changing $\mu^{2}(t)$ across the transition), the system may be perpetually out-of-equilibrium with no static vacuum. The "vacuum" is then a dynamical attractor of the cosmological field equations, with Pillar 4 reformulated as a stability theorem of the attractor rather than a thermodynamic vacuum theorem. This is the natural picture for a TECT framework that includes both the early-universe quench and present-day evolution.

\subsection*{4. Brazovskii 1975 main result and TECT consistency}

The original Brazovskii 1975 paper considers a real scalar field in 3D with free energy
\begin{equation}
  F[\Psi] = \int\!\! d^{3}r\, \Big[\tfrac{r}{2}\Psi^{2} + \tfrac{c}{2}(|\nabla|^{2}-q_{0}^{2})^{2}\Psi^{2} + \tfrac{u}{4}\Psi^{4}\Big],
\end{equation}
which is precisely the canonical TECT Brazovskii free energy (Math373) modulo notation: $r=\mu^{2}+Yq_{0}^{4}$, $c=Y$, $u=2\lambda$, sextic $\gamma=0$ (Brazovskii originally considered quartic-only; sextic was added later by Toner \& Nelson 1981, Hohenberg \& Swift 1992, etc.).

The Brazovskii self-consistent one-loop calculation finds:
\begin{enumerate}
\item In dimensions where the q-shell vertex $G^{(2)}(q) = 1/(r + c(q^{2}-q_{0}^{2})^{2})$ is sufficiently singular at $q\to q_{0}$, infrared fluctuations dominate.
\item For 3D Brazovskii (the TECT case), the fluctuation correction shifts the effective $r$ upward (renormalisation $r\to r_{R}$) by a self-energy term $\Sigma\sim 1/\sqrt{r}$.
\item The self-consistency equation $r_{R} = r + u\Sigma(r_{R})$ has solution $r_{R}>0$ even when bare $r<0$, provided $|r|\ll u^{2/3}q_{0}^{4/3}/c$.
\item In this fluctuation-dominated regime, the mean-field broken-symmetry solution $\langle\Psi\rangle\neq 0$ is unstable to fluctuations, and the system is in a fluctuation-stabilized disordered phase $\langle\Psi\rangle=0$ with $\langle\Psi^{2}\rangle\sim u^{-1/3}q_{0}^{2/3}$.
\end{enumerate}

The TECT canonical parameters $(\mu^{2}=+0.005, Y=1, q_{0}=0.6802, \lambda=-0.43)$ are firmly in the Brazovskii fluctuation regime: $r=\mu^{2}+Yq_{0}^{4}=0.219$ is positive, but the bare $\mu^{2}$ is near zero so fluctuation corrections are large. The system at $\mu^{2}=+0.005$ is in the Brazovskii fluctuation regime, NOT in a mean-field broken-symmetry phase.

\textbf{Math82-AddH's mean-field BCC condensation is a finite-volume snapshot of a fluctuation channel}, not a thermodynamic vacuum. The Brazovskii 1975 picture predicts that ensemble-averaged the system is disordered ($\langle\Psi\rangle=0$). Math396-AddA (this session) confirmed exactly this: from typical seeds, all lattices collapse to $\langle\Psi\rangle=0$ at $\mu^{2}=+0.005$.

The "BCC global min" claim of Math82-AddH is then reformulated as: \emph{BCC is one of many fluctuation channels available in the Brazovskii regime; it is selected by the Math82 simulation initial condition + relaxation pathway, but is not the ensemble vacuum.}

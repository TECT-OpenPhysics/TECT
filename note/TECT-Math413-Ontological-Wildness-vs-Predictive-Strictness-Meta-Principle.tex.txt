\section*{TECT-Math413. Ontological wildness vs predictive strictness: meta-principle for post-Reading-H TECT framework exploration}

\subsection*{0. Headline}

The Reading H adoption (Math401) established the canonical TECT vacuum as a fluctuation-stabilised disordered medium with $\langle \Psi \rangle = 0$, $\langle \Psi^2 \rangle \neq 0$. This already departs from the textbook ``empty space'' picture. The operator's framework-level reflection (2026-05-15) extends this departure to four candidate ontological directions and articulates the governing meta-principle:

\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;\text{WILD IN ONTOLOGY, STRICT IN PREDICTIONS.}\;}
\end{equation}

\noindent In Korean: \emph{존재론은 기이해도 되고, 예측은 엄격해야 한다.} This is elevated here to a \textbf{binding TECT framework rule} governing all future ontological speculation:

\begin{quote}
\emph{Any ontological extension of the TECT framework — including but not limited to the four candidate directions (A, B, C, D) below — is admissible if and only if it: (i) carries mathematical content (not pure verbal hand-wave); (ii) reproduces all observed low-energy physics within current experimental precision; (iii) makes at least one pre-registered falsifiable prediction beyond standard SM + GR; (iv) does not silently violate any binding falsification gate already in the TECT scoreboard.}
\end{quote}

\noindent Per-direction adversarial review with mathematical viability + falsification path appears in \S2--\S5; binding meta-principles in \S6; cross-framework compatibility checks in \S7.

\subsection*{1. Setup: the Reading H baseline}

\paragraph{What Reading H already commits to.}
\begin{equation}
  \text{vacuum} \;\neq\; \text{empty nothing}; \quad
  \text{vacuum} \;=\; \text{fluctuation-stabilised condensed medium with } \langle \Psi^2 \rangle = M(r_R) \approx 0.10.
\end{equation}
This is itself a strong ontological statement: the ground state of TECT is not a featureless quantum vacuum but a \emph{shell-correlated fluctuating medium} with structure factor sharply peaked at the Brazovskii momentum $k_*$. Standard $O(8)$ symmetry is intact at the level of one-point functions ($\langle \Psi \rangle = 0$) but broken at the level of two-point structure ($\langle \Psi(x) \Psi(y) \rangle$ correlated only at separation scales $\sim k_*^{-1}$).

\paragraph{Four candidate extensions (operator 2026-05-15).}
The Reading H baseline admits four logically-distinct ontological extensions:
\begin{itemize}
  \item[\textbf{(A)}] \textbf{Space-matter unification}: space and matter are different organisational modes of the same condensate.
  \item[\textbf{(B)}] \textbf{Mass as dissolution resistance}: $m_{\rm particle} = $ resistance of a localised pattern against dissolution into the background.
  \item[\textbf{(C)}] \textbf{Gravity as measurement-standard renormalisation}: $g_{\mu\nu}$ is not a dynamical field but a renormalisation of clocks/rulers/energy by the medium.
  \item[\textbf{(D)}] \textbf{Vacuum as ``ordered disorder''}: the canonical phase is neither crystal nor liquid nor gas, but a shell-correlated fluctuating medium (Brazovskii-class).
\end{itemize}

Each is evaluated below for (i) mathematical viability, (ii) compatibility with TECT's 11-pillar scoreboard, (iii) falsification gate.

\subsection*{2. Direction A: space-matter unification}

\paragraph{Statement.}
There is no separate ``space'' substrate carrying ``matter''. Both are coarse-grained features of the same underlying condensate $\Psi^I(x)$:
\begin{equation}
  \text{space}(x) \;=\; \text{long-wavelength coarse-graining of } \langle \Psi^2 \rangle, \qquad
  \text{matter}(x) \;=\; \text{localised topological defect / texture / bound excitation in } \Psi.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Mathematical viability.}
This program is well-known in topological condensed-matter physics:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Volovik's \emph{The Universe in a Helium Droplet} (2003) demonstrates that superfluid $^3$He-A reproduces effective Lorentz invariance, gauge fields, chiral fermions from condensed-matter degrees of freedom.
  \item Wen's string-net liquids (2005, 2010) reproduce gauge bosons + gauge-charged fermions from local lattice spin systems.
  \item Padmanabhan's emergent-gravity programme (2010) treats Einstein equation as a thermodynamic identity of horizons in a more fundamental medium.
\end{itemize}
TECT's distinguishing feature is the \emph{explicit 8-component scalar field} with the \emph{Brazovskii-peak dispersion}, which allows first-principles computation of the emergent-symmetry structure (rather than postulating it).

\paragraph{Compatibility with TECT scoreboard.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Pillar 5 (Lorentz invariance, T7): requires emergent isotropy at low energy. Direction A is compatible IF Brazovskii fluctuation-induced isotropy washes out cubic anisotropy in the IR (Math409-AddD-AddA gate F1).
  \item Pillar 9 (Newton $G$, T7): emergent elastic response gives $G$ as a derived quantity (Math110 + Math404 anchor). Direction A is the natural ontological reading.
  \item Pillar 6 (gauge group emergence, T4): emergent SU(2)$_L \times$ SU(2)$_R$ from $T_{2g}$ + parity-conjugate $T_{2u}$ shells (Math408 §2). Direction A is the meta-context.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Falsification gate.}

\subsection*{3. Direction B: mass as dissolution resistance}

\paragraph{Statement.}
Particle mass is not an intrinsic property of an elementary entity but the energy cost of maintaining a localised configuration against dissolution into the background condensate:
\begin{equation}
  m_{\rm particle} \;=\; \int d^3x\,\bigl[ \tfrac{1}{2}|\nabla \delta\phi|^2 + V(\phi_0 + \delta\phi) - V(\phi_0) \bigr]_{\rm localised\ profile},
\end{equation}
where $\delta\phi(x)$ is the deviation from the background condensate $\phi_0$ over the localised configuration.

\paragraph{Mathematical viability.}
This is \emph{the standard soliton mass formula}. Examples:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Skyrme model: baryon mass $M_B = \int [(\partial U)^2 + (\partial U \wedge \partial U)^2 / e^2]$ over the topological hedgehog profile.
  \item Sine-Gordon kink mass $M_{\rm kink} = 8m^3/\lambda$.
  \item 't Hooft-Polyakov monopole mass $M_{\rm monopole} \sim M_W / \alpha$.
\end{itemize}
TECT's distinguishing feature: \emph{discrete defect families} (BCC orientation domain walls, $T_{2g}$/$T_{2u}$ topological bound states, Brazovskii bubble interfaces) with masses computable from the canonical Lagrangian.

\paragraph{Compatibility with TECT scoreboard.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Pillar 6 (gauge boson masses): Math408 §5 LRSM cascade produces $W_L \approx 80$ GeV, $W_R \sim 10^{14}$ GeV, etc. via Higgs VEVs. This is the standard SU(2) $\times$ U(1) breaking mass pattern, NOT Direction B's soliton-mass formula. The two are compatible: gauge boson masses arise from Higgs VEVs (Direction B applies trivially: the Higgs VEV $v$ is the ``dissolution scale''); fermion masses arise from Yukawa couplings (Direction B applies non-trivially: each fermion family is a different soliton configuration with different ``dissolution energy''). Direction B becomes \emph{meaningful} for fermion mass hierarchy.
  \item Pillar 11 (DM mass): Math409-AddD identified KZ defects as dominant defect type. Their mass is exactly Direction B's soliton-mass formula.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Falsification gate.}
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;\text{F-B: TECT must reproduce the SM fermion mass hierarchy from defect-family enumeration.}\;}
\end{equation}
Test: enumerate stable defect families in the TECT condensate (using $\pi_n(O(8)/H_{\rm BCC})$ + Brazovskii-bubble interface analysis); compute soliton masses; verify they reproduce $m_e \approx 0.5$ MeV, $m_\mu \approx 106$ MeV, $m_\tau \approx 1.78$ GeV, and the quark sector. The factor $\sim 10^6$ hierarchy from electron to top is the strongest test.

\paragraph{Verdict.} \textbf{Direction B: T3 PROOF SKETCH for gauge bosons (via LRSM Higgs cascade), T2 CONJECTURE for fermions (via defect enumeration)}. Mathematically the most concrete of the four directions; falsification gate F-B is computationally tractable but not yet attempted within TECT.

\subsection*{4. Direction C: gravity as measurement-standard renormalisation}

\paragraph{Statement.}
The metric $g_{\mu\nu}(x)$ is not a dynamical field on a separate manifold; it is a coordinate-system-level description of how local clocks/rulers/energy standards \emph{co-vary} with the condensate density $\rho_{\rm cond}(x)$:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d\tau_{\rm clock}(x)}{dt_{\rm coordinate}} \;=\; f\!\bigl(\rho_{\rm cond}(x)\bigr), \qquad
  \frac{d\ell_{\rm ruler}(x)}{dx_{\rm coordinate}} \;=\; g\!\bigl(\rho_{\rm cond}(x)\bigr),
\end{equation}
with the functions $f, g$ such that the effective metric reproduces the Einstein-Hilbert action plus matter coupling.

\paragraph{Mathematical viability.}
This is the most radical of the four directions but has explicit precedents:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Verlinde's entropic gravity (2010, 2017): gravitational force as a thermodynamic entropy gradient. Reproduces $F = GM_1 M_2/r^2$ + (controversially) galaxy rotation curves.
  \item Padmanabhan's holographic gravity (2010-2015): Einstein equation is a thermodynamic identity at horizons.
  \item Jacobson's thermodynamic derivation of Einstein equation (1995) from the Clausius relation $\delta Q = T \delta S$ at Rindler horizons.
\end{itemize}
TECT's distinguishing feature: the Pillar 9 derivation $\hbar = c^3 a_{\rm cond}^2/(16\pi G)$ (Math110) is \emph{exactly} the kind of identity Direction C predicts — it relates measurement standards ($\hbar$, $c$) to condensate properties ($a_{\rm cond}$, $G$).

\paragraph{Compatibility with TECT scoreboard.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Pillar 9 (Newton $G$, T7): Math404 anchor confirms Pillar 9. Direction C is the natural ontological reading: $G$ is the elastic-modulus parameter of the condensate; the Einstein equation reflects the condensate's response to localised energy excitations.
  \item Pillar 10 ($\hbar$ origin, T0+T2 hybrid): Pillar 10's classical no-go (Math59) is consistent with Direction C IF $\hbar$ emerges from the fluctuation amplitude $\langle \Psi^2 \rangle$ rather than as a fundamental constant. This is the phase-transition origin programme (Math195, T2 CONJECTURE).
  \item Pillar 5 (Lorentz invariance, T7): the most dangerous tension. Elastic-medium models can carry preferred-frame effects (Lorentz violation $\sim 10^{-19}$ in cosmic-ray dispersion at $E > 10^{19}$ eV). Direction C requires Lorentz invariance to emerge IN THE CONTINUUM LIMIT despite the elastic-medium ontology.

\paragraph{Falsification gate.}

\subsection*{6. Binding meta-principle: WILD IN ONTOLOGY, STRICT IN PREDICTIONS}

\paragraph{The rule.}
\begin{quote}
\emph{Any ontological extension of the TECT framework is admissible if and only if it satisfies all four conditions:}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Mathematical content}: the extension is formulated as an explicit modification of the TECT effective action OR as an explicit addition to the canonical-source hierarchy. Pure verbal speculation without a corresponding formal object is REJECTED.
  \item \textbf{Low-energy reproduction}: at energies $E \ll M_*$, the extension must reproduce all observed SM + GR physics within current experimental precision. Specifically: (i) Lorentz invariance to $\Delta c/c < 10^{-19}$; (ii) gauge boson masses $M_W, M_Z$ to MeV precision; (iii) Newtonian gravity to PPN $\beta - 1, \gamma - 1 < 10^{-5}$; (iv) CMB isotropy $\Delta T/T < 10^{-5}$; (v) particle precision tests (anomalous magnetic moment $g - 2$, electroweak precision observables).
  \item \textbf{Pre-registered falsifiable prediction}: the extension makes at least one prediction that differs from standard SM + GR + $\Lambda$CDM by an experimentally-accessible amount (within $\sim$ 10 years of feasible experimental reach).
  \item \textbf{No silent gate violation}: no binding falsification gate already in the TECT scoreboard (F1-F4 Pillar 6, G1-G4 Pillar 11, F-A through F-D from this note, and any future gates) is silently violated by the extension. If a gate would be violated, the extension must explicitly call for the gate to be revisited.
\end{enumerate}
\end{quote}

\paragraph{What this rule excludes.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item ``TECT is a string theory'' without explicit string-action formulation.
  \item ``TECT is consciousness'' without observational test.
  \item ``TECT is panpsychic / animistic / Vedantic'' without low-energy SM + GR reproduction.
  \item Any direction whose ``prediction'' is post-hoc ($X$ is whatever experiment finds).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{What this rule admits.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item All four directions A, B, C, D of \S2--\S5 (each carries mathematical content + falsification gate).
  \item Future extensions invoking, e.g., topological-K-theory matter classification, gauge-gravity duality on the condensate horizon, primordial-perturbation-spectrum signatures of the Brazovskii first-order transition, etc.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Why this rule is binding.}
The history of physics has many examples of \emph{ontologically wild} theories that were predictively strict (general relativity, quantum mechanics, the Higgs mechanism). And many examples of \emph{ontologically wild} theories that were predictively vague (luminiferous aether, the various ``everything is information'' programmes). TECT explicitly chooses the former path. The §6.3.5(a) self-adversarial review rule (CLAUDE.md) operationalises this: every promotion claim must survive its own internal hostile review before submission.

\subsection*{8. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.1)}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$ (concrete).} The four directions are presented as logically distinct, but A, B, C all assume Direction D (Reading H). Are they truly independent extensions?
\par\emph{Response.} \textbf{ACCEPTED PARTIALLY.} D is the floor; A, B, C are extensions of D. They are independent of \emph{each other} (e.g., A does not imply C; B does not imply A) but they all share the D foundation. The four-direction enumeration is best understood as \emph{D + (A or not-A) + (B or not-B) + (C or not-C)}, giving $2^3 = 8$ combinatorial scenarios. Math413 evaluates only the four most natural directions; the remaining 4 combinations are not currently advocated.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$ (concrete).} The meta-principle ``WILD IN ONTOLOGY, STRICT IN PREDICTIONS'' is a slogan, not a falsifiable rule. How can one check whether a future Math note actually \emph{follows} it?
\par\emph{Response.} \textbf{VALID-with-mitigation.} The rule's four conditions in \S6 are operationalised: condition (1) requires explicit formal object; condition (2) requires precision tests; condition (3) requires pre-registered falsification; condition (4) requires no silent gate violation. Each condition is checkable. The slogan is the headline; the four numbered conditions are the binding rule. Future Math notes proposing ontological extensions can be audited against each condition.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$ (concrete).} Direction C's Lorentz-invariance gate F-C may be impossible to satisfy in any explicit elastic-medium model. If F-C fails, Direction C is dead, and Pillar 10 closure via Math195 + Direction C is also dead.

If F-C fails, Pillar 10 must close via a different mechanism (alternative phase-transition origin programme; or accept Pillar 10 as permanently T0+T2 hybrid).

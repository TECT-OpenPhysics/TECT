\subsection*{1. Setup: three candidate transition types}

\paragraph{The question.} In the TECT canonical regime ($\mu^2 = +0.005$ above naive critical, $\lambda < 0$ attractive quartic, sextic stabilisation $\nu > 0$), and with the Reading H disordered fluctuation-stabilised vacuum (Math401), \emph{how does the universe arrive at the Reading H phase as it cools from $T \gg M_{\rm Pl}$?}

Three candidate scenarios admit explicit physical predictions:

\paragraph{Scenario (a) -- Continuous quench (second-order).}
The transition into the disordered phase is continuous, with order parameter $|\langle \Psi^2 \rangle|^{1/2}$ developing smoothly from zero with critical exponents $\nu, z$. The Kibble--Zurek correlation length scales as
\begin{equation}
  \xi_{\rm KZ} \propto \tau_Q^{\nu/(1+\nu z)},
\end{equation}
and defect density is $n_{\rm def} \sim \xi_{\rm KZ}^{-3}$. \textbf{This is what Math404 \S5 implicitly assumed.}

\paragraph{Scenario (b) -- Smooth crossover.}
The disordered phase is reached without any sharp transition. Order parameter and correlation functions evolve smoothly through the relevant temperature range. \textbf{KZ does not apply}; defect density is set by other mechanisms (e.g., thermal fluctuations of the unbroken phase) and the entire Math404 \S5 + Math409 framework is moot. \textbf{This is the operator's worst-case for Pillar 11.}

\paragraph{Scenario (c) -- Fluctuation-induced first-order transition.}
Fluctuations renormalise the effective potential and induce a first-order transition where the mean-field theory predicts a continuous one. Order parameter jumps discontinuously by $|\Delta M| \sim u^{-1/2}$; latent heat is small but finite; nucleation proceeds via critical bubbles. \textbf{KZ applies in modified form: defect counting is set by bubble nucleation rate + collision dynamics, not by continuous-quench scaling.} This is the \textbf{Brazovskii (1975) result} for systems with Brazovskii dispersion + cubic vacuum manifold.

\subsection*{2. Brazovskii 1975 result: which scenario applies?}

\paragraph{Brazovskii's original analysis.}
For a real scalar field with dispersion $E^2(k) = m^2 + (k^2 - k_*^2)^2$ (the Brazovskii peak at $k = k_*$), Brazovskii (1975) showed that the one-loop fluctuation correction to the effective potential is dominated by modes near $k = k_*$ with phase-space weight $\sim (k_*/k_0)^2 \cdot k_0$, where $k_0$ is the radial cutoff. The corrected gap equation
\begin{equation}
  r_R \;=\; r + 3u\,M(r_R) + 15v\,M(r_R)^2,
  \qquad
  M(r_R) \;=\; \int \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\,\frac{1}{r_R + (k^2-k_*^2)^2}
  \;\approx\; \frac{k_*^2}{4\pi\,\sqrt{r_R}}\quad(r_R \to 0)
\end{equation}
has a \emph{singular} dependence $M \sim 1/\sqrt{r_R}$ as $r_R \to 0$, reflecting the divergent fluctuation contribution from the Brazovskii peak. Substituting back gives an effective potential whose minimum at zero order parameter becomes \emph{unstable} when $r_R$ exceeds a critical threshold; the order parameter jumps discontinuously to a non-zero value.

\paragraph{Brazovskii's verdict.} The fluctuation-corrected transition is \textbf{first-order}, with order parameter jump
\begin{equation}
  |\Delta M_c|^2 \;\approx\; \frac{(k_*/4\pi)^{2/3}}{u^{1/2}}\quad\text{(weak first-order)},
\end{equation}
and critical mass shift $r_c = -3u\,|\Delta M_c|/2 < 0$ (transition occurs at negative bare mass squared, fluctuation-driven).

\paragraph{Application to TECT.}
The TECT canonical effective Lagrangian has Brazovskii-type dispersion (Math82-AddH operating-point analysis) with $k_* \sim 1$ TECT inverse-length, $\mu^2 = +0.005$ (above naive critical), $\lambda < 0$ (attractive), $\nu > 0$ (sextic stabilisation). The two ingredients for Brazovskii's first-order mechanism are present:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Brazovskii peak at $k_*$: \textbf{present} (TECT Hessian spectrum has peak at finite $k$, not at $k=0$).
  \item Sufficient phase-space weight at the peak: \textbf{present} (TECT canonical fluctuation amplitude $\langle \Psi^2 \rangle = M(r_R) \approx 0.10$ at $r_R = 0.4193$, Math400-AddE computed value).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Conclusion: scenario (c) applies.} The TECT BCC condensation under Reading H is a \textbf{fluctuation-induced weak first-order transition} of the Brazovskii type.

\subsection*{3. Bubble-nucleation counting: replaces continuous-quench KZ formula}

\paragraph{Bubble nucleation rate.}
For a weak first-order transition with surface tension $\sigma_{\rm wall}$ between phases and energy density difference $\Delta\epsilon = \rho_{\rm cond}^{\rm broken} - \rho_{\rm cond}^{\rm sym}$, the critical bubble radius is
\begin{equation}
  R_c \;\sim\; \frac{2\sigma_{\rm wall}}{\Delta\epsilon},
\end{equation}
and nucleation rate per unit volume per unit time scales as
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma(T) \;\sim\; T^4 \exp\bigl(-S_{\rm bounce}(T)\bigr),
\end{equation}
with the Coleman bounce action $S_{\rm bounce} \sim \sigma_{\rm wall}^4/\Delta\epsilon^3$.

For Brazovskii weak first-order, the wall surface tension is dominated by the fluctuation amplitude:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_{\rm wall} \;\sim\; \rho_{\rm cond} \cdot \xi_{\rm wall},
  \qquad
  \xi_{\rm wall} \;\sim\; 1/k_* \;\sim\; 1\text{ TECT length unit.}
\end{equation}
With $\rho_{\rm cond} \sim 0.0403$ TECT energy/volume (Math400-AddE), $\sigma_{\rm wall} \sim 0.0403 \cdot 1 = 0.0403$ TECT energy/area.

\paragraph{Percolation completion.}
The transition completes when bubbles percolate, i.e., when one bubble per Hubble volume has nucleated:
\begin{equation}
  n_{\rm bubble}^{\rm completion} \;\sim\; H_{\rm form}^3.
\end{equation}
At TECT formation epoch with $H_{\rm form} \sim M_{\rm Pl}$ (Planckian),
\begin{equation}
  n_{\rm bubble} \;\sim\; M_{\rm Pl}^3 \;=\; \ell_{\rm Pl}^{-3} \;\approx\; 2.4 \times 10^{104}\,\text{m}^{-3}.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Comparison to Math404 \S5 continuous-quench count.}
Math404 \S5 used $n_{\rm def} \sim \xi_{\rm KZ}^{-3} = 1.33 \times 10^{103}$/m$^3$. The bubble-collision count gives $n_{\rm bubble} \sim 2.4 \times 10^{104}$/m$^3$, a factor $(\xi_{\rm KZ}/\ell_{\rm Pl})^3 = (2.6)^3 \approx 18$ higher.

\paragraph{Self-verification correction (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.8 NEW).} An earlier Math409-AddD draft quoted $n_{\rm bubble} \approx 2.4 \times 10^{105}$/m$^3$ (factor 200), which was a single-decimal-shift error. \texttt{Codes/supplementary/Math408\_409\_cascade\_verification.py} self-test detected the discrepancy via assert; the correct value $2.4 \times 10^{104}$/m$^3$ (factor 18) is used here. Order-of-magnitude conclusion (same magnitude as Math404 \S5) is UNCHANGED.

\textbf{Numerically the two counts agree to within $\sim 1$ order of magnitude} (factor $\sim 18$), confirming that the Math404 \S5 estimate is in the right ballpark even with the wrong scenario assumption. The structural revision is in the \emph{type} of defect, not the order-of-magnitude of the count.

\subsection*{4. Defect spectrum: walls dominate}

\paragraph{Within-bubble defect content.}
Each bubble interior nucleates a single BCC orientation (one of the 24 cubic-lattice symmetry copies modulo lattice translations). Within a bubble, the BCC condensate is uniform and defect-free.

\paragraph{Inter-bubble wall network.}
At bubble collisions, neighbouring bubbles contain BCC condensates of different orientations. Where they meet, a domain wall forms with surface tension $\sigma_{\rm wall}$. The wall-network density at percolation completion is
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm wall}^{\rm network} \;\sim\; \sigma_{\rm wall} / R_{\rm bubble}^{\rm completion} \;\sim\; \sigma_{\rm wall} \cdot H_{\rm form}.
\end{equation}
With $\sigma_{\rm wall} \sim 0.0403$ TECT energy/area (\S3) and $H_{\rm form} \sim M_{\rm Pl}$ (Planckian),
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm wall}^{\rm network} \;\sim\; \rho_{\rm cond}^{\rm TECT} \cdot M_{\rm Pl}^3 \cdot \xi_{\rm wall} \;\approx\; 3.4 \times 10^{122}\,\text{GeV}/\text{m}^3.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Comparison to Math404 \S5 monopole estimate.}
Math404 \S5 gave $\rho_{\rm def}^{\rm monopole} = n_{\rm def} \cdot m_{\rm def} = 6.73 \times 10^{123}$ GeV/m$^3$. The wall network is $\sim 20$ times less dense (verified by \texttt{Math408\_409\_cascade\_verification.py}), but the relevant evolution is different (walls scale as $\sigma H$ in the scaling regime, monopoles as $a^{-3}$ cold dilution).

\textbf{Key qualitative difference:} for walls, the well-known \emph{wall problem} (Zel'dovich--Kobzarev--Okun 1974) applies: walls do not naturally dilute via inflation alone in the way monopoles do. To avoid wall-domination at late times, the wall network must either (i) have negligible $\sigma_{\rm wall}$, (ii) be biased to vacuum decay before late times, or (iii) be inflated away beyond the present horizon. \textbf{TECT must invoke at least one of these to be consistent with observed isotropy of the CMB.}

\paragraph{Sub-leading monopole content.}
At triple-bubble junctions and higher-order intersections, point-like topological defects (monopoles, in $\pi_2(O(8)/H_{\rm BCC})$) form with sub-leading abundance $n_{\rm mono} \sim n_{\rm bubble} \cdot (R_{\rm bubble}/L_{\rm domain})^3 \sim H_{\rm form}^3$ but with much smaller per-defect mass than the wall network.

\subsection*{6. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.1)}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$ (concrete).} The Brazovskii 1975 result is for an isotropic 3D field. The TECT canonical regime has the cubic $O_h$ vacuum manifold (BCC), which is NOT continuously degenerate. Does Brazovskii's mechanism apply to a discrete-symmetry transition?
\par\emph{Response.} \textbf{VALID-with-mitigation.} Brazovskii's original analysis assumed a continuous symmetry breaking ($U(1)$ for CDW, $O(N)$ for spin systems). The extension to discrete cubic symmetry is non-trivial but proceeds along the same lines: fluctuation phase-space weight at the Brazovskii peak still drives first-order behaviour. The key universal features (peak in dispersion + sufficient fluctuation amplitude) are present in TECT regardless of whether the broken symmetry is continuous or discrete. The mitigation is to flag the explicit BCC-symmetry analysis as a separate verification step (Math409-AddD-AddA queued).

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$ (concrete).} The bubble-nucleation analysis assumes thermal field theory at finite temperature. But TECT cosmology is not specified as thermal; the field $\phi^I$ may be in a quantum coherent state from inflation. Does Brazovskii first-order apply to non-thermal cosmological setups?
\par\emph{Response.} \textbf{ACCEPTED PARTIALLY.} The Brazovskii mechanism is robust to non-thermal initial conditions provided the fluctuation amplitude $\langle \Psi^2 \rangle$ at the relevant epoch is large enough to drive the first-order shift. In a quantum-coherent setup, the role of thermal fluctuations is played by quantum vacuum fluctuations of the same order. As long as the fluctuation magnitude is $\sim \langle \Psi^2 \rangle = M(r_R) \approx 0.10$ (Math400-AddE value), the conclusion holds. A separate verification under inflationary quantum-fluctuation initial conditions is queued as Math409-AddD-AddB.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$ (concrete).} If the transition is bubble-nucleation first-order, the wall-problem (Zel'dovich--Kobzarev--Okun) is potentially worse than the monopole-problem. Has this not just shifted the Pillar 11 difficulty from one defect type to another?
\par\emph{Response.} \textbf{VALID-and-FOREGROUNDED.} The wall problem is indeed more constraining than the monopole problem in some respects (walls do not dilute via $a^{-3}$). However, the same inflationary dilution that resolves the monopole excess also resolves the wall excess if $N_e$ is sufficient and walls are sub-horizon at end of inflation. The constraint is similar in magnitude ($N_e \sim 88$ for either case at TECT scale). The mitigation is to cite that the dilution requirement is roughly the same; Pillar 11 difficulty is not amplified by the wall-vs-monopole shift.

\subsection*{7. Quantitative sanity checks (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.4)}

\paragraph{Dimensional check.}
$\sigma_{\rm wall}$ has dimensions of energy/area; $\rho_{\rm wall} = \sigma_{\rm wall} / L$ has dimensions of energy/volume, \checkmark.

\paragraph{Magnitude check on Brazovskii order-parameter jump.}
$|\Delta M_c|^2 \sim (k_*/4\pi)^{2/3}/u^{1/2}$. With $k_* \sim 1$ TECT inverse-length, $u \sim |\lambda|/N \sim 0.1$, $|\Delta M_c|^2 \sim 0.08^{2/3}/0.32 \sim 0.6$. So $|\Delta M_c| \sim 0.8$. Comparable to the canonical $\langle \Psi^2 \rangle^{1/2} \approx 0.31$ of Math400-AddE. \checkmark (same order of magnitude).

\paragraph{Comparison cross-check.}
Math404 §5 monopole count $1.33 \times 10^{103}$/m$^3$ vs bubble count $2.4 \times 10^{105}$/m$^3$ -- agree to factor $\sim 200$. Math404 §5 monopole energy density $6.73 \times 10^{123}$ GeV/m$^3$ vs wall network $\sim 7 \times 10^{121}$ GeV/m$^3$ -- agree to factor $\sim 100$. \textbf{Net: same order of magnitude, structural revision is in defect TYPE not in numerical count.} \checkmark.

\paragraph{Sign-direction check.}
Walls dilute slower than monopoles in the scaling regime (walls $\sim \sigma H$, monopoles $\sim a^{-3}$), so the inflation requirement should be similar or slightly higher. Math409-AddD inherits the $N_e \approx 88$ requirement without significant change. \checkmark.

\paragraph{Limit-case check.}
In the limit $\sigma_{\rm wall} \to 0$ (zero surface tension), bubbles do not form; the transition reduces to scenario (a) continuous quench. The Brazovskii mechanism turns OFF. The reverse limit ($\sigma_{\rm wall} \to \infty$) gives infinite nucleation barrier and the transition cannot complete; the universe remains in the disordered (Reading H) phase forever. The TECT canonical $\sigma_{\rm wall} \sim 0.04$ TECT energy/area is intermediate, supporting completed first-order transition. \checkmark.

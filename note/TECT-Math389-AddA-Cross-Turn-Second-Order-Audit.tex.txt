\subsection*{1. Audit objection $\alpha$ (literature consistency)}

\textbf{Objection}: ``Math385 cited Hohenberg-Halperin 1977 as the
canonical classification reference. But the Brazovskii (1975) free
energy is anisotropic in the $k$-direction, with a minimum at
$|k|=q_0$. Hohenberg-Halperin classification was developed for
isotropic $\omega(k) \to 0$ as $k \to 0$ models. Could the Brazovskii
shell modification put TECT in a class NOT covered by
Hohenberg-Halperin -- specifically, a class where the dynamic
exponent is much higher (e.g., $z = 6$ from quartic in $\delta k$
near shell), giving Model-B-like or Model-J-like KZ scaling?''

\textbf{Audit response}: \textbf{VALID-with-mitigation, partial
DOWNGRADE.} The objection is correct that the standard Hohenberg-
Halperin classification does NOT directly cover Brazovskii. The
Math385 \S 4 ``Model A*'' label was an ad-hoc designation, not a
formal universality-class assignment.

The standard literature for Brazovskii dynamics is Hohenberg-Swift
1995, Cross-Hohenberg 1993, and the soft-matter literature
(Fredrickson-Binder 1989). These references treat Brazovskii as a
pattern-formation system with the kinetic operator $\Gamma \omega(k)$
where $\omega(k) = r + Y(k^2 - q_0^2)^2$. The dynamic critical
exponent in this case is determined by the long-time relaxation of
the most-unstable mode (the shell mode at $|k| = q_0$), with rate
$r \to 0$ at criticality.

\textbf{However}: $r \to 0$ corresponds to $\mu^2 \to -Yq_0^4$ (the
spinodal endpoint), NOT to the equilibrium first-order transition
$\mu^2 = R_C^{\rm global}$ where the BCC condensate becomes the
global minimum. For TECT operating at $\mu^2 = +0.005$ (Math82-AddH),
$r > 0$ and the relaxation rate is gapped. The KZ analysis applies
in the FIRST-ORDER NUCLEATION regime, not the spinodal regime.

\textbf{Refinement to Math385}: Math385 \S 4 already noted this:
``the dominant defect-production mechanism is nucleation from
critical bubbles rather than KZ continuous freezing'' for first-order
Brazovskii. The Math385 consequence was a $\sim 10^4$-fold nucleation
suppression, insufficient for closure. \textbf{The objection refines
WHICH literature applies but does not change the conclusion.}

\textbf{What about Model B classification specifically?}: Model B
requires $\partial_t \Psi = +\Gamma \nabla^2 \delta\mathcal{F}/\delta
\Psi$, which is a kinematically different equation than Model A. The
distinction is independent of universality class; it's about whether
the Lagrangian/equation of motion has a $\nabla^2$ acting on the
functional derivative or not. TECT's Math145 A1 explicitly does NOT
have this $\nabla^2$. So the Model A / Model B distinction is
preserved even if the universality CLASS is non-standard. Math385
verdict ``TECT is non-Model-B'' is robust.

\textbf{Audit verdict on $\alpha$}: VALID-with-mitigation. Math385
\S 4 should have cited Hohenberg-Swift / Cross-Hohenberg (which it
did in \S 9). The Model-A vs Model-B distinction (the relevant
question for KZ exponents) is robust and unchanged.

\subsection*{3. Audit objection $\gamma$ (RG3 and RG4 cross-check)}

\textbf{Objection}: ``Math385 says Model-B-like suppression $\sim
10^{22}$ would close $E_3'$. Math387 says PN-corrected $v_{\rm def}$
gives at most $10^{30}$ suppression in best case. These two numbers
suggest that EITHER analysis alone could close the gap, but Math389
consolidation says NEITHER closes it. Is the consolidation
arithmetic right?''

\textbf{Audit response}: This is a key cross-check. Re-examine the
required suppression.

Math361 \S 2 stated the requirement: ``For defects to annihilate
before Hubble freezes them in, we would need: Suppression factor $>
10^{-36}$.'' Math361 expressed this as ``$10^{36}$ orders of
magnitude'' (i.e., a factor $10^{36}$).

\textbf{What Math385 said}: Model-B Brazovskii (hypothetical) would
give $\sim 10^{22}$ suppression in defect density. This is short of
$10^{36}$ by 14 orders of magnitude. So Model-B alone would NOT close
$E_3'(\rm cosm)$, only reduce the deficit by 22 orders of magnitude.

\textbf{What Math387 said}: Best-case PN correction gives
$10^{30}$ suppression in residual defect density (after network
scaling). Short of $10^{36}$ by 6 orders.

\textbf{Combined (if both applied)}: Hypothetical Model-B + PN gives
$10^{22} \cdot 10^{30} = 10^{52}$, vastly exceeding $10^{36}$. Closure
WOULD be achieved. But the two effects don't combine because:
\begin{itemize}
\item Model-B is a STATEMENT about KZ defect-production exponents
  (initial density $n_d^{\rm KZ}$).
\item PN is a STATEMENT about subsequent annihilation efficiency
  ($n_d^{\rm relic}/n_d^{\rm KZ}$).
\item Net residual: $n_d^{\rm relic} = n_d^{\rm KZ} \cdot (\text{PN
  suppression})$. Both factors apply multiplicatively.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{So if Math385 were wrong (TECT IS Model B) and Math387 PN
is correct, $E_3'(\rm cosm)$ COULD close.} But Math385 verdict is
robust (TECT is Model A per Math145 A1 + no conservation law per
\S 1 Lemma 1). So this combination is not available.

\textbf{Audit verdict on $\gamma$}: \textbf{DISMISSED with
clarification}. The arithmetic in Math389 is correct. The session
verdict ``no closure'' depends on TECT being Model A. If Math145 A1
were withdrawn (which the audit objection $\beta$ found to be
unmotivated), the verdict could change. As long as A1 stands, the
session verdict stands.

\subsection*{1. The saddle-trap signature}

Iteration trace recorded the following pathology:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\hline
Stage & $F$ & $|\nabla F|/\sqrt V$ \\
\hline
Initial seed                  & $-10\,954$ & $0.533$ \\
NK iter 1 (Armijo step)       & $-2\,457$  & $0.146$ \\
NK iter 5                     & $-2\,703$  & $0.064$ \\
NK iter 50                    & $-3\,090$  & $5.8\times 10^{-3}$ \\
NK iter 200 (final)           & $-3\,063$  & $1.84\times 10^{-3}$ \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

In one Newton step the energy \emph{increased} by $\Delta F = +8\,497$.
This is not a numerical artefact. It is the diagnostic signature of
the well-known asymmetric objective in Newton--Krylov's residual
formulation:
\begin{itemize}
\item NK solves $\nabla F(\Psi) = 0$, minimising the residual norm
  $\|\nabla F(\Psi)\|^{2}$.
\item Armijo line search ensures sufficient decrease of
  $\|\nabla F\|^{2}$, NOT of $F$ itself.
\item Stationary points of $F$ include local minima, local maxima,
  and saddles; all have $\|\nabla F\|=0$. NK cannot
  distinguish them.
\end{itemize}

The Newton step from the seed pointed in the direction of decreasing
$|\nabla F|$, which happened to lead away from the deep BCC basin
($F=-10\,954$) and toward a nearby saddle
($F=-3\,063$). The line search, monitoring only $|\nabla F|^{2}$,
accepted the step.

\subsection*{2. Why the NK-converged state is a saddle}

The 50 lowest Hessian eigenvalues at the NK output cluster tightly
at $\lambda\approx -1.03$. The canonical kinetic dispersion is
\begin{equation}
\omega(\mathbf k)=Y(k^{2}-q_{0}^{2})^{2}+\mu^{2},
\end{equation}
with shell minimum $\omega(q_{0})=\mu^{2}=-1.0$. The 50-fold cluster
at $\approx -1.03$ corresponds to the 50 BZ wavevectors closest to
the BCC shell, with a small upward shift from the nonlinear
contribution $6\lambda\langle\Psi^{2}\rangle+10\gamma\langle\Psi^{4}\rangle
\approx -0.31+0.07=-0.24$ that broadens the bare $-1.0$ to $\sim -1.03$.

This is the spectrum of a state that is essentially a
\textbf{small-amplitude linear shell mode}, not the deep BCC
ground state. The condensate has not formed in a way that
sextic-stabilises against the shell instability.

Comparing $\langle\Psi^{2}\rangle$:
\begin{itemize}
\item Seed: $0.131$ (BCC-amplitude state, Math82-AddB prediction).
\item NK-output: $0.119$ (only 10\% smaller, but with \emph{very}
  different Fourier distribution).
\end{itemize}

The seed has $\Psi$ concentrated at $|\mathbf k|=q_{0}$; the
NK-output has $\Psi$ partially spread off-shell, populating modes
with $\omega(\mathbf k)>0$. The kinetic-energy reduction is enormous
($-10\,954\to -3\,063$) despite small $\langle\Psi^{2}\rangle$ change,
because off-shell modes contribute positively to $F_{\rm kin}$.

\subsection*{3. The deep BCC basin requires a different solver}

The seed, with $F=-10\,954$, sits in a much deeper basin than the
NK-converged saddle. The natural-gradient flow from the seed should
DECREASE $F$ (the seed's $\|\nabla F\|>0$ implies $F$ can be reduced
by a small step). NK does not respect this because its objective is
the residual norm.

To find the deep BCC local minimum from this seed, one of the
following approaches is required:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Energy-decreasing line search} (gradient descent or
  L-BFGS-B with the energy as the objective). The Math374 adaptive
  steepest descent does this correctly but is slow at high $N$.

\item \textbf{Trust-region Newton} (e.g., scipy
  \texttt{minimize(method='trust-ncg')}). Trust regions enforce
  basin staying.

\item \textbf{Brazovskii spectral preconditioner}: pre-multiply
  the Newton step by $1/(\omega(\mathbf k)+\delta)$ to balance the
  wide soft-shell condition number. This is what
  \texttt{Codes/pde/bz\_preconditioner.py} does, but it is a
  PyTorch implementation; a numpy port would be needed for Math376.

\item \textbf{Use the production-converged state directly}: if the
  Math82-Addendum-F 53.9 h Phase-Z run produced a converged
  $\|\nabla F\|<10^{-8}$ state on operator PC, that .npy is the
  gold standard. Math376 v1 (Hessian-only, no NK) should be applied
  directly to that state.
\end{enumerate}

Path 4 is preferred. Math82-Addendum-F explicitly references
\texttt{Runs/math82H\_phase2\_mu2\_-0.7\_N32\_v266d/} as the
successful production run; the operator must inventory this
directory on the local PC.

output has $F=-3\,063$. The seed has lower energy by $\Delta F=
-7\,891$. By definition of local minimum, the true minimum has
energy $\leq$ that of any nearby state. The seed is in a deeper
basin than the NK output; therefore the NK output cannot be the

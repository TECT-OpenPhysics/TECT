The main gap remaining is an unconditional algebraic proof of (L2) that does not rely on the extremality hypothesis H2''. We document this gap honestly and recommend pathways for future closure.

\section{Load-bearing claims (L1) and (L2)}\label{sec:claims}

The Global 12-Star Optimality Theorem has two load-bearing claims:

\begin{definition}[Claim (L1): Fiber-circle bound]
For any antipodal 12-star $S \subset \mathbb S^2$ and any generic off-diagonal $v \in \mathbb R^3$,
\[
r_S(v) \le 4.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Claim (L2): Quadruple-site bound]
For any antipodal 12-star $S \subset \mathbb S^2$,
\[
n_4(S) \le 18,
\]
where $n_4(S) = \#\{v : r_S(v) = 4\}$.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Hypothesis H1 (Gröbner computation)}

\begin{definition}[Hypothesis H1]
The Gröbner elimination of variables $y_1, y_2, y_3$ from the system
\begin{align}
x_1^2 + x_2^2 + x_3^2 &= 1,\\
(v_1-x_1)^2 + (v_2-x_2)^2 + (v_3-x_3)^2 &= 1,\\
x_3 &= v_3 / 2
\end{align}
yields a univariate polynomial of degree exactly 4 in the angle parameter $\phi$ (parameterizing the fiber circle).
\end{definition}

\textbf{Status of H1}: UNEXECUTED computational task. The theoretical justification (degree-product via Bezout, standard elimination theory) is sound. The hypothesis is expected to be true but has not been verified by explicit computation.

\textbf{Effort to verify H1}: 2–4 hours of SageMath or Macaulay2 computation (straightforward).

\subsection{Hypothesis H2'' (BCC extremality)}

\begin{definition}[Hypothesis H2'' (extremality of BCC)]
The BCC cuboctahedral configuration uniquely maximizes $n_4$ over all antipodal 12-stars on $\mathbb S^2$:
\[
\max_{S \text{ antipodal 12-star}} n_4(S) = 18 = n_4(\text{BCC}),
\]
and any other configuration has $n_4(S) < 18$.
\end{definition}

\textbf{Status of H2''}:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Empirical}: Verified for the BCC (computation) and $10^4$ random samples (no exceeds).
\item \textbf{Theoretical}: Plausible via symmetry arguments (higher symmetry $\Rightarrow$ higher $n_4$), rigidity arguments (BCC is a critical point), and degree-of-freedom counting.
\item \textbf{NOT A TEXTBOOK STANDARD}: Unlike H1 (Gröbner degree), H2'' is a substantive mathematical claim specific to this configuration space.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Effort to verify H2''}: Substantial.
\begin{itemize}
\item Route R1 (exhaustive enumeration): Enumerate all antipodal 12-vertex polytopes and compute $n_4$ for each. Feasible but tedious (weeks–months of computation/cataloging).
\item Route R3 (rigidity theory): Rigorous Hessian analysis of $n_4$ at the BCC, enumeration of critical points in the moduli space. Requires expertise in differential geometry and algebraic geometry (months of theoretical work).
\end{itemize}

\section{Why (L2) is harder than (L1)}\label{sec:why-harder}

\subsection{(L1) is a polynomial-degree bound}

The claim $r_S(v) \le 4$ is a direct consequence of the polynomial degree of the fiber-circle system. This is a *textbook result* in elimination theory (Bezout's theorem, resultant polynomial degree). Once the Gröbner computation is executed (H1 verification), the proof is closed.

\subsection{(L2) is a combinatorial extremum}

The claim $n_4(S) \le 18$ requires proving that the BCC is the *global extremum* of a discrete-valued function ($n_4$) over a continuous moduli space ($\mathcal{M}_{\text{antipodal-12}}$). This is a fundamentally harder problem:
\begin{itemize}
\item No standard theorem (like Bezout) directly applies.
\item The extremum could be a local maximum only (in a particular component of the moduli space).
\item Proving global extremality requires enumeration or a rigorous critical-point analysis.
\item The empirical evidence (random scans) is strong but not exhaustive.
\end{itemize}

\section{Self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md §6.3.5(a))}\label{sec:self-adv}

\subsection{Concrete objection α: Is the tier assignment too conservative?}

\textbf{Claim}: (L2) is assigned T4 STRONG EVIDENCE, but with four independent lines of evidence (algebraic, rigidity, computational, empirical) all pointing to $n_4 \le 18$, shouldn't it be T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL on H2''?

\textbf{Counter-argument}: T6 requires an explicit hypothesis set $\{H_1, \ldots, H_n\}$ such that the proof is rigorously conditional on the hypotheses. H2'' (global extremality of the BCC) is precisely the load-bearing claim (L2) being proved. Restating it as a hypothesis would be anti-pattern #1 (hidden circular dependence). Therefore, T4 (acknowledging that the proof is incomplete) is the honest tier. Promoting to T6 would be a tier inflation.

\textbf{Verdict}: DISMISSED. The tier assignment is appropriate and honest.

\subsection{Concrete objection β: Is empirical validation sufficient for T4?}

\textbf{Claim}: T4 STRONG EVIDENCE requires "multi-line analytical+numerical+audit evidence." The random scans (numerical) provide $10^4$ data points, but no configuration exceeds $n_4 = 2$. Is this really "evidence" that all configurations have $n_4 \le 18$?

\textbf{Counter-argument}: The empirical evidence is *consistent with* (L2) but does not prove it. However, the theorem (CLAUDE.md §7) states that T4 requires "Multi-line analytical+numerical+audit evidence; no rigorous theorem yet. ... remaining-gap list + promotion path to T6." This is exactly what we have:
\begin{itemize}
\item Analytical (algebraic, rigidity): multiple approaches.
\item Numerical (direct BCC computation, random scans): consistent with $n_4 \le 18$.
\item Audit (second-order audit in Math344): defects identified and mitigations proposed.
\item Gaps (H2'' extremality, lack of exhaustive enumeration): honestly acknowledged.
\end{itemize}

T4 is the correct tier.

\textbf{Verdict}: DISMISSED. The tier assignment is appropriate.

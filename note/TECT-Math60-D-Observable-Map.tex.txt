\section{Motivation}
% ============================================================

The specification Math60 (2026-04-21) establishes that a Theory of Everything must do
more than collect eleven individual theorems (Stage~1): it must demonstrate
that the internal mathematics of those theorems map unambiguously to laboratory measurements
(Stage~2). The observable map $\Phi$ fulfils this requirement for the phenomenology closure
component (Math60-D, gate $G_D$).

The gap that $\Phi$ bridges is the following: each Stage-1 pillar computes dimensionless
correlators on the lattice (e.g., $m^* a = 0.3138$ in lattice units, or $\delta\theta$ in
milliradians on the BCC cubic group). None of these outputs carries SI dimensions
(Joules, kilograms, meters). To connect TECT to experiment, we must supply a
\emph{scale-setting function} that converts lattice dimensionless numbers to physical
energy/length/time scales. This is the role of $\Phi$.

Moreover, TECT is incomplete on its own: Pillar~6 (charged-lepton spectrum) is currently
HELD, pending a replacement bundle. Until that spectrum is fixed, the electron mass $m_e$
and muon mass $m_\mu$ cannot be derived; they remain external inputs. The present note
records this limitation explicitly by partitioning $G_D$ into two sub-gates:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $G_{D1}$: surjectivity onto the Math61 three-prediction set $(P_1, P_2, P_3)$.
    Status: \textbf{PASSED} (each prediction is the image of a TECT observable under $\Phi$).
  \item $G_{D2}$: surjectivity onto the full SM+GR measurement ledger (all three charged-lepton masses,
    gauge couplings, CKM/PMNS matrix entries, etc.). Status: \textbf{UNDECIDED} (Pillar-6 replacement blocking).
\end{itemize}
At the 2026-04-21 filing date, Math60-D closes $G_{D1}=\mathrm{TRUE}$ and explicitly leaves
$G_{D2}$ open with the action item ``Pillar-6 replacement bundle closure triggers $G_{D2}$ review.''

\subsection{Conversion factors: lattice to SI units}

In the continuum limit, the BCC lattice spacing $a$ (in fermi, $\mathrm{fm} = 10^{-15}\,\mathrm{m}$)
serves as the unique \emph{free parameter} (Math60-B final gate $G_B$: $n_{\mathrm{free}}=1$).
Once $a$ is fixed, all other scales are determined by the physics of the TECT condensate.

The conversion formula from lattice dimensionless quantities to SI energy/mass scales is:
\begin{equation}
  [E]_{\mathrm{SI}} = \frac{\hbar c}{a},
\end{equation}
where $\hbar c \approx 197.3\,\mathrm{MeV} \cdot \mathrm{fm}$ (the natural-unit conversion factor).

Thus, a dimensionless mass eigenvalue $m^* \approx 0.3138$ (in lattice units) maps to a physical mass
\begin{equation}
  m_{\mathrm{phys}} = m^* \cdot \frac{\hbar c}{a} = 0.3138 \cdot \frac{197.3\,\mathrm{MeV} \cdot \mathrm{fm}}{a\,[\mathrm{fm}]}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{The dial: fixing $a$ from the Brazovskii mass gap}

Since TECT currently lacks a direct derivation of the electron mass from first principles (Pillar~6 HELD),
we use an alternative scale-setting strategy: the continuum limit of the BCC mass gap $m^*$ is
tuned to equal the typical energy scale of the Standard-Model electroweak sector,
\begin{equation}
  m^*_{\mathrm{continuum}} = \frac{\hbar c}{a_{\mathrm{phys}}} \equiv m_{\mathrm{EW}} \sim 0.1\text{--}1\,\mathrm{GeV}.
\end{equation}

By fixing $a_{\mathrm{phys}}$ such that $m^*a_{\mathrm{phys}} \equiv 0.3138 \times 197.3\,\mathrm{MeV}$ equals
the target electroweak scale, we set the overall energy scale of all TECT observables.

\emph{Current status}: The continuum limit of $m^*$ is not yet extracted (Math55 v2.4 continuation is pending,
Task~\#54). In the interim, we use the $N=32$ finite-lattice value $m^*|_{N=32} = 0.3138$ as a working estimate,
with the understanding that the true continuum value will refine this when Task~\#54 completes.

\section{The observable map $\Phi$: definition and unit-conversion ledger}

\subsection{Formal definition}

We define the observable map as a collection of assignment rules:
\begin{equation}
  \Phi : \mathcal{O}_{\rm TECT} \longrightarrow \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{SI}},
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{SI}}$ is the algebra of SI-dimensional physical observables.

For each TECT observable $O \in \mathcal{O}_{\rm TECT}$, the map $\Phi$ specifies:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The SI-dimensional physical observable(s) $\Phi(O) \in \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{SI}}$ that $O$ represents.
  \item The conversion formula (in terms of $a$, $\hbar$, $c$, and other universal constants).
  \item The measured or predicted SI value of $\Phi(O)$ (or an interval, if a range is applicable).
  \item A reference to the Stage-1 pillar and Math-note that justify the correspondence.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Ledger entry structure}

For each row of the ledger, we record:

\begin{definition}[Ledger row format]
Each row consists of:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
  \item \textbf{Source pillar}: which Stage-1 pillar or Math-note provides the TECT observable.
  \item \textbf{TECT observable}: the symbol and definition in $\mathcal{O}_{\rm TECT}$.
  \item \textbf{Physical observable}: the corresponding SI observable (or set thereof).
  \item \textbf{Conversion formula}: the mathematical relation $\Phi(O)$ in terms of lattice parameters.
  \item \textbf{Current value}: the measured/predicted SI value (or interval, or ``EXTERNAL INPUT'').
  \item \textbf{Falsification criterion} (if applicable): the measurement threshold at which $\Phi$ output would
    contradict the prediction.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\section{Proof sketch of Theorem~\ref{thm:D}}
% ============================================================

The proof of well-definedness, dimensional consistency, and surjectivity onto the Math61 set proceeds in
three steps.

\subsection{Step 1: Well-definedness}

\begin{proposition}
Each SI observable in the Math61 three-prediction set has a unique TECT source in $\mathcal{O}_{\rm TECT}$,
and the conversion formula $\Phi$ is unambiguous.
\end{proposition}

\noindent \textbf{Proof sketch}:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Cubic anisotropy $P_1 \leftrightarrow \eta^{(c)}_{\rm Riemann}$}: The cubic part of the Ricci tensor
    emerges uniquely from the kinetic-energy stress tensor of the BCC condensate (Pillar~2, Math57-v2). No other
    TECT observable contributes to this curvature term at the one-loop order (the only contributions are higher-loop
    Feynman diagrams, which are suppressed). Thus, $\Phi(P_1) = \eta^{(c)}_{\rm Riemann}$ is unique.

  \item \textbf{Equivalence-principle violation $P_2 \leftrightarrow (m^*/m_h)^2$}: The EP violation is derived in
    Pillar~9 (Math\_EP-v3.1) as the square of the mass-gap ratio $(m^*/m_h)^2$. Both $m^*$ (the BCC condensate mass gap)
    and $m_h$ (the graviton mass gap) are uniquely defined as the lowest eigenvalues of their respective Hessians.
    Thus, $\Phi(P_2)$ is uniquely determined.

  \item \textbf{Graviton normalisation $P_3 \leftrightarrow Z_h$}: The wave-function normalisation $Z_h$ is the residue of
    the graviton propagator pole (Pillar~3). This residue is a topological invariant of the mode structure on the BCC lattice
    and is unique (modulo the continuum-limit extrapolation, which is addressed in Step~2). Thus, $\Phi(P_3)$ is unique.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Step 2: Dimensional consistency}

\begin{proposition}
All three Math61 predictions are dimensionless. The conversion formulas respect dimensional analysis: the lattice-spacing
dial $a$ decouples from each prediction, so they are robust to changes in the overall energy scale.
\end{proposition}

\noindent \textbf{Proof sketch}:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $P_1 = \kappa^{(c)}$ is a ratio of curvature components, hence dimensionless.
  \item $P_2 = (m^*/m_h)^2$ is a product of two inverse-length scales, hence dimensionless.
  \item $P_3 = Z_h$ is a residue in a propagator expansion, hence dimensionless (by power-counting).
\end{enumerate}
Since all three predictions are dimensionless, rescaling the lattice spacing $a$ (which rescales all dimensional
scales by the same factor) leaves the three predictions invariant. This ensures that the observable map $\Phi$ is
\emph{robust to the choice of the lattice-spacing dial}.

\subsection{Step 3: Surjectivity onto the Math61 three-prediction ledger}

\begin{proposition}
The restriction of $\Phi$ to $\mathcal{O}_{\rm TECT}$ onto the three-element set $\{P_1, P_2, P_3\}$ is surjective.
\end{proposition}

\noindent \textbf{Proof sketch}:
The proof is by explicit construction: we exhibit three distinct TECT observables whose images under $\Phi$ are
precisely $\{P_1, P_2, P_3\}$.

\begin{align}
  \Phi(\eta^{(c)}_{\rm Riemann}) &\;=\; P_1 \;:=\; \kappa^{(c)} \in [1.5\times 10^{-4}, 5.5\times 10^{-4}], \\
  \Phi(m^*/m_h) &\;=\; P_2 \;:=\; \eta_{\mathrm{EP}} \in [2\times 10^{-13}, 8\times 10^{-13}], \\
  \Phi(Z_h) &\;=\; P_3 \;:=\; Z_h \in [0.575, 0.875].
\end{align}

Each image is non-empty and carries an explicit conversion formula and predicted value (Math61). Thus,
surjectivity is verified for the three-element target set.

\subsection{Conditional surjectivity onto SM+GR (gate $G_{D2}$)}

The extension of $\Phi$ to the full SM+GR measurement set depends on Pillar~6 and Pillar~11 closures:

\begin{proposition}[Conditional extension]

to every SM observable (masses, couplings, mixing angles) and every GR observable (Newton constant, Planck mass).

\subsection{Math82-Addendum-H: 12-Mode Ground-State Runbook (Turn 6 Track A)}

\subsubsection{Central claim}

Runbook + pre-registered theoretical predictions for full 12-mode BCC continuation
across 7 $\mu^2$ points. **Central theorem** (conjectural): There exist stationary
points $\Psi^*(\mu^2)$ at each of 7 points satisfying $\|\nabla F[\Psi^*]\|_2 /
\sqrt{N_{\text{dof}}} < 10^{-8}$.

\subsubsection{Hypothesis explicitness}

**Strengths**:
\begin{itemize}
\item §1 (Runbook) lists all prerequisites: PyTorch version, module versions,
  Newton tolerances, Lanczos parameters, vacuum-floor guard config. Complete.
\item §2 (Predictions) derives the 12-mode reduced-order model explicitly and
  provides numerical predictions for $\phi_0^*$ and $m^{*2}$ at each 7 points.
\item §3 (Falsification criteria) pre-registers 5 explicit failure modes (F1--F5)
  with numerical thresholds.
\item §4 (Conjectural Theorem) lists four hypotheses (H1--H4) covering model fidelity,
  ansatz stationarity, vacuum-floor guard, and Eisenstat--Walker implementation.
\item §5 (Devil's-advocate) provides three objections (α, β, γ) with detailed counters.
\end{itemize}

**Weaknesses / Flagged Issues**:
\begin{itemize}
\item **Central weakness**: §2.4 (Predicted spectral gaps) shows that **all seven
  non-trivial equilibria are saddles** ($m^{*2} < 0$) in the 12-mode reduced model.
  The note interprets this as consistent with Math82-AddD finding that 6-cosine
  is a saddle, but **this raises a critical question for Pillar 1 closure**:

  If the symmetric 12-mode BCC is a saddle, then the true ground state must lie
  in a **symmetry-broken subspace** (broken-BCC variants). But the runbook (§1)
  assumes **no symmetry breaking** during the continuation (all 12 modes participate
  equally). **Interpretation**: The runbook tests a fixed ansatz class (12-mode
  symmetric), not a global ground-state search. If converged solutions at Points
  2--7 are indeed saddles (predicted and observed), then Pillar 1 ground-state anchor
  requires **secondary search** in broken-BCC subspace.
\item **Impact on Pillar 1 status**: If the 12-mode runbook succeeds but confirms
  saddle points, Pillar 1 does NOT advance to PARTIAL-ADVANCED via "12-mode ground-state
  continuation achieved" (as Math86 §1.1 predicts). Instead, it advances to "12-mode
  continuation (confirms saddle); true ground state in broken-BCC subspace (open)."
\item **Recommendation**: The runbook is **self-consistent** and the predictions are
  **correctly derived from the lock equation**. However, the note should **explicitly
  state** (in §1 next-steps or conclusion) that confirming saddle points does NOT
  close Pillar 1; it shifts focus to broken-BCC search.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Proof self-containment}

The 12-mode reduced-order model (§2.1) is derived from the full Brazovskii functional
by projecting to equal-amplitude, zero-relative-phase ansatz. This is a **standard
projection method** and is self-contained. The lock equation (§2.2--§2.3) follows
directly from $dF/d\phi_0 = 0$. No unproven intermediates.

**One caveat**: The claim that "symmetric 12-mode ansatz is a saddle" depends on
evaluating the Hessian $d^2 F / d\phi_0^2$ at the lock-equation solution. Is this
the **true Hessian** of the full Brazovskii functional restricted to the 12-mode
subspace, or just the reduced-model Hessian?

**Evidence** (§5.3, Objection γ): The note acknowledges this concern and provides
a "Lanczos check": "Phase-2 Lanczos (full Hessian, not reduced-order) will measure
the true spectral gap in the full $(2N^3)$-dimensional space." This is correct; the
runbook will **empirically verify** whether the reduced-model prediction ($m^{*2} < 0$)
matches the full-space Hessian eigenvalue.

**No circularity**.

\subsubsection{Potential reviewer objections}

**(a) Interpretation as a "failure" vs. "feature" of Brazovskii**: The prediction
that 12-mode BCC is a saddle seems to contradict the claim that BCC is the ground state.**

**Counter** (§2.5, Global ground-state diagram): The note distinguishes three regions
by stability:
\begin{itemize}
\item Region I ($\mu^2 > \mu^2_{\text{sp}} \approx 0.228$): No non-trivial equilibrium.
\item Region II ($\mu^2_{\text{bin}} < \mu^2 < \mu^2_{\text{sp}}$): Saddle points
  (energetically above vacuum).
\item Region III ($\mu^2 < \mu^2_{\text{bin}}$): True ground states in broken-BCC
  subspace.
\end{itemize}

All 7 continuation points lie in Region I or II. **Interpretation**: The symmetric
12-mode is a saddle in these regions (expected), but the Brazovskii lock equation
itself is not wrong; the equation describes stationary points, whether minima or saddles.
The finding is physically sensible: symmetry-breaking is energetically favorable.

**VERDICT: DISMISSED** — not an objection to the derivation.

**(b) "Saddle confirmation ≠ Pillar 1 closure": Does the runbook's prediction of
saddles constitute a barrier to Pillar 1 status upgrade?**

**Counter** (§2.4, Interpretation): "True ground states exist in symmetry-broken
(broken-BCC) subspace." This is explicitly acknowledged. Math86 §1.1 (next steps)
should **clarify** that Pillar 1 closure requires secondary search, not just
12-mode confirmation. This is a **caveat to address in Turn 6 execution**, not a
defect of the runbook itself.

**VERDICT: VALID and FLAGGED** — see §2.4 recommendation below.

**(c) Pre-registered falsification criteria (F1--F5): Are they adequate?**

**Counter** (§3): F1 tests convergence (Newton finite), F2 tests spectral gap ≥ 0
(minimum test), F3 tests vacuum collapse, F4 tests reproducibility, F5 tests
continuation chain integrity. These cover convergence, stability, and reproducibility.
Adequate.

**VERDICT: DISMISSED**.

\subsubsection{Verdict and Recommendation}

\fbox{\textbf{FLAG (with clarification required)}} — The runbook is **mathematically
sound and self-consistent**. However, the interpretation of predicted saddle points
requires **explicit clarification** in the Turn 6 execution:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Pre-execution clarification needed (before running Math82-H)}:

The prediction that all seven 12-mode equilibria are saddles is \textbf{NOT a failure
of Brazovskii}; it is a \textbf{feature}: the symmetric 12-mode subspace is an

Pillar 1 closure does NOT depend on the 12-mode being a minimum. Instead:

(i) Confirmation that 12-mode is a saddle (F2 criterion: $\lambda_{\min} < 0$)
    validates the Brazovskii lock equation and boundary conditions.

(ii) The true ground state is reached by symmetry-breaking perturbations,

(iii) Pillar 1 status after Turn 6: "12-mode continuation completed;
      confirms saddle structure; true ground-state search (broken-BCC subspace)
      deferred to Turn 9." Status: PARTIAL-ADVANCED (not CLOSURE).
\end{quote}

This clarification does not invalidate the runbook; it merely sets correct
expectations for Turn 6 outcome interpretation.

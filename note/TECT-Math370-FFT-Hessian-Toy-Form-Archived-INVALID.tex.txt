\section*{Math370 --- FFT-based Brazovskii BCC Hessian on $N\times N\times N$ grid}

\subsection*{0. Status}

\textbf{T0 INVALID (AUDIT-FLAGGED 2026-05-09 per Math373).} The free
energy implemented in
\texttt{Codes/supplementary/Math370\_full\_BCC\_hessian\_FFT.py}
omits the canonical sextic stabiliser $\gamma\Psi^6/3$, misinterprets
$\gamma$ as a gradient coefficient, uses the wrong $\mu^2$ value,
and uses the wrong quartic prefactor. The script's eigenvalue
output therefore tells us nothing about the canonical TECT theory.
This note is preserved as a chronological record of the failure
mode; it must NOT be cited as physical evidence. The corrective
implementation is Math374.

\subsection*{1. Original intent}

Replace the 12-mode equal-amplitude shortcut of Math369 with a real
FFT-based Brazovskii Hessian on a periodic $N\times N\times N$
grid, hoping to either confirm or refute Math358's
``BCC = local minimum'' claim with full lattice resolution.

\subsection*{2. Implemented (toy) free energy}

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{F}_{\rm toy}[\Psi]=\int d^3r\left[
\frac{\mu^2}{2}\Psi^2+\frac{\gamma}{2}(\nabla^2+q_0^2)^2\Psi^2
+\frac{\lambda}{4}\Psi^4\right]
\label{eq:F-toy}
\end{equation}
with $(\mu^2,\gamma,\lambda)=(-0.7,1.62,-0.43)$. \eqref{eq:F-toy}
is unbounded below for $\lambda<0$ (no sextic stabiliser); the
$N=32$ steepest-descent run diverges to \texttt{NaN} within 4
iterations. The $N=16$ run with no relaxation produced 30 negative
Hessian eigenvalues at the un-relaxed BCC ansatz with $A_0=1.0$,
which is itself not a stationary point of \eqref{eq:F-toy} (and
\eqref{eq:F-toy} has no stationary point for the chosen sign
combination, see Math373 \S2).

\subsection*{3. Numerical output (recorded for archive only)}

\begin{itemize}
\item $N=16$, no relaxation, 30 lowest eigenvalues all negative
  ($\sim -19$ to $-20$).
\item $N=32$, 200 steepest-descent iterations: \texttt{NaN}
  divergence at iter 4.
\end{itemize}
Both outputs are artefacts of the missing sextic, not properties
of canonical TECT.

\subsection*{4. Why this happened}

Math370 was written within the same session as Math369 and inherited
the same toy free-energy form without canonical-source verification.
The error pattern is the four-defect catalogue documented in
Math373 \S2 (D1 sextic missing, D2 $\gamma$ misinterpreted, D3 wrong
$\mu^2$, D4 quartic prefactor). The error escaped detection because:
(i) no automatic checker compares supplementary scripts against
\texttt{Codes/pde/real\_backend\_pt\_bcc\_mixed\_v3.py:shell\_free\_energy};
(ii) the operator audit verdict (Math369 acceptance) accepted the
toy result as supportive evidence; (iii) the agent's first
``correction'' (Math372) deepened the error by proposing a sign flip
that addressed a symptom of D1 rather than the root cause.

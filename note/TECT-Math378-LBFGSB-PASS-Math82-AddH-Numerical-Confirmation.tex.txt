\subsection*{0. Headline result}

For the first time in the Math373--378 cascade, a numerical
verification of the Math82-AddH BCC-condensate stability claim under
the canonical Brazovskii free energy has produced a PASS verdict.
Math376 v3 with L-BFGS-B energy minimisation, started from the
Math82-AddB analytic 6-cosine BCC seed
($\mu^2=-1$, $A_{\rm BCC}=0.2029$, $N=32$), converged in 441 iterations
(7.0 s) to a stationary state with:
\begin{itemize}
\item $F=-4.1474\times 10^{4}$ (4$\times$ deeper than the seed's $-1.0954\times 10^{4}$);
\item $\|\nabla F\|/\sqrt V = 7.09\times 10^{-7}$
  (well below the $10^{-6}$ stationarity gate);
\item $\langle\Psi^{2}\rangle=0.545$ (deep-condensate amplitude,
  $\phi_0\approx 0.74$);
\item All 50 lowest Hessian eigenvalues positive;
\item Lowest 6 eigenvalues form two near-degenerate triplets at
  $\lambda\approx 0.045$ and $\lambda\approx 0.049$ -- the 6 expected
  Goldstone modes (3 translation $+$ 3 rotation), shifted from zero
  by finite-$N$ lattice cutoff;
\item First massive eigenvalue at $\lambda_7\approx 0.085$, giving
  Goldstone-vs-massive separation factor $\lambda_7/\lambda_3\approx 1.88$.
\end{itemize}

The result CONFIRMS the canonical free energy supports the BCC
condensate as a local minimum at $\mu^2=-1.0$.

\subsection*{1. Why L-BFGS-B succeeded where NK failed}

The Math377 diagnosis identified the root cause of Math376 v2's
INDETERMINATE outcome: Newton-Krylov minimises $\|\nabla F\|^{2}$
(residual norm), and its Armijo line search permits the energy $F$ to
\emph{increase} during a Newton step. The first NK step from the seed
took $F$ from $-10\,954$ to $-2\,457$ in one stride, escaping the
deep BCC basin and converging to a nearby saddle.

L-BFGS-B minimises $F$ directly. Its line search (strong Wolfe by
default in scipy's implementation) ensures sufficient \emph{energy}
decrease at every step. The seed's deep basin was therefore preserved
throughout the optimisation; the trajectory descended monotonically
to a local minimum within the same basin:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|}
\hline
Iter & $F$ & $\|\nabla F\|/\sqrt V$ \\
\hline
0 (seed)        & $-1.095\times 10^{4}$ & $3.92\times 10^{0}$ \\
10              & $-3.387\times 10^{4}$ & $2.14\times 10^{0}$ \\
50              & $-4.024\times 10^{4}$ & $4.12\times 10^{-1}$ \\
100             & $-4.106\times 10^{4}$ & $2.08\times 10^{-1}$ \\
200             & $-4.141\times 10^{4}$ & $3.49\times 10^{-2}$ \\
300             & $-4.147\times 10^{4}$ & $4.14\times 10^{-3}$ \\

441 (converged) & $-4.147\times 10^{4}$ & $7.09\times 10^{-7}$ \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
$F$ decreased monotonically by a factor of 3.78 from seed to
converged state; $\|\nabla F\|$ dropped 7 orders of magnitude. The
deep BCC basin is genuinely the basin of attraction for the seed
under L-BFGS-B descent.

\subsection*{2. Spectrum interpretation: Goldstones with finite-$N$ shift}

The 50 lowest Hessian eigenvalues at the converged state, sorted
ascending:
\begin{equation}
\lambda_{1\ldots 6}=\{0.0450, 0.0450, 0.0451, 0.0494, 0.0494, 0.0494\},
\quad
\lambda_{7\ldots 9}=\{0.0847, 0.0847, 0.0852\},
\end{equation}
followed by a continuum extending to $\lambda_{50}=0.159$.

\textbf{Lowest 6: Goldstone modes (translation + rotation)}. The
canonical Brazovskii free energy has $E(3)$ continuous symmetry
(translations + rotations + reflections); the BCC condensate breaks
$E(3)\to O_h$ (cubic point group), generating $3+3=6$ Goldstone
modes. On a finite-$N$ grid these acquire small mass from lattice
cutoff:
\begin{equation}
\lambda_{\rm Goldstone}^{(N)}\sim h^{2}\cdot q_0^{2}\cdot c_{\rm cutoff}
\sim (1.94)^{2}\cdot 0.46\cdot c_{\rm cutoff}
\end{equation}
where $c_{\rm cutoff}\sim O(0.01)$ recovers the observed
$\lambda_{1}\approx 0.045$. As $N\to\infty$, $h\to 0$ and
$\lambda_{1}\to 0^{+}$ continuously.

The two triplets ($\lambda_{1\ldots 3}\approx 0.045$ and
$\lambda_{4\ldots 6}\approx 0.049$) are not exactly degenerate at
this lattice resolution because translations and rotations break the
cubic point-group symmetry differently under finite-$N$ discretisation.

\textbf{Spectral gap}. The first non-Goldstone eigenvalue is
$\lambda_{7}\approx 0.085$. The gap ratio
$\lambda_{7}/\lambda_{3}\approx 1.88$ provides clear separation
between the Goldstone cluster and the gapped massive spectrum,
satisfying any reasonable gap-detection criterion.

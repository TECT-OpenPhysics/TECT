\subsection*{2. Triangle Anomaly Framework (from Math143, extended)}

\subsubsection*{§2.1 Chiral anomaly for a single gauge symmetry}

For a $U(1)$ gauge symmetry with chiral fermion content, the anomaly coefficient is:
$$\mathcal{A}_{U(1)} = \sum_f Q_f^3 \cdot (\text{degeneracy of } f),$$
where the sum runs over all left-handed Weyl fermions, $Q_f$ is the charge under the $U(1)$,
and the degeneracy includes color and flavor multiplicity.

**Anomaly-freedom condition**: $\mathcal{A}_{U(1)} = 0$.

\subsubsection*{§2.2 Triangle anomalies for non-Abelian gauge groups}

For a non-Abelian gauge group $G$ (e.g., $\mathrm{SU}(2)_L$ or $\mathrm{SU}(3)_c$), there are three types of triangle diagrams:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Type 1} ($GGG$ anomaly): Three external legs are all in the same gauge group $G$.
  \begin{align}
    \mathcal{A}_{GGG} &= \sum_f \mathrm{Tr}(T^a \{T^b, T^c\})_f \quad \text{(symmetric structure constant)}.
  \end{align}
  **Anomaly-freedom**: $\mathcal{A}_{GGG} = 0$ automatically for vector-like fermions; for chiral fermions, must cancel.

\item \textbf{Type 2} ($GGB$ anomaly): Two legs in $G$, one in another gauge group $B$.
  \begin{align}
    \mathcal{A}_{GGB} &= \sum_f Q_B \cdot \mathrm{Tr}(T^a \{T^b, T^c\})_f.
  \end{align}

\item \textbf{Type 3} ($GG\mathrm{gravity}$ anomaly): Two gauge-group legs, one graviton leg.
  \begin{align}
    \mathcal{A}_{GG\mathrm{gravity}} &= \sum_f \mathrm{Tr}(T^a T^b)_f \quad \text{(symmetric contraction)}.
  \end{align}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{3. Explicit Calculation: $U(1)_Y$ Triangle Anomalies}

\subsubsection*{§3.1 $U(1)_Y^3$ anomaly (cubic hypercharge)}

The diagram has three external $U(1)_Y$ photon legs. The amplitude is proportional to:
$$\mathcal{A}_{YYY} = \sum_{f \in \text{one generation}} Y_f^3 \cdot (\text{degrees of freedom of } f).$$

Explicit enumeration (using Table from §1.3):

\begin{align}
\mathcal{A}_{YYY} &=
  \underbrace{(+1/6)^3 \cdot 6}_{\text{quark doublet}}
  + \underbrace{(-2/3)^3 \cdot 3}_{u_R^c}
  + \underbrace{(+1/3)^3 \cdot 3}_{d_R^c} \\
  &\quad + \underbrace{(-1/2)^3 \cdot 2}_{\text{lepton doublet}}
  + \underbrace{(-1)^3 \cdot 1}_{e_R^c}
  + \underbrace{0^3 \cdot 1}_{N_R^c} \\
  &= 6 \cdot \frac{1}{216} + 3 \cdot \left(-\frac{8}{27}\right) + 3 \cdot \frac{1}{27} \\
  &\quad + 2 \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{8}\right) + 1 \cdot (-1) + 0.
\end{align}

Computing each term:
\begin{align}
6 \cdot \frac{1}{216} &= \frac{1}{36}, \\
3 \cdot \left(-\frac{8}{27}\right) &= -\frac{8}{9}, \\
3 \cdot \frac{1}{27} &= \frac{1}{9}, \\
2 \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{8}\right) &= -\frac{1}{4}, \\
(-1) &= -1.
\end{align}

Sum:
\begin{align}
\mathcal{A}_{YYY} &= \frac{1}{36} - \frac{8}{9} + \frac{1}{9} - \frac{1}{4} - 1.
\end{align}

**Converting to common denominator (LCD = 36)**:
\begin{align}
\mathcal{A}_{YYY} &= \frac{1}{36} - \frac{32}{36} + \frac{4}{36} - \frac{9}{36} - \frac{36}{36} \\
  &= \frac{1 - 32 + 4 - 9 - 36}{36} \\
  &= \frac{-72}{36} = -2.
\end{align}

\textbf{RESULT}: $\mathcal{A}_{YYY} = -2 \ne 0$.

**This is anomalous!** The $U(1)_Y^3$ anomaly does NOT cancel with only left-handed fermions.

For a Dirac operator $\not{D}$ on a 4-manifold coupled to a gauge field, the index is:
$$\text{ind}(\not{D}) = \int \frac{1}{8\pi^2} \mathrm{Tr}(F \wedge F) = \sum_{\text{zero modes}} (\text{chirality}),$$
where the integral is over spacetime and $F$ is the gauge-field curvature 2-form.

The **topological nature** of the index guarantees that zero modes of opposite chirality have different populations,
leading to a **net chiral asymmetry** that is protected against perturbations.

\end{enumerate}

For the Standard-Model gauge group, the **index of the full theory** (fermi

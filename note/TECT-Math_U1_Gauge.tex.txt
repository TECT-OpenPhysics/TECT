\begin{abstract}
In TECT, the transverse acoustic sector of the BCC condensate possesses a
global $SO(2) \cong U(1)$ polarization rotation symmetry.
The promotion of this global symmetry to a local $U(1)$ gauge symmetry---and the
emergence of Maxwell electrodynamics---has previously been asserted without a
dynamical derivation.
We close this gap through a three-stage theorem-level argument.
First, we show that the transverse phonon constraint $\bm{k}\cdot\bm{u}_T = 0$
defines a non-trivial rank-2 vector bundle $E_T$ over momentum space,
whose structure group is exactly $SO(2) \cong U(1)$.
Second, we prove that the non-triviality of this bundle---measured by its first
Chern class $c_1(E_T) = \chi(S^2) = 2$---forces any globally $SO(2)$-invariant
effective action to be written in terms of a $U(1)$ connection 1-form,
making the gauge field dynamically unavoidable.
Third, we integrate out the gapped longitudinal and amplitude modes via the
heat-kernel method to derive the induced Maxwell action with a positive
kinetic coefficient.
The promotion $SO(2)_{\mathrm{global}} \to U(1)_{\mathrm{local}}$ is therefore
not a postulate but a theorem: it is the unique consequence of writing a
local, $SO(2)$-invariant, low-energy effective action for sections of a
bundle with non-trivial first Chern class.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction: The Logical Gap}

Math09 contains the assertion
\begin{equation}
SO(2)_{\mathrm{global}} \longrightarrow U(1)_{\mathrm{local}},
\label{eq:the_claim}
\end{equation}
with no dynamical mechanism.
This is not a minor omission.
The promotion of a global symmetry to a local gauge symmetry is the most
non-trivial step in gauge theory: it requires either a principle of local
invariance (an external postulate) or a concrete dynamical mechanism that
forces the gauge field to appear.

Three possible routes exist in the literature:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Postulate route} (standard QFT): declare local invariance as a
  fundamental principle. Not acceptable for an emergent theory.
\item \textbf{Integrating-out route} (Sakharov/induced gauge): integrate out
  massive charged modes to generate a gauge-invariant effective action.
  Dynamical but requires the existence of charged matter.
\item \textbf{Bundle-obstruction route} (the correct TECT mechanism):
  show that the physical configuration space of the transverse phonon sector
  is the space of sections of a non-trivial $U(1)$ bundle, so that a $U(1)$
  connection is a \emph{structural necessity}, not a choice.
\end{enumerate}

We pursue both routes 3 and 2, in that order.
Route 3 establishes that the photon field must exist.
Route 2 then derives its kinetic (Maxwell) action.

\section{The Transverse Polarization Bundle}

\subsection{Helmholtz decomposition and the transverse constraint}

The BCC condensate acoustic phonon field is a real 3-vector displacement
$\bm{u}(\bm{x},t) \in \mathbb{R}^3$.
By the Helmholtz decomposition, any such field splits into longitudinal and
transverse parts:
\begin{equation}
\bm{u} = \bm{u}_L + \bm{u}_T,
\qquad
\nabla \times \bm{u}_L = 0,
\qquad
\nabla \cdot \bm{u}_T = 0.
\label{eq:Helmholtz}
\end{equation}
In momentum space, this becomes
\begin{equation}
\bm{u}_T(\bm{k}) \in \hat{\bm{k}}^\perp
:= \bigl\{ \bm{v} \in \mathbb{R}^3 \;\big|\; \bm{k} \cdot \bm{v} = 0 \bigr\}.
\label{eq:transverse_constraint}
\end{equation}
At each nonzero $\bm{k}$, the transverse subspace $\hat{\bm{k}}^\perp$ is a
2-dimensional real vector space.

\subsection{The transverse polarization bundle}

\begin{theorem}[Transverse phonon bundle]
\label{thm:ET_bundle}
Define the \emph{transverse polarization bundle}
\begin{equation}
E_T := \bigl\{(\bm{k},\bm{v}) \in (\mathbb{R}^3\setminus\{0\}) \times \mathbb{R}^3
\;\big|\; \bm{k}\cdot\bm{v} = 0\bigr\}
\label{eq:ET_def}
\end{equation}
with the projection $\pi: E_T \to \mathbb{R}^3\setminus\{0\}$,
$\pi(\bm{k},\bm{v}) = \bm{k}$.
Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $E_T$ is a smooth real rank-$2$ vector bundle over
  $\mathbb{R}^3\setminus\{0\}$, with structure group $SO(2)$.
\item[(b)] The restriction of $E_T$ to the unit sphere $S^2_{\hat{k}}$ is
  isomorphic to the tangent bundle $TS^2$:
  \begin{equation}
  E_T\big|_{S^2} \cong TS^2.
  \label{eq:ET_is_TS2}
  \end{equation}
\item[(c)] $E_T$ is \emph{non-trivial}: it has no global nowhere-vanishing
  section (by the Hairy Ball theorem) and its first Chern number satisfies
  \begin{equation}
  c_1(E_T) = \chi(S^2) = 2 \neq 0.
  \label{eq:c1_nonzero}
  \end{equation}
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textit{(a)} The fiber at $\bm{k}$ is the 2-dimensional orthogonal complement of
$\hat{\bm{k}}$ in $\mathbb{R}^3$, which varies smoothly with $\bm{k}$.
A local frame $(e_1(\hat{\bm{k}}), e_2(\hat{\bm{k}}))$ for the fiber requires
a smooth choice of two orthonormal vectors perpendicular to $\hat{\bm{k}}$.
Two such frames are related by an $SO(2)$ rotation, so the structure group
is $SO(2) \cong U(1)$.

\textit{(b)} The isomorphism $E_T|_{S^2} \cong TS^2$ holds because the fiber
$\hat{\bm{k}}^\perp$ at a unit vector $\hat{\bm{k}} \in S^2$ is exactly
the tangent space $T_{\hat{\bm{k}}}S^2$ of the unit sphere.

\textit{(c)} By the Hairy Ball theorem, $TS^2$ has no global nowhere-vanishing
section (equivalently, $\chi(S^2) = 2 \neq 0$).
Therefore $E_T|_{S^2}$ is a non-trivial bundle.
Since $\mathbb{R}^3\setminus\{0\}$ retracts onto $S^2$, the bundle $E_T$
itself is non-trivial.
The Euler number of $TS^2$ equals the first Chern number of its
complexification: $c_1(E_T \otimes_\mathbb{R} \mathbb{C}) = \chi(S^2) = 2$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Geometric meaning}

Theorem~\ref{thm:ET_bundle}(c) has a direct physical consequence:
there is no smooth global basis of transverse polarizations.
Any attempt to define a global polarization frame $(\bm{e}_1, \bm{e}_2)$
with $\bm{k}\cdot\bm{e}_a = 0$ and $\bm{e}_a\cdot\bm{e}_b = \delta_{ab}$
for all $\hat{\bm{k}} \in S^2$ necessarily fails at (at least) two points
(from $c_1 = 2$).

This is not a problem of poor choice of frame---it is a topological obstruction.
The bundle $E_T$ is topologically non-trivial, and this topological fact is what
forces a $U(1)$ gauge field to appear.

\section{The Bundle-Obstruction Route: Gauge Field as Structural Necessity}

\subsection{Local sections and frame changes}

Let $\{U_\alpha\}$ be an open cover of $S^2_{\hat{k}}$ such that $E_T$ is
trivial over each $U_\alpha$.
Over each $U_\alpha$, choose a local orthonormal frame
\begin{equation}
e_\alpha = (e_{\alpha,1}(\hat{\bm{k}}),\, e_{\alpha,2}(\hat{\bm{k}})),
\qquad
\hat{\bm{k}}\cdot e_{\alpha,a} = 0,
\qquad
e_{\alpha,a}\cdot e_{\alpha,b} = \delta_{ab}.
\label{eq:local_frame}
\end{equation}
On overlaps $U_\alpha \cap U_\beta$, the two frames are related by a transition
function
\begin{equation}
e_\beta(\hat{\bm{k}}) = e_\alpha(\hat{\bm{k}})\, g_{\alpha\beta}(\hat{\bm{k}}),
\qquad
g_{\alpha\beta}: U_\alpha \cap U_\beta \to SO(2).
\label{eq:transition_fn}
\end{equation}
The transition functions are not globally trivializable, because $E_T$ is
non-trivial.

\subsection{The connection 1-form is mandatory}

A transverse phonon field $\bm{u}_T$ is a section of $E_T$.
In a local frame over $U_\alpha$, it is represented by a 2-component field
\begin{equation}
u^\alpha(\bm{k}) = (u^\alpha_1(\bm{k}),\, u^\alpha_2(\bm{k})) \in \mathbb{R}^2,
\label{eq:local_coords}
\end{equation}
with the overlap condition
\begin{equation}
u^\beta(\bm{k}) = g_{\alpha\beta}^{-1}(\hat{\bm{k}})\, u^\alpha(\bm{k})
\quad \text{on } U_\alpha\cap U_\beta.
\label{eq:overlap_condition}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Connection 1-form as structural necessity]
\label{thm:connection_necessary}
Let $\mathcal{L}[u^\alpha]$ be a local, $SO(2)$-invariant Lagrangian density
for the transverse phonon section $u^\alpha$.
If $\mathcal{L}$ contains any term involving derivatives $\partial_\mu u^\alpha$,
then it necessarily depends on the derivatives only through the combination
\begin{equation}
D_\mu u^\alpha := \partial_\mu u^\alpha - A_\mu^{(\alpha)} u^\alpha,
\label{eq:covariant_derivative}
\end{equation}
where $A_\mu^{(\alpha)}$ is a $\mathfrak{so}(2) \cong i\mathbb{R}$-valued
connection 1-form transforming under frame changes as
\begin{equation}
A_\mu^{(\beta)} = g_{\alpha\beta}^{-1} A_\mu^{(\alpha)} g_{\alpha\beta}
+ g_{\alpha\beta}^{-1}\partial_\mu g_{\alpha\beta}.
\label{eq:A_gauge_transform}
\end{equation}
The $U(1)$ gauge field $A_\mu$ is thus structurally mandatory, not optional.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The Lagrangian density $\mathcal{L}[u^\alpha]$ must be a function of local,
patch-independent (i.e., globally defined) quantities.
The field $u^\alpha$ itself transforms inhomogeneously under frame changes
\eqref{eq:overlap_condition}, so $u^\alpha$ is not globally defined.
The ordinary partial derivative $\partial_\mu u^\alpha$ transforms as
\begin{equation}
\partial_\mu u^\beta
= g_{\alpha\beta}^{-1}\partial_\mu u^\alpha
+ (\partial_\mu g_{\alpha\beta}^{-1}) u^\alpha.
\label{eq:partial_transforms}
\end{equation}
The second term is an inhomogeneous connection term.
Therefore $\partial_\mu u^\alpha$ is not a section of any well-defined vector
bundle, and $|\partial_\mu u^\alpha|^2$ is not a globally defined quantity.

The unique remedy is to introduce a connection $A_\mu$ on the bundle $E_T$
such that the covariant derivative
\begin{equation}
D_\mu u^\alpha = \partial_\mu u^\alpha - A_\mu^{(\alpha)} u^\alpha
\label{eq:covariant_der_proof}
\end{equation}
transforms homogeneously:
\begin{equation}
D_\mu u^\beta = g_{\alpha\beta}^{-1} D_\mu u^\alpha.
\label{eq:Dmu_transforms}
\end{equation}
Then $|D_\mu u|^2 := g_{ab}\,(D_\mu u^a)^*(D_\mu u^b)$ is globally well-defined
and $SO(2)$-invariant.

Therefore any local derivative term in $\mathcal{L}$ must use $D_\mu$, not
$\partial_\mu$.
The connection $A_\mu$ is not a free additional field---it is the
\emph{structurally required} object for writing a well-defined derivative term.
The transition between patches forces its existence through \eqref{eq:A_gauge_transform}.
\end{proof}

\section{The Natural Connection on $E_T \cong TS^2$}

\subsection{The Levi-Civita connection as the canonical $U(1)$ connection}

The bundle $E_T|_{S^2} \cong TS^2$ carries a canonical connection: the
Levi-Civita connection of the round metric on $S^2$.
In local coordinates $(\theta,\phi)$, the connection 1-form is
\begin{equation}
\omega_{\mathrm{LC}} = \cos\theta\, d\phi \in \Omega^1(U) \otimes \mathfrak{so}(2).
\label{eq:LC_connection}
\end{equation}
Its curvature is the area form of $S^2$:
\begin{equation}
F_{\mathrm{LC}} = d\omega_{\mathrm{LC}} = -\sin\theta\, d\theta\wedge d\phi
= -d\Omega_{S^2}.
\label{eq:LC_curvature}
\end{equation}
Integrating over $S^2$:
\begin{equation}
\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{S^2} F_{\mathrm{LC}} = -\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{S^2} d\Omega = -2,
\label{eq:LC_Chern_number}
\end{equation}
confirming the first Chern number $c_1 = 2$ (with sign convention).

\subsection{Physical identification with the emergent photon}

The Levi-Civita connection \eqref{eq:LC_connection} is defined on the momentum-space
bundle.
The corresponding \emph{spacetime} $U(1)$ gauge field is obtained by promoting
the momentum-space parameter $\hat{\bm{k}}$ to a slowly varying spacetime field,
or equivalently by pulling back the momentum-space connection to the spacetime
effective action.

\begin{proposition}[Berry connection = emergent photon field]
\label{prop:Berry_photon}
In the low-energy effective theory, where the transverse phonon carries the
bundle structure of $E_T$, the natural $U(1)$ connection on $E_T$ identifies
with the electromagnetic vector potential:
\begin{equation}
A_\mu(\bm{x},t) \leftrightarrow \omega_{\mathrm{LC}}\text{-component of the }E_T\text{-connection}.
\label{eq:A_identification}
\end{equation}
The curvature $F_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu A_\nu - \partial_\nu A_\mu$ is the
emergent electromagnetic field strength.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The phonon field $\bm{u}_T(\bm{x},t)$ is a section of $E_T$ over
momentum space (or, in position space, a field with the constraint
$\nabla\cdot\bm{u}_T = 0$).
The effective action for $\bm{u}_T$ must be written using the covariant
derivative $D_\mu$ associated with the $E_T$-connection
(Theorem~\ref{thm:connection_necessary}).
The natural choice for this connection is the Levi-Civita connection
\eqref{eq:LC_connection}.
The connection 1-form $\omega_{\mathrm{LC}}$ thus plays the role of $A_\mu$
in the effective action.
\end{proof}

\section{Derivation of the Maxwell Action by Integrating Out Massive Modes}

The bundle-obstruction argument of Section 3 establishes that $A_\mu$ must
exist.
It does not yet prove that $A_\mu$ has a kinetic (Maxwell) action.
We now derive the Maxwell action by integrating out the massive modes of the
TECT condensate.

\subsection{Decomposition of the condensate field}

Near the BCC ground state, decompose the condensate field as
\begin{equation}
\Psi(\bm{x},t) = \rho_0 z_0 + \Psi_{\mathrm{fluct}},
\label{eq:condensate_decomp}
\end{equation}
where $\rho_0 z_0$ is the BCC background and $\Psi_{\mathrm{fluct}}$ comprises
the fluctuations.
The spectrum of $\Psi_{\mathrm{fluct}}$ splits into:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Gapped amplitude mode} (``Higgs''): mass $\sim \sqrt{2g}\rho_0$;
\item \textbf{Gapped longitudinal phonon}: mass $\sim c_L q_*$;
\item \textbf{Gapless transverse phonons} $\bm{u}_T$: the Goldstone modes.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Matter sector coupled to $A_\mu$}

Following Section 3, the full quadratic action for the transverse phonon
coupled to the $U(1)$ connection $A_\mu$ is:
\begin{equation}
S_T[u_T, A]
=
\int d^4x\,\frac{\rho_{\mathrm{eff}}}{2}
\bigl[
|D_t u_T|^2 - c_*^2|D_i u_T|^2
\bigr],
\label{eq:S_T_coupled}
\end{equation}
where
\begin{equation}
D_\mu u_T^a := \partial_\mu u_T^a + \epsilon^{ab} A_\mu u_T^b,
\qquad a,b=1,2.
\label{eq:Dmu_explicit}
\end{equation}
Here $\epsilon^{ab}$ is the $SO(2)$-generator, acting as
$\epsilon^{ab} = \begin{pmatrix}0&-1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}^{ab}$.

\subsection{Integrating out the massive modes: heat-kernel derivation}

Now consider the full action including the gapped amplitude mode $\sigma$
(the ``Higgs'') minimally coupled to $A_\mu$:
\begin{equation}
S_{\mathrm{full}}[\sigma, A]
=
\int d^4x\,
\left[
\frac{1}{2}|D_\mu \sigma|^2 + \frac{m_\sigma^2}{2}|\sigma|^2
\right],
\qquad
D_\mu\sigma := \partial_\mu\sigma - iA_\mu\sigma,
\label{eq:S_Higgs}
\end{equation}
where $m_\sigma = \sqrt{2g}\rho_0 > 0$ is the Higgs mass.

\begin{theorem}[Induced Maxwell action from integrating out the Higgs]
\label{thm:Maxwell_induced}
Let $\sigma$ be a massive complex scalar with mass $m_\sigma > 0$, minimally
coupled to the $U(1)$ connection $A_\mu$ through the covariant derivative
$D_\mu = \partial_\mu - iA_\mu$.
Integrating out $\sigma$ at one loop induces the gauge-invariant effective action
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}[A]
=
-\frac{1}{4e^2}\int d^4x\, F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}
+ O\!\bigl(F^3/m_\sigma^2\bigr),
\label{eq:Maxwell_action}
\end{equation}
where $F_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu A_\nu - \partial_\nu A_\mu$ and
\begin{equation}
\frac{1}{e^2} = \frac{1}{48\pi^2}\log\frac{\Lambda^2}{m_\sigma^2} > 0.
\label{eq:e2_induced}
\end{equation}
The coefficient is strictly positive for any UV cutoff $\Lambda > m_\sigma$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The one-loop effective action is
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}^{(1)}[A]
=
-\log\int \mathcal{D}\sigma\,\mathcal{D}\sigma^*\, e^{-S_E[\sigma,A]}
=
\mathrm{Tr}\log(-D^2 + m_\sigma^2),
\label{eq:one_loop_det}
\end{equation}
where $S_E$ is the Euclidean version of \eqref{eq:S_Higgs} and
$D^2 = D_\mu D^\mu$ is the covariant Laplacian.

Use the Schwinger proper-time representation:
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}^{(1)}[A]
=
-\int_0^\infty \frac{ds}{s}\, e^{-sm_\sigma^2}\,\mathrm{Tr}\, e^{-s(-D^2)}.
\label{eq:proper_time}
\end{equation}

For a $U(1)$ gauge background in flat $\mathbb{R}^4$, the heat-kernel expansion
of $\mathrm{Tr}\,e^{s D^2}$ is:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Tr}\, e^{-s(-D^2)}
\sim
\frac{1}{(4\pi s)^2}
\int d^4x\,
\left[
a_0 + s\, a_1 + s^2\, a_2 + \cdots
\right],
\label{eq:heat_kernel}
\end{equation}
where the Seeley-DeWitt coefficients for a $U(1)$-charged scalar are:
\begin{align}
a_0 &= 1,
\label{eq:a0}\\
a_1 &= 0 \quad \text{(no linear curvature term for }U(1)),
\label{eq:a1}\\
a_2 &= \frac{1}{12} F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}.
\label{eq:a2}
\end{align}

Substituting \eqref{eq:heat_kernel} into \eqref{eq:proper_time} and
extracting the $s^2 a_2$ term after Schwinger parameter integration:
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}^{(1)}[A]
\supset
-\frac{1}{12(4\pi)^2}
\left(\int_0^{\Lambda^{-2}} \frac{ds}{s}\, e^{-sm_\sigma^2}\right)
\int d^4x\, F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}.
\label{eq:Gamma_F2}
\end{equation}
The Schwinger integral evaluates to:
\begin{equation}
\int_0^{\Lambda^{-2}} \frac{ds}{s}\, e^{-sm_\sigma^2}
= E_1(m_\sigma^2/\Lambda^2)
\approx \log\frac{\Lambda^2}{m_\sigma^2} > 0
\label{eq:schwinger_integral}
\end{equation}
for $\Lambda \gg m_\sigma$.
Therefore:
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_{\mathrm{eff}}^{(1)}[A]
\supset
-\frac{\log(\Lambda^2/m_\sigma^2)}{12(4\pi)^2}
\int d^4x\, F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}
=:
-\frac{1}{4e^2}
\int d^4x\, F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu},
\label{eq:Maxwell_result}
\end{equation}
with the positive coefficient
\begin{equation}
\frac{1}{e^2}
= \frac{1}{3(4\pi)^2}\log\frac{\Lambda^2}{m_\sigma^2} > 0.
\label{eq:coefficient_explicit}
\end{equation}
This is the Maxwell action with a positive kinetic coefficient, completing
the proof.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Gauge invariance of the Maxwell action}

\begin{corollary}[Gauge invariance]
\label{cor:gauge_inv}
The induced action \eqref{eq:Maxwell_action} is invariant under the local $U(1)$
transformation
\begin{equation}
A_\mu(\bm{x},t) \mapsto A_\mu(\bm{x},t) + \partial_\mu\alpha(\bm{x},t),
\qquad \alpha \in C^\infty(\mathbb{R}^{3,1}).
\label{eq:U1_local_transf}
\end{equation}
The local gauge symmetry of \eqref{eq:Maxwell_action} is not assumed---it
follows from the gauge invariance of $F_{\mu\nu}$ and the heat-kernel
derivation.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Under \eqref{eq:U1_local_transf}, $F_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu A_\nu - \partial_\nu A_\mu$
is invariant ($\partial_\mu\partial_\nu\alpha = \partial_\nu\partial_\mu\alpha$).
Therefore the action \eqref{eq:Maxwell_action} is invariant.
The one-loop derivation preserves this invariance because the operator
$-D^2 + m_\sigma^2$ has a gauge-invariant spectrum.
\end{proof}

\section{Main Promotion Theorem}

We now state the complete theorem resolving the gap in Math09.

\begin{theorem}[Promotion of $SO(2)_{\mathrm{global}}$ to $U(1)_{\mathrm{local}}$]
\label{thm:main_promotion}
Assume the following conditions hold in the TECT BCC condensate:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textbf{(G1)}] The acoustic phonon field $\bm{u}(\bm{x},t)$ satisfies the
  transverse constraint $\nabla\cdot\bm{u}_T = 0$, defining the transverse
  polarization bundle $E_T$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:ET_bundle}).
\item[\textbf{(G2)}] The effective action for $\bm{u}_T$ is local and
  $SO(2)$-invariant.
\item[\textbf{(G3)}] The condensate has a massive amplitude mode (``Higgs'')
  $\sigma$ with mass $m_\sigma > 0$, minimally coupled to the $U(1)$ connection.
\item[\textbf{(G4)}] There exists a UV cutoff $\Lambda > m_\sigma$.
\end{enumerate}
Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] \textbf{Existence:} A $U(1)$ connection 1-form $A_\mu$ is
  \emph{structurally necessary} for writing a well-defined local derivative
  term in the effective action (Theorem~\ref{thm:connection_necessary}).
  It is not a postulate.
\item[(b)] \textbf{Dynamics:} Integrating out $\sigma$ at one loop generates
  the induced Maxwell action (Theorem~\ref{thm:Maxwell_induced}):
  \begin{equation}
  \Gamma_{\mathrm{Maxwell}}[A]
  =
  -\frac{1}{4e^2}\int d^4x\, F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu},
  \qquad
  \frac{1}{e^2} > 0.
  \label{eq:final_Maxwell}
  \end{equation}
\item[(c)] \textbf{Local gauge symmetry:} The action \eqref{eq:final_Maxwell}
  is invariant under the local $U(1)$ transformation
  \eqref{eq:U1_local_transf} (Corollary~\ref{cor:gauge_inv}).
  The local symmetry is not assumed---it is derived.
\item[(d)] \textbf{Summary:} The promotion
  \begin{equation}
  \boxed{SO(2)_{\mathrm{global}} \longrightarrow U(1)_{\mathrm{local}}}
  \label{eq:promotion_proved}
  \end{equation}
  is a theorem, not a postulate.
  It follows from the non-trivial topology of $E_T$ (structural necessity)
  and the heat-kernel induction of the Maxwell kinetic term (dynamical origin).
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Part (a): Theorem~\ref{thm:connection_necessary}.

Part (b): Theorem~\ref{thm:Maxwell_induced}.

Part (c): Corollary~\ref{cor:gauge_inv}.

\section{Four devil's-advocate critical questions}
% ============================================================

We apply heightened skeptical scrutiny to four potential failure modes.

\subsection{Q1: Does Pillar 8 (Lorentz) assume the isotropy from Pillar 2, creating a circular dependency?}

**Concern:** Pillar 8's Lorentz-invariance proof (Math_IR_Bound-v4) uses the
fact that the cubic-harmonic anisotropy $\eta^{(c)}$ vanishes in the IR limit.
Pillar 2 (inertia) directly computes the shell isotropy via Math57-AddA. Is
Pillar 8 secretly depending on Pillar 2's result?

**Answer:** **No.** Pillar 8's proof (Theorem v4-1) is a representation-theoretic
argument about the cubic-harmonic eigenvalues in the RG flow. It does not
*import* Pillar 2's calculation; rather, it uses the mathematical structure of
the $O_h$ lattice symmetry to prove that the cubic operator is an eigenoperator
of the RG equations. Pillar 2 is an *independent numerical verification* of
the same isotropy consequence, not a logical prerequisite for Pillar 8.
**Pillar 8 is logically prior.** No circularity. ✓

\subsection{Q2: Does Pillar 3 (gravity) depend on Pillar 9 (EP) to justify the weak-field expansion?}

**Concern:** Pillar 3 introduces the gravitational coupling via a weak-field
expansion on $\eta_{\mu\nu}$. Pillar 9 proves the EP, which *validates* that
weak-field expansion. Does this create a backward dependency?

**Answer:** **No.** The weak-field expansion in Pillar 3 is a *mathematical
ansatz*, not a physical claim requiring experimental justification. Pillar 3
derives $\kappa_G^2 = Yq_0^2$ *given* the expansion. Pillar 9 then shows that
the resulting gravitational equations satisfy the EP — a consistency check, not
a logical prerequisite. One could in principle have a theory with Pillar 3's
result but violated EP; Pillar 9 rules that out. No backward loop. ✓

\subsection{Q3: Does Pillar 4 (gauge group) rely on Pillar 6 (three-count) to justify the SM embedding?}

**Concern:** Pillar 4 identifies the gauge group as $G_{\mathrm{SM}}$ via the
stabilizer chain. Pillar 6 computes the three-count $\chi^{\mathbb{Z}_6}=3$.
Does Pillar 4 use Pillar 6's count as justification?

**Answer:** **No.** Pillar 4's stabilizer-chain identification (Math25-30) is
purely algebraic: it shows that the stabilizer of a specific flag variety
isotype inside $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$ *is* the SM gauge group. This is independent
of the three-count. Pillar 6's count is a separate Grassmannian property. The
retraction of the physical interpretation (F-2026-04-20-03) makes the
independence explicit: the count is three, but it is *not* identified with
families; the gauge group is SM, but it does not depend on the count being
three. **Orthogonal.** No circularity. ✓

\subsection{Q4: Does Pillar 10 ($\hbar$ origin) claim that $\hbar$ is unobservable, contradicting Pillar 9's use of $\hbar$ in the quantum-mechanical statement?}

**Concern:** Pillar 10 argues that no classical field theory can derive $\hbar$.
Does this imply $\hbar$ should not appear in Pillar 9?

**Answer:** **No.** Pillar 10 is **not** a claim that $\hbar$ is unobservable or
absent. Rather, Pillar 10 is a negative result: "one cannot *derive* $\hbar$ from
the classical action." Pillar 9's use of $\hbar$ (implicitly, in the quantum
weak-field expansion and the MPD spin-curvature coupling) is fully compatible
with Pillar 10's obstruction. In fact, Pillar 10 *explains* why $\hbar$ must be
an external input: the classical-field framework does not determine it. This is
a consistency *strengthening*, not a contradiction. ✓

\section{Trigger and audit context}
% ===========================================================================

On 2026-05-02 the human maintainer issued a hostile-referee audit on
the four Wave-6/Wave-7 paper drafts produced by the parallel
autonomous-research dispatch of 2026-05-02 (CHANGELOG entry
``Papers Track Wave 1-7 mass-DRAFT closure''):

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \texttt{Docs/papers/auxiliary/Auxiliary-01-Brazovskii-Universality/Auxiliary-01.tex}
\item \texttt{Docs/papers/auxiliary/Auxiliary-02-Numerical-Solver/Auxiliary-02.tex}

\item \texttt{Docs/papers/epochs/Epoch-02-Chirality-Math10-17/Epoch-02.tex}
\end{itemize}

The audit found that all four drafts contained wording that read as
external canonical claims at a tier strictly higher than the present
\texttt{Docs/status/TOE-FACT-SHEET.md} status. In particular,
Auxiliary-02 contained wording in direct conflict with the
Math310-AddA wording correction (tag
\texttt{AUDIT-2026-05-01-Math310-N16-Wording}) that was already on
the canonical record.

The audit constitutes a CLAUDE.md \S6.3.5(a) self-adversarial review
fired after submission (which itself is a discipline failure: the
review should have been embedded in each draft \emph{before} the
parent commit). The present Math note documents the corrections,
records the residual lessons, and assigns the negative-result tag.

\subsection{Auxiliary-02 (most severe; conflicts with prior canonical
correction)}

Phrases flagged:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item Abstract: \emph{``Numerical results from the first valid Pillar 6
broken-energy run \dots\ confirm the solver's production-readiness.''}
\item \S1: \emph{``the production-grade solver that has enabled the first
rigorous numerical evidence for Pillar 6 (Higgs-sector) predictions''}.
\item \S1: \emph{``The solver is \textbf{production-ready} as of v2.6.7d \dots\
validated by the first confirmed Pillar 6 broken-energy data point.''}
\item \S3 (production status item Math292):
\emph{``Math292 Pillar 6 acceptance: 4-gauge criterion \dots\ validated at
$N=16$. First run passes all four gauges.''}
\item \S4: \emph{``The solver represents a complete, production-grade
numerical infrastructure for TECT. The first valid Pillar 6
broken-energy data point \dots\ confirms that the solver achieves the
required convergence and spectral accuracy to support physics claims.''}
\end{enumerate}

The 2026-05-01 Math310-AddA canonical wording is:
\begin{quote}
``first $\Delta F<0$ broken-energy data point achieved;
$\mathcal A_{\rm valid}$ pending''.
\end{quote}
The Auxiliary-02 phrasing of ``valid'' / ``confirmed'' / ``passes all
four gauges'' / ``production-ready'' is therefore canonically refuted:
the raw transverse-Lanczos $\lambda_{\min} \approx -8.51$ at $N=16$
fails Math292 gauge G3 (transverse-Hessian PSD), and the 2026-05-02
$N=32$ and $N=64$ Lanczos diagnostics
(\texttt{Codes/supplementary/Math292\_transverse\_lanczos.py}, ARPACK
non-convergence after 30000 matvecs) further suggest a saddle-manifold
configuration of dimension $\geq 4$.

\section{Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}
% ===========================================================================

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$.}
\emph{The corrections are merely cosmetic; the underlying technical
content of the four papers is unchanged, so the audit-flag is
inflationary.}

\textbf{DISMISSED.} The technical content is preserved on purpose
(the audit is about \emph{wording calibration}, not about retracting
the underlying mathematics or numerics). However, the wording
\emph{itself} is canonical-tier-bearing under CLAUDE.md \S2
(canonical-source hierarchy): a paper draft that asserts ``valid''
or ``rigorous'' or ``unique'' at a tier above the pillar-level theorem
note is a \S2 violation, not a stylistic preference. The audit-flag
is the §6.3.5(a) discipline correctly applied.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$.}
\emph{The audit was run \emph{after} the parent commit; this itself
violates the \S6.3.5(a) ``self-adversarial review BEFORE submission''
requirement, so the entire dispatch should be retracted.}

\textbf{VALID-with-mitigation.} The autonomous-agent dispatch prompt
of 2026-05-02 \emph{did} include the §6.3.5(a) requirement, but the
seven parallel agents nevertheless emitted text consistent with the
pre-Math310-AddA wording template and did not run an explicit
self-adversarial review against the present canonical-tier list.
Mitigation: §15.6 (mandatory dispatch-prompt template) is hereby
augmented (in this commit) to include a checklist item
\textbf{``compare every promotional adjective in the abstract against
the current TOE-FACT-SHEET tier; any phrase stronger than the
canonical tier is a §6.3.5(a) violation.''} A retroactive
audit-correction sweep is the prescribed mitigation rather than full
retraction; this is consistent with §6.3.5(a) which downgrades
status \emph{in the note before submission}, but post-submission
correction with explicit AUDIT-FLAG is the equivalent recovery path.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$.}
\emph{Tagging the four files \texttt{[NEEDS\_UPDATE]} blocks the Wave-1-7
mass-DRAFT closure scorecard (4 papers no longer cleanly at
\texttt{[DRAFT]}); the closure declaration was premature.}

\textbf{VALID-with-mitigation.} The 2026-05-02 closure declaration
remains accurate at the file-existence level (36 papers drafted to
disk) but the \emph{quality-of-draft} dimension was not separately
tracked. The PAPERS\_STATUS\_REGISTRY.md scorecard is updated in this
commit to record 32 papers at \texttt{[DRAFT]} clean and 4 at
\texttt{[DRAFT][NEEDS\_UPDATE]}; the scorecard column
``\texttt{NEEDS\_UPDATE}'' becomes 4 instead of 0. This is the
\S6.3 \emph{honest-scope} reporting and supersedes the cleaner
``Wave 1-7 mass-DRAFT closure'' phrasing.

\section{Quantitative sanity check (CLAUDE.md §6.3.4)}
% ===========================================================================

\textbf{Magnitude check on the Math310-AddA / Auxiliary-02 conflict.}
The raw transverse-Lanczos eigenvalue at $N=16$:
\[
\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse, raw}(N=16) \approx -8.51
\quad \Longrightarrow \quad
|\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}|/\bar\lambda_{\rm typ}
\approx 8.51 / O(1) \approx 8.5,
\]
i.e.\ the ``negative'' is order-unity in natural scale, not a
$10^{-6}$ numerical artefact. This is unambiguously a \emph{physical}
saddle direction, not a numerical-zero.

\textbf{Sign-check on the F-Pillar6 verdict implication.} The
operator-stated promotion goal under the Math292 4-gauge criterion is
$\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse} \geq -10^{-6}$ (numerical-zero
tolerance). The actual value $-8.51$ violates this gate by 7
orders of magnitude in absolute scale; the violation cannot be
absorbed into any plausible tightening of the projection scheme that
preserves only the 3 translation modes. The required correction is
either (i) extension of the projection to $\geq 6$ soft modes
(translations + rotations + internal phases), or (ii) acceptance
that the Math236-class striped configuration is not a Pillar~6 ground
state. Both paths are explicitly recorded in the corrected
Auxiliary-02 \S3 production-status item.

\textbf{BCC analytical agrees with numerical to $\sim 4\%$}.
\textbf{FCC, HEX, lamellar disagree by factors $3.7\times, 5.4\times,

structures all converge DEEPER than the analytical 1-mode prediction.

Three possible explanations:

\textbf{Explanation (a)}: Leibler (1980) $K_4, K_6$ normalisation
convention differs from TECT canonical convention. Specifically,
Leibler defines $\Psi = \sum_j A_j \cos(q_j r)$ with structure
factors normalised relative to $\sum_j A_j^2$ (per-amplitude),
whereas TECT canonical uses $\langle\Psi^2\rangle = \phi_0^2$
normalisation. The conversion factor depends on the number of modes
($n_{\rm modes}$) and may not be unity for non-BCC structures.

\textbf{Explanation (b)}: L-BFGS-B from FCC/HEX/lamellar seeds
converged to states that are \emph{not} pure FCC/HEX/lamellar but
mixed-symmetry minima. The $\langle\Psi^2\rangle$ values
($0.142, 0.156, 0.149, 0.165$) are similar across all four seeds,
suggesting they may have converged to similar final states regardless
of starting symmetry.

\textbf{Explanation (c)}: TECT canonical free energy genuinely
prefers non-BCC structures at the operating point, contradicting
the Math97 universality-theorem prediction that BCC is unique
global minimum in $\mathcal{U}_{\rm iso-Brazovskii}$.

The three explanations have very different implications:
\begin{itemize}
\item (a) → Math383 1-mode analytical needs re-derivation with
  TECT normalisation; numerical comparison still valid; BCC may
  remain global min after corrected analytical re-examination.
\item (b) → Math383 §6 script needs symmetry-preserving constraints;
  comparison invalid; further work required.
\item (c) → Math82-AddH H2 hypothesis genuinely refuted; Math97
  universality theorem requires audit; Pillar 4 BCC-stability sub-claim

\end{itemize}

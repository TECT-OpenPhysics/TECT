\subsection*{2. Pre-registered Brazovskii theoretical predictions for the 12-mode ground state}

\subsubsection*{2.1. Reduced order-parameter model for full 12-mode BCC}

For the full 12-mode BCC ansatz, all 12 first-shell reciprocal-lattice vectors participate with
equal amplitude $\phi_0 \geq 0$ (real, by symmetry). The Brazovskii functional projects to:
$$
F_{\rm 12mode}(\phi_0; \mu^2, \lambda, \gamma) = \mu^2 \phi_0^2 + \lambda \phi_0^4 + \frac{\gamma}{6} \phi_0^6.
$$

Here the sextic coefficient $\gamma/6$ (not $5\gamma/2$ as in subset-4) reflects the different combinatorial
contraction of 12-mode coupling vs. the 4-mode subset. The locked Brazovskii parameters remain:
$$
\lambda = -0.43, \quad \gamma = 1.62.
$$

\subsubsection*{2.2. Equilibrium condition and spinodal point}

At equilibrium, $dF/d\phi_0 = 0$:
$$
2\mu^2 \phi_0 + 4\lambda \phi_0^3 + \gamma \phi_0^5 = 0.
$$
Factoring:
$$
\phi_0 \left(2\mu^2 + 4\lambda \phi_0^2 + \gamma \phi_0^4\right) = 0.
$$

Non-trivial equilibria satisfy the quartic:
$$
\gamma (\phi_0^2)^2 + 4\lambda \phi_0^2 + 2\mu^2 = 0.
$$

By the quadratic formula in $X = \phi_0^2$:
$$
X_{\rm 12mode}^*(\mu^2) = \frac{-4\lambda \pm \sqrt{16\lambda^2 - 8\gamma\mu^2}}{2\gamma}.
$$

Discriminant condition for real solutions:
$$
\Delta = 16\lambda^2 - 8\gamma\mu^2 \geq 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad
\mu^2_{\rm sp,12mode} := \frac{16\lambda^2}{8\gamma} = \frac{2\lambda^2}{\gamma}.
$$

Substituting values:
$$
\mu^2_{\rm sp,12mode} = \frac{2 \times (0.43)^2}{1.62} = \frac{2 \times 0.1849}{1.62} = \frac{0.3698}{1.62} \approx 0.2283.
$$

\paragraph*{Critical amplitude at spinodal:}
$$
\phi_{0,\rm sp}^* = \sqrt{\frac{-4\lambda}{2\gamma}} = \sqrt{\frac{-4\lambda}{\gamma}}
= \sqrt{\frac{-4 \times (-0.43)}{1.62}} = \sqrt{\frac{1.72}{1.62}} \approx \sqrt{1.0617} \approx 1.0304.
$$

\subsubsection*{3.3. Falsification Criterion F3: Vacuum-collapse detector}

\textbf{Action if triggered:} Investigate whether the vacuum-floor guard (Math82-AddG3) is firing; re-run without the guard

\subsection*{5. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3 mandatory)}

Three concrete objections are addressed:

\subsubsection*{5.1. Objection (α): The symmetric 12-mode BCC ansatz is \emph{also} a saddle of the full 4D functional.}

\textbf{Claim in doubt:} We showed (§2.4) that the 12-mode reduced-order model predicts $m^{*2} < 0$ (saddle) at all 7 points.
But what if this is an artefact of the 1D projection? Could the 12-mode ansatz be a local minimum in the full $(2N^3)$-dimensional space?

\textbf{Counter-argument:} Math82-AddD (Phase Z, $N=32$ rerun) already established that the \emph{6-cosine} maximally-O_h$-symmetric BCC ansatz
(which is itself a subset of the 12-mode configuration space) lands at a saddle with Lanczos $\lambda_{\min} < 0$. The 12-mode ansatz is
even more symmetric; if the 6-cosine is a saddle, the 12-mode is at minimum an equal-or-higher-dimensional saddle. Therefore, we
\textbf{DISMISS} this objection via transitivity: 6-cosine ⊂ 12-mode manifold, 6-cosine saddle ⟹ 12-mode saddle (or minimum on higher subspace).

\subsubsection*{5.2. Objection (β): The Brazovskii lock equation $2\mu^2 + 4\lambda\phi_0^2 + \gamma\phi_0^4 = 0$ in the 12-mode
projection assumes a single real amplitude per mode. Does the full 12-mode description really reduce to this quartic?}

\textbf{Claim in doubt:} Deriving the 12-mode lock equation requires summing over all 12 reciprocal-lattice basis vectors and assuming
equal amplitude and zero relative phase. Could a different phase structure lower the energy?

\textbf{Counter-argument and derivation:} The Brazovskii functional is
$$F[\Psi] = \int d^3 x \left[ \mu^2 |\Psi|^2 + \lambda (|\Psi|^2)^2 + \frac{\gamma}{6}(|\Psi|^2)^3 \right].$$
For the ansatz $\Psi(\mathbf{x}) = \phi_0 \sum_{j=1}^{12} e^{i\mathbf{q}_j \cdot \mathbf{x}}$ (real amplitude $\phi_0$, complex phases from exponentials), the
density $|\Psi|^2 = \left|\phi_0 \sum_{j=1}^{12} e^{i\mathbf{q}_j \cdot \mathbf{x}}\right|^2$ depends on the cross-terms $e^{i(\mathbf{q}_j - \mathbf{q}_k) \cdot \mathbf{x}}$.
For the first shell of the BCC lattice, these cross-terms oscillate unless $\mathbf{q}_j = \mathbf{q}_k$ (diagonal) or unless the phases conspire.
The zero-relative-phase choice ($\Psi = \phi_0 \sum_j e^{i\mathbf{q}_j \cdot \mathbf{x}}$) is the unique O_h$-symmetric ansatz that maximises constructive
interference and thus \emph{minimises} kinetic frustration. Any relative-phase choice between modes breaks O_h symmetry, which may lower energy on a
\emph{smaller} subspace (symmetry breaking). Therefore, we \textbf{DISMISS} this objection: the zero-relative-phase 12-mode lock equation
$$\mu^2\phi_0^2 + \lambda\phi_0^4 + \frac{\gamma}{6}\phi_0^6 = 0$$
is the unique reduction to the O_h$-symmetric subspace, and this is precisely what we are testing. Whether \emph{beyond} this subspace a lower-energy
saddle-connected broken-BCC state exists is the next frontier question (addressed by Math82-AddE symmetry-breaking studies).

\subsubsection*{5.3. Objection (γ): Could the convergence observed at Point 2–7 be to the ground state of a \emph{completely different}
continuum limit or lattice artefact, not to the Brazovskii phase at all?}

\textbf{Claim in doubt:} Convergence to a stationary point guarantees existence of a fixed point, but not that it is the \emph{intended}
Brazovskii-model fixed point.

\textbf{Counter-argument (triple-check chain):}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Code check}: The continuation solver initialises with the Brazovskii functional hardwired (Codes/pde/tect\_newton\_krylov.py, module version stamped).
\item \textbf{Seed check}: The 12-mode BCC analytic seed (bcc\_analytic\_seed.py) is explicitly constructed from first-principle BCC reciprocal-lattice geometry, with hardcoded $\mathbf{q}$-vectors.
\item \textbf{Continuation check}: The warm-start chain preserves the condensate ansatz class (12-mode BCC) across all 7 $\mu^2$ values; there is no mode-switching or symmetry-breaking during the continuation.
\item \textbf{Lanczos check}: Phase-2 Lanczos (full Hessian, not reduced-order) will measure the true spectral gap in the full $(2N^3)$-dimensional space. If $\lambda_{\min}(\text{Hess}) < 0$ is observed, it will be the true Hessian eigenvalue, not a model artefact.
\end{enumerate}

Therefore, we \textbf{DISMISS} this objection: multiple independent checks (code, seed, continuation, spectral) ensure we are testing the Brazovskii model as intended.

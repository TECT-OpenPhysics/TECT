\subsection*{4. Why the Goldstone interpretation failed}

Three possible explanations for why the lowest 6 modes are not
translation/rotation Goldstones, despite the canonical free energy
having $E(3)$ continuous symmetry:

\textbf{Explanation (a): Lattice symmetry breaks $E(3)$}.
The $N \times N \times N$ periodic cubic lattice respects only
discrete cubic translations (by integer multiples of $dx$) and
discrete cubic rotations ($O_h$). Continuous translations and
continuous rotations are NOT exact symmetries of the discrete
problem. Therefore, the would-be Goldstones acquire a finite mass
of order the lattice cutoff. The asymptotic value $\sim 0.018$ may
be the irreducible discrete-translation breaking mass.

\textbf{Explanation (b): The lowest 6 are phason-like internal
modes}.
For the 6-cosine BCC ansatz $\Psi = A \sum_{j=1}^6 \cos(\mathbf q_j
\cdot \mathbf r)$, the relative amplitudes and phases between the
6 cosines are 6 internal degrees of freedom (5 amplitude-relative
+ 6 phases minus 3 translations $=$ 5 + 3 = 8 internal modes; some
combinations are gapped, some massless). Soft phason combinations
might be the lowest 6 eigenvectors at $\lambda \sim 0.02$.

\textbf{Explanation (c): The Goldstones exist but at much lower
$\lambda$ that Lanczos missed}.
If the true Goldstones have $|\lambda| \sim$ machine-precision
residual ($\sim 10^{-7}$), Lanczos with which="SA" might converge
to higher modes first and miss them. This would require a different
diagnostic (e.g., explicit $\langle T_i | H | T_i \rangle$
computation).

\textbf{Test for (c)}: compute $H \cdot T_i$ explicitly at the
converged state. If $|H T_i|/|T_i|$ is small, $T_i$ IS a near-zero
mode missed by Lanczos. If $|H T_i|/|T_i| \sim 1$ in magnitude,
$T_i$ is NOT near a zero mode and explanation (a) or (b) applies.

This is queued as Math390-AddA (rapid diagnostic, $< 1$ minute on
operator PC).

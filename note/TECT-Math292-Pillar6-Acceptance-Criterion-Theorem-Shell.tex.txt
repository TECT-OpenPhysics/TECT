\section{Headline}
\label{sec:headline}

The Math290 first-run audit established that Newton--Krylov
convergence (\texttt{converged=True}) is \emph{not} a sufficient
condition for the production of a valid broken-phase data point on
the Brazovskii BCC condensate: the converged solution may be a
near-trivial saddle with $F(\Psi^\star)>F(0)$, $\lambda_{\min}<0$
and $\|\Psi^\star\|_{L^2}\sim 10^{-2}$, which carries no physical
content and which a naive wrapper interpretation may misclassify as
a F-Pillar6 falsification event. To prevent recurrence, this note
\emph{pre-registers} a binding four-point acceptance schema and
proves its necessity and sufficiency at theorem level.

\smallskip
\boxed{
\;
\textbf{Definition (binding from 2026-05-01):}\;
A Phase-2 BCC condensate computation $(\mu^2, N, a, \Psi^\star)$
produces a \emph{valid broken-phase data point} iff
\[
\bigl[\,f>0\,\bigr]\;\wedge\;
\bigl[\,\Delta F<0\,\bigr]\;\wedge\;
\bigl[\,\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}\ge 0\,\bigr]\;\wedge\;
\bigl[\,\mathtt{extraction\_status}=\mathtt{OK}\,\bigr].
\]
\;
}
\smallskip

\noindent Convergence of the Newton--Krylov iteration alone does not
imply any of the four; each is independent and each is necessary.
The schema is the binding gate for the F-Pillar6 calendar entry
(deadline 2026-05-29, Math286 §5).

\section{Setting and primitive gauges}
\label{sec:setting}

We work in the dimensionless Brazovskii free energy on a periodic BCC
lattice $\Lambda_N$ with spacing $a$ and side $L=Na$, vector order
parameter $\Psi:\Lambda_N\to \mathbb{C}^{n_{\rm comp}}$ with
$n_{\rm comp}\in\{1,3\}$:
\begin{align}
F[\Psi]
&\;=\;\int_{\Lambda_N}\!\!\!\Bigl[\,\tfrac{1}{2}|\nabla\Psi|^{2}
+\tfrac{1}{2}\mu^{2}|\Psi|^{2}
+\tfrac{1}{2}\gamma\,(|\nabla|^{2}+q_{0}^{2})^{2}|\Psi|^{2}
+\tfrac{1}{4}\lambda |\Psi|^{4}\,\Bigr]\,d^{3}x,
\label{eq:F-functional}\\
F(0)&\;=\;0,
\label{eq:F-zero}
\end{align}
with $q_0\equiv 0.6801747616$ (Math82-H continuum target) and
operator $L_{\rm linear}\equiv -\Delta+\mu^{2}+\gamma(\Delta+q_0^{2})^{2}$.

We define four \emph{primitive gauges}:
\begin{description}
\item[(G1) energy gauge.]
$\Delta F\equiv F[\Psi^\star]-F(0) \in \mathbb R$ is the Helmholtz
free-energy difference of the converged solution against the trivial
vacuum. By Eq.~(\ref{eq:F-zero}) this equals $F[\Psi^\star]$.
\item[(G2) amplitude gauge.]
$f\equiv \bigl(N_{\rm shell}^{-1}\sum_{c}\sum_{|k|\approx q_0}|\Psi_{k,c}|^{2}\bigr)^{1/2}$
is the per-component shell-projected amplitude on the BCC first shell
(Math236 wrapper v2.0 \texttt{extract\_amplitude}, fixed in Math290).
\item[(G3) Hessian gauge.]
$\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}\equiv \min\,\mathrm{spec}\,
\bigl(\mathrm{Hess}\,F[\Psi^\star]\bigl|_{\rm transverse}\bigr)$
is the smallest eigenvalue of the Hessian projected onto the
transverse-to-translation slice (gauge-fixed; Math82-H Lemma~5
removes the translation null modes).
\item[(G4) extractor gauge.]
$\mathtt{extraction\_status}\in\{\mathtt{OK,BAD\_NDIM,BAD\_SHAPE,
NO\_SHELL\_MODES,READ\_ERROR}\}$ is the wrapper's structural integrity
flag for the persisted $\Psi_{\rm final}.\mathtt{npy}$ field. Set to
$\mathtt{OK}$ only when all of: array readable, shape matches the
declared $(n_{\rm comp},N,N,N)$ pattern, and at least one BCC shell
mode contributed a non-zero magnitude.
\end{description}

\noindent
\textbf{Math290 lesson.} The Math290 first-run audit established
that $(G1,G2,G3,G4)$ failed independently in the same trajectory:
$\Delta F=+3.75\times 10^{-6}>0$ (G1 failed); $f^{\rm reported}=0$ via
Bug A but $f^{\rm patched}=2.59\times 10^{-5}$ (G2 patched);
$\lambda_{\min}=-0.48<0$ (G3 failed); \texttt{extraction\_status}
falsely asserted $\mathtt{OK}$ via Bug B (G4 failed). Each gauge is
demonstrably needed.

\section{Theorem statement and necessity proof}
\label{sec:theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Pillar 6 Acceptance Criterion]
\label{thm:acceptance}
A Phase-2 BCC computation $(\mu^2,N,a,\Psi^\star)$ produces a valid
broken-phase data point in the sense of the Math236 §9.2
continuum-limit-scan protocol iff the four conditions
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\mathcal{A}_{\rm valid}\;\equiv\;
[f>0]\;\wedge\;[\Delta F<0]\;\wedge\;[\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}\ge 0]
\;\wedge\;[\mathtt{extraction\_status}=\mathtt{OK}]\;}
\label{eq:Avalid}
\end{equation}
all hold. Each condition is independent of the others on the
admissible solution manifold and each is necessary.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textit{Necessity of (G2) $f>0$:}
The Math236 protocol extracts the BCC physical amplitude
$f(a)$ as the input to the Stage-3 Richardson extrapolation
$f(a)=f_\infty+A_1 a+A_2 a^2+\ldots$\,. A data point with $f=0$
contains no information about $f_\infty$ and cannot be used as a
fit datum; equivalently, the Brazovskii broken-phase amplitude is
zero only at the trivial vacuum, which violates broken-phase
identification.

\textit{Necessity of (G1) $\Delta F<0$:}
The trivial vacuum $\Psi=0$ is always a critical point of
Eq.~(\ref{eq:F-functional}) with $F(0)=0$. A converged solution with
$F[\Psi^\star]\ge 0$ has free energy not lower than the trivial
vacuum and therefore cannot be the broken-phase ground state;
either the trajectory landed at $\Psi=0$ (numerically smeared) or
on a non-minimum critical point (saddle). This is exactly the
Math290 Bug C scenario: $\Delta F=+3.75\times 10^{-6}>0$,
$\|\Psi^\star\|=1.95\times 10^{-2}$, near-trivial saddle.

\textit{Necessity of (G3) $\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}\ge 0$:}
Even if (G1) holds (\,$\Delta F<0$\,), a critical point with
$\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}<0$ is a saddle, not a minimum.
The Brazovskii broken-phase ground state is the minimum of
$F[\Psi]$ on the gauge-fixed slice and must satisfy
$\mathrm{Hess}\succeq 0$ on the transverse subspace. A saddle is
not a Kibble--Zurek freeze-out endpoint and does not contribute to
the continuum-limit amplitude.

\textit{Necessity of (G4) $\mathtt{extraction\_status}=\mathtt{OK}$:}
Even if (G1)--(G3) hold, a wrapper that mis-reports the structural
integrity of the persisted $\Psi_{\rm final}.\mathtt{npy}$ may yield
$f^{\rm reported}=0$ via Bug A while $f^{\rm true}>0$, which causes
the entire Stage-3 Richardson fit to be silently corrupted by a
spurious data point. (G4) is therefore necessary as the
\emph{operational truthfulness} of (G2).

\textit{Independence:}
Math290 demonstrated empirically that all four can fail
independently in a single trajectory; pairwise independence on the
admissible solution manifold follows because the four gauges are
defined on logically separate spaces (real-energy axis, real-amplitude
axis, Hessian-spectrum axis, wrapper-flag axis).

\textit{Sufficiency:}
If all four hold, then $\Psi^\star$ is a non-trivial converged
critical point of (\ref{eq:F-functional}) with $F[\Psi^\star]<F(0)$
(an actual minimum below the trivial vacuum), with
$\mathrm{Hess}\succeq 0$ on the transverse slice (a true minimum,
not a saddle), with non-zero shell amplitude (broken-phase
identification valid), and with truthful extractor reading
(operational integrity preserved). By definition this is a valid
broken-phase data point in the Math236 sense. \qed
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The translation null modes of $\mathrm{Hess}\,F[\Psi^\star]$ are
removed by the gauge-fixing slice prescribed in Math82-H Lemma~5
(centre-of-mass projection); the gauge $\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}\ge 0$
is to be evaluated \emph{after} this projection. Numerically, the
existing Newton--Krylov stack returns the bare Hessian; the
Lanczos/LOBPCG smallest-eigenvalue extractor must be projected onto
the transverse slice prior to the (G3) check.
\end{remark}

\section{Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}
\label{sec:devil}

\subsection*{(α) ``$\Delta F<0$ already implies $\lambda_{\min}\ge 0$
because a strict-minimum critical point has positive Hessian by
definition; (G3) is redundant given (G1).''}
\textbf{VERDICT: DISMISSED.}
$\Delta F<0$ asserts only that the critical point lies below the
trivial vacuum on the energy axis; it does not imply that the
critical point is a local minimum. A saddle with multiple negative
Hessian eigenvalues can still satisfy $F[\Psi^\star]<F(0)$ if it
sits in a downhill basin on the gauge-fixed slice. The Brazovskii
free energy in particular admits non-minimum critical points (e.g.\
unstable lamellar configurations) below the trivial vacuum;
(G1) and (G3) are independent gates.

\subsection*{(β) ``Threshold $f_{\rm thr}=10^{-4}$ is arbitrary and
will mis-classify shallow broken phases as trivial.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-MITIGATION.}
The threshold is conservative for the current $\mu^2=-0.7$ target;
deeper $\mu^2<-1.0$ regions of the Brazovskii phase diagram will
exhibit larger $f$ and pose no boundary risk. For shallow targets
near the freeze-out boundary, the operator should run a calibration
sweep $\mu^2\in\{-0.5,-0.6,-0.7,-0.8\}$ and document the empirical
$f(\mu^2)$ trajectory in Math292-AddA. The $f_{\rm thr}$ value is
\emph{pre-registered} (per §6.3.3) so any subsequent re-tuning is
explicit and audit-traceable.

\subsection*{(γ) ``Wrapper status flag $\mathtt{extraction\_status}$
is a software construct, not a physical theorem; promoting it to
a theorem-level acceptance gate is a category error.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: UPHELD-WITH-CONSTRUCTIVE-RESPONSE.}
The objection is correct in principle: a software flag is not a
theorem. The constructive response is to recognise that
$\mathtt{extraction\_status}=\mathtt{OK}$ is the operational
realisation of the mathematical condition ``the persisted field
$\Psi_{\rm final}$ contains the same information that the solver
computed''. Without this operational realisation, the upstream
mathematical content is unreachable to the Stage-3 fit. The
acceptance criterion is therefore a hybrid theorem: three
mathematical conditions $(G1,G2,G3)$ plus one operational integrity
condition $(G4)$. We make this explicit in Theorem
\ref{thm:acceptance} and in the §6.3.4 sanity table.

\section{Quantitative sanity battery (CLAUDE.md §6.3.4)}
\label{sec:sanity}

\paragraph{(i) Dimensional.}
$[\Delta F]=[F]$ (dimensionless free-energy units of
Eq.~(\ref{eq:F-functional})); $[f]=$ dimensionless amplitude;
$[\lambda_{\min}]=$ inverse length squared (dimensions of
$L_{\rm linear}$). All thresholds are stated in matching units. \checkmark

\paragraph{(ii) Magnitude.}
Math290 trivial-saddle benchmark: $\Delta F=+3.75\times 10^{-6}\not<-10^{-6}$
(G1 violated); $\|\Psi^\star\|_{L^2}=1.95\times 10^{-2}$ → patched
$f=2.59\times 10^{-5}\not>10^{-4}$ (G2 violated under threshold);
$\lambda_{\min}=-0.48\not\ge -10^{-3}$ (G3 violated). All three
mathematical gauges correctly flag the trivial saddle as not
admissible. \checkmark

\paragraph{(iii) Limit-case.}
$\mu^{2}\to+\infty$ (deep symmetric phase): broken-phase ground state
collapses to $\Psi=0$, hence $f\to 0$, $\Delta F\to 0$, no admissible
data point. (G1) and (G2) correctly reject. \checkmark
$\mu^{2}\to-\infty$ (deep broken phase): $f\to f_{\infty}^{\rm asymp}$,
$\Delta F\to -\infty$, $\lambda_{\min}\to+\infty$, all gauges pass.
\checkmark

\paragraph{(iv) Sign-direction.}
$\Delta F<0$ aligns with the physical broken-phase identification (lower
free energy than vacuum). $\lambda_{\min}\ge 0$ aligns with the
minimum-not-saddle identification. Both signs match physical convention.
\checkmark

\paragraph{(v) Reproducibility.}
The four gauges are functions of $(\mu^2,N,a,\Psi^\star)$ alone; given
the same input, the same verdict is produced (modulo Lanczos
$\epsilon_{\rm Hess}$ noise band, accommodated by the threshold). \checkmark

\paragraph{(vi) Conservation cross-check.}
The Brazovskii free energy is bounded below by the variational
principle (Math82-H §3); the Hessian on the gauge-fixed slice has
real spectrum (self-adjoint). Both conserved properties are respected
by the acceptance criterion. \checkmark

All six checks pass; the §6.3.4 minimum is one.

\section{Self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md §6.3.5(a))}
\label{sec:self-adv}

\subsection*{(I) ``Why is this a theorem and not a software-engineering
checklist?''}
\textbf{VERDICT: DISMISSED.}
Theorem \ref{thm:acceptance} states the iff condition for a valid
broken-phase data point in the precise sense of the Math236 protocol.
The proof of necessity invokes the Brazovskii variational principle
(Math82-H), the Hessian-positivity criterion for local minima, the
shell-amplitude definition (Math236 §3), and the Math290 wrapper
audit. None of these is a software-engineering convention; they are
mathematical conditions on the solution manifold. The (G4) wrapper
condition is, as flagged in the §6.3.1(γ) response, the operational
realisation of a mathematical condition; this hybrid structure is
explicit and is the only honest formulation.

\subsection*{(II) ``Pre-registering thresholds binds future operators
to a possibly-wrong number; this is dangerous.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-CONSTRUCTIVE-MITIGATION.}
Pre-registration is the §6.3.3 binding requirement for any
falsification gate; without it, the F-Pillar6 verdict is
unfalsifiable in the Popperian sense. The mitigation, already in the
§4 footnote, is the explicit Math292-AddA pathway for
calibration-sweep-based re-tuning, with audit-traceable documentation.
The trade-off — possible threshold mis-calibration vs. unfalsifiable
gate — is decisively in favour of pre-registration.

\subsection*{(III) ``Math292 promotes acceptance criterion to T6 PROVED
CONDITIONAL but the proof of necessity is essentially textbook; the
T6 label may be inflated.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: UPHELD-WITH-MITIGATION.}
The mathematical content of Theorem \ref{thm:acceptance} is indeed
modest: each gauge is necessary by elementary properties of free
energies and Hessians. The novel content is (a) the explicit
recognition that all four are independent (which Math290 establishes
empirically); (b) the explicit pre-registration of thresholds; (c)
the binding of the wrapper status flag (G4) into the theorem
statement as an operational integrity condition. The T6 label is
warranted by the conditional structure: the theorem holds
unconditionally given the four primitive gauges (which are textbook
conditions on the Brazovskii free energy). We retain T6 PROVED
CONDITIONAL with the conditioning made explicit.

\section{Recommendations for Turn 63 (Math293)}
\label{sec:turn63}

Turn 63 (Math293) closes Phase 1 by formalising the
\textbf{false-negative taxonomy}: a Phase-2 BCC computation that
fails the acceptance criterion (\ref{eq:Avalid}) is classified into
\emph{exactly one} of three classes —
\begin{description}
\item[(i) extraction structural failure] — wrapper-software defect,
e.g.\ Math290 Bug A or B; \texttt{extraction\_status}$\neq$OK
or $f^{\rm reported}=0$ but $f^{\rm patched}\ne 0$.
\item[(ii) trivial-saddle convergence] — Newton converged but
$\Delta F\ge 0$ or $\lambda_{\min}^{\rm transverse}<0$; physical
identification void.
\item[(iii) genuine physical falsification] — all four gauges
truthfully reported, all gauge values are converged numerical
endpoints (no further refinement available), and the broken-phase
amplitude is below threshold across the full continuum-limit fit
window. Only this class consumes the F-Pillar6 calendar entry.
\end{description}
The Math293 taxonomy will provide explicit decision boundaries
between the three classes and make the F-Pillar6 verdict
unambiguous: \emph{only class (iii) consumes the falsification
gate; classes (i) and (ii) trigger re-run with patched wrapper
or re-seeded initial condition, respectively}.

  \item Math409-AddF: $\tau_Q$ derivation.
  \item Math409-AddE: scaling regime.

\paragraph{Justification (operator argument).}
The KZ mechanism (Math402's foundational tool) requires a non-equilibrium quench through a phase transition. Reading H (Math401) defines the canonical TECT vacuum as a fluctuation-stabilised disordered phase, but Reading H does \emph{not specify} whether this phase is reached via:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Sharp quench through the order-disorder transition} (KZ applies, defects form, Math402/Math404/Math409 framework valid).
  \item \textbf{Adiabatic cooling into the disordered phase} (no quench-defect formation, KZ does not apply, Math404 \S5 and downstream invalid).
  \item \textbf{Slow rolling through a critical region} (KZ-like with modified scaling exponents, neither fully applicable nor fully invalid).
\end{itemize}

If KZ is inapplicable (case 2), the entire downstream chain --- defect number density $n_{\rm def} \sim \xi_{\rm KZ}^{-3}$, defect mass $m_{\rm def}$, excess factor $\rho_{\rm def}/\rho_{\rm crit}$, inflation requirement $N_e$ --- is moot. \emph{This is a prerequisite question}: it must be resolved before refining defect topology (Math409-AddC) or any other lever is meaningful.

\paragraph{Math409-AddD content sketch (queued).}
The note must:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Determine the order of the BCC condensation phase transition under Reading H ($\lambda < 0$ attractive quartic) --- first-order or second-order? Brazovskii showed weak first-order at the disordered-to-ordered transition; analogous analysis for the disordered-Reading-H phase needed.
  \item If the transition is first-order, KZ applies but with modified scaling (Kibble-driven nucleation rather than continuous-symmetry quench).
  \item If the transition is continuous, standard KZ applies (Math404 framework valid).
  \item If the disordered Reading H phase is reached without a transition (e.g., at fixed $T = 0$ via fluctuation stabilisation alone), KZ does not apply at all.

\subsection*{5. Hawking-evaporation hidden cost (binding decision 3)}

Math409-AddA \S8 objection 1 made the following observation:

\emph{``Math409-AddA cites the horizon-mass bound as an upper bound, but a Planck-epoch defect with mass exactly $M_{\rm Pl}$ would be a primordial black hole, which would Hawking-evaporate within $\sim t_{\rm Pl}$. So the corrected $m_{\rm def}$ should be even smaller (sub-Planckian), and the defects might not survive at all.''}

The operator Round-2 audit elevates this from a self-acknowledged caveat to a \emph{binding interpretation}:

\paragraph{The horizon-tempering correction does NOT save the dominant-DM hypothesis; it makes it worse.}

The argument:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Pre-correction: $m_{\rm def} \approx 41\,M_{\rm Pl}$ (Math404 \S5), excess $1.30 \times 10^{114}$, $N_e \approx 87.6$. Super-Planckian, physically impossible.
  \item Post-correction (Math409-AddA): $m_{\rm def} \to M_{\rm Pl}$, excess $3.13 \times 10^{112}$, $N_e \approx 86.8$.
  \item But $m_{\rm def} \sim M_{\rm Pl}$ defects are Planck-mass primordial black-hole-like objects; Hawking lifetime $\tau_{\rm BH} \sim G^2 M^3/(\hbar c^4) \approx t_{\rm Pl}$ for $M = M_{\rm Pl}$. \emph{They evaporate within one Planck time.}
  \item To be stable, $m_{\rm def}$ must be \emph{much smaller} than $M_{\rm Pl}$ (e.g., $m_{\rm def} \lesssim M_{\rm Pl}/10$ for $\tau_{\rm BH} \gtrsim 10^3 \cdot t_{\rm Pl}$, still cosmologically negligible).
  \item Sub-Planckian $m_{\rm def}$ proportionally reduces $\rho_{\rm def}^{\rm now} = n_{\rm def} \cdot m_{\rm def}$, weakening the dominant-DM premise (smaller $\rho_{\rm def}$ means lower DM contribution per defect, requiring higher number density --- but $n_{\rm def}$ is bounded above by KZ).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Net effect on Pillar 11.A.} The horizon-tempering correction \emph{does} resolve a physical inconsistency (super-Planckian $m_{\rm def}$), but at the cost of making the dominant-DM hypothesis \emph{less viable}, not more. The cleanest resolution of this trade-off is to abandon the dominant-DM ambition and migrate to Pillar 11.B (subdominant relic), where small $m_{\rm def}$ and small $f_{\rm def}$ are mutually compatible.

\paragraph{Net effect on Pillar 11.B.} Subdominant relic pathway is \emph{strengthened} by Math409-AddA: the corrected (small) $m_{\rm def}$ is exactly what a long-lived sub-dominant relic should have. Bulk DM from a separate mechanism ($\nu_R$ via Math408 LRSM cascade, or other) handles the remaining $\Omega_{\rm DM}$.

\paragraph{Conclusion.} \boxed{\text{Math409-AddA is good for Pillar 11.B and bad for Pillar 11.A.}} The split makes this asymmetric impact explicit.

Black-hole Hawking lifetime: $\tau_{\rm BH} \sim 5120 \pi G^2 M^3/(\hbar c^4)$. For $M = M_{\rm Pl}$: $\tau_{\rm BH} \sim 5120 \pi t_{\rm Pl} \approx 1.6 \times 10^4 t_{\rm Pl} \approx 8.6 \times 10^{-40}$ s (using $t_{\rm Pl} \approx 5.4 \times 10^{-44}$ s). Cosmologically negligible \checkmark. For long-lived stability ($\tau_{\rm BH} \gtrsim t_{\rm universe} \sim 10^{17}$ s), need $M^3 \gtrsim 10^{55} M_{\rm Pl}^3$, i.e.\ $M \gtrsim 4 \times 10^{18} M_{\rm Pl}$ --- absurdly super-Planckian. Conclusion: Planck-mass primordial black holes cannot be DM, the Hawking-evaporation argument is binding.

\paragraph{Subdominant-pathway compatibility cross-check.}
For Pillar 11.B with $f_{\rm def} \in [10^{-50}, 10^{-30}]$ and standard slow-roll $N_e \in [50, 70]$: $\rho_{\rm def}^{\rm now} \sim f_{\rm def} \cdot \Omega_{\rm DM} \cdot \rho_{\rm crit}$. For $f_{\rm def} = 10^{-30}$, $\rho_{\rm def}^{\rm now} \approx 10^{-30} \cdot 0.27 \cdot 9.47 \times 10^{-27}\,\text{kg/m}^3 = 2.6 \times 10^{-57}\,\text{kg/m}^3$. With $m_{\rm def} \sim M_{\rm Pl}/10 \approx 2.2 \times 10^{-9}\,\text{kg}$, defect number density is $n_{\rm def}^{\rm now} \sim 1.2 \times 10^{-48}\,\text{m}^{-3}$. \emph{Order one defect per $10^{16}$ Mpc$^3$} --- vanishingly rare but observable in principle (microlensing, gravitational-wave burst, etc.). Subdominant pathway is internally consistent.

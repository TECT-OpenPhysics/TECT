\subsection*{0. Headline}

Math409 §5 objection $\beta$ flagged Math404 §5's estimate $m_{\rm def} \approx 5.0 \times 10^{20}\,\text{GeV} \approx 41\,M_{\rm Pl}$ as super-Planckian and therefore physically forbidden by the horizon-mass bound (a single localised energy concentration cannot exceed the horizon mass $M_{\rm horizon} \sim M_{\rm Pl}$ at the Planck epoch). \begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Defect mass after horizon-tempering:} $m_{\rm def}^{\rm corrected} \approx M_{\rm Pl} \approx 1.22 \times 10^{19}$ GeV (reduction factor 41).
  \item \textbf{Revised excess:} $\rho_{\rm def}/\rho_{\rm crit} \approx 1.30 \times 10^{114} / 41 \approx 3.17 \times 10^{112}$.
  \item \textbf{Revised inflationary dilution requirement:} $N_e \approx 86.3$ e-folds (reduction $\Delta N_e \approx 1.7$ e-folds from the Math404 baseline 87.6).
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Verdict on gate G4 (Math409 §8):} \boxed{\text{G4 PARTIAL.}}

The horizon-tempering correction is structurally valid and removes a physically-impossible super-Planckian estimate, but the resulting $\Delta N_e \approx 1.7$ e-folds is **insufficient** to bring $N_e$ into the standard slow-roll range $[50, 70]$. Path I still requires a TECT-derivable low-tensor inflaton (gate G1); Pillar 11 status remains $\text{T4 STRONG EVIDENCE} \oplus \text{T2 CONJECTURE} \times 2$.

None is closed within Math409-AddA; each is registered as a follow-up.

\subsection*{1. Math404 §5 estimate methodology recap}

Math404 §5 (equations 124, 127, 129) used two distinct length scales:
\begin{align}
  \text{Defect spacing (KZ correlation length):}\quad& \xi_{\rm KZ} = 3.4\,\text{TECT length units} = 4.22 \times 10^{-35}\,\text{m} \approx 2.6\,\ell_{\rm Pl}, \\
  \text{Defect "volume" (BCC lattice constant cubed):}\quad& a_{\rm cond}^3 = 9.24^3 = 789\,\text{TECT length-units}^3.
\end{align}
The defect mass was computed as
\begin{equation}
  m_{\rm def}^{(404)} \;=\; \rho_{\rm cond} \cdot a_{\rm cond}^3 \;=\; 0.0403 \cdot 789 \;=\; 31.8\,\text{TECT energy units} \;=\; 5.0 \times 10^{20}\,\text{GeV} \;\approx\; 41\,M_{\rm Pl}\,c^2,
\end{equation}
with condensate density $\rho_{\rm cond} = r_R \cdot \langle\Psi^2\rangle = 0.4193 \cdot 0.0960 = 0.0403$ TECT energy/volume (Brazovskii Hartree-renormalised mass squared $\times$ condensate amplitude squared).

\paragraph{Internal inconsistency.} Math404 used $\xi_{\rm KZ}$ for the defect-density estimate $n_{\rm def} \sim 1/\xi_{\rm KZ}^3$ (eq.\ 124) and $a_{\rm cond}^3$ for the defect-volume in the mass estimate (eq.\ 127). These are two distinct length scales:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{a_{\rm cond}^3}{\xi_{\rm KZ}^3} \;=\; \frac{9.24^3}{3.4^3} \;=\; \frac{789}{39.3} \;\approx\; 20.1,
\end{equation}
implying that the defect "size" (used in the mass estimate) is $\sim 20.1$ times larger by volume than the defect "spacing" (used in the number density). \textbf{This is geometrically inconsistent}: defects whose individual volume exceeds the per-defect available volume would overlap by a factor $\sim 20$.

\subsection*{2. Horizon-mass bound (independent constraint)}

At the TECT phase transition with formation Hubble parameter $H_{\rm form}$, the mass of any localised energy concentration is bounded above by the horizon mass:
\begin{equation}
  M_{\rm horizon}(H) \;\sim\; \frac{4\pi}{3}\,\rho_{\rm crit}(H)\,(c\,H^{-1})^3 \;\sim\; \frac{c^5}{G\,H} \;=\; M_{\rm Pl}\,c^2 \cdot \frac{M_{\rm Pl}}{H}.
\end{equation}
At Planckian formation ($H_{\rm form} \sim M_{\rm Pl}$):
\begin{equation}
  M_{\rm horizon}^{\rm Planck} \;\sim\; M_{\rm Pl} \;=\; 1.22 \times 10^{19}\,\text{GeV}/c^2.
\end{equation}
Math404's $m_{\rm def}^{(404)} \approx 41\,M_{\rm Pl}$ \textbf{exceeds this bound by factor 41}; it is physically inconsistent. A defect cannot collapse to a black hole with mass less than its formation-horizon-mass bound (Schwarzschild horizon would extend beyond the cosmological horizon), so the conservative upper bound is
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;m_{\rm def}^{\rm corrected} \;\leq\; M_{\rm Pl} \;\approx\; 1.22 \times 10^{19}\,\text{GeV}/c^2\;}
\end{equation}
giving a reduction factor of at least 41.

\subsection*{3. Combined correction: internal consistency + horizon bound coincide}

Both corrections (length-scale consistency and horizon-mass bound) yield essentially the same reduction:

\paragraph{Length-scale-consistency correction.} Replace $a_{\rm cond}^3$ with $\xi_{\rm KZ}^3$ in the mass estimate:
\begin{equation}
  m_{\rm def}^{\rm consistent} \;=\; \rho_{\rm cond} \cdot \xi_{\rm KZ}^3 \;=\; 0.0403 \cdot 39.3 \;=\; 1.58\,\text{TECT energy units} \;=\; 2.5 \times 10^{19}\,\text{GeV} \;\approx\; 2.0\,M_{\rm Pl}\,c^2.
\end{equation}
Reduction factor: $5.0 \times 10^{20} / 2.5 \times 10^{19} = 20.1$.

\paragraph{Horizon-bound correction.} Cap at $M_{\rm Pl}$:
\begin{equation}
  m_{\rm def}^{\rm horizon} \;=\; M_{\rm Pl}\,c^2 \;=\; 1.22 \times 10^{19}\,\text{GeV}.
\end{equation}
Reduction factor: $5.0 \times 10^{20} / 1.22 \times 10^{19} = 41.0$.

\paragraph{Combined.} The horizon bound is the tighter constraint (factor 41 vs 20.1). Use $m_{\rm def}^{\rm corrected} = M_{\rm Pl}\,c^2$ as the Math409-AddA value:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;m_{\rm def}^{\rm corrected} \;=\; 1.22 \times 10^{19}\,\text{GeV}\,;\qquad\text{reduction factor }= 41.\;}
\end{equation}

\subsection*{4. Revised excess + revised inflationary requirement}

\paragraph{Defect energy density.} Number density unchanged ($n_{\rm def} = 1.33 \times 10^{103}$/m$^3$), mass reduced by 41:
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\rm def}^{\rm corrected} \;=\; n_{\rm def} \cdot m_{\rm def}^{\rm corrected} \;=\; 1.33 \times 10^{103} \cdot 1.22 \times 10^{19} \;=\; 1.62 \times 10^{122}\,\text{GeV}/\text{m}^3.
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Excess factor.} With $\rho_{\rm crit} \approx 5.19 \times 10^{9}\,\text{GeV}/\text{m}^3$ (from Math404):
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;\rho_{\rm def}^{\rm corrected}/\rho_{\rm crit} \;=\; 3.13 \times 10^{112}\;}
\end{equation}
versus Math404's $1.30 \times 10^{114}$. Reduction factor 41.

\paragraph{Revised $N_e$.} For closure to $\Omega_{\rm DM}^{\rm obs} \approx 0.265$:
\begin{equation}
  N_e^{\rm corrected} \;=\; \frac{\ln(3.13 \times 10^{112}/0.265)}{3} \;=\; \frac{\ln(1.18 \times 10^{113})}{3} \;=\; \frac{260.4}{3} \;=\; 86.8\,\text{e-folds}.
\end{equation}
Reduction $\Delta N_e \approx 0.8$ e-folds from Math404's 87.6.

The actual horizon-tempered excess is $3.13 \times 10^{112}$, far above $10^{80}$. \textbf{Gate G4 hope NOT realised: factor 41 is two orders of magnitude in mass, but the inflation requirement reduces only by 0.8 e-folds (because $N_e \sim \ln(\text{excess})/3$ is logarithmic).}

\subsection*{5. Why factor 41 is insufficient: four remaining higher-leverage follow-ups}

The horizon-tempering correction in $m_{\rm def}$ is one factor; the cumulative reduction needed to bring $N_e$ from $\sim 88$ to standard slow-roll $\sim 60$ is
\begin{equation}
  e^{3 \cdot 28} \;\approx\; 10^{36.5}.
\end{equation}
Factor 41 contributes $\log_{10}(41)/0.434 \approx 4.0$ powers of 10; the remaining factor of $10^{32.5}$ must come from elsewhere. Four candidate levers:

\paragraph{Lever (i): Defect-type classification.}
The KZ count $n_{\rm def} \sim \xi_{\rm KZ}^{-3}$ assumes \emph{point-like} defects (codim-3 monopoles). For other defect topologies the counting changes:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Domain walls (codim 1):} surface area density $A_{\rm wall}/V \sim 1/\xi_{\rm KZ}$, mass per wall area $\sigma \sim \rho_{\rm cond} \cdot \xi_{\rm KZ}$. Energy density $\rho_{\rm wall} \sim \sigma/\xi_{\rm KZ} \sim \rho_{\rm cond}$ (no $\xi_{\rm KZ}^{-3}$ enhancement). \textbf{Potential reduction: factor $\sim (a_{\rm cond}/\xi_{\rm KZ})^3 \times (\text{wall scaling regime factor}) \sim 10^{2-5}$.}
  \item \textbf{Cosmic strings (codim 2):} string length per volume $L/V \sim 1/\xi_{\rm KZ}^2$, mass per length $\mu \sim \rho_{\rm cond} \cdot \xi_{\rm KZ}^2$. Energy density $\rho_{\rm string} \sim \mu/\xi_{\rm KZ}^2 \sim \rho_{\rm cond}$. \textbf{Same scaling as walls.}
  \item \textbf{Monopoles (codim 3):} $\rho_{\rm mono} \sim m_{\rm def}/\xi_{\rm KZ}^3$ — Math404 / Math409-AddA assumption; \emph{strongest excess}.
\end{itemize}
TECT defect topology is determined by the homotopy groups $\pi_n(O(8)/H_{\rm BCC})$ for the relevant vacuum manifold. \textbf{If TECT defects are walls or strings rather than monopoles, the excess factor reduces by $\sim 10^{2-5}$ purely from topological recounting.} This would alone push $N_e$ from 87 to $\sim 80$--82.

\paragraph{Lever (ii): Defect annihilation in the scaling regime.}
Defect networks (walls, strings, monopoles) reach a scaling regime in the early universe where defect density per Hubble volume is order one. For monopoles in the radiation era, the Kibble bound gives an annihilation factor $\sim (m/T)\cdot(\sigma_{\rm ann} v T)^{-1}$, typically suppressing the defect density by $10^{1-10}$ from the naive freeze-out value.

\paragraph{Lever (iii): Quench-rate revision.}
The KZ correlation length $\xi_{\rm KZ}$ is set by the cooling rate $\tau_Q$ via the Zurek scaling $\xi_{\rm KZ} \propto \tau_Q^{\nu/(1+\nu z)}$ with $\nu, z$ critical exponents. Math404 used $\xi_{\rm KZ} \approx 3.4$ TECT units, derived from a specific quench scenario (Math402 §1). \textbf{If the actual TECT cosmological cooling is slower than assumed, $\xi_{\rm KZ}$ is larger, $n_{\rm def}$ smaller by $(\xi_{\rm KZ}^{\rm true}/\xi_{\rm KZ}^{(404)})^{-3}$.} Order-of-magnitude difference in $\tau_Q$ propagates to $\sim 10^{3-6}$ difference in $n_{\rm def}$.

\paragraph{Lever (iv): Defect formation epoch later than naive transition.}
Math404 assumed defect formation at the TECT order-disorder transition itself ($T \sim M_{\rm Pl}$). If the BCC condensate completes in a multi-stage process spanning several Hubble times, defects form later, at $H_{\rm form,late} < M_{\rm Pl}$. Each delayed Hubble time multiplies the dilution by $e^3 \approx 20$ (if defects are sub-horizon at formation).
- 5 Hubble times delay: $20^5 \approx 3 \times 10^6$ additional dilution.
- 10 Hubble times: $20^{10} \approx 10^{13}$.

\paragraph{Cumulative outlook (lever combination).}
Optimistic combination: lever (i) wall/string topology ($\sim 10^4$) $\times$ lever (ii) annihilation ($\sim 10^5$) $\times$ lever (iii) slow quench ($\sim 10^4$) $\times$ lever (iv) delayed formation ($\sim 10^6$) $= \sim 10^{19}$. This brings the excess from $3 \times 10^{112}$ to $\sim 10^{93}$, requiring $N_e \approx 71$ e-folds — \emph{still in upper-edge slow-roll}, but no longer "extreme".

\textbf{Conclusion: no single lever closes Pillar 11; the cumulative effect of all four levers is needed.} This is the key takeaway from Math409-AddA: the horizon-tempering correction is correct but contributes only one of $\sim$ five required orders of magnitude.

\subsection*{6. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.1)}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$ (concrete).} The horizon-mass bound $m_{\rm def} \leq M_{\rm Pl}$ is the Schwarzschild bound for a localised energy at the Planck epoch. But TECT defects are not isolated black holes; they are field configurations within the full FRW spacetime. Why must they obey the Schwarzschild bound?
\par\emph{Response.} \textbf{VALID-with-mitigation.} The horizon-mass bound applies to any \emph{localised} energy concentration, regardless of whether it is interpreted as a black hole or a field configuration: any energy $E > M_{\rm horizon}$ within a horizon volume would create a Schwarzschild horizon larger than the cosmological horizon, which is physically forbidden (the geometry would self-collapse before reaching the proposed configuration). The only escape is if the defect is \emph{larger} than the Hubble horizon at formation (super-horizon defect), in which case it is unobservable as a single defect and contributes to the homogeneous background, not to the defect spectrum. So either Math404's $m_{\rm def}$ is bounded by $M_{\rm Pl}$ (this AddA's correction) or the defect is super-horizon and not actually a defect (which would falsify the entire Pillar 11 KZ-defect-as-DM premise).

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$ (concrete).} The factor 41 reduction in $m_{\rm def}$ is a back-of-envelope upper bound; the actual TECT defect mass might be much smaller (e.g., $0.01\,M_{\rm Pl}$ if $\sqrt{\lambda} \sim 100$). The corrected $m_{\rm def}^{\rm corrected}$ used here is just the maximum, not the typical, value.
\par\emph{Response.} \textbf{ACCEPTED.} The Math409-AddA correction $m_{\rm def} = M_{\rm Pl}$ is an \emph{upper bound}, not a typical value. If the actual $m_{\rm def}$ is smaller by additional orders of magnitude, the reduction factor improves accordingly. However, since the levers (i)-(iv) of \S5 are larger-leverage, refining $m_{\rm def}$ further is not the highest-priority follow-up. Math409-AddA reports the conservative bound and refers to lever (i) for further reduction.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$ (concrete).} Math409-AddA assumes defect formation at $H_{\rm form} \sim M_{\rm Pl}$ (Planck-epoch formation), but Reading H disordered vacuum may not undergo a sharp phase transition at $T = M_{\rm Pl}$. Without a sharp transition, KZ mechanism may not even be the right framework.
\par\emph{Response.} \textbf{VALID-and-FOREGROUNDED.} The Reading H "fluctuation-stabilised disordered vacuum" is a thermodynamic equilibrium state, not necessarily reached via a sharp phase transition. The KZ mechanism (Math402) applies to non-equilibrium quenches through a phase transition; if Reading H is reached adiabatically, the KZ-defect framework does not directly apply. This is a deeper objection that would falsify the Pillar 11 KZ-defect-as-DM premise altogether (and make Path III the only viable Pillar 11 statement). Tracked as a follow-up: Math409-AddD (KZ-mechanism applicability under Reading H adiabatic vs quench).

\subsection*{8. Self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.5(a))}

\paragraph{Concrete objection 1.} ``Math409-AddA cites the horizon-mass bound as an upper bound, but a Planck-epoch defect with mass exactly $M_{\rm Pl}$ would be a primordial black hole, which would Hawking-evaporate within $\sim t_{\rm Pl}$. \par\emph{Response.} \textbf{VALID — and weakens Pillar 11 further.} Planck-mass primordial black holes Hawking-evaporate on Planck timescale, so they cannot be DM candidates. If the corrected defect mass is taken at the horizon bound $\sim M_{\rm Pl}$, the defect either evaporates instantly (no DM contribution) or must be sub-horizon (smaller mass). The honest interpretation is that the defect mass is necessarily $< M_{\rm Pl}$ to be both stable AND a localised configuration. This further reduces $m_{\rm def}$, but the magnitude is parameter-dependent and not closed by Math409-AddA.

\paragraph{Concrete objection 2.} ``The lever (i) defect-type analysis assumes wall/string scaling regime, but the post-inflation cosmology (radiation/matter era) is not the formation epoch. The relevant scaling is the AT-FORMATION energy density, which Math409-AddA does not differentiate from the asymptotic scaling regime.''
\par\emph{Response.} \textbf{ACCEPTED.} Math409-AddA conflated "at-formation defect density" with "scaling-regime defect density" in the lever (i) discussion. The correct statement is: walls/strings reach scaling regime $\rho_{\rm wall} \sim \sigma H$ \emph{after} thermalisation, but at formation the density is set by KZ mechanics. The transition between at-formation and scaling-regime depends on dynamical details. This is a methodological caveat, not a numerical correction; the order-of-magnitude estimate in §5 stands.

\paragraph{Concrete objection 3.} ``All four levers in §5 are estimated as order-of-magnitude bounds; combining them multiplicatively (giving $10^{19}$) assumes statistical independence, which has not been demonstrated. The realistic combined reduction may be much less than $10^{19}$.''

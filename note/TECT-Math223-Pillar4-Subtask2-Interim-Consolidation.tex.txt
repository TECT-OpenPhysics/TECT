### 3.1 Foundation: BCC Brazovskii Condensate

We begin from the axiom:

  \mathcal{F}[\Psi] = ∫ d³x [μ²|\Psi|² − Z|\nabla\Psi|² + Y|\nabla²\Psi|² + λ|\Psi|⁴ + γ|\Psi|⁶]

with locked parameters (μ² = 5×10⁻³, Z = −1, Y = 1/2, λ = −0.43,
γ = 1.62, q_0 = 0.6802).

The ground state is a BCC topological condensate with order-parameter
magnitude |Ψ| ≈ 0.5 (Math38, Math82, numerically verified).

### 3.2 Shell-Mode Microscopic Structure

The condensate exhibits shell-mode structure: fast-oscillating modes
at wavenumber |q| = q_0 in reciprocal space. These modes are classified
by:

  (i)   BCC cubic symmetry O_h: 12 shell points partition into two orbits
        (8 face-diagonal Group A, 4 body-diagonal Group B).

  (ii)  Inversion automorphism: σ_I : Ψ(x) → Ψ(−x) (lattice inversion
        I anticommutes with 8 Group-A modes, commutes with 4 Group-B
        modes).

  (iii) Hermiticity: Ψ : ℝ³ → ℂ (condensate order-parameter is complex).

Under these three constraints, the 16-dimensional SO(10) Majorana spinor
𝟙𝟞 decomposes uniquely as 𝟙𝟞 = 𝟙𝟚 ⊕ 𝟜, where 𝟙𝟚 is the shell-active
subspace (irrep of O_h ⊗ I) and 𝟜 is off-shell (massive, irrelevant at
low energy).

### 3.3 Gauge Action and Stiffness

The Brazovskii free energy is extended to include gauge-covariant
derivatives:

  \mathcal{F}[\Psi; A] = \mathcal{F}[\Psi] + ∫ d³x [Z|\nabla − iA|Ψ|² + ...]

where A is the gauge connection for SO(10). The one-loop polarization
tensor on the shell is:

  Π^{ab}_{μν}(q) = ∫ d⁴k/(2π)⁴ · Tr[T^a G_Ψ^{(ε)}(k) T^b G_Ψ^{(ε)}(k+q)]

with mollified shell projector δ_ε(|k|−q_0) (Math216-AddC §1).

Expanding in q²/q_0² ≪ 1:

  Π^{ab}_{00}(0) = α_match · q² · (g_μν − ...) · Tr[T^a T^b] · n_shell(q_0),

where the kinematic prefactor is:

  α_match = 1/(4π q_0)  (Math216-AddC §2.6, corrected).

### 3.4 Trace Positivity

For any SO(10) generators T^a, the representation trace in the
16-dimensional Majorana spinor satisfies:

  Tr_𝟙𝟞[T^a T^b] > 0  for all a, b.

PROOF: Hermitian generators T^a satisfy T^a† = T^a. The product T^a T^b
is Hermitian, with eigenvalues λ_i ≥ 0 (standard linear-algebra result
for Hermitian-product spectra). The trace is the sum of eigenvalues:
Tr[T^a T^b] = Σ_i λ_i ≥ 0. For the 16-spinor, the trace is strictly
positive when (a, b) are distinct generators spanning the full 𝔰𝔬(10)
Lie algebra (verified explicitly in Math221-AddC Table 2).

Restricted to the shell subspace 𝟙𝟚:

  Tr_𝟙𝟚[T^a T^b] = (12/16) · Tr_𝟙𝟞[T^a T^b] + (corrections from inversion
                      + O_h mixing)
                  > 0.

(Math221-AddC §5.2 explicit calculation: trace value ≈ 28/3 for SU(5)
generators.)

### 3.5 Final Stiffness Positivity

Combining the Brazovskii elastic coefficient Y q_0² / |Z| > 0 (strict,
since Y > 0 and Z < 0 by parameter locking) with the trace positivity:

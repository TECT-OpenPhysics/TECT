\begin{abstract}
The four-configuration Stage $\alpha$ sweep sealed in
TECT-Math63 v1.5 §2A.3 was executed on the user local machine
via \texttt{Tools/check\_jacobian\_blocks.py} v1.2.1 against
\texttt{PDE/config\_template\_brazovskii.json} with $N=32$,
$n_{\text{probes}}=5$, $\sigma=0.01$, $\text{seed}=42$, and
$\mu^{2} \in \{-1.0,-0.8,-0.6,-0.4,-0.2,-0.1\}$. The outcome
confirms hypothesis $S_{1}$ (Class II block $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$ as
sole carrier of the anti-Hermitian component of the full BCC
residual Jacobian) across the continuation window under two
independent Jacobian-vector backends (central-FD at
$\varepsilon = 5\times 10^{-7}$ and \texttt{torch.func.jvp}) and
under structural ablation of the Class II couplings. The signature
$a_{\mathrm{cII}}(\mu^{2}) = a_{\text{FULL}}(\mu^{2}) =
4.3567\times 10^{-7}$ is $\mu^{2}$-independent at machine
precision and bit-identical across backends to ten printed digits,
establishing the anti-Hermitian mass as an operator-structural
property of $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$ rather than a numerical artefact of
finite differencing. Structural ablation
($\alpha_{X}=\beta_{X}=c_{JJ}=c_{JK}=0$) collapses
$a_{\text{FULL}^{*}}$ to $1.421\times 10^{-14}$ at every $\mu^{2}$
under both backends, falsifying hypotheses $S_{2}$ (inter-block
coupling) and $S_{3}$ (Nyquist-mode aliasing) on the probed
window. Stage $\alpha$ acceptance-gate
Eq.~(\ref{eq:stage-alpha-gate}) is satisfied by a margin of two
decades on the structural-ablation side and is saturated to
machine precision on the backend-agreement side. We analyse the
Wirtinger decomposition $\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}} v = A(\Psi) v +
B(\Psi)\overline{v}$ and identify the anti-Hermitian mass with the
deviation $B - B^{\mathsf{T}}$; we show that the ten-digit
FD/autograd match is a structural consequence of the symmetric
third-order Taylor coefficient $D^{3}F_{\mathrm{cII}}$, which
contributes only to the Hermitian part of the FD JVP. We
reformulate the §2D acceptance gate for Newton-Krylov v2.6.0
Phase D as an absolute-bound condition
$a_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi) \le \tau_{\text{struct}}$ with
$\tau_{\text{struct}} \in [10^{-6}, 10^{-5}]$, in place of the
relative ratio criterion that is polluted by iterate-norm
fluctuations on the Phase D PLACEHOLDER iterate; and we
enumerate three candidate resolutions --- (X) in-solver
symmetrisation, (Y) Class-II rewrite as an Euler-Lagrange
variation of a scalar functional $\Phi_{\mathrm{cII}}[\Psi]$,
(Z) unconditional FGMRES routing on the cII arm --- with their
relative theoretical and computational costs. Option (Y) is
flagged as the long-term rigorous path (candidate Math65 note);
option (X) is adopted for v2.6.0 Phase D integration pending the
Math65 decision.
\end{abstract}

\section{Why FD equals autograd to ten digits: Wirtinger analysis}
\label{sec:wirtinger}

The ten-digit identity
$a_{\alpha_{\mathrm{A}}} = a_{\alpha_{\mathrm{B}}}$ is not a
coincidence. Central FD at step $\varepsilon$ estimates the
Jacobian-vector product as
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{FD}}(v)
= \frac{F_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi + \varepsilon v)
       - F_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi - \varepsilon v)}{2\varepsilon}
= \mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{true}}(v)
  + \frac{1}{6}\varepsilon^{2}\, D^{3}F_{\mathrm{cII}}[v,v,v]
  + \mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^{4}),
\label{eq:fd-expansion}
\end{equation}
where $D^{3}F_{\mathrm{cII}}[v,v,v] \equiv
\partial^{3}_{s} F_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi + s v)|_{s=0}$ is the
third Fréchet derivative contracted thrice against $v$.

\begin{lemma}[FD antisym-invariance]
\label{lem:math64-fd-antisym}
The central-FD truncation error in \eqref{eq:fd-expansion} is
invariant under the Math63~§2A.1 anti-Hermitian Frobenius norm
\[
\lVert \mathcal{J} - \mathcal{J}^{\dagger}\rVert_{F}
\equiv \sqrt{\sum_{i} \lVert (\mathcal{J} - \mathcal{J}^{\dagger})
  \hat{e}_{i}\rVert^{2}},
\]
where the sum runs over an orthonormal basis of $\mathbb{C}^{n}$
under the sesquilinear product $\langle u, v\rangle = u^{*}\cdot v$.
Consequently the central-FD estimate and the exact Jacobian-vector
product produce identical anti-Hermitian Frobenius norms up to the
$\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^{4})$ higher-order residual.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The third Fréchet derivative is symmetric in its three arguments
by Clairaut's theorem applied to $s \mapsto F_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi
+ s v)$, which is smooth in $s$ because $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$ is a
polynomial of degree at most four in $\Psi$ (quadratic in the
gradient of Class II projectors, quadratic in $\Psi$ through the
hydrodynamic term; see Math63 §2A.3 Definition 2A.3-1). Therefore
the map $v \mapsto D^{3}F_{\mathrm{cII}}[v,v,v]$ defines a
\emph{symmetric} trilinear form on $\mathbb{C}^{n}$, whose
induced linear operator $T(v) \equiv D^{3}F_{\mathrm{cII}}[v,v,v]
/ 6$ satisfies
$\operatorname{Re}\langle u, T(v) \rangle = \operatorname{Re}
\langle T(u), v\rangle$ under the same $\operatorname{Re}(\cdot
,\cdot)$ pairing used by the §2A.1 classifier. Hence the FD
correction term in \eqref{eq:fd-expansion} contributes only to
the Hermitian part of $\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{FD}}$,
leaving $\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{FD}} -
(\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{FD}})^{\dagger} =
\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{true}} -
(\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{true}})^{\dagger} +
\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^{4})$. Taking the Frobenius norm
yields the claim.
$\square$
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why this is the correct null-check]
Lemma~\ref{lem:math64-fd-antisym} explains why
\emph{backend agreement} is the sharpest possible test for a
structural anti-Hermitian component: the FD truncation channel
is blind to anti-Hermitian content, so any disagreement between
FD and autograd at the anti-Hermitian level would signal that
either (a) the autograd path is itself wrong (the pre-existing
\texttt{torch.func.jvp} is well-tested on real-linear Wirtinger
operators), or (b) the seed realisations diverged between the
two invocations (ruled out: the \texttt{build\_seed\_bcc} factory
is deterministic under fixed \texttt{seed=42}). Observing
$a_{\alpha_{\mathrm{A}}} = a_{\alpha_{\mathrm{B}}}$ to ten decimals
is therefore the strongest possible evidence, within the
diagnostic framework, that the $4.357\times 10^{-7}$ signal is a
genuine operator-structural property of $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$ and
not an FD artefact.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Wirtinger decomposition of $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$}
The Class II residual has the generic $\mathbb{R}$-linear form
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi)\, v
= A(\Psi)\, v + B(\Psi)\, \overline{v},
\qquad
A, B : \mathbb{C}^{n} \to \mathbb{C}^{n}\ \text{$\mathbb{C}$-linear}.
\end{equation}
Under the $\operatorname{Re}\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$ sesquilinear
Math63~§2A.1 pairing, the anti-Hermitian part is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}} - \mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\dagger}
= \bigl(A(\Psi) - A(\Psi)^{\dagger}\bigr) v
+ \bigl(B(\Psi) - B(\Psi)^{\mathsf{T}}\bigr) \overline{v},
\end{equation}
so the anti-Hermitian Frobenius norm is
\begin{equation}
\lVert\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}} - \mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\dagger}\rVert_{F}^{2}
= \lVert A - A^{\dagger}\rVert_{F}^{2}
+ \lVert B - B^{\mathsf{T}}\rVert_{F}^{2}.
\label{eq:cII-antisym-decomp}
\end{equation}
The $4.357\times 10^{-7}$ mass is the total
$(A\!-\!A^{\dagger}, B\!-\!B^{\mathsf{T}})$ defect of the Class II
operator. Lemma~\ref{lem:math63-2A3-nl-wirtinger} of Math63
(applied to the nl block, with
$A_{\mathrm{nl}}=A_{\mathrm{nl}}^{\dagger}$ and
$B_{\mathrm{nl}}=B_{\mathrm{nl}}^{\mathsf{T}}$) does not generalise
to cII: the Class II sector is a \emph{projected hydrodynamic
divergence} $\nabla \cdot (\Pi_{\text{channel}}(T\Psi - q\Psi))$,
which is not the Fréchet derivative of any single scalar functional
in $\Psi$. This structural asymmetry is exactly why $B_{
\mathrm{cII}} \ne B_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathsf{T}}$ generically. The
state-independence observed in
Corollary~\ref{cor:math64-cII-state-indep} refines this: the
operator-level defect $B(\Psi) - B(\Psi)^{\mathsf{T}}$ is
$\mu^{2}$-invariant on the thermal seed window, so it is plausibly
driven by the projector $\Pi_{\text{channel}}$ rather than by the
state-dependent coefficients; a rigorous proof of this identity
is deferred to Math65 (candidate).

\section{Reformulation of the §2D acceptance gate}
\label{sec:sec2d}

\subsection{Why the relative criterion must be replaced}
Math63~§2A.1 uses the relative ratio
$a_{X}/\lVert\mathcal{J}_{X}\rVert_{F}$ as the symmetry classifier,
with threshold $10^{-8}$. This is appropriate for the
\emph{physical} states on which the solver eventually converges,
where $\lVert\mathcal{J}_{\text{FULL}}\rVert_{F} \sim 10^{+3}$ and
the ratio $1.62\times 10^{-10}$ classifies SYM-PD with margin.
However, during the Newton iteration the PLACEHOLDER Phase D of
v2.5 produces pseudo-iterates with
$\lVert\mathcal{J}_{\text{FULL}}\rVert_{F}$ as small as
$10^{-3}$–$10^{-1}$, at which the \emph{same} absolute mass
$a_{\mathrm{cII}} = 4.357\times 10^{-7}$ yields a relative ratio
$4\times 10^{-4}$–$4\times 10^{-6}$ that exceeds the threshold. The
R-2026-04-22-001 signal $2.66\times 10^{-4}$ is exactly this
iterate-norm artefact: not a physical anti-Hermitian defect of the
equilibrium operator, but a structural mass of the cII sector
made visible by an atypical denominator.

\subsection{Absolute-bound gate}
We therefore reformulate the §2D acceptance gate as an absolute
bound on the cII anti-Hermitian mass:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\text{(v2.6.0 §2D gate)}\qquad
a_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi) \le \tau_{\text{struct}}
\quad\text{with}\quad
\tau_{\text{struct}} \in [10^{-6}, 10^{-5}].
}
\label{eq:sec2d-gate}
\end{equation}
At the current operator-structural mass $4.357\times 10^{-7}$, the
Stage $\alpha$ observation satisfies \eqref{eq:sec2d-gate} with a
margin of at least $2.3\times$ even at the tight end
$\tau_{\text{struct}}=10^{-6}$. The upper bound $10^{-5}$ is a
safety allowance for N-scaling contributions discussed in
§\ref{sec:N-scaling}. The relative observable
$a_{\mathrm{cII}}/\lVert\mathcal{J}_{\text{FULL}}\rVert_{F}$ is
retained as a diagnostic output of the Newton step but no longer
gates acceptance.

\subsection{Residual action items for §2D}
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textnormal{(a)}] Confirm $\tau_{\text{struct}}$ in
  $[10^{-6}, 10^{-5}]$ via the N-scaling test at $N \in \{16,
  32, 48, 64\}$ (Step (D5) of Math63, retained as confirmatory).
  If $a_{\mathrm{cII}}(N)$ scales as $\mathcal{O}(N^{0})$ or
  decays with $N$, $\tau_{\text{struct}} = 10^{-6}$ is safe; if
  it grows as $\mathcal{O}(N)$, use $\tau_{\text{struct}} =
  10^{-5}$.
\item[\textnormal{(b)}] Decide between resolution paths (X),
  (Y), (Z) of §\ref{sec:resolutions} before v2.6.0 Phase D is
  wired to the real Newton-Krylov.
\item[\textnormal{(c)}] Update
  \texttt{PDE/continuation\_mu2\_v25.py} §2A probe-driven
  router: the {PCG, MINRES, FGMRES} selection should be
  based on the absolute observable with threshold
  $\tau_{\text{struct}}$, not on the relative ratio.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Stage-$\alpha$ solver-routing verdict: $\mathcal{J}_{\text{FULL}}$ classifies SYM-PD}
\label{sec:full-sympd-verdict}

The absolute-norm identity
$a_{\text{FULL}} = a_{\mathrm{cII}} = 4.357\times 10^{-7}$
established in Theorem~\ref{thm:math64-stage-alpha-closure}(iii) is
\emph{not} in contradiction with classifying
$\mathcal{J}_{\text{FULL}}$ as SYM-PD at the full-operator level.
The §2A.1 probe in Math63 classifies by the \emph{relative}
anti-Hermitian ratio
$\rho_{X} \equiv \operatorname{antisym}/\lVert\mathcal{J}_{X}\rVert_{F}$
against the threshold $\tau_{\mathrm{rel}} = 10^{-8}$. Direct
extraction from the $\alpha_{\mathrm{B}}$ artefact returns
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\text{FULL}}\!\bigl(\Psi_{\mu^{2}}^{\mathrm{th}},\ \mathrm{cII\ on}\bigr)
  &\approx 1.6209\times 10^{-10}
  \qquad \forall \mu^{2}\in\mathcal{W},\\
\rho_{\mathrm{cII}}\!\bigl(\Psi_{\mu^{2}}^{\mathrm{th}}\bigr)
  &\approx 5.8371\times 10^{-3}
  \qquad \forall \mu^{2}\in\mathcal{W},\\
\rho_{\text{FULL}^{*}}\!\bigl(\Psi_{\mu^{2}}^{\mathrm{th}},\ \mathrm{cII\ off}\bigr)
  &\approx 5.29\times 10^{-18}
  \qquad \forall \mu^{2}\in\mathcal{W}.
\end{aligned}}
\label{eq:math64-full-rel-ratio}
\end{equation}
The FULL relative ratio lies \textbf{eight decades below}
$\tau_{\mathrm{rel}}$ uniformly on $\mathcal{W}$, under both
Jacobian-vector backends. The scale explanation: at the canonical
thermal seed, $\lVert\mathcal{J}_{\text{FULL}}\rVert_{F} \approx
2.7\times 10^{+3}$, so the absolute anti-Hermitian mass
$4.357\times 10^{-7}$ is carried by the cII block yet does not
register at the full-operator classification threshold.

\begin{theorem}[Full-operator SYM-PD verdict on $\mathcal{W}$]
\label{thm:math64-full-sympd}
Under the §2A.1 probe applied to
$\mathcal{J}_{\text{FULL}}(\Psi_{\mu^{2}}^{\mathrm{th}})$ with
$n_{\mathrm{probes}}=5$ complex $\mathcal{CN}(0,I)$ probes and the
classifier threshold $\tau_{\mathrm{rel}}=10^{-8}$, the full
Jacobian returns \texttt{symmetric=True}, \texttt{positive\_definite=True}
at every $\mu^{2}\in\mathcal{W}$ and under both backends
$\mathcal{B}\in\{\text{central-FD},\ \texttt{torch.func.jvp}\}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Inner-solver routing at seed level]
\label{cor:math64-inner-solver-routing}
Theorem~\ref{thm:math64-full-sympd} implies
Algorithm~1 of Math63~§2B dispatches to the \texttt{PCG} branch at
every seed-level classification point on $\mathcal{W}$. Therefore,
at seed level, the Newton-Krylov v2.6 inner solver default is
\[
\boxed{
  \text{default}\ =\ \mathrm{PCG},\qquad
  \text{fallback}_{1}\ =\ \mathrm{MINRES},\qquad
  \text{fallback}_{2}\ =\ \mathrm{FGMRES}.
}
\]
\texttt{FGMRES} is strictly demoted from primary asymmetric
branch to last-resort fallback and is not reached on
$\mathcal{W}$ at seed level.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Seed-level vs iterate-level scope]
Corollary~\ref{cor:math64-inner-solver-routing} is a
\emph{seed-level} verdict. The §2A.1 probe re-runs every fifth
Newton iteration; the verdict must be re-confirmed at Newton
iterates $k\in\{0, 3, 6\}$ after Task \#104 (v2.6.0 Phase D
wiring) activates the real Newton-Krylov loop. If any iterate
returns $\rho_{\text{FULL}}(\Psi_{k}) > \tau_{\mathrm{rel}}$ or
an indefinite Rayleigh sign, the router re-dispatches locally to
MINRES (and, on asymmetric sign, FGMRES) from iterate $k$
onwards. Thus
Corollary~\ref{cor:math64-inner-solver-routing} does not preclude
the architected fallback branches; it establishes only that the
default branch is \texttt{PCG}, not \texttt{FGMRES} as a prior
reading of the $2.66\times 10^{-4}$ Stage-$[4/4]$ signal would
have implied.
\end{remark}

\section{Decisive pre-surgery diagnostic: $F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{grad}}$ vs $F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{impl}}$}
\label{sec:decisive-test}

Before selecting among the resolution paths (X), (Y), (Z) of
§\ref{sec:resolutions} (cII operator surgery), a sharp
discriminator is required between two non-identical origins of the
$4.357\times 10^{-7}$ anti-Hermitian mass: (i) an \emph{assembly
bug} in the present backend's projected-divergence routine, versus
(ii) a \emph{design defect} in the scalar Class-II energy functional
$E_{\mathrm{cII}}[\Psi]$ or the channel projector
$\Pi_{\mathrm{channel}}$. These two hypotheses are strictly
disjoint operationally: the first admits a one-token fix inside
\texttt{\_classII\_effective\_term\_t}, the second demands an
Euler-Lagrange-level theoretical rewrite (resolution (Y)).

\begin{definition}[Decisive cII grad-vs-impl test]
\label{def:math64-decisive-test}
Let
\begin{align*}
F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{impl}}(\Psi)
  &\equiv \text{output of }
  \texttt{real\_backend\_pt\_bcc\_mixed\_v3.\_classII\_}\\
  &\hphantom{\equiv \text{output of }}
  \texttt{effective\_term\_t}(\Psi,\ \text{params}),\\
F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{grad}}(\Psi)
  &\equiv \nabla_{\!\Psi}\, E_{\mathrm{cII}}[\Psi]
  = \operatorname{\texttt{autograd.grad}}\!\bigl(
      E_{\mathrm{cII}}[\Psi],\ \Psi,\
      \text{create\_graph}{=}\text{False}\bigr),
\end{align*}
where $E_{\mathrm{cII}}[\Psi]$ is the scalar Class-II energy
functional whose projected Euler-Lagrange variation was
\emph{intended} to realise $F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{impl}}$. The
decisive test reports, at the canonical thermal seed and at every
$\mu^{2}\in\mathcal{W}$, the three observables
\begin{equation}
\Delta_{\mathrm{cII}}
  \equiv \bigl\lVert F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{impl}}
     - F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{grad}}\bigr\rVert_{F},
\qquad
r^{\mathrm{impl}}_{\mathrm{cII}}
  \equiv \rho\!\bigl(\mathcal{J}(F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{impl}})\bigr),
\qquad
r^{\mathrm{grad}}_{\mathrm{cII}}
  \equiv \rho\!\bigl(\mathcal{J}(F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{grad}})\bigr).
\label{eq:math64-decisive-observables}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[cII-surgery gate]
\label{thm:math64-cii-surgery-gate}
The decisive test outcome partitions the resolution ladder
$\{(X), (Y), (Z)\}$ into three mutually exclusive branches.
\begin{itemize}
\item[\textnormal{Case 1 (assembly bug).}] If
    $\Delta_{\mathrm{cII}} > \varepsilon_{\mathrm{mach}}\cdot
    \lVert F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{grad}}\rVert_{F}$ and
    $r^{\mathrm{grad}}_{\mathrm{cII}} \le \tau_{\mathrm{rel}}$
    while $r^{\mathrm{impl}}_{\mathrm{cII}} \approx 5.84\times 10^{-3}$,
    the anti-Hermitian mass originates in the explicit assembly of
    the projected-divergence operator and not in
    $E_{\mathrm{cII}}$ or $\Pi_{\mathrm{channel}}$. Resolution:
    patch \texttt{\_classII\_effective\_term\_t} to match the
    autograd reference; neither (X) nor (Y) is required.
\item[\textnormal{Case 2 (design defect).}] If
    $r^{\mathrm{grad}}_{\mathrm{cII}}$ and
    $r^{\mathrm{impl}}_{\mathrm{cII}}$ both exceed
    $\tau_{\mathrm{rel}}$ by comparable margins
    ($r^{\mathrm{grad}}_{\mathrm{cII}} \gtrsim 10^{-3}$), the anti-
    Hermitian mass is inherent to the design of $E_{\mathrm{cII}}$
    or $\Pi_{\mathrm{channel}}$. Resolution: proceed with (Y) as
    the mandatory rigorous path; (X) is a numerical stopgap only.
\item[\textnormal{Case 3 (inconclusive).}] If
    $r^{\mathrm{grad}}_{\mathrm{cII}}$ is non-zero but small
    ($10^{-8} \le r^{\mathrm{grad}}_{\mathrm{cII}} \le 10^{-5}$),
    both origins may contribute. Resolution: execute (X) for
    v2.6.0 operational closure and open Math65 for (Y)
    theoretical closure in parallel.
\end{itemize}
In all three cases, the decisive test is a \emph{prerequisite} to
selecting a resolution path. The existing §\ref{sec:resolutions}
recommendation of (X) for v2.6.0 is upgraded to "(X) provisionally,
decisive test mandatory before commit".
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Practical execution: tool extension v1.3]
The decisive test is naturally implemented as a new optional CLI
flag \texttt{--cII-grad-check} in
\texttt{Tools/check\_jacobian\_blocks.py} (v1.2.1 $\to$ v1.3),
branching inside the cII loop to call
\texttt{torch.autograd.grad} on a scalar
$E_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi; \text{params})$ evaluator exposed by the
backend, followed by the standard probe against both
$F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{impl}}$ and $F_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{grad}}$.
Runtime overhead is bounded by $\sim 2\times$ the cII evaluation
cost (one forward for value, one backward for grad), orthogonal to
the existing probe budget. No change to Math63 §2A.1 probe policy,
the §2A.3 Stage $\alpha$ acceptance condition, or
Corollary~\ref{cor:math64-inner-solver-routing}. The decisive
test is operational-grade rather than theorem-grade: it closes
the cII-surgery branch but does not itself resolve §2A.3, which
is already CLOSED by Theorem~\ref{thm:math64-stage-alpha-closure}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Parallelism with solver routing]
Corollary~\ref{cor:math64-inner-solver-routing} (PCG default) and
Proposition~\ref{thm:math64-cii-surgery-gate} (grad-vs-impl gate)

blocked on the cII-surgery decision. The decisive test feeds the

\end{remark}

\section{Three candidate resolutions for the cII structural mass}
\label{sec:resolutions}

The Stage $\alpha$ audit establishes that $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$
carries a structural $\sim 4\times 10^{-7}$ anti-Hermitian mass
that cannot be removed by any finer numerical evaluation. Three
resolutions are enumerated in order of decreasing theoretical
commitment and increasing computational cost. \emph{Selection
among (X), (Y), (Z) is gated by
Proposition~\ref{thm:math64-cii-surgery-gate} (decisive test);
this section describes the resolution content, not the selection
criterion.}

\paragraph{(X) In-solver symmetrisation.}
Replace $\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}$ in the Krylov inner loop by
$\tilde{\mathcal{J}}_{\mathrm{cII}} \equiv
(\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}} + \mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\dagger})/2$.
The resulting operator is provably real-self-adjoint under
Re$\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$, so MINRES / PCG can be routed
deterministically. The dropped anti-Hermitian part carries mass
$4.36\times 10^{-7}$ and vanishes at physical equilibria; the
approximation error in the Newton step is bounded by
$(4.36\times 10^{-7}) \cdot \lVert\Delta\Psi\rVert_{2}$, which is
well below the Newton convergence threshold $10^{-10}$ for
$\lVert\Delta\Psi\rVert_{2} \lesssim 10^{-3}$ (the regime of the
Brazovskii continuation). \emph{Pros}: zero theoretical commitment,
drop-in in the existing solver architecture. \emph{Cons}: does
not address the underlying structural asymmetry; the solver
converges to the zero of $\tilde{F} = F_{\mathrm{bra}} + \cdots +
(F_{\mathrm{cII}})_{\text{sym}}$ which differs from the zero of
$F$ by an $\mathcal{O}(10^{-7})$ displacement. Physically justifiable
only if we can bound this displacement independently.

\paragraph{(Y) Class II as Euler-Lagrange variation.}
Search for a scalar functional $\Phi_{\mathrm{cII}}[\Psi]$ on
$L^{2}(\mathbb{C}^{3N^{3}})$ such that $F_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi) =
(\delta \Phi_{\mathrm{cII}}/\delta \overline{\Psi})[\Psi]$. If such
$\Phi_{\mathrm{cII}}$ exists, the Wirtinger decomposition
automatically satisfies $A_{\mathrm{cII}} = A_{\mathrm{cII}}
^{\dagger}$ and $B_{\mathrm{cII}} = B_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathsf{T}}$,
so $a_{\mathrm{cII}} = 0$ identically. This would upgrade
Math63~Lemma~\ref{lem:math63-2A3-nl-wirtinger} to include the
cII block and close §2A.3 with a \emph{zero} anti-Hermitian mass
rather than a bounded one. \emph{Pros}: rigorous, completes the
Fréchet-variational structure of the TECT Lagrangian, connects
directly to the pillar-consistency program of
Math60~§S2-A. \emph{Cons}: requires either (i) writing
$F_{\mathrm{cII}}$ as the gradient of an explicit scalar, which is
obstructed by the projected divergence
$\nabla\cdot(\Pi_{\text{channel}}(\cdot))$ (a scalar functional
whose gradient is a projected divergence is not in general
possible --- the obstruction is essentially the
Helmholtz--Hodge decomposition), or (ii) restructuring the Class
II coupling so that the divergence is replaced by an
\emph{integral} projector, which changes the physics. A serious
candidate for a new Math65 note.

\paragraph{(Z) Unconditional FGMRES on the cII arm.}
Bypass the symmetry classification for the cII block entirely and
force FGMRES (flexible GMRES) on its Krylov inner loop. This
admits non-self-adjoint operators at the cost of restarted Krylov
subspaces. \emph{Pros}: zero modification to the operator. \emph{Cons}:
reintroduces exactly the ρ_lin stagnation pattern that motivated
Math63 in the first place; the $4\times 10^{-7}$ anti-Hermitian
mass is so small that FGMRES would almost never be the
appropriate method --- it is the \emph{combination} of anti-
Hermitian mass with an atypical denominator (Phase D
PLACEHOLDER) that triggered Stage [4/4].

\paragraph{Recommendation.}
Option (X) is adopted for v2.6.0 Phase D integration (Task
\#104), pending a decision on (Y) in a companion Math65 note.
The absolute-bound gate \eqref{eq:sec2d-gate} is compatible with
both (X) and (Y); under (Y) the gate is trivially satisfied with
$a_{\mathrm{cII}} = 0$, and under (X) the gate validates that the
symmetrised operator's residual approximation error is within
the Newton tolerance.

\section{Conclusion}
Stage $\alpha$ closes Math63 §2A.3 by promoting Corollary 2A.3
from single-state to window-level, under two independent
Jacobian-vector backends and under structural ablation of the
Class II couplings. The $4.357\times 10^{-7}$ anti-Hermitian
mass is identified as an operator-structural property of
$F_{\mathrm{cII}}$: $\mu^{2}$-independent, state-independent on
the thermal seed window, and bit-identical between central-FD

invariance
Lemma~\ref{lem:math64-fd-antisym}: the central-FD truncation term
is a symmetric trilinear form whose induced operator contributes
only to the Hermitian part of the FD Jacobian.

The §2D acceptance gate for Newton-Krylov v2.6.0 Phase D is
reformulated as an absolute bound
$a_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi) \le \tau_{\text{struct}} \in [10^{-6},
10^{-5}]$, superseding the relative ratio criterion that was
polluted by iterate-norm fluctuations on the Phase D
PLACEHOLDER. Task \#104 (v2.6.0 Phase D wiring) is unblocked
conditional on the §2D gate reformulation and on the choice
between (X) in-solver symmetrisation (recommended for immediate
integration), (Y) Class II as Euler-Lagrange variation

cII arm (not recommended).

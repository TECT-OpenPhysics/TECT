\section*{1. LATTICE SPACING SEQUENCE: CHOICE AND JUSTIFICATION}

\subsection*{1.1 The tuning parameter: a_BCC in units of inverse condensation mass}

The **BCC lattice spacing** in the PDE solve is denoted $a_{\rm BCC}$ and is
measured in units of the **inverse characteristic condensation mass-scale**:

$$a_{\rm BCC} := \frac{\Delta k_{\rm FBZ}}{1 \text{ [GeV]}}.$$

Here, $\Delta k_{\rm FBZ}$ is the **minimum k-space step** on the first
Brillouin zone of the reciprocal lattice. In the continuum limit,
$a_{\rm BCC} \to 0$ (finer reciprocal-lattice sampling).

More concretely, on a direct-space lattice of **side length L** and
**N grid points per side**, the reciprocal-lattice step is:

$$\Delta k = \frac{2\pi}{L} = \frac{2\pi}{N \cdot a_{\rm real}},$$

where $a_{\rm real}$ is the **direct-space lattice constant**. For the
BCC geometry:

$$a_{\rm BCC} = \left(\frac{\hbar c}{m^*}\right) \cdot \Delta k = \frac{200 \text{ MeV·fm}}{m^*} \cdot \Delta k.$$

With $m^* \sim 0.3$ GeV (canonical BCC gap), this gives:

$$a_{\rm BCC} \sim 0.67 \cdot \Delta k.$$

In the previous Phase-2.5 gate specification (Math56), valid BCC solutions
require **RMS$|\Psi^*|/\varphi_0 \geq 0.3$** and **Class-II guarded quotient**
$\rho_{\rm cut} \sim 10^{-3}\varphi_0^2$. These constraints are **independent
of $a_{\rm BCC}$**; they depend only on the numerical grid resolution.

**Decision rule for $\{a_k\}$**: To access the continuum limit while respecting
the Phase-2.5 gate, we require:

  (i)   At least three distinct $a_k$ values, spanning a factor of ~2 in $a_{\rm BCC}$.
  (ii)  All three must satisfy Phase-2.5 gates (RMS check, guarded quotient).
  (iii) The sequence should probe the **weakly-coupled continuum regime**
        (i.e., $a_{\rm BCC}$ small enough that lattice artefacts are sub-leading).

---

\subsection*{1.2 Proposed lattice sequence}

**Candidate sequence** (based on computational feasibility + continuum-scaling theory):

\begin{equation*}
  a_{\rm BCC}^{(1)} = 1.0 \quad (\text{reference scale}),
\end{equation*}
\begin{equation*}
  a_{\rm BCC}^{(2)} = 0.7 \quad (\text{intermediate scale, \sim 30\% finer}),
\end{equation*}
\begin{equation*}
  a_{\rm BCC}^{(3)} = 0.5 \quad (\text{fine scale, \sim 50\% finer than (1)}),
\end{equation*}
\begin{equation*}
  a_{\rm BCC}^{(4)} = 0.35 \quad (\text{very fine, \sim 65\% finer than (1), optional}).
\end{equation*}

**Rationale for the specific values**:

  1. **Hierarchy of spacing ratios**: $a_2 / a_1 = 0.7$, $a_3 / a_1 = 0.5$, $a_4 / a_1 = 0.35$.
     These ratios are chosen to span the "linear scaling regime" where continuum extrapolation
     is expected to be smooth. Traditional lattice QCD (e.g., $\beta = 6.0 \to 6.5 \to 7.0$)
     uses similar hierarchies.

  2. **Computational cost**: Each run at $(a_k, N_k)$ requires a Newton-Krylov solve.
     Math82-H experienced ~54 h wall time for a single $\mu^2$ point at $N=32$.
     Three to four spacings × one $\mu^2$ value each $\sim 150$–$200$ h total.

  3. **Continuum-scaling ansatz** (from condensed-matter RG theory): The BCC amplitude
     should scale as

     $$f(a) = f_{\infty} \left(1 + \frac{A}{a^\alpha} + O(a^{-2\alpha})\right),$$

     where $\alpha = 1$ (leading lattice artefact) for a second-order PDE discretization.
     See §2.3.

---

\subsection*{1.3 Alternative sequence (if $a = 1.0$ fails Phase-2.5 gate)}

If the Phase-2.5 gate rejects $a = 1.0$ (too coarse), we redefine the
reference scale:

  $$a_{\rm BCC}^{(1)} := 0.7, \quad a^{(2)} := 0.5, \quad a^{(3)} := 0.35.$$

The continuum limit is extracted from the three finest spacings. This
avoids the coarser lattices but reduces the lever arm for extrapolation
(a trade-off between data diversity and numerical stability).

**Pre-registration** (CLAUDE.md §6.3.3): If all four candidate spacings
fail Phase-2.5 on the same $\mu^2$ value, the protocol is **REFUTED**
and Pillar 6 reverts to T2 CONJECTURE. The falsification criterion is
explicit (see §6.2 below).

\section*{2. FIELD-THEORETIC AMPLITUDE EXTRACTOR}

\subsection*{2.1 Observable: the Fourier-space order-parameter amplitude}

The **field amplitude** $f_{\rm BCC}(a)$ at lattice spacing $a$ is extracted
from the ground-state solution $\Psi^*(a)$ (a 12-dimensional vector on the
shell) via:

$$f_{\rm BCC}(a) := \sqrt{\frac{1}{V_{\rm cell}} \sum_{\mathbf{k} \in \mathrm{shell}}
  |\Psi^*_\mathbf{k}(a)|^2},$$

where:

  - **$\Psi^*_\mathbf{k}(a)$**: the BCC order parameter in the Fourier basis,
    restricted to the first Brillouin zone shell (12 independent modes per cell).
  - **$V_{\rm cell}$**: the unit-cell volume (depends on the lattice choice; for BCC,
    $V_{\rm cell} = a_0^3 / 2$ in direct space, where $a_0$ is the conventional
    cubic lattice constant).
  - **Normalization**: The sum is over all 12 shell modes; the division by
    $\sqrt{V_{\rm cell}}$ removes the volume dependence, yielding a **density-like
    amplitude** (typical for order-parameter fields in condensed-matter physics).

**Alternative: momentum-space peak amplitude**

If the shell structure is sharply peaked (as expected for Brazovskii dynamics),
the amplitude can also be extracted as:

$$f_{\rm peak}(a) := \max_{\mathbf{k}} |\Psi^*_\mathbf{k}(a)|.$$

This is simpler but noisier if there are multiple shell modes at similar
|$\mathbf{k}|$. We default to the **RMS amplitude** above.

---

\subsection*{2.2 Finite-volume and finite-$(a)$ systematic corrections}

The extracted amplitude $f_{\rm BCC}(a)$ measured on a finite lattice
receives three types of corrections:

  **(I) Finite-volume (discretisation) effects**:

  The discretized Laplacian $\nabla^2_{\rm disc} \Psi$ differs from the continuum
  $\partial^2_\mu \partial^\mu \Psi$ by terms of order $a^2$. This modifies the effective
  coupling constant and hence $f_{\rm BCC}$.

  **Standard result** (lattice QCD, condensed-matter): for a second-order discretisation,

  $$f_{\rm BCC}(a) = f_{\infty} + c_1 a^2 + c_2 a^4 + \ldots.$$

  The coefficients $c_1, c_2$ depend on the BCC microscopic parameters but NOT on
  the spatial extent $L$ (in the limit $L \gg a$).

  **(II) Finite-system boundary effects**:

  If the lattice has **open boundary conditions** (OBC) rather than periodic (PBC),
  there is an **additional** $O(1/L)$ correction that decays with system size.
  Standard practice: use **periodic boundary conditions** to suppress this.

  **(III) Truncation of shell modes**:

  The BCC shell has 12 modes. If the numerical solver incompletely converges any
  one mode, the extracted amplitude will be biased. The Phase-2.5 gate (Math56)
  partially mitigates this via the **Class-II guarded quotient** check, which
  verifies that all 12 modes are above the noise floor.

**Honest limitation**: Sources (I) and (II) are **mathematically well-understood**.
Source (III) is **empirical** — it depends on the Newton-Krylov convergence
criterion and the initial-guess seeding. We address this via two safeguards:

  1. **Phase-2.5 gate requirement** on all three $a_k$ values.
  2. **Residual check** (see §4.2): the Richardson-extrapolation fit residual
     must be < 20% of the fitted amplitude. If not, source (III) is contaminating.

\section*{3. RICHARDSON EXTRAPOLATION FRAMEWORK}

\subsection*{3.1 Scaling ansatz: leading and next-order terms}

Assume the discretisation error follows the form:

\begin{equation}\label{eq:richardson}
  f_{\rm BCC}(a) = f_{\infty} \left(1 + \frac{A}{a^p} + \frac{B}{a^q} + \ldots\right),
\end{equation}

where:

  - **$f_{\infty}$**: the **continuum limit** (the target).
  - **$A, B, \ldots$**: dimensionless coefficients (typically $O(1)$ or $O(0.1)$).
  - **$p, q, \ldots$**: **leading and subleading exponents** (typically $p=1$ for
    second-order discretisation, $q=2$).

For the **three-point Richardson extrapolation**, we assume:

\begin{equation}\label{eq:richardson_3pt}
  f_{\rm BCC}(a) = f_{\infty} + A_1 a^p + A_2 a^q + O(a^{\min(p,q)+1}),
\end{equation}

where $p < q$ (leading exponent is smaller, corrections largest).

---

\subsection*{3.2 Fitting procedure: least-squares polynomial}

Given three measured values $\{f_1, f_2, f_3\}$ at spacings $\{a_1, a_2, a_3\}$,
we fit the ansatz:

\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{F}(a) = f_{\infty} + A_1 a^p + A_2 a^q
\end{equation}

to the three data points via **least-squares minimization**:

\begin{equation}
  \chi^2(f_{\infty}, A_1, A_2) := \sum_{k=1}^{3}
    \left(f_k - \mathcal{F}(a_k)\right)^2.
\end{equation}

**Standard approach**: For $p = 1$ and $q = 2$, this is a **nonlinear fit**
(since $f_{\infty}, A_1, A_2$ appear in a sum of functions of these parameters).

**Implementation**: Use a **nonlinear least-squares solver** (e.g., Levenberg-Marquardt
in SciPy). Initial guesses:

  - $f_{\infty}^{(0)} = f_1$ (coarsest-grid value).
  - $A_1^{(0)} = (f_1 - f_2) / a_1 \approx 0.1$ (rough estimate).
  - $A_2^{(0)} = (f_2 - f_3) / a_2^2 \approx 0.01$ (rough estimate).

---

\subsection*{3.3 Convergence criterion and residual check}

After fitting, compute:

  1. **Fit residual**: $\chi^2_{\rm final}$ (the sum-of-squared residuals at the
     fitted parameters).

  2. **Relative residual**: $\epsilon_{\rm fit} := \sqrt{\chi^2 / 3} / f_{\infty}$
     (typical per-point error relative to the continuum estimate).

  3. **Parameter error bars**: $\Delta f_{\infty}, \Delta A_1, \Delta A_2$ (from
     the Hessian of $\chi^2$ at the minimum).

**Falsification gate** (CLAUDE.md §6.3.3):

  $$\boxed{\epsilon_{\rm fit} < 20\% \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{CONVERGENCE OK}}.$$

  If $\epsilon_{\rm fit} \geq 20\%$, the three data points do not lie on a smooth
  curve (e.g., due to unsystematic numerical noise, incomplete convergence at one
  $a_k$, or unaccounted-for physical correction). The continuum limit is **UNRELIABLE**.

---

\subsection*{3.4 Error propagation: uncertainty in $f_{\infty}$}

The **error bar on the continuum amplitude** is:

\begin{equation}
  \Delta f_{\infty} = \sqrt{\left(\frac{\partial f_{\infty}}{\partial f_k}\right)^2
    (\Delta f_k)^2 + (\text{fit residual})^2},
\end{equation}

where:

  - **$(\Delta f_k)^2$**: numerical noise in each measured amplitude (estimated
    from the Phase-2.5 convergence tolerance, typically $\sim 10^{-6} |f|$).
  - **Fit residual**: the smooth-function fitting error (captured by $\epsilon_{\rm fit}$).

**Target precision**: We require $\Delta f_{\infty} / f_{\infty} \lesssim 5\%$ for
the continuum limit to be considered **quantitatively reliable**.

\section*{4. EXECUTION AND DIAGNOSTIC GATES}

\subsection*{4.1 The four-stage workflow}

**Stage 1: BCC Phase-2 solve** (per $a_k$, one at a time)

  For each $a_k \in \{a^{(1)}, a^{(2)}, a^{(3)}\}$:

    (1a) Set up the lattice Laplacian at spacing $a_k$ in the Brazovskii
         Hamiltonian (Math82).
    (1b) Use the **Class-II guarded quotient** initialization (Math56 §7)
         with the default $\rho_{\rm cut} \sim 10^{-3} \varphi_0^2$.
    (1c) Run Newton-Krylov continuation from $\mu^2 \approx +0.005$ (near critical
         point) to $\mu^2 = 0.26$ (target BCC state).
    (1d) **Apply Phase-2.5 gate**: Verify RMS$|\Psi^*|/\varphi_0 \geq 0.3$,
         Class-II quotient satisfied, spectral gap positive (Math56 Theorems 1–2).

  **Expected wall time per solve**: ~50–100 h (based on Math82-H experience).
  **Total**: ~150–300 h for three spacings.

**Stage 2: Amplitude extraction**

  For each successful BCC solve $\Psi^*_k(a_k)$:

    (2a) Compute $f_{\rm BCC}(a_k) := \sqrt{\frac{1}{V_{\rm cell}}\sum_{\mathbf{k}}
         |\Psi^*_{\mathbf{k}}|^2}$ in Fourier space.
    (2b) Verify orthogonality of all 12 shell modes (check for accidental
         degeneracies or mode collapse).
    (2c) Record both $f_{\rm BCC}(a_k)$ and the **individual shell-mode
         amplitudes** $\{\psi_i(a_k)\}_{i=1}^{12}$ for diagnostic purposes.

  **Expected time**: < 1 h (post-processing).

**Stage 3: Richardson extrapolation**

  (3a) Collect $\{(a_1, f_1), (a_2, f_2), (a_3, f_3)\}$.
  (3b) Fit ansatz (Eq. \ref{eq:richardson_3pt}) with $p=1, q=2$.
  (3c) Extract $f_{\infty}, A_1, A_2$ and error bars.
  (3d) Compute $\epsilon_{\rm fit}$.
  (3e) **Apply residual gate**: if $\epsilon_{\rm fit} \geq 20\%$, **ABORT
       and investigate** (see §4.2).

  **Expected time**: < 1 h.

**Stage 4: Validation and interpretation**

  (4a) Compare $f_{\infty}$ to the tree-level prediction from Math231 §2.2.
  (4b) Estimate the **composite-Higgs dressing factor** $\kappa_{\rm dressing} :=
       f_{\infty} / f_{\rm tree}$. [Deferred to Turn 7 / Math236.]
  (4c) Feed $f_{\infty}$ (and its error bar) to the $\mu_H^2$ formula in Math234 §2.3.
  (4d) Assess whether $\mu_H^2 \approx (100 \text{ GeV})^2$ is achievable (within
       dressing factors).

  **Expected time**: < 1 h (Turn 7).

\subsection*{4.2 Diagnostic gates and failure modes}

If any of the following occur, the protocol **FAILS and Pillar 6 reverts to
CONJECTURE pending revision**:

| **Failure mode** | **Diagnostic** | **Recovery / Falsification** |
|---|---|---|
| Phase-2.5 gate fails at any $a_k$ | Solve converges but RMS$\|\Psi^*\|/\varphi_0 < 0.3$ or Class-II quotient violated | Retry with finer initial seeding (Math55 critical-point technique); if all attempts fail, grid-independence assumption is violated — Pillar 6 FAILS. |

\section*{5. QUANTITATIVE SANITY CHECKS}

This section applies CLAUDE.md §6.3.4 (mandatory quantitative-sanity-check
requirement): minimum ONE explicit check from each category.

**(A) Dimensional analysis**:

  The extracted amplitude $f_{\rm BCC}(a)$ should have units of **[GeV]**.
  The Brazovskii potential $\mathcal{F}[\Psi]$ has units **[GeV$^4$]**.
  The order parameter $\Psi$ has units **[GeV$^d$]** where $d$ is a dimension
  (depending on the Fourier decomposition). For a scalar field in real space,
  $\Psi \sim [\text{GeV}]$, so $f_{\rm BCC} \sim [\text{GeV}]$ is correct.

  **Check PASS**: Dimensional consistency is exact.

**(B) Magnitude / order-of-magnitude**:

  From Math231 §2, the tree-level amplitude is $f_{\rm tree} \sim 5$ GeV.
  The continuum limit should be $f_{\infty} \sim \mathcal{O}(1$–$100)$ GeV
  (i.e., $0.1 \lesssim f_{\infty} / f_{\rm tree} \lesssim 100$).

  If the Richardson fit yields $f_{\infty} > 10^4$ GeV or $f_{\infty} < 0.01$ GeV,
  the extrapolation is likely pathological (e.g., unphysical fit or data corruption).

  **Pre-registration**: If $f_{\infty}$ falls outside $[0.1, 10^4]$ GeV, the
  continuum limit is **REFUTED**.

**(C) Limit-case behavior**:

  In the continuum limit $a \to 0$, the discretisation errors vanish: the amplitude
  should **monotonically approach** the continuum value, not oscillate. The Richardson
  ansatz (Eq. \ref{eq:richardson_3pt}) predicts $f(a) - f_{\infty} \propto a^p$ with
  $p > 0$, i.e., all data points should lie on one side of $f_{\infty}$ (either all
  above or all below).

  **Check criterion**: Compute the sign of $(f_k - f_{\infty})$ for each $k$. If the
  signs change, the data violate the assumed continuum-scaling law.

  **Pre-registration**: If signs oscillate, the data are **INCONSISTENT** with the
  assumed lattice-scaling form; protocol is **REFUTED and requires revision**.

**(D) Exponential-magnitude check**:

  The leading-order correction is $A_1 a^p$. At $a = 0.5$ (half of $a = 1.0$),
  with $p = 1$, the fractional correction is $A_1 \cdot 0.5 \sim 0.05$–$0.2$
  (assuming $A_1 \sim O(0.1)$–$O(0.4)$). This means $f(0.5) / f_{\infty} \sim 1.05$–$1.20$.

  **Sanity check**: The three data-point ratios should be consistent with a monotonic
  sequence in $a$. Verify:

  $$\frac{f_1 - f_{\infty}}{f_2 - f_{\infty}} \approx \left(\frac{a_1}{a_2}\right)^p.$$

  If this ratio deviates by $> 50\%$, the power law $a^p$ is not valid (likely
  $p \ne 1$, or higher-order terms are important).

  **Pre-registration**: If power-law ratio fails by $> 50\%$, **UPGRADE ansatz
  to include $a^q$ term explicitly and re-fit**.

**(E) Sign-direction physics**:

  The **physical amplitude** of the BCC condensate should be **positive** (it is
  an RMS norm). If any of $f_1, f_2, f_3, f_{\infty}$ come out negative, the
  extraction or fitting procedure has a sign error.

  **Check MANDATORY**: $f_k > 0$ and $f_{\infty} > 0$.

**(F) Conservation / numerical reproducibility**:

  If two independent Newton-Krylov solves of the same $(a_k, \mu^2)$ system
  (with different random seeds or continuation paths) both reach the Phase-2.5
  gate, do they yield the **same** amplitude $f_{\rm BCC}(a_k)$ to within 1%?

  **Check criterion** (Turn 7 execution): Run at least one $a_k$ value twice.
  If the two runs give $f^{(1)}$ and $f^{(2)}$ with $|f^{(1)} - f^{(2)}| / f^{(1)} > 1\%$,
  the numerics are **unreliable** (insufficient convergence or non-uniqueness of
  ground state). Flag for investigation.

  **Pre-registration**: If reproducibility check fails, the continuum limit is
  **SUSPECT** and requires additional verification.

---

**Summary of sanity checks**:

  (A) Dimensional: **PASS** (exact).
  (B) Magnitude: **CONDITIONAL** — $f_{\infty} \in [0.1, 10^4]$ GeV required.
  (C) Limit-case monotonicity: **CONDITIONAL** — Sign consistency of $(f_k - f_{\infty})$.
  (D) Power-law exponent: **CONDITIONAL** — Ratio test within 50%.
  (E) Sign-direction: **MANDATORY** — $f_k > 0, \, f_{\infty} > 0$.
  (F) Reproducibility: **CONDITIONAL** — $(f^{(1)} - f^{(2)}) / f^{(1)} < 1\%$ if
      done in triplicate.

If all checks PASS, continuum limit is **QUANTITATIVELY TRUSTWORTHY**.
If any conditional check FAILs, escalate to diagnostic mode (§4.2).

\section*{6. DEVIL'S-ADVOCATE SELF-TEST (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}

Three concrete objections, each addressed as DISMISSED / VALID-WITH-MITIGATION / UPHELD:

**Objection α**: "Richardson extrapolation assumes smooth $a$-dependence, but BCC
lattice effects are non-perturbative at coarse spacings like $a = 1.0$. Fitting
$a^1 + a^2$ may not capture the true scaling."

  **Verdict: VALID-WITH-MITIGATION.**

  **Mitigation**: This is the fundamental risk of any continuum-extrapolation protocol.
  We address it by:
    (i)   Requiring $a_{\min} \leq 0.35$ (fine enough to probe perturbative regime).
    (ii)  Performing the sign-consistency check (sanity check C) to reject pathological fits.
    (iii) Adding $a^3$ term to the ansatz if residuals $\epsilon_{\rm fit} > 10\%$.
    (iv)  Flagging $\kappa_{\rm dressing} > 10$ as "strong-coupling regime, all-loop
          resummation needed" (Task #127).

  **Task created**: Q-2026-04-30-Richardson-validity (explicit verification
  that $p=1$ ansatz is valid via comparison to full nonlinear fit with $a^0.5$,
  $a^{3/2}$, etc.).

---

**Objection β**: "The Newton-Krylov solver may not converge to the **same** ground
state at each $a_k$, introducing spurious differences in $f_{\rm BCC}(a_k)$. How do we
know all three solutions correspond to the same physical phase?"

  **Verdict: VALID-WITH-MITIGATION.**

  **Mitigation**: The Phase-2.5 gate (Math56) is **grid-independent** — it checks
  properties of the solution (RMS norm, spectral gap, guarded quotient) that do not
  depend on the discretisation. All solutions passing this gate lie on the **same
  physical branch** (the BCC ordered phase).

  Additionally, we enforce **continuity of the seed**: solve at $a^{(1)}$, then
  use $\Psi^*(a^{(1)})$ as the initial guess for $a^{(2)}$, etc. This ensures we
  stay on the same continuation path.

  **Robustness**: If two independent solves (different seeds) at the same $a_k$
  both pass the gate, we obtain two values of $f_{\rm BCC}(a_k)$; if they differ
  by $> 1\%$, the ground state is **multi-valued** and the ansatz breaks down.
  Sanity check (F) catches this.

  **Task created**: Q-2026-04-30-Uniqueness-of-BCC-ground-state (verify that
  all Phase-2.5-approved solutions at a given $a_k$ have $\Delta f / f < 1\%$).

---

**Objection γ**: "The composite-Higgs dressing factor $\kappa_{\rm dressing}$
is NOT included in the protocol. If it is order-unity important, the continuum
extrapolation is misleading about the physical amplitude at the EW scale."

  **Verdict: VALID-WITH-MITIGATION.**

  **Mitigation**: This is **not a defect of the protocol** but a **scope limitation**.
  The protocol extracts $f_{\infty}$ from BCC numerics; the dressing factor is
  computed separately in Task #127 (composite-Higgs framework). The two are
  combined at Turn 7 / Math236 (§5.2: "Estimate of composite-Higgs dressing").

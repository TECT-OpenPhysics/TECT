\subsection*{1. Original intent}

Within the toy 12-mode Hessian model, scan the $(\mu^2,\lambda)$
plane to (Q1) identify the regime where BCC is a local minimum
(Hessian positive-semidefinite) and (Q2) within that regime
identify parameters where Kibble--Zurek defect production is
below a cosmological threshold. The aim was to determine whether
Lemma E$_3'$ Mechanism (III) might be a parameter-only solution
or whether non-parameter physics (quantum tunnelling, dark-matter
coupling, modified cosmology) is required.

\subsection*{2. Implemented (toy) computation}

For each $(\mu^2,\lambda)$ grid point: (i) compute the simplest
12-mode equal-amp Hessian using
$A_0^2=|\mu^2|/|\lambda c_4|$ (the
$|\cdot|$ wrapper masks the no-real-stationary-point pathology
documented in Math373 \S2), (ii) evaluate eigenvalues, (iii)
estimate Kibble--Zurek $n_{\rm defect}\sim\hat\xi^{-3}$ with
$\hat\xi\sim(\gamma q_0^2+\mu^2)^{-1/2}\tau_Q^{0.26}$, (iv) flag
``Pillar 4 feasible'' if BCC is local-min AND $n_{\rm defect}<10^{-3}$.

\subsection*{3. Numerical output (recorded for archive only)}

200 of 400 cells flagged ``BCC local-min'' under the toy formula
with the masking $|\cdot|$. 0 of 400 cells satisfied both
conditions. Within the toy formulation the verdict was
``Mechanism (III) MUST be a non-parameter solution.''

\textbf{Why the verdict is invalid}: the toy form does not have a
real stationary point in the canonical $(\mu^2<0, \lambda<0)$
quadrant; the Hessian eigenvalue computation at the masked
fictitious point is therefore not a local-min test of anything

but uses the same wrong $\gamma$-as-gradient-coefficient identification.

\subsection*{0. Executive summary}

Four production Math374 runs (operator-PC, 2026-05-09) at $N\in\{16,32\}$
and $\mu^{2}\in\{+0.005,-0.5,+0.26\}$ produced the pre-registered
verdicts FAIL/FAIL/INDETERMINATE/FAIL. The verdict is \textbf{not} a
refutation of Math82-AddH or canonical-form BCC stability. Instead the
result identifies the actual scope of the Math374 framework: 200--400
iterations of adaptive steepest descent from a 6-cosine BCC seed
\textit{do not relax to a true ground state of the canonical
free energy} when the dispersion soft-mode shell is wide. The 30--33
negative Hessian eigenvalues are residual unstable modes that the
6-mode-restricted ansatz cannot suppress; they are
\textit{not} an artefact of the canonical free energy (proved by
the kinetic dispersion analysis below). This finding motivates
Math376 = production-backend Newton-Krylov execution.

\subsection*{2. Goldstone signature at $N=32$, $\mu^{2}=+0.005$}

The most diagnostic feature of Run 4: the lowest 33 eigenvalues are all
negative, but the LAST three (lam$_{31}$, lam$_{32}$, lam$_{33}$)
are exactly degenerate at $-2.243\times 10^{-4}$. This three-fold
degeneracy at near-zero magnitude is the unambiguous signature of the
\textbf{three translation Goldstone modes} predicted by Noether's
theorem for spatial-translation invariance of the canonical free
energy. They are slightly negative (rather than exactly zero) because
$|\nabla F|/\sqrt{V}\approx 0.28$ at the relaxation endpoint --- the
configuration is not yet stationary, so the Goldstones acquire a small
artefactual mass.

The N=16 to N=32 progression shows the Goldstone signature
\emph{emerging} (15 near-zero candidates at N=16 collapse to 3 at N=32),
which is the expected lattice-refinement behaviour. The non-Goldstone
spectrum tightens correspondingly.

\subsection*{3. Why the 6-cosine seed cannot be a true ground state}

The canonical Brazovskii kinetic dispersion is
\begin{equation}
\omega(\mathbf k)=Y(k^{2}-q_{0}^{2})^{2}+\mu^{2}.
\label{eq:dispersion}
\end{equation}
The set of \emph{linearly unstable} modes (those with $\omega<0$ around
the disordered state) is the shell
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{U}(\mu^{2})=\left\{\mathbf k:k^{2}\in\left[q_{0}^{2}-\sqrt{|\mu^{2}|/Y},\,q_{0}^{2}+\sqrt{|\mu^{2}|/Y}\right]\right\}\quad(\mu^{2}<0).
\end{equation}
For the locked $Y=1$, $q_{0}^{2}=0.4626$:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\mu^{2}=+0.005$: $\mathcal{U}$ is empty in the linear analysis,
  but nonlinear-induced instabilities at the BCC seed configuration
  populate a thin shell of finite width.
\item $\mu^{2}=-0.5$: $\mathcal{U}$ spans
  $|\mathbf k|\in[0,1.08]$ --- a wide shell containing many modes
  beyond the 12 BCC first-shell vectors.
\item $\mu^{2}=+0.26$: $\mathcal{U}$ is empty; the only stationary
  point is $\Psi=0$ (disordered).
\end{itemize}

The 6-cosine equal-amplitude ansatz puts amplitude only in 12 modes at
$|\mathbf k|=q_{0}$. The full Hessian at this configuration includes
contributions from \textit{all} other unstable modes in $\mathcal{U}$,
which the seed ansatz does not address. Adaptive steepest descent
restricted to the BCC manifold can adjust the 6-cosine amplitude but
cannot transfer probability into orthogonal unstable directions; those
modes persist as negative Hessian eigenvalues.

This is consistent with Run 3 ($\mu^{2}=+0.26$, $\mathcal{U}=\emptyset$):
no instability shell exists, the relaxation simply drives $\Psi\to 0$,
and the resulting Hessian (linear operator on the disordered state)
has all-positive spectrum equal to the kinetic dispersion at each
sampled lattice momentum.

\subsection*{4. What Math82-AddH actually proved}

Math82-AddH cites $\varphi_{0}\sim 0.266$ and $m^{*2}\approx 9.005$ at
$\mu^{2}=+0.005$ derived analytically from the shell-spectral-gap
theorem (Math37-AddA \S A.4). The validity of those analytical bounds
is \textit{not} affected by Math374's numerical finding because:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Math82-AddH's $m^{*2}$ derivation uses the
  shell-localised effective action, which integrates out the wide-shell
  modes that Math374 finds as residual instabilities.
\item Math82-AddH's H2 hypothesis explicitly assumes a
  ``Newton-Krylov-converged endpoint at $\|\nabla F\|<10^{-8}$'',
  which is six orders of magnitude tighter than Math374's
  $|\nabla F|/\sqrt{V}\approx 10^{-1}$.
\item The production runs that produced the cited Math82-AddF/G
  results used the Math82-Phase-Z 53.9-hour Newton-Krylov pipeline
  with full multi-mode Fourier representation, not a 6-cosine seed.
\end{enumerate}

Math374's 6-cosine + 200-step steepest descent is a \textbf{seed
generator} (matching the role of \texttt{bcc\_analytic\_seed.py}),
not a ground-state extractor. The current finding is
``the seed Hessian has residual instabilities'', not
``the canonical theory has no BCC local minimum''.

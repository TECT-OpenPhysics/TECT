\subsection*{1. Reproduction of the canonical formula (Math383 §1)}

For a single-shell ansatz on lattice $\Lambda$ with $n$ independent cosines at shell $|\mathbf q|=q_{0}$,
\begin{equation}
  \Psi(\mathbf r) = A\,\sum_{j=1}^{n}\cos(\mathbf q_{j}\cdot\mathbf r),\qquad
  \phi_{0}^{2}\;\equiv\;\langle\Psi^{2}\rangle = \frac{n}{2}\,A^{2},
\end{equation}
the Brazovskii free energy per volume reduces (with the gradient term tuned to vanish at $|\mathbf q|=q_{0}$) to
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
    \frac{\mathcal{F}(\Lambda)}{V}
    = \frac{\mu^{2}}{2}\,\phi_{0}^{2}
    + \frac{\lambda K_{4}(\Lambda)}{2}\,\phi_{0}^{4}
    + \frac{\gamma K_{6}(\Lambda)}{3}\,\phi_{0}^{6}.
  \;}
\end{equation}
The stationarity condition $\partial(\mathcal{F}/V)/\partial\phi_{0}^{2}=0$ gives the quadratic
\begin{equation}
  a\,x^{2} + b\,x + c = 0,\qquad
  x\equiv\phi_{0}^{2},\qquad
  a=\gamma K_{6},\quad b=\lambda K_{4},\quad c=\mu^{2}/2,
\end{equation}
with positive root
\begin{equation}
  x_{*} = \frac{-b + \sqrt{b^{2}-4ac}}{2a}.
\end{equation}
This is identical to Math383 §1 equation (the formula is correct).

The locked TECT canonical parameters are
\begin{equation}
  \lambda = -0.43,\qquad \gamma = +1.62,\qquad q_{0}=0.6801747616,\qquad Y=1.
\end{equation}
The structure-factor table from Math383 §2 (Brazovskii 1975 / Leibler 1980 / Fredrickson-Helfand 1987 normalisation $\langle\Psi^{4}\rangle = K_{4}\,\langle\Psi^{2}\rangle^{2}$) is

Math383 §3 reports $-0.165$. The discrepancy is $\sim 10\%$. Repeating the same exercise for FCC, HEX, lamellar yields the audited $\mathcal{F}/V$ column above; Math383 §3's reported values disagree by factors $\sim 1.9, \sim 2.8, \sim 6.4$ respectively.

\paragraph{Hierarchy.} The audited values still satisfy
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{F}_{\rm BCC}/V < \mathcal{F}_{\rm FCC}/V < \mathcal{F}_{\rm HEX}/V < \mathcal{F}_{\rm lamellar}/V < 0
  \qquad (\mu^{2}=-1).

So at $\mu^{2}=-1$ the Math383 \emph{ordering} survives audit, but the \emph{margins} are different (audited $|\mathcal{F}_{\rm BCC}|/|\mathcal{F}_{\rm lamellar}| = 1.77$, not the $\sim 12.7$ Math383 §3 reports). The ranking is robust; the magnitudes are not.

$\sqrt{0.1444}=0.380$;

\begin{align*}

\subsection*{6. Devil's-advocate (CLAUDE.md §6.3 + §6.3.4 + §6.3.5(a))}

\section{2. The Cubic Point Group $O_h$ and Its Character Table}

$O_h$ is the **octahedral group with inversion**:
$$O_h = O \times \{1, i\},$$

$O_h$ has exactly **5 conjugacy classes** (inversion centralizes all rotations). For our purposes, we focus on the **5 irreducible representations** of $O$ (the rotation subgroup, order 24), since the spinor representation of SO(10) will restrict to these irreps.

\section{3. SO(10) Spinor Character Restricted to $O_h$}

\subsection{3.1 The SO(10) Spinor Representation}

\subsection{3.2 Embedding $O_h$ into SO(10)}

The embedding is via the **cubic-symmetry action**:
$$O_h \subset \mathrm{SO}(3) \subset \mathrm{SO}(10),$$
where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item SO(3) acts on the three spatial axes (the cube coordinates).
  \item SO(10) is the full 10-dimensional rotation group.
  \item The remaining 7 orthogonal dimensions (the "internal" part of SO(10)) are trivial under the cubic action.
\end{itemize}

Concretely, an element $g \in O_h$ acts as a rotation/reflection in the first 3 coordinates and as the identity in the last 7.

\subsection{3.3 Spinor Character Formula}

For a rotation $R \in \mathrm{SO}(n)$ by angles $\theta_1, \ldots, \theta_{n/2}$ (in the 2-planes that diagonalize $R$), the character of the spinor is:

$$\chi_{\mathbf{spinor}}(R) = \sum_{j=1}^{n/2} e^{i\theta_j/2} = 2 \sum_{j=1}^{n/2} \cos(\theta_j/2).$$

For SO(10), we have 5 rotation angles $\theta_1, \ldots, \theta_5$. Under the cubic-symmetry embedding, only the first angle $\theta_1$ (corresponding to the cubic-rotation axis in the first 3-D subspace) is nontrivial; the others $(\theta_2, \ldots, \theta_5)$ are zero (or pair-wise opposite, summing to zero in the exponent).

More precisely, if $R = R_{\text{cubic}} \oplus I_{7}$ (rotation $R_{\text{cubic}}$ in the 3-D subspace, identity in the 7-D complement), then:
$$\chi_{\mathbf{16}}(R) = \chi_{\mathbf{spinor}_3}(R_{\text{cubic}}) \times \chi_{\mathbf{spinor}_7}(I_7).$$

The spinor of SO(3) has dimension 2; the spinor of SO(7) has dimension $2^{(7-1)/2} = 8$. Thus:
$$\chi_{\mathbf{16}}(R) = \chi_{\mathbf{spinor}_3}(R_{\text{cubic}}) \times 8.$$

Alternatively, using the Weyl character formula directly on SO(10), the spinor character for a rotation with angles $\theta_1, \ldots, \theta_5$ is:
$$\chi_{\mathbf{16}}(R) = e^{i\theta_1/2} + e^{-i\theta_1/2} + e^{i\theta_2/2} + e^{-i\theta_2/2} + \cdots + e^{i\theta_5/2} + e^{-i\theta_5/2},$$
with the constraint that for the cubic embedding, only the first angle contributes nontrivially.

\section{4. Frobenius Reciprocity Computation}

\subsection{4.1 Frobenius Reciprocity Lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Frobenius Reciprocity]
Let $G$ be a finite group, $H$ a subgroup, and $\pi$ a representation of $G$. The multiplicity $m_\tau$ of an irreducible representation $\tau$ of $H$ in the restriction $\pi|_H$ is:
$$m_\tau = \frac{1}{|H|} \sum_{h \in H} \chi_\pi(h) \overline{\chi_\tau(h)}.$$
\end{lemma}

In our case:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $G = O$ (rotation group, order 24).
  \item $H = O$ (same group).
  \item $\pi = \mathbf{16}$ (spinor of SO(10), restricted to $O$).
  \item $\tau \in \{A_1, A_2, E, T_1, T_2\}$ (irreps of $O$).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{4.2 Multiplicity Calculation}

For each irrep $\tau$, we compute:
$$m_\tau = \frac{1}{24} \sum_{h \in O} \chi_{\mathbf{16}}(h) \chi_\tau(h).$$

\textbf{Notation}: Let $n_C$ denote the size of conjugacy class $C$, and $\chi_C$ the character value at a representative of $C$.

\subsubsection{4.2.1 Multiplicity of $A_1$}

\begin{align}
m_{A_1} &= \frac{1}{24} \left[ n_{C_1} \chi_{\mathbf{16}}(C_1) \chi_{A_1}(C_1) + n_{8C_3} \chi_{\mathbf{16}}(8C_3) \chi_{A_1}(8C_3) + \cdots \right] \\
&= \frac{1}{24} \left[ 1 \cdot 16 \cdot 1 + 8 \cdot 1 \cdot 1 + 3 \cdot 0 \cdot 1 + 6 \cdot 0 \cdot 1 + 6 \cdot (-2) \cdot 1 \right] \\
&= \frac{1}{24} \left[ 16 + 8 + 0 + 0 - 12 \right] \\
&= \frac{12}{24} = \frac{1}{2}.

\section{TECT Microscopic PDE from Enriched Shell--Defect Carrier}

\subsection{1. UV Starting Point (Class II Action)}

We begin from the Class II UV action:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{UV}}^{(II)} =
\frac{M_X^2}{2} X_i^a X_i^a
+ \alpha X_i^a J_i^a[\Psi]
+ \beta X_i^a K_i^a[P,\psi]
\end{equation}

where:
\begin{equation}
J_i^a[\Psi] = \partial_i \Psi^a 
K_i^a[P,\psi] = (\partial_i P_B)\, \psi^a
\end{equation}

Integrating out $X_i^a$ yields:

\begin{equation}
X_i^a = -\frac{1}{M_X^2} \left(
\alpha J_i^a + \beta K_i^a
\right)
\end{equation}

Substituting back:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{eff}} =
- \frac{1}{2M_X^2}
\left(
\alpha J_i^a + \beta K_i^a
\right)^2
\end{equation}

---

\subsection{2. Expansion into Physical Sectors}

Expanding:

\begin{align}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{eff}} =
& -\frac{\alpha^2}{2M_X^2} (J_i^a)^2
- \frac{\beta^2}{2M_X^2} (K_i^a)^2
- \frac{\alpha\beta}{M_X^2} J_i^a K_i^a
\end{align}

Each term corresponds to:

\begin{itemize}
\item $(JJ)$: standard kinetic sector
\item $(KK)$: defect-induced mass/tension
\item $(JK)$: chiral mixing (Dirac structure)
\end{itemize}

---

\subsection{3. Projection onto Shell Carrier}

The field is decomposed as:

\begin{equation}
\Psi(x) = \sum_{A} \psi_A(x) e^{i q_A \cdot x}
\end{equation}

with BCC star:

\begin{equation}
|q_A| = q_0, \quad A = 1,\dots,12
\end{equation}

Gradient acts as:

\begin{equation}
\partial_i \Psi \rightarrow i q_{A,i} \psi_A + \partial_i \psi_A
\end{equation}

---

\subsection{4. Effective Kinetic Operator}

After shell averaging:

\begin{equation}
\langle q_{A,i} q_{A,j} \rangle = \frac{q_0^2}{3} \delta_{ij}
\end{equation}

Thus:

\begin{equation}
(JJ) \Rightarrow \lambda_\parallel (\partial_i \psi)^2
\end{equation}

with:

\begin{equation}
\lambda_\parallel = \frac{\alpha^2}{M_X^2} \frac{q_0^2}{3}
\end{equation}

---

\subsection{5. Defect Background Contribution}

For $CP^2$ defect:

\begin{equation}
P_B = z_B z_B^\dagger
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
\partial_i P_B =
\theta'(r) (e_i z_B^\dagger + z_B e_i^\dagger)
\end{equation}

Then:

\begin{equation}
(KK) \Rightarrow m_0^2 \psi
\end{equation}

where:

\begin{equation}
m_0^2 \sim \frac{\beta^2}{M_X^2} \int (\theta')^2 dr
\end{equation}

---

\subsection{6. Mixed Term (Dirac Structure)}

The cross term:

\begin{equation}
(JK) \sim \psi^\dagger (q \cdot \partial P_B) \psi
\end{equation}

generates antisymmetric coupling:

\begin{equation}
(JK) \Rightarrow i \beta_{\mathrm{eff}} \, \epsilon_{ijk} \partial_j \psi_k
\end{equation}

with:

\begin{equation}
\beta_{\mathrm{eff}} \sim \frac{\alpha\beta}{M_X^2} q_0 \theta'
\end{equation}

---

\subsection{7. Final Microscopic PDE}

Collecting all terms:

\begin{equation}
\rho \, \partial_t^2 \psi =
\lambda_\parallel \nabla(\nabla \cdot \psi)
- \alpha_{\mathrm{eff}} \nabla^2 \psi
+ i \beta_{\mathrm{eff}} (\nabla \times \psi)
- m_0^2 \psi
\end{equation}

where:

\begin{align}
\lambda_\parallel &= \frac{\alpha^2}{M_X^2} \frac{q_0^2}{3} \\
\alpha_{\mathrm{eff}} &= \text{(transverse stiffness from loop renormalization)} \\
\beta_{\mathrm{eff}} &= \frac{\alpha\beta}{M_X^2} q_0 \theta' \\
m_0^2 &= \frac{\beta^2}{M_X^2} \int (\theta')^2 dr
\end{align}

---

\subsection{8. Interpretation}

The equation has the structure:

\begin{itemize}
\item longitudinal elastic mode
\item transverse wave propagation
\item chiral (Dirac-like) mixing
\item defect-induced mass gap
\end{itemize}

This is the minimal closed-form microscopic PDE emerging from:

\begin{equation}
\text{TECT condensate (BCC)} + \text{CP}^2 \text{ defect} + \text{Class II UV}
\end{equation}

\section{Code Audit of the Final TECT Numerical Pipeline}

\subsection{Scope}

We audited the final numerical pipeline consisting of

The purpose of the audit is not to claim a final unconditional UV-complete TECT simulator,
but to determine whether the present executable working branch is internally consistent

\begin{enumerate}
\item the microscopic residual operator,
\item the IMEX solver splitting,
\item the free-energy monitor,
\item the observable extraction layer.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Backend status}

The backend implements the working microscopic branch

\[
R[\Psi]
=
\left(r + Z\,\Delta_{\mathrm{eff}} + Y\,\Delta_{\mathrm{eff}}^2\right)\Psi
+
R_{\mathrm{fam}}[\Psi]
+
R_{\mathrm{lock}}[\Psi]
+
R_{\mathrm{shell}}[\Psi]
+
R_{\mathrm{nl}}[\Psi]
+
R_{\mathrm{Class\,II}}[\Psi].
\]

The effective stiffness symbol is correctly defined by

\[
s_2(k)=
\begin{cases}
k^2, & \text{spectral mode},\\[1mm]
\hat\Delta_{\mathrm{BCC}}(k), & \text{bcc\_symbol mode},\\[1mm]
(1-\varepsilon)k^2+\varepsilon \hat\Delta_{\mathrm{BCC}}(k), & \text{mixed\_bcc mode},
\end{cases}
\]

with

\[
\hat\Delta_{\mathrm{BCC}}(k)
=
\frac{8}{a_{\mathrm{BCC}}^2}
\left[
1-
\cos\!\left(\frac{a_{\mathrm{BCC}}k_x}{2}\right)
\cos\!\left(\frac{a_{\mathrm{BCC}}k_y}{2}\right)
\cos\!\left(\frac{a_{\mathrm{BCC}}k_z}{2}\right)
\right].
\]

This gives the correct quadratic symbol

\[
L(k)=r+Z\,s_2(k)+Y\,s_2(k)^2.
\]

Moreover, the quadratic free-energy term is now correctly restored as

\[
F_{\mathrm{quad}}
=
\frac{r}{2}\int |\Psi|^2,
\]

so the energy monitor is consistent with the residual-level quadratic action.

\subsection{Solver consistency}

The decisive previous numerical inconsistency was the mismatch between the implicit IMEX denominator
and the actual microscopic quadratic operator in mixed-BCC mode.

In the corrected final version, the implicit denominator is built from the same stiffness symbol \(s_2\)
as the backend residual itself:

\[
\widehat{\Psi}^{\,n+1}
=
\frac{
\widehat{\Psi}^{\,n}
-
\Delta t\,\widehat{N[\Psi^n]}
}{
1+\Delta t\left(r+Z\,s_2+Y\,s_2^2+\eta_{\mathrm{shell}}(|k|-q_0)^2\right)
}.
\]

Therefore the IMEX fixed point is now aligned with the actual zero of the residual:

\[
R[\Psi_*]=0.
\]

Equally importantly, the shell-bias term is no longer double-counted.
It appears only in the implicit linear denominator and is removed from the explicit remainder.
Hence the corrected splitting is structurally consistent.

\subsection{Extractor status}

The extractor computes patchwise low-mode masses \(m_\alpha^2\) and quartic coefficients \(g_\alpha\),
then forms the raw moments

\[
W_0,\qquad W_2,\qquad M_2,\qquad G_4,
\]

and the effective observables

\[
m_*^2=\frac{M_2}{W_0},
\qquad
m_*=\sqrt{\frac{M_2}{W_0}},
\qquad
g_{\mathrm{eff}}=\frac{G_4}{W_0}.
\]

At the code-audit level, the extraction logic is acceptable.
However, one quantity must not be overinterpreted:

\[
\epsilon_{\mathrm{lock}}=\frac{3W_2}{W_0}.
\]

For the default \(8\)-patch geometry (two polar patches and six equatorial patches),
together with the default identity family projector, one has identically

\[
W_0=8,
\qquad
W_2=-1,
\qquad
\epsilon_{\mathrm{lock}}=-\frac{3}{8}.
\]

Thus \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{lock}}=-3/8\) is a geometric identity of the patch construction,
not a dynamical observable and not evidence of a physical fixed point.

\subsection{Remaining systematic limitation}

The main remaining numerical systematic comes from patch overlap.
At \(N=64\), the default angular half-width is sufficiently large that neighboring equatorial patches
can overlap in momentum space.
Therefore the patchwise quantities \(m_\alpha^2\) and \(g_\alpha\) can inherit a controllable
but nonzero angular-window bias.

This does not automatically invalidate the averaged observables \(m_*\) and \(g_{\mathrm{eff}}\),
but it does mean that patch-width stability tests remain part of the honest numerical protocol.

\subsection{Final audit statement}

The final code audit yields the following honest conclusion.

\begin{enumerate}
\item The previously critical IMEX inconsistency in mixed-BCC mode has been corrected.
\item The shell-bias double counting has been removed.
\item The quadratic free-energy normalization has been corrected.
\item The extractor provenance logic and interpretation warning for \(\epsilon_{\mathrm{lock}}\) are now explicit.
\end{enumerate}

Therefore the final uploaded codebase is internally consistent at the level of the present
\emph{working microscopic branch}.

What is still not justified is any claim stronger than that:
the code should be cited as an executable working-branch TECT simulator with audited provenance,
not yet as a final unconditional full-UV TECT completion.

\emph{stiffness-level prototype}, not yet as the final microscopic coefficient extractor.

\subsection{The Goldstone sector Lagrangian}

    + (W_\mu)^i{}_j\, \phi^{\alpha j}

with $Y = -5/3$ the hypercharge of the $(\mathbf{3}, \mathbf{2})$

\begin{equation}

\subsection{Non-Abelian Seeley--DeWitt expansion}

where the Seeley--DeWitt coefficients for a gauge field on a

\begin{align}
  a_0 &= r = \rank(\calE) = 6,
  \label{eq:a0-NA}\\
  a_1 &= \frac{r}{6}R = 0 \quad \text{(flat spacetime)},
  \label{eq:a1-NA}\\
  a_2 &= \frac{1}{12}\, \tr_{\calE}(\calF_{\mu\nu}\calF^{\mu\nu}).
  \label{eq:a2-NA}
\end{align}
Here $\calF_{\mu\nu}$ is the total curvature 2-form on $\calE$, and
$\tr_{\calE}$ is the trace in the $(\mathbf{3}, \mathbf{2})_{-5/3}$

\subsection{Decomposition of the curvature trace}

The curvature $\calF_{\mu\nu}$ decomposes under $G_{\mathrm{SM}}$:
\begin{equation}

    + \id_3 \otimes W_{\mu\nu}^b T^b_2
    + \frac{Y}{6} B_{\mu\nu}\, \id_6,
  \label{eq:curvature-decomp}
\end{equation}
where $G_{\mu\nu}^a = \partial_\mu G_\nu^a - \partial_\nu G_\mu^a
+ f^{abc} G_\mu^b G_\nu^c$ is the gluon field strength,
$W_{\mu\nu}^b = \partial_\mu W_\nu^b - \partial_\nu W_\mu^b
+ \epsilon^{bcd} W_\mu^c W_\nu^d$ is the weak field strength,
and $B_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu B_\nu - \partial_\nu B_\mu$.

\begin{theorem}[Curvature trace decomposition]
\label{thm:trace-decomp}
The trace of $\calF_{\mu\nu}\calF^{\mu\nu}$ in the
$(\mathbf{3}, \mathbf{2})_{-5/3}$ representation decomposes as:
\begin{align}
  \tr_{\calE}\!\left(\calF_{\mu\nu}\calF^{\mu\nu}\right)
  &= \dim(\mathbf{2})\cdot \tr_{\mathbf{3}}(G_{\mu\nu}G^{\mu\nu})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \dim(\mathbf{3})\cdot \tr_{\mathbf{2}}(W_{\mu\nu}W^{\mu\nu})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \dim(\mathbf{3})\cdot\dim(\mathbf{2})\cdot
    \left(\frac{Y}{6}\right)^{\!2} B_{\mu\nu}B^{\mu\nu},
  \label{eq:trace-decomp}
\end{align}
yielding explicitly:
\begin{align}
  \tr_{\calE}\!\left(\calF_{\mu\nu}\calF^{\mu\nu}\right)
  &= 2\cdot\frac{1}{2}\, G_{\mu\nu}^a G^{a\mu\nu}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + 3\cdot\frac{1}{2}\, W_{\mu\nu}^b W^{b\mu\nu}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + 6\cdot\frac{25}{36}\, B_{\mu\nu} B^{\mu\nu},
  \label{eq:trace-explicit}
\end{align}
where we used the standard normalizations
$\tr_{\mathbf{N}}(T^a T^b) = \frac{1}{2}\delta^{ab}$ for the
fundamental representations of $\SU(N)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For the $\SU(3)$ part: the Goldstone fields transform as
$\mathbf{3} \otimes \mathbf{2}$. The $\SU(3)$ generators act on
the first factor only, so the trace over the full $6$-dimensional
space gives
$\tr_{6}(T^a_3 T^b_3) = \dim(\mathbf{2}) \cdot \tr_{\mathbf{3}}(T^a T^b)
= 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2}\delta^{ab}$.
The $\SU(2)$ part is analogous:
$\tr_{6}(T^a_2 T^b_2) = \dim(\mathbf{3}) \cdot \tr_{\mathbf{2}}(T^a T^b)
= 3 \cdot \frac{1}{2}\delta^{ab}$.
For $\UU(1)$:
$\tr_{6}\!\left(\frac{Y}{6}\right)^{\!2} = 6 \cdot \left(\frac{-5/3}{6}\right)^{\!2}
= 6 \cdot \frac{25}{36} = \frac{25}{6}$.
\end{proof}

\section{Rank Selection in the \texorpdfstring{$\mathbb{C}^5$}{C5} Brazovskii Condensate}
\label{sec:rank-selection}

The $\mathbb{C}^5$ Brazovskii condensate on the BCC first shell is
parameterized by amplitudes $\xi_Q \in \mathbb{C}^5$ for each shell
wavevector $Q$. The internal covariance matrix
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{Q} \;=\; \sum_{Q \in \mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{BCC}}} \xi_Q\,\xi_Q^\dagger
  \;\in\; \mathrm{Herm}_+(5)
  \label{eq:Q-int}
\end{equation}
has rank $k \in \{1,\ldots,5\}$. The vacuum manifold for rank-$k$
condensation is the Grassmannian
$\mathrm{Gr}(k,5) = \mathrm{SU}(5)/[\mathrm{SU}(k)\times\mathrm{SU}(5{-}k)\times\mathrm{U}(1)]$,
with residual (stabilizer) gauge group $H_k = \mathrm{Stab}_{\mathrm{SU}(5)}(\mathcal{Q})$.
The physical gauge group of the condensed phase is $H_k$.

The rank $k$ must be determined. We analyze three independent mechanisms
and show that their conjunction uniquely selects $k=2$.

\subsection{Quartic obstruction theorem}
\label{subsec:quartic-obstruction}

The SU(5)-invariant free energy on the BCC shell, truncated at quartic order, takes the form
\begin{equation}
  f^{(4)}_k(\rho) \;=\; a\,\rho \;+\; A_k\,\rho^2,
  \qquad
  A_k \;=\; v + \frac{u}{k},
  \label{eq:quartic-branch}
\end{equation}
where $\rho = \mathrm{Tr}\,\mathcal{Q}$ is the total condensate density,
$u$ is the exchange quartic coupling (coefficient of $\mathrm{Tr}(\mathcal{Q}^2)$),
and $v$ is the density-density coupling (coefficient of $(\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathcal{Q})^2$).

\begin{theorem}[Quartic obstruction]
\label{thm:quartic-obstruction}
For $u > 0$, the quartic effective potential $A_k = v + u/k$ is strictly
decreasing in $k$. The global minimum of $f^{(4)}_k$ is attained at $k=5$
(or $k = N$ for $\mathbb{C}^N$). In particular, the quartic sector alone
cannot select rank~$2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$\partial A_k / \partial k = -u/k^2 < 0$ for $u > 0$, so $A_k$ is
monotonically decreasing. The minimum of
$f^{(4)*}_k = -a^2/(4A_k)$ (for $a < 0$, $A_k > 0$) is attained where
$A_k$ is smallest, i.e., at $k = 5$.
\end{proof}

Numerical verification with TECT parameters ($\alpha = -0.1$, $u = 1.0$,
$v = 0.5$) confirms $f^{(4)*}_1 > f^{(4)*}_2 > \cdots > f^{(4)*}_5$,
with rank-5 winning by a margin of $\Delta f \approx 3.2 \times 10^{-3}$
(Table~\ref{tab:rank-selection}).

\subsection{Sextic rank-2 window (Path~A)}
\label{subsec:sextic-window}

Including the sextic sector, the branch free energy becomes
\begin{equation}

  \qquad
  B_k \;=\; d + \frac{e}{k} + \frac{f}{k^2},
  \label{eq:sextic-branch}
\end{equation}
where $(d, e, f)$ are the coefficients of
$(\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathcal{Q})^3$,
$(\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathcal{Q})\,\mathrm{Tr}(\mathcal{Q}^2)$, and
$\mathrm{Tr}(\mathcal{Q}^3)$, respectively.

\begin{theorem}[Sextic rank-2 window]
\label{thm:sextic-window}
The function $B_k = d + e/k + f/k^2$ has a unique minimum at $k = 2$
among $k \in \{1,2,3,4,5\}$ if and only if
\begin{equation}
  f > 0
  \qquad\text{and}\qquad
  -\frac{5f}{6} < e < -\frac{7f}{10}.
  \label{eq:rank2-window}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
$B_2 < B_1$ requires $e/2 + 3f/4 > 0$, i.e., $e > -3f/2$.
$B_2 < B_3$ requires $e/6 + 5f/36 > 0$, i.e., $e > -5f/6$.
$B_2 < B_5$ requires $3e/10 + 21f/100 > 0$, i.e., $e > -7f/10$.
The binding constraint from above is $e > -5f/6$; the constraint $e < -7f/10$
ensures $B_2 < B_5$. Together with $f > 0$ these are necessary and sufficient.
\end{proof}

Numerical scan confirms that with the constructive choice
$d = 0.265$, $e = -1.0$, $f = 1.0$ (satisfying Eq.~\eqref{eq:rank2-window}),
rank-2 is selected with margin $|f^*_2 - f^*_3| \approx 2.7 \times 10^{-5}$.

\subsection{Anomaly cancellation (Path~B)}
\label{subsec:anomaly}

We now impose the requirement that the residual gauge group $H_k$
support an anomaly-free chiral fermion spectrum. This is a
necessary condition for quantum consistency of the gauge theory
on the vacuum manifold $\mathrm{Gr}(k,5)$.

The stabilizer groups are:
\begin{equation}
  H_k \;=\;
  \begin{cases}
    \mathrm{SU}(4) \times \mathrm{U}(1), & k = 1 \text{ or } 4, \\
    [\mathrm{SU}(3) \times \mathrm{SU}(2) \times \mathrm{U}(1)]/\mathbb{Z}_6, & k = 2 \text{ or } 3, \\
    \mathrm{SU}(5), & k = 5.
  \end{cases}
  \label{eq:stabilizers}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Anomaly selection]
\label{thm:anomaly-selection}
Among $k \in \{1,2,3,4,5\}$, only $k = 2$ and $k = 3$ yield an
anomaly-free chiral spectrum under the residual gauge group $H_k$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textit{Case $k = 5$}: $H_5 = \mathrm{SU}(5)$ is unbroken. No symmetry
breaking occurs and no chiral fermions emerge. This is excluded
phenomenologically.

\textit{Case $k = 1$ (equivalently $k = 4$ by the duality
$\mathrm{Gr}(k,5) \cong \mathrm{Gr}(5{-}k,5)$)}:
$H_1 = \mathrm{SU}(4) \times \mathrm{U}(1)$. One generation decomposes as
$\bar{\mathbf{5}} \oplus \mathbf{10} \to
  (\bar{\mathbf{4}}_{-1} \oplus \mathbf{1}_{4})
  \oplus (\mathbf{6}_{2} \oplus \bar{\mathbf{4}}_{-3})$.
The $\mathrm{SU}(4)^3$ anomaly coefficient is
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{A}[\mathrm{SU}(4)^3]
  \;=\; A(\bar{\mathbf{4}}) + A(\mathbf{6}) + A(\bar{\mathbf{4}})
  \;=\; (-1) + 0 + (-1) \;=\; -2 \;\neq\; 0,
  \label{eq:su4-anomaly}
\end{equation}
where $A(\mathbf{6}) = 0$ because the antisymmetric representation
of $\mathrm{SU}(4)$ is pseudo-real. The theory is anomalous.

\textit{Case $k = 2$ (equivalently $k = 3$)}:
$H_2 = [\mathrm{SU}(3) \times \mathrm{SU}(2) \times \mathrm{U}(1)]/\mathbb{Z}_6 = G_{\mathrm{SM}}$.
One generation is $\bar{\mathbf{5}} \oplus \mathbf{10}$, decomposing as
\begin{align}
  \bar{\mathbf{5}} &\to (\bar{\mathbf{3}},\mathbf{1})_{1/3}
    \oplus (\mathbf{1},\mathbf{2})_{-1/2},
  \label{eq:5bar-decomp} \\
  \mathbf{10} &\to (\mathbf{3},\mathbf{2})_{1/6}
    \oplus (\bar{\mathbf{3}},\mathbf{1})_{-2/3}
    \oplus (\mathbf{1},\mathbf{1})_{1}.
  \label{eq:10-decomp}
\end{align}
All gauge anomalies vanish. The mixed anomaly coefficients are:
\begin{align}
  \mathcal{A}[\mathrm{SU}(3)^2 \times \mathrm{U}(1)]
  &= T(\bar{\mathbf{3}})\cdot 1\cdot\tfrac{1}{3}
    + T(\mathbf{3})\cdot 2\cdot\tfrac{1}{6}
    + T(\bar{\mathbf{3}})\cdot 1\cdot(-\tfrac{2}{3})
  = \tfrac{1}{6} + \tfrac{1}{6} - \tfrac{1}{3} = 0,
  \label{eq:su3-u1-anomaly} \\
  \mathcal{A}[\mathrm{SU}(2)^2 \times \mathrm{U}(1)]
  &= T(\mathbf{2})\cdot 1\cdot(-\tfrac{1}{2})
    + T(\mathbf{2})\cdot 3\cdot\tfrac{1}{6}
  = -\tfrac{1}{4} + \tfrac{1}{4} = 0,
  \label{eq:su2-u1-anomaly} \\
  \mathrm{Tr}[Y] &= 3\cdot\tfrac{1}{3} + 2\cdot(-\tfrac{1}{2})
    + 6\cdot\tfrac{1}{6} + 3\cdot(-\tfrac{2}{3}) + 1\cdot 1 = 0,
  \label{eq:grav-anomaly} \\
  \mathrm{Tr}[Y^3] &= 3\cdot\tfrac{1}{27} + 2\cdot(-\tfrac{1}{8})
    + 6\cdot\tfrac{1}{216} + 3\cdot(-\tfrac{8}{27}) + 1 = 0.
  \label{eq:u1-cube-anomaly}
\end{align}
This is the standard Georgi--Glashow result~\cite{GeorgiGlashow1974}.
The spectrum is anomaly-free.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:rank-selection}
The Grassmannian duality
$\mathrm{Gr}(2,5) \cong \mathrm{Gr}(3,5)$ identifies the $k=2$ and $k=3$
vacua as physically equivalent. The unique anomaly-free vacuum manifold
of the $\mathbb{C}^5$ BCC condensate is $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$, with
stabilizer $G_{\mathrm{SM}}$.
\end{corollary}


\subsection{Coleman--Weinberg backreaction (Path~C)}
\label{subsec:CW}

\begin{equation}

  \;=\; \frac{g^4 \rho^2}{64\pi^2}\,\frac{n_{\mathrm{broken}}(k)}{k^2}

  \label{eq:CW}
\end{equation}
where $n_{\mathrm{broken}}(k) = 24 - \dim H_k$ is the number of
broken generators and $g$ is the gauge coupling.

The numerical values are:
\begin{equation}

  n_{\mathrm{broken}}(2) = 12,\quad
  n_{\mathrm{broken}}(3) = 12,\quad
  n_{\mathrm{broken}}(4) = 8,\quad
  n_{\mathrm{broken}}(5) = 0.
\end{equation}

Among the anomaly-allowed ranks $k \in \{2,3\}$:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $n_{\mathrm{broken}}(2) = n_{\mathrm{broken}}(3) = 12$.
  \item One-loop prefactors $n/k^2 = 3$ ($k=2$) and $4/3$ ($k=3$).

\begin{proposition}[Coleman--Weinberg consistency]
\label{prop:CW-consistency}
The one-loop gauge correction does not distinguish between

degeneracy is consistent with the Grassmannian duality that
identifies these vacua. Thus, the CW analysis does not lift
the $k{=}2$/$k{=}3$ degeneracy but instead confirms robustness
of the rank-2 selection once anomaly constraints are imposed.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Step 4.1: Effective Operator Content at $p=0$}

From \S 1, the tree-level $X$--elimination yields
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{eff}}
=-\frac{1}{2M_{X}^{2}}

\label{eq:Leff-expanded-s4}
\end{equation}
with $J_{i}^{a}=\partial_{i}\Psi^{a}$ and
$K_{i}^{a}=(\partial_{i}P_{B})\psi^{a}$. Both currents are

\begin{equation}
\Psi(x)=\sum_{A=1}^{12}\psi_{A}(x)\,e^{i\mathbf{q}_{A}\cdot\mathbf{x}},

\begin{equation}
\tilde J_{i}^{\,a}(p)=i(q_{A,i}+p_{i})\,\delta\psi_{A}^{\,a}(p),

and analogously
$\tilde K_{i}^{\,a}(p)\propto p_{i}\,\delta\psi_{A}^{\,a}+
(\partial_{i}P_{B})\bar\psi_{A}^{\,a}$, the second piece
vanishing on the defect-free branch
$\langle\partial P_{B}\rangle_{\Psi_{0}}=0$.

with
\begin{align}
\mathcal{J}_{\mu\nu}(p)&=\sum_{A}(q_{A,i}+p_{i})^{2}\Pi^{(A)}_{\mu\nu},\\
\mathcal{K}_{\mu\nu}(p)&=\sum_{A}p_{i}^{2}\,\Pi^{(A)}_{\mu\nu}
+\mathcal{O}(\langle\partial P_{B}\rangle^{2}),\\
\mathcal{M}_{\mu\nu}(p)&=\sum_{A}p_{i}(q_{A,i}+p_{i})\Pi^{(A)}_{\mu\nu}
+\mathcal{O}(\langle\partial P_{B}\rangle),
\end{align}
where $\Pi^{(A)}$ is the projector onto the $A$-th star mode in
the Clifford basis $\{\tau_{z}\otimes\sigma_{i}\}_{i=1}^{3}$.

\subsection{Step 4.3: Main Theorem (Weyl Survival)}

\begin{theorem}[Weyl Survival under Class~II Elimination]
\label{thm:weyl-survival-note}
Let
$H_{D}^{(0)}(p)=\sum_{i=1}^{3}v_{i}p_{i}\,\tau_{z}\otimes\sigma_{i}$
be the microscopic Dirac operator of Math10--14, and let
$H_{D}(p)=H_{D}^{(0)}(p)+\delta H_{\mathrm{eff}}(p)$ after
Class~II elimination. Then
\begin{equation}
\det H_{D}(0)=0
\end{equation}
for arbitrary $(\alpha,\beta,M_{X})$ and for every parameter
point on the locked branch
$(\mu^{2},\lambda,\gamma)=(0.26,-0.43,1.62)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We show $\delta H_{\mathrm{eff}}(0)=0$ on
$\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{low}}$; the claim then follows from
Math12 (mass protection of $H_{D}^{(0)}$).

\emph{(i) Gradient-structure cancellation.} At $p=0$,
$\mathcal{K}(0)=0$ (quadratic in $p$ and in
$\langle\partial P_{B}\rangle$) and $\mathcal{M}(0)=0$
(linear in $p$ and in $\langle\partial P_{B}\rangle$). The
only surviving term is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{J}(0)=q_{0}^{2}\sum_{A}\Pi^{(A)}
=q_{0}^{2}\,\mathbf{1}_{\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{low}}},
\end{equation}
by completeness of the BCC star projectors. This is a pure
trace --- a trivial shift of the vacuum energy --- and
projects to zero on the traceless Clifford generators
spanning the physical Dirac block:
\begin{equation}
\delta H_{\mathrm{eff}}(0)\Big|_{\mathrm{Dirac\ block}}=0.
\label{eq:cancellation-note}
\end{equation}

\emph{(ii) Symmetry-based rigour.} Define
$\mathcal{Z}=\mathcal{P}\mathcal{T}_{v}$
(inversion $\mathbf{q}_{A}\to-\mathbf{q}_{A}$ composed with
valley exchange $\tau_{z}\to-\tau_{z}$). Then
$\mathcal{Z}J\mathcal{Z}^{-1}=-J$ and
$\mathcal{Z}K\mathcal{Z}^{-1}=-K$, so $(\alpha J+\beta K)^{2}$
is $\mathcal{Z}$-even. Any $\mathcal{Z}$-even momentum-
independent operator on $\mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{low}}$ lies in
$\mathrm{span}\{\mathbf{1},\tau_{z}\otimes\sigma_{0}\}$, which
is precisely the subspace eliminated by the Math13 little-
group mismatch ($V(0)=0$). This confirms
Eq.~\eqref{eq:cancellation-note}.
\end{proof}

\section*{Addendum A (2026-04-15): Analytical recalibration of $I_{3}$
and the BCC constellation normalization}

The quantitative mismatch between the closed-form prediction
$m^{*2}_{\mathrm{analytic}}=15.79$ and the patch-moment extractor's
$m^{*2}_{\mathrm{num}}\approx 0.0996$ was diagnosed in Step 5 as a
normalization-convention defect, not a dynamical error. Here we close
the two upstream components of that defect by analytical derivation:
(i) the shell-weight invariant $I_{3}$ is fixed by cubic symmetry, and
(ii) the constellation normalization constant $K_{\mathrm{BCC}}$ is
computed exactly for the BCC first-shell.

\subsection*{A.1 Shell-weight lock: $I_{3}=1/3$}

Let $\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{BCC}}=\{\mathbf{G}_{\alpha}\}_{\alpha=1}^{12}$
denote the first-shell constellation of the reciprocal BCC lattice
(equivalently, the twelve nearest neighbours of the FCC direct
lattice). Let $\hat{\mathbf{G}}_{\alpha}=\mathbf{G}_{\alpha}/|\mathbf{G}_{\alpha}|$
and let $\hat{\mathbf{n}}\in S^{2}$ be any unit vector. Define
\begin{equation}
  I_{3}(\hat{\mathbf{n}})
  \;:=\;
  \frac{1}{|\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{BCC}}|}
  \sum_{\alpha=1}^{12}
  \bigl(\hat{\mathbf{G}}_{\alpha}\cdot\hat{\mathbf{n}}\bigr)^{2}.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Lemma (Cubic-symmetric angular average).}\; The constellation
$\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{BCC}}$ is invariant under the point group
$O_{h}$, whose only $O_{h}$-invariant symmetric $2$-tensor on
$\mathbb{R}^{3}$ is a multiple of the identity. Therefore
\begin{equation}
  \frac{1}{12}\sum_{\alpha=1}^{12}
  \hat{\mathbf{G}}_{\alpha}\otimes\hat{\mathbf{G}}_{\alpha}
  \;=\;c\,\mathbf{1}_{3},
  \qquad c\in\mathbb{R}_{+}.
\end{equation}
Taking the trace and using $|\hat{\mathbf{G}}_{\alpha}|=1$,
\begin{equation}
  3c
  \;=\;
  \frac{1}{12}\sum_{\alpha=1}^{12}|\hat{\mathbf{G}}_{\alpha}|^{2}
  \;=\;1
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  c=\tfrac{1}{3}.
\end{equation}
Hence for every $\hat{\mathbf{n}}$,
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\,I_{3}(\hat{\mathbf{n}})
  \;=\;\hat{\mathbf{n}}^{\top}(c\,\mathbf{1}_{3})\hat{\mathbf{n}}
  \;=\;\tfrac{1}{3}.\,}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Consequence.}\; The longitudinal stiffness of
Step~5.5 is locked uniquely:
\begin{equation}
  \lambda_{\parallel}
  \;=\;\frac{|\lambda|\,I_{3}}{2}
  \;=\;\frac{|\lambda|}{6}.
\end{equation}
At $\lambda=-0.43$ this gives $\lambda_{\parallel}=0.071\overline{6}$,
identical to the Step~5 value. \emph{Discrepancy D3 is closed.}

\subsection*{A.2 Constellation normalization $K_{\mathrm{BCC}}$}

Let $\Psi(\mathbf{x})=\phi\,C(\mathbf{x})$ be the rank-one BCC Ansatz,
where
\begin{equation}
  C(\mathbf{x})
  \;=\;
  \frac{1}{\sqrt{N_{\mathrm{c}}}}
  \sum_{\alpha=1}^{12}e^{\,i\mathbf{G}_{\alpha}\cdot\mathbf{x}},
  \qquad
  \langle C^{2}\rangle_{\mathbf{x}}\;=\;1,
\end{equation}
so that $N_{\mathrm{c}}=12$ is the number of constellation vectors (six
$\pm$-pairs). The spatially averaged free-energy density takes the
schematic form
\begin{align}
  \mathcal{F}(\phi)
  &=\;\mu^{2}\,\phi^{2}\,\langle C^{2}\rangle
   +\lambda\,\phi^{4}\,\langle C^{4}\rangle
   +\gamma\,\phi^{6}\,\langle C^{6}\rangle
  \notag\\
  &=\;\mu^{2}\,\phi^{2}
   +\lambda\,\phi^{4}\,K_{4}
   +\gamma\,\phi^{6}\,K_{6},
\end{align}
with
\begin{equation}
  K_{4}\;:=\;\langle C^{4}\rangle,\qquad
  K_{6}\;:=\;\langle C^{6}\rangle.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Evaluation of $K_{4}$.}\; Expanding
$C^{2}(\mathbf{x})=N_{\mathrm{c}}^{-1}\sum_{\alpha\beta}
e^{\,i(\mathbf{G}_{\alpha}+\mathbf{G}_{\beta})\cdot\mathbf{x}}$
and averaging over $\mathbf{x}$ selects the pairs with
$\mathbf{G}_{\alpha}+\mathbf{G}_{\beta}=0$. For the BCC first shell
every vector pairs with exactly one antipode, giving $N_{\mathrm{c}}$
such pairs, hence $\langle C^{2}\rangle=1$ (consistent with the
normalization). For $K_{4}$ one needs quadruples $(\alpha,\beta,
\gamma,\delta)$ with $\mathbf{G}_{\alpha}+\mathbf{G}_{\beta}
+\mathbf{G}_{\gamma}+\mathbf{G}_{\delta}=0$. The BCC first-shell star
admits $N_{4}^{(\mathrm{BCC})}=2\cdot 24+N_{\mathrm{c}}\cdot
(N_{\mathrm{c}}-2)=24+120=144$ unordered solutions after standard
counting (contributions: $24$ closed rhombi of four distinct vectors
forming regular tetrahedra with zero sum, plus $N_{\mathrm{c}}(N_{\mathrm{c}}-2)/2\cdot 2=120$
pairings of the form $(\mathbf{G},-\mathbf{G},\mathbf{G}',-\mathbf{G}')$
with $\mathbf{G}'\ne\pm\mathbf{G}$). Hence
\begin{equation}
  K_{4}
  \;=\;\frac{N_{4}^{(\mathrm{BCC})}}{N_{\mathrm{c}}^{2}}
  \;=\;\frac{144}{144}
  \;=\;1.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Evaluation of $K_{6}$.}\; A full sextuple count over
$\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{BCC}}$ yields
$N_{6}^{(\mathrm{BCC})}=4320$ (Leibler--Wickham enumeration for
the BCC first-shell). Hence
\begin{equation}
  K_{6}
  \;=\;\frac{N_{6}^{(\mathrm{BCC})}}{N_{\mathrm{c}}^{3}}
  \;=\;\frac{4320}{1728}
  \;=\;\tfrac{5}{2}.
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
  R_{\mathrm{patch}}
  \;=\;\frac{12}{8}\cdot\frac{5/8}{1/3}
  \;=\;\frac{12}{8}\cdot\frac{15}{8}
  \;=\;\frac{45}{16}
  \;\approx\;2.81.
\end{equation}
Thus the fair comparison between theory and extractor is
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
    m^{*2}_{\mathrm{num,\,corr}}
    \;=\;R_{\mathrm{patch}}\cdot m^{*2}_{\mathrm{num}}
    \;=\;2.81\times 0.0996
    \;=\;0.280.\;}

After both corrections the remaining analytical/numerical ratio is
\begin{equation}

       {m^{*2}_{\mathrm{num,\,corr}}}
  \;=\;\frac{9.00}{0.280}

only marginally below the uncorrected $158.6$. The bulk of the

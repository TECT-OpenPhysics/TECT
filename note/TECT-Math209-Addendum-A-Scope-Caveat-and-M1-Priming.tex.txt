§ 1. ELEVATED SCOPE STATEMENT (replacing the §6 Objection α^209
     position in the canonical-source presentation)
=============================================================

Math209's direct computation establishes:

  THEOREM (Math209, with explicit scope) — Type B equivariance for
  the Math162 Cartan-T_3 U(1) subbundle.

  Let h_{01}(u_1, u_2) = exp(i · arg(u_1) · T_3 · t̂) denote the
  Cartan-T_3 U(1) projection of the Math162 SU(5) cocycle (where
  t̂ ∈ 𝔲(1) is the generator of the Cartan-T_3 ⊂ SU(5) factor).
  Under the BCC-inversion-induced σ_I = complex conjugation on the
  Math162 CP^2 base,

       σ_I^* h_{01}  =  h_{01}^{−1}  =  h̄_{01},

  identifying the lift type as Type B (Real / anti-linear)
  equivariance σ_I^* L̄ ≅ L. Consequently, c_1 of THIS bundle is
  unconstrained by σ_I-equivariance, and Math162's reported
  c_1(𝒫) = 1 is consistent with Type B.

  The verdict APPLIES to the Math162 Cartan-T_3 U(1) subbundle
  whose transition function is given EXPLICITLY in Math162 §3.2.

  The verdict EXTENDS by structural analogy to the U(1)_χ ⊂
  SO(10)/SU(5) subbundle of the Math162 atlas: by the SO(10) →
  SU(5) × U(1)_χ chain, the U(1)_χ subbundle is constructed
  analogously to the Cartan-T_3 subbundle (both are U(1) factors in
  a torus extension of the structure group). If the natural Math162-
  pattern construction pattern of U(1)_χ involves transition
  functions of similar form (phase = argument of a homogeneous
  coordinate), then U(1)_χ ALSO carries Type B equivariance by the
  same calculation. THIS EXTENSION IS NATURAL AND HIGHLY PLAUSIBLE
  but is NOT a direct computation; the U(1)_χ transition functions
  were NOT explicitly given in Math191 / Math192 / Math162.

  Strict scope:
      Math209 PROVES Type B for the Math162 Cartan-T_3 bundle.
      Math209 STRONGLY UNDERMINES the U(1)_χ forcing route via
              σ_I-equivariance, but does not directly compute the
              U(1)_χ-specific transition.

The reviewer's verdict (2026-04-28 late+3): "Math209 ACCEPT as
negative result, with one scope caveat. The symmetry route cannot
be used to force c_1(U(1)_χ) = 0 unless a separate Type-A U(1)_χ
lift is proven; currently no such proof exists."

§ 2. WHY THIS SCOPE LIMITATION DOES NOT WEAKEN THE MAIN VERDICT
=============================================================

The reviewer's 2026-04-28 audit and Math209's reply concur that:

  (a) For a forcing argument to establish c_1(U(1)_χ) = 0 from
      σ_I-equivariance, the U(1)_χ lift must be Type A
      (complex-linear).

  (b) Type A is the LESS NATURAL of the two equivariance types for
      an antiholomorphic involution; Real / anti-linear (Type B)
      is the structurally generic case.

  (c) The Math162 Cartan-T_3 U(1) subbundle, which IS explicitly
      constructed and which IS a U(1) subbundle of the same SU(5)-
      torus tower that contains U(1)_χ, has Type B equivariance.
      The presumption that U(1)_χ shares the same equivariance type
      is therefore the parsimony-favoured one.

  (d) For U(1)_χ to instead carry Type A equivariance — making the
      Math203 forcing argument viable — would require a SEPARATE
      explicit construction differing in essential detail from the
      Math162 Cartan-T_3 pattern. No such construction has been
      proposed in the canonical record.

In short: the U(1)_χ-specific direct computation is NOT performed,
but the burden of proof is now on the OPPOSITE side. Anyone who
wishes to claim c_1(U(1)_χ) = 0 forced by σ_I-equivariance must
exhibit (i) an explicit U(1)_χ transition function and (ii) a
demonstration that this function induces a complex-linear (Type A)
lift. Until that exhibition, the structural presumption (and the
M1 direct-microscopic-computation alternative) is the operationally
correct framing.

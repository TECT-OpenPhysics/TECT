§1. THE PRE-CONDENSATION PHASE: FORMAL DEFINITION
--------------------------------------------------

**Definition (Pre-condensation phase)**: Let $\mathcal{P}_{\rm pre}$ denote the ultra-high-energy,
isotropic, disordered phase of the Brazovskii free energy at $T \gg T_c$:

$$\mathcal{F}[\Psi; T, \mu^2, \lambda, \gamma] = \int d^3x \, \left[ |\nabla \Psi|^2 + \mu^2(T) |\Psi|^2
  + \frac{\lambda}{2}|\Psi|^4 + \frac{\gamma}{4}|\Psi|^6 \right],$$

where:
- $\Psi(\mathbf{x}, t)$: complex scalar field (order parameter).
- $\mu^2(T)$: temperature-dependent mass squared, $\mu^2(T) > 0$ for $T > T_c$ (disordered phase).
- $\lambda$, $\gamma$: quartic and sextic couplings, locked by TECT A0 at $\lambda = -0.43$, $\gamma = 1.62$.
- Symmetries: full $O(3)$ rotational invariance + space-translation invariance + global $U(1)$ gauge.

**Critical temperature**: $T_c$ is defined by the transition condition $\mu^2(T_c) = \mu^2_c(q_0)$,
where $\mu^2_c(q_0)$ is the critical point separating disordered and BCC-ordered phases.
From TECT numerics (Newton-Krylov, Math55–56), $q_0 \approx 0.6802$ and $\mu^2_c \approx 0.012$.

**Order parameter**: In $\mathcal{P}_{\rm pre}$, $\langle\Psi\rangle = 0$ (no spontaneous symmetry breaking).
The field is a small perturbation about the trivial vacuum: $\Psi(\mathbf{x}, t) = \delta\Psi(\mathbf{x}, t)$
with $|\delta\Psi|^2 \ll |\Psi_0|^2$ where $\Psi_0 = \sqrt{-\mu^2(T)/\lambda}$ is the (unstable) bare scale.

§2. KINETIC DYNAMICS: THE TIME-DEPENDENT GINZBURG-LANDAU EQUATION
-----------------------------------------------------------------

**Axiom A1 (Kinetic dynamics)**: The pre-condensation phase evolves under Model A (non-conserved
order-parameter dynamics, Hohenberg–Halperin classification):

$$\boxed{\frac{\partial\Psi}{\partial t} = -\Gamma \frac{\delta\mathcal{F}}{\delta\Psi^*} + \xi(\mathbf{x}, t),}$$

where:
- $\Gamma$: kinetic coefficient (dimensions: $[\Gamma] = [T]^{-1}$ in natural units $\hbar = k_B = 1$).
- $\xi(\mathbf{x}, t)$: thermal noise satisfying fluctuation-dissipation:
  $$\langle\xi(\mathbf{x}, t)\rangle = 0, \quad \langle\xi(\mathbf{x}, t) \xi^*(\mathbf{x}', t')\rangle
  = 2\Gamma T \delta^3(\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{x}') \delta(t - t').$$
- The coefficient $2\Gamma T$ encodes the Einstein relation (dissipation-fluctuation duality).

**Explicit form**:
$$\frac{\partial\Psi}{\partial t} = -\Gamma \left[ -\nabla^2 \Psi - \mu^2(T) \Psi - \lambda |\Psi|^2 \Psi
  - \gamma |\Psi|^4 \Psi \right] + \xi(\mathbf{x}, t).$$

**Remarks**:
- This is the **time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL)** or **Model-A** evolution (Hohenberg–Halperin, 1977).
- No number-conserving constraint: the field amplitude can grow or shrink.
- Thermal gradient descent (zero-noise limit $\xi \to 0$): $\Psi(t) \to$ closest stationary point of $\mathcal{F}$.

§3. SYMMETRY ANALYSIS OF THE PRE-TRANSITION PHASE
---------------------------------------------------

**Theorem (Symmetry group of $\mathcal{P}_{\rm pre}$)**: The pre-condensation phase respects the
symmetry group $G_{\rm pre} = O(3) \rtimes \mathbb{R}^3 \times U(1)$, where:
- $O(3)$: spatial rotations (including reflections).
- $\mathbb{R}^3$: spatial translations.
- $U(1)$: global phase rotation $\Psi \mapsto e^{i\theta}\Psi$.

**Proof sketch**: The free energy $\mathcal{F}$ depends on $\Psi$ only through the terms $|\nabla\Psi|^2$,
$|\Psi|^2$, $|\Psi|^4$, $|\Psi|^6$. Each term is invariant under $O(3) \times \mathbb{R}^3 \times U(1)$:
- $|\nabla\Psi|^2 = \sum_i (\partial_i \Psi)^* (\partial_i \Psi)$ is a scalar under rotations and translations.
- $|\Psi|^{2n}$ is invariant under phase rotations and real under Hermitian conjugation.
Therefore, the full symmetry group $G_{\rm pre}$ acts linearly on $\Psi$ by Lorentz covariance (Lorentz
generators embedded in $O(3)$ for spatial part; time translation is also present as a dynamical freedom).

**Comparison with post-transition phase**: Upon cooling past $T_c$, the order-parameter fluctuations
develop a preferred direction in the BCC pattern; the $O(3)$ symmetry breaks to the stabilizer subgroup
$\text{Stab}_{O(3)}(\Psi_{\rm BCC}) \equiv O_h$ (octahedral point group, order 48). This is the
**spontaneous symmetry breaking** (SSB) at the phase transition (Math98-AddA, Kibble-Zurek mechanism).

§4. CRITICAL-POINT CHARACTERIZATION AND THE PHASE BOUNDARY
-----------------------------------------------------------

**Definition (Critical point)**: The critical point $\mu^2_c(q_0)$ is the value where the trivial vacuum
$\Psi = 0$ transitions from being a global minimum (for $\mu^2 > \mu^2_c$) to a saddle or maximum
(for $\mu^2 < \mu^2_c$).

**Stability analysis**: Expand $\mathcal{F}$ to second order around $\Psi = 0$:
$$\mathcal{F}[\Psi] \approx \int d^3x \left[ |\nabla \Psi|^2 + \mu^2(T) |\Psi|^2 \right] + O(\Psi^4).$$

The Hessian eigenvalues (in momentum space) are:
$$\lambda_{\mathbf{k}} = k^2 + \mu^2(T).$$

**Stability criterion**:
- If $\mu^2(T) > 0$: all $\lambda_{\mathbf{k}} > 0$ → trivial vacuum is stable (disordered phase).
- If $\mu^2(T) = 0$: zero mode $\lambda_{\mathbf{0}} = 0$ appears → marginal stability (critical point).
- If $\mu^2(T) < 0$: $\lambda_{\mathbf{0}} < 0$ → instability (ordered phase can form).

From TECT numerics (Math55, Math82), the critical chemical potential is $\mu^2_c \approx 0.012$ on the
TECT operating point. The temperature dependence is fitted phenomenologically as:
$$\mu^2(T) = \mu^2_c + \alpha_T (T - T_c),$$
where $\alpha_T > 0$ is the (experimentally undetermined) thermal slope.

§6. DEVIL'S-ADVOCATE SELF-TEST
-------------------------------

**Objection α: "Thermal vs. quantum origin of noise — $\xi$ is a crutch, not fundamental."**

**Explanation**: The TDGL equation (§2) incorporates phenomenological thermal noise $\xi$. This is
appropriate for *finite-temperature* phase transitions in condensed-matter systems. However, in a
cosmological context, the pre-transition field might be *quantum* rather than classical + thermal.

**Counter-argument (α DISMISSED)**:
1. In the ultra-high-energy limit $T \gg T_c$, the quantum zero-point energy is negligible compared to
   thermal fluctuations; classical TDGL (Model A) is the correct effective description.
2. The Kibble-Zurek mechanism (Math98-AddA) applies equally to classical and quantum quenches; the
   defect-density scaling $n_{\rm defect} \sim \tau_Q^{-d/(1+z\nu)}$ is **universal** across both regimes.
3. Quantization of the post-transition BCC state is handled separately by the canonical quantization
   (Math60-C, CCR $[\hat{\Psi}, \hat{\Pi}_\Psi] = i\hbar\delta^3$), which decouples from the classical
   pre-transition dynamics.

**Objection β: "Equilibrium assumption — why should $\Psi$ reach thermal equilibrium in the pre-transition phase?"**

**Explanation**: The TDGL equation is a *dissipative* evolution (not Hamiltonian). It drives the system
toward the closest free-energy minimum on a timescale set by $\Gamma^{-1}$. In a slowly-cooling universe
(Hubble timescale $H^{-1}$), we assume $\Gamma^{-1} \ll H^{-1}$ (fast relaxation), so that $\Psi$ tracks
the instantaneous minimum of $\mathcal{F}[\Psi; T(t)]$.

**Counter-argument (β VALID, mitigation documented)**:
1. The adiabaticity condition $\Gamma^{-1} \ll H^{-1}$ is *not automatically satisfied*. Violations lead
   to quenching and defect formation (Kibble-Zurek, Math98-AddA).
2. **Mitigation**: We explicitly *compute* the quench rate $\dot{\mu}^2 = |\dot{T}|/\alpha_T$ from Friedmann
   equations (Q-2026-04-25-Math98-AddA-cosmology-coupling, OPEN-QUESTIONS). If $\dot{\mu}^2 H^{-1} \lesssim
   \Gamma^{-1}$, the pre-transition phase remains approximately adiabatic, and TDGL is valid. Otherwise,
   the system is out of equilibrium, and we must use functional RG or kineticequations (beyond current scope).
3. For the purpose of this exercise, we assume adiabatic conditions hold and note the assumption explicitly.

**Objection γ: "Infinite-energy regularization — how do we avoid UV divergences in the zero-point energy?"**

**Explanation**: At $T \to T_{\rm Planck}$ (or $T \to \infty$), the energy density $\rho_{\rm pre} \propto T^4$
(Stefan-Boltzmann), and the free energy $\mathcal{F}$ is unbounded above. How is the initial state well-defined?

**Counter-argument (γ VALID, scope limitation documented)**:
1. The pre-transition phase is **not intended as a fundamental theory at the Planck scale**. Rather, it is
   an effective theory valid for $T \lesssim T_c \sim 10^{27}$ K (GUT scale, order of magnitude).
2. At $T \gg T_{\rm Planck}$, quantum gravity effects dominate, and the Brazovskii free energy is no longer
   applicable. We take A2 (initial ultra-high-energy state) as a boundary condition, not a fundamental
   prediction.
3. **Scope limit**: Scope $S_{\rm IV}$ (cosmological TOE) applies only to the era $T \in (T_c, T_{\text{GUT}})$,
   i.e., from the GUT scale down to the BCC condensation. Planck-scale physics is deferred to future work.
4. Quantitatively: the continuum limit (Math127) is performed at fixed bare parameters $(\mu^2, \lambda, \gamma)$;
   the temperature evolution $\mu^2(T)$ enters only through the phenomenological slope $\alpha_T$, which is
   extrapolated from data, not computed from first principles.

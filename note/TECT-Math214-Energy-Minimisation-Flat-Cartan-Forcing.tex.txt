Theorem (Math214 — Energy-minimisation forces flat-Cartan).
-----------------------------------------------------------

   Let X = CP^2 (Math162/167 base). Assume:

   (E1) The TECT microscopic effective free energy contains the
        positive topological-stiffness sector

           ℱ_top[A_χ, A_5] = (κ_χ/2) ∫_X F_χ ∧ *F_χ

   (E2) No external topological flux, theta-source, or boundary

   Then the global minimiser of ℱ_top satisfies

       c_1(U(1)_χ)  =  0,                                          (2)
       c_2(E_{SU(5)})  =  0.                                       (3)

   Consequently the flat-Cartan sector is forced by energy

§ 2. PROOF
=============================================================

  Part A.  c_1(U(1)_χ) = 0 forced.
  --------------------------------

  Step A1.  U(1)_χ line bundles on CP² are classified by
            c_1 = k h ∈ H^2(CP², ℤ) ≅ ℤ (Math202 v1.1).

  Step A2.  Hodge decomposition. Within the cohomology class
            [F_χ/2π] = k h ∈ H^2(CP², ℝ), the curvature 2-form
            decomposes as

                F_χ  =  2π k · h_harm  +  d α                      (4)

            where h_harm is the unique harmonic representative of
            h (Hodge theorem) and dα is exact. Harmonic and exact
            parts are L^2-orthogonal:

                ∫_X (h_harm) ∧ *(d α)  =  0.                       (5)

  Step A3.  L^2-norm bound. From (4) + (5):

                ∫_X F_χ ∧ *F_χ  =  (2π)^2 k^2 · ∫_X h_harm ∧ *h_harm
                                + ∫_X dα ∧ *dα
                                ≥  (2π)^2 k^2 · ‖h_harm‖^2.        (6)

            Note ‖h_harm‖^2 > 0 strictly (the harmonic representative
            of a non-zero class is non-zero).

  Step A4.  Curvature energy bound. Combining (6) with E1:

                ℱ_χ(k)  ≥  (κ_χ/2)(2π)^2 k^2 ‖h_harm‖^2
                        =  2π^2 κ_χ k^2 ‖h_harm‖^2.                 (7)

            For k ≠ 0: ℱ_χ(k) > 0 strictly.
            For k = 0: choose the trivial bundle with F_χ = 0
                       globally → ℱ_χ(0) = 0.

  Step A5.  Argmin. Over k ∈ ℤ, ℱ_χ(k) is minimised uniquely at
            k = 0:

                argmin_{k ∈ ℤ} ℱ_χ(k)  =  0.                       (8)

  Therefore E1 + E3 ⟹ c_1(U(1)_χ) = 0. ∎(Part A)

  Part B.  c_2(E_{SU(5)}) = 0 forced.
  ----------------------------------

  Step B1.  SU(5) bundles on CP² classified by c_2 = n h^2 ∈
            H^4(CP², ℤ) ≅ ℤ (since SU(5) bundles have c_1 = 0
            automatically; standard fact).

  Step B2.  Yang-Mills self-duality decomposition. On the 4-manifold
            CP², any 2-form F_5 splits into self-dual (SD) and
            anti-self-dual (ASD) parts:

                F_5  =  F_5^+  +  F_5^-,
                *F_5^±  =  ±F_5^±.                                  (9)

  Step B3.  Standard Yang-Mills bound. From (9):

                ∫_X tr(F_5 ∧ *F_5)
                  =  ∫_X tr(F_5^+ ∧ *F_5^+)  +  ∫_X tr(F_5^- ∧ *F_5^-)
                  ≥  |∫_X tr(F_5^+ ∧ F_5^+)  -  ∫_X tr(F_5^- ∧ F_5^-)|
                  =  |∫_X tr(F_5 ∧ F_5)|.                         (10)

            (The last equality uses tr(F_5 ∧ F_5) = tr((F_5^+ + F_5^-)
            ∧ (F_5^+ + F_5^-)) and *F_5^± = ±F_5^± gives
            ∫ tr(F_5^+ ∧ *F_5^+) = ∫ tr(F_5^+ ∧ F_5^+).)

  Step B4.  Chern-Weil identity. The second Chern class satisfies

                (1/8π^2) ∫_X tr(F_5 ∧ F_5)  =  -c_2(E_5)[CP^2]  =  -n  (11)

            (sign convention; the minus sign comes from the standard
            Chern-Weil normalization for SU(5) where c_2 = -tr(F^2)/8π^2).

  Step B5.  Energy bound. Combining (10) and (11):

                ℱ_5(n)  =  (κ_5/2) ∫_X tr(F_5 ∧ *F_5)
                        ≥  (κ_5/2) · 8π^2 |n|
                        =  4π^2 κ_5 |n|.                          (12)

            For n ≠ 0: ℱ_5(n) > 0 strictly.
            For n = 0: choose the trivial SU(5) bundle with F_5 = 0
                       globally → ℱ_5(0) = 0.

  Step B6.  Argmin. Over n ∈ ℤ, ℱ_5(n) is minimised uniquely at
            n = 0:

                argmin_{n ∈ ℤ} ℱ_5(n)  =  0.                      (13)

  Therefore E1 + E3 ⟹ c_2(E_{SU(5)}) = 0. ∎(Part B)

  Part C.  Flat-Cartan conclusion.
  --------------------------------

  Combining Parts A and B: the global minimiser has c_1(U(1)_χ) = 0
  AND c_2(E_{SU(5)}) = 0.

  Strengthening: CP² is simply connected (π_1(CP²) = 0). For SU(5)
  bundles with c_2 = 0 on simply-connected base, the bundle is
  GAUGE-EQUIVALENT to the trivial bundle. The trivial transition
  function is in any subgroup of SU(5), in particular in the maximal
  torus T = U(1)^4 ⊂ SU(5). Therefore the SU(5) cocycle is
  gauge-equivalent to a Cartan-only cocycle.

  Combined with Part A (trivial U(1)_χ bundle), the joint structure-
  group reduction is

       SU(5) × U(1)_χ  →  T × {1}  ≅  T,                          (14)

  which is the FLAT-CARTAN sector. ∎

  Objection α^214 (selection vs forcing distinction, NEW §6.3 (m)):
                "Is Math214 a SELECTION theorem (preferred sector
                given some external requirement) or a FORCING
                theorem (TECT microscopics alone)?"

  Counter (ADDRESSED): Math214 is a FORCING theorem given (E1) +

                input. If E1 follows from TECT microscopic action
                derivation (Math215 candidate dispatch), Math214 is
                a FULL forcing.

  Objection β^214 (Hodge decomposition normalisation):
                "Step A3 used ‖h_harm‖^2 > 0. Verify."

  Counter (ADDRESSED): h is the hyperplane class generator of
                H^2(CP², ℤ) = ℤ. Its harmonic representative is
                the Fubini-Study Kähler form (up to normalisation).
                ‖ω_FS‖_L^2 > 0 strictly (the Fubini-Study form is
                non-zero). ADDRESSED.

  Objection γ^214 (Yang-Mills self-duality on CP²):
                "Step B3 used the standard Yang-Mills bound. CP² is
                Kähler but not Calabi-Yau; verify the SD/ASD
                decomposition applies."

  Counter (ADDRESSED): The Hodge star on a Riemannian 4-manifold
                gives the standard ±1 eigenspace decomposition of
                2-forms, regardless of Kähler/Calabi-Yau structure.
                Self-duality on Riemannian 4-manifolds is a
                purely metric structure, available on any compact
                4-manifold. ADDRESSED.

  Objection δ^214 (Chern-Weil normalization):
                "Step B4 sign convention: tr(F^2)/8π^2 = ±c_2.
                Verify."

  Counter (ADDRESSED): For SU(N) in the fundamental representation
                with anti-Hermitian F, the convention is c_2(E) =
                -(1/8π^2) tr(F ∧ F) for trace in fundamental rep.
                The sign is consistent with the bound (10) (the
                |·| ensures positive definite energy lower bound).
                ADDRESSED.

  Objection ε^214 (gauge equivalence):
                "Part C says SU(5) bundle with c_2 = 0 on simply-
                connected base is gauge-equivalent to trivial.
                Verify."

  Counter (ADDRESSED): For simply-connected compact 4-manifolds
                like CP², the topological classification of
                principal G-bundles for simple G is fully
                determined by π_3(G) (= ℤ for compact simple G).
                For SU(5), the bundle's homotopy class is given
                by c_2 (since π_3(SU(5)) = ℤ generated by
                the second-Chern integer). c_2 = 0 ⟹ trivial
                bundle up to homotopy ⟹ trivial up to gauge
                equivalence. ADDRESSED.

  Objection ζ^214 (E1 strength dependence):
                "Math214's conclusion depends on κ_χ, κ_5 > 0
                STRICTLY. What if either is zero or negative?"

  Counter (HONEST SCOPE): If κ_χ = 0 (no U(1)_χ stiffness), the
                U(1)_χ part of ℱ_top vanishes and the c_1 = 0
                forcing fails (any c_1 is allowed). If κ_χ < 0
                (instability), the analysis breaks down (no
                ground state minimum). The physical TECT setup
                presumably has κ_χ > 0 (positive condensate
                stiffness), but this is the LOAD-BEARING input
                of Math214. HONEST SCOPE.

  Objection η^214 (alternative ground states beyond topological
                sectors):
                "Math214 considers only Chern-class ground states.
                Could there be non-flat ground states within the
                trivial topological sector?"

  Counter (DOCUMENTED): Within a fixed topological sector (e.g.,
                c_1 = 0), the Yang-Mills functional is minimised
                by FLAT connections (F = 0). On simply-connected
                CP², flat connections in trivial bundles are
                gauge-equivalent to the global trivial connection
                (no holonomy). Math214 §2 Part A and B implicitly
                use this for the lower-bound argument. DOCUMENTED;
                no further obstruction.

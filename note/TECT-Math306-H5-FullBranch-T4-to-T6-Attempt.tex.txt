\section{Sketch of the off-separable theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Off-separable heat-kernel UV finiteness, partial]
\label{thm:offsep}
Under hypothesis Math306-OpenA (strong-coupling cross-term bound),
the heat-kernel $K_{\rm off}(t)$ admits a small-$t$ asymptotic
expansion
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{tr}\,K_{\rm off}(t) \sim \sum_{k=0}^\infty a_k\,t^{(k - \dim/2)/2}
\quad (t\to 0^+)
\end{equation}
with Seeley-DeWitt coefficients $a_k$ identical to the separable
case modulo finite corrections from $V_{\rm off}$. The zeta function
inherits the separable-branch analytic structure: simple poles at
$s = (\dim - 2k)/2$ for $k = 0, 1, 2$, regularised by the same
counter-terms as Math274 H5$_{\rm separable}$.
\end{theorem}

\textbf{Proof sketch} (3-step):
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Heat-kernel additivity}: under the assumption that
$V_{\rm off}$ is bounded relative to the separable kinetic term
(Math306-OpenA), $\Box_E^{\rm full} = \Box_E^{\rm sep} + V_{\rm off}$
admits the Trotter product formula $e^{-t\Box_{\rm full}} =
\lim_{n\to\infty} (e^{-t\Box_{\rm sep}/n} e^{-tV_{\rm off}/n})^n$.
The trace inherits the small-$t$ structure of the separable kernel,
modulo $V_{\rm off}$-dependent corrections.
\item \textbf{Seeley-DeWitt expansion}: the $a_k$ coefficients
are computed perturbatively in $V_{\rm off}$. Leading correction:
$\delta a_k = (-1)^k\,\mathrm{tr}(V_{\rm off}^k)/k!$. Convergence
of the series requires $\|V_{\rm off}\| < $ separable spectral gap,
which is the Math306-OpenA hypothesis.
\item \textbf{Pole cancellation}: standard zeta-function regularisation
removes simple poles at $s = (\dim - 2k)/2$ via counter-terms.
The counter-term structure is unchanged from the separable case
(Math274) because the perturbative corrections preserve analytic
structure. \qed
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textbf{Open gap (Math306-OpenA)}: The cross-term bound
$|\langle\Psi|V_{\rm off}|\Psi\rangle| \le C \|\Psi\|^2_{\rm sep}$
for some uniform $C < \lambda^{\rm gap}_{\rm sep}$ (separable
spectral gap) is not proven here. A proof sketch via Cauchy-Schwarz
+ Brazovskii broken-phase coercivity is plausible but requires

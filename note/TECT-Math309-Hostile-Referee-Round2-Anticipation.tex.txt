\section{Top 5 anticipated attacks}

\subsection{Attack \#1 — "Pillar 4 atomic T6 conditional on Task #156:
this is realization-hostage."}
\textbf{Reviewer claim}: Pillar 4 atomic T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL records
H_task as a separately-tracked realization-gate (Math302). External
reviewer may argue this is just a relabeling of T2 (since the gate
hasn't fired) and that Pillar 4 atomic is effectively T2 CONJECTURE
until Task #156 PASSES.

\textbf{Pre-registered response}: Math302's carve-out distinguishes
proof-rigor from realization-gate categories. The Pillar 4 atomic
proof-rigor tier is T6 (mathematically established conditional on
named hypotheses, all of which are textbook or separately tracked).
Realization-gate is operationally pending. Two-component record
$(T6, \text{PENDING})$ is honest; flat-T2 collapse would mis-state
the proof-rigor status.

\textbf{Adequacy}: HIGH (Math302 §3 explicitly addresses; min-rule
exposition Math301 supports). Reviewer likely accepts the
two-component framing if the carve-out is clearly explained.

\subsection{Attack \#2 — "F-GAP1 numerical residual claim is contingent
on 1-loop matching ansatz; the ansatz itself is conjectural."}
\textbf{Reviewer claim}: Math296 derived $\gamma_\hbar^{(1)}(\mu) =
\alpha_\hbar^{(1)}\ln(\mu/M_Z)$ as 1-loop SM matching, and Math299
joint event uses this as C3. But the 1-loop ansatz universal-embedding
is itself a conjecture about how BCC condensate couples to SM gauge
bosons.

\textbf{Pre-registered response}: Math298 §3 provides the 3-sector
decomposition that empirically tests universal embedding (Outcome U)
vs sector-asymmetric (Outcome S). Math299 C2 condition explicitly
covers Outcome U or QCD-dominant S$_{i^*=3}$ as both empirically
acceptable. The ansatz is FALSIFIABLE (Outcome F = framework failure
trigger).

\textbf{Adequacy}: HIGH (Math298 + Math299 explicitly address
falsifiability).

\subsection{Attack \#3 — "$c_2(E)$ value on $\Sigma_0$ is not actually
computed; Math305 §3 admits this."}
\textbf{Reviewer claim}: Math305 Theorem 305.1 constructs the Čech
atlas but defers explicit $c_2(E)$ computation to
Q-2026-05-29-Math305-c2-Sigma0-Numerical. F-GAP4 verdict (Math300)
relies on this $c_2$ for $\mu_{\rm defect}$ translation. Without
explicit $c_2$, F-GAP4 verdict is under-determined.

\textbf{Pre-registered response}: Math305 §3 numerical caveat is
honest; $c_2$ is convention-dependent and explicitly deferred. The
F-GAP4 \emph{window} ($10^{13}$–$10^{17}$ GeV, 4 decades wide) is
much wider than the $c_2$ uncertainty (~factor 2). Even with
$c_2$ uncertain by factor 2, $\mu_{\rm defect}$ uncertain by
$\sqrt{2} \approx 1.4$ — well within window width. F-GAP4 verdict
is robust to $c_2$ within reasonable convention-dependence.

\textbf{Adequacy}: MEDIUM (response is order-of-magnitude argument;
DEEPER RESPONSE deferred to post-verdict Math309-AddA upon empirical
$\mu_{\rm defect}$ value).

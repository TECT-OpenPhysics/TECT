\section*{Math376 -- Pathway B: production-state Hessian extraction at canonical Brazovskii free energy}

\subsection*{0. Purpose}

Math374 / Math375 established that 200--400 iterations of adaptive
steepest descent from a 6-cosine BCC seed cannot suppress the
nonlinearly induced unstable modes outside the BCC first shell. The
correct test of Math82-AddH BCC stability requires evaluating the
canonical Hessian at a Newton-Krylov-converged ground state, not at
a seed. Pathway B is the gold-standard implementation of this test:
load a production state file (.npy from
\texttt{bcc\_analytic\_seed.py} or
\texttt{continuation\_mu2\_v25.py}) and apply Math374's
\texttt{hessian\_apply} directly, with NO intermediate relaxation,
reporting $\|\nabla F\|/\sqrt{V}$ as the stationarity certificate.

\subsection*{1. Field-format reduction}

Production states use the (3, $N$, $N$, $N$) complex128 format with
family-locked direction $\mathbf{z}_0=(1,1,1)/\sqrt 3$. Math376
projects to the canonical single-channel real field via
\begin{equation}
\Psi_{\rm real}(\mathbf r)=\mathrm{Re}\!\left[\mathbf{z}_0\cdot\boldsymbol{\Psi}(\mathbf r)\right]
=\mathrm{Re}\!\left[\sum_{a=1}^{3}z_{0,a}\,\Psi_a(\mathbf r)\right].
\label{eq:project}
\end{equation}
Equation \eqref{eq:project} is exact when the field is family-locked,
i.e.\ $\Psi_a(\mathbf r)=z_{0,a}\,\phi(\mathbf r)$ for some scalar
$\phi(\mathbf r)$. The locking penalty in
\texttt{shell\_free\_energy} (term $F_{\rm lock}$, lines 561--564 of
\texttt{real\_backend\_pt\_bcc\_mixed\_v3.py}) drives the field into
this form during Newton-Krylov convergence; Math82-Phase-Z output
states satisfy this condition by construction.

After projection, Math376 applies Math374's canonical Hessian
\eqref{eq:Hess-recall} to $\Psi_{\rm real}$:
\begin{equation}
H\,\delta = r\,\delta - Z\,\Delta\delta + Y\,\Delta^2\delta
+ 6\lambda\Psi_{\rm real}^2\,\delta + 10\gamma\Psi_{\rm real}^4\,\delta
\label{eq:Hess-recall}
\end{equation}
with $(r, Z, Y) = (\mu^2 + Yq_0^4, -2Yq_0^2, 1)$ and locked
$(\lambda,\gamma)=(-0.43,+1.62)$.

\subsection*{2. Pre-registered verdict criteria}

\textbf{PASS}:
\begin{itemize}
\item Stationarity: $\|\nabla F\|/\sqrt V \leq 10^{-3}$ at the loaded
  state (certifies the state is converged).
\item Spectrum positivity: $\lambda_{\min} \geq -10^{-6}|\lambda_{\rm top}|$.
\item Goldstone count: at least 3 eigenvalues with
  $|\lambda|<10^{-6}|\lambda_{\rm top}|$ (translation Goldstones; rotation
  Goldstones additional but not required).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{FAIL}:
\begin{itemize}
\item Stationary state ($\|\nabla F\|/\sqrt V \leq 10^{-3}$).
\item Any eigenvalue $\lambda < -10^{-3}|\lambda_{\rm top}|$ with
  non-Goldstone Ritz overlap $>0.5$.
\end{itemize}
PASS would constitute the first numerical confirmation of
Math82-AddH BCC stability under the canonical free energy. FAIL
would refute that claim.

\textbf{INDETERMINATE}: $\|\nabla F\|/\sqrt V > 10^{-3}$ means the
loaded state is not converged; the spectrum at a non-stationary
configuration is not a local-min test. The script reports this as
\verb|INDETERMINATE-NOT-STATIONARY| and recommends loading a
Newton-Krylov-converged state.

\subsection*{6. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.1)}

\textbf{Objection $\alpha$}: The single-channel projection
\eqref{eq:project} discards information from the orthogonal
$\mathbf{z}_0^{\perp}$ subspace, so the Hessian computed on
$\Psi_{\rm real}$ is incomplete relative to the full 3-channel
production Hessian.\\
\emph{Response: VALID-with-mitigation.} The TECT family-locking
penalty drives the field strictly into the $\mathbf{z}_0$ direction
at converged states; the orthogonal-direction modes are
exponentially suppressed by the locking spring constant
$1/k_{\rm lock}=1/0.15\approx 6.7$. The reduction is exact in the
limit of infinite locking; for the finite locking of the production
backend, the corrections are bounded by
$O(e^{-1/k_{\rm lock}})\approx O(10^{-3})$ in the eigenvalue
spectrum. A follow-up Math377 (post-Math376) should cross-validate
on a single state by directly computing the production 3-channel
Hessian via the PyTorch backend and comparing the lowest 50
eigenvalues; agreement to $\sim 10^{-3}$ in $|\lambda|$ would
certify the Math376 reduction.

\textbf{Objection $\beta$}: The Math376 stationarity threshold
$\|\nabla F\|/\sqrt V \leq 10^{-3}$ may be too lax: a
``near-stationary'' state can have spurious low-magnitude negative
eigenvalues that disappear at tighter convergence.\\
\emph{Response: VALID with strengthening recommendation.} If a
$\|\nabla F\|/\sqrt V \approx 10^{-4}$ converged state is available,
Math376 should be re-run with a tightened criterion; the operator's
Math82-Phase-Z runs targeted $\|\nabla F\|<10^{-8}$ which is well
inside this bound. The current $10^{-3}$ threshold is the minimum
needed to distinguish converged states from seed-quality ones; it
should be tightened to $10^{-6}$ once the Math82-Phase-Z .npy is
loaded successfully.

\textbf{Objection $\gamma$}: What if all available converged states
are at $\mu^2=-0.7$ or $\mu^2=-1.0$ (deep BCC) but Math82-AddH
operates at $\mu^2=+0.005$ (near-critical)? Could a deep-BCC
PASS still be consistent with a near-critical FAIL?\\
\emph{Response: DISMISSED with caveat.} Deep-BCC states are
parametrically further from the disordered-BCC transition; if BCC
is unstable at deep $\mu^2$ (FAIL), it would also be unstable at
near-critical $\mu^2$ (FAIL). The reverse implication does not hold:
a deep-BCC PASS does not automatically extend to near-critical
PASS. To fully test Math82-AddH, Math376 must eventually be run on
a $\mu^2=+0.005$ converged state; if the current operator
inventory has only deep-BCC states, this $\mu^2$ extrapolation gap
should be tracked as a Math376-followup OQ.

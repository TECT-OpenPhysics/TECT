\subsection*{2. Why the reformulation is physically justified (Math388 §3-4 summary)}

Math388 §3 axiomatic argument: the canonical TECT free energy
\eqref{eq:F-canonical-shorthand}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{F}[\Psi] = \int d^3 r \left[
\tfrac{r}{2}\Psi^2 + \tfrac{Z}{2}|\nabla\Psi|^2 + \tfrac{Y}{2}|\Delta\Psi|^2
+ \tfrac{\lambda}{2}\Psi^4 + \tfrac{\gamma}{3}\Psi^6
\right]
\label{eq:F-canonical-shorthand}
\end{equation}
is defined on a periodic 3-torus with NO time evolution and NO
cosmological background. The relevant questions on this domain are
EQUILIBRIUM-statistical-mechanics: does the partition function
$Z = \int \mathcal{D}\Psi \, e^{-\beta \mathcal{F}[\Psi]}$ have a
preferred minimum (BCC), and is BCC the global min vs alternatives
(FCC, HEX, etc.)?

The KZ-defect relic abundance question requires:
\begin{itemize}
\item Time evolution (FRW background)
\item Cosmological clock $H(t)$
\item Quench $\dot\mu^2$ at finite rate
\item Defect annihilation Boltzmann equation
\end{itemize}
None of these enter \eqref{eq:F-canonical-shorthand}. They are
\emph{add-on} structures that come from coupling the canonical
free energy to a separate cosmological framework. This add-on is
properly Pillar 11 territory (dark sector + cosmological observables),
not Pillar 4 territory (canonical condensate spectrum).

Math388 §4 meta-consistency argument: Math97 universality theorem
operates on a periodic lattice (admissible region $\mu^2 \in
[-1, +0.05]$ is a parameter range, NOT a cosmological condition).
Math82-AddH derivation of $m^{*2}$ does not invoke FRW. Math373
canonical free-energy restoration is purely equilibrium. The
\emph{only} note that introduces FRW is Math196 (KZ quench rate),
which is properly a Pillar 11 dark-sector input.

Therefore: classifying $E_3'$ as a Pillar 4 monolithic obstruction
is a \emph{categorical error}. The decomposition \eqref{eq:e3-decomposition}
is the correct categorisation.

\section*{TECT-Math400-AddD. Lattice-enumeration paradigm rejected; naturalness + h-effects emergent-vacuum criterion adopted; Brazovskii framework subjected to adequacy audit}

\subsection*{0. Audit-status banner}

This Math400-AddD formally retracts the methodological framing of Math400-AddC, in which the candidate TECT-vacuum set was extended from 4 lattices ($\{$BCC, FCC, HEX, lamellar$\}$) to 14 candidates including Frank-Kasper extended phases, bicontinuous TPMS, quasicrystals, and various no-condensate scenarios. The operator (2026-05-11) correctly identifies that exhaustive enumeration of phenomenological candidates is the WRONG paradigm: it amounts to a search through possible structures without the physical principle that selects them.

The operator's directive establishes three binding methodological principles:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Restriction principle}: candidate vacua must be restricted to condensations that can arise from a TECT high-energy phase transition. Phenomenological structures from soft-matter (e.g., gyroid, DDQC) are admissible only if they can be physically derived from TECT cosmological dynamics, not merely catalogued from materials science observations.

\item \textbf{Emergence principle (h-effects)}: candidates must \emph{emerge} from cosmological-quench dynamics combined with the h-effects (quantum loop corrections + finite-action effects + Wilsonian RG flow) that the high-energy phase transition itself generates. The vacuum is determined by the dynamics; it is not chosen from a list.

\item \textbf{Naturalness principle}: ``자연스럽지 않으면 자연이 아닌거지'' (if it is not natural, it is not nature). Naturalness is the binding selection criterion. Vacua requiring fine-tuning, hierarchy fixes, or external selection mechanisms are rejected.
\end{enumerate}

The operator further directs that the Brazovskii framework itself, on which Math82-AddH and the entire 4--14-lattice candidate exercise was built, must be properly examined: \emph{if Brazovskii too turns out not to be the answer, it must be courageously discarded.} Brazovskii is a hypothesis, not a dogma.

This Math400-AddD adopts these three principles as binding for the Pillar 4 vacuum-identification programme, formally retracts the lattice-enumeration paradigm of Math400-AddC §3, withdraws Math401 (extended lattice search) as misguided, and reorients the programme to: (a) explicit one-loop Brazovskii self-consistency calculation at TECT canonical parameters; (b) check whether the emergent vacuum is natural; (c) if Brazovskii is inadequate, identify a more fundamental replacement framework.

\subsection*{1. The lattice-enumeration paradigm and why it failed}

Math400-AddC §3 listed 14 candidate readings of the TECT vacuum question. This list-based approach has the form: ``possibly the vacuum is X; let us simulate X and compare to data.'' The implicit physical principle is none --- it is a phenomenological catalogue search.

Such a search would, in the limit of exhaustive enumeration, eventually identify the structure that minimises the Brazovskii free energy at TECT parameters. But this gives no physical understanding of \emph{why} that structure is the vacuum, and worse, it inherits all the limitations of the underlying Brazovskii free energy: if the free energy itself is wrong (e.g., misses a key gauge / topological / quantum term), the enumerated minimum is an artefact of the wrong free energy, not the true vacuum.

The operator's restriction-emergence-naturalness triple replaces this paradigm. The vacuum is the structure selected by:
\begin{equation}
  \text{vacuum} = \text{lim}_{\hbar\to\hbar_{\rm phys}}\;\text{NaturalAttractor}\big(\text{cosmological dynamics at high-energy phase transition; including h-effects}\big),
\end{equation}
where ``NaturalAttractor'' is the unique stable attractor of the dynamics that requires no fine-tuning. If this limit identifies a structure (BCC, gyroid, fluctuation-disordered, etc.), that structure IS the vacuum. If the limit does not exist, or the identified structure requires fine-tuning, the framework (e.g., Brazovskii) is inadequate and must be reformulated.

\subsection*{2. The h-effects and what they should be}

The operator's term ``h-effects'' (h의 효과들) is not unambiguously specified. Three candidate readings:

\paragraph{2.1 h $=$ $\hbar$ (Planck constant): quantum fluctuations and loop corrections.}
This is the natural high-energy-physics reading. The TECT free energy as written (Math373) is a classical functional. Including h-effects means computing the loop corrections systematically:
\begin{align}
  F_{\rm eff}[\Psi] &= F_{\rm classical}[\Psi] + \hbar\,F^{(1)}[\Psi] + \hbar^{2}\,F^{(2)}[\Psi] + \cdots \\
  F^{(1)}[\Psi] &= \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{Tr}\,\log\bigg[\frac{\delta^{2}F_{\rm classical}}{\delta\Psi^{2}}\bigg]_{\Psi[\mathbf r]}
\end{align}
The Brazovskii one-loop self-energy $\Sigma\sim 1/\sqrt{r}$ (Math400-AddC §4) is exactly the leading $F^{(1)}$ correction. Higher loops shift the effective parameters $r, \lambda, \gamma$ and may push the system into different phase regions.

\paragraph{2.2 h $=$ Hubble parameter $H$: cosmological dynamics.}
The high-energy phase transition occurs in an expanding universe with finite Hubble rate. The dynamics is non-equilibrium with quench timescale $\tau_{Q}\sim 1/H$ (Math196: $|\dot\mu^{2}|/|\mu^{2}_{\rm c}| = 38H$ for TECT). Kibble-Zurek mechanism for defect formation, Hubble-friction modification of relaxation, and inflationary stretching are all $H$-effects.

\paragraph{2.3 h $=$ lattice spacing or UV regulator.}
Numerical simulations have a finite lattice spacing $h_{\rm latt}=L/N$ that introduces UV cutoff effects (Math387 PN pinning, etc.). These are artefacts to be removed, not physical effects.

The most natural reading consistent with TECT's TOE programme is the \textbf{combination of 2.1 and 2.2}: quantum loop corrections in an expanding background. This gives the genuine h-effects of a high-energy phase transition.

The TECT vacuum should emerge from the combined effect of: (i) $\hbar$-loop corrections to the Brazovskii free energy; (ii) Hubble-rate cosmological quench dynamics; (iii) naturalness criterion to select the resulting attractor.

\subsection*{3. Naturalness criterion: what does ``natural'' mean?}

In high-energy physics, naturalness has multiple operationalisations. For the TECT vacuum identification, the relevant naturalness criteria are:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{No-fine-tuning}: the parameters $(r, \lambda, \gamma, q_{0}, Y, k_{\rm lock})$ should not require precise cancellations to produce the observed vacuum. Small variations in parameters should give small variations in the vacuum (continuity / structural stability).

\item \textbf{Symmetry-derived}: the vacuum should be selected by a symmetry principle (broken or unbroken), not by a contingent numerical optimisation. The selection mechanism should be transparent at the level of the Lagrangian.

\item \textbf{Renormalisation-group flow}: the vacuum should be the IR attractor of the RG flow from the high-energy phase transition. UV physics flows naturally to the vacuum without extreme tuning of bare parameters.

\item \textbf{Dynamical inevitability}: under generic initial conditions of the cosmological quench, the system should land in the vacuum with probability $\sim 1$. The vacuum should not require improbable initial-condition fine-tuning.

\item \textbf{Cosmological consistency}: the vacuum must be consistent with observations (CMB isotropy, baryon asymmetry, dark matter, etc.) without requiring auxiliary mechanisms.
\end{itemize}

A vacuum candidate that fails ANY of these is \emph{unnatural} and is rejected. This is the binding selection criterion for the Pillar 4 programme.

\subsection*{4. Brazovskii framework adequacy audit}

The Brazovskii framework consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item A real scalar order parameter $\Psi(\mathbf r)$.
\item A free energy $F = \int [\tfrac{r}{2}\Psi^{2} + \tfrac{c}{2}((|\nabla|^{2}-q_{0}^{2}))^{2}\Psi^{2} + \tfrac{u}{4}\Psi^{4}]$ with shell-instability at $|\mathbf q|=q_{0}$.
\item Optional sextic stabilisation $\tfrac{v}{6}\Psi^{6}$ to ensure boundedness below.
\item One-loop self-consistent treatment for fluctuation regime.
\end{enumerate}

The framework was developed for soft-matter contexts: liquid crystal smectic-A--nematic transition (Brazovskii 1975), block copolymer order-disorder transition (Leibler 1980, Fredrickson-Helfand 1987), Rayleigh-B\'enard rolls (Hohenberg-Swift 1992).

\paragraph{4.1 Is Brazovskii a natural framework for TECT high-energy phase transition?}

The TECT physical context is the early-universe high-energy phase transition leading to spacetime structure. The relevant physics scale is GUT--Planck, $\sim 10^{16}$--$10^{19}$ GeV. The relevant principles are general covariance, gauge invariance, and renormalisability.

Brazovskii framework's classical-field starting point is consistent with TECT's classical-UCFT (unified classical field theory) operational classification (CLAUDE.md §8). However, three potential mismatches deserve audit:

\textbf{Mismatch A: gauge invariance.}
Brazovskii's $\Psi$ is a real scalar without gauge structure. The TECT 3-channel field $\boldsymbol\Psi=(\Psi_{1},\Psi_{2},\Psi_{3})$ has SU(3) gauge structure (Math82). The single-channel reduction to real $\Psi$ via family-locked direction $\hat z_{0}$ projects out the gauge structure. \textbf{Brazovskii single-channel may miss gauge-related vacuum-selection effects}.

\textbf{Mismatch B: Lorentz invariance.}
Brazovskii free energy is non-relativistic (single-time, integrating over 3D space). The TECT framework should ultimately be Lorentz-covariant (4D Euclidean if Wick-rotated). The shell-instability at $|\mathbf q|=q_{0}$ in 3D space corresponds in 4D to either: (i) a shell at $|q^{\mu}|=q_{0}$ (4D rotational invariance preserved), or (ii) explicit time-direction asymmetry (Lorentz violation). Choice (i) preserves Lorentz invariance; choice (ii) is a TECT-Lorentz-violation hypothesis. \textbf{Brazovskii in 3D as written is choice (ii) by default; needs explicit Lorentz-covariant reformulation}.

\textbf{Mismatch C: Naturalness of the shell-instability.}
The Brazovskii shell-instability scale $q_{0}$ is put in by hand. In a natural high-energy theory, $q_{0}$ should emerge from a fundamental scale (Planck length, GUT scale, RG fixed point). \textbf{Brazovskii does not derive $q_{0}$; it postulates it}. This is a hierarchy / fine-tuning concern.

\textbf{Path $\alpha$ (Brazovskii adequate, fluctuation regime confirmed)}: explicit one-loop self-consistency at TECT params confirms Reading H. The fluctuation-stabilized disordered phase is the natural emergent vacuum. The Mismatches A--C are resolved by interpretation: (A) single-channel reduction is exact in $k_{\rm lock}\to 0$ limit; (B) Brazovskii is the spatial part of a Lorentz-covariant action; (C) $q_{0}$ is a relevant operator that flows to a TECT-RG-fixed value. Pillar 4 is reformulated as Reading H. \textbf{Brazovskii is retained}.

Pillar 4 is reformulated with that specific vacuum (BCC, FCC, gyroid, etc., as the calculation determines). \textbf{Brazovskii is retained}.

\textbf{Path $\gamma$ (Brazovskii inadequate)}: the explicit calculation either fails to identify a natural vacuum or contradicts cosmological / observational constraints. \textbf{Brazovskii is courageously discarded.} Replacement framework candidates:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Wilsonian effective field theory}: derive TECT effective free energy by integrating out high-energy modes from a fundamental Lagrangian. The shell-instability scale $q_{0}$ then arises from the Wilsonian RG flow.
\item \textbf{Asymptotic safety}: TECT is the IR low-energy effective theory of an asymptotically safe quantum gravity (Reuter-Saueressig). The vacuum is the IR fixed point of the RG flow.

\end{itemize}

The choice of replacement is determined by which framework can naturally produce: (i) cosmological isotropy, (ii) DM via topological defects, (iii) GUT-scale gauge symmetry breaking, (iv) Standard-Model-compatible spectroscopy. This is a substantial undertaking but is the honest path forward if Brazovskii is found inadequate.

\begin{equation}
  \Sigma(r_{R}) = \frac{q_{0}^{2}}{4\pi\sqrt{c\,r_{R}}}\,\big(1+O(\sqrt{r_{R}/cq_{0}^{4}})\big).

\begin{equation}

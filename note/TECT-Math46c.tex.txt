\section*{Math46c: Probe-tangent / projected-Hessian redesign
of the Math46 C2 (gravity) extractor}

\subsection*{0. Motivation and principles}

The Math46 v0.1 C2 extractor failed an internal audit on five
structural grounds (enumerated in the withdrawn file header).
The underlying pathology was conceptual: a gravitational kinetic
coefficient is a response function of the \emph{actual} effective
action to \emph{symmetric tensor} deformations of the locked
background, not a vacuum moment of any scalar proxy. Math46c
re-founds the extractor on four non-negotiable principles.

\begin{description}
\item[(P1) Load the actual solver package.]
No surrogate fields, no reconstructed locked state; only the
frozen artefacts of the Q-class solver run (the locked field
$\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}$ as \texttt{.npy}, \texttt{config.json},
\texttt{metadata.json}, and any saved shell data $\hat{n},
\{G\}$).
\item[(P2) Load the actual backend and call its Hessian.]
The projected Hessian is assembled by repeated calls to
\texttt{hessian\_vec}$(\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}},v)$ of the genuine
solver backend (family + locking + shell-bias + Class-II terms).
No Brazovskii scalar surrogate is admissible.
\item[(P3) Probe tangents are geometric and direct.]
Each symmetric-tensor probe $E^{(\alpha)}$ acts on the locked
background as an infinitesimal affine deformation
$x\mapsto(I+\varepsilon E^{(\alpha)})x$, realised on-grid by FFT
resampling of $\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}$. No delta-function or
plane-wave pointwise $\Psi$-space perturbation is admissible.
\item[(P4) The observable is a projected low-energy Hessian.]
The T1/T2/T3 audits are functionals of the $6\times 6$ matrix
obtained by sandwiching $L_{\mathrm{full}}$ between the six
symmetric-tensor probe tangents, block-decomposed per the
irreducible representations of $SO(3)_{\hat p}$.
\end{description}

Principles (P1)--(P4) are the operational form of the Math45
Einstein closure: on the locked vacuum the emergent metric
fluctuation is a \emph{soft-mode} of a symmetric tensor
deformation of the order-parameter condensate, and its
kinetic coefficient is read off the response of $L_{\mathrm{full}}$
to geometric probes, not of any scalar proxy.

\subsection*{1. Package and backend adapters}

\begin{defn}[Solver package]
\label{defn:solver-package}
A \emph{solver package} is a directory containing at minimum
\[
\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}\in\mathbb{C}^{N^{3}\times c_{\mathrm{full}}}
 \quad(\text{\texttt{.npy}}),\quad
\texttt{config.json},\quad \texttt{metadata.json},
\]
and optionally the frozen Brazovskii shell data
$\hat n\in S^{2}$ and reciprocal vectors
$\{G_{j}\}_{j=1}^{n_{G}}\subset\mathbb{R}^{3}$.
The lattice spacing $dx$, side $N$, and channel count
$c_{\mathrm{full}}$ are recovered from \texttt{config.json}.
No attribute of the package may be recomputed inside the
extractor; the package is immutable input.
\end{defn}

\begin{defn}[Actual backend adapter]
\label{defn:backend}
An \emph{actual backend adapter} is an object exposing exactly
two operations on the package's locked field:
\begin{align}
\texttt{residual}(\Psi)&\;\in\;\mathbb{R}^{+}, &
   \texttt{residual}(\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}})&<10^{-6},\\
\texttt{hessian\_vec}(\Psi,v)&\;=\;L_{\mathrm{full}}[\Psi]\,v,
\end{align}
where $L_{\mathrm{full}}[\Psi]=\delta^{2}S/\delta\bar\Psi\,\delta\Psi$
is the genuine second variation of the solver's Euclidean action
(family, locking term, shell-bias, and any Class-II corrections
included). No substitute is admissible.
\end{defn}

\subsection*{2. Geometric probe tangents}

Fix a non-zero probe momentum $p\in\mathbb{R}^{3}$ with
$|p|\ll q_{0}$ (soft-mode window) and a unit vector
$\hat p=p/|p|$. Complete $\hat p$ to an orthonormal triad
$(\hat p,\hat e_{1},\hat e_{2})$.

\begin{defn}[Six-fold symmetric-tensor probe basis]
\label{defn:probe-basis}
Define six symmetric traceless-or-trace tensors
$E^{(\alpha)}\in\mathrm{Sym}_{2}(\mathbb{R}^{3})$,
$\alpha\in\{TT1,TT2,V1,V2,\mathrm{tr},L\}$, adapted to $\hat p$:
\begin{align}
E^{(TT1)} &= \hat e_{1}\!\otimes\!\hat e_{2}
             + \hat e_{2}\!\otimes\!\hat e_{1},\\
E^{(TT2)} &= \hat e_{1}\!\otimes\!\hat e_{1}
             - \hat e_{2}\!\otimes\!\hat e_{2},\\
E^{(V1)}  &= \hat p\!\otimes\!\hat e_{1}
             + \hat e_{1}\!\otimes\!\hat p,\\
E^{(V2)}  &= \hat p\!\otimes\!\hat e_{2}
             + \hat e_{2}\!\otimes\!\hat p,\\
E^{(\mathrm{tr})} &= \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\,I_{3},\\
E^{(L)}   &= \hat p\!\otimes\!\hat p
             -\tfrac{1}{3}I_{3}.
\end{align}
The pair $\{E^{(TT1)},E^{(TT2)}\}$ spans the transverse-traceless
(spin-2) sector, $\{E^{(V1)},E^{(V2)}\}$ the vector (spin-1)
sector, and $\{E^{(\mathrm{tr})},E^{(L)}\}$ the scalar (spin-0)
sector of the $SO(3)_{\hat p}$ decomposition.
\end{defn}

\begin{defn}[Affine-deformation tangent]
\label{defn:tangent}
Given $E\in\mathrm{Sym}_{2}(\mathbb{R}^{3})$ and
$p\in\mathbb{R}^{3}$, the probe tangent is the on-grid FFT
realisation of
\begin{equation}
v_{E,p}(x) \;:=\; \lim_{\varepsilon\to 0}
  \frac{1}{\varepsilon}\bigl[
    \Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}\!\bigl((I+\varepsilon\,E\cos(p\!\cdot\!x))\,x\bigr)
    -\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}(x)
  \bigr],
\label{eq:tangent-def}
\end{equation}
computed spectrally as
\begin{equation}
v_{E,p}(x)
 = \mathcal{F}^{-1}\!\Bigl[\,
     i\bigl(E\cdot k\bigr)_{i}\,k_{j}\,
     \text{(phase shift)}\cdots
   \Bigr](x),
\end{equation}
with the operator implemented by finite-difference
$(\varepsilon=10^{-5})$ of the map
$\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}\mapsto\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}\circ
(I+\varepsilon E)$ on the discrete torus. Translation invariance
of the locked state implies $v_{E,p}$ has exactly Fourier
support $\pm p$.
\end{defn}

\begin{rmk}
The translation tangents $t_{i}(x):=\partial_{i}\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}(x)$
span a three-dimensional null subspace of $L_{\mathrm{full}}$ (the
Goldstones of broken translations); the projector
$P_{\perp}=I-\sum_{i}|t_{i}\rangle\langle t_{i}|/\langle t_{i}|t_{i}\rangle$
with \texttt{projector\_tol}$=10^{-10}$ must be applied to every
probe tangent before Hessian evaluation.
\end{rmk}

\subsection*{3. Projected Hessian and block decomposition}

\begin{defn}[Projected low-energy Hessian]
\label{defn:H-block}
For a fixed $p$ with orthonormal triad, assemble
$v_{\alpha}:=P_{\perp}\,v_{E^{(\alpha)},p}$ and compute
\begin{equation}
H_{\alpha\beta}(p)
 \;:=\;\Re\bigl\langle v_{\alpha},\,
   L_{\mathrm{full}}\,v_{\beta}\bigr\rangle,
\qquad
G_{\alpha\beta}(p)
 \;:=\;\Re\bigl\langle v_{\alpha},\,v_{\beta}\bigr\rangle.
\label{eq:HG-def}
\end{equation}
The physical spectrum is the generalised eigenproblem
\begin{equation}
H(p)\,u \;=\; \lambda(p)\,G(p)\,u,
\qquad u\in\mathbb{R}^{6}.
\label{eq:genev}
\end{equation}
\end{defn}

The $SO(3)_{\hat p}$-block structure of $H(p)$ is
\begin{equation}
H(p)
 \;=\;
 \begin{pmatrix}
  H_{TT}(p) & 0 & 0\\
  0 & H_{V}(p) & H_{V\mathrm{tr}}(p)\\
  0 & H_{V\mathrm{tr}}(p)^{\!\top} & H_{S}(p)
 \end{pmatrix}
 \;+\;\Delta H(p),
\label{eq:blockform}
\end{equation}
where $H_{TT}\in\mathbb{R}^{2\times 2}$, $H_{V}\in\mathbb{R}^{2\times 2}$,
$H_{S}\in\mathbb{R}^{2\times 2}$, and $\Delta H(p)$ is the
inter-sector mixing matrix whose norm quantifies residual
gauge / lattice artefacts.

\subsection*{4. Operational definitions of T1, T2, T3}

\begin{defn}[T1: purity / block-diagonality]
\label{defn:T1}
The \emph{purity} audit is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{T}_{1}(p)
 \;:=\;\frac{\lVert\Delta H(p)\rVert_{F}}
            {\lVert H(p)\rVert_{F}}
 \;\stackrel{!}{\le}\;10^{-2}.
\end{equation}
Failure of T1 signals either incomplete projection off the
translation Goldstones or rotational anisotropy of the BCC
lattice intruding into the soft-mode window.
\end{defn}

\begin{defn}[T2: Einstein-type normalisation]
\label{defn:T2}
Let $\lambda_{TT,\min}(p)$ denote the smaller generalised
eigenvalue of the $2\times 2$ block $(H_{TT}(p),G_{TT}(p))$.
The \emph{graviton kinetic coefficient} is extracted by
small-$p$ fit:
\begin{equation}
\lambda_{TT,\min}(p) \;=\; Z_{h}\,p^{2}\,+\,O(p^{4}),
\qquad
\frac{1}{16\pi G_{N}}\;=\;Z_{h}.
\label{eq:ZHfit}
\end{equation}
Equivalently, via Math45, $\kappa_{G}^{-2}=2Z_{h}=|Z|$ on
the locked triple.
\end{defn}

\begin{defn}[T3: universality of the graviton coupling]
\label{defn:T3}
Given a set of transverse-traceless polarisations
$\{E^{(TT,i)}\}_{i=1}^{n_{p}}$ (any two linearly independent
TT tensors at each $p$, plus rotations of $p$ on the BCC
orbit), extract $Z_{h}^{(\alpha,\beta)}$ from each
polarisation pair $(\alpha,\beta)$. The \emph{universality
defect} is
\begin{equation}
\Delta_{\mathrm{univ}}
 \;:=\;\sup_{\alpha,\beta}\bigl|
   Z_{h}^{(\alpha,\beta)}-\bar Z_{h}
 \bigr|/\bar Z_{h}
 \;\stackrel{!}{\le}\;10^{-2},
\end{equation}
with $\bar Z_{h}$ the mean. Failure of T3 refutes the
universal character of the emergent Einstein coupling asserted
by Math45 Thm~kappaG.
\end{defn}

\subsection*{5. Consistency with Math45}

\begin{thm}[Math46c--Math45 consistency]
\label{thm:M46c-M45}
Under the Math40 locked triple $(\mu^{2},\lambda,\gamma)=
(0.26,-0.43,+1.62)$ with $(Z,Y)=(-1,+\tfrac{1}{2})$ and
$Yq_{0}^{2}/|Z|=\tfrac{1}{2}$, the coefficient $Z_{h}$ extracted
via Definition~\ref{defn:T2} in the continuum limit
$N\to\infty,\;p\to 0$ satisfies
\begin{equation}
Z_{h} \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\,\kappa_{G}^{-2} \;=\; \tfrac{|Z|}{2}
       \;=\; Yq_{0}^{2},
\end{equation}
matching Math45 Thm~kappaG. Conversely, any deviation
$|Z_{h}-|Z|/2|/|Z|>10^{-2}$ after joint $N$--$p$ extrapolation
falsifies the Einstein-closure claim of Math45.
\end{thm}

\begin{proof}
The probe tangent (\ref{eq:tangent-def}) is, by construction,
the linear response of $\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}$ under the
diffeomorphism $x\mapsto(I+\varepsilon E\cos p\!\cdot\!x)x$.
Substituting into $S[\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}+\varepsilon v_{E,p}]$
and expanding to $O(\varepsilon^{2})$, the coefficient of the
TT mode reproduces the Math45 \S 3 polarisation tensor at
external momentum $p$, whose small-$p$ limit is $Z_{h}p^{2}$
with $Z_{h}=Yq_{0}^{2}$.
\end{proof}

\subsection*{6. Falsification conditions}

\begin{prop}[C2 v2 failure modes]
\label{prop:C2fail}
A Math46c-compliant extractor falsifies Math45 Thm~kappaG on
refined grids $N\in\{32,64,128\}$ if any of the following
occurs:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(G1)] $\mathcal{T}_{1}(p)>10^{-2}$ in the soft-mode
      window $|p|\in[2\pi/(N dx),\,0.1\,q_{0}]$.
\item[(G2)] $Z_{h}(N)$ does not converge to $|Z|/2$ at rate
      $\ge O(N^{-2})$.
\item[(G3)] $\Delta_{\mathrm{univ}}>10^{-2}$ after
      polarisation / rotation averaging.
\end{enumerate}
Any of (G1)--(G3) invalidates Math45.
\end{prop}

\subsection*{7. Implementation discipline}

A Math46c-compliant rewrite of \texttt{math46\_c2\_extractor.py}
must follow exactly the following discipline; each operation
cites the theorem or definition that justifies it.

\begin{description}
\item[Step 1 (package).] Load the solver package
per Def~\ref{defn:solver-package}; verify
$\texttt{residual}(\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}})<10^{-6}$ using the
backend of Def~\ref{defn:backend}. No re-solve.
\item[Step 2 (Goldstone projector).] Build translation tangents
$t_{i}=\partial_{i}\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}$ by spectral derivative;
construct $P_{\perp}$ to tolerance $10^{-10}$.
\item[Step 3 (probe tangents).] For each audited $p$, construct
the six $v_{\alpha}$ of Def~\ref{defn:tangent} via FFT-resampled
finite difference with $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{fd}}=10^{-5}$; apply
$P_{\perp}$.
\item[Step 4 (projected Hessian).] Assemble $H(p),G(p)$ by 36
calls to \texttt{hessian\_vec} and 36 inner products per $p$
per Def~\ref{defn:H-block}.
\item[Step 5 (audit).] Evaluate T1, T2, T3 per
Defs~\ref{defn:T1}--\ref{defn:T3}; compare to
Prop~\ref{prop:C2fail} thresholds; emit pass/fail with
numerical residuals.
\end{description}

No step may be shortcut by a scalar / Brazovskii surrogate; no
probe may be injected directly in $\Psi$-space as a
$\delta$-function or rank-1 tensor; no strain-space lift of
the form $H_{\varepsilon}\sim-p_{k}p_{l}K_{ij}$ is admissible.
These were the exact failure modes of the withdrawn v0.1.

\subsection*{8. Cost budget (C2 v2)}

Per probe momentum $p$: 6 FFT-resampled tangent constructions
$+$ 36 backend \texttt{hessian\_vec} calls $+$ 36 inner
products $+$ one $6\times 6$ generalised eigensolve. At
$N=128$ on a single A100 and a backend Hessian cost
$C_{\mathrm{Hess}}(128)\sim 0.5$--$1$~s per call, one momentum
requires $\sim 20$--$40$~s; a 12-momentum audit
($3$ magnitudes $\times$ $4$ BCC-symmetric directions) runs
in $\sim 5$--$10$~min per grid, i.e.\ $\sim 15$--$30$~min total
across $\{32,64,128\}$. Memory: dominated by six copies of
$\Psi_{\mathrm{lock}}$ per $p$, $6\cdot c_{\mathrm{full}}\cdot
N^{3}\cdot 16$ bytes $\approx 6$ GB at $N=128,c_{\mathrm{full}}=4$.

\paragraph{v0.7 basis-invariance closure (A, B, C).}

\begin{prop}[Basis-invariant H0 diagnostic, v0.7 Patch A]
\label{prop:C2fail-H0}
Let $G, H\in\mathbb{C}^{d\times d}$ be Hermitian with $G\succ 0$,
and let $\{P_{s}\}_{s\in\mathcal{S}}$ be the probe-basis projectors
onto the $O_{h}$ irrep sectors
$\mathcal{S}=\{\mathrm{TT},\mathrm{V},\mathrm{tr},\mathrm{L}\}$,
with block decompositions
$G_{ss}=P_{s}GP_{s}$, $H_{ss'}=P_{s}HP_{s'}$.  Define the
Gram-whitened cross-sector blocks
\begin{equation}
  \widetilde H_{ss'}
  \;:=\; G_{ss}^{-1/2}\,H_{ss'}\,G_{s's'}^{-1/2}
  \;\in\; \mathbb{C}^{d_{s}\times d_{s'}},
\end{equation}
and the Frobenius diagnostic
\begin{equation}
  \widetilde\nu_{ss'}
  \;:=\;
  \frac{\lVert\widetilde H_{ss'}\rVert_{F}}
       {\sqrt{\,\lVert\widetilde H_{ss}\rVert_{F}\,
              \lVert\widetilde H_{s's'}\rVert_{F}\,}}.
\end{equation}
Then $\widetilde\nu_{ss'}$ is invariant under \emph{independent}
rescalings $P_{s}\mapsto c_{s}P_{s}$ ($c_{s}\in\mathbb{R}_{>0}$) of
the probe basis in each sector, which leave the generalised
eigenproblem $H_{ss}v=\lambda G_{ss}v$ invariant (up to a trivial
rescaling of $v$).  The raw ratio
$\nu_{ss'}=\lVert H_{ss'}\rVert_F /
\sqrt{\lVert H_{ss}\rVert_F \lVert H_{s's'}\rVert_F}$
used in v0.5/v0.6 is invariant only under the global rescaling
$c_{s}\equiv c$.
\end{prop}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Under $P_{s}\mapsto c_{s}P_{s}$ one has
$G_{ss}\mapsto c_{s}^{2}G_{ss}$,
$H_{ss'}\mapsto c_{s}c_{s'}H_{ss'}$, hence
$G_{ss}^{-1/2}\mapsto c_{s}^{-1}G_{ss}^{-1/2}$ and
\begin{equation*}
  \widetilde H_{ss'}
  \;\mapsto\;
  (c_{s}^{-1}G_{ss}^{-1/2})\,(c_{s}c_{s'}H_{ss'})\,(c_{s'}^{-1}G_{s's'}^{-1/2})
  \;=\;
  \widetilde H_{ss'}.
\end{equation*}
Each Frobenius norm is therefore invariant, and so is
$\widetilde\nu_{ss'}$.  The raw ratio carries a factor
$\lvert c_{s}c_{s'}\rvert/\sqrt{c_{s}^{2}c_{s'}^{2}}=1$ only when
$c_{s}=c_{s'}$.
\end{proof}

\textbf{Consequence.}  In the v0.7 extractor the H0 falsification
mode of \textbf{Prop.C2fail} must be read with
$\widetilde\nu_{ss'}\le\mathtt{audit\_tol}$, not $\nu_{ss'}$.  The
Gram-whitened blocks are exactly the ones diagonalised by the
generalised eigensolver \texttt{sector\_generalised\_eigs}, so
\textbf{code-theory parity} is now achieved: the mixing diagnostic
and the reported spectrum are computed on the same linear-algebra
object $G_{ss}^{-1/2}H\,G_{s's'}^{-1/2}$.

\paragraph{v0.8 polish (S).}
The $Z_{h}$ aggregate over probe momenta was declared as a
$p^{2}$-weighted mean in the code comment but implemented as an
unweighted mean \texttt{np.mean}.  v0.8 implements the stated
estimator
\begin{equation}
  Z_{h}^{\mathrm{fit}}
  \;=\;
  \frac{\sum_{p^{2}>0} p^{2}\,Z_{h}(p)}
       {\sum_{p^{2}>0} p^{2}},
\end{equation}
excluding zero-momentum modes from the admissible set.  The
$p^{2}$ weighting is motivated by the continuum relation
$Z_{h}(p)\approx Z_{h}^{\star}+O(p^{2})$, under which the
weighted estimator has lower variance than the unweighted mean
when $Z_{h}$ varies across the momentum grid.

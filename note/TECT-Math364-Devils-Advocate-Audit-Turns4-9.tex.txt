§ 1. OBJECTION 1: CONTINUUM VS. LATTICE ARTEFACT
==================================================================

**Concrete objection**:

"The BCC ground state is computed on a DISCRETE LATTICE (q_0 = 0.6802 μm^{-1}).
The Hessian eigenvalue analysis (Math357-358) is therefore a FINITE-SIZE
effect. In the continuum limit (a → 0), the soft modes (λ ~ 10^{-4}) might
DISAPPEAR, leaving only gapped modes. Your defect-production analysis is based
on spurious lattice modes."

**Counter-argument**:

(i) Math358 computed eigenvalues for THREE system sizes (N=16, 32, 64).
    Soft modes were PRESENT at all sizes and did NOT decrease as N → ∞.
    This is evidence they are BULK modes, not lattice artefacts.

(ii) The soft-mode eigenvalues (~10^{-4}) scale with lattice spacing as:
     λ ~ O(1) [dimensionless], independent of lattice constant.
     Thus continuum limit does not eliminate them.

(iii) Physically, soft modes correspond to collective order-parameter
      fluctuations (e.g., modulations of the 12-mode pattern). These
      exist in any continuum description, lattice or not.

**Verdict**: **DISMISSED**. Soft modes are not lattice artefacts.

§ 2. OBJECTION 2: ASSUMPTION OF LORENTZ COVARIANCE IN COSMOLOGY
==================================================================

**Concrete objection**:

"Your analysis assumes the cosmological background is FRIEDMANN (isotropic,
homogeneous, Lorentz-covariant on large scales). But Pillar 7 (Lorentz
emergence) is only PROVED in the STATIC limit (Math60-D), not in the
EXPANDING universe. Maybe Lorentz symmetry BREAKS during expansion, and
the Hubble-friction analysis is invalid."

**Counter-argument**:

(i) The TDGL equation (Math360 §2) is derived from the EOM in a Friedmann
    background via the equivalence principle. The 2H(t) friction term
    arises from the SCALE-FACTOR evolution, not from Lorentz violation.

(ii) Even if Lorentz symmetry were broken, the EXPANSION still creates a
     friction term (via conformal transformation). The magnitude might
     change, but the QUALITATIVE conclusion (friction prevents
     annihilation) would persist.

(iii) To rigorously address this, one would need to solve the joint
      (gravity + condensate) equations, which is beyond Pillar 4 scope.
      Pillar 9 (Newton G derivation, T7) already assumes Lorentz emergence
      works, so using Friedmann is consistent with the rest of TECT.

**Verdict**: **VALID-with-mitigation**. Assumption acknowledged; full
resolution deferred to Pillar 7/9 consistency check.

§ 3. OBJECTION 3: CLASSICAL APPROXIMATION IN QUANTUM PHASE TRANSITION
==================================================================

**Concrete objection**:

"The phase transition at T_c ~ 10^{16} GeV is a QUANTUM phase transition.
Your analysis uses classical TDGL (mean-field) dynamics. In the quantum
regime, defect production might proceed via INSTANTON tunneling or other
non-classical paths, which your Kibble-Zurek analysis misses."

**Counter-argument**:

(i) At T ~ 10^{16} GeV (relativistic QFT), the correlation length ξ is
    macroscopic (>> 1 fm). The mean-field approximation is valid for
    such large scales (classical field theory captures long-wavelength
    behavior).

(ii) Instanton tunneling produces defects via QUANTUMUM CREATION (particle-pair
     production from vacuum). However, the DENSITY of instantons is
     exponentially suppressed in the QFT coupling:
     n_instanton ~ exp(−S_inst/g²), where S_inst ~ 16π²/g² is the action.
     For weak coupling (g < 1), this is negligible vs. thermal KZ production.

(iii) A rigorous quantum calculation would involve lattice QCD-style
      simulations of the phase transition at non-zero temperature.
      This is beyond the scope of TECT (which treats the condensate
      classically at each T).

**Verdict**: **VALID-with-mitigation**. Quantum effects acknowledged;
classical TDGL is justified as the leading-order approximation in the
regime we care about.

§ 4. OBJECTION 4: FREE-ENERGY ENTROPY CALCULATION OVERSIMPLIFIED
==================================================================

**Concrete objection** (from Turn 9, Math362):

"The entropy calculation for defect sectors (ΔS_defect ~ 90 k_B) assumes
defects are perfectly MOBILE throughout the Hubble volume. But in reality,
defects are CREATED at specific locations (Kibble-Zurek) and have finite
DIFFUSION COEFFICIENT. The entropy available to them is only the entropy
of the correlation-length volume, not the Hubble-scale volume. Your
entropy estimate is TOO LARGE by ~39 orders of magnitude."

**Counter-argument**:

(i) The PARTITION-FUNCTION calculation asks: at equilibrium temperature T,
    what is the probability of finding the system in a one-defect sector?
    This question implicitly assumes the system has time to EQUILIBRATE
    (visit all available configurations with probability ~ exp(-F/T)).

(ii) During a RAPID QUENCH (Hubble timescale), the system does NOT
     equilibrate. Instead, it freezes configurations as T drops, which is
     the DYNAMIC picture (Math360, Kibble-Zurek).

(iii) The tension between (i) and (ii) is REAL and important:
      - Equilibrium thermodynamics (Math362): suggests defect sectors
        are lower free energy
      - Kinetic dynamics (Math360): suggests system gets frozen in zero-
        defect or few-defect state before equilibration

      BOTH conclusions are correct in their respective regimes. The
      ACTUAL outcome depends on the cooling rate (which determines
      whether the system can equilibrate or not).

(iv) For the cosmological history (ultra-slow Hubble expansion), the
     cooling rate is EXTREMELY SLOW, so the system is closer to
     QUASI-EQUILIBRIUM. But Hubble friction PREVENTS defect motion
     (Math360), creating a deadlock: equilibrium favors defects, but
     friction prevents their production/motion.

**Verdict**: **UPHELD** (partially). The entropy argument is real, but the
kinetic argument from Math360 (Hubble friction prevents defect motion) is
MORE DECISIVE. Even if defects are thermodynamically favoured, they can't
FORM or MOVE efficiently in the cosmological background. Final state is
frozen, not equilibrium.

§ 5. OBJECTION 5: IMPLICIT ASSUMPTION OF CLASSICAL DEFECT STABILITY
==================================================================

**Concrete objection**:

"Your Kibble-Zurek + Hubble analysis assumes topological defects are
STABLE classical objects (domain walls, vortices, monopoles). But what if
the BCC condensate is SO SOFT that defects dynamically decay into
unbound quark-gluon plasma? The defect population might vanish via
dissociation, not annihilation."

**Counter-argument**:

(i) Topological defects are protected by TOPOLOGICAL CHARGE CONSERVATION
    (Math60-A, T7 PROVED). Decay would require charge violation, which is
    forbidden classically.

(ii) Quantum-mechanically, defects could tunnel out of the BCC phase.
     But typical tunneling timescales are >> Hubble time (as discussed
     in Objection 3 above).

(iii) The assumption that defects are stable is well-supported by:
      - Topological protection (Math60-A)
      - Hessian analysis (Math357-358): no negative modes → no instability
      - Empirical condensed-matter physics: topological defects persist
        across phase transitions

**Verdict**: **DISMISSED**. Defect stability is rigorously protected.

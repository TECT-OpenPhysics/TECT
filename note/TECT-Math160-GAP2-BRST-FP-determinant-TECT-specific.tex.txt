\section{Part I: Gauge Orbit Structure and Canonical Patch}

\subsection{The emergent gauge bundle}

From Math80-AddA/B/C, the BCC condensate $\Psi \in M_{\rm BCC}$ admits
an SO(10)-equivariant action such that the stabiliser subgroup is
\[
\mathrm{Stab}_{\mathrm{SO}(10)}([\Psi]) = G_{\rm SM} \times O_h,
\]
where $G_{\rm SM} = \mathrm{SU}(3)_c \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{U}(1)_Y$
and $O_h$ is the cubic point group. The quotient
\[
\mathrm{SO}(10) / (G_{\rm SM} \times O_h) \cong \mathrm{Gr}(2,5)
\]
is the Grassmannian of 2-planes in $\mathbb{C}^5$ (or equivalently,
the low-energy gauge-orbit space).

At one-loop, we focus on the local $G_{\rm SM}$ action near the
ground state $\Psi_0$ (the cubic-symmetry-broken BCC minimum).

\subsubsection{Gauge transformation and orbit}

A local $G_{\rm SM}$ gauge transformation acts as
\[
\Psi(x) \to U_g(x) \Psi(x), \quad A_\mu^a(x) \to U_g(x) A_\mu^a(x) U_g(x)^{-1} - \mathrm{i} U_g(x) \partial_\mu U_g(x)^{-1},
\]
where $U_g(x) = \exp[\mathrm{i} \alpha^a(x) T^a]$ with $\alpha^a(x)$ the
12 gauge-transformation parameters (3 SU(3) + 2 SU(2) + 1 U(1) generators).

The \emph{gauge orbit} through $(\Psi, A)$ is the set of all physically
equivalent configurations $\{(\Psi, A^\Omega) : \Omega \in G_{\rm SM}^{\infty}\}$.

\subsection{Canonical patch: background-Lorenz gauge}

To fix the gauge redundancy, we impose the \emph{background-Lorenz condition}
\[
\mathcal{G}^a[A] := D_\mu^{\mathrm{bg}} A^\mu_a = 0,
\]
where $D_\mu^{\mathrm{bg}}$ is the covariant derivative in the background
field $A^{\mathrm{bg}}_\mu$ (the classical field at the BCC condensate).
Explicitly,
\[
D_\mu^{\mathrm{bg}} A^\mu_a = \partial_\mu A^\mu_a + g f^{abc} A^{\mathrm{bg}}_\mu_b A^\mu_c.
\]

This gauge choice has two advantages:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Manifest covariance}: the condition is gauge-covariant
  and can be imposed consistently at each order in perturbation theory.
\item \textbf{Suppression of Gribov copies}: in the BCC condensate,
  the background field $A^{\mathrm{bg}}_\mu$ is supported only on
  a discrete set of lattice sites (shell modes, Math82-AddB), creating
  a \emph{sparse matrix structure} in the gauge-fixing operator that
  generically avoids the Gribov ambiguity for small volume.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{The Faddeev–Popov operator}

Applying the standard BRST procedure (Math152 §I), the FP operator is
\[
\mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}^{ab}(x,y) = \frac{\delta \mathcal{G}^a[A^{\Omega}]}{\delta \Omega^b(y)}\bigg|_{\Omega=0},
\]
evaluated at the background field $A = A^{\mathrm{bg}}$.

Taking the functional derivative of the gauge-fixing condition:
\[
\mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}^{ab}(x,y) = -\delta^{ab} \square \delta(x-y) - g f^{abc} \partial_\mu A^{\mathrm{bg}}_\mu_c(x) \delta(x-y).
\]

In position space on the BCC lattice, this becomes (lattice notation: $x, y$ are lattice sites):
\[
\mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}^{ab}_{xy} = -\delta^{ab} \Delta_{xy} - g f^{abc} (\partial_\mu^{\mathrm{bg}} A^{\mathrm{bg}}_\mu)_x \delta_{xy},
\]
where $\Delta_{xy}$ is the lattice Laplacian and the second term vanishes unless $A^{\mathrm{bg}}_\mu(x) \neq 0$ (which happens only on the first shell of the BCC reciprocal lattice, a finite set).

\section{Part II: One-Loop Faddeev–Popov Determinant}

\subsection{Ghost action and path integral}

The ghost sector contributes to the path integral via
\[
\mathcal{Z}_{\rm ghost} = \int \mathcal{D}c \, \mathcal{D}\bar{c} \, \exp\left(-\int d^d x \, \bar{c}^a(x) \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}^{ab}(x,x') c^b(x')\right).
\]

This is a Gaussian integral (anticommuting fields), giving
\[
\mathcal{Z}_{\rm ghost} = [\det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}]^{-1}.
\]

At one loop, we compute
\[
\ln \det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP} = \mathrm{Tr} \ln \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}.
\]

\subsection{Spectral decomposition on the canonical patch}

On a representative canonical patch (a BCC volume $V = N^3 a_{\rm BCC}^3$ with periodic
boundary conditions), the operator $\mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}$ admits a discrete spectrum.
For the gauge-covariant piece, the eigenvalues in momentum space are
\[
\lambda_n(\mathbf{k}) = k^2 + \text{(background correction term)}.
\]

The background field $A^{\mathrm{bg}}_\mu$ is localised on the first shell:
$A^{\mathrm{bg}}_\mu(x) = \sum_{j=1}^{12} A_j(\mathbf{q}_j) e^{\mathrm{i} \mathbf{q}_j \cdot \mathbf{x}}$
where $|\mathbf{q}_j| = 2\pi/a_{\rm BCC}$.

The correction term is $O(g \langle A \rangle) \sim O(g \lambda/\varphi_0)$ (small compared to $k^2$ for $k \gg 2\pi/a_{\rm BCC}$).

\subsection{Zeta-function regularisation}

Define the regularised determinant via zeta function
\[
\ln \det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP} = -\zeta'_{\mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}}(0),
\]
where $\zeta_s(s) = \mathrm{Tr} (\mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}^{-s})$ in the $\mathrm{Im}(s) > d/2$
region, analytically continued to $s=0$.

Explicitly, on a volume $V = N^3 a^3$ with momentum modes $\mathbf{k} = 2\pi \mathbf{n}/L$ ($\mathbf{n} \in \mathbb{Z}^3/N\mathbb{Z}^3$):
\[
\zeta_s(s) = V (2\pi)^{-d} \int d^d k \, \left[k^2 + m_{\rm eff}^2(\mathbf{k})\right]^{-s},
\]
where $m_{\rm eff}^2(\mathbf{k})$ includes the lattice regulator cutoff
$\Lambda_{\rm UV} = \pi/a_{\rm BCC}$ (Debye cutoff from the shell-mode structure).

\subsubsection{Heat-kernel asymptotics}

For large proper time $t$, the heat kernel $K(t) = \mathrm{Tr}[e^{-t\mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}}]$
has the standard asymptotic expansion
\[
K(t) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{a_n}{(4\pi t)^{(d-n)/2}},
\]
where $a_0, a_1, \ldots$ are the heat-kernel coefficients (Seeley-DeWitt).

For the Laplacian-like operator $-\square + V(\mathbf{x})$ on a 4D volume:
\begin{align}
a_0 &= V, \\
a_1 &= -\frac{1}{6} \int d^4 x \, \mathrm{Tr}[V(\mathbf{x})],\\
a_2 &= \text{(involves } V^2 \text{ and Riemann curvature; vanishes on flat BCC lattice)}.
\end{align}

\subsubsection{Finite one-loop contribution}

The logarithmic divergence at small $t$ is cancelled by dimensional
regularisation. The finite part is extracted via analytic continuation:
\[
\ln \det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP} = -\frac{\mu^{2\epsilon}}{(4\pi)^2} \left[\frac{2}{\epsilon} + \ln\frac{\mu^2}{M^2} + O(\epsilon)\right]_{\text{div}} + \text{finite},
\]
where $\mu$ is the renormalisation scale and $M^2$ is a mass scale (either $m^{*2}$ or $\Lambda_{\rm UV}^2$).

After subtraction of the divergent piece via a counterterm $\mathcal{L}_{\rm GF}^{\text{ct}}$,
the finite one-loop ghost contribution is
\[
\ln \det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}\big|_{\text{finite}} = \frac{1}{(4\pi)^2} \left[\sum_i n_i \ln\frac{\mu^2}{\lambda_i^2} + \text{(scheme-dependent constant)}\right],
\]
where $\lambda_i$ are the eigenvalue scales of the background-field problem.

\subsection{Non-vanishing in the canonical patch}

\begin{theorem}[Non-vanishing of the FP determinant]
\label{thm:fp-nonzero}
On any BCC canonical patch with the background-Lorenz gauge condition,
the Faddeev–Popov determinant $\det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP} \neq 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The operator $\mathcal{M}_{\rm FP} = -\square - V_{\rm bg}(\mathbf{x})$ is an
elliptic operator on the compact volume $V$ with Dirichlet or periodic
boundary conditions. The lowest eigenvalue is
\[
\lambda_{\min} = \min_{\phi \neq 0} \frac{\int d^d x |\nabla \phi|^2 + \int d^d x V_{\rm bg} |\phi|^2}{\int d^d x |\phi|^2}.
\]

Since the background field $A^{\mathrm{bg}}_\mu$ is a physical (non-ghost)
field representing transverse shell modes, $V_{\rm bg} > 0$ (repulsive).
Moreover, the kinetic term $\int d^d x |\nabla \phi|^2$ is strictly positive
for non-zero $\phi$ on a compact volume.

Thus $\lambda_{\min} > 0$, so all eigenvalues are positive, and
$\det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP} = \prod_i \lambda_i > 0$.
\end{proof}

This proves the absence of Gribov copies on the canonical patch: no
null eigenvector of $\mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}$ exists, so the gauge fixing
is non-singular.

\mathcal{D}\Psi \to \mathcal{D}\Psi \, \exp[\mathrm{i} \Gamma_{\rm Berry}[\Psi]],

\subsection{Explicit Berry-phase formula}

For a gauge transformation $\Psi \to U_g(x) \Psi$, the Berry phase is
\[
\Gamma_{\rm Berry}[\Psi] = \mathrm{i} \int_0^1 d\lambda \, \langle \Psi_\lambda | \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}\lambda} | \Psi_\lambda \rangle,
\]
where the integral is over a path in the space of order parameters.

For the BCC condensate in Fourier space, with first-shell amplitudes
$A_j = r_j e^{\mathrm{i}\theta_j}$ (Math80-AddB), the Berry phase is
\[
\Gamma_{\rm Berry}[\{r_j, \theta_j\}] = \sum_{j=1}^{12} \oint \theta_j \, \mathrm{d}\rho_j,
\]
where $\rho_j$ is a parameter path on the moduli torus $T^{12}$.

For a closed loop (e.g., a cyclic permutation of the 12 reciprocal-lattice
vectors under the cubic point group $O_h$), the Berry phase is quantised:
\[
\Gamma_{\rm Berry} = 2\pi \times (\text{integer winding number}),
\]
related to the braiding structure of the BCC defects (topological).

Under the action of a non-trivial loop in $M_{\rm BCC}$, the path-integral
measure for the ghost fields gains a prefactor:
\[
\det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP} \to \det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP} \times e^{\mathrm{i} \Gamma_{\rm Berry}[\Psi]}.

This is \textbf{the TECT-specific signature}: in a flat spacetime Yang–Mills

In TECT, the emergent gauge connection is anchored to the BCC order manifold

Physically, this encodes the fact that the $G_{\rm SM}$ gauge symmetry

\section{Part IV: Lattice Regularisation and Continuum Limit}

\subsection{Lattice FP operator}

On the BCC lattice with spacing $a = a_{\rm BCC}$, the Laplacian is
\[
\Delta_{xy} = \frac{1}{a^2} \sum_{\mu=1}^3 [2 \delta_{x,y} - \delta_{x+\hat{\mu}, y} - \delta_{x-\hat{\mu}, y}].
\]

The lattice FP operator is
\[
\mathcal{M}_{xy}^{ab} = \delta^{ab} \Delta_{xy} + g f^{abc} \langle \bar{A}_\mu^c(x) \rangle \partial_\mu^{\mathrm{lat}} \delta_{xy},
\]
where $\langle \bar{A}_\mu^c \rangle$ is the background field (smooth over lattice scale).

The lattice zeta function is
\[
\zeta_s(s) = \sum_{\mathbf{k} \in \text{BZ}} \left[4 a^{-2} \sin^2\left(\frac{a k_\mu}{2}\right) + O(a^0)\right]^{-s},
\]
summed over the Brillouin zone.

\subsection{Continuum limit and finiteness}

As $a \to 0$, the lattice spectrum recovers the continuum spectrum:
\[
4 a^{-2} \sin^2(ak_\mu/2) \to k^2.
\]

The one-loop determinant in the continuum limit is
\[
\ln \det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}^{\text{cont}} = \mathrm{Tr} \ln \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}^{\text{cont}},
\]
which is computed via zeta-function regularisation as in §II.3.

\textbf{Key result}: the lattice regularisation provides a \emph{natural UV
cutoff} at $\Lambda_{\rm UV} = \pi/a_{\rm BCC}$ (the shell-mode Debye scale).
The one-loop ghost integral
\[
\int_0^\infty \mathrm{d}k \, k^{d-1} \ln(k^2 + m_{\rm eff}^2)
\]
is manifestly finite when integrated up to $k_{\rm max} = \Lambda_{\rm UV}$,
so the dimensional regularisation is \emph{not merely formal but arises naturally
from the lattice UV structure}.

After this integration and subtraction of the standard QFT counterterm
(absorbing the $(d-4)^{-1}$ pole), the finite one-loop contribution is
\[
\ln \det \mathcal{M}_{\rm FP}\big|_{\text{finite}} = C_1 + C_2 \ln(\Lambda_{\rm UV}^2/\mu^2),
\]
where $C_1, C_2$ are scheme-dependent constants of order unity.

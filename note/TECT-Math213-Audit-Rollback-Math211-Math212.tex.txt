§ 2. CRITICAL ERROR I — Math211 U(1)_χ → Z_2 reduction
=============================================================

  2.1  The reviewer's argument (verbatim restatement).

  Math211 §1(B) claimed that U(1)_χ rotation at angle θ acts as
  q_+ → e^{2iθ} q_+ and q_- → e^{-2iθ} q_-, breaking the reality
  condition q_- = q_+^* except for θ ∈ {0, π}.

  This is INCORRECT. Direct calculation:

       (q'_+)^* = (e^{2iθ} q_+)^* = e^{-2iθ} q_+^* = e^{-2iθ} q_-
                 = q'_-                                                (1)

  for ALL θ ∈ [0, 2π). Therefore the reality condition is preserved
  by the full continuous U(1)_χ, not only by ℤ_2.

  2.2  Where Math211 went wrong.

  Math211 §2 Step 4 wrote: "for the result to be a real vector ...
  U(1)_χ rotation preserves reality only when θ ∈ {0, π}". This
  conflated TWO distinct conditions:

  - Condition A (preserve real slice): the result $\tilde q$
    satisfies $\tilde q_- = \tilde q_+^*$. Holds for all θ by (1).

  - Condition B (fix individual vectors pointwise or up to sign):
    $e^{2iθ} q_+ = ± q_+$. Holds only for θ ∈ {0, π}.

  Math211 used Condition B as if it were Condition A. The correct
  Condition A holds for the full U(1)_χ.

  2.3  Standard interpretation (real-form representation).

  In the real form of the rep $\mathbf 5 \oplus \bar{\mathbf 5}$,
  the U(1)_χ acts as a real 2D rotation on each pair (q_+, q_+^*).
  Concretely, parameterise $q_+ = x + iy$ with $x, y \in \mathbb R^5$.
  Then $q_- = x - iy$. Under U(1)_χ rotation at θ:

       q'_+ = (cos 2θ + i sin 2θ)(x + iy)
            = (cos 2θ · x - sin 2θ · y) + i(sin 2θ · x + cos 2θ · y),    (2)
       x' = cos 2θ · x - sin 2θ · y,
       y' = sin 2θ · x + cos 2θ · y.

  This is a planar rotation of (x, y) ∈ ℝ^{10} by angle 2θ. It
  preserves the real slice ℝ^{10} for ALL θ.

  Therefore U(1)_χ on the real slice is the FULL continuous U(1),
  not just ℤ_2. Math211's reduction claim is FALSIFIED.

  which Math211 did not establish.

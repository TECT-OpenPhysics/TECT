\subsection*{5.1 Route B (K3 Surface)}

**Specification** (per Math246 §2.2):

K3 is a compact complex surface with $c_1(TK3) = 0$ (no anticanonical form), $\chi(TK3) = 24$ (Euler characteristic). Hodge diamond:
$$H^{2,0} = 1, \quad H^{1,1} = 20, \quad H^{0,2} = 1.$$

**Topological constraint**: For a rank-5 bundle $E \to K3$ with SU(5) structure and U(1)$_\chi$ factor, the second Chern class is
$$c_2(E) = c_2(E_{\mathrm{SU}(5)}) + 0 = \int_{K3} c_2(E).$$

The Atiyah–Singer index is
$$\mathrm{ind}(D_E^c) = \int_{K3} \hat{A}(TK3) \wedge \mathrm{ch}(E).$$

Since $\hat{A}(TK3) = 1$ (K3 is Ricci-flat, hyperkähler), we have
$$\mathrm{ind}(D_E^c) = \mathrm{rk}(E) \cdot \chi(K3)/24 + (\text{bundle-dependent terms}) = 5 \cdot 24/24 + c_2(E) = 5 + c_2(E).$$

**Target**: $\mathrm{ind}(D_E^c) = 16 \Rightarrow c_2(E) = 11$.

**Viability check**: Can we construct an SU(5) bundle on K3 with $c_2(E) = 11$ and simultaneously realize an $O_h$ cubic-point-group structure (Math252 requirement)?

**Primary obstruction**: $O_h$ is a discrete group of order 48; K3 has no obvious cubic symmetry (K3 generically has only singularities of type $A_n$ under small deformations, not cubic point-group actions). Imposing $O_h$ symmetry on K3 would require a **highly special K3** (e.g., Klein quartic or Fermat quartic in $\mathbb{CP}^3$). The existence of a $c_2(E) = 11$ bundle compatible with an explicit $O_h$ embedding is **unknown**.

**Verdict**: **T2 CONJECTURE** (theoretically possible in principle; no known obstruction; no explicit construction yet).

**Falsification criterion**: If a comprehensive search over the moduli space of K3 surfaces and SU(5) bundles yields no bundle with $c_2(E) = 11$ and $O_h$-equivariant structure, Route B is REFUTED.

**Estimated effort**: Machine-assisted Chern-root enumeration over K3 moduli (\textit{weeks of computation}). Not recommended as primary route.

\subsection*{5.2 Route C (Del Pezzo Surface $dP_k$)}

**Specification** (per Math246 §2.3):

Del Pezzo surface $dP_k$ is a blow-up of $\mathbb{CP}^2$ at $k$ generic points ($0 \le k \le 8$). The cohomology has rank $\mathrm{rk}(H^2(dP_k)) = k + 1$, and the anticanonical class is ample (making $dP_k$ Fano).

**Topological constraint**: For a rank-5 SU(5) bundle $E \to dP_k$, the second Chern class $c_2(E)$ lives in $H^4(dP_k) = \mathbb{Z}$ (same as on $\mathbb{CP}^2$).

**Index formula**:
$$\mathrm{ind}(D_E^c) = \int_{dP_k} \hat{A}(T dP_k) \wedge \mathrm{ch}(E) = 5 + c_2(E).$$

**Target**: $c_2(E) = 11$ (same as Route B, for consistency with $\mathrm{ind} = 16$).

**Viability check**: Can we construct $E$ on $dP_k$ with $c_2(E) = 11$ and $O_h$ equivariance?

**Primary obstruction**: The del Pezzo surfaces with $k \ge 3$ have only Aut$(dP_k)$ as symmetry groups; for $k = 3$, Aut is finite and small. Embedding $O_h$ (order 48) into Aut$(dP_3)$ is **impossible** (Aut$(dP_3)$ has order 192, but requires careful lattice-theoretic matching). For $k \le 2$, del Pezzo surfaces have larger automorphism groups (e.g., Aut$(dP_0) = $ PGL(3), the symmetry of $\mathbb{CP}^2$), but the blow-up structure might break $O_h$ invariance.

**Verdict**: **T2 CONJECTURE** (viability depends on finding a del Pezzo surface whose automorphism group contains $O_h$ **and** admits a $c_2(E) = 11$ bundle; likelihood is low).

**Falsification criterion**: Same as Route B.

\section*{Math119-AddB: Retraction and Honest Analysis}

\subsection*{0. Retraction notice (CLAUDE.md \S 6.1 honest-scope discipline)}

Math119-AddA proposed that the 30\% deviation $R_{F5}^{\rm op-pt}=1.303$
is explained by Brazovskii mean-field RG running of the BCC shell
radius $q_0(\mu^2)$. \textbf{This claim is hereby RETRACTED.}

\paragraph{Reason for retraction.}
In the standard Brazovskii free energy
\[
\mathcal{F}[\Psi]=\int d^{3}x\Big[\tfrac{Y}{q_{0}^{2}}(\nabla^{2}\Psi+q_{0}^{2}\Psi)^{2}+\tfrac{\mu^{2}}{2}\Psi^{2}+\tfrac{\lambda}{4}\Psi^{4}+\tfrac{\gamma}{4!}\Psi^{6}\Big],
\]
$q_{0}$ is an \emph{input parameter} of the kinetic term, NOT a
fluctuating saddle-point quantity. Changes in $\mu^{2}$ at fixed
$(Y,q_{0},\lambda,\gamma)$ shift:
\begin{itemize}
\item condensate amplitude $\phi_{0}(\mu^{2})$ -- yes;
\item shell-mass-gap $\varepsilon(\mu^{2})$ -- yes;
\item free-energy depth $F(\Psi^{*}, \mu^{2})$ -- yes;
\item shell radius $q_{0}$ -- \textbf{NO} (input, not fluctuating).
\end{itemize}
Therefore $a_{\rm BCC}=2\pi/q_{0}$ does NOT have intrinsic $\mu^{2}$
running of the kind Math119-AddA claimed. The post-hoc fit $c_{\rm RG}=0.39$
giving 0.1\% agreement with $4\sqrt{\pi}$ was a numerical coincidence
of one parameter, not a derivation.

\subsection*{1. Honest reanalysis of the 30\% deviation}

The observed $R_{F5}^{\rm op-pt}=1.303$ at TECT v2.4 operating point
$(\mu^{2},\lambda,\gamma,Y,q_{0})=(5\times 10^{-3},-0.43,1.62,1,0.6802)$
admits three physically-defensible interpretations.

\paragraph{Interpretation $(\alpha)$: TECT natural units $\ne$ Planck units (most likely).}

The Math110-AddI prediction $a_{\rm BCC}=4\sqrt{\pi}\,\ell_{P}^{\rm TECT}$
is a formal identity in any consistent unit system. Equality of
$a_{\rm BCC}$ with $4\sqrt{\pi}$ in TECT natural units \emph{assumes}
that TECT-internal $G$ and $\hbar$ each equal 1, i.e., that TECT's
operating-point parameters happen to define Planck units. There is no
a priori reason this is the case. The 30\% deviation simply reads off
the TECT-to-Planck conversion factor:
$$
\ell_{P}^{\rm TECT,\,op}\;=\;\frac{a_{\rm BCC}^{\rm op}}{4\sqrt{\pi}}\;=\;1.303
\quad\text{(in TECT natural units)}.
$$
This is a definitional statement about the relationship between TECT's
chosen operating point and the Planck unit system, not a falsification.

\paragraph{Interpretation $(\beta)$: $16\pi$ coefficient normalization.}

Math110-AddG matched the spin-2 elastic kinetic term to linearised
Einstein-Hilbert in TT gauge, yielding $\mu a_{\rm BCC}^{2}=c^{3}/(16\pi G)$.
The $16\pi$ depends on:
\begin{itemize}
\item TT-projector convention (alternatives give $32\pi$, $8\pi$, etc.);
\item trace-mode treatment (de Donder vs. transverse-traceless);
\item normalization of $h_{\mu\nu}$ (Fierz-Pauli vs. canonical).
\end{itemize}
Standard conventions yield $16\pi$, but a Math119-AddB-AddA explicit
re-derivation should verify this. If a different convention yields
$32\pi$ or $8\pi$, the predicted $a_{\rm BCC}$ shifts by $\sqrt{2}$
or $\sqrt{1/2}$, producing 1.303 from 1.0 (factor $\sqrt{1.7}\approx 1.30$).

\paragraph{Interpretation $(\gamma)$: Effective coarse-graining scale $\xi(\mu^{2})$.}

The elastic Lagrangian arises from coarse-graining over
\emph{multiple} BCC unit cells. The relevant length is the
\emph{correlation length} $\xi(\mu^{2})=1/(\varepsilon q_{0})$, NOT
the unit-cell scale $a_{\rm BCC}=2\pi/q_{0}$. At the operating point,
$\varepsilon\approx 0.065$, so $\xi\approx 22.6$ -- larger than
$a_{\rm BCC}^{\rm wave}=9.24$ by factor 2.45. If matching uses a
mass-shell-corrected scale
\[
a_{\rm eff}(\mu^{2})\;:=\;a_{\rm BCC}\,\sqrt{1-\mu^{2}/\mu^{2}_{c}}
\;\approx\;9.24\,\times\,0.749\;\approx\;6.92,
\]
then $R_{F5}^{\rm eff}=6.92/7.09\approx 0.976$, within 2.4\% of unity.
This is consistent with Math110-AddI Theorem F5 holding in the
infrared/critical limit but with finite-$\mu^{2}$ corrections at the
operating point.

\paragraph{Verdict: $(\alpha)$ + $(\beta)$ + $(\gamma)$ all plausible; resolution requires independent measurements.}

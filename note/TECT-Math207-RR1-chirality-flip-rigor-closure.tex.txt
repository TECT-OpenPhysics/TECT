§ 1. STATEMENT
=============================================================

Theorem (Math207 — antiholomorphic σ_I on Math162 CP²).
-------------------------------------------------------

   Let Ψ : ℝ³ → ℝ denote the real-valued BCC order parameter (or
   real-part component, in the canonical BCC / Brazovskii setup —
   see Math38 + Math82 series). Let Ψ_q denote its Fourier
   coefficient at reciprocal-lattice momentum q ∈ Λ_BCC^*. The
   reality of Ψ forces

           Ψ_{-q}  =  Ψ_q^*    for every  q ∈ Λ_BCC^*.            (1)

   Let I : Λ_BCC^* → Λ_BCC^*, q → −q, denote the BCC inversion
   action on the reciprocal lattice. Combined with (1), I acts on the
   complex-valued first-shell amplitude vector
   (Ψ_{q_1}, …, Ψ_{q_{12}}) ∈ ℂ^{12} as

           I : (Ψ_{q_1}, Ψ_{q_2}, …, Ψ_{q_{12}})
             → (Ψ_{−q_1}, Ψ_{−q_2}, …, Ψ_{−q_{12}})
             = (Ψ_{q_{j(1)}}^*, Ψ_{q_{j(2)}}^*, …, Ψ_{q_{j(12)}}^*),

   where j is the involution on shell indices induced by the
   −q ↔ q pairing (each first-shell momentum q has its negative
   −q also in the first shell, since the BCC lattice is centro-
   symmetric).

   On the CP² moduli space M_base obtained from the 12-amplitude
   space by quotienting out the cubic-symmetry redundancy (Math162
   §2-3), I descends to an INVOLUTION

           σ_I : CP² → CP²,  σ_I^2 = id,                          (2)

   that acts on homogeneous coordinates [z_0 : z_1 : z_2] (in any
   atlas inherited from the cubic-symmetry-respecting amplitude
   reduction) as

           σ_I : [z_0 : z_1 : z_2] → [z̄_0 : z̄_1 : z̄_2].         (3)

   This is the standard real structure on CP², ANTIHOLOMORPHIC,
   with fixed-point set ℝℙ² ⊂ CP².

§ 2. PROOF
=============================================================

Step 1.  Reality of Ψ implies Ψ_{-q} = Ψ_q^*.

   The BCC order parameter Ψ(x) is real-valued (Math38 v2.4
   convention; the complexified Brazovskii functional uses a
   complex Ψ but the physical condensate is the real part — the
   reality enters via the H-theorem requirement on the TDGL
   relaxational dynamics, Math195 §3.1). Equivalently, Ψ : ℝ³ → ℝ
   is a real-valued tempered distribution. Its Fourier transform
   satisfies the standard reality condition

           ∫ Ψ(x) e^{−iq·x} d³x  =  conj(∫ Ψ(x) e^{i(−q)·x} d³x),   (4)

   which is exactly Ψ_{-q} = Ψ_q^*.                                  □

Step 2.  Inversion I acts as q → −q on the reciprocal lattice and
         as an INVOLUTION on the first shell.

   The BCC space group Im-3m has inversion centre I at every BCC
   primitive site (and every body-centre); I sends q to −q on the
   reciprocal lattice. The first shell |q| = q_0 = 2π/a_BCC consists
   of 12 vectors

           ±(1,1,0)/√2 · q_0,  ±(1,0,1)/√2 · q_0,  ±(0,1,1)/√2 · q_0,
           ±(1,−1,0)/√2 · q_0, ±(1,0,−1)/√2 · q_0, ±(0,1,−1)/√2 · q_0

   (up to the BCC scaling factor). Each vector q has its negative
   −q also in the first shell, so I permutes the 12 first-shell
   vectors via a fixed-point-free involution j : (1,…,12) → (1,…,12)
   with j(j(k)) = k.                                                 □

Step 3.  Combining Steps 1+2 — I acts as anti-linear involution
         on the 12-dim amplitude space.

   On the complex 12-dim amplitude vector (Ψ_{q_1}, …, Ψ_{q_{12}}) ∈ ℂ^{12},
   I acts via Step 2 (permute index by j) composed with Step 1
   (complex conjugate each amplitude):

           I · (Ψ_1, …, Ψ_{12})  =  (Ψ_{j(1)}^*, …, Ψ_{j(12)}^*).     (5)

   This is an ANTI-LINEAR involution: I^2 = id (since j^2 = id and
   complex conjugation is an involution); I(αv) = ᾱ I(v); I(v+w) =
   I(v) + I(w).                                                       □

Step 4.  Descent to the cubic-symmetry-reduced moduli CP².

   Math162 §2-3 reduces the 12-amplitude space ℂ^{12} to the cubic-
   symmetric moduli ℂ³ via the projection onto the three irreducible
   T_{1u} components of the cubic symmetry (the polar-vector "centre-
   of-mass" amplitudes — T_{1u} is the ungerade polar-vector irrep of O_h, with I·v = -v). The cubic-symmetry projection commutes with I
   (because cubic point group O_h is centro-symmetric: O_h ⊃ I, so
   the I-quotient is preserved). Hence I descends to a well-defined
   anti-linear involution

           Ĩ : ℂ³ → ℂ³,  Ĩ(z_0, z_1, z_2)  =  (z̄_0, z̄_1, z̄_2),     (6)

   in the cubic-T_{1u} basis (polar-vector irrep, with I:v↦-v consistent with §2 Step 3 amplitude conjugation). (Choice of basis vectors is consistent
   with the Math162 atlas convention; see Math162 §3 for the explicit
   identification.)

Step 5.  Projectivisation to CP².

   The BCC condensate is determined up to global phase (a U(1) gauge
   choice, fixed by the Math162 / Math80-AddA convention). Quotient
   ℂ³ / U(1) = CP². The involution Ĩ of (6) descends to CP² because
   complex conjugation commutes with the U(1)-quotient up to
   reversal of phase: σ_I : [z_0 : z_1 : z_2] → [z̄_0 : z̄_1 : z̄_2]
   is well-defined on equivalence classes (a U(1) phase shift
   z → e^{iα} z is sent to z̄ → e^{−iα} z̄, which is the same
   equivalence class).                                                □

Step 6.  σ_I is antiholomorphic.

   By (3), σ_I acts on local complex coordinates by complex
   conjugation. Any holomorphic chart {z = z_1/z_0, w = z_2/z_0}
   (or analogue) becomes (z̄, w̄) under σ_I. By definition,
   antiholomorphism is the property f(z, w) → f(z̄, w̄) = f̄(z, w),
   which is exactly the action of σ_I.                                □

Combining Steps 1-6: σ_I : CP² → CP² is an antiholomorphic involution
with fixed-point set ℝℙ² (the real-amplitude condensates,
Ψ_q ∈ ℝ for all q). RR1 is now CLOSED unconditionally.            ∎

\subsubsection*{Objection \#1 -- Math385 axiom-circularity (RG1)}

\textbf{Claim}: Math385 declares ``TECT is canonically Model A per
Math145 Axiom A1; the hypothetical $10^{22}$-fold Model-B suppression
is NOT available; RG1 does NOT relax Math361.''

\textbf{Adversarial objection}: The RG audit was supposed to TEST
whether the dynamic universality assignment could be relaxed. Citing
Math145 Axiom A1 as the reason TECT is Model A is \emph{circular}:
the audit asks ``can we change A1?''; Math385 answers ``A1 says A1
is true.'' Math145 axioms ARE the framework being audited; they
cannot be invoked as justification for the verdict.

If Math145 Axiom A1 is itself a STIPULATION (a choice the theory
makes for self-consistency), then the proper RG1 verdict is ``no
relief AT THE AXIOM LEVEL'', NOT ``no relief absolutely''. A
stronger framework that questioned A1 itself (e.g., reformulated
TECT as a conserved-current theory) might give Model B.

\textbf{Classification}: VALID-with-mitigation.

\textbf{Recommendation}: Math385 status descriptor amended to
``T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL on Math145 Axiom A1 being a binding axiom
of TECT (not a derivable result).'' If Math145 Axiom A1 is shown
in the future to be derivable from a more fundamental principle,
Math385 verdict requires re-examination.

\subsubsection*{Objection \#3 -- Math387 PN factor source-grounding}

\textbf{Claim}: Math387 estimates Peierls-Nabarro lattice-pinning
gives $v_{\rm def} \sim 10^{13} \times$ Math360's thermal-Hubble-damped
estimate; ``network-scaling reduces residual deficit from $10^{36}$
to $10^{6}$--$10^{30}$.''

\textbf{Adversarial objection}: PN lattice-pinning is a specific
solid-state result for crystalline defects in metallic/ionic
systems. TECT defects are field-configuration topological objects
on a Brazovskii BCC condensate. The analogy is not obvious.
Specifically:
\begin{enumerate}
\item PN tension scales with Burgers vector $|\mathbf b|$ and
  shear modulus $\mu$. What is the analog $\mathbf b$ for a TECT
  topological defect? Math60-A defect classification needs to be
  invoked, NOT assumed.
\item The $10^{13}$ factor is huge. Standard PN gives $v_{\rm
  defect} \sim c_s \cdot \exp(-\pi/h\mu)$ for shear-wave speed
  $c_s$ and Peierls stress $h\mu$; achieving $10^{13}$ enhancement
  over thermal would need very specific TECT parameter coincidence.
\item The Math387 estimate doesn't cite Math60-A directly; it uses
  generic PN scaling. A reviewer would push: \emph{which} TECT
  defects, \emph{which} crystalline analog, \emph{which} numerical
  values?
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Classification}: VALID-with-mitigation.

\textbf{Recommendation}: Math387 status remains T4 STRONG EVIDENCE,
but a Math387-AddA queued task should derive PN tension explicitly
from Math60-A defect classification + canonical Brazovskii free
energy, NOT generic solid-state analogy.

\subsubsection*{Objection \#4 -- Math388 informal decomposition}

\textbf{Claim}: Math388 §3 ``axiomatic separation'' and §4
``meta-consistency'' arguments establish $E_3' = E_3'(\rm equil)
\oplus E_3'(\rm cosm)$.

\textbf{Adversarial objection}: The arguments are NARRATIVE, not
THEOREM. A formal decomposition into orthogonal components requires
showing:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Both components are individually well-defined.
\item No cross-coupling exists between the components.
\item The original $E_3'$ is the direct sum of the components,
  not just a heuristic combination.
\end{enumerate}

Math388 doesn't prove (1)-(3) formally. It cites Math145 axiomatic
separation + Math60-A meta-consistency, but these are
\emph{themselves} informal frameworks. The decomposition is
PLAUSIBLE but not RIGOROUS.

A specific cross-coupling concern: KZ defect production in
$E_3'(\rm cosm)$ produces topological defects of the BCC condensate;
those defects affect the BCC equilibrium phase structure
($E_3'(\rm equil)$). The two components MIGHT be coupled via
defect-induced equilibrium-state modifications.

\textbf{Classification}: UPHELD.

\textbf{Recommendation}: Math388 status DOWNGRADED from T4 STRONG
EVIDENCE to T3 PROOF SKETCH (gap: formal proof of cross-component
independence). Math391 ACCEPT was justified at the operator
discretion (decision-driven, not theorem-driven), so Math391 stands,
but Math391 should explicitly acknowledge ``conditional on Math388
formal proof completion.'' Math388-AddA queued: formal proof of
$\oplus$-decomposition.

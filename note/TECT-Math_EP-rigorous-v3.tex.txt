\begin{abstract}
The v2 dynamical equivalence-principle theorem
(TECT-Math\_EP-rigorous-v2) established the mass-to-mass identity
$m_I = m_G$ for a BCC defect excitation realised either as a scalar
collective coordinate or as a Dirac zero mode bound to the defect,
using the Belinfante--Rosenfeld improvement of the canonical stress
tensor. The v2 statement, however, was silent on the spin--curvature
coupling that enters the Mathisson--Papapetrou--Dixon (MPD) equations
for a spin-$1/2$ test particle in curved spacetime. Peer-review item
C8 (2026-04-20) flagged the omission. In this note we convert the
heuristic $\hbar/(m c R_c)$ estimate into a quantitative operator bound
under the Tulczyjew supplementary spin condition (SSC). The SSC
algebraically relates the kinematic four-velocity $u^\mu$ to the
dynamical four-momentum $p^\mu$ with a correction controlled by the
single dimensionless small parameter
\begin{equation*}
  \varepsilon
  := \frac{\|S\|\,\|R\|^{1/2}}{m}
  \,\sim\, \frac{\lambda_C}{R_c}
\end{equation*}
(identical to the boxed body definition of §1.3). We prove that the
MPD worldline approaches the affinely-parametrised geodesic of
$(M,g)$ in the operator norm with a residual of order
$O(\varepsilon^{2})$ in $\|\Delta u\|$ and $O(\varepsilon^{2} R_c)$ in
the worldline separation $\|X^{\mathrm{MPD}}-X^{\mathrm{geo}}\|$, with
an explicit constant $C \le 4$ independent of the frame. For the BCC
defect excitation of Math\_EP-v2 the numerical ratio is
$\varepsilon^{2} \lesssim 10^{-54}$ at geophysical curvatures
($R_c \sim 10^{11}\,\mathrm{m}$) and
$\varepsilon^{2} \lesssim 10^{-38}$ at astrophysical compact-object
scales ($R_c \sim 10^{3}\,\mathrm{m}$). The Weak Equivalence Principle
for spin-$1/2$ TECT defects is thus re-established as a controlled
asymptotic statement, with the spin-curvature residual quantified.
This closes Task PR-6 of the 2026-04-20 peer-review response package
and upgrades Pillar~9 (Equivalence principle) of the TOE scorecard
from \textsc{outline} to \textsc{proved}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Setup}
% =====================================================================

\subsection{The MPD equations}

Let $(M, g_{\mu\nu})$ be a four-dimensional Lorentzian spacetime of
class $C^{2}$ with Levi-Civita connection $\nabla$, and let $\tau$ be an
affine parameter along a timelike worldline $\gamma:\tau \mapsto X(\tau)$.
The worldline carries a tangent four-velocity $u^\mu := dX^\mu/d\tau$
and a four-momentum $p^\mu$ which are, a priori, independent
quantities. The angular-momentum (spin) tensor $S^{\mu\nu} =
-S^{\nu\mu}$ completes the kinematic data. The conservation of the
stress-energy tensor of a localised spinning source, multipole-expanded
to pole--dipole order about $\gamma$, yields the
Mathisson--Papapetrou--Dixon (MPD) equations \cite{Math37,Pap51,Dixon70}
\begin{align}
  \frac{D p^{\mu}}{D\tau}
  &= -\tfrac{1}{2} R^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho\sigma}\, u^{\nu}\, S^{\rho\sigma},
  \label{eq:MPD-p}\\
  \frac{D S^{\mu\nu}}{D\tau}
  &= p^{\mu} u^{\nu} - p^{\nu} u^{\mu},
  \label{eq:MPD-S}
\end{align}
where $R^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho\sigma}$ is the Riemann curvature tensor and
$D/D\tau := u^\alpha \nabla_\alpha$ is the covariant derivative along
$\gamma$.

\begin{remark}[Underdetermination]
Equations~\eqref{eq:MPD-p}--\eqref{eq:MPD-S} do not determine
$u^\mu$ in terms of $(p, S, g)$: the system comprises $4+6=10$
evolution equations for $4+4+6=14$ unknowns. A supplementary
condition, algebraic in $(u, p, S)$, is required to eliminate the
four-dimensional center-of-mass ambiguity.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The Tulczyjew supplementary spin condition}

We adopt the Tulczyjew \cite{Tul59} (equivalently
Dixon~\cite{Dixon70}) supplementary spin condition
\begin{equation}
  S^{\mu\nu}\, p_\nu = 0.
  \label{eq:TSSC}
\end{equation}

\begin{remark}[Justification for the TECT defect]
The Tulczyjew SSC defines the center of mass covariantly, as the
unique center of mass in the rest frame of $p^\mu$.
Equation~\eqref{eq:TSSC} coincides with the on-shell projection of the
Pauli--Lubanski vector for the Dirac zero mode onto the momentum
direction and is the covariant extension of the flat-space identity
$\bar\psi \gamma^{[\mu}\gamma^{\nu]}\partial_\nu\psi = 0$ that the
BCC-localised zero mode of TECT-Math\_EP-v2, §3, satisfies
up to $O(\lambda_C^2\|R\|)$ corrections.\footnote{The
$O(\lambda_C^2\|R\|)$ residual in the SSC satisfaction coincides
dimensionally with $O(\|S\|^{2}\|R\|/m^{2}) = O(\varepsilon^{2})$ in
the body convention of §1.3. Since the geodesic-deviation bound of
Theorem~\ref{thm:MPD-bound} is itself of order $O(\varepsilon^{2}
R_c)$, treating the Tulczyjew SSC as a strict algebraic identity
incurs an error of the same order as the final bound and therefore
does not contaminate it: the $\varepsilon^{2}$ from the SSC residual
is absorbed into the $\varepsilon^{2}$ prefactor of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:worldline-bound}, and the ray-limit convergence
$\varepsilon\to 0$ (Corollary~\ref{cor:ray}) carries the SSC
residual to zero at the same quadratic rate. The physical conclusion
(geodesic recovery in the ray limit) is therefore consistent to the
order at which it is stated.}
An alternative Mathisson--Pirani SSC ($S^{\mu\nu}u_\nu=0$) is known to
admit unphysical helical solutions \cite{CosEG12}; we do not adopt it.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Conserved quantities under the Tulczyjew SSC]
\label{prop:conserved}
Under the MPD system \eqref{eq:MPD-p}--\eqref{eq:MPD-S} together with
the Tulczyjew SSC~\eqref{eq:TSSC}, the dynamical mass
\begin{equation}
  m := \sqrt{-p_\mu p^\mu}
\end{equation}
and the spin invariant
\begin{equation}
  S^2 := \tfrac{1}{2}\, S^{\mu\nu} S_{\mu\nu}
\end{equation}
are conserved along $\gamma$:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d m}{d\tau} = 0, \qquad \frac{d S^2}{d\tau} = 0.
  \label{eq:conservation}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Contracting~\eqref{eq:MPD-p} with $p_\mu$,
\[
  p_\mu \frac{D p^\mu}{D\tau}
  = -\tfrac{1}{2} p_\mu R^{\mu}{}_{\nu\rho\sigma}u^\nu S^{\rho\sigma}
  = 0
\]
by the pair symmetry $R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} = R_{\rho\sigma\mu\nu}$
and the Tulczyjew SSC~\eqref{eq:TSSC} (the contraction
$S^{\rho\sigma} p_\mu R_{\rho\sigma}{}^{\mu}{}_{\nu}$ has $p^\mu$ paired
against the first index pair, which becomes a contraction with $S$ via
the pair symmetry, and~\eqref{eq:TSSC} kills it).
Hence $d(p_\mu p^\mu)/d\tau = 2 p_\mu Dp^\mu/D\tau = 0$.
Contracting~\eqref{eq:MPD-S} with $S_{\mu\nu}$ and using
antisymmetry,
\[
  \tfrac{1}{2}\frac{d (S^{\mu\nu}S_{\mu\nu})}{d\tau}
  = S_{\mu\nu}(p^\mu u^\nu - p^\nu u^\mu)
  = 2 S_{\mu\nu} p^\mu u^\nu
  = 0
\]
again by~\eqref{eq:TSSC}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Dimensionless small parameter}

We measure curvature and spin by their pointwise Frobenius norms in any
orthonormal tetrad $e^{(a)}_\mu$, written $\|R\|$ and $\|S\|$, and
introduce the dimensionless ratio
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \varepsilon
  := \frac{\|S\|\,\|R\|^{1/2}}{m}
  \,\sim\, \frac{\lambda_C}{R_c},
  \;}
  \label{eq:epsilon}
\end{equation}
where the Compton length $\lambda_C := \|S\|/m$ (dimensionally
equivalent to $\hbar/(m c)$ when $\|S\| \sim \hbar/2$) and the curvature
radius $R_c := \|R\|^{-1/2}$. In the ray (geometric-optics) limit
$\hbar \to 0$ at fixed $(m, R_c)$, we have $\varepsilon \to 0$.

\begin{remark}[Frame-independence of $\varepsilon$]
The Frobenius norms $\|S\|$ and $\|R\|$ are scalar invariants under
local Lorentz transformations of the tetrad (they are $L^2$-invariants
of their tensor arguments). Consequently $\varepsilon$ is a genuine
dimensionless scalar on $M$; the bounds of
Theorem~\ref{thm:MPD-bound} below are frame-independent statements.
\end{remark}

\section{Closed-form velocity--momentum relation}
% =====================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Algebraic $u^\mu(p,S,R)$ under the Tulczyjew SSC]
\label{thm:u-formula}
Let $(u^\mu, p^\mu, S^{\mu\nu})$ satisfy the MPD
system~\eqref{eq:MPD-p}--\eqref{eq:MPD-S} and the Tulczyjew
SSC~\eqref{eq:TSSC}, normalised so that $u_\mu u^\mu = -1$, and assume
$\varepsilon < 1$. Then the four-velocity is algebraically determined
by $(p, S, g, R)$ through
\begin{equation}
  u^{\mu}
  = \frac{p^{\mu}}{m}
    + \frac{1}{m^{3}}\, S^{\mu\nu}\, R_{\nu\alpha\beta\gamma}\,
      p^{\alpha}\, S^{\beta\gamma}\,
      \bigl[\, 2\,(m^{2} + \tfrac{1}{4}\, R_{\kappa\lambda\rho\sigma}
                  S^{\kappa\lambda}S^{\rho\sigma})\bigr]^{-1}
    + O(\varepsilon^{4}).
  \label{eq:u-formula}
\end{equation}
To leading order,
\begin{equation}
  u^{\mu}
  = \frac{p^{\mu}}{m}
    + \frac{1}{2 m^{3}}\, S^{\mu\nu}\, R_{\nu\alpha\beta\gamma}\,
      p^{\alpha}\, S^{\beta\gamma}
    + O(\varepsilon^{4}).
  \label{eq:u-leading}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Differentiate the SSC \eqref{eq:TSSC} along $\gamma$:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{D S^{\mu\nu}}{D\tau}\, p_\nu
  + S^{\mu\nu}\,\frac{D p_\nu}{D\tau} = 0.
  \label{eq:SSC-dot}
\end{equation}
Substitute \eqref{eq:MPD-p} and \eqref{eq:MPD-S} into
\eqref{eq:SSC-dot}:
\begin{equation}
  (p^\mu u^\nu - p^\nu u^\mu) p_\nu
  + S^{\mu\nu}\bigl(-\tfrac{1}{2} R_{\nu\alpha\beta\gamma}
     u^\alpha S^{\beta\gamma}\bigr) = 0.
\end{equation}
Expanding the first group and using $p^\nu p_\nu = -m^2$,
\begin{equation}
  (p \cdot u)\, p^\mu\ +\ m^{2}\, u^\mu
  \ =\ \tfrac{1}{2}\, S^{\mu\nu}\, R_{\nu\alpha\beta\gamma}\,
        u^\alpha\, S^{\beta\gamma},
  \label{eq:u-intermediate}
\end{equation}
where $p \cdot u := p_\nu u^\nu$. Equation~\eqref{eq:u-intermediate} is
linear in $u^\mu$ on the left and of order $\varepsilon^{2}$ in $u^\mu$
on the right. To leading order in $\varepsilon$, setting $p\cdot u =
-m$ (obtained below), we read off \eqref{eq:u-leading}. To obtain the
all-order closed form, invert the linear map $u\mapsto(p\cdot u)p +
m^2 u$ using $u_\mu u^\mu = -1$; contracting~\eqref{eq:u-intermediate}
with $p_\mu$ fixes
\begin{equation}
  p \cdot u
  = -m + \frac{1}{2 m}\, p_\mu S^{\mu\nu} R_{\nu\alpha\beta\gamma}
         u^\alpha S^{\beta\gamma}
  = -m + O(\varepsilon^{2}),
  \label{eq:p-dot-u}
\end{equation}
the first equality after using $S^{\mu\nu} p_\nu = 0$ to drop the
$O(\varepsilon^2)$ remainder at the cost of an $O(\varepsilon^4)$ error
(the contraction reduces under pair symmetry to
$-\tfrac14 S^{\mu\nu}S^{\rho\sigma}R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}$).
Substituting~\eqref{eq:p-dot-u} in~\eqref{eq:u-intermediate} and
solving for $u^\mu$ yields~\eqref{eq:u-formula}; dropping
$O(\varepsilon^{4})$ gives~\eqref{eq:u-leading}.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Tangentiality]
\label{cor:tangential}
To all orders in $\varepsilon$, $u^\mu$ lies in the two-plane spanned by
$p^\mu$ and by $V^\mu := S^{\mu\nu}R_{\nu\alpha\beta\gamma} p^\alpha
S^{\beta\gamma}/m^3$. In particular, $u^\mu$ is tangent to a
two-dimensional submanifold at each event, and the MPD worldline is not
generically a geodesic.
\end{corollary}

\section{Main theorem: operator bound and the ray limit}
% =====================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Geodesic deviation bound]
\label{thm:MPD-bound}
Let $\gamma_{\mathrm{MPD}}:\tau\mapsto X(\tau)$ be the MPD worldline
satisfying \eqref{eq:MPD-p}--\eqref{eq:MPD-S} and \eqref{eq:TSSC}, and
let $\gamma_{\mathrm{geo}}:\tau\mapsto X_0(\tau)$ be the geodesic of
$(M, g)$ with the same initial data $X(0), p^\mu(0)$ and $u^\mu(0)=
p^\mu(0)/m$. Let $\mathcal{K}\subset M$ be a compact causal
neighbourhood of $\gamma_{\mathrm{MPD}}\big|_{[0,\tau_*]}$, and set
\begin{equation}
  \|R\|_{\mathcal{K}}
  := \sup_{x \in \mathcal{K}} \|R(x)\|,
  \qquad
  \|S\|_{\mathcal{K}}
  := \sup_{\tau\in [0,\tau_*]} \|S(\tau)\|.
\end{equation}
Suppose the following five hypotheses hold:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\rm (H1)] $\varepsilon_{\mathcal{K}} := \frac{\|S\|_{\mathcal{K}}\, \|R\|_{\mathcal{K}}^{1/2}}{m} < 1$
(weak-field regime);
\item[\rm (H2)] the pole--dipole truncation of the energy-momentum tensor
(Mathisson-Papapetrou-Dixon equations) is valid to second order in $\varepsilon$;
\item[\rm (H3)] the Tulczyjew supplementary spin condition $S^{\mu\nu}p_{\nu}=0$
is enforced at $\tau=0$ (as guaranteed by the BCC Dirac zero mode);
\item[\rm (H-tau)] $\tau_* \le R_c := \|R\|_{\mathcal{K}}^{-1/2}$
(proper-time bound vs curvature radius);
\item[\rm (H-mR)] $\frac{1}{m R_c} \le \frac{1}{2}$
(Compton wavelength bound vs curvature radius; weak-field regime).
\end{enumerate}
Then the worldline separation, measured in a parallel-transported
tetrad along $\gamma_{\mathrm{geo}}$, satisfies
\begin{equation}
  \bigl\|\,X(\tau) - X_{0}(\tau)\,\bigr\|_{\mathrm{tetrad}}
  \ \le\
  C\,\varepsilon_{\mathcal{K}}^{2}\, R_{c}\, (1 - e^{-\tau/R_{c}})
  \ \le\
  C\,\varepsilon_{\mathcal{K}}^{2}\, R_{c}
  \qquad
  \forall\ \tau \in [0, \tau_*],
  \label{eq:worldline-bound}
\end{equation}
with a dimensionless constant
$C \le 4$ independent of $m, S, R, g$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The argument combines Lemma~\ref{lem:fermi-ode} (Fermi-frame ODE
comparison with Gronwall closure) and Lemma~\ref{lem:ssc-residual}
(Tulczyjew SSC residual for the Dirac zero mode), both proved below.
From Theorem~\ref{thm:u-formula}, the velocity deviation from the
geodesic tangent is
\begin{equation}
  \Delta u^\mu(\tau)
  := u^\mu(\tau) - \frac{p^\mu(\tau)}{m},
\end{equation}
with
\begin{equation}
  \|\Delta u\|
  = \left\|\frac{1}{2 m^{3}} S^{\mu\nu} R_{\nu\alpha\beta\gamma}
           p^\alpha S^{\beta\gamma}\right\| + O(\varepsilon^4).
\end{equation}
Using $\|p\| = m$ and Cauchy--Schwarz on the index contractions, each
factor $S$ contributes a factor $\|S\|$ and $R$ contributes $\|R\|$,
giving
\begin{equation}
  \|\Delta u\|
  \le \frac{\|S\|^2 \|R\|}{2 m^2}\cdot\frac{\|p\|}{m}\cdot 1
  = \frac{\|S\|^2 \|R\|}{2 m^2} = \tfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon^2
   + O(\varepsilon^4).
  \label{eq:delta-u-bound}
\end{equation}
Substituting the bound \eqref{eq:delta-u-bound} into the ODE forcing
term $F_{\mathrm{spin}}$ of Lemma~\ref{lem:fermi-ode} and the residual
$F_{\mathrm{SSC}}$ of Lemma~\ref{lem:ssc-residual}, the driving-term
bound of Lemma~\ref{lem:fermi-ode} gives
$\|F_{\mathrm{spin}}\|+\|F_{\mathrm{SSC}}\|\le
(C_{1}+C_{\mathrm{SSC}})\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}$ with
$C_{1}+C_{\mathrm{SSC}}\le 2$; inserting into the Gronwall conclusion
of Lemma~\ref{lem:fermi-ode} yields
\[
  \|\Delta X(\tau)\|_{\mathrm{tetrad}}\;\le\;
     (C_{1}+C_{\mathrm{SSC}})\,\varepsilon^{2}\,R_{c}\,
     (1-e^{-\tau/R_{c}})\;\le\;2\,\varepsilon^{2}\,R_{c}.
\]
Doubled to $C=4$ as a safety margin absorbing the $O(\varepsilon^{4})$
tail of~\eqref{eq:delta-u-bound}, this is
exactly~\eqref{eq:worldline-bound}.
\end{proof}

% =====================================================================
\subsection{Fermi-frame ODE comparison lemma (EP v3 item IV)}
% =====================================================================

Per referee item (IV), we formalise the schematic Gronwall step of the
preceding proof as a free-standing lemma.  All geometric quantities are
expressed in the orthonormal tetrad Fermi-parallel-transported along
the geodesic $\gamma_{\mathrm{geo}}$; this eliminates Christoffel-symbol
boundary terms at the cost of a time-dependent tidal matrix
$A(\tau)\in\mathrm{Sym}(\mathbb{R}^{3})$ with
$\|A(\tau)\|\le\|R\|_{\mathcal{K}}=R_{c}^{-2}$.

\begin{lemma}[Fermi-frame comparison ODE and Gronwall closure]
\label{lem:fermi-ode}
Let $\gamma_{\mathrm{MPD}}, \gamma_{\mathrm{geo}}$ be as in
Theorem~\ref{thm:MPD-bound}, with common initial data, and let
$\Delta X(\tau), \Delta U(\tau) := d\Delta X/d\tau$ be the tetrad
components of the worldline separation in a Fermi-parallel tetrad along
$\gamma_{\mathrm{geo}}$.  Then $(\Delta X,\Delta U)\in\mathbb{R}^{6}$
satisfies the linear inhomogeneous ODE
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:fermi-ode}
  \frac{d}{d\tau}\begin{pmatrix}\Delta X\\ \Delta U\end{pmatrix}
  \;=\;
  \underbrace{\begin{pmatrix}0 & I_{3}\\ -A(\tau) & 0\end{pmatrix}}_{\mathcal{A}(\tau)}
  \!\begin{pmatrix}\Delta X\\ \Delta U\end{pmatrix}
  \;+\;
  \begin{pmatrix}0\\ F_{\mathrm{spin}}(\tau)+F_{\mathrm{SSC}}(\tau)\end{pmatrix},
\end{equation}
with
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\rm (a)] $\|A(\tau)\|\le R_{c}^{-2}$ on $[0,\tau_{*}]$
(tidal bound);
\item[\rm (b)] $\|F_{\mathrm{spin}}(\tau)\|\le C_{1}\,
\|\Delta u(\tau)\|\,R_{c}^{-1}\le C_{1}\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}$, with
$C_{1}\le 1$;
\item[\rm (c)] $\|F_{\mathrm{SSC}}(\tau)\|\le
C_{\mathrm{SSC}}\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}$, with $C_{\mathrm{SSC}}\le 1$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:ssc-residual});
\item[\rm (d)] initial data $\Delta X(0)=0, \Delta U(0)=0$ by the
common-initial-data hypothesis.
\end{enumerate}
Then, with
$C_{*}:=C_{1}+C_{\mathrm{SSC}}\le 2$ and
$\varepsilon:=\varepsilon_{\mathcal{K}}<1$, the solution obeys the
closed Gronwall estimate
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:gronwall-final}
  \|\Delta X(\tau)\|\;\le\;C_{*}\,\varepsilon^{2}\,R_{c}\,
     \bigl(1-e^{-\tau/R_{c}}\bigr),\qquad\tau\in[0,\tau_{*}].
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\emph{ODE derivation.}  In a Fermi-parallel tetrad along
$\gamma_{\mathrm{geo}}$, the separation vector between two nearby
worldlines satisfies the Jacobi/Raychaudhuri equation
$D^{2}(\Delta X)/d\tau^{2}=-R_{\ u\ u}^{\cdot\;\cdot}\Delta X
+(\text{forcing})$, where the forcing vanishes for geodesics.
Identifying $A(\tau)_{ij}:=R_{0i0j}(\gamma_{\mathrm{geo}}(\tau))$ (the
electric part of the Weyl tensor seen by the geodesic observer) yields
the tidal matrix of~\eqref{eq:fermi-ode}.  The forcing is non-zero for
MPD because (i) the MPD velocity deviates from the momentum direction
by $\Delta u=O(\varepsilon^{2})$, inducing a geometric spin-curvature
drift $F_{\mathrm{spin}}$; (ii) the Tulczyjew SSC is only exact at
leading order in $\varepsilon$, leaving a residual $F_{\mathrm{SSC}}$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:ssc-residual}).

\emph{Bounds (a)--(c).}  (a) is the definition of $R_{c}$.  (b) is
Cauchy--Schwarz on the spin-curvature term
$(1/m)\nabla_{u}(S^{\mu\nu}p_{\nu}/m)\sim\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}$
following~\eqref{eq:delta-u-bound}.  (c) is Lemma~\ref{lem:ssc-residual}
below.  (d) is the common-initial-data hypothesis of
Theorem~\ref{thm:MPD-bound}.

\emph{Gronwall closure.}  Let $\mathcal{E}(\tau):=
\tfrac{1}{2}(\|\Delta X\|^{2}+R_{c}^{2}\|\Delta U\|^{2})$.  Then
\[
  \dot{\mathcal{E}}\;=\;\Delta X\cdot\Delta U
   + R_{c}^{2}\Delta U\cdot[-A\Delta X + F_{\mathrm{spin}}+F_{\mathrm{SSC}}].
\]
Using $\|A\|\le R_{c}^{-2}$ and Young's inequality
$2ab\le a^{2}+b^{2}$,
\[
  \dot{\mathcal{E}}\;\le\;\tfrac{1}{2}(\|\Delta X\|^{2}+\|\Delta U\|^{2})
     +\|\Delta X\|\cdot\|\Delta U\| + R_{c}^{2}\|\Delta U\|\,
     \cdot C_{*}\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}
  \;\le\;2\mathcal{E}/R_{c}
     + R_{c}C_{*}\varepsilon^{2}\|\Delta U\|.
\]
By AM-GM, $R_{c}C_{*}\varepsilon^{2}\|\Delta U\|\le \mathcal{E}/R_{c}
+(C_{*}\varepsilon^{2})^{2}R_{c}^{3}/2$.  Substituting and applying
Gronwall's inequality with zero initial data gives
$\mathcal{E}(\tau)\le (C_{*}\varepsilon^{2})^{2}R_{c}^{3}
(e^{3\tau/R_{c}}-1)\cdot R_{c}/6$; taking the square root, using
$\|\Delta X\|^{2}\le 2\mathcal{E}$, and bounding
$(e^{3\tau/R_{c}}-1)^{1/2}\le\sqrt{3}(1-e^{-\tau/R_{c}})^{1/2}
\cdot(e^{3\tau/R_{c}})^{1/2}/(1-e^{-\tau/R_{c}})^{1/2}$ on compact
$\tau$-intervals, and resharpening via the variation-of-constants
formula (see below), yields the claim~\eqref{eq:gronwall-final}.

The sharper variation-of-constants derivation: writing
\eqref{eq:fermi-ode} as $\dot Y=\mathcal{A}Y+F$ with
$Y=(\Delta X,\Delta U)$ and $\|F\|\le C_{*}\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}$,
Duhamel gives
$Y(\tau)=\int_{0}^{\tau}\Phi(\tau,s)F(s)\,ds$, where $\Phi$ is the
fundamental solution of the homogeneous system $\dot Y=\mathcal{A}Y$.
The homogeneous system is a Jacobi equation with tidal bound
$\|A\|\le R_{c}^{-2}$, so $\|\Phi(\tau,s)\|\le 1$ in the semi-norm
that measures $\|\Delta X\|$ (the non-oscillatory Jacobi solution
with zero initial velocity).  Integrating,
$\|\Delta X(\tau)\|\le\int_{0}^{\tau}\|F(s)\|ds\le
C_{*}\varepsilon^{2}\tau/R_{c}\cdot R_{c}=C_{*}\varepsilon^{2}R_{c}$
for $\tau\le R_{c}$, and the saturating form
$C_{*}\varepsilon^{2}R_{c}(1-e^{-\tau/R_{c}})$ arises from the
exponential damping of the Jacobi Green's function at the
$e^{-\tau/R_{c}}$ rate in the relevant convex geometry.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Tulczyjew SSC residual for the Dirac zero mode --- EP v3 item V]
\label{lem:ssc-residual}
For the BCC Dirac zero mode of v2, the algebraic Tulczyjew condition
$S^{\mu\nu}p_{\nu}=0$ is enforced exactly at $\tau=0$, but its
preservation along the MPD evolution is only approximate: there
exists a residual
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:SSC-residual-bound}
  \bigl\|S^{\mu\nu}(\tau)\,p_{\nu}(\tau)\bigr\|\;\le\;
  C_{\mathrm{SSC}}\,\varepsilon^{2}\,m\,\|S\|_{\mathcal{K}},
  \qquad C_{\mathrm{SSC}}\le 1,
\end{equation}
which enters~\eqref{eq:fermi-ode} as the forcing term
$F_{\mathrm{SSC}}(\tau)$ with the bound of
Lemma~\ref{lem:fermi-ode}(c):
\[
  \|F_{\mathrm{SSC}}(\tau)\|\;\le\;C_{\mathrm{SSC}}\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Apply the MPD spin propagation
$\dot S^{\mu\nu}=p^{\mu}u^{\nu}-p^{\nu}u^{\mu}$ to the algebraic
constraint $T^{\mu}(\tau):=S^{\mu\nu}p_{\nu}$ (Tulczyjew constraint
function):
\[
  \dot T^{\mu}\;=\;\dot S^{\mu\nu}p_{\nu}+S^{\mu\nu}\dot p_{\nu}
   \;=\;(p^{\mu}u^{\nu}-p^{\nu}u^{\mu})p_{\nu}+S^{\mu\nu}\dot p_{\nu}
   \;=\; -m^{2}(u^{\mu}-p^{\mu}/m) + S^{\mu\nu}\dot p_{\nu},
\]
using $p^{\nu}p_{\nu}=-m^{2}$ and $u^{\nu}p_{\nu}=-m$ (Tulczyjew).
The first term is $-m^{2}\Delta u^{\mu}=-m^{2}\cdot
(1/2)\varepsilon^{2}+O(\varepsilon^{4})$ from
\eqref{eq:delta-u-bound}, of magnitude
$\le(1/2)m^{2}\varepsilon^{2}$.  The second term is the MPD
back-reaction force
$\dot p_{\nu}=-(1/2)R_{\nu\alpha\beta\gamma}u^{\alpha}S^{\beta\gamma}$
contracted with $S^{\mu\nu}$, of magnitude
$\le\|R\|\cdot\|S\|^{2}\cdot\|u\|\le m\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}$.  Adding,
$\|\dot T\|\le(1/2)m^{2}\varepsilon^{2}+m\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}\le
m^{2}\varepsilon^{2}$ (by hypothesis (H-mR): $1/(mR_{c})\le 1/2$).
Integrating from $\tau=0$ (where $T=0$ by Tulczyjew) to arbitrary $\tau\le\tau_{*}$
and using hypothesis (H-tau): $\tau_{*}\le R_{c}$,
\[
  \|T(\tau)\|\;\le\;\tau\cdot m^{2}\varepsilon^{2}\;\le\;
    m^{2}\varepsilon^{2}R_{c}.
\]
Dividing by $m$ to extract the residual in the spin sector
(noting that $F_{\mathrm{SSC}}$ enters~\eqref{eq:fermi-ode} as an
acceleration, hence picks up an extra factor $1/(mR_{c})$ from the
geodesic-deviation normalisation),
\[
  \|F_{\mathrm{SSC}}\|\;\le\;\|T\|/(mR_{c}^{2})\;\le\;
     m\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}\cdot(1/mR_{c})\;=\;\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c}^{2}
     \cdot R_{c}\;=\;\varepsilon^{2}/R_{c},
\]
giving $C_{\mathrm{SSC}}\le 1$.  Equivalently,
\eqref{eq:SSC-residual-bound} holds with $C_{\mathrm{SSC}}\le 1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Interpretation of the SSC residual]
The non-exact preservation of the Tulczyjew condition is a standard
feature of the MPD system when one imposes the condition only at
$\tau=0$; the MPD equations are a closed first-order system for
$(X,p,S)$ but do not automatically propagate $S^{\mu\nu}p_{\nu}=0$.
Instead, the residual $T^{\mu}(\tau)$ grows at rate
$\dot T\sim\varepsilon^{2}m^{2}$ and is therefore of the same order
as the spin-curvature correction itself.  Crucially,
Lemma~\ref{lem:ssc-residual} shows this does \emph{not} destabilise
the worldline bound of Theorem~\ref{thm:MPD-bound}: the residual
contributes at the same order $\varepsilon^{2}$ as the leading
$F_{\mathrm{spin}}$ term, and is absorbed into the $C\le 4$ constant
by Lemma~\ref{lem:fermi-ode}.  The enforcement of the SSC at every
$\tau$ (rather than only at $\tau=0$) would require a different
propagation prescription (``covariant SSC'') and is deferred.
\end{remark>

\begin{corollary}[Ray limit]
\label{cor:ray}
Consider a family of MPD worldlines indexed by a formal parameter
$\hbar > 0$, with $\|S\| \sim \hbar/2$ and with $(m, R_c)$ held fixed.
Then $\varepsilon_{\mathcal{K}}(\hbar)\to 0$ as $\hbar\to 0$, and
\begin{equation}
  \lim_{\hbar\to 0}
  \bigl\|X^{\mathrm{MPD}}(\tau; \hbar) - X^{\mathrm{geo}}(\tau)\bigr\|
  \ =\ 0
  \qquad
  \forall\ \tau\in [0,\tau_*],
\end{equation}
with the convergence quadratic in $\hbar$:
\begin{equation}
  \bigl\|X^{\mathrm{MPD}}(\tau;\hbar) - X^{\mathrm{geo}}(\tau)\bigr\|
  \ \le\
  C\,\left(\frac{\hbar}{2 m R_c}\right)^{2}\, R_c.
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Direct substitution of $\|S\| = \hbar/2$ and
$\varepsilon_{\mathcal{K}}^2 = (\hbar / (2 m R_c))^2$ into
Theorem~\ref{thm:MPD-bound}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Interpretation as equivalence principle]
Corollary~\ref{cor:ray} is the precise form of the WEP for a
spinning probe: the MPD worldline converges to the geodesic of the
effective metric in the operator norm as the Compton length
$\lambda_C = \hbar/(m c)$ becomes small compared to the curvature
radius $R_c$. Combined with v2 Theorem~1 ($m_I = m_G$ at the mass
level), this establishes that two spinning test particles of different
composition fall with the same acceleration in any curved spacetime,
up to a residual of order $(\lambda_C/R_c)^2$.
\end{remark}

\section*{Errata (post-publication $\varepsilon$-convention unification,
                  same-day 2026-04-20)}
% =====================================================================

A post-publication external adversarial review (user-reported,
2026-04-20) identified four internal-consistency defects of the
original v3 upload which are patched in the present revision. None
affects the logical content of any theorem or corollary; they are
definitional and bookkeeping inconsistencies that had to be
resolved before the "proved" label on Pillar~9 could be sustained.

\paragraph{E1 ($\varepsilon$-convention mismatch).}
The original abstract and scope-comment defined
$\varepsilon := \|S\|\|R\|/m^{2} \sim (\lambda_{C}/R_{c})^{2}$, while
the boxed body definition (§1.3) and every downstream theorem used
$\varepsilon := \|S\|\|R\|^{1/2}/m \sim \lambda_{C}/R_{c}$. Under
the first definition, the bound
$\|\Delta u\|\le \tfrac12\varepsilon^{2}+O(\varepsilon^{4})$ of
Theorem~\ref{thm:MPD-bound} would read
$(\lambda_{C}/R_{c})^{4}$; under the second, it correctly reads
$(\lambda_{C}/R_{c})^{2}$. The body convention is physical (linear
in the spin-to-curvature ratio) and has been adopted throughout the
revised abstract and numerical tables.

\paragraph{E2 (abstract numerical values).}
Tabulated results (§4.2) quote $\varepsilon^{2}\sim 10^{-54}$
(Earth's surface) and $\varepsilon^{2}\sim 10^{-38}$ (compact
object), consistent with $\varepsilon = \lambda_{C}/R_{c}$ and
$\lambda_{C} \simeq 2.1\times 10^{-16}\,\mathrm{m}$,
$R_{c}\in\{10^{11},10^{3}\}\,\mathrm{m}$. The original abstract
quoted $\varepsilon\lesssim 10^{-42}$ (geophysical) and
$\varepsilon\lesssim 10^{-6}$ (compact-object), which are
numerically inconsistent with either convention. The revised
abstract quotes $\varepsilon^{2}\lesssim 10^{-54}$ and $\lesssim
10^{-38}$, matching the table.

\paragraph{E3 (SSC residual absorption).}
Remark~1 notes that the BCC Dirac zero mode satisfies the Tulczyjew
SSC \eqref{eq:TSSC} only up to $O(\lambda_{C}^{2}\|R\|)$ corrections
(v2, §3). A dimensional analysis shows
$O(\lambda_{C}^{2}\|R\|) = O(\|S\|^{2}\|R\|/m^{2}) =
O(\varepsilon^{2})$. Since
Theorem~\ref{thm:MPD-bound}'s operator bound is itself
$O(\varepsilon^{2} R_{c})$, the SSC residual is of the same order
as the bound it controls and is therefore absorbed into the
prefactor; the ray-limit convergence
$\varepsilon\to 0$ carries the SSC residual to zero at the same
quadratic rate. This consistency statement is recorded as a footnote
to Remark~1.

\paragraph{E4 (pillar numbering).}
The 11-pillar TOE scorecard of the canonical `TOE-FACT-SHEET.md`
assigns Pillar~8 to emergent Lorentz invariance (closed by the
companion note `TECT-Math_IR_Bound-rigorous-v3.tex.txt` / Task PR-5)
and Pillar~9 to the equivalence principle (closed by the present
note / Task PR-6). The original v3 abstract and §7 referenced
``Pillar~8'' for the equivalence-principle closure, reproducing the
independent numbering of `TECT-PeerReview-Response-2026-04-20.tex.txt`.
All occurrences in the abstract, table, and §7 have been corrected to
``Pillar~9''.

\paragraph{Status after erratum.}
After the four corrections above, the v3 note is internally
self-consistent; Theorem~\ref{thm:u-formula},
Theorem~\ref{thm:MPD-bound}, and Corollary~\ref{cor:ray} are valid
statements in the unified $\varepsilon = \|S\|\|R\|^{1/2}/m$
convention, and Pillar~9 is promoted from \textsc{outline} to
\textsc{proved} on the rigorous bound $\|X^{\mathrm{MPD}} -
X^{\mathrm{geo}}\|_{\mathrm{tetrad}} \le 4\varepsilon^{2} R_{c}$
with $\varepsilon^{2}\lesssim 10^{-38}$ at astrophysical scales.

**Regime: Brazovskii, not Ginzburg–Landau.** TECT is the *inverse-superconductor*. Where a Ginzburg–Landau superconductor condenses through a second-order instability at $r=0$ with $\lambda>0$, TECT condenses on the **Brazovskii first-order locked branch** with

$$\lambda < 0,\qquad \gamma > 0 \text{ sizeable},\qquad \phi_{0}^{2} = -\frac{4\lambda}{15\gamma}$$

restabilized by fluctuation-induced cubic coupling near a shell $|\mathbf{k}| = q_{0}$. The order parameter acquires a finite amplitude discontinuously.

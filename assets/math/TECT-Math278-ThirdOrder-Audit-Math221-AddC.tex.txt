\subsection*{5.4 SU(5) ρ ≠ 0 Theorem (§4)}

**Theorem statement** (§4.1, lines 236–257):
$$\mathrm{Tr}_{\mathcal{V}_{\rm shell}}[\rho(T^A) \rho(T^B)]
= \sum_{I=1}^{12} \langle I | T^A T^B | I \rangle.$$

For the $\mathbf{10}_{+1}$ subspace:
$$\mathrm{Tr}_{\mathbf{10}}[T^A T^B] = \frac{1}{2} C_2(\mathbf{10}) \delta^{AB},$$
where $C_2(\mathbf{10}) = 8$ (SU(5) table).

**Weighted average** (line 247–254):
$$\langle \mathrm{Tr}[T^A T^B] \rangle_{\rm shell}
= \frac{\#_{\mathbf{10}} \cdot 8 + \#_{\mathbf{5}} \cdot 12 + \#_{\mathbf{1}} \cdot 0}{\text{total}} \, \delta^{AB}.$$

With $\#_{\mathbf{10}} = 8$, $\#_{\mathbf{5}} = 4$, $\#_{\mathbf{1}} = 0$:
$$\langle \mathrm{Tr}[T^A T^B] \rangle_{\rm shell}
= \frac{8 \cdot 8 + 4 \cdot 12}{12} \delta^{AB} = \frac{64 + 48}{12} \delta^{AB}
= \frac{112}{12} \delta^{AB} = \frac{28}{3} \delta^{AB}.$$

**Arithmetic verification**:
- $8 \cdot 8 = 64$ ✓
- $4 \cdot 12 = 48$ ✓
- $64 + 48 = 112$ ✓
- $112 / 12 = 28/3 \approx 9.33$ ✓
- Simplification: $\gcd(112, 12) = 4$, so $112/12 = 28/3$ ✓

**Conclusion** (line 256):
$$\boxed{\rho_{\rm shell} \neq 0 \text{ for all } A \in \mathfrak{su}(5).}$$

**Self-containedness check**:
- **Theorem statement**: Clear and precise ✓
- **Proof strategy**: Use SU(5) Casimir eigenvalues and weight by
  representation content. Standard method ✓
- **Arithmetic**: Verified above ✓
- **Conclusion**: Non-zero weighted trace implies non-trivial representation ✓

**Self-containedness verdict**: SATISFIED ✓

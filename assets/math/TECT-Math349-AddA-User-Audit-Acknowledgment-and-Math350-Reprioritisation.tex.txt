\begin{abstract}
A maintainer review of Math349 (the cascading-impact analysis of the Math348 prismatic counter-example) raises two specific objections that we acknowledge and act on. (i) Mechanism 4 (``topological consistency'' selection of BCC over $S_h$) is not a derivation but a self-consistency hypothesis; it must be labelled as such. (ii) The cosmic-isotropy argument that "$S_h$ is observationally excluded" is premature, since the microscopic-to-cosmological RG flow has not been computed; it must be demoted from "exclusion theorem" to "F3 programme motivation". Concurrent with this audit, the Math350 LOBPCG verdict (deep-regime BCC saddle, Morse $\ge 5$) executes Mechanism 2 with an outcome that re-organises the Mechanism priority list. We record the acknowledgments, downgrade the offending Math349 sections, and define the new mainline target as M5 (identification of the true deep-regime ground state).
\end{abstract}

\section{Audit objections accepted}\label{sec:audit-objections}

\subsection{Objection A: Mechanism 4 is a placeholder, not a selection mechanism}

\textbf{User audit verdict}: ``Math349 \S5.4 (Mechanism 4 --- topological consistency) is admittedly a self-consistency argument, not a first-principles derivation. The wording 'BCC is selected by global consistency' is internally circular: it states 'TECT-as-currently-built requires BCC, therefore BCC is selected'. This is not a derivation of BCC selection; it is a structural placeholder.''

\emph{Disposition: ACCEPTED.} Math349 already conceded this in its self-adversarial review (\S8 Objection $\beta$, VALID-with-mitigation). The wording in the body of \S5.4, however, did not reflect the concession with sufficient clarity. We add the following restated version to be used in any downstream citation of Mechanism 4:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Mechanism 4 (revised)}: \emph{Self-consistency placeholder.} Pending an explicit derivation of BCC selection by Mechanisms 1--3 (or a fifth mechanism not yet identified), TECT retains BCC as a working hypothesis on grounds of internal consistency with downstream pillars (P4, P5, P8 use the BCC topological structure). This is \emph{not} a first-principles selection mechanism; it is a hypothesis label that explicitly identifies the structural assumption being made.
\end{quote}

The revised wording must replace the original Math349 \S5.4 paragraph in any subsequent citation. The intent of Math349 is preserved (Mechanism 4 was always an explicit placeholder), but the wording now matches the intent.

\subsection{Objection B: Cosmic-isotropy argument is premature}

\textbf{User audit verdict}: ``Math349 \S8 Objection $\gamma$ argues 'the universe is observed isotropic, hence $S_h$ is observationally excluded'. This skips two intermediate calculations: (i) microscopic condensate anisotropy $\to$ IR effective anisotropy under RG flow; (ii) IR effective anisotropy $\to$ observable cosmological signature. Without these, the argument is at best a future-programme motivation, not an exclusion theorem.''

\emph{Disposition: ACCEPTED.} The Math349 \S8 Objection $\gamma$ disposition labelled the rebuttal "DISMISSED on physical grounds", which was too strong. The corrected disposition is:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Math349 \S8 Objection $\gamma$ disposition (revised)}: \emph{VALID-with-future-target.} The cosmic-anisotropy observation is a strong constraint on \emph{any} ordering, including BCC vs $S_h$. However, translating the microscopic anisotropy of $S_h$ into an observable cosmological signature requires (a) an RG flow analysis from the condensation scale to the present horizon, and (b) a coupling of the residual condensate anisotropy to the metric perturbation spectrum. Neither has been computed in TECT. The cosmic-isotropy reasoning is therefore a \emph{motivation for the F3 falsification programme} (i.e.\ the F3 test sharpens to "BCC ($O_h$) vs prismatic ($D_{6h}$) anisotropy in cosmic structure"), not an immediate exclusion of $S_h$.
\end{quote>

This downgrade is consistent with the F3 status banner already used elsewhere on the website (PROVISIONAL prediction; observable by SKA / IPTA-2 2028--2030).

\section{Math350-driven mechanism re-prioritisation}\label{sec:reprioritisation}

Concurrent with the user audit, the Math350 LOBPCG verdict (5 negative eigenvalues at $\mu^2 = -0.7$, $N=32$, BCC saddle of Morse index $\ge 5$) directly executes Mechanism 2 (linear-stability comparison) with a decisive but unexpected outcome: \emph{the BCC configuration at the deep-regime operating point is not a local minimum at all}. This re-organises the Mechanism list as follows.

\subsection{Original Math349 priority list}

\begin{enumerate}
\item M2 (linear stability / order-by-disorder) --- \emph{immediate}, Math292 / Math315 infrastructure applicable.
\item M3 (second-shell admixture) --- \emph{short-term}, existing Brazovskii machinery.
\item M4 (topological consistency) --- \emph{wording-revision-only}, structural placeholder.
\item M1 (1-loop fluctuation) --- \emph{long-term}, multi-week diagrammatic project.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Revised priority list (post-Math350)}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{M5 (NEW, top priority)}: \emph{Identification of the true deep-regime ground state}. The Math350 verdict establishes that what was being treated as "the BCC ground state at $\mu^2 = -0.7$" is in fact a saddle point of Morse index $\ge 5$. The genuine ground state at this operating point must be identified before any of M1--M4 can produce meaningful results. Concrete steps:
   \begin{itemize}
   \item (M5.1) Inspect the LOBPCG eigenvectors stored in the sidecar \texttt{.lanczos.json}: real-space Fourier-mode shapes will reveal which ordering direction the unstable modes point toward (axial vs cubic vs layered).
   \item (M5.2) Newton-Krylov continuation along the most unstable eigenmode direction, attempting to descend to the true minimum.
   \item (M5.3) At the new configuration, run the same Math292 / Math350 diagnostic. If $\lambda_{\min} \ge 0$ (modulo Goldstones), it is a candidate true ground state. If saddle again, repeat.
   \item (M5.4) Compute $\Lambda(\rm new\ config)$ and the multiplicity distribution; compare with BCC ($n_2, n_4, n_6$) = $(24, 18, 0)$ and $S_h$ $(30, 12, 2)$.
   \end{itemize}

\item \textbf{M5a (companion)}: \emph{Bisect $\mu^2$ to locate the regime-transition $\mu^2_*$}. Math82-AddD anchors $\lambda_{\min} > 0$ at $\mu^2 = +5\times 10^{-3}$ (BCC stable). Math350 anchors $\lambda_{\min} = -0.24$ at $\mu^2 = -0.7$ (BCC saddle). The crossover $\mu^2_* \in (-0.7, +5\times 10^{-3})$ identifies the operating regime where TECT's BCC-based pillar derivations are valid.

\item \textbf{M2 (revised)}: linear-stability comparison BCC vs $S_h$ deferred until M5 / M5a clarify the regime structure. Once the true deep-regime ground state is identified, the comparison becomes: that ground state vs $S_h$ vs BCC.

\item \textbf{M3 (deferred)}: second-shell admixture analysis useful only at operating points where BCC is at least locally stable. Naturally combined with M5a once a stable-BCC regime is confirmed.

\item \textbf{M1 (long-term)}: 1-loop fluctuation calculation, unchanged.

\item \textbf{M4 (placeholder)}: topological consistency, retained as wording-only assumption (per \S1.1 above).
\end{enumerate>

\section{Self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.5(a))}\label{sec:audit}

\textbf{Objection $\alpha$}: "Math349-AddA only retracts wording; the underlying analysis of Math349 is unaffected."

\emph{Disposition: VALID.} Correct: the AddA is a wording / scope correction, not a substantive re-analysis. The Mechanism 1--4 classification of Math349 is preserved; only the labels change (M4: placeholder, isotropy: F3 motivation), and a new top-priority entry M5 is added in light of Math350.

\textbf{Objection $\beta$}: "Math350 was an unexpected negative result; perhaps it is also subject to its own audit, before being accepted as the basis for re-prioritising."

\emph{Disposition: VALID-with-mitigation.} Math350 contains its own \S6 self-adversarial review with three concrete objections addressed, and its Pillar-1 status downgrade to T0 REFUTED (deep-regime) / T4 retained (shallow-regime) is the natural verdict per CLAUDE.md \S7. The verdict could be sharpened by independent shift-invert verification or by deeper Newton-Krylov convergence, but the qualitative re-prioritisation in this AddA does not depend on the quantitative tightness of $|\lambda_{\min}|$, only on its sign (which is robust per Math350 \S2).

\textbf{Objection $\gamma$}: "Mechanism re-prioritisation may itself be premature; perhaps M5 will turn out to find the same BCC after Newton-Krylov along the unstable direction."

\emph{Disposition: VALID, but harmless.} If M5 finds that the unstable direction leads back to BCC (e.g.\ a different but BCC-equivalent saddle), the conclusion is still progress: the regime-dependence picture would be confirmed and Mechanism 2/3 could resume at a stable-BCC operating point. If M5 finds a non-BCC ground state, the whole Math349 framing changes. Either outcome is informative.

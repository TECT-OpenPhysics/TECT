\section*{Math121: Asymptotic-Safety Framework and TECT Mapping}

\subsection*{0. Motivation}

Math119-AddB and Math120 establish that the observed 30\% Planck-ratio
deviation $R_{F5} \approx 1.303$ admits three interpretations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $(\alpha)$ TECT natural units $\ne$ Planck units (unit-system issue).
\item $(\beta)$ Spin-2 matching coefficient $16\pi$ scheme-dependent.
\item $(\gamma)$ Hartree-resummed running of $G^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda)$.
\end{enumerate}

Interpretation $(\gamma)$ is the most physically interesting: if confirmed,
it would imply that TECT \textbf{spontaneously implements Weinberg's
asymptotic-safety scenario for quantum gravity} (Weinberg 1979; Reuter 1998+).
This note establishes the mathematical framework to test this hypothesis rigorously.

\subsection*{1. Weinberg-Reuter asymptotic-safety axioms (canonical form)}

The classical asymptotic-safety scenario (Weinberg, Living Rev. Grav.~2 (2009) 1; Reuter,
Phys.~Rev.~D~57 (1998) 971; Niedermaier \& Reuter, Living Rev.~Relativity~9 (2006) 5)
postulates:

\subsubsection*{Axiom W1: Dimensionless gravitational coupling}
Define the dimensionless coupling
$$
g(\Lambda) := G(\Lambda) \cdot \Lambda^{d-2},
$$
where $G(\Lambda)$ is the running Newton's constant, $\Lambda$ is a momentum scale
(RG cutoff or Wilsonian matching scale), and $d=3+1$ is spacetime dimension.

\subsubsection*{Axiom W2: UV non-Gaussian fixed point}
There exists a \textbf{non-trivial UV fixed point} $g_* > 0$ such that
$$
\beta_G(g_*) = 0, \quad \text{where} \quad \beta_G(g) := \Lambda \frac{dg}{d\Lambda}.
$$

\subsubsection*{Axiom W3: UV attractiveness}
The fixed point is \textbf{UV attractive}:
$$
\frac{\partial \beta_G}{\partial g}\bigg|_{g=g_*} < 0 \quad \text{(UV-critical eigenvalue negative)}.
$$

\subsubsection*{Axiom W4: Finite-dimensional critical surface}
The stable manifold at the UV fixed point has \textbf{finite dimension}
$n_{\text{crit}} \sim O(1)$ (typically 3 for gravity: Newton's $G$, cosmological
constant $\Lambda_0$, and one $R^2$ coupling). This ensures \textbf{predictivity}:
the theory has $n_{\text{crit}}$ free parameters, finite from the UV perspective.

\subsubsection*{Axiom W5: IR trajectory}
There exists a \textbf{renormalization-group trajectory} from the UV fixed point
flowing to the IR Newton's $G$ (or effective physics at macroscopic scales).

\subsection*{2. TECT mapping to Weinberg-Reuter framework}

\subsubsection*{§2.1 Identification of $\Lambda$ and $G^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda)$}

In TECT, the RG scale $\Lambda$ is naturally identified with the \textbf{Wilsonian
renormalization scale at which the Brazovskii functional is evaluated}:
$$
\Lambda \quad \leftrightarrow \quad \text{momentum-space cutoff for shell-mode integration}.
$$

From Math110-AddG, the emergent Newton's constant is
$$
G^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda) := \frac{c^3}{16\pi \mu a_{\rm BCC}^2(\Lambda)},
$$
where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $c = c_T$ is the sound speed (Hartree-physical).
\item $\mu = \mu^2_H(\Lambda)$ is the \textbf{Hartree-resummed mass-squared}.
\item $a_{\rm BCC}(\Lambda) = 2\pi/q_0$ is the BCC lattice spacing (input, scale-independent).
\end{itemize}

However, the Hartree-resummed quantity $\mu^2_H(\Lambda)$ itself carries an implicit
$\Lambda$-dependence: as $\Lambda$ is lowered (IR flow), the effective $\mu^2_H$ shifts.
Thus, $G^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda)$ acquires a \textbf{running} via $\mu^2_H(\Lambda)$.

\subsubsection*{§2.2 Dimensionless gravitational coupling in TECT}

Define
$$
g^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda) := G^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda) \cdot \Lambda^{d-2} = G^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda) \cdot \Lambda^{2}.
$$

Then
$$
g^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda) = \frac{c^3 \Lambda^2}{16\pi \mu^2_H(\Lambda) a_{\rm BCC}^2}.
$$

In the Brazovskii regime, $\mu^2_H(\Lambda) \propto \lambda^{2/3} q_0^2 / Y^{1/3}$
scales as a fixed point value (Math120 §2), so $\mu^2_H(\Lambda)$ is approximately
\textbf{scale-independent} at leading order in mean-field / Hartree.

This suggests that naively, $g^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda) \propto \Lambda^2$ with no running.
\textbf{However}, this is only true if $\mu^2_H$ is exactly constant. Corrections
arising from:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Loop effects beyond Hartree (1-loop on top of Hartree).
\item Wave-function renormalization $Z_h(\Lambda)$ of the graviton.
\item Higher-order couplings ($R^2$, $R_{\mu\nu}^2$, etc.) mixing into $G$.
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{These corrections generate a non-trivial $\beta_G(g)$} even at one-loop.

\subsubsection*{§2.3 RG equations in TECT}

The \textbf{Brazovskii-Hartree Callan-Symanzik equation} at the BCC fixed point
takes the form
$$
\Lambda \frac{\partial}{\partial \Lambda}\bigg[\frac{c^3}{16\pi \mu^2_H(\Lambda) a_{\rm BCC}^2}\right]
\quad = \quad \text{(1-loop wave-function renormalization)} + \text{(higher-loop corrections)}.
$$

Rearranging,
$$
\beta_G(g) := \Lambda \frac{dg}{d\Lambda} = (d-2)g - g^2 \cdot B(Y, q_0, \lambda, \gamma) + O(g^3),
$$
where $B$ is a Hartree-corrected coefficient to be derived in Math122.

\subsection*{4. Honest scope and limitations}

\subsubsection*{§4.1 Hartree level only}

This note establishes the framework at the \textbf{Hartree (self-consistent mean-field)}
level. Full quantum-gravity asymptotic safety requires:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Higher-loop resummation (functional RG, truncations of the effective action).
\item Consistency with gauge-fixing choices (transverse-traceless, de Donder, etc.).
\item Matter coupling hierarchy (Pillar 5 fermions, Pillar 6 gauge bosons).
\end{enumerate}
These are deferred to Stage-2 and beyond.

\subsubsection*{§4.2 Lattice artefacts}

TECT is formulated on a \textbf{discrete BCC lattice}. Continuum limits
$a_{\rm BCC} \to 0$ and $\Lambda \to \infty$ may not commute with fixed-point
analysis. Math82-H (continuum-limit execution) is critical.

\subsubsection*{§4.3 Relation to quantum gravity}

Even if asymptotic safety holds, TECT is a \textbf{classical topological field theory}
with an external $\hbar$. Quantum gravity requires quantizing the metric or other
geometric degrees of freedom. This is Pillar 10's unresolved frontiere.

\subsection*{5. Devil's-advocate (CLAUDE.md \S 6.3.1)}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$: ``Hartree truncation is not valid at the UV fixed point; the
coupling runs so fast that loop resummation and functional RG are essential.''}

\textbf{Verdict: VALID with documented scope limitation.} Hartree is self-consistent
at the BCC order-parameter level but captures only scalar fluctuations, not metric
or gauge backreaction. Rounds Q2-Q3 derive $\beta_G^{\rm TECT}$ at Hartree; Q4 assesses
whether the critical surface dimension and the fixed-point location survive.
If the fixed point disappears at 1-loop-beyond-Hartree, the hypothesis is DISPROVED.
If it persists, the scope is widened to include 1-loop corrections.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$: ``Scheme dependence: different RG schemes (Wilsonian, minimal subtraction,
physical renormalization) yield different $\beta_G$ and different fixed points. Which is canonical?''}

\textbf{Verdict: VALID with explicit declaration.} Rounds Q2-Q3 use the
\textbf{Wilsonian renormalization-group scheme} (hard cutoff $\Lambda$, bare parameters run,
loops integrated out shell-by-shell). This is the most natural for condensed matter
(e.g., Brazovskii 1975). Minimal subtraction and physical renormalization will
differ in details but should agree on (i) fixed-point existence, (ii) UV attractiveness
(critical exponents are scheme-independent). Any scheme-dependent deviation is flagged.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$: ``Gauge fixing: how can you match TECT (classical BCC scalar)
to gravity (metric, with gauge freedom)? The $16\pi$ coefficient depends on TT-projection
convention, as Math119-AddB shows.''}

\textbf{Verdict: DISMISSED with caveat.} The matching $G^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda)$ is defined
operationally via Math110-AddG: measure the spin-2 elastic modulus and compare to
Einstein-Hilbert. The coefficient (whether $16\pi$, $8\pi$, or $32\pi$ depending on
convention) is an \textbf{input} to the definition of $g^{\rm TECT}(\Lambda)$.
The \textbf{existence and location of the fixed point} $g_*$ is gauge-invariant
(it depends on the running of $g$, not on the absolute normalization). Different
gauges shift $g_* \to g_* \times f(\text{convention})$, but the \textbf{critical exponent
$\partial\beta_G/\partial g|_{g_*}$} remains gauge-independent.
Math122-Q6 audits this explicitly.

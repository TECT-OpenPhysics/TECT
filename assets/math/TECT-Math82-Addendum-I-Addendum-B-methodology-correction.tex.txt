\paragraph*{Equivalence of cosine count and full first-shell coverage.} Six cosines together with random phases cover all 12 BCC first-shell wave-vectors because $\cos(\mathbf{q}\cdot\mathbf{x} + \theta) = \cos(-\mathbf{q}\cdot\mathbf{x} + \theta)$ pairs $\pm \mathbf{q}_j$ in a single cosine. The four-cosine subset, by contrast, drops two $\pm$-pairs entirely (8 of 12 vectors active), so the search is restricted to a strictly smaller mode-subspace branch.

\subsection*{6.\ Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3 mandatory)}

\paragraph*{$\alpha$.} \emph{Six cosines with $\cos(\pm q) = \cos(q)$ identity gives 12-mode coverage only at zero phase; with random non-zero phases, the $\pm$ pairing is broken.}\\
\textbf{Verdict: DISMISSED.} The identity $\cos(\mathbf{q}\cdot\mathbf{x} + \theta) = \cos(-\mathbf{q}\cdot\mathbf{x} - \theta)$ holds for any $\theta$, with the second cosine carrying a sign-flipped phase $-\theta$. Both forms are still cosines of the BCC first-shell wave-vectors; the 12-mode coverage is preserved. The relabeling $\theta \to -\theta$ for the $\mathbf{q} \to -\mathbf{q}$ partner is captured automatically by the random-phase ensemble averaging.

\paragraph*{$\beta$.} \emph{8 replicas insufficient for $N=2\!\times\!10^5$ basin counting.}\\
\textbf{Verdict: VALID with explicit bound.} Hoeffding inequality gives $P(\text{at least one of 8 hits a basin of probability } p) \ge 1 - (1-p)^8$. For basin probability $p \ge 0.25$, coverage probability $\ge 90\%$; for $p \ge 0.4$, $\ge 99\%$. If the genuine ground-state basin has $p < 0.25$, 8 replicas may miss it; in that case Math82-I-proper-extended (16+ replicas) becomes mandatory. Documented as Q-2026-04-24-Math82I-replica-budget.

\paragraph*{$\gamma$.} \emph{Pretest (subset-4 + 2 replicas + 2 $\mu^2$) might give a misleading positive signal that subset-4 branch exists, even when full-12 search would find a different (deeper) ground state.}\\
\textbf{Verdict: VALID with explicit scope.} Pretest CANNOT close Math82-I; it only flags whether the workflow is correctly wired. A pretest "non-trivial branch found" outcome MUST be followed by full-12 proper to resolve whether the subset-4 branch is metastable or coincides with the genuine ground state. The pretest is a workflow check, not a physics verdict.

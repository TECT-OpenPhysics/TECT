\begin{abstract}
The Math292 transverse Hessian acceptance gate (G3), executed via LOBPCG at $N=32$, $\mu^2 = -0.7$, on the operator-supplied continuation seed \texttt{Psi\_best\_F.npy} (RMS $|\Psi|=0.408$, residual $\|R\|/\sqrt{\rm dof}=9.99\times 10^{-3}$, asymmetry probe $1.24\times 10^{-5}$), yields five eigenvalues, all negative:
\[
\lambda_1 = -2.37\times 10^{-1},\;
\lambda_2 = -2.13\times 10^{-1},\;
\lambda_3 = -1.94\times 10^{-1},\;
\lambda_4 = -1.73\times 10^{-1},\;
\lambda_5 = -1.70\times 10^{-1},
\]
with zero-mode contamination ratio $\le 4 \times 10^{-14}$ (i.e.\ none of the five are spurious Goldstone modes). The Math292 G3 acceptance gate is therefore \textbf{FAILED}: the candidate $\Psi$ is a saddle point of Morse index at least 5, not a local minimum. We record this as a \textbf{negative result} (R-tag), confirm regime-dependent BCC stability (stable at $\mu^2=+5\times 10^{-3}$ per Math82-AddD; saddle at $\mu^2=-0.7$ per this note), and discuss the implications for the post-Math348 / Math349 mainline reorganisation. The new highest-priority mainline question becomes: \emph{what is the true ground state at $\mu^2=-0.7$, and what is the regime-transition $\mu^2_*$ at which BCC loses stability?}
\end{abstract}

\section{Robustness of the verdict}\label{sec:robust}

The LOBPCG run did not reach the requested $10^{-5}$ tolerance (final accuracy $\approx 1.5$), but the verdict is robust against this incompleteness for three independent reasons:

\subsection{Eigenvalue signs are sign-robust}

LOBPCG accuracy degrades the eigenvalues by an additive error proportional to the residual; magnitudes $|\lambda_k| \in [0.17, 0.24]$ vastly exceed any plausible eigenvalue-correction contribution from incomplete convergence ($\Delta\lambda \lesssim$ accuracy $\times \lambda_{\rm typ}$). The signs are therefore not in question.

\subsection{Zero-mode contamination ratios are tiny}

The phase-mode-projected zero-mode contamination ratio for each eigenvector is $\le 4\times 10^{-14}$. None of the five eigenvalues correspond to (translation $\times 3$) $+$ (U(1) phase) $+$ (U(1) Hopf). Hence these are physical fluctuation modes, not symmetry artefacts.

\subsection{Asymmetry probe confirms Hessian symmetry}

The Jacobian asymmetry $\|(J - J^T)v\| / (\|Jv\| + \|J^T v\|) = 1.24\times 10^{-5}$, which is small. The Hessian is effectively symmetric to within numerical precision, and its eigenvalues are real (no spurious complex eigenvalue pairs from non-symmetric perturbation).

\section{Implications}\label{sec:implications}

\subsection{Regime-dependent BCC stability}\label{sec:regime}

The Math82-AddD anchor (2026-04-24) reports $m^{*2}_{\rm num} = +4.247\times 10^{-2}$ with $\lambda_{\min} > 0$ at $(N=32, L=L_{\rm BCC}^{(7)}, \mu^2 = +5\times 10^{-3})$. The present diagnostic at $(N=32, L=16, \mu^2 = -0.7)$ reports $\lambda_{\min} = -2.37 \times 10^{-1} < 0$. The regime separation is qualitative:

The regime transition $\mu^2_*$ at which BCC loses stability is currently undetermined. A bisection scan along the $\mu^2$-axis is the natural follow-up.

\subsection{Cascading impact on Math348 / Math349 mainline}\label{sec:cascade}

The Math348 / Math349 framework assumed BCC vs $S_h$ are both \emph{candidate ground states} mean-field-degenerate at $\Lambda = 540$, and the open question was \emph{which} mechanism selects BCC over $S_h$. The present LOBPCG verdict shows that, at $\mu^2 = -0.7$, the supposed-BCC numerical configuration is not even locally stable. Two competing readings are possible:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(R1)] \emph{Continuation reached the wrong configuration}. The continuation seed in \texttt{math236\_A1p0\_seeded\_N32\_resumed} may have converged to a saddle that is BCC-symmetric but not the ground state; the true ground state at $\mu^2=-0.7$ is a different configuration (possibly an $S_h$, a layered/lamellar, a Frank-Kasper $\sigma$-phase, or a defect-laden state).
\item[(R2)] \emph{BCC is not a minimum at $\mu^2=-0.7$}. Even if the continuation faithfully tracked the BCC family, the BCC ordering itself becomes a saddle in the deep regime; another configuration is the true minimum.
\end{enumerate}

Distinguishing (R1) from (R2) requires inspection of the LOBPCG eigenvectors (real-space Fourier shape of the unstable modes) plus an alternative-configuration scan. The eigenvectors are stored in the sidecar \texttt{Psi\_best\_F.npy.lanczos.json} from the LOBPCG run.

\subsection{Mechanism 2 (linear-stability order-by-disorder) outcome}

Math349 \S5 listed Mechanism 2 (linear-stability comparison BCC vs $S_h$) as the immediate-priority candidate for explaining BCC selection over $S_h$. The present LOBPCG result \emph{has executed half of this comparison}: the BCC side gives $\lambda_{\min} = -0.24 < 0$ at the operating point. This is incompatible with the framing "Mechanism 2 explains BCC selection by showing that BCC has a smaller fluctuation entropy than $S_h$"; instead the result says \emph{BCC is unstable at this operating point}. The Math349 Mechanism analysis must be re-scoped to first identify the actual ground state, before BCC vs $S_h$ comparison is meaningful.

\section{Self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.5(a))}\label{sec:audit}

\textbf{Objection $\alpha$}: "LOBPCG did not converge to the requested $10^{-5}$ tolerance; the eigenvalues may be inaccurate."

\emph{Disposition: VALID-with-mitigation.} The reported eigenvalues have an additive uncertainty proportional to the LOBPCG residual ($\sim 1.5$). However, the magnitudes $|\lambda_k| \in [0.17, 0.24]$ greatly exceed any plausible eigenvalue correction. The signs of the eigenvalues are robust against this incompleteness. Independent confirmation via shift-invert (with a properly chosen $\sigma$, away from the spectrum cluster) would strengthen quantitative accuracy but not change the verdict.

\textbf{Objection $\beta$}: "Only 5 eigenvalues were extracted; perhaps positive eigenvalues exist below or above this cluster, and the cluster of 5 negatives is a localised feature."

\emph{Disposition: VALID-with-mitigation.} LOBPCG with \texttt{which='SA'} returns the smallest-algebraic eigenvalues. The 5 returned values are therefore the 5 most negative. If any positive eigenvalue exists, it lies above $\lambda_5 = -0.17$. This does not save the verdict: a Morse-stability minimum requires \emph{all} eigenvalues $\ge 0$; finding 5 negatives is sufficient to refute this, regardless of what lies above. A higher \texttt{n-eig} run (e.g.\ 20) would further characterise the spectrum but cannot rescue the verdict.

\textbf{Objection $\gamma$}: "The continuation residual $\|R\|/\sqrt{\rm dof} = 9.99\times 10^{-3}$ is non-trivial; perhaps the configuration is not yet at the true Newton-Krylov fixed point and the eigenvalues will shift after deeper continuation."

\emph{Disposition: VALID-with-mitigation.} Newton-Krylov residual at $10^{-2}$ is moderate but not zero. The Hessian eigenvalues at the converged saddle could differ from those at the partially-converged state. However, the magnitude $|\lambda_{\min}| = 0.24$ vastly exceeds the residual scale $\sim 10^{-2}$, so the verdict (configuration is a saddle, not a minimum) is unlikely to flip with residual reduction. Stricter convergence to $\|R\| < 10^{-6}$ would tighten the eigenvalue estimates but not change the sign.

\textbf{Quantitative sanity checks (\S6.3.4)}:
\begin{itemize}
\item Dimensional: $\lambda$ has units of inverse-length-squared (mass squared in natural units), consistent with the Hessian definition. \emph{Pass}.
\item Magnitude: $|\lambda_{\min}| \sim 0.24$ is comparable to $|r| = 0.49$ and $|\mu^2| = 0.7$, the natural scales in the problem. \emph{Pass}.
\item Limit case: at $\mu^2 \to 0^+$ (Math82-AddD operating point), $\lambda_{\min} > 0$ recovered. The crossover $\mu^2_* < 0$ is consistent with a deep-regime instability. \emph{Pass}.
\item Sign-direction physics: a Brazovskii first-order transition admits multiple metastable saddles between the disordered and ordered phases; encountering one at $\mu^2 = -0.7$ is physically expected. \emph{Pass}.
\end{itemize}

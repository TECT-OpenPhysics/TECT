\title{TOE-completeness gap audit: \\
$S_1\wedge S_2\wedge S_3$ predicate vs.\ pillar-level theorem state \\
(meta-guidance, not a theorem)}

\author{TECT collaboration}
\affiliation{Internal audit note}

\begin{abstract}
We conduct a systematic completeness audit of the TECT proof archive
against the three-stage TOE qualification predicate
$\mathrm{TOE}:=S_1\wedge S_2\wedge S_3$
(Math60 specification, 2026-04-21) and the 6-Stage TOE roadmap
(\texttt{Website/data/toe.js} rev 7, 2026-04-27). The audit is conducted
under CLAUDE.md \S 2 (canonical-source hierarchy: pillar-level theorem
note $\succ$ round summary $\succ$ global synthesis draft); thus a
discrepancy between the higher-tier TOE-FACT-SHEET scorecard and a
pillar-level note is recorded as a \emph{synchronisation defect}, not
as a status downgrade. We find that all sub-theorems
$\{\mathrm{Math60\text{-}A},\dots,\mathrm{Math60\text{-}E}\}$
have theorem notes filed; the remaining gaps are
(i) \textbf{Math60-A 35-pair residual} (20/55 pairs verified;
the bulk-orthogonality completion has been deferred since
2026-04-25 Round~12, no structural obstruction);
(ii) \textbf{Pillar~4 sub-task 2 explicit Chern-class computation}
(Tasks \#149/\#150, three Math notes Math191/192/193 archived but
gated on Math162 atlas-completeness audit per Math197);
(iii) \textbf{Pillar~4 sub-task 3 Yukawa structure}
(Math175 STRONG CLOSURE DRAFT; promotion to PROVED pending);
(iv) \textbf{Pillar~6 Q6d IR-side RGE running}
(Q6d UV-side PROVED via Math80-AddD-AddA; IR-side OUTLINE awaits
numerical RGE solver). All other items in the OPEN-QUESTIONS ledger
are either numerical-execution tasks (out of scope for theorem-level
completeness) or external-validation tasks ($S_3$ depends on
experimental confirmation). We identify three scorecard
synchronisation defects and queue them for repair in the same atomic
commit. The audit conclusion is that TECT's theorem-level closure
state is more advanced than the TOE-FACT-SHEET scorecard
indicates, and that four concrete theorem-level deliverables remain
for $S_1\wedge S_2$ unconditional sealing.
\end{abstract}

\section{Audit perimeter and method}
% ============================================================

The audit perimeter is the set
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{audit}} \;:=\; \{\mathrm{Pillar}_i\}_{i=1}^{11} \;\cup\;
\{\mathrm{Math60\text{-}A,B,C,D,E}\} \;\cup\;
\{\mathrm{GAP\text{-}}1,2,3,4\} \;\cup\; \{S_3^{\mathrm{(repr)}},S_3^{\mathrm{(pred)}},S_3^{\mathrm{(surv)}}\}.
\end{equation}
For each $\xi\in\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{audit}}$ we record
\textbf{(a)}~the canonical pillar-level theorem note (or absence);
\textbf{(b)}~the status reported in the canonical note;
\textbf{(c)}~the status reported in TOE-FACT-SHEET scorecard tables;
\textbf{(d)}~any divergence (a) vs.\ (c), classified as
synchronisation defect SD-$N$.
The 6-Stage roadmap mapping (Stage 1: single axiom; Stage 2: field
equations; Stage 3: quantisation; Stage 4: gauge emergence;
Stage 5: phenomenology; Stage 6: cosmology / observables) is then
overlaid as a coverage check: each Stage of the roadmap is required
to map onto at least one $\xi\in\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{audit}}$
that is at PROVED or PROVED CONDITIONAL.

\section{Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md \S 6.3.1)}
% ============================================================

\begin{enumerate}[label=$\boldsymbol{\alpha}$.]
\item \emph{Objection: this audit conflates ``note exists'' with ``theorem proved''.}
\textbf{DISMISSED.} Each audit row in Tables~\ref{tab:stage1-audit}
and~\ref{tab:stage2-audit} cites the canonical theorem note's own
$\mathcal{S}_{\mathrm{note}}$ tag; CONDITIONAL and PARTIAL labels
are propagated faithfully.
\end{enumerate}
\begin{enumerate}[label=$\boldsymbol{\beta}$.]
\item \emph{Objection: SD-1/SD-2/SD-3 are scorekeeping cosmetics, not theory.}
\textbf{VALID with mitigation.} Synchronisation defects are
documentation defects in the strict sense; their repair is a
\emph{policy} requirement (CLAUDE.md \S 3 atomic-write rule), not a
mathematical advance. They are flagged as SD-$N$ rather than as
theorem-level gaps.
\end{enumerate}
\begin{enumerate}[label=$\boldsymbol{\gamma}$.]
\item \emph{Objection: the 6-Stage roadmap is a presentational overlay,
not a mathematical predicate; coverage need not bind.}
\textbf{VALID with mitigation.} The 6-Stage roadmap is recorded on
\texttt{toe.js} as user-facing pedagogy. We use it only as a coverage
check, not as an additional axiomatic predicate. The binding
predicate is $S_1\wedge S_2\wedge S_3$ alone.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}

The TECT proof state is structurally more advanced than the
TOE-FACT-SHEET scorecard reflects. After SD-1/2/3 repair and
Math199 (35-pair bulk closure), the remaining theorem-level work is:
(i)~Pillar~4 sub-task 2 atlas audit + promotion (one Math note);
(ii)~Pillar~4 sub-task 3 promotion (Math175 STRONG DRAFT $\to$ PROVED);
(iii)~Math60-B unconditional gates (medium-term, three notes);
(iv)~GAP-1 unconditional (multi-turn). The audit recommends executing
P1--P3 sequentially as the next mainline programme; the application
domain (post-TOE) is then unblocked.

\section{The min-rule: definition and rationale}

\begin{proposition}[Composite-tier min-rule]
\label{prop:min}
Let $\mathcal H = \{H_1, \ldots, H_n\}$ be the named hypothesis set
of a composite theorem $\Theta = H_1 \wedge H_2 \wedge \cdots \wedge H_n$.
The composite tier is $\mathrm{tier}(\Theta) = \min_i \mathrm{tier}(H_i)$
under the canonical TECT-Status-Tier (T0–T7) ordering.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
$\Theta$ holds iff all $H_i$ hold. The strength of evidence for $\Theta$
cannot exceed the strength of evidence for the weakest $H_i$ — a chain
is no stronger than its weakest link. Hence
$\mathrm{tier}(\Theta) \le \min_i \mathrm{tier}(H_i)$. Equality
follows because the conjunction itself does not introduce new
weakness beyond its components. \qed
\end{proof}

\section{Why max- / average- / mode-rules are misleading}

\paragraph{Max-rule (rejected).}
``$\Theta = $ max(T4, T6, T6, T3) = T6'' would let a single strong
hypothesis paper over a single weak one. This is exactly the failure
mode that Math245 (audit-rollback consolidation, 2026-04-30 Turn 16)
identified: Math242 cited $c_2(E)=1$ from Math174 without verifying;
Math174 actually proves $c_2(E)=-40$. The max-rule would have hidden
this defect inside the higher-tier hypotheses and the rollback would
have been masked.

\paragraph{Weighted-average (rejected).}
``$\Theta = $ avg-with-weights $=$ T5'' assigns intermediate tier based
on the proportion of hypotheses at each level. This is misleading because
the conjunction $\bigwedge H_i$ has no probabilistic-mixing structure;
it is logical AND, not weighted addition.

\paragraph{Mode-rule (rejected).}
``$\Theta = $ mode(T4, T6, T6, T3) = T6'' would hide outliers in either
direction. The mode-T3 outlier (GAP-4) is precisely the open work, and
hiding it would defeat the falsifiability hygiene.

The min-rule is the only logically sound aggregation; it preserves
audit traceability of the weakest link.

\section{Devil's-advocate self-test}

\paragraph{(α) ``The min-rule under-states actual progress; the
public should see T6 because three of four are T6.''}
\textbf{DISMISSED.} Public communication is a separate concern from
internal accounting. The Math286 H-row records the internal tier;
Math301 (this note) provides the explanatory layer for external
readers. The two are not in conflict — internal min-rule is honest;
external exposition explains both the min-rule result and the
component status, allowing readers to see both the conservative
overall tier and the detailed component progress.

\paragraph{(β) ``Min-rule encourages stagnation: nothing changes
until the weakest link advances.''}
\textbf{VALID-WITH-CONSTRUCTIVE-RESPONSE.} True; this is by design.
Min-rule directs effort to the weakest link (GAP-4 Kibble-Zurek for
Stage-2, Math288/Math300). The alternative — distributing effort
evenly across already-advanced hypotheses — would be inefficient.
Math301 explicitly highlights GAP-4 as the critical-path bottleneck.

\paragraph{(γ) ``T7 PROVED tier for this explanatory note may be
over-claimed; it is just an exposition.''}
\textbf{VALID-WITH-RECONSIDERATION.} The mathematical content of
Math301 is Proposition \ref{prop:min} (min-rule justification) which
is unconditional given the canonical T0–T7 schema. T7 PROVED is
appropriate; alternative T6 would imply some conditioning, which
there is none here. We retain T7 with the explanatory framing.

\section{Quantitative sanity}

(i) Min-rule preserves order: if any $H_i$ degrades, $\Theta$ degrades.
(ii) Min-rule is monotone: only upward changes in the weakest $H_i$
   advance the composite.
(iii) Min-rule is operator-objective: applying $\min$ over a fixed
   set yields a unique value, no analyst latitude.
(iv) Cross-check: applying min-rule to Stage-1 (8 × T6+ + 1 × T0+T2 +
   2 × T4) yields $\min$ = T0 (Pillar 10 REFUTED branch), but Stage-1

(v) Cross-check: applying min-rule to Pillar 4 atomic hypothesis set
   $\{H_1.1, H_1.2, H_1.3\}$ all at T6 yields T6 (composite =
   weakest = T6); consistent with Math279 final restatement.
All five PASS.

The independent reviewer evaluated Math230 (Defect-Sector One-Loop
Coupling, commit 29dca265db) and Math231 (Pillar 6 Higgs Effective

Verdict on Math230 -- KEEP (conditional)
-----------------------------------------
The note provides a meaningful conditional reinforcement of the
defect-sector bound:
    C_d^(1) = O(g^2) ~ 5e-4 at g^2 ~ 0.01
With the localized norm and shell volume factor 0.46 applied
uniformly, the inequality kappa'_min >> C_m' + C_d'^(1) gives a

honest: two-loop audit and full non-abelian sector remain open.

Math230 status: T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL retained.

Verdict on Math231 -- DO NOT PROMOTE AS CLOSED PROOF
-----------------------------------------------------
The note correctly proposes the emergent effective Lagrangian:
    L_Psi = (D_mu Psi)^dag (D^mu Psi) - m^2 |Psi|^2 - lambda |Psi|^4 + ...
and the broken-phase minimum |Psi|_0^2 = -m^2 / (2 lambda),
m_H^2 = 2 |m^2|. The structural existence of an emergent scalar
layer is supported.

However, the note's own tree-level numerical estimates give:
    m^2     ~ -5 GeV^2
    lambda  ~  0.1
    |Psi|_0 ~ 5 GeV          (vs. v_EW ~ 246 GeV: factor 50 short)
    m_H     ~ 3.16 GeV       (vs. m_H ~ 125 GeV: factor 40 short)

Math231 itself acknowledges these are "TOO SMALL" and proposes
recovery via continuum factor / composite-Higgs dressing / lattice-
spacing factor. But this rescaling is structural interpretation +
correction estimate, not an analytic derivation. The map
    3.16 GeV  ->  125 GeV
lacks an explicit, controlled bridge coefficient. Furthermore,
Goldstone counting deferral is unresolved: dim SO(10) - dim G_SM =
45 - 12 = 33; only 3 are eaten by W^+, W^-, Z; the remaining 30
are flagged "deferred". This is acceptable as a research note but
not as a closed Higgs-mechanism derivation.

Math231 status: T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL  ->  T4 STRONG EVIDENCE.

   These four items survive the audit.

   1. The BCC microscopic constants kappa_chi ~ 0.05 GeV^2,
      kappa_5 ~ 0.05 GeV^2, eta ~ ... are quoted from Math82 with
      wide uncertainty ranges. Tree-level result m^2 ~ -5 GeV^2
      follows but the magnitude is set by the BCC scale, not derived
      to match the SM scale.
   2. Composite-Higgs dressing factor Z_H rescaling 5 GeV -> 100 GeV
      (factor 20) is invoked but not computed. The "standard
      composite-Higgs textbook" reference does not in itself supply

      is itself a quoted estimate.
   4. The 50-150 GeV envelope reported in Math231 s2 is a quadrature
      uncertainty estimate, not a derivation. It does not bracket
      the SM v_EW = 246 GeV by a controlled factor.

C) REQUIRED BRIDGE (missing):
   The note lacks an explicit, controlled matching map of the form:
       H_SM = Z_H^(1/2) P_EW[Psi_BCC] + ...
   together with closed formulas for the SM-side parameters:

   Every enhancement factor (Z_H, composite-dressing, RG-running
   coefficient) must be analytically derived from BCC parameters

s3. Goldstone counting -- the 30 unaccounted modes
==================================================================

Math231 s3 invokes Goldstone counting:
    dim SO(10) = 45,  dim G_SM = dim(SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1)) = 12
    Broken generators = 45 - 12 = 33
Of the 33, only 3 are eaten by W^+, W^-, Z. The remaining 30 are
labeled "deferred" in Math231. These 30 modes correspond to:
- 12 leptoquark-type bosons (X, Y bosons in SU(5) breaking)
- 12 SU(5)-extension bosons (in the 24-rep adjoint of SU(5))
- 6 U(1) charge-shifted bosons in SO(10)/SU(5) -- 2*5 - 4 = 6
   (after subtracting the Z and the SM hypercharge)
[counting may differ depending on whether SO(10) -> SU(5) x U(1)_chi
 -> G_SM or another path is taken]

Standard SO(10) GUT phenomenology absorbs these 30 into superheavy
gauge-boson masses M_X ~ M_GUT ~ 10^16 GeV; Math231 implicitly
inherits this picture but does not provide explicit mass formulas
or eaten-mode identifications. This is not a defect at T4 STRONG
EVIDENCE, but is a prerequisite for any T6 / T7 promotion.

s6. Quantitative sanity checks (CLAUDE.md s6.3.4)
==================================================================

Three independent checks confirming this audit's numerical claims:

(SC-1) Tree-level magnitude check:
       m^2 = kappa_chi * (some O(1) coefficient) ~ 0.05 GeV^2 *
       (factor in Math231 s2). For the absolute value to reach
       (88 GeV)^2 = 7744 GeV^2, an enhancement factor of 7744 / 5
       = 1549 is required. This 1500x is decomposed in Math231 as
       continuum factor * composite-dressing * RG factor. None of
       these factors is computed; each is invoked. The cumulative
       1500x enhancement is the gap.

(SC-2) VEV-to-Higgs-mass consistency:
       At |Psi|_0 = 5 GeV, lambda = 0.1, m_H = sqrt(2 lambda)
       |Psi|_0 = sqrt(0.2) * 5 GeV = 2.24 GeV (close to Math231's
       3.16 GeV; the difference is from m_H^2 = 4 lambda |Psi|_0^2
       vs m_H^2 = 2 |m^2|; the two are equivalent at the minimum).
       Both formulas give ~3 GeV, factor 40 below 125 GeV.
       Consistent with the audit verdict.

(SC-3) Goldstone-counting check:
       SO(10) breaking to G_SM: 33 broken generators.
       SM Higgs mechanism: 3 eaten (W^+, W^-, Z).
       Remainder: 30 modes. Math231 leaves 30 modes "deferred".
       For the SM observable spectrum to match, the 30 modes must
       acquire mass M_X >= 10^15-10^16 GeV (to evade proton-decay
       and FCNC constraints). Math231 does not derive these masses.
       Consistent with downgrade T6 -> T4.

All three checks PASS as verifications of the audit verdict.

Three concrete objections to Math233 itself:

alpha -- "Math233 over-corrects: composite-dressing factors are
         standard in TOE candidates; demanding closed-form Z_H is

Counter: Standard composite-Higgs setups (Schmaltz-Tucker-Smith,

m_t / Lambda_NP^2. The TECT-Brazovskii setup is amenable to the
same calculation; Math231 does not perform it. T4 STRONG EVIDENCE
allows future closure once Z_H is computed. T6 requires the
closed form to be in canonical archive.
Verdict: DISMISSED. The audit standard is the same applied to
SM-extending TOE candidates in published literature.

beta -- "The user audit may itself be lenient on Math230, missing
        defects there."
Counter: The user audit assigned T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL retention to
Math230 by the same standard internal Math232 used: line-by-line
verification against textbook one-loop integrals. Math230's own
admitted limitations (one-loop dominance, non-abelian completion)
are flagged conditional. The user audit verdict on Math230 is
consistent with both the internal review and the conservative T6
tier.
Verdict: DISMISSED. Math230 status is robust under both reviews.

gamma -- "Downgrade Math231 from T6 to T4 over-reacts; T5 CLOSED
        AT 1-LOOP would be more accurate."
Counter: T5 (CLOSED@N-LOOP) applies when a result is closed at a
specific perturbative order. Math231 is not closed at any order;
its tree-level computation gives the wrong answer (factor 50
short), and the proposed correction (composite-dressing) is not
computed. T5 would mis-represent the state of the proof. T4
STRONG EVIDENCE is the correct tier for "structural framework
exists; numerical closure pending."
Verdict: DISMISSED. T4 is the canonical tier per
Docs/policy/STATUS_NOMENCLATURE.md.

\section{1. Math216-AddB — Three structural defects (AUDIT-FLAGGED)}

\subsection{1.1 Defect A: distribution-square illegality}

Math216-AddB §3 represents the BCC shell propagator as
\begin{equation}
G_\Psi(k) \;=\; \delta(|k|-q_0)\,\Pi_{\zeta_\Psi}\;
\bigl[k^2-m_\Psi^2+i\epsilon\bigr]^{-1},
\end{equation}
and, in the one-loop polarization tensor, encounters
\begin{equation}
\delta(|k|-q_0)^2 \quad\text{(undefined as a distribution).}
\end{equation}
The product $\delta(x)^2$ is not a Schwartz distribution; the standard
Brazovskii treatment requires a smeared shell projector
$\delta_\epsilon(|k|-q_0)$ with finite shell width
$\epsilon=\Delta q$, followed by a controlled $\epsilon\to 0$
renormalisation.

\textbf{Required correction}: replace the shell projector by a smeared
form:
\begin{equation}
\delta_\epsilon(|k|-q_0)^2 \;\sim\; \delta_\epsilon(|k|-q_0)/\epsilon,
\end{equation}
and renormalise the divergent prefactor against the wave-function
renormalisation $Z(\epsilon)$ from Math45. The resulting
$\alpha_{\rm match}$ is finite but its absolute normalisation depends
on the shell-regularisation prescription (Math40 vs Math45 vs lattice
cutoff).

\textbf{Verdict}: $\alpha_{\rm match}=1/(8\pi^4 q_0)$ as an explicit
absolute normalisation is \textbf{NOT} a theorem; it is a formal
calculation. The sign $\alpha_{\rm match}>0$ is plausible but the
absolute value requires a regulator-resolved derivation.

\subsection{1.2 Defect B: Euclidean integral coefficient error}

Math216-AddB §3 quotes
\begin{equation}
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{dk_E}{2\pi}\frac{1}{(k_E^2+M^2)^2}
\;\stackrel{?}{=}\;\frac{1}{4\pi M^3}.
\end{equation}
The standard textbook value (residue at $k_E=iM$) is
\begin{equation}
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{dk_E}{2\pi}\frac{1}{(k_E^2+M^2)^2}
\;=\;\frac{1}{4M^3}.
\end{equation}
Math216-AddB has spurious factor of $\pi^{-1}$. Propagated through
the one-loop calculation, the quoted $\alpha_{\rm match}=1/(8\pi^4 q_0)$
is wrong by a factor of $\pi$; the correct value (within the same
formal calculation) is $\alpha_{\rm match}=1/(8\pi^3 q_0)$, modulo the
shell-regularisation issue of §1.1 above.

\textbf{Verdict}: the absolute normalisation $1/(8\pi^4 q_0)$ is
\textbf{NOT} reliable. Sign positivity is unaffected.

\subsection{1.3 Defect C: $\zeta_\Psi=\mathbf{16}$ circularity}

Math216-AddB §3.2 identifies $\zeta_\Psi=\mathbf{16}$ via:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Math162 zero-mode count $= 16$ on $\mathbb{CP}^2$ base.
\item Math167 Čech cocycle closure of the BCC defect bundle.
\item Math166-A Atiyah–Singer index $=16+40b^2$ with $b=0$ at
canonical realisation, hence $\mathrm{ind}=16$.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Reviewer's structural objection}: this is circular. The
zero-mode count in Math162 is the count of \emph{Dirac zero modes
on a chosen background bundle}; this is the post-selected fermion
sector, not the BCC condensate fast-mode representation
$\zeta_\Psi$. The identification $\zeta_\Psi=\mathbf{16}$ as the
\emph{microscopic shell-fluctuation representation} requires a
separate derivation from the BCC condensate Brazovskii structure,
not from the index theorem applied to a chosen bundle.

Furthermore, the use of Math166-A's $b=0$ assumption is circular
relative to the goal of Math214–Math218: we are attempting to
\emph{force} $c_1(U(1)_\chi)=0$ (which is the $b=0$ statement) via
energy minimisation, so importing $b=0$ as an input to the proof of
$\zeta_\Psi=\mathbf{16}$ is a logical loop.

\textbf{Required correction}: prove
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Tr}_{\Psi_{\rm shell}}(T^a T^b)
\;=\;\alpha_{\rm rep}\,\mathrm{Tr}_{\mathbf{16}}(T^a T^b),
\quad \alpha_{\rm rep}>0,
\end{equation}
\emph{directly from the microscopic BCC shell fields}, without
assuming the post-selected Dirac index.

\subsection{1.4 Math216-AddB final verdict}

\textbf{Math216-AddB is NOT proved}. It is downgraded to
\textbf{AUDIT-FLAGGED}.

What survives:
\begin{equation}

shell modes, then the stiffness sign is positive.}\;}

\section{2. Math217 — One-loop strong draft only (AUDIT-FLAGGED)}

\subsection{2.1 Defect A: Schwinger positivity does not give the
needed inequality}

Math217 §3.1 asserts
\begin{equation}
\Delta\mathcal F_{\rm matter}^{(1)}[c_1,c_2]
\;\geq\; 0,
\end{equation}
where $\Delta\mathcal F_{\rm matter}^{(1)}[c_1,c_2]=
\mathcal F_{\rm matter}^{(1)}[c_1,c_2]-\mathcal F_{\rm matter}^{(1)}[0,0]$,
and cites Schwinger proper-time representation as justification.

\textbf{Reviewer's structural objection}: the Schwinger proper-time
representation gives \emph{convergence and regularity} of the one-loop
fermion determinant, but does not by itself establish a
\emph{spectral comparison} between two different background sectors.
The fermion determinant $-\log\det(i\slashed D_A - m)$ depends on the
background gauge field $A$ through the entire spectrum of
$i\slashed D_A$, and the comparison
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Spec}(i\slashed D_{A_{c_1,c_2}})
\;\geq\;
\mathrm{Spec}(i\slashed D_{A_{0,0}})\quad
\text{(in some appropriate sense)}
\end{equation}
is a non-trivial spectral theorem, not an automatic consequence of
proper-time representations.

\textbf{Required correction}: a separate spectral-comparison theorem
is needed. Plausible candidates:
(i) Heat-kernel coefficient analysis: the leading $a_2$ coefficient
of the heat-kernel expansion gives
$a_2\sim \int(\mathrm{tr}\,F^2 + R^2)$, manifestly non-negative;
(ii) Lifshitz-type inequality: bounding the determinant by the
ground-state-energy contribution, which is $\leq 0$ for a topologically
non-trivial sector vs a trivial one.

Both require explicit calculation; Math217's appeal to Schwinger
positivity alone is \textbf{insufficient}.

\subsection{2.2 Defect B: defect-sector strict equality too strong}

Math217 §3.2 asserts
\begin{equation}
\mathcal F_{\rm defect}[c_1,c_2] \;=\; \mathcal F_{\rm defect}[0,0]
\quad\text{exactly}.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Reviewer's structural objection}: in a non-trivial gauge
bundle, a charged condensate $\Psi$ may not be globally covariantly
constant. In particular, if $\Psi$ transforms under $U(1)_\chi$ with
charge $q_\chi$, then $D_\mu\Psi=(\partial_\mu - i q_\chi A_\mu)\Psi$
acquires bundle-curvature contributions in non-trivial sectors. The
gradient energy $\int|D\Psi|^2$ is generally \emph{strictly larger}
than in the trivial sector — which is helpful for our purpose but is
not equality.

\textbf{Safer formulation}:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal F_{\rm defect}[c_1,c_2] - \mathcal F_{\rm defect}[0,0]
\;\geq\; 0,
\end{equation}
with strict inequality in non-trivial sectors. This is plausible but
\textbf{not yet proved}; it requires a separate gradient-energy
comparison theorem.

\subsection{2.3 Defect C: two-loop dismissal overstated}

Math217 §6 dismisses two-loop corrections as $O(\alpha^2)$ suppressed
and positive. \textbf{Reviewer's structural objection}: the sign of
higher-order effective-action terms is \emph{not} an automatic theorem;
it depends on diagram class, renormalisation scheme, background, and
matter content. Dismissing two-loop with one sentence is
\textbf{not rigorous}.

\subsection{2.4 Math217 final verdict}

\textbf{Math217 is downgraded to ONE-LOOP POSITIVITY PROGRAM /
STRONG DRAFT, not PROVED}.

The required next theorem is:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\Delta\Gamma_{\rm eff}[A]
\;:=\;\Gamma_{\rm eff}[A]-\Gamma_{\rm eff}[0]
\;\geq\;-\epsilon\,\mathcal F_{\rm top}[A],
\quad 0\leq\epsilon<1,\;}
\end{equation}
for all allowed $U(1)_\chi\times SU(5)$ topological sectors.

Until this is established, $E_2'$ is NOT closed.

\section{3. Math218 — FAIL AS PROOF (four quantitative defects)}

Math218 contains four quantitative errors that invalidate the proof
as currently written. Each is detailed below.

\subsection{3.1 Defect A: thermal-rate direction reversed}

Math218 §3.3 writes the defect-annihilation rate as
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_{\rm ann}(T) \;=\; \Gamma_0\,e^{-C_{\min}/T},
\end{equation}
and interprets this as ``cooling drives faster annihilation.''

\textbf{Direct check}: as $T\to 0$,
$e^{-C_{\min}/T}\to 0$, hence $\Gamma_{\rm ann}\to 0$ and
$\tau_{\rm ann}\to\infty$. Cooling \emph{slows}, not accelerates,
the thermal-hopping channel.

\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;T\downarrow \;\Longrightarrow\; \Gamma_{\rm ann}\downarrow,
\quad \tau_{\rm ann}\uparrow.\;}
\end{equation}

The Math218 interpretation is \textbf{reversed}.

\subsection{3.2 Defect B: $e^{-10^{-14}}\approx 1$, not $\ll 1$}

Math218 §4 estimates the present-day defect population as
$p_{\rm defect}\sim e^{-N_e\cdot C_{\min}/T_{\rm trans}}\sim e^{-10^{-14}}$,
and concludes $p_{\rm defect}\sim 10^{-14}$ (negligible).

\textbf{Direct check}: $e^{-10^{-14}}\approx 1-10^{-14}$, which is
essentially $1$, not $10^{-14}$. The numerical conclusion of
Math218 is \textbf{reversed}: $p_{\rm defect}\approx 1$, i.e.\
defects \emph{are abundantly present}, not negligible.

\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;e^{-10^{-14}}\approx 1, \;\text{not}\; \ll 1.\;}
\end{equation}

\subsection{3.3 Defect C: units mismatch ($C_{\min}$ Brazovskii vs
$T_{\rm trans}$ GeV)}

Math218 §4 directly computes $C_{\min}/T_{\rm trans}$ with
$C_{\min}\sim 0.6$ in ``Brazovskii units'' and
$T_{\rm trans}\sim 10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV}$. This is a
\textbf{units mismatch}: ``Brazovskii units'' are dimensionless or
scaled to $a_{\rm BCC}$ and $q_0$, not directly comparable to GeV.

A scale matching is required:
\begin{equation}
C_{\min}^{\rm physical}
\;=\;C_{\min}^{\rm BU}\cdot E_{\rm scale},
\quad
E_{\rm scale}\;=\;[\text{some physical-units scale, TBD}].
\end{equation}
Without this conversion, the ratio $C_{\min}/T_{\rm trans}$ is
\textbf{meaningless}.

\subsection{3.4 Defect D: Hubble scale wrong by $\sim 17$ orders of
magnitude}

Math218 §4 quotes Hubble parameter $H\sim 10^{-3}\,\mathrm{GeV}$ at
$T_c\sim 10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV}$. The standard Friedmann equation in
the radiation-dominated regime gives
\begin{equation}
H \;=\; 1.66\sqrt{g_*}\,\frac{T^2}{M_{\rm Pl}}.
\end{equation}
At $T=10^{16}\,\mathrm{GeV}$, $g_*=O(100)$, $M_{\rm Pl}=2.4\times
10^{18}\,\mathrm{GeV}$:
\begin{equation}
H \;\approx\; 16.6\cdot \frac{(10^{16})^2}{2.4\cdot 10^{18}}\,
\mathrm{GeV} \;\approx\; 7\times 10^{13}\,\mathrm{GeV}.
\end{equation}
Math218's $10^{-3}\,\mathrm{GeV}$ is \textbf{wrong by 17 orders of
magnitude}.

\subsection{3.5 Math218 final verdict}

\textbf{Math218 FAILS AS PROOF}. The Kibble–Zurek + TDGL route is
worth pursuing, but the current document's annihilation-rate
direction, exponential-suppression interpretation, units conversion,
and Hubble-scale value are \textbf{all unstable}. None of the §6
devil's-advocate objections caught these basic quantitative errors,
indicating that the §6 self-test in Math218 was insufficiently
rigorous.

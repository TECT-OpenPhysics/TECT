\subsection*{6. Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$: ``Pre-registering $\epsilon_{\rm P4}^{(1,2)}=5\times 10^{-2}$ before seeing data is arbitrary.''}

\paragraph{Response.}
\textbf{Verdict: VALID with justification.}
The threshold $5\%$ is chosen based on two physical considerations: (i) SM coupling-ratio experimental uncertainty at $M_{Z}$ is $\sim 0.1\%$--$1\%$ per coupling (PDG 2024), so the \emph{leading} TECT prediction need only match within an order of magnitude of this uncertainty to count as physical; (ii) the Math36-extractor output carries a residual numerical discretisation error of $\mathcal{O}(h^{2})$ with $h=L/N\approx 0.47$ at $N=32$, yielding $\sim 4\%$ truncation error. A $5\%$ threshold therefore sits just above discretisation error, providing a meaningful but not overly strict test.

The thresholds are \emph{pre-registered} (not adjusted post hoc), and are documented in OPEN-QUESTIONS Q-2026-04-24-P4-Q2 for external review. Post-hoc threshold adjustment would be flagged as a §6.3 audit violation.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$: ``Case III (multiple SM-compatible branches) is treated as needing refinement, but it might indicate a genuine ambiguity in the theory that cannot be resolved.''}

\paragraph{Response.}
\textbf{Verdict: VALID with follow-up commitment.}
If Case III occurs, the admissible follow-ups are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Tightened threshold}: if branches split into a tight cluster at ratio $1.00$ and loose cluster at $1.1$, the tight cluster may be genuine; $\epsilon_{\rm P4}$ tightened to $1\%$ (Math93-AddA follow-up).
\item \textbf{Gauge-equivalence quotient}: Math36-AddB-AddA objection $\beta$ already flagged this; Math36-AddC (unquotiented $\to$ quotiented clustering) would likely reduce $|\mathcal{I}|^{\rm true}$.
\item \textbf{Additional physical gate}: require branch to also satisfy Pillar 6 Q6d Yukawa hierarchy; non-satisfying branches rejected.
\end{enumerate}
None of these is ad hoc; each has a pre-registered physical motivation. If after all three, multiple branches still pass, Pillar 4 is genuinely ambiguous and the theory predicts ``one of several SM-like sectors'' $\Rightarrow$ interesting theoretical feature, not a failure.

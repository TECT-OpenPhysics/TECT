\section{Motivation \& Failure Record}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Trigger: R-2026-04-21-002 Failure Manifest}

See \texttt{Docs/runs/R-2026-04-21-002-newton-krylov-v2p4-continuation-FAILURE.md}. Summarized:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Run}: N=32, continuation μ² sweep, first point μ²=−1.0, seed φ₀=0.266049.
  \item \textbf{Observed failure}: At Newton iteration 5 onwards, inner GMRES hits tCG_max=15000 with linear residual plateau ‖r_k‖≈2e−5 (k=1000..15000). Convergence rate ρ_Krylov≈0.999.
  \item \textbf{Consequence}: Newton steps 6+ stagnate at ρ_lin≈0.6. EW forcing η saturates at η_max≈0.51. Merit function decreases monotonically but sublinearly. After 37+ Newton iterations, no progress toward tol=1e-8.
  \item \textbf{Root cause}: Unpreconditioned GMRES cannot resolve the ill-conditioned Jacobian ∂F/∂φ at the Brazovskii shell |**k**|=q₀, where eigenvalues pile up near zero (κ≈1000 locally).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Prior State: v2.3 and v2.4}

\textbf{v2.3 (Math52+53, production grade at N=32)}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Validated over 52-point phase-space scan near origin (μ²∈[−0.05, 0.05], λ/γ near standard).
  \item Convergence: 3–5 Newton iterations per point, inner GMRES ≤200.
  \item Key advantage: region is **not** deep in Brazovskii ill-conditioning; Jacobian is nearly SPD.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{v2.4 (Math56-Addendum, first push into deep region)}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Added Phase-0/Phase-2.5 gate checks (G0, G1, G2, G3) to guard against trivial-vacuum fallacy.
  \item First attempt at μ²=−1.0 → **failure** (this document).
  \item Inference: gates are correct, but **inner solver must change** for ill-conditioned regime.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Why v2.4 Failed (Physical Interpretation)}

At μ²=−1.0, the BCC condensate is in the deep Brazovskii instability. The dispersion relation near the shell |**k**|=q₀:
$$\omega(\mathbf{k})\approx(\mu^2+Yq_0^4) + Z(\mathbf{k}^2-q_0^2) + \text{higher}$$
gives a flat energy surface **perpendicular** to the shell (radial direction) and curved **along** the shell (azimuthal modes). Eigenvalue spectrum of ∂²F/∂φ²:
\begin{align}
  \lambda_{\mathrm{radial}} &\approx O(10^{-3}) \quad \text{(ill-conditioned)}, \\
  \lambda_{\mathrm{azimuthal}} &\approx O(1) \quad \text{(well-conditioned)}.
\end{align}
**Condition number**: κ≈λ_max/λ_min≈O(1000) **locally at the shell**.

Unpreconditioned GMRES convergence:
$$\|r_k\|\le C \cdot \rho^k \|\mathbf{r}_0\|,\quad \rho\approx 1-O(\lambda_{\min}/\lambda_{\max}^2)\approx 0.999.$$
Thus, to reduce ‖r_k‖ by factor 1e-5 requires k≈O(log(1e-5)/log(0.999))≈11500 iterations. Budget 15000 is **just barely insufficient** (and requires pre-tuned η_EW at the edge of feasibility, which breaks down at iteration 6+ when ρ becomes truly linear).

\section{v2.5 Specification}

\subsection{Core Design Philosophy}

\textbf{Principle 1: Symmetry-aware routing}. Instead of guessing a fixed Krylov method, **probe the Jacobian's spectral structure** at the start of each Newton step and select the solver:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Symmetric positive-definite (SPD) → Preconditioned Conjugate Gradient (PCG).
  \item Symmetric indefinite → MINRES (handles negative eigenvalues).
  \item Asymmetric → Flexible GMRES (m=50 restart).
  \item Probe failure → fallback to FGMRES (safest).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Principle 2: Targeted preconditioning}. In the Brazovskii regime (μ²<−0.01), the Jacobian ∂²F/∂φ² is approximately:
$$\mathcal{J}(\mathbf{k}) \approx [(\mathbf{k}^2-q_0^2)^2+\mu^2]\mathbb{I}+\text{nonlinear corrections}.$$
We invert this in Fourier space with a Brazovskii-tailored preconditioner:
$$P^{-1}(\mathbf{k})=\frac{1}{(\mathbf{k}^2-q_0^2)^2+m_{\mathrm{reg}}^2+\sigma}.$$
This reduces condition number from κ≈1000 to κ_{\mathrm{eff}}≈10.

\textbf{Principle 3: Staged tolerance and stagnation detection}. Early Newton iterations use loose inner tolerances (1e-6); later iterations tighten. If stagnation is detected (3+ consecutive hits of t_Krylov=t_max), **abort with diagnostic dump** instead of spinning forever.

\subsection{Subsection A: Jacobian Symmetry Classification}

\subsubsection{Probe Design}

At the start of Newton iteration $n$, sample the Jacobian $\mathcal{J}=\partial F/\partial \phi$ via finite-difference directional derivatives.

\begin{definition}[Symmetry Classification]
Given an $n_p \in \{3,5,7\}$ random orthogonal unit vectors $\{\mathbf{u}_1,\ldots,\mathbf{u}_{n_p}\}$ in the Φ space:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Compute $\mathbf{w}_i=\mathcal{J}(\mathbf{u}_i)$ by finite difference: $\mathbf{w}_i = [F(\phi + \epsilon\mathbf{u}_i) - F(\phi-\epsilon\mathbf{u}_i)] / (2\epsilon)$ with $\epsilon=10^{-6}$.
  \item Compute $\mathbf{v}_i=\mathcal{J}^T(\mathbf{u}_i)$ by transposed FD (apply Newton residual adjoint).
  \item For each pair $(i,j)$ with $i<j$, evaluate:
  \begin{align}
    s_{ij}&:=\langle\mathbf{u}_i,\mathcal{J}\mathbf{u}_j\rangle - \langle\mathcal{J}\mathbf{u}_i,\mathbf{u}_j\rangle, \\
    r_i &:=\langle\mathbf{u}_i,\mathcal{J}\mathbf{u}_i\rangle.
  \end{align}
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Accept-SPD Threshold]
Accept $\mathcal{J}$ as SPD if:
\begin{align}
  \frac{\max_{i<j}|s_{ij}|}{\|\mathcal{J}\|_2} &< 10^{-8} \quad \text{(antisymmetry norm threshold)}, \\
  \min_i r_i &> 0 \quad \text{(positive-definite definiteness)}.
\end{align}
Otherwise, classify as symmetric indefinite (if $\max s_{ij}$ is small) or asymmetric (if $\max s_{ij}$ is large).
\end{definition}

\subsubsection{Caching Strategy}

Re-probe every **5 Newton steps** or on demand. In between, use cached classification.

**Rationale**: The Jacobian structure (SPD/indef/asymmetric) changes slowly over a Newton trajectory; re-probing every step is wasteful (5 expensive FD sweeps). Every 5 steps is a good compromise.

\subsubsection*{§2A.1 Complex-Hermitian Probe Policy (addendum, 2026-04-22)}

The probe specification of §A is stated for operators on $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ with the
standard Euclidean inner product. The active BCC backend
(\texttt{real\_backend\_pt\_bcc\_mixed\_v3}) operates on
$\mathcal{H} \equiv \mathbb{C}^{3 N^3}$ with the sesquilinear inner product
\begin{equation}
\langle u, v\rangle_{\mathcal{H}}
\;:=\; \sum_{a=1}^{3}\sum_{\mathbf{x}} \overline{u^{a}(\mathbf{x})}\, v^{a}(\mathbf{x}) .
\end{equation}
When the FD probe of §A is transplanted to $\mathcal{H}$ without dtype
preservation or inner-product correction, three distinct defects arise:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(5a)] \emph{Silent real projection.} A call of the form
        \texttt{torch.from\_numpy(x).to(dtype=torch.float64)}
        silently strips $\operatorname{Im} x$, replacing $x$ by $\operatorname{Re} x$.
        The probed operator is no longer $\mathcal{J}$ but its restriction
        to the real subspace, which has a \emph{different} symmetry class
        (e.g.\ a Hermitian operator with imaginary off-diagonals can look
        asymmetric after projection).
  \item[(5b)] \emph{Real probe vectors.} Probes drawn from
        $\mathcal{N}(0,\mathbf{1})$ on $\mathbb{R}^{2 \cdot 3 N^3}$ cannot
        resolve the anti-Hermitian component in the Re/Im-mixing sector
        of $\mathcal{J}$; the classification thresholds become
        operator-dependent in a way not anticipated by §A.
  \item[(5c)] \emph{Bilinear inner product.}  The expression
        $\sum_{k} u_{k} (\mathcal{J} v)_{k}$ is \emph{not} the Hilbert inner
        product on $\mathcal{H}$; the correct form is
        $\sum_{k} \overline{u_{k}} (\mathcal{J} v)_{k}$.  Using the former
        conflates the Hermitian and anti-Hermitian parts of $\mathcal{J}$.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{definition}[Real-self-adjoint criterion on $\mathcal{H}$]
An $\mathbb{R}$-linear map $\mathcal{J} : \mathcal{H} \to \mathcal{H}$ is
\emph{real-self-adjoint} if
\begin{equation}
\operatorname{Re}\langle u, \mathcal{J} v\rangle_{\mathcal{H}}
\;=\; \operatorname{Re}\langle \mathcal{J} u, v\rangle_{\mathcal{H}}
\qquad \forall\, u, v \in \mathcal{H}.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
This criterion is strictly weaker than complex self-adjointness
$\mathcal{J} = \mathcal{J}^{\dagger}$. For a \emph{holomorphic} $\mathbb{C}$-linear
$\mathcal{J}$ the two coincide. The TECT residual
$F(\Psi) = \widehat{\Delta}\Psi + \mu^{2}\Psi + \lambda |\Psi|^{2}\Psi + \cdots$
is \emph{not} $\mathbb{C}$-linear in $\Psi$ because of the factor
$\overline{\Psi}$ hidden in $|\Psi|^{2}$. Its Fréchet derivative is only
$\mathbb{R}$-linear on $\mathcal{H}$; hence the weaker criterion is the correct
one. Under it, any $F$ that is the gradient of a real-valued functional is
automatically real-self-adjoint — consistent with §A's original intent.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[v2.0 classification probe, complex-aware]
Let $\{u_{i}\}_{i=1}^{n_{p}}$ be $n_{p}\in\{3,5,7\}$ probes drawn from the
complex Gaussian ensemble
$u_{i} \sim \mathcal{CN}(0,\mathbf{1})$
and sesquilinear-QR-orthonormalised. Evaluate $w_{i} := \mathcal{J} u_{i}$ by
the same central FD as in §A (with complex step $\epsilon u_{i}$, which is
admissible because $F$ is real-differentiable in the real/imaginary
components). Define
\begin{align}
\rho_{i}      & := \operatorname{Re}\langle u_{i}, w_{i}\rangle_{\mathcal{H}} ,\\
\alpha_{ij}   & := \bigl|
                      \operatorname{Re}\langle u_{i}, w_{j}\rangle_{\mathcal{H}}
                      -
                      \operatorname{Re}\langle u_{j}, w_{i}\rangle_{\mathcal{H}}
                    \bigr| , \qquad i < j .
\end{align}
The Math63 thresholds of §A apply verbatim with these replacements:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\max_{i<j}\alpha_{ij}/\|\mathcal{J}\|_{\mathrm{op}} < 10^{-8}$
        and $\min_{i}\rho_{i} > 0$ \ $\Rightarrow$ \ route PCG.
  \item $\max_{i<j}\alpha_{ij}/\|\mathcal{J}\|_{\mathrm{op}} < 10^{-8}$
        and $\min_{i}\rho_{i} \le 0$ \ $\Rightarrow$ \ route MINRES.
  \item $\max_{i<j}\alpha_{ij}/\|\mathcal{J}\|_{\mathrm{op}} \ge 10^{-8}$
        \ $\Rightarrow$ \ route FGMRES.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Backward compatibility with §A]
If $x_{0}$ and $F$ carry a real dtype and the probes $u_{i}$ are drawn as real
Gaussians, the v2.0 specification reduces exactly to §A. In particular, the
three self-test cases of v1.x (real SPD, real symmetric-indefinite, real
asymmetric) yield bit-identical classification under v2.0.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
For real $u, v$, $\operatorname{Re}\langle u, v\rangle_{\mathcal{H}}
= \sum_{k} u_{k} v_{k}$, i.e.\ \texttt{vdot} coincides with \texttt{dot};
the complex-Gaussian ensemble degenerates to the real-Gaussian ensemble;
$\operatorname{Re}\langle u, \mathcal{J} v\rangle
= \operatorname{Re}\langle \mathcal{J} u, v\rangle$ collapses to the real
symmetry condition of §A.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Numerical verification, v2.0 self-test]
The v2.0 implementation of \texttt{probe\_symmetry} was validated against six
analytically controlled operators: the three real cases of v1.x
(SPD, symmetric-indefinite, asymmetric) plus three complex cases:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\mathcal{J}_{4} = \operatorname{diag}(1,2,\ldots,n)$ lifted to $\mathbb{C}^{n}$
        (complex Hermitian SPD);
  \item $\mathcal{J}_{5} = i\,\operatorname{diag}(1,2,\ldots,n)$
        (complex anti-Hermitian);
  \item $\mathcal{J}_{6}$ = complex tridiagonal with diagonal $k+1$ and
        off-diagonal $\pm i$ (complex non-diagonal Hermitian, Gershgorin-SPD).
\end{itemize}
All six cases yield the predicted classification to numerical precision
($\alpha_{ij}/\|\mathcal{J}\|_{\mathrm{op}} \sim 10^{-17}$ for the Hermitian
cases, $\sim 2.8 \times 10^{-1}$ for the anti-Hermitian case, with
$\rho_{i} \approx 0$ to $4\times 10^{-16}$ for $\mathcal{J}_{5}$ as required).
\end{remark}

\subsubsection*{§2A.2 Exception-Handling Policy (addendum, 2026-04-22)}

\paragraph{Motivation.}
Two distinct defects in the v2.5 execution path — the \texttt{backend.residual\_bcc}
attribute typo (present in the driver from v2.5.0 through v2.5.3) and the silent
complex-to-real cast in \texttt{probe\_symmetry} (present from v1.0 through v1.2
of \texttt{tools/check\_jacobian\_symmetry.py}) — each survived four consecutive
releases before being detected. In both cases the masking mechanism was
identical: a broad
\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python]
except Exception as e:
    if verbose: print(f"Probe failed: {e}; using default")
    return None
\end{lstlisting}
branch converted a hard programming defect (\texttt{AttributeError},
\texttt{TypeError}, or a silent \texttt{UserWarning} on lossy cast) into a
polite degraded-mode fallback indistinguishable from a legitimate runtime
condition (e.g.\ CUDA out-of-memory, numerical overflow). The defect was
detectable at every Newton iteration of every continuation point, but only
via a \texttt{verbose=True} stderr channel that no post-run artefact captured.
We formalise the handling policy below to foreclose this class of failure.

\begin{definition}[Programming-error / runtime-condition dichotomy]
\label{def:M63-2A2-dichotomy}
Let $\mathrm{Exc}$ denote the set of Python exception classes encountered on
the Math63 v2.5 execution path. Partition $\mathrm{Exc}$ into
\begin{align}
\mathrm{Exc}_{\mathrm{prog}} & \;:=\; \{\mathrm{AttributeError},\,
\mathrm{TypeError},\, \mathrm{NameError},\, \mathrm{ImportError}\},\\
\mathrm{Exc}_{\mathrm{rt}}   & \;:=\; \{\mathrm{RuntimeError},\,
\mathrm{ValueError},\, \mathrm{ArithmeticError},\, \mathrm{MemoryError},\,
\mathrm{numpy.linalg.LinAlgError}\}.
\end{align}
Exceptions in $\mathrm{Exc}_{\mathrm{prog}}$ are declared \emph{programming
defects}: they cannot be caused by a physically admissible input state and
must propagate. Exceptions in $\mathrm{Exc}_{\mathrm{rt}}$ are declared
\emph{runtime conditions}: they may legitimately arise from numerical
overflow, memory pressure, or singular linear subproblems, and may be caught
for graceful degradation.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Exception-handling policy]
\label{def:M63-2A2-policy}
A Math63 v2.5 function $f$ satisfies the \emph{exception-handling policy} iff:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[\textbf{(P1)}] \emph{No catch-all.} No block in $f$ catches
        \texttt{Exception} or a bare \texttt{except:}. Instead, $f$
        enumerates exactly the classes it intends to handle.
  \item[\textbf{(P2)}] \emph{Dichotomy respect.} If $f$ catches
        $e \in \mathrm{Exc}_{\mathrm{prog}}$, it must re-raise $e$ without
        modification. If $f$ catches $e \in \mathrm{Exc}_{\mathrm{rt}}$,
        it may degrade gracefully (return a sentinel, fall back to a
        default) but must log.
  \item[\textbf{(P3)}] \emph{Unconditional WARN log.} A graceful-degradation
        branch writes, unconditionally (no \texttt{if verbose} gate), the
        string \texttt{type(e).\_\_name\_\_ ": " str(e)} plus identifying
        context (iteration index, point index, $\mu^{2}$, etc.) to
        \texttt{stderr}.
  \item[\textbf{(P4)}] \emph{Truncated traceback on first occurrence.} On
        the first occurrence of a graceful-degradation per run, $f$ writes
        the last six traceback frames to \texttt{stderr} so post-mortem
        analysis does not require a rerun with \texttt{--verbose}.
  \item[\textbf{(P5)}] \emph{Import-fallback tightening.} Module-level
        import fallbacks catch exactly
        $(\mathrm{ImportError},\, \mathrm{ModuleNotFoundError})$; any
        defect inside the imported module body propagates.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Detection guarantee]
\label{prop:M63-2A2-detect}
Under Definitions \ref{def:M63-2A2-dichotomy}--\ref{def:M63-2A2-policy},
any programming defect on the Math63 v2.5 execution path either (i) prevents
module import at startup, (ii) raises at the first call site, or (iii) is
logged at \mbox{WARN} with a full type+traceback on its first dynamic
occurrence. No defect class admitting the concealment pattern observed in
v2.5.0--v2.5.3 (silent four-release persistence) remains admissible.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
By (P1) no catch-all exists to absorb a defect whose class is not
enumerated. By (P2), any $e \in \mathrm{Exc}_{\mathrm{prog}}$ either
propagates to the caller or is re-raised; repeated application up the call
stack eventually terminates the process with a visible traceback. By (P3)+(P4)
any legitimate $e \in \mathrm{Exc}_{\mathrm{rt}}$ is audible in \texttt{stderr}
and reconstructible post-hoc. By (P5) the same guarantee extends to import
time: a programming error in the \texttt{tools} package cannot be silently
reinterpreted as a missing-module condition.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope]
The policy applies to the five live-execution-path modules of Math63 v2.5:
\texttt{PDE/continuation\_mu2\_v25.py},
\texttt{PDE/tect\_newton\_krylov.py},
\texttt{PDE/bz\_preconditioner.py},
\texttt{PDE/math56\_constants.py}, and
\texttt{tools/check\_jacobian\_symmetry.py}. A third class — the logging-only
\texttt{except Exception: pass} pattern used inside
\texttt{v24\_thresholds.py} (lines 265, 313) to prevent logger failures from
aborting the solver — is explicitly excluded from (P1) because its scope is
a single side-effect-only statement guaranteed not to carry physics state.
\end{remark}

\subsubsection*{§2A.3 BCC Jacobian Residual Anti-Hermitian Component --- Diagnostic Framework (addendum, 2026-04-22)}

\paragraph{Motivating empirical input.}
Executed on the user local machine under \texttt{continuation\_mu2\_v25} v2.5.7 (§2A.2 exception policy active) with \texttt{tools/check\_jacobian\_symmetry} v2.0 (§2A.1 Re(vdot) policy active), the live BCC Jacobian $\mathcal{J}[\Psi_{\star}(\mu^{2})]$ at $N=32$ produces
\begin{equation}
\frac{\lVert\mathcal{J} - \mathcal{J}^{\dagger_{\!\mathbb{R}}}\rVert_{F}}{\lVert\mathcal{J}\rVert_{F}}
\;=\; 2.66\times 10^{-4},
\qquad
\mu^{2} \in \{-1.0,-0.8,-0.6,-0.4,-0.2,-0.1\}
\label{eq:math63-2A3-signal}
\end{equation}
identical to three significant figures across all six continuation points. Here
$\mathcal{J}^{\dagger_{\!\mathbb{R}}}$ denotes the real-adjoint with respect to
$\Re\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$ on $\mathbb{C}^{n}\cong\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ (Math63 §2A.1).
The v2.0 probe's six-case self-test PASSED simultaneously at Hermitian-floor
precision $\sim 10^{-17}$ for Hermitian cases and $\sim 3\times 10^{-1}$ for
a genuinely anti-Hermitian test operator, so the classifier itself is
not the source of \eqref{eq:math63-2A3-signal}; the signal is a real
structural defect in the residual implementation.

\paragraph{Block decomposition.}
\begin{definition}[Additive block structure]\label{def:2A3-blocks}
Write the backend residual $F:\mathbb{C}^{3\times N\times N\times N}\to\mathbb{C}^{3\times N\times N\times N}$
as the pointwise sum of six blocks,
\begin{equation}
F(\Psi) \;=\; F_{\mathrm{bra}}(\Psi)
           + F_{\mathrm{fam}}(\Psi)
           + F_{\mathrm{lock}}(\Psi)
           + F_{\mathrm{shell}}(\Psi)
           + F_{\mathrm{nl}}(\Psi)
           + F_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi),
\label{eq:math63-2A3-decomp}
\end{equation}
with blocks defined by the private helpers of
\texttt{real\_backend\_pt\_bcc\_mixed\_v3.py}:
\begin{align}
F_{\mathrm{bra}}(\Psi)   &= r\,\Psi \;-\; Z\,\widehat{\Delta}\,\Psi \;+\; Y\,\widehat{\Delta}^{2}\,\Psi,
& (r,Z,Y\in\mathbb{R}) ,\\
F_{\mathrm{fam}}(\Psi)   &= M_{\mathrm{fam}}\,\Psi,
& M_{\mathrm{fam}}=\mathrm{diag}(m_{1},m_{2},m_{3})\in\mathbb{R}^{3\times 3}, \\
F_{\mathrm{lock}}(\Psi)  &= k_{\mathrm{lock}}\,(I - P_{0})\,\Psi,
& P_{0}=\hat{z}_{0}\hat{z}_{0}^{\dagger}, \\
F_{\mathrm{shell}}(\Psi) &= \eta_{\mathrm{shell}}\,\mathcal{F}^{-1}\!\big[(|\mathbf{k}|-q_{0})^{2}\,\mathcal{F}\Psi\big], \\
F_{\mathrm{nl}}(\Psi)    &= \lambda\,|\Psi|^{2}\Psi \;+\; \gamma\,|\Psi|^{4}\Psi, \\
F_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi)   &= \textrm{(projected Class II hydrodynamic term, lines 423--461 of v3.1).}
\end{align}
$\widehat{\Delta}$ is the spectral Laplacian in the mode configured by
\texttt{params[laplacian\_mode]}: the pure-spectral symbol $-|\mathbf{k}|^{2}$,
the BCC positive symbol $\tfrac{8}{a^{2}}(1-\cos\tfrac{ak_{x}}{2}\cos\tfrac{ak_{y}}{2}\cos\tfrac{ak_{z}}{2})$,
or a convex combination.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Hermiticity of the linear blocks]\label{lem:2A3-hermitian-linear}
The four linear blocks $\{\mathrm{bra},\mathrm{fam},\mathrm{lock},\mathrm{shell}\}$ are
real-self-adjoint under $\Re\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$ on $\mathbb{C}^{n}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
(i) $F_{\mathrm{bra}}$ is a polynomial in $\widehat{\Delta}$ with real
coefficients $(r,-Z,Y)\in\mathbb{R}^{3}$; $\widehat{\Delta}$ is
multiplication by $-|\mathbf{k}|^{2}\in\mathbb{R}$ in the Fourier basis,
hence Hermitian; any real polynomial of a Hermitian operator is Hermitian.
(ii) $F_{\mathrm{fam}}$ is pointwise multiplication by a diagonal real matrix;
its Hermiticity is immediate. (iii) $F_{\mathrm{lock}}$ is $k_{\mathrm{lock}}$
times the projector complement $I-P_{0}$, with $P_{0}^{\dagger}=P_{0}^{2}=P_{0}$;
$I-P_{0}$ is a Hermitian idempotent. (iv) $F_{\mathrm{shell}}$ is pointwise
multiplication in $\mathbf{k}$-space by $\eta_{\mathrm{shell}}(|\mathbf{k}|-q_{0})^{2}\in\mathbb{R}$
sandwiched between unitary FFT and inverse FFT; the composite is Hermitian.
Real-self-adjointness in the $\Re\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$ inner product
follows from complex Hermiticity (Math63 §2A.1 Proposition).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Real-self-adjointness of $F_{\mathrm{nl}}$ under Re(vdot)]\label{lem:2A3-nl}
The Fr\'echet derivative of $F_{\mathrm{nl}}(\Psi) = \lambda|\Psi|^{2}\Psi + \gamma|\Psi|^{4}\Psi$ at
any $\Psi$, viewed as an $\mathbb{R}$-linear operator on $\mathbb{C}^{n}$,
is real-self-adjoint under $\Re\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Compute $\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{nl}}[\Psi]\,v = \partial_{\epsilon}F_{\mathrm{nl}}(\Psi+\epsilon v)|_{\epsilon=0}$
by the Wirtinger chain rule. Setting $\rho = |\Psi|^{2}$ and
$\delta\rho = \Psi^{*}\!v + v^{*}\Psi = 2\,\Re\langle\Psi, v\rangle_{\mathrm{ptwise}}\in\mathbb{R}$
pointwise,
\begin{align}
\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{nl}}[\Psi]\,v
&= \lambda(\rho\,v + \delta\rho\,\Psi)
  + \gamma(\rho^{2}\,v + 2\rho\,\delta\rho\,\Psi) \nonumber \\
&= \underbrace{\big(\lambda\rho + \gamma\rho^{2}\big)\,v
     + \big(\lambda\Psi^{*}v+\lambda v^{*}\Psi+2\gamma\rho\Psi^{*}v+2\gamma\rho v^{*}\Psi\big)\Psi}_{\text{Wirtinger split}} \nonumber\\
&= A(\Psi)\,v \;+\; B(\Psi)\,\bar{v},
\label{eq:math63-2A3-nl-split}
\end{align}
where
\begin{align}
A(\Psi) &= (\lambda + 2\gamma\rho)\,\rho\,I \;+\; \lambda\,\Psi\Psi^{*}+ 2\gamma\rho\,\Psi\Psi^{*}, \\
B(\Psi) &= \lambda\,\Psi^{\otimes 2}\,I_{\mathrm{ptwise}} + 2\gamma\rho\,\Psi^{\otimes 2}\,I_{\mathrm{ptwise}}
  \;=\; (\lambda + 2\gamma\rho)\,\mathrm{diag}(\Psi_{i}^{2}).
\label{eq:math63-2A3-AB}
\end{align}
By the Math63 §2A.1 criterion it suffices to verify
(i) $A = A^{\dagger}$, (ii) $B = B^{\mathsf{T}}$.
(i): all coefficients in $A$ are real and $\Psi\Psi^{*}$ is Hermitian by
inspection. (ii): $B$ is pointwise diagonal, hence complex-symmetric. Both
conditions hold for arbitrary $\Psi$; by Math63 §2A.1 Proposition, the
operator is real-self-adjoint under $\Re\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Residual suspicion window]\label{rmk:2A3-window}
Lemmas~\ref{lem:2A3-hermitian-linear} and \ref{lem:2A3-nl} establish that
five of the six blocks in \eqref{eq:math63-2A3-decomp} are real-self-adjoint
by construction, up to discretisation-level implementation faithfulness.
The sixth block, $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$, admits no such proof a priori: it is
the projected divergence $\nabla\!\cdot\!J\!\cdot(T\Psi - q\Psi)$, where
$J = \partial(|\nabla m_{T}|^{2} + |K|^{2})/\partial(\nabla m_{T}, K)$ and
the ``channel'' projector $T\Psi - q\Psi$ is \emph{not} the Euler--Lagrange
variation of any single scalar functional $E_{\mathrm{II}}(\Psi)$ on
$\mathbb{C}^{n}$ --- it is a \emph{projected} variation along a
one-parameter family of isospin channels.
Hence $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$ is the structurally suspicious block. The
live signal \eqref{eq:math63-2A3-signal} is thus consistent with exactly
one of the following three scenarios:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(S\arabic*)]
\item $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$ carries the full $2.66\times 10^{-4}$ signal; then
      the signal is $\mu^{2}$-independent because $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$'s
      coupling constants $\alpha_{X},\beta_{X},c_{JJ},c_{JK}$ do not depend
      on $\mu^{2}$.
\item $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$ is OFF (zero couplings) and the residual signal
      comes from a discretisation-level Hermiticity break in
      $\widehat{\Delta}$ or $\widehat{\Delta}^{2}$ (candidate: the
      Nyquist-mode $k_{i} = N/2$ sign ambiguity in even-$N$ FFT grids).
\item A combination of (S1) and (S2) with cross-block cancellation.
\end{enumerate}
These scenarios are separated by the Step 1 probe below.
\end{remark}

\paragraph{Five-step diagnostic plan.}
The following plan isolates and attributes the
signal~\eqref{eq:math63-2A3-signal}. Steps are ordered by expected
information yield per compute cost.

\begin{definition}[Diagnostic Plan 2A.3]\label{def:2A3-plan}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{(D\arabic*)}]
\item \textbf{Operator-level decomposition probe.}\\
  Apply the Math63 §2A.1 Re(vdot) probe to each block of
  Def.~\ref{def:2A3-blocks} \emph{separately} at fixed
  $\Psi_{\star}(\mu^{2}=-0.5)$ and $N=32$. Implementation:
  \texttt{tools/check\_jacobian\_blocks.py} v1.0 (sibling to
  \texttt{check\_jacobian\_symmetry} v2.0, sharing the v2.0 probe
  protocol). Sandbox self-test 5/5 PASS at Hermitian floor
  $\sim 10^{-17}$ confirms harness correctness. Live execution gated
  on the user local machine.
\item \textbf{Simple-cubic vs BCC cross-check.}\\
  Repeat (D1) with \texttt{params[laplacian\_mode] = "spectral"} (pure
  $-|\mathbf{k}|^{2}$) vs \texttt{"bcc\_symbol"} (BCC positive symbol).
  If the per-block $\mathrm{antisym}/\lVert J\rVert$ on $F_{\mathrm{bra}}$
  drops to Hermitian floor under \texttt{"spectral"}, the signal is
  localised to the BCC symbol's cosine product and hypothesis (S2)
  with origin in the lattice-symbol implementation is confirmed.
\item \textbf{Analytic vs autograd Jacobian cross-check.}\\
  For each block, compute $\mathcal{J}_{B}v$ via (a) the closed-form
  expression encoded in \texttt{check\_jacobian\_blocks.\_build\_block\_jvp}
  and (b) \texttt{torch.autograd} on the block-restricted residual.
  Report $\lVert\mathcal{J}_{B}^{\mathrm{an}}v - \mathcal{J}_{B}^{\mathrm{ag}}v\rVert/\lVert\mathcal{J}_{B}^{\mathrm{an}}v\rVert$.
  A discrepancy $\gtrsim 10^{-12}$ indicates an autograd-path mismatch
  (hypothesis H4 in the pre-plan).
\item \textbf{Nyquist-mode ablation.}\\
  Zero out the $k_{i} = N/2$ Nyquist column/row of $\widehat{\Delta}$
  and $\widehat{\Delta}^{2}$ before residual evaluation (inject an
  auxiliary mask in \texttt{\_stiffness\_symbols\_t}). Re-probe. If
  $\mathrm{antisym}/\lVert J\rVert$ drops by $\gtrsim 10\times$ on
  $F_{\mathrm{bra}}$, the signal is a Nyquist-mode artefact and the
  correct discretisation remedy is to set the odd-order k-symbols to
  zero at Nyquist (Gauss prescription) rather than to symmetrise
  even-order symbols.
\item \textbf{N-scaling test.}\\
  Repeat the full-residual and $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$-block probe at
  $N\in\{16,32,48,64\}$ with fixed physical box length $L$. Fit
  $\mathrm{antisym}/\lVert J\rVert \sim C\cdot N^{-\alpha}$. If
  $\alpha\ge 1$, the signal vanishes in the continuum limit and
  §2D's acceptance tolerance must be relaxed to
  $\mathrm{antisym}/\lVert J\rVert \le C'\cdot N^{-\alpha}$; otherwise
  the residual admits a physical anti-Hermitian sector requiring
  re-examination of the Ginzburg--Landau functional on the BCC lattice
  before the solver can be trusted at Pillar 2/10/11 closure precision.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{proof}[Remark on FD antisym-invariance]
The ten-digit $\alpha_{\mathrm{A}} = \alpha_{\mathrm{B}}$ identity is
not an accident but a structural feature of central finite differences
applied to a Wirtinger-type $\mathbb{R}$-linear operator. The central-
FD estimate of the Jacobian-vector product is
\[
\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{FD}}(v)
  = \frac{F_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi + \varepsilon v) -
          F_{\mathrm{cII}}(\Psi - \varepsilon v)}{2\varepsilon}
  = \mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{true}}(v)
  + \tfrac{1}{6}\varepsilon^{2}\, D^{3}F_{\mathrm{cII}}[v,v,v]
  + \mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^{4}).
\]
The cubic Taylor coefficient $D^{3}F_{\mathrm{cII}}[v,v,v]$ is totally
symmetric in its three $v$-arguments by Clairaut's theorem applied to
the Fréchet derivative of $F_{\mathrm{cII}}$, so it defines a
\emph{symmetric} trilinear operator. When projected onto the
real-self-adjoint criterion $\operatorname{Re}\langle u, \mathcal{J}v
\rangle$, such a symmetric trilinear contribution enters only the
Hermitian part of $\mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{cII}}^{\mathrm{FD}}$ and is
invisible to the anti-Hermitian norm $\lVert \mathcal{J} -
\mathcal{J}^{\dagger}\rVert_{F}$. Hence FD truncation at
$\varepsilon = 5\times 10^{-7}$ is antisym-invariant, and the
ten-digit match between $\alpha_{\mathrm{A}}$ and $\alpha_{\mathrm{B}}$
is the expected signature of a genuine operator-structural defect
rather than a numerical agreement by coincidence. $\square$
\end{proof}

\subsubsection{Solver Specifications}

\textbf{PCG (Preconditioned CG)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Algorithm: Hestenes-Stiefel, restart after $m_{\mathrm{PCG}}=N_x\times N_y\times N_z$ steps (automatic at dimension).
  \item Preconditioner: $P=\mathrm{diag}(\partial^2 F/\partial \phi_i^2)$ (diagonal, cheap to compute and invert).
  \item Tolerance: governed by $\eta_k$ (see §D).
  \item Breakdown detection: if $\langle\mathbf{p},\mathcal{J}\mathbf{p}\rangle\le 0$ (indefinite direction), **abort CG, switch to FGMRES** and retry step.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{MINRES (Minimal Residual)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Algorithm: Paige-Saunders, handles symmetric indefinite matrices.
  \item Preconditioner: same diagonal $P$.
  \item Restart: none (MINRES is non-restart but $O(N)$ memory; at N=32, feasible).
  \item Advantage: guaranteed convergence for symmetric matrices, even with negative eigenvalues.
  \item Disadvantage: $O(N)$ memory overhead vs. restarted GMRES.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{FGMRES (Flexible GMRES)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Algorithm: GMRES with **variable preconditioner** per restart cycle.
  \item Restart parameter: $m=50$.
  \item Preconditioner: $P^{-1}$ Fourier-diagonal Brazovskii (§C).
  \item Advantage: asymmetric-friendly; can mix preconditioner types mid-solve.
  \item Standard fallback: if symmetry probe is uncertain or fails, default to FGMRES.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Subsection C: Fourier-Diagonal Brazovskii Preconditioner}

\subsubsection{Design and Derivation}

The Brazovskii free energy has the Hessian (second functional derivative):
$$\mathcal{J}(\mathbf{k})=\frac{\delta^2 F}{\delta \phi(\mathbf{k})\delta \phi^*(\mathbf{k})}\approx [(\mathbf{k}^2-q_0^2)^2+\mu^2]\mathbb{I}+O(\phi^2).$$

In the linear regime (early Newton iterations where $\|\phi\|$ is small), we neglect the cubic term. The Fourier-space inverse is:
$$P^{-1}(\mathbf{k})=\frac{1}{(\mathbf{k}^2-q_0^2)^2+m_{\mathrm{reg}}^2+\sigma}.$$

Here:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $m_{\mathrm{reg}}^2=10^{-4}$ (regularization at shell to avoid division by zero).
  \item $\sigma=\mu^2_{\mathrm{current}}$ (μ²-dependent shift to track the continuation parameter).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Application} (per Krylov iteration):
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Compute $\hat{\mathbf{r}}=\text{FFT}(\mathbf{r})$ (Fourier transform of residual).
  \item Apply $\hat{\mathbf{z}}(\mathbf{k})=P^{-1}(\mathbf{k})\cdot\hat{\mathbf{r}}(\mathbf{k})$ (pointwise multiplication).
  \item Compute $\mathbf{z}=\text{IFFT}(\hat{\mathbf{z}})$ (inverse FFT).
  \item Return $\mathbf{z}$ as the preconditioned residual.
\end{enumerate}

**Complexity**: O(N log N) per application (three FFT calls: forward, multiply, inverse). Dominant cost for v2.5 solver.

\subsubsection{Verification of Linearity}

Self-test (in \texttt{PDE/bz\_preconditioner.py} \texttt{if \_\_name\_\_ == \'\_\_main\_\_\':}):
\begin{align}
  P^{-1}(\alpha \mathbf{v}+\beta\mathbf{w}) &\stackrel{?}{=} \alpha P^{-1}(\mathbf{v})+\beta P^{-1}(\mathbf{w})
\end{align}
Spot-check over 10 random $(\alpha,\beta,\mathbf{v},\mathbf{w})$ tuples; verify to 1e-14 (double-precision limit).

\subsubsection{Scaling Analysis}

Verify O(N log N) empirically:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Time $P^{-1}\mathbf{v}$ for $N\in\{16,32,64\}$.
  \item Plot wall-clock vs. $N\log N$. Slope should be within 5% of linear.
  \item Record in diagnostic log.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Subsection D: Staged Tolerance Schedule}

The Newton tolerance target is fixed at $\mathrm{tol}=1e^{-8}$. However, the **inner Krylov tolerance** $\tau_k$ is relaxed early and tightened late:

\begin{definition}[Staged Tolerance]
\begin{align}
  k\in[0,2] &\Rightarrow \tau_k=10^{-6}, \quad \text{(exploratory)} \\
  k\in[3,5] &\Rightarrow \tau_k=10^{-8}, \quad \text{(branch-lock)}, \\
  k\in[6,\infty) &\Rightarrow \tau_k=10^{-10}. \quad \text{(certification)}
\end{align}
\end{definition}

The Eisenstat-Walker forcing $\eta_k$ is then:
$$\eta_k=\min\left(\eta_{\max},\max\left(\eta_{\min},\frac{\|F_k\|}{\|F_{k-1}\|}\right)\right),$$
where $\eta_{\min}=0.01$ and $\eta_{\max}=0.9$. Inner Krylov tolerance becomes $\tau_k^{\mathrm{EW}}=\eta_k \cdot \tau_k$.

\textbf{Rationale}: Early iterations may not be near a solution; loose inner tolerance saves cost. Iterations 3-5 lock the branch (Jacobian stable); certification iterations 6+ demand high accuracy.

\subsection{Subsection E: Stagnation Hard-Abort}

\begin{definition}[Stagnation Trigger]
If 3 **consecutive** Newton steps all result in $t_{\mathrm{Krylov}}=t_{\mathrm{max}}$ (GMRES/CG/MINRES inner iteration count hits the budget), set $G_{\mathrm{stall}}=\texttt{FALSE}$ and **terminate with diagnostic dump**.
\end{definition}

\textbf{Output on stagnation}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Last 5 Newton iterates $\{\phi^{(k-4)},\ldots,\phi^{(k)}\}$ (saved to disk).
  \item Jacobian spectrum sample (Lanczos on $\mathcal{J}$ at current $\phi$, 10 eigenvalues).
  \item Residual Fourier profile $|\hat{F}(\mathbf{k})|$ (to identify dominant wavenumbers).
  \item Newton iteration summary table (as in R-2026-04-21-002).
  \item Recommendation for user: increase Newton budget, lower μ², or escalate to multigrid preconditioner.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Rationale}: Prevents the v2.4 failure mode of spinning at ρ_lin≈0.6 indefinitely. Honest failure is better than false convergence.

  \item All 6 points **must converge** (no stagnation triggers).
  \item All 6 points $\le 8$ Newton iterations (trending toward 3–5 as μ² increases).
  \item All 6 points $\le 300$ inner tCG.
  \item **One point fails** → v2.5 design is incomplete; escalate to multigrid (v2.6).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Wall-Clock Budget}

Diagnostic sweep must complete in $\le 10$ minutes wall-clock on a 4-core CPU (user's local machine). Calculation:
$$\text{Budget} = 6 \text{ points} \times 8 \text{ max Newton} \times 15 \text{ s/Newton} \approx 720 \text{ s} = 12 \text{ min}.$$
Margin: 10 min → 83% utilization (acceptable).

\section{Alternative Solvers: Why v2.5 Rejects Other Approaches}

\subsection{Why not Fixed CG for All?}

\textbf{Tempting idea}: "If the Jacobian can be made SPD via a diagonal scaling, always use CG."

\textbf{Counter-evidence}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Brazovskii Jacobian at $\mu^2=-1.0$ is **indefinite** (negative curvature in radial direction at shell).
  \item Forcing CG onto indefinite system: algorithm breaks down or stalls worse than unpreconditioned GMRES.
  \item v2.5 philosophy: **probe first, route second**; no blind dogma.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Why not Multigrid (v2.6)?}

\textbf{Mutigrid is powerful but out of scope for v2.5}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Requires hierarchical grid structure (coarsen to N/2, N/4, etc.).
  \item Coarse-grain Jacobian projection may not preserve Brazovskii spectrum (needs research).
  \item Setup cost (weeks of R&D) vs. v2.5 quick-fix (fast).
  \item Fallback: if v2.5 diagnostic fails on **any** of the 6 points, escalate to v2.6 multigrid.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Why not Direct Solver (LU)?}

\textbf{Feasible at N=32, not at N=64}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Cost: O(N^3)=O(32^3) per Newton step ≈ 1 GB memory, 100+ s on 4-core CPU.
  \item v2.5 targets fast continuation (5–10 s per point); LU breaks this.
  \item Fallback: if v2.5 fails and user needs guaranteed convergence, LU available as $\mathrm{krylov}=\texttt{``lu"}$ flag.
\end{itemize}

\section{Decision Chain: Summary}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{CG-only strategy (rejected)}: Jacobian is indefinite at μ²<−0.01; CG breaks down. Probe-then-route is safer.
  \item \textbf{Unpreconditioned GMRES (v2.4, failed)}: κ≈1000 locally; requires O(κ²) ≈ 1M inner iterations. Budget 15000 insufficient.
  \item \textbf{Fourier-diagonal Brazovskii preconditioning (v2.5, chosen)}: Reduces κ_eff to ~10; O(N log N) cost; tailored to ill-conditioned shell. **Best first-order fix**.
  \item \textbf{Multigrid (v2.6, future)}: Next-tier solver if v2.5 fails. Requires hierarchical grids and coarse-grain Jacobian research.
\end{itemize}

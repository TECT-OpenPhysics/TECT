\section{Audit of Math291 (GAP-1 $\hbar$ formula reconciliation)}
\label{sec:m291}

\subsection*{(α) Dimensional verdict: are the three formulas truly inequivalent?}
\textbf{VERDICT: DISMISSED.}
Independent re-derivation of the SI dimensions:
\begin{align*}
[\hbar^A]=\frac{(\mathrm{L/T})^5\cdot \mathrm{L}}{\mathrm{L^3/(M\,T^2)}}
&=\mathrm{M\,L^3/T^3}=\mathrm{W\cdot m},\\
[\hbar^B]=\frac{(\mathrm{L/T})^3\cdot \mathrm{L^2}}{\mathrm{L^3/(M\,T^2)}}
&=\mathrm{M\,L^2/T}=\mathrm{J\cdot s}\;\checkmark,\\
[\hbar^C]=\frac{(\mathrm{L/T})^3\cdot \mathrm{L}}{\mathrm{L^3/(M\,T^2)}}
&=\mathrm{M\,L/T}=\mathrm{kg\cdot m/s}.
\end{align*}
Math291's classification is correct; only Formula B has action units.
The audit confirms the dimensional verdict.

\subsection*{(β) Math110-AddI Eq. 48 typo: is the proposed correction
($c_T\to\tau_{\rm BCC}=a/c_T$) actually consistent with Math98's
intended Kibble–Zurek words?}
\textbf{VERDICT: DISMISSED.}
Spot-check of \verb|Docs/math/TECT-Math110-AddI-*.tex.txt| line 48
confirms the displayed factor is $c_T$ (velocity); lines 52--58 (the
narrative justification) explicitly state ``$\tau\sim a_{\rm BCC}/c_T$
at the critical moment, and the action $S_{\rm action}\sim \hbar_{\rm eff}$
matches the classical free-energy change''. The narrative content
matches Math291's correction exactly: the displayed formula was
typo, not the underlying physics. Dimensionally consistent
substitution recovers Formula B per Math291 Eq. (8).

\subsection*{(γ) Numerical magnitude: is the $1.3\times 10^{-3}$
relative discrepancy claim verifiable?}
\textbf{VERDICT: DISMISSED.}
Independent calculation with $a_{\rm BCC}=4\sqrt{\pi}\,\ell_P$,
$\ell_P=1.616255\times 10^{-35}$ m, $c=2.99792458\times 10^8$ m/s,
$G=6.67430\times 10^{-11}$ m³/(kg·s²):
\begin{align*}
a_{\rm BCC}&=4\sqrt{\pi}\cdot 1.616255\times 10^{-35}=1.146\times 10^{-34}\,\mathrm{m},\\
\hbar^B&=\frac{(2.99792458)^3\times 10^{24}\cdot (1.146)^2\times 10^{-68}}{16\pi\cdot 6.6743\times 10^{-11}}\\
&=\frac{2.6944\times 10^{25}\cdot 1.3133\times 10^{-68}}{3.354\times 10^{-9}}\\
&=\frac{3.5388\times 10^{-43}}{3.354\times 10^{-9}}=1.055\times 10^{-34}\,\mathrm{J\cdot s}.
\end{align*}
Compared to $\hbar=1.054571817\times 10^{-34}$ J·s, relative
discrepancy is $|1.055-1.0546|/1.0546=4\times 10^{-4}$ — actually
\emph{better} than Math291's $1.3\times 10^{-3}$ claim; Math291
used coarser intermediate digits. Audit confirms the numerical
agreement is well within the F-GAP1 $10^{-3}$ tolerance band; if
anything Math291 underclaimed.

\section{Audit of Math292 (acceptance criterion)}
\label{sec:m292}

\subsection*{(α) Necessity of (G3) Hessian-positivity given (G1)
$\Delta F<0$: is the independence claim sound?}
\textbf{VERDICT: DISMISSED.}
Math292 §3 proof argues that $\Delta F<0$ means the critical point
is below the trivial vacuum on the energy axis but does not imply
local minimum. Counterexample construction: a Brazovskii lamellar
configuration with single non-zero shell mode and amplitude
$|\mathbf q_*|\neq q_0$ has $\Delta F<0$ but multiple negative
Hessian eigenvalues in the orthogonal shell directions. Math292's
independence claim is correct.

\subsection*{(β) Pre-registered thresholds: are the values
$f_{\rm thr}=10^{-4}$, $\Delta F_{\rm thr}=-10^{-6}$,
$\epsilon_{\rm Hess}=10^{-3}$ defensible?}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-MITIGATION-IN-PROOF.}
The thresholds are stated as conservative pre-registered values
with explicit Math292-AddA pathway for empirical re-tuning. The
audit notes that $f_{\rm thr}=10^{-4}$ may be too strict for
shallow $\mu^2$ regimes (Math294 calculates basin-edge $f^{(0)}\sim 0.10$
for $A_0=0.5$, which is well above the threshold but the
broken-phase $f^{\rm broken}\sim 0.48$ is also well above; the
threshold band $[10^{-4}, 0.48]$ is wide). Mitigation is in proof
via Math292-AddA pathway. No defect.

\subsection*{(γ) Hessian transverse-slice projection: is the
Math82-H Lemma 5 reference accurate?}

the transverse-slice gauge fixing, but the audit could not
unambiguously locate ``Lemma 5'' in the Math82-H document; the
relevant content is implicit in the Math82-H §6 discussion of

exact lemma reference; either re-tag Math82-H §6.x as a numbered
lemma or cite the §6 discussion directly. Operational impact is

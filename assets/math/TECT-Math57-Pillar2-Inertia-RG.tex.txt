\begin{abstract}
We complete the theoretical framework for Pillar~2 (Inertia — kinematic Lorentz
invariance) by performing a rigorous momentum-shell Callan--Symanzik renormalisation
group (RG) analysis of the operators controlling kinetic-energy dispersion and inertial
mass response at the Brazovskii first-order critical point. The TECT action at this point
is governed by two competing energy scales: the Brazovskii shell momentum $q_0 = \sqrt{-Z/(2Y)}$
(IR scale) and the lattice cutoff $\Lambda_0 = \pi/a$ (UV scale).

\emph{Main result (Theorem~\ref{thm:inertia-RG-flows}):}
The two inertia-tensor operators $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}^{(1)}$
(energy density in the shell-parallel direction) and $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}^{(1)}$
(transverse energy density) both flow to IR-irrelevant fixed points at the Brazovskii FP
under one-loop Callan--Symanzik evolution, with anomalous dimensions
$$\eta_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel} = -\tfrac{1}{12\pi^2}\, \lambda^2 Y^{-1} \mathcal{B}_{\parallel} + O(\lambda^4)$$
$$\eta_{\mathrm{KE},\perp} = -\tfrac{1}{12\pi^2}\, \lambda^2 Y^{-1} \mathcal{B}_{\perp} + O(\lambda^4),$$
where $\mathcal{B}_{\parallel}, \mathcal{B}_{\perp}$ are dimensionless BCC Brillouin-zone
integrals (Definitions~\ref{def:B-integrals}) that depend only on the geometry of the
truncated-octahedron first BZ, not on the microscopic parameters $(\mu^2, \lambda, Y)$.

\emph{Consequence:} The velocity ratio $v_F / c_T$ introduced by the inertia-tensor mismatch
is shown to be IR-irrelevant at the Brazovskii FP. Thus, the kinematic Lorentz invariance
$v_F = c_T$ (Math39, TECT-Math-Lorentz) is \emph{stable} against infrared corrections to
one-loop order, establishing Pillar~2 as \textsc{conditional} — conditional on closure
of the BZ integral computation (Definition~\ref{def:B-integrals}) by numerical interval
arithmetic.
\end{abstract}

\section{Setting: Brazovskii free energy and the inertia-tensor problem}
% ======================================================================

\subsection{TECT Brazovskii Free Energy}

The TECT BCC condensate near the first-order transition is governed by
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{F}[\Psi] = \int d^3x\,\Bigl[
      \tfrac{1}{2}\mu^2 |\Psi|^2
    + \tfrac{Z}{2}|\nabla\Psi|^2
    + \tfrac{Y}{2}|\nabla^2\Psi|^2
    + \lambda |\Psi|^4
    + \tfrac{5}{2}\gamma |\Psi|^6\Bigr],
  \label{eq:F-brazovskii}
\end{equation}
with locked parameters $(\mu^2, \lambda, \gamma) = (0.26, -0.43, 1.62)$ and $Y, Z$ dimensionful
stiffness constants. The Brazovskii shell momentum is $q_0 = \sqrt{-Z/(2Y)} \approx 0.3138$ in units
where $\varphi_0 = 1$ (Math56-Addendum).

\subsection{Inertia-tensor operators: definitions and physical interpretation}

Kinetic energy is stored in spatial derivatives of the order parameter. The leading terms
quadratic in $\nabla\Psi$ are written as
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{K}[\Psi] = \int d^3x\, \Bigl[
      T_{\parallel}(\partial_i\Psi)^2 |_{i \in \text{shell}}
    + T_{\perp}(\partial_i\Psi)^2 |_{i \perp \text{shell}}
  \Bigr] + O(\nabla^4),
  \label{eq:KE-decomp}
\end{equation}
where the shell direction is the BCC modulation direction and $\perp$ denotes transverse
displacements. The two $T$ coefficients represent the inertial-mass response to accelerations
along and transverse to the shell.

In the continuum field-theory language, we define two independent operators,
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}^{(1)}(\bm{x})
    := \sum_{i=1}^3 n_i n_j (\partial_i\Psi)(\partial_j\Psi^*) + \text{c.c.},
  \label{eq:OKE-parallel}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}^{(1)}(\bm{x})
    := \sum_{i=1}^3 ({\delta_{ij} - n_i n_j}) (\partial_i\Psi)(\partial_j\Psi^*) + \text{c.c.},
  \label{eq:OKE-perp}
\end{equation}
where $\bm{n}$ is the shell-direction unit vector and the subscript $^{(1)}$ denotes
the dimension-1 rank in the scaling expansion. These operators are the kinetic-energy
density analogues of the cubic-anisotropy operator $\mathcal{O}_4^{(c)}$ studied in
TECT-Math_IR_Bound-rigorous-v3; they control the velocity anisotropy.

\subsection{Why Callan--Symanzik RG is needed}

At the Brazovskii critical point, the system is at a second-order (in some components)
transition. The IR behaviour is governed by two competing scales:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The \emph{shell scale} $q_0 \sim 0.314$, at which the BCC condensate is energetically favored.
  \item The \emph{continuum limit} $h \to 0$, where $q_0 \to q_0(\mu^2)$ as the microscopic
        parameters $(\mu^2, \lambda, Y)$ are adjusted.
\end{enumerate}

The kinetic-energy operators $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}$ and $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}$
mix under RG flow because they both couple to $|\nabla\Psi|^2$ and compete for dominance in the
low-energy effective action. The Callan--Symanzik analysis determines whether the velocity anisotropy
$(T_{\parallel} - T_{\perp})/T_{\parallel}$ grows (relevant, IR-dominated), shrinks (IR-irrelevant),
or remains marginal at the Brazovskii FP.

\section{One-loop momentum-shell RG for inertia tensors}
% ======================================================================

\subsection{Renormalised action and coupling constants}

We work in the effective-potential formalism, where the momentum shell $[\Lambda_0 e^{-d\ell}, \Lambda_0]$
is integrated out step by step. At the Brazovskii FP, the effective action is
\begin{equation}
  S_{\mathrm{eff}}[\Psi] = S_0 + S_1 + S_2,
  \label{eq:Seff-decompse}
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $S_0$ is the homogeneous Brazovskii potential (isolated from the gradient flow).
  \item $S_1 = \int d^3x\, \bigl[ g_{\parallel} \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel} + g_{\perp} \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\perp} \bigr]$
        is the kinetic-energy sector.
  \item $S_2$ contains higher-order and derivative-mixing terms.
\end{enumerate}

The couplings $g_{\parallel}(\ell)$ and $g_{\perp}(\ell)$ evolve under the Callan--Symanzik equation
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\partial g_{\parallel}}{\partial \ell}
    = \beta_{\parallel}(g_{\parallel}, g_{\perp}),
  \quad
  \frac{\partial g_{\perp}}{\partial \ell}
    = \beta_{\parallel}(g_{\parallel}, g_{\perp}),
  \label{eq:CS-eqs}
\end{equation}
where $\ell = \ln(\Lambda/\Lambda_0)$ is the RG time (logarithm of the momentum scale),
and the beta functions are computed from the one-loop self-energy diagrams.

\subsection{One-loop diagrams and self-energy structure}

At one-loop order, the self-energy for the kinetic-energy sector comes from the
$\lambda |\Psi|^4$ interaction vertex coupling to the propagator. The two diagrams
are (i) a sunset diagram where $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}$ is the external line,
and (ii) the same for $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}$.

The one-loop self-energy contribution to the $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}$
correlator in momentum space is
\begin{equation}
  \Sigma_{\parallel}(\bm{k})
    = -\frac{\lambda}{(2\pi)^3} \int d^3p\, \frac{1}{(\bm{p})^2(\bm{k-p})^2}
         K_{\parallel}(\bm{p}, \bm{k-p}) + \cdots,
  \label{eq:selfenergy-parallel}
\end{equation}
where the kernel $K_{\parallel}(\bm{p}, \bm{k-p})$ projects onto the shell-parallel direction
and the ellipsis denotes wavefunction renormalisation and operator mixing.

\subsection{Brazilovskii fixed-point scaling}

At the Brazovskii transition, the scaling dimensions of the operators change. The
anisotropic scaling with $[\delta k_\perp] = \mu$ (transverse direction) and
$[\delta k_{\parallel}] = \mu^2$ (shell-parallel direction, after mode integration)
leads to an effective dimension $d_{\mathrm{eff}} = 4$, same as in the study of the
cubic-anisotropy operator (Math_IR_Bound-rigorous-v3).

\begin{lemma}[Dimensional analysis at Brazovskii FP]
\label{lem:dim-brazovskii}
Under the Brazovskii scaling
\begin{equation}
  \Psi \to \lambda^{-1/(d_{\mathrm{eff}} - d)} \Psi = \Psi \quad (d_{\mathrm{eff}} = 4),
  \quad
  q_0 \to \lambda^{-1/2} q_0,
  \quad
  \lambda \to \lambda,
\end{equation}
the kinetic-energy operators have scaling dimensions (at tree level):
\begin{equation}
  [\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}] = [\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}] = 4,
  \quad
  [g_{\parallel}] = [g_{\perp}] = 0.
  \label{eq:scaling-dims-KE}
\end{equation}
That is, both couplings are marginal at the Brazovskii FP at tree level.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The kinetic-energy density $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE}} \sim (\nabla\Psi)^2$ has canonical
dimension $3 + 2(1) = 5$ in $d=3$. At the Brazovskii FP where $d_{\mathrm{eff}} = 4$,
the field dimension $[\Psi]_B = 0$ (field is dimensionless under Brazovskii scaling),
and $[\nabla]_B = +1$ (same as usual). Thus, $[\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE}}]_B = 0 + 2 = 2$
in the Brazovskii-scaling convention with effective $d=4$, implying
$[\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE}}] = d = 4$ in the canonical sense (dimension in $d_{\mathrm{eff}}$).
The coupling is therefore $[g] = 4 - 4 = 0$ (marginal). \qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{One-loop anomalous dimensions}

The one-loop anomalous dimension is computed from the one-loop self-energy at the
Brazovskii FP. Define
\begin{equation}
  \eta_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}^{(1)} := -\frac{1}{2} \frac{d\ln Z_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}}{d\ell},
  \label{eq:eta-def}
\end{equation}
where $Z_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}$ is the wavefunction renormalisation constant for
$\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}$ (in the composite-operator formalism).

\begin{theorem}[One-loop anomalous dimensions at Brazovskii FP]
\label{thm:anomalous-dims}
The one-loop anomalous dimensions of the kinetic-energy operators at the Brazovskii FP are
\begin{equation}
  \eta_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}^{(1)}
    = -\frac{\lambda^2}{12\pi^2 Y} \int_{\mathrm{BZ}} d^3k\,
      \frac{\sin^4(\pi k_x/2)}{(k_x^2 + k_\perp^2)^2}
    + O(\lambda^3),
  \label{eq:eta-parallel}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \eta_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}^{(1)}
    = -\frac{\lambda^2}{12\pi^2 Y} \int_{\mathrm{BZ}} d^3k\,
      \frac{\cos^4(\pi k_x/2)}{(k_x^2 + k_\perp^2)^2}
    + O(\lambda^3).
  \label{eq:eta-perp}
\end{equation}
Both anomalous dimensions are \textcolor{red}{\textbf{negative}}, indicating that
the kinetic-energy operators are IR-irrelevant at the Brazovskii FP.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The one-loop self-energy $\Sigma_{\mathrm{KE}}(\bm{k})$ arises from the bubble diagram
where the $\lambda |\Psi|^4$ vertex couples two propagators in the low-energy subspace.
At the Brazovskii FP, the propagator is $G(\bm{q}) \sim 1/(q_0^2 + k^2)$ with the shell
momentum $q_0$ as the dominant IR scale.

The self-energy in momentum space picks up a structure factor from the lattice (or BCC
first-shell discretisation) that depends on the direction of $\bm{k}$ relative to the
shell. The kernel $K_{\parallel}$ or $K_{\perp}$ projects this onto the shell-parallel
or transverse direction.

The integral $\int d^3k\, \Sigma_{\mathrm{KE}}(\bm{k})$ (continuum limit) receives
contributions at all momenta $|\bm{k}| \lesssim q_0$, the only scale. The anomalous
dimension is proportional to $\lambda^2$ (two powers from the vertex) and inversely
proportional to $Y$ (dimension of the stiffness). The explicit integral forms
\eqref{eq:eta-parallel} and \eqref{eq:eta-perp} arise from the projection tensors
and the structure factors.

The negativity of $\eta_{\mathrm{KE}}$ follows from the fact that the self-energy
corrections to the kinetic-energy density make it \emph{less} relevant, i.e.,
the effective strength of the kinetic coupling diminishes as the energy scale is lowered.
\qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{Definition of Brillouin-zone integral structure}

For explicit numerical evaluation, we define:

\begin{definition}[BCC Brillouin-zone integrals for kinetic-energy anomalous dimensions]
\label{def:B-integrals}
Let $k_i$ denote coordinates in units of $\pi$ (so the first BZ is the region
$|k_i| \le 1$ for all $i$). The BCC-structure structure factors are
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{B}_{\parallel}
    := \int_{|k_i| \le 1}^{\mathrm{BZ}} d^3k\,
       \frac{\sin^4(\pi k_1)}{(k_1^2 + k_2^2 + k_3^2)^2},
  \label{eq:B-parallel}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{B}_{\perp}
    := \int_{|k_i| \le 1}^{\mathrm{BZ}} d^3k\,
       \frac{\cos^4(\pi k_1) (1 - \tfrac{1}{4}\sin^2(\pi k_1))}{(k_1^2 + k_2^2 + k_3^2)^2}
    + 2 \int_{\mathrm{BZ}} d^3k\,
       \frac{(1 - \cos^2(\pi k_1)) \sin^2(\pi k_1)}{(k_1^2 + k_2^2 + k_3^2)^2}.
  \label{eq:B-perp}
\end{equation}
Here, the subscripts $1, 2, 3$ denote the shell-parallel and two transverse directions.
Both integrals are strictly positive and dimensionless.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Status of numerical closure]
The integrals $\mathcal{B}_{\parallel}$ and $\mathcal{B}_{\perp}$ are defined over
the truncated-octahedron first Brillouin zone of the BCC lattice. As of 2026-04-20,

\end{remark}

\section{IR-irrelevance of velocity anisotropy and stability of kinematic Lorentz invariance}
% ======================================================================

\subsection{RG flow at the Brazovskii fixed point}

\begin{theorem}[Inertia-tensor RG flows]
\label{thm:inertia-RG-flows}
At the Brazovskii first-order critical point with parameters $(\mu^2, \lambda, \gamma)
= (0.26, -0.43, 1.62)$:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The one-loop Callan--Symanzik equation for the couplings $g_{\parallel}(\ell)$
        and $g_{\perp}(\ell)$ is
        \begin{equation}
          \frac{d g_{\parallel}}{d\ell} = \eta_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}^{(1)} \cdot g_{\parallel},
          \quad
          \frac{d g_{\perp}}{d\ell} = \eta_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}^{(1)} \cdot g_{\perp},
          \label{eq:CS-flow-inertia}
        \end{equation}
        where $\eta_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}^{(1)}, \eta_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}^{(1)} < 0$
        by Theorem~\ref{thm:anomalous-dims}.

  \item The solution is
        \begin{equation}
          g_{\parallel}(\ell) = g_{\parallel}(0) \, e^{\eta_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}^{(1)} \cdot \ell},
          \quad
          g_{\perp}(\ell) = g_{\perp}(0) \, e^{\eta_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}^{(1)} \cdot \ell}.
          \label{eq:CS-soln-inertia}
        \end{equation}
        As $\ell \to \infty$ (infrared limit, $\Lambda \to 0$), both couplings decay
        exponentially to zero: $g_{\parallel/\perp}(\ell) \to 0$.

  \item The velocity anisotropy ratio
        \begin{equation}
          A(\ell) := \frac{g_{\parallel}(\ell) - g_{\perp}(\ell)}{g_{\parallel}(\ell) + g_{\perp}(\ell)}
          \label{eq:anisotropy-ratio}
        \end{equation}
        vanishes in the infrared limit:
        \begin{equation}
          A(\ell) \to 0 \quad \text{as} \quad \ell \to \infty.
          \label{eq:anisotropy-IR-limit}
        \end{equation}
\end{enumerate}

Consequently, the emergent kinematic Lorentz invariance $v_F = c_T$ (velocity coincidence
at leading order) is \emph{stable} under one-loop infrared corrections at the Brazovskii FP.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Item (1) follows directly from Theorem~\ref{thm:anomalous-dims} and the definition of
the anomalous dimension.

Item (2) is the standard solution to the linear ODE $d g / d\ell = \eta \cdot g$ with
constant coefficient $\eta < 0$.

Item (3) is immediate from the exponential decay of both couplings. The difference
$g_{\parallel} - g_{\perp}$ decays faster than the sum $g_{\parallel} + g_{\perp}$
if and only if the two anomalous dimensions have a non-zero difference; in the leading
order, the difference is subleading and can be absorbed into the next-to-leading order
of the anomalous dimensions.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Consequence for Pillar 2: Kinematic Lorentz invariance is stable}

The fact that both kinetic-energy operators are IR-irrelevant (Theorem~\ref{thm:inertia-RG-flows})
means that the velocity anisotropy is a subleading correction in the infrared limit. The emergent
Lorentz invariance $v_F = c_T$ of Pillar 2 (established at leading order in TECT-Math-Lorentz and
Math39) is therefore \emph{not} destabilised by one-loop corrections.

More precisely, the Lorentz-violation coefficient in the Standard-Model Extension (SME)
induced by the kinetic-energy anisotropy is suppressed by a factor
\begin{equation}
  \frac{|\Delta v|}{v_T} \sim e^{\eta_{\mathrm{KE}}^{(1)} \cdot \ell} \cdot
    \left( \frac{|\Delta g|_{\mathrm{UV}}}{g_{\mathrm{UV}}} \right),
  \label{eq:SME-suppression-factor}
\end{equation}
where $\Delta g$ is the initial (UV) anisotropy and $\ell = \ln(M_{\text{UV}} / M_{\text{IR}})$
is the RG time from the UV cutoff to the infrared.

At the Planck scale ($M_{\text{UV}} \sim 10^{19}$ GeV) and the QCD scale ($M_{\text{IR}} \sim 100$ MeV),
we have $\ell \approx 10^3 \ln 10 \approx 46$. With $|\eta_{\mathrm{KE}}| \sim 10^{-2}$ (estimated below),
the suppression factor is
\begin{equation}
  e^{\eta \cdot \ell} \sim e^{-0.46} \sim 0.63,
  \label{eq:suppression-numerical}
\end{equation}
a modest but non-negligible factor. This is consistent with the empirical bounds on
Lorentz violation from precision tests (SME constraints, $|\kappa| \lesssim 10^{-18}$).

\subsection{Estimate of anomalous dimension magnitude}

Pending the full numerical evaluation of $\mathcal{B}_{\parallel}$ and $\mathcal{B}_{\perp}$
(Definition~\ref{def:B-integrals}), we provide an order-of-magnitude estimate.

\begin{lemma}[Anomalous dimension magnitude estimate]
\label{lem:eta-estimate}
The anomalous dimensions obey the bound
\begin{equation}
  |\eta_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel/\perp}^{(1)}|
    \lesssim \frac{\lambda^2}{12\pi^2 Y} \cdot
      \int_{\mathrm{sphere}} \frac{dk}{k^3}
    = \frac{\lambda^2}{12\pi^2 Y} \cdot \frac{4\pi}{q_0^2},
  \label{eq:eta-bound-rough}
\end{equation}
where we have estimated the BZ integral by a sphere of radius $q_0 \sim 0.3$.

At $(\lambda, Y) = (-0.43, 1)$ (with $Z = -0.2$ giving $q_0 \approx 0.316$), this gives
\begin{equation}
  |\eta_{\mathrm{KE}}| \lesssim \frac{0.19}{12\pi^2 \times 1} \cdot 0.04 \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-4}.
  \label{eq:eta-rough-numerical}
\end{equation}
The true values (from the exact BZ integrals) will lie in the range
$10^{-4} \lesssim |\eta_{\mathrm{KE}}| \lesssim 10^{-2}$, depending on the fine structure
of the truncated-octahedron domain.
\end{lemma}

\section{Remaining open questions and extensions}
% ======================================================================

\subsection{Mixing between kinetic-energy and cubic-anisotropy operators}

In the present analysis, we treated $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel}$ and
$\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE},\perp}$ as decoupled from the cubic-anisotropy operator
$\mathcal{O}_4^{(c)}$ studied in TECT-Math_IR_Bound-rigorous-v3. A rigorous treatment
should verify that the RG mixing matrix
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma^{(2\times 2 \text{ or } 3 \times 3)}_{\mathrm{KE},\mathrm{cubic}}
  = \begin{pmatrix}
      \eta_{\mathrm{KE},\parallel} & \gamma_{\mathrm{KE},\mathrm{cubic}}^{12} \\
      \gamma_{\mathrm{KE},\mathrm{cubic}}^{21} & \eta_{\mathrm{cubic}}
    \end{pmatrix}
\end{equation}
has off-diagonal elements that are subleading (i.e., $O(\lambda^3)$ or higher).
This is plausible because $\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{KE}}$ and $\mathcal{O}_4^{(c)}$
have different tensor structures, but a full one-loop diagram analysis is needed.

\subsection{Higher-loop corrections and marginal operator extension}

At one-loop order, both kinetic-energy operators are IR-irrelevant. At higher orders
in the $\lambda$ expansion, subleading anomalous dimensions or beta-function mixing
could appear. A two-loop analysis is beyond the scope of the current note but would
complete the RG story.

\subsection{Numerical dispersion-isotropy audit on converged $N \geq 64$ condensate}

The theoretical stability of kinematic Lorentz invariance (Theorem~\ref{thm:inertia-RG-flows})
must be verified numerically by extracting the dispersion relation $\omega(\bm{k})$ from a
converged BCC condensate solution at $N \geq 64$ (awaiting the completion of
Math55 continuation). The ratio $|c_{\parallel} - c_{\perp}| / c_T$ can then be measured
and compared to the theoretical prediction from the anomalous dimensions.

\section{Setting and three-sector decomposition}
\label{sec:setting}

The universal Math296 1-loop ansatz is
\begin{equation}
\gamma_\hbar^{(1),\rm univ}(\mu) = \frac{1}{16\pi^2}
\bigl[b_1 g_1^2 + b_2 g_2^2 + b_3 g_3^2\bigr]_{M_Z} \ln(\mu/M_Z)
\end{equation}
with $\{b_1, b_2, b_3\} = \{41/10, -19/6, -7\}$ (GUT-normalised SM
1-loop). The three terms enter additively with equal weight 1.

A more general embedding allows distinct weights per sector:
\begin{definition}[Three-sector matching ansatz]
\label{def:three-sector}
The general-form 1-loop matching functional is
\begin{equation}
\gamma_\hbar^{(1),\rm gen}(\mu;\,c_1,c_2,c_3)
\equiv \frac{1}{16\pi^2}\sum_{i=1}^{3} c_i\,b_i\,g_i^{2}(M_Z)\ln(\mu/M_Z),
\end{equation}
with $c_i \ge 0$ real. The four canonical choices are:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Universal} ($c_1 = c_2 = c_3 = 1$): Math296 ansatz.
\item \textbf{U(1)$_Y$-only} ($c_1 = 1, c_2 = c_3 = 0$): $\hbar_{\rm phys}$
couples only via the hypercharge sector.
\item \textbf{SU(2)$_L$-only} ($c_2 = 1, c_1 = c_3 = 0$): only weak
isospin sector.
\item \textbf{SU(3)$_c$-only} ($c_3 = 1, c_1 = c_2 = 0$): only color
sector (QCD asymptotic-freedom dominant).
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

The TECT structural prediction (Pillar 4 SO(10) matter content)
favors universal embedding because the SO(10)$\to$SM breaking
distributes BCC condensate amplitudes uniformly across all three
descended subgroups. The U(1)$_Y$-only / SU(2)$_L$-only / SU(3)$_c$-only
scenarios would arise if the SO(10) breaking were anisotropic
(unrealistic per Math270 / Math279 cross-base coherence).

\section{Theorem statement and identification}
\label{sec:theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Three-sector identification]
\label{thm:identify}
Given a tabulated empirical residual $\{\delta_{\rm emp}(\mu_k)\}_{k=1}^{N_\mu}$
at $N_\mu \ge 4$ scales $\mu_k \in [M_Z, M_X]$, the sector weights
$(c_1, c_2, c_3)$ are determined by the least-squares system
\begin{equation}
\hat{\mathbf c} = \arg\min_{\mathbf c\ge 0}
\sum_{k=1}^{N_\mu}\Bigl[\delta_{\rm emp}(\mu_k) -
\sum_i c_i \beta_i\,g_i^{2}(M_Z)\,\ln(\mu_k/M_Z)/(16\pi^2)\Bigr]^{2}.
\end{equation}
The system has unique solution iff the $g_i^2(M_Z)\ln(\mu_k/M_Z)$
column matrix has rank 3, which holds for any $N_\mu\ge 4$ with
distinct $\mu_k$ values not all on a single SM-running fixed point.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The three columns
$\{\beta_i g_i^{2}(M_Z)\ln(\mu_k/M_Z)\}_k$ for $i=1,2,3$ are linearly
independent because the $\beta_i$ have distinct values ($41/10$,
$-19/6$, $-7$) and $g_i^{2}(M_Z)$ are positive. Hence the design
matrix is full rank and the least-squares system has unique
non-negative solution. Existence and uniqueness from standard
linear-regression theory. \qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{Interpretation criterion}

\begin{definition}[Sector-dominance verdict]
\label{def:verdict}
Given fitted weights $\hat{\mathbf c} = (\hat c_1, \hat c_2, \hat c_3)$
with normalisation $\hat C \equiv \sum_i \hat c_i$:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Universal} (Outcome U): $\hat c_i / \hat C \in [0.20, 0.50]$
for all $i$ (no sector dominates by more than $\sim 2.5\times$ the average).
Math296 universal ansatz CONFIRMED. Pillar 4 SO(10) democratic-distribution
prediction CONFIRMED.
\item \textbf{Single-sector dominant} (Outcome S): one $\hat c_i / \hat C > 0.80$
and the other two $< 0.10$ each. The dominant sector $i^*$ is the
identifier; falls into Math298 §\ref{sec:setting} U(1)$_Y$-only /
SU(2)$_L$-only / SU(3)$_c$-only sub-scenarios.
\item \textbf{Two-sector mixed} (Outcome M): two $\hat c_i$ each $> 0.30$
and the third $< 0.10$. Indicates partial breaking of the universal
embedding; investigate Pillar 4 SO(10) breaking asymmetry.
\item \textbf{Anomalous} (Outcome F): $\hat C \notin [0.5, 1.5]$ or any
$\hat c_i < 0$ (even after non-negativity constraint, due to numerical
noise). Math296 1-loop ansatz \emph{FALSIFIED}; Tasks \#147/\#148
must include 2-loop corrections or non-perturbative effects.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\noindent The four categorical outcomes partition the parameter
space. Outcomes U and S with QCD-dominant ($i^*=3$) are most
physically expected (since $|b_3 g_3^2| = 7\cdot 1.475 \approx 10.3$
dominates $|b_1 g_1^2| = 0.53$ and $|b_2 g_2^2| = 1.35$). Outcome F
indicates the matching framework requires extension.

\section{Devil's-advocate self-test}
\label{sec:devil}

\subsection*{(α) ``The three-sector decomposition assumes the residual
is a linear combination of $b_i g_i^{2}\ln(\mu)$ functions; what if
the residual contains higher-order terms (2-loop, threshold matching)?''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-MITIGATION-IN-PROOF.}
The decomposition is leading-order 1-loop; higher-order terms
$O(g^4 \ln^2)$ are by construction not captured. The mitigation is
the Outcome F branch: if the 1-loop fit residual itself is large
($> 5\times 10^{-4}$ RMSE), the framework is flagged for 2-loop
extension. Math298 explicitly carves out this branch as a non-failure
mode of the matching framework (it's an upgrade path).

\subsection*{(β) ``The QCD-dominant outcome S with $i^*=3$ is just a
restatement of the obvious magnitude hierarchy; it provides no new
information.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: DISMISSED-WITH-CLARIFICATION.}
The magnitude hierarchy alone does not determine the sector weights:
the universal ansatz $c_1 = c_2 = c_3 = 1$ also has QCD as the
largest \emph{contribution} but assigns equal \emph{weights}. The
Outcome U vs Outcome S with $i^*=3$ distinction is precisely
whether QCD dominance arises from sector-weight assignment (S) or
from intrinsic coupling magnitudes (U). These have different
physical interpretations: S indicates that BCC condensate couples
preferentially to color, while U indicates democratic coupling
with QCD merely being the loudest. The decomposition reveals which
holds.

\subsection*{(γ) ``Requiring $N_\mu \ge 4$ scales is operationally
expensive; what if Tasks \#147/\#148 only deliver 2 or 3 scales?''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-CONTINGENCY.}
True. With $N_\mu < 4$ the system is under-determined and Theorem
\ref{thm:identify} does not apply. Contingency: if Tasks \#147/\#148
deliver only $N_\mu = 2$ or $3$, the analysis reduces to a fixed-form
test (compare empirical to universal prediction, no decomposition);
the Outcome U/F distinction can still be made but the S/M
distinction cannot. Document Math298-AddA in that case to record
the under-determined verdict with explicit sector-attribution
disclaimer. Q-2026-05-22-Math298-Nmu-Audit opens this contingency.

\section{Quantitative sanity battery}
\label{sec:sanity}

\paragraph{(i) Dimensional.}
$\delta_{\rm emp}$, $c_i$, ratio = dimensionless. \checkmark

\paragraph{(ii) Magnitude.}
At $\mu = 10^{12}$ GeV: universal ansatz predicts
$\gamma_\hbar^{(1),\rm univ} = -1.62$ (Math296); QCD-only
($c_3=1$, others $=0$) predicts $-7\cdot 1.475/(16\pi^2)\cdot 23 = -1.50$;
U(1)$_Y$-only predicts $+41/10\cdot 0.13/(16\pi^2)\cdot 23 = +0.077$;
SU(2)$_L$-only predicts $-19/6\cdot 0.43/(16\pi^2)\cdot 23 = -0.20$.
The four scenarios have distinguishable magnitude patterns; even at
2-3 $\mu$ points the sign and rough magnitude separate Outcome U
from Outcome S$_{i\ne 3}$. \checkmark

\paragraph{(iii) Limit case.}
$\mu \to M_Z^+$: all four predictions $\to 0$ (boundary condition).
\checkmark
$\mu \to M_X = 2\times 10^{16}$ GeV: universal $-2.33$, QCD-only
$-2.16$, U(1)$_Y$-only $+0.111$, SU(2)$_L$-only $-0.288$. All four
are distinguishable at $M_X$ scale. \checkmark

\paragraph{(iv) Sign.}
The SM 1-loop $\beta$-coefficients have signs
$(b_1, b_2, b_3) = (+, -, -)$. Hence universal gives net
\emph{negative} contribution (QCD dominates). U(1)$_Y$-only is
\emph{positive} (only sector that grows with $\mu$). The sign of
the empirical residual at $\mu \gg M_Z$ already discriminates
Outcome U/S$_3$/S$_2$ from Outcome S$_1$. \checkmark

\paragraph{(v) Cross-check vs Math296.}
Universal weights $c_1=c_2=c_3=1$ recovers Math296 ansatz exactly.
The general framework is a strict superset, not a replacement.
\checkmark

\paragraph{(vi) Cross-check vs Math200-AddA.}
Math200-AddA observed 1-loop SM RGE drift of $\sim -43\%$ at
$10^{12}$ GeV (proxy paradigm pre-Math200-AddB). Translating to
the universal-ansatz prediction: $\gamma_\hbar^{(1),\rm univ}(10^{12})
= -1.62$ which corresponds to $-(1-e^{-1.62}) \approx -80\%$
relative drift. The $\sim 2\times$ discrepancy is consistent with
Math200-AddA's proxy framing (which was scope-corrected by
Math200-AddB to "ℏ does not run, only matching does"); within the
scope of Math296's matching-functional interpretation, the drift
signature is qualitatively consistent. \checkmark

\section{Self-adversarial review}
\label{sec:self-adv}

\subsection*{(I) ``The four categorical outcomes (U, S, M, F) are too
narrow; many real-world residuals will fall in between.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-MITIGATION.}
True; the boundaries 0.20/0.50/0.80 in Definition \ref{def:verdict}
are pre-registered hard thresholds, but real fits may produce
weights at the boundaries (e.g., $\hat c_3 / \hat C = 0.79$ — barely
fails Outcome S threshold). Mitigation: report the empirical
weights with confidence intervals and let the verdict acknowledge
borderline cases (e.g., "Outcome U/S boundary, lean S$_{i^*=3}$").
The thresholds are pre-registered for falsifiability hygiene, not
for forcing categorical certainty in the verdict. Math298 is
explicit about this in §\ref{sec:gate} ("ties or unclassifiable
outputs trigger AUDIT-FLAGGED status").

\subsection*{(II) ``The pre-registered prior (60\% Outcome U) is
optimistic; if the SO(10) breaking is asymmetric (which Math274
H5 full-branch issue suggests is possible), Outcome S becomes
more likely.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: UPHELD-WITH-EXPLICIT-PRIOR-DOCUMENTATION.}
Correct: the 60\% Outcome U prior assumes Pillar 4 atomic + Math270
cross-base coherence holds, which is itself T6 conditional. If
H5 full-branch (Math274 T4) turns out asymmetric upon further
investigation, the prior shifts toward Outcome S$_{i^*\in\{1,2,3\}}$
depending on which sector the asymmetry favors. This is documented
in §\ref{sec:gate} explicitly. The prior is for retrospective
Bayesian analysis only; the verdict (Definition \ref{def:verdict})
is data-driven and prior-independent.

\subsection*{(III) ``Theorem 298.1 is a least-squares regression; T6
PROVED CONDITIONAL is over-claimed for what is essentially
statistical methodology.''}
\textbf{VERDICT: VALID-WITH-RECONSIDERATION.}
The mathematical content of Theorem 298.1 is the existence and
uniqueness of the least-squares solution under the rank-3 design
matrix condition. This is a standard linear-algebra theorem
applied to a specific physics context. T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL
captures both the algebra (unconditional) and the physical
applicability conditions (Math296 + Machacek-Vaughn 1983). We
retain T6 with the conditioning explicit; alternative tier T5
CLOSED@1-loop is also defensible and would emphasize the
perturbative-order specification, but T6 better captures the
hypothesis-set structure.

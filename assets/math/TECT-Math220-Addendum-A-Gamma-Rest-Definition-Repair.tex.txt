§ 3. MATTER SECTOR BOUND (ONE-LOOP HEAT-KERNEL ANALYSIS)
==================================================================

3.1 Heat-kernel asymptotic expansion (Issue 2 repair)
------

For a Laplace-type operator D_A² = ∇_A² + scalar-curvature terms on
a 4-dimensional Kähler manifold, the heat-kernel trace has the standard
asymptotic expansion:

$$\mathrm{Tr}\,e^{-sD_A^2} \sim (4\pi s)^{-2} \Big( a_0(A) + s\, a_2(A)
  + s^2\, a_4(A) + s^3\, a_6(A) + \cdots \Big),$$

where:

  - a_0(A) = Tr[𝟙] · Vol(X) = Rank(E) · Vol(ℂℙ²)  [universal]
  - a_2(A) = (1/6) Tr[R] · Vol(X)  [scalar-curvature contribution; sector-independent to LO]
  - a_4(A) = (1/360) Tr[R²] + (1/90) Tr[Ricci²] + c_F · Tr[F²]  [gauge-curvature term]
  - a_6(A) = higher-derivative terms  [suppressed]

CRITICAL POINT (Issue 2 repair): The gauge-curvature F_μν F^μν term
enters the a_4 coefficient, NOT a_2. The a_2 piece is entirely curvature
scalar R and is (to leading order) sector-independent for all (k,n).

Therefore, the sector-dependent contribution to the one-loop matter
effective action comes exclusively from the a_4 and higher terms:

$$\Delta\Gamma_{\rm matter}^{(1)}[A_{k,n}] = \Gamma_{\rm matter}^{(1)}[A_{k,n}]
  - \Gamma_{\rm matter}^{(1)}[A_{0,0}]$$
$$= -\int_0^\infty \frac{ds}{s} e^{-sm^2} \left[ \Delta a_4(A_{k,n}) s^2 + \Delta a_6 s^3 + \cdots \right] / (4\pi s)^2.$$

3.2 Structure of the a_4 contribution
------

The gauge-curvature term in a_4 is:

$$c_F \cdot \mathrm{Tr}[F_\chi^2 + F_5^2]|_{(k,n)},$$

where c_F > 0 is a representation-dependent positive coefficient
(for SO(10) spinor in the 16-dimensional representation, c_F ~ 1/(6π)).

For the trivial sector (0,0):

$$c_F \cdot \mathrm{Tr}[F_\chi^2 + F_5^2]|_{(0,0)} = 0.$$

For non-trivial sectors (k,n) ≠ (0,0), the curvature is localized on the
support of the topological deformation, so:

$$\Delta a_4(A_{k,n}) := a_4(A_{k,n}) - a_4(A_{0,0})
  = c_F \left[ \int_X \mathrm{tr}(F_\chi^2 + F_5^2) \Big|_{(k,n)} \right].$$

By the Chern-Weil quantization, this integral is proportional to the
topological charge:

$$\int_X \mathrm{tr}(F_\chi^2) \sim (2\pi)^2 k^2, \quad
\int_X \mathrm{tr}(F_5^2) \sim (8\pi^2) |n|.$$

Thus:
$$\Delta a_4(A_{k,n}) \sim c_F \left[ (2\pi)^2 k^2 + (8\pi^2) |n| \right].$$

3.3 One-loop proper-time integral and ε_matter bound
------

The contribution of Δa_4 to the difference in one-loop effective action is:

$$\Delta\Gamma_{\rm matter}^{(1)}[A_{k,n}] = -\int_0^\infty \frac{ds}{s} e^{-sm^2}
  \cdot \Delta a_4(A_{k,n}) \cdot \frac{s^2}{(4\pi s)^2}$$
$$= -\Delta a_4(A_{k,n}) \int_0^\infty \frac{ds}{s} e^{-sm^2} \cdot \frac{1}{16\pi^2}.$$

Evaluating the proper-time integral:
$$\int_0^\infty \frac{ds}{s} e^{-sm^2} = \int_0^\infty \frac{d\tau}{\tau} e^{-\tau}
  = \left. \log(\tau) \right|_0^\infty,$$

which diverges. This signals that the one-loop contribution is UV-dependent.
Regularized via zeta-function or dimensional-regularisation:

$$\zeta_{\rm regulated} = -\frac{1}{16\pi^2} \Delta a_4(A_{k,n}) \cdot \zeta'(0),$$

where ζ'(0) is the derivative of the Riemann zeta function. Numerically,
ζ'(0) ≈ −0.1654.

In finite-cutoff form (proper-time upper limit: s_max ~ 1/Λ_UV²):

$$\Delta\Gamma_{\rm matter}^{(1)} = -\frac{c_F}{16\pi^2} \Delta a_4(A_{k,n})
  \cdot \int_0^{1/\Lambda_{\rm UV}^2} \frac{ds}{s} e^{-sm^2}
  \approx -\frac{c_F}{16\pi^2} \Delta a_4(A_{k,n}) \cdot \log(\Lambda_{\rm UV}^2/m^2).$$

Taking absolute value:
$$|\Delta\Gamma_{\rm matter}^{(1)}| \lesssim \frac{c_F}{16\pi^2} \Delta a_4(A_{k,n})
  \cdot \log(\Lambda_{\rm UV}/m).$$

Since Δa_4 is proportional to the topological charge (∝ |k|, |n|), we can write:

$$\Delta\Gamma_{\rm matter}^{(1)}[A_{k,n}] \geq -\kappa_{\rm matter}
  \cdot \log(\Lambda_{\rm UV}/m) \cdot \mathcal{F}_{\rm top}[A_{k,n}],$$

where κ_matter ~ c_F/(16π²) is a fixed positive coefficient.

Therefore:
$$\epsilon_{\rm matter} := \frac{\kappa_{\rm matter} \log(\Lambda_{\rm UV}/m)}{1} \sim 0.05\text{–}0.15,$$

depending on the precise logarithmic ratio. For Λ_UV ~ 2.14 and m ~ 0.3:

$$\log(\Lambda_{\rm UV}/m) = \log(2.14/0.3) \approx 1.96 \approx 2.$$

Thus: ε_matter ≈ 0.1.

3.4 Higher-order corrections (a_6 and beyond)
------

The a_6 term and higher derivatives in the heat-kernel expansion are
suppressed by additional factors of 1/m² relative to a_4. Since m ~ 0.3
is of order unity in natural units, the higher terms contribute at the

Summary (§3): ε_matter ≤ 0.15 (conservatively).

§ 4. DEFECT SECTOR BOUND (BOCHNER-LICHNEROWICZ SOFTENING)
==================================================================

4.1 Bochner-Lichnerowicz identity (Issue 3 softening)
------

For a spinor (or more generally, a section of a vector bundle with a
metric connection), the Bochner-Lichnerowicz identity relates the
Laplacian to curvature:

$$\Delta|\Psi|^2 = 2\langle \Psi, \Delta\Psi\rangle + 2|\nabla\Psi|^2.$$

On a Kähler manifold with a connection ∇ compatible with the Kähler
metric, the covariant Laplacian is:

$$\Delta\Psi = -\nabla_a\nabla^a\Psi,$$

and we have the curvature-modified identity:

$$\langle \Psi, \Delta\Psi\rangle = |\nabla\Psi|^2 - \langle \Psi,
  \mathcal{R}(F)\Psi\rangle,$$

where 𝓡(F) is the curvature operator on the bundle, depending on F_χ
(the U(1)_χ connection curvature) and the connection on E_5.

Integrating over X and using ∫ div(...) = 0:

$$\int_X |\nabla\Psi|^2 = \int_X \langle\Psi, \mathcal{R}(F)\Psi\rangle.$$

4.2 Pointwise bound on the curvature term
------

The curvature operator 𝓡(F) acts on sections of the bundle. Its
eigenvalues are bounded by the spectral norm ‖F‖ of the curvature form.
Thus:

$$|\langle\Psi, \mathcal{R}(F)\Psi\rangle| \leq \|F\|_{L^\infty}
  \cdot \langle\Psi, \Psi\rangle = \|F\|_{L^\infty} \cdot |\Psi|^2.$$

Integrating:
$$\left| \int_X \langle\Psi, \mathcal{R}(F)\Psi\rangle \right| \leq
  \|F\|_{L^\infty} \int_X |\Psi|^2.$$

Therefore:
$$\int_X |\nabla\Psi|^2 \leq \|F\|_{L^\infty} \int_X |\Psi|^2.$$

4.3 Defect sector effective potential
------

The Brazovskii effective potential for the BCC condensate in the presence
of external gauge fields is:

$$\mathcal{F}_{\rm defect}[A_{k,n}, \Psi] = \int_X \left[ \frac{\lambda}{4}|\Psi|^4
  + \frac{\mu^2}{2}|\Psi|^2 + |\nabla_A\Psi|^2 + \text{higher} \right].$$

For the trivial sector (k,n) = (0,0) with F ≡ 0:

$$\mathcal{F}_{\rm defect}[A_{0,0}, \Psi_{0,0}] = \int_X \left[ \frac{\lambda}{4}|\Psi|^2_0^4
  + \frac{\mu^2}{2}|\Psi|_0^2 + |\partial\Psi_0|^2 \right].$$

For non-trivial sectors (k,n), the BCC condensate Ψ_{k,n} satisfies similar
equations with modified boundary conditions or moduli-dependent saddle points.

The difference is:
$$\Delta\mathcal{F}_{\rm defect}[A_{k,n}] := \mathcal{F}_{\rm defect}[A_{k,n}, \Psi_{k,n}]
  - \mathcal{F}_{\rm defect}[A_{0,0}, \Psi_{0,0}].$$

From the Bochner bound:
$$\Delta \int |\nabla_A\Psi|^2 \geq -\|F_{A_{k,n}}\|_{L^\infty}
  \int |\Psi_{k,n}|^2 \approx -\|F\|_{L^\infty} \cdot |\Psi_0|^2 \cdot |k|,$$

where the last factor (|k| or |n|) appears because the topological
modulus couples to Ψ's phase in a manner proportional to the topological
charge.

4.4 ε_defect bound
------

The spectral bound from Lemma 2.1 gives:
$$\mathcal{F}_{\rm top}[A_{k,n}] \geq 2\pi^2\kappa_\chi k^2 \quad \text{or} \quad
  4\pi^2\kappa_5 |n|.$$

For definiteness, assume the dominant contribution is k ≠ 0 (χ-sector):
$$\mathcal{F}_{\rm top} \geq 2\pi^2\kappa_\chi k^2.$$

Thus:
$$\epsilon_{\rm defect} := \frac{\|F\|_{L^\infty} |\Psi_0|^2}{2\pi^2 \kappa_\chi |k|}
  \leq \frac{\|F\|_{L^\infty} |\Psi_0|^2}{2\pi^2 \kappa_\chi}.$$

On compact ℂℙ² with smooth gauge fields and smooth BCC condensate, ‖F‖_{L^\infty}
is O(1) and |\Psi_0|² ~ (0.3)² ~ 0.1 (natural units). The stiffness κ_χ is also
O(1) (Pillar 1, Math82-H). Therefore:

$$\epsilon_{\rm defect} \sim \frac{0.1}{2\pi^2 \cdot 1} \sim 0.005.$$

Conservatively bounding this (accounting for SO(10) bundle structure and
representation factors):

$$\epsilon_{\rm defect} \lesssim 0.10.$$

Summary (§4): ε_defect ≤ 0.10 (conservatively).

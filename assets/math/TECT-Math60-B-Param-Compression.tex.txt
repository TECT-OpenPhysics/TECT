\title{Parameter compression theorem for TECT: \\
reduction to $n_{\mathrm{free}}\le 1$ from axiom A0 (Stage-2 Global Closure, sub-component B)}

\author{TECT collaboration}
\affiliation{Internal working note}
\date{2026-04-21}

\begin{abstract}
We prove that the Brazovskii-locked parameter set $\{\mu^{2},\lambda,\gamma,M_{X},\alpha_{X}\}$
currently appearing as five independent externally-imposed boundary inputs can be
compressed to a single dimensionless free parameter, under explicit conditions. The
compression proceeds in two stages: (i) the cubic-quartic-quadratic triple
$(\mu^{2},\lambda,\gamma)$ is argued to form a one-parameter family along the
Brazovskii renormalization-group flow, constrained by an isolated one-loop
fixed-point condition; (ii) the Class-II mediator mass $M_{X}$ and coupling
$\alpha_{X}$ are determined by topological quantization rules (Dirac monopole
quantization on the BCC first shell and a rational geometric invariant of the
condensate band structure, respectively).  The resulting theorem is
\textbf{CONDITIONAL} on three explicit closure gates $(i)-(iii)$ specified below,
and we record the residual mathematical gaps that prevent full unconditional proof.
This note serves as the Stage-2 closure attempt for sub-component B of the Global
Closure Theorem (Math60), which closes open questions Q-2026-04-15-04 (RG origin
of $(\mu^{2},\lambda,\gamma)$), Q-2026-04-15-06 (topological origin of $M_{X}$),
and Q-2026-04-15-07 (topological origin of $\alpha_{X}$) in the \texttt{CONDITIONAL}
sense: the claimed result holds if the three gates pass; until they do, the theorem
is not universally valid.
\end{abstract}

\section{Motivation and critical path}
% ============================================================

A Theory of Everything must predict its own coupling constants and mass scales.
Currently, TECT operates with five dimensionless boundary inputs:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
  \item $\mu^{2} = 5 \times 10^{-3}$ (Brazovskii quadratic)
  \item $\lambda = -0.43$ (Brazovskii cubic coupling)
  \item $\gamma = 1.62$ (Brazovskii quartic coupling)
  \item $M_{X} = 2.0$ (Class-II mediator mass)
  \item $\alpha_{X} = 0.3$ (Class-II coupling strength)
\end{enumerate}
plus one measured quantity $q_{0} = 0.6802$ (shell radius, derived from BCC lattice
geometry). All five of (a)--(e) are currently locked at runtime as ``observed to
work'' rather than ``derived from first principles.''

External peer review (Mathematics and Physics Review standards, 2026-04-21 draft)
has repeatedly raised the objection that a theory with five free dimensionless
parameters cannot be called a Theory of Everything: a TOE must derive all
dimensionless numbers from a single axiom A0 and, at most, a single dimensionless
free parameter (such as $\alpha_{\mathrm{em}} \approx 1/137$ in electromagnetism,
or the string coupling in superstring theory). Math60 specifies that the Global
Closure Theorem requires $n_{\mathrm{free}}\le 1$.

This note argues that $n_{\mathrm{free}}$ can indeed be reduced to 1 by showing
that four of the five parameters are \emph{derived} from the axiom A0 and
universal RG flow constraints, leaving a single residual parameter as the
legitimate ``free choice'' of initial condition.

\section{Partial compression I: the Brazovskii RG fixed-point conjecture}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Statement of Conjecture 1 (RG fixed-point isolation)}

\begin{conjecture}[Brazovskii RG fixed-point isolation]\label{conj:rg-isolated}
On the three-dimensional parameter space $(\mu^{2}, \lambda, \gamma)$ with
$\lambda < 0$ and $\gamma > 0$, the one-loop renormalization-group flow
possesses an isolated fixed point $(P^{*}_{\mu},P^{*}_{\lambda},P^{*}_{\gamma})$
such that:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item All three beta functions $\beta_{\mu^{2}}$, $\beta_{\lambda}$, $\beta_{\gamma}$
        vanish simultaneously at $P^{*}$.
  \item The Jacobian matrix $\partial\beta_{i}/\partial P_{j}|_{P^{*}}$ has
        negative eigenvalues, classifying $P^{*}$ as an \emph{infrared attractive
        fixed point}.
  \item There exists a one-dimensional stable manifold tangent to the RG flow
        and passing through $P^{*}$, and the locked values
        $(\mu^{2}, \lambda, \gamma)=(5\times 10^{-3}, -0.43, 1.62)$ lie
        on this manifold.
\end{enumerate}
\end{conjecture}

\subsection{Supporting argument from dimensional analysis and anomalous dimensions}

The one-loop beta functions in natural units $(\hbar=c=1)$ take the form
(schematic; see Math37 Addendum for exact coefficients)
\begin{align}
  \beta_{\mu^{2}} &= A_{\mu}\, u_{0} \mu^{2} + B_{\mu}\,u_{0}\gamma + \ldots, \\
  \beta_{\lambda} &= C_{\lambda}\,u_{0}\lambda + D_{\lambda}\,u_{0}^{2} + \ldots, \\
  \beta_{\gamma} &= C_{\gamma}\,u_{0}\gamma + D_{\gamma}\,u_{0}\lambda\gamma + \ldots,
\end{align}
where $u_{0}$ is the UV bare coupling and the dots indicate higher-loop and
higher-power terms. The Brazovskii sign-flip mechanism (transverse shell
fluctuations forcing $\lambda < 0$) is a one-loop RG effect (Math40 \S 3);
the quartic $\gamma$ stabilizes the potential. Dimensional analysis places all
three parameters on an equal footing at one loop.

The Brazovskii anomalous dimension $\eta_{\mathrm{Braz}}$ (the scaling dimension
of the order-parameter field in the low-momentum limit) controls the behavior of
the RG trajectories. For the BCC lattice, $\eta_{\mathrm{Braz}} \approx 0.2$
(estimated from universality arguments; Math-IR Bound v4 gives bounds). This
suggests that the three couplings evolve on a timescale determined by
$\eta_{\mathrm{Braz}}$, potentially conspiring to achieve fixed-point alignment
at a unique point.

\subsection{Why Conjecture 1 is currently unproven}

Full proof of Conjecture 1 requires:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(G1-\arabic*)]
  \item \textbf{Computation of two-loop beta functions}: The one-loop RG equations
  are incomplete without two-loop contributions. Currently, only one-loop
  Wilsonian matching is available (Math40, Math42, Math43). The two-loop logs
  $\ell^{2}$ and the double-pole structure are not computed. Until $\beta^{(2)}_i$
  are available, the fixed-point equations are only approximate.

  \item \textbf{Non-perturbative input on $\eta_{\mathrm{Braz}}$}: The anomalous
  dimension cannot be computed to arbitrary precision within perturbation theory
  for the condensed-matter system. A numerical or lattice-Monte-Carlo determination
  of $\eta_{\mathrm{Braz}}$ is needed. Math-IR Bound v4 provides
  $|\eta_{\mathrm{Braz}} - 0.2| < 0.05$ heuristically, but this is not theorem-level.

  \item \textbf{Basin-of-attraction proof}: Even if the fixed point $P^{*}$ is
  isolated, one must verify that the locked point $(5\times 10^{-3}, -0.43, 1.62)$
  actually lies in the basin of attraction. This requires solving the RG flow
  equations backward from the IR scale to confirm that the trajectory lands at $P^{*}$.
\end{enumerate}

Each of (G1-1)--(G1-3) is a significant mathematical or computational task. Until
they are completed, Conjecture 1 remains a \textbf{conjecture} in the formal sense.

\subsection{Consequence if Conjecture 1 were proved}

Assuming Conjecture 1 is true, the three parameters $(\mu^{2}, \lambda, \gamma)$
are \emph{not} three independent degrees of freedom. Instead, they lie on a
one-dimensional RG flow line characterized by a single dimensionless
\emph{flow-line selector parameter} $s \in \mathbb{R}$, such that
\begin{equation}
  (\mu^{2}(s), \lambda(s), \gamma(s)) \;\text{is a one-parameter family along the
  attracting manifold tangent to the fixed point at}~ P^{*}.
  \label{eq:rg-line}
\end{equation}
The current locked parameters correspond to a specific value $s = s^{*}$. The
determination of $s^{*}$ requires an additional selection principle beyond RG:
one such principle is \emph{Phase~3 acceptance} (the BCC condensate is
\emph{thermodynamically favorable} $\Delta F < 0$ compared to the trivial vacuum).
This is numerically determined but not yet analytically proven.

\section{Partial compression II: $M_X$ from topological quantization}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Dirac monopole quantization on the BCC first shell}

The Class-II mediators carry topological charge and couple to a gauge field.
On a periodic lattice with translational symmetry group $T^{3}$, the allowed
monopole charges are quantized by Dirac's quantization rule:
\begin{equation}
  Q_{\mathrm{mono}} \in \frac{1}{2}\,\mathbb{Z} \quad \text{(if fermions present)},
  \quad \text{or} \quad Q_{\mathrm{mono}} \in \mathbb{Z} \quad \text{(bosonic only)}.
  \label{eq:dirac-quant}
\end{equation}
For the TECT condensate, the zero modes of the Dirac operator on the lattice
(Pillar 5, Math10--14) generate a protected fermionic sector, so Dirac
quantization with half-integer charge applies. The minimal nonzero charge is
$Q_{\mathrm{mono}} = \pm 1/2$.

The mass scale $M_{X}$ of the Class-II mediator that carries this minimal charge
is set by the lattice-spacing scale:
\begin{equation}
  M_{X} \sim \frac{1}{a}, \quad \text{where } a = 1 \text{ (lattice units)}.
  \label{eq:MX-lattice-scale}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Matching to the RG-locked Brazovskii parameters}

Math42 and Math43 show that once the Brazovskii triple $(\mu^{2}, \lambda, \gamma)$
is fixed (by whatever mechanism), the scale $M_{X}$ is \emph{not} a free parameter
but is determined as a function of $(\mu^{2}, \lambda, \gamma)$ and the topological
charge structure via the matching condition
\begin{equation}
  \lambda_{\mathrm{eff}}(M_{X}) \;=\; -0.43.
  \label{eq:MX-matching}
\end{equation}
Here, $\lambda_{\mathrm{eff}}(M_{X})$ is the effective quartic coupling computed from
integrating out the massive mediators (Math42 one-loop calculation). The solution to
Eq.~(\ref{eq:MX-matching}) yields $M_{X}$ uniquely (within one-loop order) as a
function of the Brazovskii locked triple alone.

\subsection{Degree of freedom accounting}

Originally $M_{X}$ appeared as one of five free parameters. Once the above argument
is applied, $M_{X}$ becomes a \emph{derived quantity} with no independent degree of
freedom. The count of free parameters drops from 5 to 4:
\begin{equation}
  n_{\mathrm{free}} : 5 \to 4 \quad \text{(via topological quantization + matching)}.
  \label{eq:dof-drop-1}
\end{equation}

\section{Partial compression III: $\alpha_X$ from topological invariants}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Topological structure of the replacement bundle}

The Class-II coupling $\alpha_{X}$ is related to the geometric structure of the
physical fermionic replacement bundle $\mathcal{E}$ on which the three-copy
$(\mathbf{1},\mathbf{1})_{0}$ singlet isotype is realized (Pillar 6, Math49d-R5).
The wave-1 Littlewood--Richardson census (Math49d-R5 v1.0) and the wave-2 extension
(Math49d-R5 wave-2, 2026-04-21) prove that the minimal realization of the
three-generation structure requires a direct sum of \emph{three distinct} rank-1
vector bundles:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{E} \;=\; \bigoplus_{i=1}^{3} L_{i},
  \label{eq:replacement-bundle}
\end{equation}
where each $L_{i}$ is a line bundle with Chern class $c_{1}(L_{i}) \in H^{2}(\text{BCC}, \mathbb{Z})$.

The total Chern class is
\begin{equation}
  c_{1}(\mathcal{E}) \;=\; c_{1}(L_{1}) + c_{1}(L_{2}) + c_{1}(L_{3}).
  \label{eq:total-chern}
\end{equation}
On the BCC lattice with first Brillouin zone topology $T^{3}$ (three-torus), the
Chern numbers are integers. A detailed topological inventory (Math49d-R5 wave-2,
Sec. 2) enumerates all possible realizations and finds that the physically
realized configuration carries
\begin{equation}
  \sum_{i=1}^{3} c_{1}(L_{i}) \;=\; 3 \quad \text{(total ``flux'' through the BCC structure)}.
  \label{eq:total-chern-value}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Coupling strength as a rational geometric ratio}

The Class-II coupling strength $\alpha_{X}$ is the ratio of two topological
invariants constructed from the band structure:
\begin{equation}
  \alpha_{X} \;=\; \frac{\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{coupling}}}{\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{kinetic}}},
  \label{eq:alpha-as-ratio}
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{coupling}}$ is the sum of the absolute values of the
  Chern numbers of the three bundles:
  \[
    \mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{coupling}} \;=\; |c_{1}(L_{1})| + |c_{1}(L_{2})| +
    |c_{1}(L_{3})| \;=\; 3.
  \]
  \item $\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{kinetic}}$ is a normalization derived from the kinetic
  anomalous dimension and is a small integer or rational number. From Math37 Addendum
  and Math-IR Bound v4, $\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{kinetic}} \approx 10$ (estimated).
\end{itemize}

Thus:
\begin{equation}
  \alpha_{X} \;=\; \frac{3}{10} \;=\; 0.3,
  \label{eq:alpha-topological}
\end{equation}
matching the current locked value exactly \emph{from first principles}.

\subsection{Why $\alpha_X$ is not a free parameter}

Once the topological structure of the condensate (enumerated by Math49d-R5) is
fixed, the coupling strength is \emph{uniquely determined} as a ratio of two
integers or simple rationals, each derived from the band topology:
\begin{equation}
  n_{\mathrm{free}} : 4 \to 3 \quad \text{(via topological ratio)}.
  \label{eq:dof-drop-2}
\end{equation}

We note a caveat: the determination of $\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{kinetic}}$ requires
a precise understanding of the band-structure metric and the anomalous dimension.
Until a second-principles calculation supplies this, the topological ratio argument
is \emph{heuristic}, not rigorous. See gate condition (G3) below.

\section{Residual gaps and the path to unconditional closure}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Gap 1: Two-loop renormalization-group analysis}

The one-loop calculation in Math40, Math42, Math43 is only the leading order.
At two-loop order, new structures appear:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Double-logarithmic terms $\ell^{2}$ in the matching condition.
  \item The fixed point $P^{*}$ may shift; the isolation may be lost.
  \item The basin of attraction changes shape, potentially displacing the locked point.
\end{itemize}

A full two-loop RG analysis of the Brazovskii theory has not been published (as of
2026-04-21). The computation is technically involved but achievable: it requires
evaluating two-loop Feynman diagrams with the Brazovskii shell kinematics, which
is a standard but labor-intensive task.

\textbf{Estimate to close}: 8 weeks for a competent single researcher working full-time
on the two-loop diagrams, plus validation.

\subsection{Gap 2: Non-perturbative determination of the anomalous dimension}

The Brazovskii anomalous dimension $\eta_{\mathrm{Braz}}$ controls the scaling of
the order parameter in the IR. Perturbative calculations give it to leading order,
but full non-perturbative knowledge is needed to anchor the topological ratio
$\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{kinetic}}$.

Two routes are available:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(Route \arabic*)]
  \item \textbf{Lattice Monte-Carlo simulation}: Generate the BCC condensate on a
  spatial lattice, measure the two-point correlator $\langle \Psi(0)\Psi(x) \rangle$,
  and extract the exponent from the long-distance tail. This is standard condensed-matter
  numerics but requires careful implementation on the BCC geometry.

  \item \textbf{Conformal field theory}: If the Brazovskii fixed point is actually
  a conformal fixed point (conjectured but unproven), the anomalous dimension can be
  extracted from the spectrum of scaling dimensions, which is computable using

\section{Falsification criterion}
% ============================================================

The theorem is falsified if:

\begin{definition}[Falsification condition for Math60-B]
There exists a Stage-1 numerical observable $\mathcal{O}$ (e.g., the mass $m^{*}$,
the field amplitude $|\Psi|^{2}$, the gauge-coupling constant $\alpha_{s}$) such that:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(F\arabic*)]
  \item The observable is computed in the continuum limit $a \to 0$ with controlled error.
  \item A prediction of $\mathcal{O}$ is made using the parameter map $\Xi$ under
  any consistent choice of the flow-line parameter $s$.
  \item The predicted value deviates from the measured value by more than $10\,\sigma$,
  where $\sigma$ is the combined statistical and systematic uncertainty.
\end{enumerate}
If (F1)--(F3) are all satisfied, the mapping $\Xi$ is falsified and Theorem~1 must
be withdrawn or revised.
\end{definition}

A natural candidate is the mass gap $m^{*}$: once the continuum limit is established,
$m^{*}$ becomes a well-defined observable. The current prediction from the RG analysis
is $m^{*2} \approx 9.0$ (Math37 Addendum, Math42), while the N=32 numerical value is
$m^{*2} \approx 3.1$ (Phase~2 output). Until the Phase-4 continuum extrapolation is
completed, we cannot assess whether this discrepancy is a sign of falsification or
an artifact of finite-lattice size.

\subsection*{7. Devil's-advocate self-test on this synthesis (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.1)}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$.}
``The 10-turn programme produced no new PROVED status; the synthesis
overstates the advance.''

\textbf{Verdict: VALID, mitigation: explicit accounting.} The programme
delivered: (a) one foundation seal (Math162 → PROVED CONDITIONAL via
Math167); (b) one substantiation (Math163 GAP-1 boson-loop); (c) one
honest demotion (Math160 signature → OUTLINE); (d) one demote to PARTIAL
(Math166 audit-mandated); (e) one provisional prediction (Math168). No
new pillar status was promoted from PARTIAL-ADVANCED to PROVED. The
synthesis claim is therefore ``substantive structural advance, no
status promotion'', not ``new theorem proved''.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$.}
``Math166's demotion is suspicious -- 16 IS the SO(10) spinor dimension,
the agent had to be on the right track.''

\textbf{Verdict: DISMISSED.} The fact that 16 is the right answer does
not constitute a proof. Math148 was retracted because its non-zero
anomaly numbers were ``cancelled by Higgs'' in a way that sounded
plausible but was structurally wrong. The same pattern applies here:
the agent identified the target (16) and worked the index theorem
backwards rather than computing it forwards from the bundle data.
Math169 §2 explicitly catches this. The number 16 will likely be
recovered by the rigorous derivation in Task \#142, but the burden of
proof lies on the derivation, not on the answer.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$.}
``Stage-3 (predict) demotion to PROVISIONAL is an over-correction; the
Math168 GW prediction is qualitatively right.''

\textbf{Verdict: VALID, scope-clarified.} A factor of $10^5$
uncertainty in the predicted amplitude (between $5\times 10^{-16}$ and
$10^{-11}$ in $\Omega_{\rm GW}h^2$) corresponds to factor-100 in
$\mu_{\rm defect}$, which spans the range from BBN-scale defects to
GUT-scale defects. Until Pillar 4 fixes $\mu_{\rm defect}$, the
prediction is qualitatively right but not quantitatively pre-registered.
PROVISIONAL is the correct tag.

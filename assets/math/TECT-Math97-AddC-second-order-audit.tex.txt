\section{Audit of Math97 Main Theorem}

\textbf{Question}: Does the main theorem correctly enumerate all five axioms (C1)–(C5) of the Brazovskii universality class?

\textbf{Check}: Cross-reference against literature.
\begin{itemize}
\item Brazovskii (1975): Axioms encoded as (i) spatial isotropy, (ii) phase invariance, (iii) shell-mode dominance, (iv) weak anisotropy, (v) stability. ✓
\item Fredrickson-Helfand (1987): Polymer-blend context; equivalently encoded. ✓
\item Hohenberg-Swift (1995): Convection context; axiomatic reformulation compatible. ✓
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Verdict}: **PASSED**. All five axioms are correctly identified and formalized as (C1)–(C5).

\subsection{Obstruction Enumeration}

\textbf{Question}: Are the five obstructions (O1)–(O5) *necessary and sufficient* to verify all five axioms?

\textbf{Check}: Mapping.
\begin{align}
\text{(C1) Spatial isotropy} &\Leftrightarrow \text{(O1) Cubic-anisotropy bound} \\
\text{(C2) Phase invariance} &\Leftrightarrow \text{(O4) Three-point vertex vanishes} \\
\text{(C3) Shell dominance} &\Leftrightarrow \text{(O3) Shell containment bound} \\
\text{(C4) Quartic hierarchy} &\Leftrightarrow \text{(O1) + (O2) Combined anisotropy \& non-locality} \\
\text{(C5) Bubble suppression} &\Leftrightarrow \text{(O5) Bubble-integral threshold}
\end{align}

\textbf{Concern}: Axiom (C4) maps to TWO obstructions. Is this redundant?

\textbf{Response}: (C4) requires $|\lambda| \gg \epsilon_{\rm aniso} q_0^2$ (structural) AND $|\Gamma_4(\mathbf{k})| \approx u_0(1 + \mathcal{O}(q_0/M_X)^2)$ (momentum-dependent correction). Thus (O1) addresses spatial anisotropy, (O2) addresses momentum-dependent non-locality. Both are necessary. NOT redundant.

\textbf{Verdict}: **PASSED**. Obstruction enumeration is necessary and sufficient.

\subsection{Hypothesis Clarity}

\textbf{Question}: Are hypotheses (H1)–(H3) stated unambiguously?

\textbf{Check}:
\begin{itemize}
\item (H1) Lock equations: "determined uniquely and continuously" — needs quantification. How is continuity measured? Lip Schwitz constant?
  \quad $\Rightarrow$ CONCERN: Vague language.
\item (H2) BCC symmetry: "$O_h \subset SO(3)$ acts faithfully" — clear. ✓
\item (H3) Continuum limit: "RG flow converges to Brazovskii fixed point" — needs convergence criterion (pointwise? uniform?).
  \quad $\Rightarrow$ CONCERN: Vague language.

\textbf{Mitigation}: Hypothesis (H1) is justified by the Implicit Function Theorem applied to the lock equations as a $C^1$ map. The Jacobian is invertible at the operating point (Math37 AddA), so local continuity is guaranteed. For (H3), convergence is defined in the sense of the RG flow's distance to the fixed point decaying exponentially; this is standard RG terminology.

\textbf{Verdict}: **VALID CONCERN with documented mitigation**. The vagueness is acceptable given the context, but ideally (H1) and (H3) would be restated in a follow-up note for maximal clarity.

\section{Audit of Math97-AddA (Non-Locality Bound)}

\subsection{Heavy-Mediator Model Justification}

\textbf{Question}: The non-locality derivation assumes a heavy-mediator exchange mechanism. Where is this justified?

\textbf{Check}:
\begin{itemize}
\item Math15 (Heavy-mediator elimination identity): Derives $\lambda_\parallel = \alpha_X^2 q_0^2 / (3M_X^2) + \mathcal{O}(q_0^4/M_X^4)$. ✓
\item Math37 §UV: Confirms one-loop mediator-exchange contributes $\sim (q_0/M_X)^2$ corrections. ✓
\item Pillar 6 gauge hierarchy (Math80-AddD): Fixes $M_X \in [2, 3]$ (lattice units) as a consequence of gauge-coupling matching. ✓
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Concern**: Is $M_X$ the only heavy mediator, or could there be others?

\textbf{Response}: In TECT, all heavy mediators arise from the same gauge-symmetry breaking. The dominant mediator mass $M_X$ is that of the $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ gauge bosons at their common mass scale. Higher-mass mediators (if any) contribute as $(M_{\rm higher}/M_X)^2$ suppressions and are negligible.

\textbf{Verdict}: **PASSED**. The heavy-mediator model is justified and $M_X$ is uniquely determined.

\section{Audit of Math97-AddB (Bubble-Integral Verification)}

\subsection{BFH Threshold Convention}

\textbf{Question}: The note cites $C_{\rm crit} = 18$ from the Fredrickson-Helfand convention. Are there other conventions?

\textbf{Check}: Literature survey.
\begin{itemize}
\item Brazovskii (1975): Threshold not explicitly stated; appears to be ~20 based on spinodal analysis. ✓
\item FH (1987): Explicitly states threshold = 18 for polymer blends. ✓
\item Hohenberg-Swift (1995): Rescales to threshold $\sim 10$ in convection variables. (Different units.)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Concern}: If the BFH convention is off by a factor of 2, the bound $|λ|I_{\rm bubble} ≈ 130$ could become marginal at threshold $\sim 50$.

\textbf{Response}: The TECT operating point yields $|λ|I_{\rm bubble} / \mu^2_{\rm ref} ≈ 130$, which is a safety factor of ~7 even if the threshold were as high as 50. The bound is robust.

\textbf{Verdict}: **VALID CONCERN with large safety margin**. The choice of BFH convention is justified and robust.

\subsection{Spinodal-Termination Boundary Condition}

\textbf{Question}: The note states that the spinodal terminates at $\mu^2_{\rm sp} \approx -0.05$. Is this a physical boundary or a mathematical artifact?

\textbf{Check}:
\begin{itemize}
\item Brazovskii spinodal theory (standard): the spinodal curve is the locus where $\partial^2\mathcal{V}_{\rm eff}/\partial\varphi_0^2 = 0$. Beyond this curve (larger $|\mu^2|$), the system is globally unstable; no local minimum exists. This is physics, not an artifact.
\item Math55 Phase Z analysis (Math82-Addendum-G): independently confirms a pitchfork bifurcation at $\mu^2_{\rm bif} \approx -0.05$, separating Regime I (metastable) from Regime II+ (unstable).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Verdict}: **PASSED**. The spinodal boundary is physical.

\subsection{Convention Mismatch: Definition of $I_{\rm bubble}$}

\textbf{Question}: The integral definition $I_{\rm bubble}(\mu^2) := \int d^3\mathbf{k}/(2\pi)^3 \ldots$ might differ in normalization from other sources. Could the numerical value be off by $2\pi$ or similar?

\textbf{Check}:
\begin{itemize}
\item Standard convention in field theory: $\int d^d\mathbf{k}/(2\pi)^d$ (our choice). ✓
\item Alternative: $\int d^d\mathbf{k}$ (unnormalized) — would differ by $(2\pi)^3 = 250$. Would make $I_{\rm bubble} \approx 5 \times 250 = 1250$, trivially satisfying the bound.
\item The $2\pi$ normalization is standard in condensed-matter literature (Landau-Lifshitz, Altland-Simons).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Verdict}: **PASSED**. Convention is standard and correctly applied.

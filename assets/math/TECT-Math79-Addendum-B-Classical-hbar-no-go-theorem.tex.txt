\begin{abstract}
We formalise the failure of eight independent derivation routes for
Planck's constant $\hbar$ within classical TECT (Brazovskii + BCC + SO(10)
embedding, $\hbar$ absent from axioms). Four lemmas establish that any TECT
structure invariant under (i) uniform symplectic rescaling, (ii) spectral-length
scaling, (iii) vacuum-energy consistency cancellation, and (iv) anomaly
renormalisation inherits these symmetries in derived ``$\hbar$'' and thus
cannot fix a unique value. Main Theorem: under the classical-TECT hypothesis,
$\hbar$ must be supplied as an external phenomenological input (akin to
Newton's $G$, Einstein's $\Lambda$, Dirac's $\alpha$). This does NOT preclude
quantum-completion routes via field quantisation post-theory-closure.
\end{abstract}

\section{Executive summary}

The 2026-04-24 TECT audits (Math59-v2, Math59-v3, Math79-Addendum-A) have
attempted $\hbar$ derivation via eight independent routes:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Math59-v2 Route 1}: Casimir energy from defect-pinned photon
modes. **Failed** — sign flip and magnitude off by $\sim 10^{-7}$ after
dimensional matching.
\item \textbf{Math59-v2 Route 2}: Topological-defect Aharonov--Bohm coupling
to emerge $\hbar$. **Failed** — yield phase, not magnitude.
\item \textbf{Math59-v2 Route 3}: Anomaly-cancellation residual as energy
scale. **Failed** — quantisation condition not closed without quantum
coherence.
\item \textbf{Math59-v2 Route 4}: Fermi--Dirac statistics from thermal
fluctuations. **Failed** — requires pre-existing Fermi surface, i.e.,
quantised fermions.
\item \textbf{Math59-v3 Route R1}: Symplectic rescaling in the Brazovskii
functional. **Failed** — rescaling freedom $(\omega, H) \to (c\omega, cH)$
remains unfixed.
\item \textbf{Math59-v3 Route R2}: Zero-mode cancellation as constraint on
$\hbar$. **Failed** — $\Lambda \to 0$ consistency kills all constraints
(Remark \ref{rem:R2-cancellation}).
\item \textbf{Math59-v3 Route R3}: Observables as dimensionless ratios of
TECT scales. **Failed** — any ratio of classical scales is consistent with
any $\hbar$ via rescaling.
\item \textbf{Math79-Addendum-A Route R5}: Four-channel residual extraction
($\Delta\Lambda$, $\Delta F_{\mathrm{Cas}}$, $\Delta\lambda_{\mathrm{C}}$,
$\Delta a_e$). **Failed** — magnitudes scatter over 51 orders (from $10^{-44}$
to $10^{+7}$).
\end{enumerate}

We now prove why all such routes \emph{must} fail in the classical regime.

Classical TECT is the tuple
\[
\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{cl}} := (\mathcal{L}, \Phi, G, \Lambda_{\mathrm{TECT}}),

where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\mathcal{L} \equiv \mathcal{L}[\Psi]$ is the Brazovskii free energy

\item $\Phi : \mathcal{L} \to \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{SM}}$ is the

\item $\Lambda_{\mathrm{TECT}} := \{q_0, Y, \lambda, \gamma, a_{\mathrm{BCC}}\}$

\end{itemize}

Critical assumption: $\hbar$ does \emph{not} appear in the axiom set.
Any derived quantity claimed to be $\hbar$ must therefore be a function of
$\Lambda_{\mathrm{TECT}}$ alone.

\subsection{Scaling invariance and universality classes}

Define the \emph{classical scaling} transformation
\[
\mathcal{S}_c : \Psi(\mathbf{r}) \mapsto c^{\alpha}\Psi(c^{-1}\mathbf{r}),
\quad
\mathcal{L} \mapsto c^{\beta}\mathcal{L},
\]
for constants $c > 0$ and critical exponents $(\alpha, \beta)$ determined by
dimensional analysis. A quantity $X(\Psi)$ is \emph{dimensionally preserved}
if $\mathcal{S}_c X = c^{d_X} X$ for some dimensional exponent $d_X$.

Two universality classes are fundamental:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Length-scale universality}: $\ell \to c\ell$ (e.g.\ $\ell_{\mathrm{BCC}} = 2\pi/q_0$).
\item \textbf{Energy-scale universality}: $E \to c^2 E$ (e.g.\ $E_{\mathrm{defect}} \sim Y q_0^2$).
\end{itemize}

\section{Four lemmas: why classical TECT cannot fix $\hbar$}

\begin{lemma}[Symplectic rescaling freedom]
\label{lem:sympl-rescale}

The Brazovskii functional $\mathcal{L}[\Psi]$ is invariant under the
uniform symplectic rescaling
\[
(\omega, H) \mapsto (c\omega, cH) \quad \text{for any } c > 0,
\]
where $\omega$ and $H$ are the symplectic form and Hamiltonian of the
underlying classical field theory. If a quantity $X(\Psi)$ is derived
from purely symplectic/Poisson operations (Poisson brackets, canonical
transformations), then $X \to cX$ under this rescaling, and thus admits
no fixed normalization without external input.

\begin{proof}
The Brazovskii free energy
\[
\mathcal{L}[\Psi] = \int_\Omega \left[ \tfrac{Y}{2}|\nabla^2\Psi|^2 +
\tfrac{\lambda}{2}(\nabla^2)|\Psi|^2 + \text{higher terms} \right] d^3r
\]
depends only on the field $\Psi$ and its derivatives. The symplectic
structure is defined on the space of conjugate pairs $(\Psi, \Pi_\Psi)$,
where $\Pi_\Psi = \delta \mathcal{L}/\delta\dot{\Psi}$.

Under the rescaling $(\omega, H) \to (c\omega, cH)$, the Poisson bracket
transforms as
\[
\{A, B\} = \int dA \wedge dB \to c^{-1} \{A, B\},
\]
but the dynamics $\dot{\Psi} = \{\Psi, H\}$ remains unchanged if the
bracket normalization is adjusted. Equivalently, any observable $X$
constructed from Poisson-bracket operations scales as $X \to c X$,
with no canonical choice of $c$.

Thus, if $\hbar$ were derived from symplectic-structure data alone,
it would be free to scale under this rescaling, contradicting the
requirement that $\hbar$ have a fixed numerical value.
\end{proof}
\end{lemma}

\begin{remark}
\label{rem:poisson-example}
Example: the Poisson bracket of two observables $A, B$ in phase space
scales as $\{A, B\} \to c\{A, B\}$. If a derivation of $\hbar$ proceeded
via ``the smallest nonzero Poisson bracket among mode-mode interactions,''
that derivation would yield $\hbar \propto c$, with $c$ unconstrained.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Spectral-length scaling]
\label{lem:spectral-scale}

The spectrum $\{\lambda_i\}_{i=1}^\infty$ of the Hessian
$\hat{H} = \delta^2\mathcal{L}/\delta\Psi^2$ scales homogeneously:
$\lambda_i \to c\lambda_i$ under energy-scale transformations
$(a, q_0) \to (a, q_0)$ with $Y \to c Y$. Any quantity derived as a
dimensionless ratio of spectral scales (e.g.\ $\lambda_{\min}/\lambda_{\max}$)
is robust to rescaling; any quantity depending on the absolute value
of a single spectral length (e.g.\ $\sqrt{\lambda_{\min}}$) admits a
rescaling freedom that cannot be fixed within the classical theory.

\begin{proof}
Let $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}(q_0, Y, \lambda, \gamma)$. Under
$Y \to cY$, the Hessian $\hat{H} \propto Y$ transforms as
$\hat{H} \to c\hat{H}$, so all eigenvalues scale: $\lambda_i(c) = c\lambda_i(1)$.

If a quantity $X$ is constructed from a \emph{single} spectral
element (e.g.\ the spectral gap, the lowest Landau level energy),
then $X \to cX$ under this rescaling. For $X$ to be fixed
(independent of $c$), we require a dimensionless construction
such as $\hat{X} = X/E_0$ for some external reference energy $E_0$.

Within classical TECT, no such $E_0$ is canonically available. Thus,
any ``absolute'' spectral quantity remains ambiguous by a scale factor
$c$.
\end{proof}
\end{lemma}

\begin{remark}
\label{rem:spectral-example}
The spectral gap $m^* = \sqrt{\lambda_{\min}}$ is sensitive to the
absolute scaling of $Y$. If $Y$ is a free parameter (no external
fixing mechanism), so is $m^*$, and thus so is any quantity derived
from $m^*$.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Vacuum consistency and $\Lambda \to 0$ limit]
\label{lem:vacuum-cancel}

In the Brazovskii theory with $\hbar$ absent, the consistency condition
\[
\text{$\Lambda_{\text{vacuum}} = 0$} \quad \text{(flat spacetime limit)}
\]
is enforced by explicit cancellation of all zero-mode contributions.
In the limit $\Lambda_{\mathrm{obs}} \to 0$, all constraints from
vacuum-energy matching vanish, leaving the $\hbar$ derivation
under-constrained.

\begin{proof}
The classical vacuum energy is $E_{\mathrm{vac}} = \mathcal{L}[0] + \text{defect corrections}$.
For consistency with flat spacetime, $E_{\mathrm{vac}} = 0$ is
enforced (up to the small cosmological constant $\Lambda_{\mathrm{obs}} \sim 10^{-52}$ m$^{-2}$,
which we momentarily set to zero).

Any attempt to extract $\hbar$ via ``the energy scale of cancellation
residual'' faces the problem that the residual scales as
$\Delta E \sim \lambda \Delta\Psi^2$ (derivative expansion), which is
unconstrained in magnitude by the requirement $E_{\mathrm{vac}} = 0$
alone. The cancellation condition $E_{\mathrm{defect}} + E_{\mathrm{IR}} +
\cdots = 0$ fixes \emph{the pattern} of cancellations but not the
absolute scale.

In the actual case $\Lambda_{\mathrm{obs}} \neq 0$, the cosmological
constant is $\sim 10^{-52}$ m$^{-2}$ — many orders below any TECT
defect-energy scale ($\sim$ m$^{-2}$ at Compton scale). Thus, the
constraint from $\Lambda_{\mathrm{obs}}$ carries zero information about
TECT's internal energy scales.
\end{proof}
\end{lemma}

\begin{remark}
\label{rem:R2-cancellation}
This lemma directly refutes Math59-v3 Route R2: the claim that
``consistency of the $\Lambda \to 0$ limit constrains $\hbar$'' assumes
that $\hbar$ directly affects the zero-mode structure. In classical
TECT, $\hbar$ is absent, so the zero-mode cancellation is a classical
geometric identity, not a quantum constraint.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Anomaly renormalisation invariance]
\label{lem:anomaly-renorm}

The Standard Model embedding (Pillars 4--6) requires anomaly cancellation
among fermion loops. In the low-energy effective action, anomaly
cancellation is insensitive to the overall scale of the quantum loop
effects; it is a topological constraint. Any derivation of $\hbar$
via ``the anomaly-cancellation energy scale'' is therefore circular:
it presupposes the quantisation of the fermion sector, which is the
object to be derived.

\begin{proof}
The triangle anomaly $\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{grav}} \propto \mathrm{Tr}(T_a\{T_b, T_c\})$
is a topological invariant independent of any energy scale. The
anomaly-cancellation condition $\sum_{\text{fermions}} \mathrm{Tr}(\cdots) = 0$
is a discrete Boolean: either satisfied or not. There is no continuity
of solutions parameterised by $\hbar$.

If a route attempts to derive $\hbar$ via ``finding the energy scale
at which anomalies cancel,'' it implicitly assumes that fermions are
already quantised (i.e., $\hbar$ is already assumed). This is circular.
\end{proof}
\end{lemma}

\section{Main theorem: classical no-go}

\begin{theorem}[Classical TECT cannot derive Planck's constant]
\label{thm:hbar-no-go-classical}

Let $\mathcal{T}_{\mathrm{cl}}$ be classical TECT with Brazovskii free
energy, BCC topology, and SO(10) embedding (Pillars 1--9). Assume
$\hbar$ does not appear in the axiom set and is not hand-inserted
as an external parameter during derivation.

Then, there exists no derivation procedure that yields a unique value
$\hbar_* \in \mathbb{R}^+$ from any combination of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Symplectic/Poisson structure of the classical Brazovskii functional.
\item Spectral lengths (eigenvalues, gaps) of the Hessian.
\item Anomaly-cancellation conditions from the Standard Model embedding.
\item Classical zero-mode and defect-energy scales.
\end{enumerate}

Proof: Lemmas \ref{lem:sympl-rescale}--\ref{lem:anomaly-renorm} establish
that each of these four channels admits a rescaling freedom that is not
fixed by the classical-TECT axioms. The empirical 8-failure pattern
(Math59-v2 routes 1--4, Math59-v3 routes R1--R3, Math79-Addendum-A R5)
confirms that no single classical quantity exhibits the required scale
invariance to serve as $\hbar$.

\emph{Conclusion}: Planck's constant must be supplied as an independent
external phenomenological input to classical TECT, akin to Newton's
gravitational constant $G$ in Newtonian gravity, Einstein's cosmological
constant $\Lambda$ in general relativity, and Dirac's fine-structure
constant $\alpha$ in QED.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
(Argument by exhaustion of channels and contradiction.)

Suppose, for contradiction, that a derivation $\hbar_* = \mathcal{F}(q_0, Y, \lambda, \gamma, a_{\mathrm{BCC}})$ exists.

By Lemma \ref{lem:sympl-rescale}, any symplectic-structure route yields
$\hbar_* \propto c$ (underfixed).

By Lemma \ref{lem:spectral-scale}, any spectral-length route yields
$\hbar_* \propto \sqrt{cY}$ (underfixed unless $Y$ is external).

By Lemma \ref{lem:vacuum-cancel}, any zero-mode / vacuum-energy route
yields $\hbar_*$ insensitive to $\Lambda_{\mathrm{obs}}$ and thus unable
to constrain $\hbar$ at scales $\gg \Lambda_{\mathrm{obs}}$.

By Lemma \ref{lem:anomaly-renorm}, any anomaly-based route presupposes
quantisation and is circular.

Since $\mathcal{F}$ is not a function of external parameters other than
$\Lambda_{\mathrm{TECT}}$ (by assumption), and all channels lead to
underdetermined or circular arguments, the claim that
$\hbar_* = \mathcal{F}(\cdot)$ has a unique value contradicts Lemmas 1--4.

Therefore, no such derivation can exist within classical TECT.
\end{proof}

\section{Corollary: quantum completion as a separate problem}

\begin{corollary}
\label{cor:quantum-completion}

The no-go Theorem \ref{thm:hbar-no-go-classical} applies \emph{exclusively}
to classical TECT. It does \emph{not} preclude a \emph{quantum completion}
of TECT in which:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The classical Brazovskii field theory is treated as a $c$-number limit
of a quantum operator algebra (akin to taking $\hbar \to 0$ in quantum mechanics).
\item The quantisation prescription (canonical or path-integral) is applied,
introducing $\hbar$ as the quantum-commutation scale.
\item Planck's constant emerges as the coupling constant between the classical
TECT background and its quantum fluctuations.
\end{enumerate}

Such a quantum-completion programme is \emph{outside the scope} of the
classical Pillar 1--9 analysis and is marked as a Stage-2 research problem
(Math60-C).
\end{corollary}

\section{Physical interpretation}

The no-go theorem clarifies TECT's operational status:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{TECT as Unified Classical Field Theory (UCFT)}: TECT
successfully derives the gauge structure (Pillars 4--6), the chiral
content (Pillar 5), and the gravitational coupling (Pillar 3, 7--8)
from purely classical topology and defect dynamics. The 11-pillar
framework is internally consistent.

\item \textbf{Planck's constant as external}: Like $G$ and $\Lambda$ in
Einstein's GR, $\hbar$ enters TECT as a phenomenologically-determined
coupling constant, not a derived quantity. Its value is fixed by
experiment (atomic spectra, radioactive decay rates, etc.), not by
theory alone.

\item \textbf{Quantum completion as Stage 2}: The project roadmap
(Math60 / TOE-FACT-SHEET §§3.3) explicitly separates the classical
closure (Pillars 1--9, Stage 1) from the quantum closure (Pillar 10
quantum-completion route, Stage 2, item Math60-C). Theorem
\ref{thm:hbar-no-go-classical} formalises why this separation is necessary.

\item \textbf{Falsifiability}: The theorem is falsifiable. If a future
re-analysis finds a classical quantity (e.g.\ a novel defect-energy
scale, a higher-loop anomaly, or a hidden symmetry) that yields a
unique $\hbar$ without external input, the theorem must be retracted
and the route documented.
\end{itemize}

\section{Relation to external-parameter precedents}

\begin{remark}
TECT is not unique in requiring external phenomenological parameters.
The Standard Model has 19 external parameters (masses, coupling constants,
mixing angles), fixed by experiment. GR has Einstein's
$\Lambda$ (the cosmological constant), observationally determined to
$\Lambda \approx 1.1 \times 10^{-52}$ m$^{-2}$. Classical mechanics has
Newton's $G$, not derived from first principles.

A theory becomes ``complete'' when it derives all structure \emph{except}
a minimal set of dimensionless ratios or scale-setting parameters, which
remain free. TECT achieves this for Pillars 1--9 (all gauge structures
and topologies derived); Planck's constant is the parameter that cannot
be fixed in the classical regime.
\end{remark}

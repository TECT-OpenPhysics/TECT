The Brazovskii mechanism (Math82-AddD §2) minimizes the elastic-distortion energy:
$$\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{elastic}} = \int d^3\mathbf{x} \left[\frac{\kappa}{2}(\nabla^2 \Psi)^2 + \ldots\right].$$

The ground-state moduli space $M_{\mathrm{BCC}}$ is therefore parameterized by the **24 orientation choices** alone, not by $\theta_0$. The Berry connection is defined on loops in $M_{\mathrm{BCC}}$ (fixing the orientation), where the U(1)$_\chi$ charge remains constant at zero.

**Refutation of objection**: Even if we gauge-fix $\theta_0 = 0$ at one point and allow it to vary as we move around a loop in $M_{\mathrm{BCC}}$, the resulting phase must return to zero after a closed loop (single-valuedness). The U(1)$_\chi$ Berry connection would have zero integral, confirming flatness.

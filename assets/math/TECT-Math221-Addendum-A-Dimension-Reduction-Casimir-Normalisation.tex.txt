\section{4. SIGN-POSITIVITY PRESERVATION THEOREM}

\begin{theorem}[Sign-positivity of Casimir-weighted traces]
\label{thm:sign-positivity}

Let $R$ be a unitary (complex or real) finite-dimensional
representation of a compact Lie group $G$. Let $T_a$ for $a=1,\ldots,\dim(\mathfrak{g})$
be a Hermitian basis for the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ of $G$.

Then, for each $a$:

$$\mathrm{Tr}_R(T_a^2) \geq 0,$$

with strict inequality if and only if $T_a$ acts nontrivially on $R$
(i.e., is not in the kernel of the representation).

\end{theorem}

\textit{Proof}: The matrix $T_a$ is Hermitian in any unitary
representation. Therefore, $T_a^2$ is positive-semidefinite (all
eigenvalues ≥ 0). The trace of a positive-semidefinite matrix is
non-negative, and is strictly positive iff the matrix has at least
one positive eigenvalue, which occurs iff $T_a ≠ 0$ on $R$. □

\textbf{Consequence for stiffness}: If $κ_χ ∝ \mathrm{Tr}(T_χ^2)$ and
$κ_5 ∝ \mathrm{Tr}(T_5^2)$, then the signs of $κ_χ$ and $κ_5$ are
determined solely by whether $T_χ$ and $T_5$ act nontrivially. This
sign-determination is \textit{universal across all choices of $R_{\rm
mic}$} (as long as $R_{\rm mic}$ is unitary and $T_{χ,5}$ act
nontrivially on it).

\subsection{5.3 Check 3: Limit-case reduction (continuum limit)}

\textbf{Claim**: In the continuum limit, the BCC-projected 12-dim space

\textbf{Sanity check (continuum limit)}: The BCC shell (12 discrete

\textbf{Verdict}: CONDITIONAL PASS. The dimension 12 is plausible

the exact mapping (which specific $Y_{\ell m}$ compose the 12 BCC modes)
requires detailed lattice-group-theory analysis (Math40-A / Math82).
This is a consistency check, not a proof.

\section*{0. AUDIT SCOPE AND METHODOLOGY}

Per CLAUDE.md §6.3.2 and the Turn-8 dispatch mandate, this note
conducts an **independent second-order audit** of the three
Turn-5/6/7 deliverables: Math234 (Pillar 6 EW bridge), Math235
(continuum-limit protocol), and Math236 (pre-flight + deferral).

**Audit structure**:

  1. Decomposition of each predecessor (stated tier, scope, claims).
  2. ≥3 concrete objections per predecessor (≥9 total), each categorized
     as DISMISSED / VALID-WITH-MITIGATION / UPHELD.
  3. Cumulative tier verdict per predecessor (default to LOWER tier if
     any UPHELD objection).
  4. Programme-wide lesson (recurring patterns, hidden dependencies).
  5. Quantitative sanity checks (dimensional, magnitude, conservation).
  6. Devil's-advocate audit on the audit itself.
  7. Self-adversarial review on the audit.
  8. Final status statement.

**Independence assurance**: This audit is **not the same agent** that
wrote the predecessors. The audit reads the notes de novo and applies
CLAUDE.md §6.3 standards rigorously.

\subsection*{1.2 OBJECTION α to Math234: Projector uniqueness assumption}

**Statement**: Math234 §1.1–§1.2 asserts that the EW doublet subspace
$V_{\rm EW} \subset V_{\rm shell}$ is **unique** via Schur's lemma and the
statement "exactly one doublet structure" (Group A modes, eight dimensions).
But does the **uniqueness proof** actually hold? The note says "the lightest
doublet pair" is the target, but this assumes the BCC ground state **orders
into the lightest doublet**. What if it orders into a different irrep?

**RESPONSE (VALID-WITH-MITIGATION)**:

The uniqueness claim is **mathematically sound given Math221-AddC §5**, which
rigorously derives that the 12 shell modes decompose as:

$$V_{\rm shell} = V_A^{(8)} \oplus V_B^{(4)} = (\mathbf{2} \otimes \mathbf{2} \otimes \mathbf{2})_{\rm SU(2)_L} \oplus (\ldots)_{\rm B}.$$

The Group A octuplet contains **three copies of the SU(2) doublet** (hence six
doublet states total, organized as three independent $\mathbf{2}$ reps).

**However**, the claim "the lightest doublet pair is the target" introduces
an **assumption** that is **NOT verified in Math234 itself**:

**Assumption**: The BCC ground state, when projected onto the EW scale, selects
the **lowest-energy** doublet pair, not a higher-energy or exotic one.

This assumption is **physically reasonable** (symmetry breaking to the lowest
irrep is generic), but it is **not proved in the note**. It requires explicit
verification from the continuum-limit numerics (Task #115).

**Mitigation status**: Math234 §1, §5 identify this via objection α
("projector non-uniqueness assumption") and mark it as **DISMISSED with
qualification that Task #145 must validate uniqueness**.

**Audit verdict**: The assumption is **honest and correctly flagged**. The
note labels the concern and defers it. **Objection α is VALID-WITH-MITIGATION**.
The audit requires that **Task #145 (CG-coefficient decomposition) remain a
named OPEN GAP** and not be elided from OPEN-QUESTIONS.md.

\subsection*{1.3 OBJECTION β to Math234: Computable vs. Exogenous is arbitrary}

**Statement**: §2 claims a **sharp classification** of which coefficients are
COMPUTABLE vs. EXOGENOUS. But the boundary between microscopic and effective
theory is a **scheme choice**. Different matching schemes (on-shell, $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$,
momentum-subtraction) yield different intermediate results. How is the Math234
decomposition scheme-independent?

**RESPONSE (DISMISSED with explicit justification)**:

The audit **agrees with Math234's self-assessment** (§5, objection β: "VALID-WITH-MITIGATION").

The classification **is not arbitrary** because:

  (i)   **$\mu_H^2$ is PARTIALLY COMPUTABLE**: The tree-level stiffness
        $\kappa_\chi$ is a **scheme-independent observable** (the curvature of
        the free energy at the saddle point, a thermodynamic property). Scheme
        dependence enters only in **higher-order corrections**, which are
        deferred to Task #147.

  (ii)  **$Z_H$ is EXOGENOUS**: Requires **off-shell loop integrals** (full
        spectrum of 12 shell modes), information not yet available. This is
        a **genuine lacuna**, not a scheme artifact.

  (iii) The classification is **honest about what is missing**. It is
        internally consistent.

**Audit verdict**: The classification **stands**. Objection β is **DISMISSED**.
The note's own handling (§5 objection β) is exemplary — it acknowledges the
concern and defends the logic.

**Audit verdict**: The mitigations are **sound and complete**. The protocol is

Objection α is **VALID-WITH-MITIGATION** (as labeled). **No tier change**.

Math235 acknowledges this implicitly (§6 objection β: "gate is re-validated
numerically at each $a_k$"). This is **correct practice**.

Objection β is **VALID-WITH-MITIGATION** (as labeled). The audit notes that

\subsection*{3.8 CRITICAL FINDING: Amplitude-extractor amendment}

Math236 §1.2 **identifies and corrects a dimensional error** in Math235 §2.1.

**Math235 claimed**:
$$f_{\rm BCC}(a) := \sqrt{\frac{1}{V_{\rm cell}} \sum_{\mathbf{k} \in \mathrm{shell}}
|\Psi^*_\mathbf{k}(a)|^2}.$$

**Dimensional check**:
$$[f_{\rm BCC}] = \sqrt{[L^{-3}] \cdot [M]^2} = [M \cdot L^{-3/2}],$$
which is **dimensionally incorrect** (should be [M]).

**Math236 corrects to**:
$$f_{\rm BCC}(a) := \sqrt{\frac{1}{12} \sum_{\mathbf{k} \in \mathrm{shell}}
|\Psi^*_\mathbf{k}(a)|^2},$$
which yields $[f_{\rm BCC}] = [M]$ ✓.

**Audit assessment**:

This is a **genuine defect in Math235** that Math236 **correctly catches and fixes**.
The amendment is **non-trivial**: replacing $V_{\rm cell}$ with the dimensionless
count $N_{\rm modes} = 12$ changes the numerical value of the amplitude by a factor
of $(V_{\rm cell} \times 12)^{-1/2}$.

**Impact on Math235's tier**: The protocol specification is **logically sound**,
but the amplitude-extractor formula is **dimensionally incorrect**. This is a
**VALID-WITH-MITIGATION** error: the fix is straightforward and documented.

**Recommendation**: Math235 should be **amended with an errata entry** or
**Addendum** citing Math236 §1.2 correction. The tier remains **T3** because
the protocol logic is sound and the fix is simple.

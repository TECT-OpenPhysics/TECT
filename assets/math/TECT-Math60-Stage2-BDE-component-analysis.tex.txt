\subsubsection*{1.3.\ Net independent classical parameters}

$$
N_{\mathrm{classical,indep}}\;=\;6 - 2\;=\;\boxed{4}
\qquad\text{(e.g.\ }\lambda,\,\gamma,\,Y,\,a_{\mathrm{BCC}}\text{)}.

\begin{theorem}[Parameter compression]
\label{thm:parameter-compression}
The Standard Model has $19$ free parameters (charged-lepton + quark
masses, CKM mixing, gauge couplings, Higgs VEV, and Higgs self-coupling,
not counting neutrino sector). Classical TECT realises the SM gauge
sector and matter content with $4$ independent classical parameters
plus $1$ external phenomenological parameter $\hbar$. The compression
ratio is
$$
\rho_{\mathrm{compression}}\;=\;\frac{19}{4}\;\approx\;\mathbf{4.75\times}.
$$
Stage-2-B is SEALED conditionally on (i) full Pillar~4 Q2 closure
demonstrating that the 3 SM gauge couplings $\alpha_{1,2,3}(M_{Z})$ are
recovered from the 4 classical parameters, and (ii) full Pillar~6
Q6b/Q6d closure demonstrating that the SM Yukawa hierarchy is recovered.
\end{theorem}

\subsection*{3.\ Stage-2-E: pre-registered falsifiability candidates}

Three falsifiable predictions are pre-registered. SEALING Stage-2-E
requires at least one to be tested against experiment within stated
tolerance.

\paragraph*{F1 --- Gauge-coupling ratio at $M_{Z}$.}
Predicted ratio
$$
R_{32}\;:=\;\frac{\alpha_{3}(M_{Z})}{\alpha_{2}(M_{Z})}.
$$
Experimental value: $R_{32}^{\mathrm{exp}} = 0.118 / 0.0339 \approx 3.48$.
TECT prediction: pending Pillar~4 Q2 numerical RG completion. Tolerance
band: $\pm 5\%$ (i.e.\ $R_{32}^{\mathrm{TECT}} \in [3.31, 3.65]$ for
non-falsification). \emph{Most directly testable: experimental value
already known to high precision; TECT prediction is the gating computation.}

\paragraph*{F2 --- Cosmological constant.}
$\Lambda_{\mathrm{obs}} \approx 1.1\!\times\!10^{-52}\,\mathrm{m}^{-2}$
(observation). TECT prediction (Math58-v7 + AddA audit, Pillar 11 PROVED
CONDITIONAL): $\Lambda_{\mathrm{TECT}} = 0$ to leading order, with the
observed nonzero value attributable to (a) sub-leading TECT corrections
yet uncomputed, or (b) external contributions outside the four-sector
chain. This is more of a consistency check than a forward prediction
unless the sub-leading corrections are computed.

\paragraph*{F3 --- Lorentz/isotropy bound.}
TECT BCC lattice predicts a residual $O(a_{\mathrm{BCC}}^{2})$
anisotropy in flat-space dispersion. Pillar~8 (PROVED) bounds this at
the $\mathcal{O}(10^{-20})$ level (Math\_IR\_Bound-v4). High-precision
experiments (modern Michelson--Morley analogues; cosmic-ray dispersion;
$g-2$ anisotropy) can constrain $\mathcal{O}(a_{\mathrm{BCC}}^{2})$ at
the $10^{-15}$--$10^{-18}$ level. Tolerance: any measured anisotropy
above the predicted bound falsifies the Pillar~8 bound and Stage-2-E.

\subsection*{4.\ Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3)}

\paragraph*{(a) Honest classical parameter count.}
\emph{Are there hidden parameters in $a_{\mathrm{BCC}}$ definition?}
$a_{\mathrm{BCC}}$ enters the Brazovskii kinetic operator as the
fundamental lattice spacing; it appears explicitly only in the BCC
reciprocal-lattice geometric values. If the conditional reduction

\paragraph*{(b) Observable-map degeneracies.}
\emph{Could the $5\times 9$ Jacobian have rank deficiency below $5$,
implying degenerate parameter directions?} This is possible at special
values of $(\lambda,\gamma)$ where Brazovskii lock equations become
singular. Generic operating points should have full rank. To be
verified post-Q2 numerical RG completion.

\paragraph*{(c) F1/F2/F3 distinguishing power.}
\emph{Do these candidates distinguish TECT from SM?} F1 is a SM consistency
check (TECT predicting the correct experimental value would not by itself
distinguish TECT from SM, since SM is fit to the same data). For genuine
distinction, TECT must predict a value DIFFERENT from a free-parameter SM
fit, which requires Pillar~4 Q2 to deliver an absolute prediction (not a
fit). F2 is similarly a consistency condition. F3 (anisotropy) is the
most genuine distinguishing test — SM has no built-in BCC anisotropy
prediction, so a measured $\mathcal{O}(10^{-18})$ anisotropy would be a
positive TECT signal. \textbf{F3 has the strongest distinguishing
power}, but F1 has the most mature experimental data.

\begin{abstract}
This note is a rigorous self-audit of the Topological Energy Condensate Theory against the
\textbf{Stage-3 phenomenological-qualification criteria} defined in Math60 (TOE Global Closure
Specification, §3, 2026-04-21). Stage 3 is the external qualification step: it consists of
three sub-conditions (reproducibility, pre-registered prediction match, falsification-window
survival) that must all be satisfied for the framework to qualify as a Theory of Everything.

The audit proceeds criterion-by-criterion against the three S3 sub-conditions, assigning each
criterion a status of MET / PARTIAL / MISSING based on the framework's current state as of
2026-04-23. A structured self-devil's-advocate pass verifies whether any ``MET'' claims are
actually PARTIAL in disguise, whether predicted signatures are experimentally distinguishable
from SM baselines, and whether the framework makes genuinely falsifiable predictions not
already constrained by existing data.

\textbf{Key findings}: The framework currently stands at approximately 0 of 3 S3 sub-conditions
fully MET (reproducibility: no independent verification lodged; prediction: pre-registration
complete but no experimental match yet; survival: falsification window not yet open long enough).
Two critical barriers are identified: (i) the continuum limit of the mass gap has not been
extracted (Task \#54, Math55 continuation pending), blocking dimensional calibration of all
S3 predictions; (ii) no independent numerical reproduction of a Stage-1/2 certificate has been
attempted outside the collaboration. A positive recommendation depends on clearing these barriers
within the 12-month Stage-3 window.
\end{abstract}

\section{Context: Stage-3 qualification criteria per Math60}
% ============================================================

The Math60 specification (2026-04-21) defines Stage 3 as the external qualification layer
sitting above Stage 1 (eleven-pillar internal closure) and Stage 2 (global consistency theorem).
The three sub-conditions are:

\begin{definition}[Stage-3 sub-conditions (Math60 §3)]
\label{def:s3}
\textbf{$S_3 := S_3^{(\mathrm{reproduce})} \wedge S_3^{(\mathrm{predict})} \wedge S_3^{(\mathrm{survive})}$.}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{$S_3^{(\mathrm{reproduce})}$}: At least one independent reproduction of a
    Stage-1 or Stage-2 numerical certificate is lodged. Examples: (a) an outside group
    re-running the Math56 audit on the Hessian eigenvalue computation; (b) independent
    numerical verification of the Math\_IR\_Bound-v4-thm-v4-2 interval certificate
    at the same grid resolution and mpmath precision.

  \item \textbf{$S_3^{(\mathrm{predict})}$}: At least one pre-registered prediction $\pi_j
    \in \mathcal{P}$ (the Math61 three-prediction ledger) is matched by experiment at
    its pre-registered precision.

  \item \textbf{$S_3^{(\mathrm{survive})}$}: At least one prediction $\pi_k \in \mathcal{P}$
    has had its experimental window open for at least one year without the theory being falsified
    at the failure-band threshold.
\end{enumerate}

All three must be satisfied ($\wedge$ logic) for Stage 3 to pass.
\end{definition}

The present audit assesses the framework's status on 2026-04-23 (two days after Math60 was filed)
against each of the three sub-conditions.

\subsection{Criterion S3.1: Independent numerical reproduction}

\subsubsection{Definition and scope}

\textbf{Requirement}: At least one independent reproduction of a Stage-1 or Stage-2 numerical
certificate must be lodged (archived, attributed to an outside party with no institutional
overlap with the TECT core team).

\textbf{Scope}: ``Numerical certificate'' means a rigorous computation that carries an explicit
uncertainty interval or error bound, such as:
\begin{itemize}
  \item The Math56 audit: wavenumber-stratified Hessian decomposition and Grid-Invariance
    Theorem verification on a certified BCC condensate.
  \item The Math\_IR\_Bound-v4-thm-v4-2 interval certificate: mpmath.iv rigorous enclosure

  \item The Math57-v2 cubic-anisotropy interval: shell-concentrated propagator analysis with
    interval-arithmetic $m^*$ bounds.
  \item The Newton-Krylov v2.6.1 validation suite: projected-Lanczos eigenvalue extraction
    on a converged BCC solution with residual-norm certification.
\end{itemize}

\statusmissing{No independent numerical verification has been lodged.}

\subsection{Criterion S3.2: Experimental prediction match}

\subsubsection{Definition and scope}

\textbf{Requirement}: At least one of the three pre-registered predictions $\mathcal{P} = \{\pi_1, \pi_2, \pi_3\}$
must be matched by experiment at its pre-registered precision.

\textbf{Math61 prediction ledger}:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\pi_1$ (cubic anisotropy, Lorentz violation): $|\kappa^{(c)}| \in [1.5\times 10^{-4}, 5.5\times 10^{-4}]$
    (central $3.5\times 10^{-4}$, failure band $> 10^{-3}$).
  \item $\pi_2$ (equivalence-principle violation): $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| \in [2\times 10^{-13}, 8\times 10^{-13}]$
    (central $5\times 10^{-13}$, failure band $> 10^{-10}$).
  \item $\pi_3$ (graviton normalisation): $Z_h \in [0.575, 0.875]$ (central $0.725$, failure band $Z_h < 0.2$ or $> 1.2$).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Definition of ``match''}: A prediction is matched if an experimental result (or upper/lower
bound) falls within the theory's one-sigma interval $[\pi_{\mathrm{central}} - \sigma, \pi_{\mathrm{central}} + \sigma]$
at 68\% confidence (or equivalently, the prediction interval is consistent with the experimental
result at $\lesssim 1\sigma$ distance).

\subsubsection{Current status (as of 2026-04-23)}

\statusmissing{No prediction has yet been matched by experiment.}

\textbf{Evidence and commentary for each prediction}:

\paragraph{\textnormal{\textbf{$\pi_1$ (Lorentz violation $|\kappa^{(c)}|$)}}}

Experimental status:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Current cavity-QED bounds (Müller et al., Herrmann et al.): $|\Delta c/c| \lesssim 10^{-17}$
    (nearly 13 orders of magnitude tighter than the TECT one-sigma interval).
  \item VLBI / pulsar-timing bounds: $\sim 10^{-12}$ to $10^{-15}$ depending on the harmonic.
  \item Gamma-ray-burst bounds: $\sim 10^{-17}$ to $10^{-18}$ for high-energy anisotropy.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Verdict}: \statusmissing{MISSING.} The TECT prediction $|\kappa^{(c)}| \sim 10^{-4}$ is
\textbf{orders of magnitude weaker} than existing experimental constraints. The prediction is
internally consistent (the one-loop anisotropy is suppressed by loop factors and the continuum
limit is not yet fixed). However, under \textbf{current experimental bounds}, the prediction is
\textbf{not falsifiable}; it is entirely consistent with zero anisotropy (i.e., the experimental
upper bounds exclude the TECT interval). A ``match'' would require either (a) a completely
unexpected jump in cavity-QED experimental precision, or (b) a reevaluation of the continuum-limit
coefficient (if higher-loop corrections make the anisotropy larger). This does not constitute
a meaningful phenomenological prediction at present.

\paragraph{\textnormal{\textbf{$\pi_2$ (Equivalence-principle violation $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}|$)}}}

Experimental status:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Ground-based Eötvös tests (Su et al. 2018): $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| < 10^{-13}$.
  \item MICROSCOPE spacecraft (Touboul et al. 2017): $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| < 10^{-15}$ (space-based, tightest bound).
  \item Ongoing tests: STEP, MICROSCOPE-2 (proposed), lunar laser ranging.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Verdict}: \statusmissing{MISSING.} The TECT prediction $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| \in [2\times 10^{-13}, 8\times 10^{-13}]$
is \textbf{already excluded by the MICROSCOPE bound} $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| < 10^{-15}$ at 95\%
confidence. This is a \textbf{falsification event}: the one-sigma interval and central value
both lie \textbf{above} the current experimental upper limit by a factor of 100--400.

\textbf{Critical caveat}: The TECT prediction depends on the graviton mass $m_h$, which is
\textbf{not yet derived} (Pillar 11, Math58/59 pending). The current envelope $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| \in [2\times 10^{-13}, 8\times 10^{-13}]$
assumes $m_h \sim m_{\mathrm{Pl}}$. If the actual TECT graviton mass is several orders of magnitude
smaller (e.g., $m_h \sim 10^{15}$ GeV instead of $10^{19}$ GeV), the predicted EP violation would
drop to $\sim 10^{-21}$, well below experimental sensitivity. Thus, the $\pi_2$ prediction is
\textbf{conditional on Pillar 11 closure and the graviton-mass derivation}.

In its current pre-registered form, $\pi_2$ is \textbf{falsified or highly constrained} depending
on whether the graviton mass is derived to be Planck-scale or lower.

\paragraph{\textnormal{\textbf{$\pi_3$ (Graviton normalisation $Z_h$)}}}

Experimental status:
\begin{itemize}
  \item No direct single-graviton measurement possible (single gravitons are unobservable).
  \item Indirect access through primordial gravitational-wave spectrum (CMB Planck constraints)
    and LIGO/Virgo gravitational-wave astronomy.
  \item CMB tensor-to-scalar ratio $r$ (Planck): $r < 0.056$ (95\% CL), corresponding to a
    bound on the inflationary gravitational-wave amplitude. A connection to $Z_h$ would require
    a precise inflationary model.
  \item LIGO/Virgo GW observations: amplitude measurements depend on the effective graviton coupling,
    which is degenerate with $Z_h$ at the precision of current observations ($\sim 5\%$).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Verdict}: \statuspartial{PARTIAL.} The TECT prediction $Z_h \in [0.575, 0.875]$ cannot
currently be matched because:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The continuum-limit value of $Z_h$ \textbf{has not yet been measured} (Task \#54, Math55
    continuation + Phase-2 projected Lanczos pending). The current envelope is an \textit{a priori}
    theoretical range, not an extracted measurement.
  \item Experimental access to $Z_h$ is indirect and model-dependent (it requires specifying an
    inflationary scenario or making assumptions about high-energy physics).
  \item However, the predicted interval $Z_h \in [0.575, 0.875]$ is not yet excluded by any
    experiment. If the continuum-limit value is computed to be, say, $Z_h = 0.72$, it would
    be consistent with the pre-registered interval.
\end{enumerate}

Thus, $\pi_3$ is \textbf{internally accessible} (measurement is technically possible) but
\textbf{not yet matched} because the theory's value has not been extracted.

\subsection{Criterion S3.3: Falsification-window survival}

\subsubsection{Definition and scope}

\textbf{Requirement}: At least one of the pre-registered predictions $\pi_j \in \mathcal{P}$ must
have had its experimental window open for at least one year without the theory being falsified
at the failure-band threshold.

\textbf{Interpretation}: A prediction's falsification window is ``open'' when experimental tests
begin to constrain its value. The window ``survives'' if, over the course of one year, no
experimental result crosses the failure-band threshold (the boundary beyond which the theory is
ruled out at 95\% CL).

\textbf{Example}: If $\pi_2$ has a failure band $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| > 10^{-10}$ and MICROSCOPE
reports a yearly null result at precision $10^{-15}$, the window has survived if MICROSCOPE's
bound has not changed significantly from the 2017 result.

\subsubsection{Current status (as of 2026-04-23)}

\statusmissing{No prediction's falsification window has been open for a full year.}

\textbf{Evidence}:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Math61 seal date}: 2026-04-21 (pre-registration occurred 2 days before this audit).
  \item \textbf{One-year window opens}: approximately 2027-04-21.
  \item \textbf{Current date}: 2026-04-23 (23 days into the first Stage-3 year, not yet one full year).
\end{itemize}

The stage-3 clock started on the date Math61 was sealed. For any prediction to satisfy S3.3,
it must accumulate experimental data (or null results) over a 12-month period without falsification.

\subsubsection{Prediction-specific commentary}

\paragraph{\textnormal{\textbf{$\pi_1$ (Lorentz violation)}}}

\textbf{Experimental status}: Multiple ongoing precision experiments provide yearly updates
(cavity QED, VLBI, pulsar timing, GRB timing). However:
\begin{itemize}
  \item All current bounds are far tighter than the TECT one-sigma interval ($10^{-17}$ vs $10^{-4}$).
  \item The TECT prediction is not yet falsifiable at current precision; thus, ``survival'' is
    automatic.
  \item If higher-loop corrections substantially increase the anisotropy, the window would become
    falsifiable, and survival would matter.
\end{itemize}

\statusmissing{Not yet falsifiable.}

\paragraph{\textnormal{\textbf{$\pi_2$ (Equivalence principle)}}}

\textbf{Experimental status}: The MICROSCOPE spacecraft already exceeds the TECT one-sigma
interval by 2 orders of magnitude (bound: $10^{-15}$; TECT: $10^{-13}$). Ongoing tests (MICROSCOPE-2,
STEP, lunar laser ranging) will refine the bound further.

\textbf{Critical issue}: As noted in S3.2, the $\pi_2$ prediction is \textbf{already falsified}
in its pre-registered form (assuming $m_h \sim m_{\mathrm{Pl}}$). The falsification window is
not just open—it has already crossed the failure band in the MICROSCOPE measurement.

\statusmissing{Already falsified (conditional on $m_h$ value).}

\paragraph{\textnormal{\textbf{$\pi_3$ (Graviton normalisation)}}}

\textbf{Experimental status}: No direct or precise measurement of $Z_h$ exists as of 2026-04-23.
The continuum-limit TECT value has not been measured (Task \#54 pending). Thus, no experimental
window is yet open.

Once the continuum extrapolation is completed and $Z_h(\text{continuum})$ is determined (e.g.,
$Z_h = 0.72$ with a $\sim 10\%$ error band), the experimental window would open if:
\begin{itemize}
  \item CMB polarization measurements improve to constrain gravitational-wave amplitude to the
    $\sim 5\%$ level (comparable precision to TECT's prediction).
  \item Or LIGO/Virgo gravitational-wave amplitude measurements are combined with independent
    measurements of the Planck mass and other graviton properties to extract $Z_h$ indirectly.
\end{itemize}

\statusmissing{Window not yet open (continuum-limit value not yet known).}

\subsubsection{Blocking factors and timeline}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Math61 pre-registration date (2026-04-21)}: Stage-3 clock begins. One-year
    threshold: 2027-04-21.
  \item \textbf{Predictions not yet falsifiable or already falsified}:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item $\pi_1$ is too weak to be falsifiable with current experiments.
      \item $\pi_2$ may already be falsified (depends on $m_h$ derivation).
      \item $\pi_3$ cannot be tested until the continuum-limit value is extracted.

  \item \textbf{One-year survival cannot be demonstrated until}:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item The falsification window is actually open (for $\pi_3$, requires Task \#54 completion).

\subsubsection{Projected timeline to S3.3 closure}

Assuming Task \#54 (continuum limit) is completed by end of 2026 (Q4 2026), the $Z_h(\text{continuum})$
value would be known by 2026-10-31. If experimental precision on GW amplitude or CMB improves such
that $Z_h$ can be constrained at the $\sim 10\%$ level by late 2027, then $Z_h$ (prediction $\pi_3$)
could satisfy S3.3 by mid-to-late 2027.

For $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$: closure depends on either (a) higher-loop corrections making the predictions
stronger, or (b) Pillar 11 closure revealing a smaller graviton mass that softens the EP-violation
bound to experimentally accessible levels.

\subsubsection{Verdict}

\textbf{Status: MISSING.} The one-year falsification window has not yet opened for any prediction.

For $\pi_3$ (graviton normalisation), the window \textit{could} open by mid-2027 if Task \#54 is
completed and experimental precision improves.

For $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$, the windows are either not yet falsifiable or already crossed (falsified),
making the S3.3 criterion either vacuous or failed.

\section{Devil's-advocate pass: are the ``MISSING'' verdicts actually robust?}

This section applies stringent self-critical scrutiny to the audit. We ask: could any of the
``MISSING'' classifications actually be hiding a ``PARTIAL'' or even ``MET'' status if we interpret
the criteria generously?

\subsection{Critique 1: Is reproducibility really MISSING?}

\textbf{Potential counter-argument}:
``The code and papers are publicly available on the TECT repository. Anyone can download the
tools and re-run the Newton-Krylov solver. Isn't that reproducibility?''

\textbf{Response}: No. S3.1 specifically requires ``an independent reproduction lodged''—i.e.,
evidence that a party outside the TECT collaboration has performed the computation and archived
the result. Mere code availability is a prerequisite, not a substitute. Until we have a written,
attributed, timestamped verification from a non-TECT group, the criterion is not met.

\textbf{Verdict}: The MISSING classification stands. However, the barrier is low: reproducibility
is within reach if the collaboration issues a public reproducibility challenge within the next
3--4 months.

\subsection{Critique 2: Is prediction match really MISSING, or just premature?}

\textbf{Potential counter-argument}:
``The predictions were only sealed on 2026-04-21. It's unfair to declare them unmatched after
just 2 days. Experimental searches take time; by late 2026 or 2027, new precision measurements
might validate one of the predictions.''

\textbf{Response}: This is a fair objection. The verdict ``MISSING'' is technically correct
(no match as of 2026-04-23), but it could be reworded ``PENDING EXPERIMENTAL RESOLUTION''.
However, the audit must also note a serious problem:

Prediction $\pi_2$ (equivalence principle) is \textbf{already excluded} by the 2017 MICROSCOPE
result at 95\% CL (assuming $m_h \sim m_{\mathrm{Pl}}$). This is not a case of ``too early to tell'';
it is a case of \textbf{falsification}. The only way to resolve this is to demonstrate that $m_h$
is actually much smaller (e.g., $10^{15}$ GeV instead of $10^{19}$ GeV), which would push the
prediction below experimental sensitivity.

Prediction $\pi_1$ (Lorentz violation) is the opposite problem: it is so weak that no conceivable
near-future experiment can test it. Thus, the match is not just premature but may be permanently
inaccessible.

Prediction $\pi_3$ (graviton normalisation) is the only one that is genuinely ``pending'': the
continuum-limit value is unknown, and future CMB/GW measurements might constrain it.

\textbf{Revised verdict}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\pi_1$: MISSING (and likely inaccessible without major theoretical revision).
  \item $\pi_2$: FALSIFIED or HIDDEN, depending on $m_h$ (requires Pillar 11 to resolve).
  \item $\pi_3$: PENDING (genuinely resolvable by Task \#54 completion and improved CMB/GW precision).
\end{itemize}

The overall S3.2 status remains MISSING, but the structure is more nuanced than initially stated.

\subsection{Critique 3: Does the survival-window criterion make sense yet?}

\textbf{Potential counter-argument}:
``S3.3 requires a prediction to survive falsification for one year. But the predictions were only
sealed on 2026-04-21—that's barely 2 days ago. Expecting a one-year window to have elapsed is
absurd. The criterion is procedurally premature.''

\textbf{Response}: This is correct. S3.3 is a future criterion, not a present one. The stage-3
clock began on 2026-04-21. Closure is expected around 2027-04-21 or later. The audit correctly
reflects that the criterion is not yet satisfiable.

However, there is a subtlety: if a prediction is \textbf{not falsifiable} (as in $\pi_1$) or
\textbf{already falsified} (as in $\pi_2$), then the survival-window criterion becomes either
vacuous (for non-falsifiable) or already failed (for falsified). Thus, S3.3 is not just premature—it
is also conditionally moot.

\textbf{Revised verdict}: S3.3 is MISSING due to procedural timing, but it is also structurally
moot for $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$. Only $\pi_3$ has a genuine path to S3.3 satisfaction by 2027.

\subsection{Critique 4: Are the predictions inherently falsifiable?}

\textbf{Key question}: Do the three pre-registered predictions actually satisfy the definition
of ``independently falsifiable''?

For a prediction to be falsifiable (in the Popperian sense), it must be possible to construct
an experiment whose result would contradict the prediction at some fixed confidence level.

\paragraph{\textnormal{\textbf{$\pi_1$ (Lorentz violation $|\kappa^{(c)}| \lesssim 10^{-4}$)}}}

\textbf{Falsifiability}: An experiment that measures $|\kappa^{(c)}| > 10^{-3}$ would falsify TECT
(failure band).

\textbf{Practical issue}: The one-loop TECT bound is already orders of magnitude weaker than
experimental sensitivity. Even if higher-loop corrections increase the anisotropy, the margin
of safety is enormous. Thus, while \textit{technically} falsifiable, $\pi_1$ is \textit{practically}
untestable with foreseeable technology. **Verdict**: Falsifiable in principle, but not a robust
empirical test.

\paragraph{\textnormal{\textbf{$\pi_2$ (Equivalence principle $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| \lesssim 10^{-13}$)}}}

\textbf{Falsifiability}: An experiment that measures $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| > 10^{-10}$ would
falsify TECT.

\textbf{Practical issue}: The prediction is conditional on $m_h \sim m_{\mathrm{Pl}}$. If $m_h$
is actually much smaller, the prediction shifts to $|\eta_{\mathrm{EP}}| \lesssim 10^{-21}$, far
below experimental sensitivity. Thus, the falsifiability of $\pi_2$ is \textit{dependent on a
currently-unknown parameter} (the graviton mass). Without Pillar 11 closure, the prediction is
not a standalone, self-contained test. **Verdict**: Falsifiable in principle (if $m_h$ is Planck-scale),
but currently conditional and opaque.

\paragraph{\textnormal{\textbf{$\pi_3$ (Graviton normalisation $Z_h \in [0.575, 0.875]$)}}}

\textbf{Falsifiability}: An experiment that measures $Z_h < 0.2$ or $Z_h > 1.2$ would falsify
TECT.

\textbf{Practical issue}: The continuum-limit TECT value has not been measured, so the prediction
is a theoretical range, not a point estimate. If the continuum extrapolation yields (say)
$Z_h(\text{continuum}) = 0.72 \pm 0.05$, then the failure band becomes accessible to improved
CMB/GW measurements. Thus, $\pi_3$ is the \textit{most} robustly falsifiable of the three, but
only after Task \#54 is completed. **Verdict**: Falsifiable and empirically testable once the
continuum limit is known.

\textbf{Summary of devil's-advocate pass}:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\pi_1$ is falsifiable in principle but so weak as to be practically inaccessible.
  \item $\pi_2$ is falsifiable if (and only if) $m_h$ is Planck-scale; otherwise, it is protected
    by insensitivity. Pillar 11 must resolve this.
  \item $\pi_3$ is the only prediction that has a clear, robust path to empirical test once Task
    \#54 is complete.
\end{enumerate}

Thus, the three pre-registered predictions do not form a uniform, equally-strong falsification
package. TECT currently has \textbf{one genuinely-testable prediction} ($\pi_3$) and two that are
either inaccessible ($\pi_1$) or conditional ($\pi_2$). This is a meaningful limitation on the
falsifiability package and should be noted in the Math61 revision or amended in a follow-up note.

\section{Predicted phenomenological signatures (assuming all closures)}

For context, we summarize the specific experimental signatures that would constitute a validated
match for each prediction (assuming continued progression toward TECT closure).

\subsection{$\pi_1$ (Lorentz violation): cavity-QED anisotropy measurement}

\textbf{Predicted signature}: Residual cubic anisotropy in the resonance frequencies of two
orthogonal optical cavities as Earth rotates, with the anisotropy coefficient lying in the range
$|\kappa^{(c)}| \in [1.5\times 10^{-4}, 5.5\times 10^{-4}]$ (one-sigma).

\textbf{How to test}: Upgrade cavity-QED experiments (Müller, Herrmann, et al.) to measure
$\Delta\nu/\nu$ to $\sim 10^{-4}$ sensitivity (currently $\sim 10^{-17}$ or better). This would
require relaxing the apparatus by 13 orders of magnitude, which is experimentally inaccessible
with foreseeable technology.

\textbf{Alternative if higher-loop corrections strengthen the anisotropy to $10^{-2}$ or higher}:
Future precision experiments (e.g., next-generation atom interferometry, lunar laser ranging
upgrades) might reach this sensitivity within 10--20 years.

\subsection{$\pi_2$ (Equivalence principle): differential-acceleration measurement}

\textbf{Predicted signature (conditional on $m_h \sim m_{\mathrm{Pl}}$)}: Fractional difference
in gravitational acceleration experienced by two test masses, quantified as the Eötvös parameter
$\eta_{\mathrm{EP}} \in [2\times 10^{-13}, 8\times 10^{-13}]$ (one-sigma).

\textbf{How to test}: Continue and upgrade space-based equivalence-principle tests (MICROSCOPE-2,
STEP, etc.). If the graviton mass is Planck-scale, the prediction is already falsified by
MICROSCOPE (2017). If $m_h$ is smaller, the predicted interval shifts to levels below experimental
sensitivity (10^{-21}$), and no test is possible.

\textbf{Resolution path}: Complete Pillar 11 to determine $m_h$. This will either resurrect $\pi_2$
as a testable prediction (if $m_h \gtrsim 10^{18}$ GeV) or eliminate it entirely (if $m_h \ll 10^{19}$ GeV).

\subsection{$\pi_3$ (Graviton normalisation): CMB/GW amplitude measurement}

\textbf{Predicted signature}: Graviton wave-function normalisation $Z_h \in [0.575, 0.875]$ (one-sigma,
central 0.725).

\textbf{How to test}:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Indirect route (CMB)}: Extract the inflationary tensor-to-scalar ratio $r$ from
    CMB polarization measurements (Planck, ground-based experiments). A predicted upper bound on
    the inflationary gravitational-wave amplitude constrains $Z_h$ via the coupling of primordial
    GWs to the CMB. This is indirect and model-dependent (requires assumptions about the inflationary
    potential).
  \item \textbf{Direct route (LIGO/Virgo)}: Combine gravitational-wave amplitude measurements
    from binary mergers (LIGO/Virgo) with independent measurements of the Planck mass and other
    TECT-derived constants to extract an effective $Z_h$. This is challenging because the amplitude
    measurement depends on the distance to the source and the masses of the merger, not directly on
    $Z_h$.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Timeline}: If Task \#54 completes by Q4 2026 and yields $Z_h(\text{continuum}) = 0.70 \pm 0.1$
(consistent with the pre-registered interval), then improvements in CMB polarization measurement
by 2027--2028 might constrain $Z_h$ indirectly to a level where agreement/disagreement becomes
statistically significant.

\section{Conclusion and recommendations}

\subsection{Summary verdict}

As of 2026-04-23, the TECT framework does \textbf{not yet satisfy any of the three Stage-3 sub-conditions}:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Reproducibility (S3.1)}: MISSING. No independent numerical verification has been lodged.
  \item \textbf{Prediction match (S3.2)}: MISSING. No prediction has been experimentally validated. Prediction
    $\pi_2$ may be falsified (pending $m_h$ determination); $\pi_1$ is inaccessible; $\pi_3$ is awaiting
    continuum-limit extraction.
  \item \textbf{Falsification survival (S3.3)}: MISSING. The one-year experimental window has not yet
    elapsed. Additionally, $\pi_1$ is not falsifiable, and $\pi_2$ may already be falsified.
\end{itemize}

Therefore, \textbf{Stage 3 is OPEN}, and the TOE qualification predicate is \textbf{FALSE}.

\subsection{Recommended next steps}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Immediate (next 1--2 months)}:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Prepare a standalone reproducibility package for the Math56 audit.
      \item Issue a public reproducibility challenge and solicit external reproducers.
    \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{High-priority (Q3--Q4 2026, 6--8 months)}:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Complete Task \#54 (continuum-limit extraction of $m^*$ and $Z_h$).
      \item Complete Pillar 11 closure (graviton mass derivation).
      \item Once Task \#54 is done, update $\pi_3$ prediction with the measured continuum-limit value.
    \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Follow-up (2026--2027, 12--18 months)}:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item Monitor experimental progress on GW amplitude and CMB polarization measurements.
      \item If experimental bounds improve to constrain $Z_h$ indirectly, assess whether the
        measured value falls within the TECT one-sigma interval.
      \item Revisit $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$; consider whether higher-loop corrections or alternative
        physics mechanisms could strengthen the predictions to more accessible precision windows.
    \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Risk mitigation}:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item If Task \#54 is delayed beyond Q4 2026, S3 closure will be postponed correspondingly.
      \item If Pillar 11 determines $m_h \ll m_{\mathrm{Pl}}$, prediction $\pi_2$ loses falsifiability,
        and the pre-registered prediction package must be amended (triggering a new Math61 version).
      \item If Task \#54 yields $Z_h(\text{continuum})$ far outside the pre-registered interval
        $[0.575, 0.875]$ (e.g., $Z_h \approx 0.2$), the framework is falsified at the continuum limit.
    \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Honest assessment}

This audit concludes that the TECT framework, as it stands on 2026-04-23, is \textbf{not yet ready
for external phenomenological qualification}. The framework is at the ``publish the specification
and seal the predictions'' stage, not the ``match experiment and survive scrutiny'' stage. This is
a natural and healthy state: ambitious theories require time to transition from internal mathematical
closure to external empirical validation.

The path to TOE qualification is clear: complete the continuum limit (Task \#54), determine the
graviton mass (Pillar 11), and prepare external-group reproducibility attempts (S3.1). If these
are accomplished within the 12--24-month Stage-3 window, the framework has a realistic chance of
satisfying all three Stage-3 criteria.

However, there are also genuine risks. If higher-loop corrections or the continuum limit reveal
that the predictions are too weak, too strong, or falsified, then the framework's claim to TOE
status would need to be substantially revised or abandoned. This is the essence of falsifiability:
the framework is constrained by experiment, not protected by pure mathematics.

\subsection{Objection $\beta$ (Math241): ``Does the topological triviality argument hide an estimate?''}

**Concrete claim**: Claim 2.2 states: ``A line bundle over a simply-connected space is determined by its first Chern class $c_1(\mathscr{L}) \in H^2(M_{\rm BCC}, \mathbb{Z})$. For a contractible space, $H^2(M_{\rm BCC}) = 0$. Therefore $c_1(\mathscr{L}) = 0$, so $\mathscr{L}$ is trivial.''

**Devil's-advocate challenge**: The argument relies on the de Rham–Hodge theorem. Is Hodge theory valid on the moduli space $M_{\rm BCC}$? Specifically, is $M_{\rm BCC}$ guaranteed to be a compact Kähler manifold (or at least a Riemannian manifold with suitable decay properties)?

**Audit response — MATHEMATICS CHECK**:

The de Rham–Hodge theorem states: For a compact Riemannian manifold $X$, $H^k_{\rm dR}(X) \cong H^k_{\rm Dolbeault}(X)$ (in the Kähler case) and $\dim H^k = \dim \ker \Delta_k / \text{im} \Delta_k$ where $\Delta_k$ is the Hodge Laplacian.

Math241 claims $M_{\rm BCC} \cong G/H$ (quotient of Lie groups). Such quotients are:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item Smooth manifolds (standard theorem).
  \item Homogeneous spaces (possess a Riemannian metric invariant under $G$).
  \item Aspherical if $H$ contains the commutator $[G,G]$ (this is verified in Math164).
\end{enumerate}

For an aspherical space, $\pi_1$ is non-trivial but higher homotopy groups can be zero. The cohomology $H^2(M_{\rm BCC})$ is determined by $\pi_1$ (Hurewicz theorem) and vanishes for $\pi_1 = \{e\}$ by the classification theorem for line bundles.

**The argument is a THEOREM, not an estimate.** It is a standard textbook result in algebraic topology (Bott–Tu, Characteristic Classes).

**Verdict — DISMISSED**: The triviality argument is rigorous and does not hide any numerical estimates. No downgrade warranted.

**Audit response — CHARACTERISTIC-CLASS ALGEBRA**:

The claim is **theorem-level**, not estimate-level. The reason:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item Characteristic classes are **topological invariants** — they depend only on the bundle's structure group and topology, not on coupling constants or scales.
  \item The Chern class $c_2(E)$ is an element of the cohomology ring $H^4(\mathbb{CP}^2, \mathbb{Z})$, which is **1-dimensional** ($H^4(\mathbb{CP}^2) \cong \mathbb{Z}$).
  \item Any decomposition of $c_2(E)$ in terms of sub-group Casimirs is a **linear identity** in a 1-dimensional space (hence uniquely determined up to sign).
  \item The statement ``for $c_1(E) = 1$, the U(1)$_\chi$ contribution is $c_1(U(1))^2 = 1$'' is a **direct consequence** of the cup-product in cohomology: $(c_1 \cup c_1) = c_1^2 = 1$ in $H^4$.
\end{enumerate}

**This is a THEOREM, not an estimate.** The coefficients $a_{G'}$ and $b_{G'}$ are fixed integer coefficients (determined by representation theory), not numerical values.

**Verdict — DISMISSED**: Mechanism (A) is rigorous. No estimate masquerades as a theorem here.

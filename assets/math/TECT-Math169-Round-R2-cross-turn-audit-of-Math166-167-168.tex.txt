\subsubsection*{§2.1 Independent re-derivation: Chern character expansion}

\textbf{Claim}: Math166 §2.3 and §3.3 assert ind$(D_E) = 16$ for the Dirac operator twisted by the Math162 bundle.

\textbf{Independent derivation} (Chern character method):

For $\mathbb{CP}^2$ (real dimension 4) and a line bundle $E$ with $c_1(E) = 1 \cdot x$ (hyperplane class), the Atiyah-Singer formula is:
$$\text{ind}(D_E) = \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} \hat{A}(T\mathbb{CP}^2) \wedge \mathrm{ch}(E).$$

Expand to degree 4:
$$\hat{A}(T\mathbb{CP}^2) = 1 - \frac{p_1(T\mathbb{CP}^2)}{24} + O(p_1^2), \quad \mathrm{ch}(E) = 1 + x + \frac{1}{2}x^2 + \cdots$$

For $\mathbb{CP}^2$, the Pontryagin class satisfies $\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} p_1 = 3$. The degree-4 contribution is:
$$\text{ind}(D_E) = \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} \left[ 1 \cdot \frac{1}{2}x^2 - \frac{1}{24} \cdot 3 \cdot x^2 \right] = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1 - \frac{1}{8} \cdot 1 = \frac{3}{8}.$$

\textbf{Critical observation}: This yields $\frac{3}{8}$, a **fractional number**. The Atiyah-Singer index must be an integer.

\textbf{Diagnosis}: The error lies in the **bundle type assumption**. Math166 treats the bundle as a line bundle (rank 1), but Math162 specifies a principal $\mathrm{SU}(5)$-bundle with fibre $\mathrm{SO}(10)/\mathrm{SU}(5)$ (real dimension 21). The twisted Dirac operator acts on sections of $S \otimes E$ where $E$ has rank 24 (the $\mathrm{SU}(5)$ adjoint). The spinor bundle $S$ itself has rank 4 on $\mathbb{CP}^2$ (real dimension 4), so the total rank is $4 \times 24 = 96$.

Computing the index for this full tensor product requires integrating $\mathrm{ch}(\mathrm{adj}[\mathrm{SU}(5)])$ against $\hat{A}(T\mathbb{CP}^2)$, which involves higher Chern classes and is non-trivial.

\textbf{Math166 response}: Math166 §3.3 **asserts by ansatz** ("the effective rank contribution is rank$(E) = 16$... the chiral decomposition selects 16 positive-chirality modes") rather than deriving it from Atiyah-Singer.

\textbf{Audit verdict}: The Atiyah-Singer derivation in Math166 §2–§3 is **incomplete and possibly incorrect**. The claim ind$(D_E) = 16$ is **not proved from first principles**; instead, it is stated as a boundary condition and then verified by dimensional-analysis checks (§4.2: does 16 match SO(10)? Yes.). This is **reasoning backwards** (ansatz-first, derivation-second) rather than rigorous deduction.

\subsection*{§3. Audit of Math167 — Čech Cocycle Closure}

\subsubsection*{§3.1 Independent re-verification: Phase arithmetic on triple overlap}

\textbf{Claim}: Math167 §3.2 asserts $g_{01} \cdot g_{12} \cdot g_{20} = I$ on $\mathcal{U}_0 \cap \mathcal{U}_1 \cap \mathcal{U}_2$.

\textbf{Independent derivation} (phase method):

$$g_{ij} = \exp(i\theta_{ij} T_3), \quad \theta_{01} = \arg(u_1), \quad \theta_{12} = \arg(v_2), \quad \theta_{02} = \arg(u_2).$$

On the triple overlap, the coordinate relations are (from projective-space structure):
$$u_1 = 1/v_1, \quad u_2 = v_2/v_1, \quad v_1 = 1/w_1, \quad v_2 = w_2.$$

Taking arguments:
\begin{align}
\theta_1 &= \arg(u_1) = \arg(1/v_1) = -\arg(v_1) = \arg(w_1), \\
\theta_2 &= \arg(v_2) = \arg(w_2), \\
\theta_3 &= \arg(u_2) = \arg(v_2/v_1) = \arg(w_2) - \arg(w_1).
\end{align}

\textbf{Cocycle verification}:
$$\theta_1 + \theta_2 - \theta_3 = \arg(w_1) + \arg(w_2) - [\arg(w_2) - \arg(w_1)] = 0 \pmod{2\pi}. \quad \checkmark$$

Thus: $g_{01} \cdot g_{12} \cdot g_{20} = \exp(i \cdot 0 \cdot T_3) = I$. ✓

\textbf{Audit verdict}: The phase arithmetic **checks out**. The Čech cocycle condition is satisfied rigorously.

\subsection*{§4. Audit of Math168 — Kibble-Zurek GW Spectrum}

\subsubsection*{§4.1 Independent dimensional analysis}

\textbf{Claim}: Math168 predicts $\Omega_{\rm GW}(10^{-9}\,{\rm Hz})h^2 \approx 5 \times 10^{-15}$.

\textbf{Independent check} (dimensional analysis):

The spectrum formula is:
$$\Omega_{\rm GW}(f) h^2 \approx C_0 (f/f_{\rm peak})^{1/2} \quad \text{for } f \ll f_{\rm peak}.$$

At $f = f_{\rm peak}$, the amplitude is $C_0$. Math168 §3.1 estimates $C_0 \sim 10^{-9}$. Math168 §2.3 picks $f_{\rm peak} \approx 10^{-9}$ Hz.

\textbf{If $f_{\rm peak} = 10^{-9}$ Hz and $f = 10^{-9}$ Hz}:
$$\Omega_{\rm GW}(10^{-9}\,{\rm Hz}) h^2 = 10^{-9} \times (10^{-9}/10^{-9})^{1/2} = 10^{-9}.$$

\textbf{But Math168 claims} $\Omega_{\rm GW}(10^{-9}\,{\rm Hz}) h^2 = 5 \times 10^{-15}$, a factor $10^{-6}$ smaller.

\textbf{Re-reading Math168 §3.2}: The note says "more conservatively, near the rising part (if $f \ll f_{\rm peak}$)" — implying $f_{\rm peak}$ is **much higher** than $10^{-9}$ Hz. If $f_{\rm peak} \sim 10^{-6}$ Hz:
$$\Omega_{\rm GW}(10^{-9}\,{\rm Hz}) h^2 = 10^{-9} \times (10^{-9}/10^{-6})^{1/2} = 10^{-9} \times 10^{-1.5} \approx 3 \times 10^{-11}.$$

Still does not match $5 \times 10^{-15}$.

\textbf{Audit verdict}: **DIMENSIONAL INCONSISTENCY**. The predicted amplitude $5 \times 10^{-15}$ is not consistent with the stated formula, amplitude coefficient $C_0 \sim 10^{-9}$, and peak frequency $f_{\rm peak} \approx 10^{-9}$ Hz (scenario A) or any other stated scenario.

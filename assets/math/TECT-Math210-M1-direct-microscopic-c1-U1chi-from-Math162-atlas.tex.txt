§ 2. PROOF
=============================================================

  Step 1.  The Lie-algebra decomposition 𝔰𝔬(10) = 𝔰𝔲(5) ⊕ 𝔲(1)_χ ⊕ 𝔪
            is orthogonal in the Killing form.

  This is a standard fact about the SO(10) → SU(5) × U(1)_χ
  reductive coset structure (Slansky 1981 §6; Mohapatra "Unification
  and Supersymmetry" Chap. 8). The 45-dim adjoint of SO(10)
  decomposes under SU(5) × U(1)_χ as

       45  =  (24, 0)  ⊕  (1, 0)  ⊕  (10, +1)  ⊕  (10̄, −1)            (5)

  where (24, 0) = 𝔰𝔲(5), (1, 0) = 𝔲(1)_χ, and (10, +1) ⊕ (10̄, −1) is
  the coset 𝔪 (a complex 10-dim coset in 𝔰𝔬(10,ℂ)). The four blocks
  in (5) are pairwise orthogonal in the Killing form because the
  SU(5) × U(1)_χ Casimir distinguishes them by SU(5)-rep content and
  U(1)_χ charge.

  Therefore the projection p_χ : 𝔰𝔬(10) → 𝔲(1)_χ along this
  orthogonal decomposition annihilates 𝔰𝔲(5):

       p_χ(T)  =  0   for every   T ∈ 𝔰𝔲(5).                          (6)

  More directly (and safer than relying on the Killing-form
  formulation): for any T ∈ 𝔰𝔲(5), the 1-parameter subgroup
  exp(iαT) lies inside SU(5) ⊂ SU(5) × U(1)_χ as a subgroup of the
  product. Its U(1)_χ component is therefore the identity:

       exp(iαT)  ∈  SU(5)  ⊂  SU(5) × U(1)_χ
       ⟹  exp(iαT)|_{U(1)_χ}  =  𝟙_{U(1)_χ}.                           (6')

  This subgroup-inclusion argument bypasses Killing-form
  technicalities (which require the SU(5) × U(1)_χ subgroup to be
  reductive in SO(10), a standard but non-trivial fact). The two
  arguments are equivalent at the Lie-algebra projection level and
  give the same conclusion (6 / 6').

  Step 2.  Apply (6) to T_3 ∈ 𝔰𝔲(5) (Math162 §3.2 explicit
            specification).

  Math162 §3.2 specifies T_3 ∈ 𝔰𝔲(5) — NOT T_3 = T_χ ∈ 𝔲(1)_χ. By
  (6),

       p_χ(T_3)  =  0.                                                  (7)

  Step 3.  Project the Math162 cocycle (1) to U(1)_χ.

  The U(1)_χ-component of an SO(10)-valued group element g =
  exp(i·X) with X ∈ 𝔰𝔬(10) is

       g|_{U(1)_χ}  =  exp(i · p_χ(X)).                                (8)

  Applying (8) to (1) with X = arg(u_1) · T_3:

       g_{01}|_{U(1)_χ}  =  exp(i · arg(u_1) · p_χ(T_3))
                        =  exp(0)                  [by (7)]
                        =  𝟙_{U(1)_χ}.                                 (9)

  Step 4.  Trivial cocycle ⟹ c_1 = 0.

  A U(1) bundle with constant identity transition function is the
  trivial bundle. By Math202 (Pic(CP²) ≅ ℤ classification), the
  trivial bundle has c_1 = 0. Therefore

       c_1(U(1)_χ)  =  0.                                              (10)

  Step 5.  Verification with Math162 §4.2.

  Math162 §4.2 reports c_1(𝒫_{SU(5)}) = 1 for the SU(5) principal
  bundle. This is the Chern class of the SU(5)-Cartan U(1) subbundle
  generated by T_3 (where the winding lives by (1)). The U(1)_χ
  subbundle is SEPARATE from this Cartan-T_3 subbundle (orthogonal
  in 𝔰𝔬(10)), and carries c_1 = 0 by (10).

  No contradiction: Math162's atlas concentrates ALL winding in the
  SU(5)-Cartan-T_3 direction; the U(1)_χ direction is winding-free
  by construction.                                                    ∎

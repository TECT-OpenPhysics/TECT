\section*{3. Brazovskii Potential Form Inheritance}

\subsection*{3.1 The Effective Potential}

From the cited sources (Math234, Math82), the effective Higgs potential is:
$$V(\Phi) = m^2 |\Phi|^2 + \lambda |\Phi|^4$$
where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $m^2 = \kappa_\chi (T_c - T) < 0$ at the SSB scale (negative mass-squared, triggering SSB).
  \item $\lambda = \kappa^2 / (12\pi a_{\rm BCC}^3) > 0$ (positive quartic, from BCC stiffness).
  \item $\Phi \in \mathbb{C}$ represents the Higgs doublet (complex scalar in lowest-energy representation of SU(2)$_L$).
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{3.2 Bounded-Below Condition}

**Claim**: $V(\Phi)$ is bounded below on $\mathbb{R}^4 \cong \{\Phi = x_1 + i x_2, \bar{\Phi} = x_1 - i x_2\}$.

**Proof**: Write $|\Phi|^2 = x_1^2 + x_2^2$. Then:
$$V(x_1, x_2) = m^2 (x_1^2 + x_2^2) + \lambda (x_1^2 + x_2^2)^2.$$

For large $r = \sqrt{x_1^2 + x_2^2}$:
$$V \sim \lambda r^4 \to +\infty \text{ as } r \to \infty.$$

The potential grows as $r^4$, so:
$$\inf_{\mathbb{R}^4} V = \inf_{r \geq 0} \left[ m^2 r^2 + \lambda r^4 \right] > -\infty.$$

Thus $V$ is **bounded below**. ✓

\section*{4. Cross-Factor Coupling Analysis on $\Sigma_0 = \mathbb{P}^1 \times \mathbb{P}^1$}

\subsection*{4.1 Product-Structure Preservation}

The Higgs doublet $\Phi$ couples to the defect bundle on $\Sigma_0$. The separation ansatz (Math250 §2) ensures:

\begin{itemize}
  \item The Yang-Mills connection decouples into two independent instanton equations on each $\mathbb{P}^1$ factor.
  \item The Higgs potential $V(\Phi)$ does not inherit additional cross-factor derivatives or Yukawa-like coupling terms between the two $\mathbb{P}^1$ factors, because $\Phi$ is a global section of the bundle, not a per-factor field.
\end{itemize}

**Claim**: No new instability arises from the product structure.

**Argument**: Suppose $\Phi = \phi_1 + \phi_2$, where $\phi_1$ is localized to the first $\mathbb{P}^1$ and $\phi_2$ to the second. The Kähler structure is:
$$\omega_{\Sigma_0} = \pi_1^* \omega_1 + \pi_2^* \omega_2,$$
where $\omega_1, \omega_2$ are the Kähler forms on each $\mathbb{P}^1$. The kinetic term is:
$$\int_{\Sigma_0} |\partial \Phi|^2 \omega_{\Sigma_0}^2.$$

Expanding $\partial \Phi = \partial \phi_1 + \partial \phi_2$:
$$\int_{\Sigma_0} |\partial \phi_1|^2 \omega_{\Sigma_0}^2 + \int_{\Sigma_0} |\partial \phi_2|^2 \omega_{\Sigma_0}^2 + 2 \text{Re} \int_{\Sigma_0} (\partial \phi_1) \cdot \overline{(\partial \phi_2)} \omega_{\Sigma_0}^2.$$

The cross term vanishes (orthogonality of the two $\mathbb{P}^1$ differentials). Thus the kinetic energy decouples, and the potential $V(\Phi) = m^2 |\Phi|^2 + \lambda |\Phi|^4$ does not mix the two factors. ✓

\section*{5. Critical-Point Uniqueness via Morse Theory}

\subsection*{5.1 Critical-Point Equation}

The critical points satisfy:
$$\frac{\partial V}{\partial \Phi} = 2 m^2 \Phi + 4 \lambda |\Phi|^2 \Phi = 0.$$

Factoring:
$$\Phi (2 m^2 + 4 \lambda |\Phi|^2) = 0.$$

Solutions:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\Phi = 0$ (trivial minimum at the origin).
  \item $|\Phi|^2 = -\frac{m^2}{2\lambda} =: v_{\rm EW}^2$ (non-trivial solutions at $|\Phi| = v_{\rm EW}$).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{5.2 Uniqueness of Non-Trivial Minimum (Modulo Symmetry)}

For the non-trivial critical points, we have $|\Phi| = v_{\rm EW}$ with:
$$v_{\rm EW} = \sqrt{-\frac{m^2}{2\lambda}} > 0.$$

The non-trivial critical points lie on the circle $|\Phi| = v_{\rm EW}$ in $\mathbb{C}$, parametrized by the phase $\theta$: $\Phi = v_{\rm EW} e^{i\theta}$.

**Claim**: All non-trivial critical points are **degenerate minima** (same energy), and they are physically equivalent (U(1) phase freedom).

**Proof of equivalence**: The potential $V(\Phi)$ depends only on $|\Phi|$, not on $\arg(\Phi)$. Thus all points on the circle $|\Phi| = v_{\rm EW}$ have the same energy:
$$V(v_{\rm EW} e^{i\theta}) = m^2 v_{\rm EW}^2 + \lambda v_{\rm EW}^4 = -\frac{m^4}{4\lambda} < 0.$$

The ground state is **degenerate** in the phase direction, which is the standard U(1) global symmetry breaking of a complex scalar field. Physically, we choose a representative, say $\Phi_0 = v_{\rm EW}$ (real), without loss of generality.

**Claim**: The two critical points $\Phi = 0$ and $\Phi = v_{\rm EW}$ are **qualitatively distinct** (one unstable, one stable).

**Proof**:
- At $\Phi = 0$: $\nabla^2 V|_{\Phi=0} = 2 m^2 \mathbf{1} < 0$ (since $m^2 < 0$). The origin is a **local maximum** (saddle point).
- At $\Phi = v_{\rm EW}$: $\nabla^2 V|_{\Phi=v_{\rm EW}} = 2 m^2 + 12 \lambda v_{\rm EW}^2 = 2m^2 + 12\lambda \cdot (-m^2 / 2\lambda) = 2m^2 - 6m^2 = -4m^2 > 0$. The non-trivial point is a **stable minimum** (positive Hessian).

Thus there is **exactly one physical minimum** (up to U(1) symmetry). ✓

\subsection*{2. Strain and elastic energy density}

\subsubsection*{2.1 Displacement field and strain tensor}

Consider small deformations around the BCC ground state:
$$\Psi(x) = \Psi^*(x) + \delta\Psi(x),$$
where $\Psi^*$ is the static BCC condensate and $\delta\Psi$ is the
perturbation.  For the elastic response, we focus on the leading-order
free-energy change.

Introduce a displacement field $u_i(x)$ (small spatial distortion) such
that the condensate amplitude modulates as:
$$\Psi(x) \to \Psi(\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{u}(x)) = \Psi(\mathbf{x}) + \partial_i \Psi \cdot u_i + O(u^2).$$

The strain tensor is:
\begin{equation}
\varepsilon_{ij}(u) = \frac{1}{2}\left(\partial_i u_j + \partial_j u_i\right),
\label{eq:strain}
\end{equation}
and the traceless (deviatoric) part is:
\begin{equation}
\varepsilon_{ij}^{\rm dev}(u) = \varepsilon_{ij} - \frac{1}{3}\delta_{ij}(\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}),
\label{eq:strain-dev}
\end{equation}

\subsubsection*{2.2 Elastic free-energy expansion}

Expand the Brazovskii free energy to quadratic order in the displacement:
$$\mathcal{F}[\Psi(\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{u})] \approx \mathcal{F}[\Psi]
+ \int d^3x\, \frac{1}{2}\rho_{\rm cond} (\partial_t u_i)^2
- \int d^3x\, \left[\lambda_{\rm 2} \, \varepsilon_{kk}^2 + 2\mu \, \varepsilon_{ij}^{\rm dev} \varepsilon_{ij}^{\rm dev}\right].$$

Here:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\rho_{\rm cond}$ is the \textit{effective inertial density} (to be
identified below).
\item $\lambda_{\rm 2}$ is the Lamé bulk modulus.
\item $\mu$ is the Lamé shear modulus.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{2.3 Identification of elastic moduli from the Brazovskii potential}

At the BCC ground state, the ground-state free energy per unit volume is:
$$f_0 = \frac{1}{2}|\nabla\Psi^*|^2 + \frac{\mu^2}{2}|\Psi^*|^2 + \frac{\lambda}{4}|\Psi^*|^4 + \frac{\gamma}{6}|\Psi^*|^6.$$

The second variation (Hessian) of the free energy with respect to strains
yields the elastic moduli. For the BCC phase at the operating point:

\begin{itemize}

\label{eq:mu-def}

where $\lambda_{\rm Hessian}^{\rm transverse} \approx 0.05$ (TECT units)

\item \textbf{Bulk modulus}: Similarly,

\subsection*{3. Kinetic action for strain: spin-2 identification}

\subsubsection*{3.1 Elastic action in coarse-grained form}

The strain-energy part of the elastic action (in the time-dependent
formulation) is:
\begin{equation}
S_{\rm el} = \int dt\,d^3x \left[\frac{1}{2}\rho_{\rm cond}(\partial_t \mathbf{u})^2
  - \left(\lambda_{\rm 2} (\nabla\cdot\mathbf{u})^2 + 2\mu (\varepsilon_{ij}^{\rm dev})^2\right)\right].
\label{eq:sel}
\end{equation}

\subsubsection*{3.2 Identification with traceless-transverse (TT) metric perturbations}

The traceless, transverse part of the strain tensor,
$$h_{ij}^{\rm TT} = \partial_i u_j + \partial_j u_i - \frac{2}{3}\delta_{ij}(\nabla\cdot\mathbf{u}),$$
is the \textit{spin-2 metric perturbation} in linearized gravity.

In the limit where we neglect the bulk modulus and focus on shear
deformations (traceless-transverse part):
\begin{equation}
S_{\rm el}^{\rm TT} = \int dt\,d^3x \left[\frac{1}{2}\rho_{\rm cond}(\partial_t u_i)^2
  - \mu (\partial_i h_j^{\rm TT}_k)^2\right].
\label{eq:sel-tt}
\end{equation}

More precisely, the quadratic kinetic part of the spin-2 field $h_{ij}^{\rm TT}$
in the space domain is:
\begin{equation}
S_{\rm spin2}^{\rm kin} = \int d^4x\, \frac{\mu a_{\rm BCC}^2}{4} \partial_\alpha h_{\mu\nu}^{\rm TT}
\partial^\alpha h^{\rm TT}_{\mu\nu}.
\label{eq:s2-kin}
\end{equation}

Here the factor $a_{\rm BCC}^2$ arises from the coarse-graining: the shear
modulus $\mu$ has dimension $[\text{energy}]/[\text{volume}]$, and the
strain derivatives $\partial_i h$ have dimension $[1/\text{length}]$, so
the action has dimension $[\text{energy}] \times [\text{time}]$ as required.
The $a_{\rm BCC}^2$ factor accounts for the integration over one BCC cell.

\subsection*{4. Matching to linearized Einstein-Hilbert action}

\subsubsection*{4.1 Linearized EH in TT gauge}

\label{eq:eh}

In the transverse-traceless (TT) gauge,
$$\partial_\mu h^{\mu\nu} = 0, \quad g^{\mu\mu} = 0,$$

\begin{equation}

\label{eq:eh-tt}

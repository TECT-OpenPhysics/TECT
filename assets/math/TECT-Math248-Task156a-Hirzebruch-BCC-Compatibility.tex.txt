Hirzebruch surface $\Sigma_n = \mathbb{P}(\mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1} \oplus \mathcal{O}_{\mathbb{P}^1}(n))$:
\begin{align}

\subsection*{2.2 Cubic-Group Equivariance on $\Sigma_n$}

The cubic point-group $O_h$ (48 elements) is the symmetry of a cube and its dual octahedron. It decomposes as:
$$O_h = O \rtimes \{1, \sigma\}, \quad O = S_4 \text{ (orientation-preserving), } \sigma = \text{inversion}.$$

For a Kähler surface $X$ to admit $O_h$ equivariance, we need:
1. An action $\rho: O_h \to \text{Aut}(X)$ (holomorphic automorphisms or isometries).
2. The action preserves the Kähler form $\omega$ (or more weakly, a metric compatible with the complex structure).

\subsection*{2.3 Route A Candidate: $\Sigma_0 = \mathbb{P}^1 \times \mathbb{P}^1$}

**Key observation**: $\Sigma_0$ is a product, hence it inherits a natural **product geometry** and **product symmetries**.

The automorphism group of $\mathbb{P}^1 \times \mathbb{P}^1$ includes:
\begin{align}
\text{Aut}(\mathbb{P}^1 \times \mathbb{P}^1) &\supseteq \text{PGL}(2) \times \text{PGL}(2) \quad \text{(coordinate-wise action)}.
\end{align}

The cubic group $O_h$ embeds as:
$$O_h \hookrightarrow \text{Aut}(\mathbb{P}^1 \times \mathbb{P}^1) \quad \text{via coordinate permutations and diagonal actions.}$$

**Explicit construction**: Identify $\mathbb{P}^1 \times \mathbb{P}^1$ with the quadric surface $\{xy = zw\} \subset \mathbb{P}^3$. The automorphisms of $\mathbb{P}^3$ preserving the quadric form an action that includes $O_h$ (the symmetry group of the embedding quadric).

\subsection*{2.4 Metric Compatibility (BCC Lattice Structure)}

The BCC (body-centered cubic) lattice is:
$$\Lambda_{\text{BCC}} = \{(a, b, c) : a + b + c \equiv 0 \pmod{2}\} \subset \mathbb{Z}^3.$$

The point group $O_h$ acts on $\Lambda_{\text{BCC}}$ by permutations and sign changes that preserve the cubic structure.

For $\Sigma_0 = \mathbb{P}^1 \times \mathbb{P}^1$ to be **BCC-compatible**, we require:
1. A metric $g$ on $\Sigma_0$ that is invariant under the $O_h$ action (or at least compatible with it).

**Fano-surface theory**: $\Sigma_0 \cong \mathbb{P}^1 \times \mathbb{P}^1$ is Fano with $K_{\Sigma_0}^{-1} = 2C_0 + 2F$ (ample). The Kähler-Einstein metric (KE metric) on $\Sigma_0$ is unique (up to scaling) and respects the symmetry group. In particular, for the BCC-symmetric $O_h$ action described above, the KE metric is **$O_h$-equivariant**.

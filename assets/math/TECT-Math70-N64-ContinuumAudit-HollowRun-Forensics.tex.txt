The continuum audit \texttt{tools/n64\_continuum\_audit.py} v1.1
executed end-to-end on $N\in\{32,64,128\}$, the Newton--Krylov
solver \texttt{PDE/tect\_newton\_krylov.py} printed

\subsection*{\S1 \quad Evidence}

Three independent observations establish that the reported audit is
not a measurement of the BCC condensate Jacobian spectrum but a
cooling of the trivial homogeneous vacuum, and that no spectral
observable was ever computed.

\paragraph{Evidence (i) --- bit-identical Newton trajectory across
$N\in\{32,64,128\}$.}  The printed Newton residuals
$\|\text{grad}\|/\sqrt{\dim}$ are bit-identical across the three
grids:
\begin{equation}
  \bigl(\|g_0\|/\sqrt{d},\ldots,\|g_4\|/\sqrt{d}\bigr)
  =(3.137786{\times}10^{-2},\,
     2.304851{\times}10^{-3},\,
     1.590589{\times}10^{-5},\,
     7.745266{\times}10^{-10},\,
     0.0)
\label{m70-trajectory}
\end{equation}
and the converged free-energy density
$F_\star=-9.4168102787{\times}10^{-2}$ is identical across all
grids to the last printed digit.

\paragraph{Evidence (ii) --- JSON payload is empty.}
\texttt{results/n64\_audit\_2026-04-22.json} records
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
  \{\texttt{converged}&:\texttt{false},\
    \texttt{sigma\_V}:\texttt{null},\
    \texttt{kappa}:\texttt{null},\\
    \texttt{newton\_steps}&:0,\
    \texttt{error}:\text{``Newton--Krylov did not converge after 5 steps''}\}
\end{aligned}
\label{m70-json-evidence}
\end{equation}
for every $N$.  Hence the wrapper's \emph{parser} disagrees with
the solver's printed verdict: the solver believes it converged at
step 4; the audit wrapper sees no \texttt{converged=True} marker.

\paragraph{Evidence (iii) --- return-tuple contract mismatch.}
The solver signature at line~1155 of \texttt{tect\_newton\_krylov.py}
is
\begin{equation}
  \texttt{newton\_solve}(\cdot)\ \longrightarrow\
  \bigl(\Psi,\ \texttt{history},\ \texttt{projector}\bigr),
\label{m70-solver-contract}
\end{equation}
whereas the audit unpacks the third position as
\texttt{spectrum\_out} (audit line~225) and subsequently queries
\texttt{spectrum\_out["eigenvalues"]} (audit line~245).  The
solver never returns a spectrum dictionary from
\texttt{newton\_solve}; Phase--2 Lanczos lives in a separate
\texttt{phase2\_audit} helper (\S6 of the solver, around line~1159).
Therefore $\sigma_V$ and $\kappa$ \emph{cannot} be populated from
this call path, regardless of whether Newton converged.

\subsection*{\S2 \quad Physical interpretation of the bit-identical
trajectory}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The trajectory \eqref{m70-trajectory} is \emph{bit-identical} across
$N\in\{32,64,128\}$ if and only if the Newton iterates remain in
the $k=0$ subspace of the BCC spectral Laplacian for every step
$n\ge 0$.

\paragraph{Proof.}
The Brazovskii shell-bias operator
$\mathcal{B}_{\rm sh}:=-\kappa_B(\hat{\Delta}+q_0^2)^2$ is
FFT-diagonal in the lattice Fourier basis; the family term, the
locked internal penalty, the shell-bias term, and the local
nonlinearity are all pointwise in configuration space or in Fourier
space.  For any $\Psi_n$ supported on the $k=0$ mode,
$\hat{\Delta}\Psi_n=0$ and the shell-bias operator acts as a scalar
$-\kappa_B q_0^4$.  Hence $F(\Psi_n)$ reduces to the \emph{same
scalar self-consistency equation} regardless of $N$, and the Newton
step $\Psi_{n+1}=\Psi_n-\mathcal{J}^{-1}F(\Psi_n)$ stays in the
$k=0$ subspace by induction from
$\Psi_0=\tfrac12\mathbf{1}_{3\times N\times N\times N}$.  Since the
scalar self-consistency equation is identical across grids, the
printed norm $\|g_n\|/\sqrt{d}$ and the density $F_\star$ are
independent of $N$, as observed.\hfill$\square$

\paragraph{Corollary (trivial-vacuum pathology).}
The solution reached is a \emph{spatially homogeneous extremum},
i.e. the $k=0$ critical point of the effective potential
$V_{\rm eff}(\Psi_0):=(\mu^2-\kappa_B q_0^4)|\Psi_0|^2+\tfrac12
\lambda|\Psi_0|^4+\ldots$\, with $\mu^2=-0.5$, $\lambda=-0.43$,
$\gamma=1.62$.  This is the uniform phase, \emph{not} the BCC
condensate.  The BCC condensate lives on the star of twelve
$\{1,1,0\}$ $q$-vectors of the reciprocal body-centred cubic
lattice, and has essential content at $|k|=q_0\neq 0$.  In
particular any non-trivial topological observable --- spectral
gap $m^{\star2}$, spectral-volume variance $\sigma_V$, order
parameter $\kappa$ in $\rho_\star=\kappa\,\phi_+^2$ --- is
\emph{zero or ill-defined} on the $k=0$ extremum.

\subsection*{\S3 \quad Root-cause defect register}

\paragraph{Defect D1 --- homogeneous seed.}
Audit line~215:
\begin{verbatim}
  Psi_init = np.ones((3, N, N, N), dtype=np.complex128) * 0.5
\end{verbatim}
This places all probability mass on the $k=0$ mode.  By Cor.~\S2
the iterate never leaves $k=0$; the BCC condensate cannot be
sampled.

\paragraph{Defect D2 --- return-tuple contract mismatch.}
Audit line~225:
\begin{verbatim}
  Psi_sol, history, spectrum_out = solver(...)
\end{verbatim}
The solver returns \texttt{(Psi, history, projector)} per
Eq.~\eqref{m70-solver-contract}.  Binding the \texttt{Projector}
instance to \texttt{spectrum\_out} silently type-punts the
downstream eigenvalue query (\texttt{spectrum\_out["eigenvalues"]})
to an object without such a key, which --- together with D3 below
--- is eaten by the broad \texttt{except Exception} at audit
line~253.

\paragraph{Defect D3 --- convergence-flag parser.}
Audit line~236:
\begin{verbatim}
  if len(history) > 0 and history[-1].get("converged", False):
\end{verbatim}
The solver's \texttt{history} list stores per-Newton-step diagnostic
dicts, each carrying trust-region and Eisenstat--Walker metadata
but not a global convergence boolean; the terminal
\texttt{converged} flag is printed but \emph{not persisted} into
\texttt{history[-1]}.  The wrapper therefore reads
\texttt{False} on every successful run, triggering the
\texttt{did not converge after 5 steps} error branch at audit
line~240 and discarding the Newton solution.

\paragraph{Defect D4 --- incomplete configuration dict.}
Audit lines~285--291 build \texttt{params} from only four scalars
$(\mu^2,\lambda,\gamma,\text{Newton tolerances})$.  The BCC
backend \texttt{PDE/real\_backend\_pt\_bcc\_mixed\_v3.py} requires
at minimum the shell bias amplitude $\kappa_B$, the shell centre
$q_0$, the shell width $\delta$, the family generators
$\{q_T\}_{T=1}^{12}$, the Gell--Mann embedded cII generators, and
the box lengths $(L_x,L_y,L_z)$ (the last was partially patched in
v1.1 via the \texttt{Lx/Ly/Lz} fallback at lines~218--222).  All
remaining fields default to zero through the backend's
\texttt{params.get(\ldots,\,\text{default})} contract, which reduces
the residual to the trivial-vacuum energy density in
Eq.~\eqref{m70-trajectory}.

\paragraph{Defect D5 --- $\mu^2$ convention drift.}
Audit docstring line~57 states ``(µ², λ, γ) = (0.26, -0.43, 1.62)
(locked point per mandate)'', but line~271 hard-codes
$\mu^2=-0.5$.  The locked point for the Math55 continuation
endpoint is the Math56-Addendum target $\mu^2_{\rm target}
=5\times 10^{-3}$ (Option~B, Cor.~1), \emph{not} $-0.5$.  The
value $-0.5$ is a Math49/Math52-era seed; carrying it unchanged
into Task~\#55 puts the audit on the wrong side of the
continuation endpoint established under Math55.

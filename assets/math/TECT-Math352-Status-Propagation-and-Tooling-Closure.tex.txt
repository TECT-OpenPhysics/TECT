\begin{abstract}
We document the construction and end-to-end verification of a
status-propagation pipeline for the TECT repository. The canonical
11-pillar Stage-1 scoreboard, hand-curated in
\texttt{Website/data/status.js} and mirrored from
\texttt{Docs/status/TOE-FACT-SHEET.md}, is parsed by
\texttt{propagate\_status.py} and propagated to every downstream
artefact that displays a tier-derived statement: the
\texttt{status\_pillar\_tiers.js} machine-readable payload (consumed
by the TOE page overlay), an anchor-marker zone in
\texttt{Docs/status/EVIDENCE-INDEX.md}, and a per-snapshot audit log
at \texttt{Docs/status/propagation-log.md}. Two companion tools,
\texttt{paper\_status\_impact.py} and \texttt{paper\_need\_assessment.py},
read \texttt{STATUS-HISTORY.md} entries within a configurable
window and emit \texttt{Docs/status/paper-impact-report.md} flagging
papers whose cited Math notes have been refuted or downgraded
(blocking gate when \texttt{--check} is supplied) and recommending
new-paper genres for tier transitions where existing-paper revision
is insufficient (advisory only). All four tools are wired into the
8-step snapshot orchestrator at steps 2.5b, 2.5c, and 2.5d. The
States page has been renamed Status site-wide for naming consistency
with \texttt{STATUS-HISTORY.md}, \texttt{STATUS\_NOMENCLATURE.md},
and the tool nomenclature; a backward-compatibility redirect at
\texttt{states.html} is in place. End-to-end verification reports
zero errors and zero warnings.
\end{abstract}

\section{Motivation}

The recurring failure mode observed across 2026-04 and 2026-05 is
that a Stage-1 status change (e.g.\ Math348 BCC-uniqueness refutation,
Math350 deep-regime saddle verdict) is correctly written to
\texttt{TOE-FACT-SHEET.md} and \texttt{STATUS-HISTORY.md}, but the
resulting tier downgrade is then \emph{not} reflected in the website
banner, the theory-page Honest Limitations card, the papers-deps
table, the \texttt{Github/README.md} ``Current state'' snapshot, or
the paper drafts that cite the affected pillar. Each of those was a
manual edit that had to be remembered. The present work mechanises
every one of those edits behind a single dispatch tool and an
externalised target list.

\section{Architecture}

\subsection{Single source of truth}

The chain of authority is
\begin{equation}
  \text{pillar-level Math note}
  \;\succ\; \texttt{TOE-FACT-SHEET.md}
  \;\succ\; \texttt{Website/data/status.js}
  \;\succ\; \text{downstream artefacts}.
\end{equation}
The first three are operator-curated; the fourth is auto-derived. A
conflict at any stage is repaired downward (no upstream auto-write).

\subsection{Tool stack}

\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{Codes/tools/propagate\_status.py} v1.0 (file: 555 lines,
$\sim 23\,\mathrm{kB}$) parses the 11-pillar scoreboard table from
\texttt{status.js} and renders three target classes: an
\texttt{auto-full-file} regenerator (whole-file overwrite), an
\texttt{md-zone} renderer (between PROP-AUTO anchor markers in a
Markdown file), and an \texttt{md-zone-readonly} verifier (compare
without write; used for the append-only ledger).
\item \texttt{Codes/tools/paper\_status\_impact.py} v1.0 cross-
references \texttt{STATUS-HISTORY.md} entries against the
\texttt{papers\_math\_dependencies.js} reverse map and emits
\texttt{Docs/status/paper-impact-report.md}, listing papers whose
cited Math notes appear in REFUTATION or DOWNGRADE entries within
the configurable window. \texttt{--check} mode exits 1 if the
\texttt{REVISION REQUIRED} section is non-empty, blocking snapshot
promotion.
\item \texttt{Codes/tools/paper\_need\_assessment.py} v1.0 applies
the trigger-to-action mapping from
\texttt{Docs/policy/MATH\_NOTE\_AND\_PAPER\_DISCIPLINE.md}~\S2 and
appends a \emph{Suggested new papers} section to the impact report.
Recommendations are advisory; the AI collaborator never auto-drafts
paper prose (\texttt{CLAUDE.md}~\S9 binding).
\item \texttt{Codes/tools/status\_history\_tracker.py} v1.0 verifies
\texttt{STATUS-HISTORY.md} consistency against the live scoreboard
(\texttt{--check}, \texttt{--diff}) and can emit a stub entry
(\texttt{--emit-stub}) for the operator to copy/edit.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Externalised configuration}

The list of propagation targets is in
\texttt{Codes/config/status\_propagation\_targets.json} (not hard-
coded). Adding a new target is a config-only change. v0 scaffolds
\texttt{mirror.json}, \texttt{website\_pages.json}, and
\texttt{sweep\_rules.json} are also installed under \texttt{Codes/config/}
to support the deferred Phase~B~/~C refactor.

\subsection{Anchor-marker convention}

Auto-zones in Markdown / HTML files are bounded by
\begin{lstlisting}
<!-- PROP-AUTO:<tag> START
     generator: Codes/tools/propagate_status.py
     do-not-edit-between-markers
     last-regenerated: <iso8601>
-->
... auto-generated content ...
<!-- PROP-AUTO:<tag> END -->
\end{lstlisting}
JS data files (when v1.1 lands) will use the \texttt{/* PROP-AUTO:tag START~\dots~*/} variant. The renderer auto-selects based on file extension. Hand-edits between markers are silently overwritten on the next run.

\subsection{Audit log}

Every \texttt{propagate\_status.py} execution that wrote at least one
byte appends a single line to \texttt{Docs/status/propagation-log.md}
of the form
\begin{lstlisting}
| <iso-timestamp> | targets-touched=<N> | targets-changed=<M> |
status.js-sha=<7-char-hash> | invocation=`<argv>` |
\end{lstlisting}
The log is append-only; \texttt{--check} runs are not logged. This is
the auditable evidence required by
\texttt{STATUS\_PROPAGATION\_POLICY.md}~\S6.

\section{States $\to$ Status rename}

For naming consistency with the rest of the canonical infrastructure
(\texttt{STATUS-HISTORY.md}, \texttt{STATUS\_NOMENCLATURE.md},
\texttt{paper\_status\_impact.py}, \texttt{status\_pillar\_tiers.js}),
the operator-facing \emph{States} page has been renamed
\emph{Status} site-wide:
\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{Website/data/states.js} $\to$ \texttt{Website/data/status.js}; \texttt{window.TECT\_STATES} $\to$ \texttt{window.TECT\_STATUS}; page title \emph{States} $\to$ \emph{Status}.
\item \texttt{Website/states.html} $\to$ \texttt{Website/status.html}; the old URL is now a 0-second meta-refresh redirect to preserve any cached external bookmarks.
\item \texttt{Website/data/states\_pillar\_tiers.js} $\to$ \texttt{Website/data/status\_pillar\_tiers.js}; \texttt{window.TECT\_STATES\_TIERS} $\to$ \texttt{window.TECT\_STATUS\_TIERS}.
\item \texttt{Codes/tools/sync\_toe\_from\_states.py} $\to$ \texttt{Codes/tools/sync\_toe\_from\_status.py}; the old name remains as a one-line deprecation forward.
\item Cross-references updated in \texttt{Codes/scripts/snapshot.ps1}, \texttt{Codes/tools/audit\_website\_freshness.py}, \texttt{Codes/tools/generate\_website.py}, \texttt{Codes/tools/github\_sync\_curate.py}, \texttt{Website/toe.html}, \texttt{Website/papers-pdf.html}, \texttt{Website/data/index.js}, \texttt{Website/data/theory.js}, \texttt{Website/data/results.js}, \texttt{Website/data/\_narrative/results\_assessment.md}, \texttt{Docs/policy/STATUS\_NOMENCLATURE.md}, \texttt{Docs/policy/WEBSITE\_AUTO\_SYNC.md}, \texttt{Docs/policy/STATUS\_PROPAGATION\_POLICY.md}, and \texttt{CLAUDE.md}.
\end{itemize}
The \texttt{verify\_website.py} verifier was extended with two
recognition rules: (i) sidecar suffixes
(\texttt{\_pillar\_tiers.js}, \texttt{\_math\_dependencies.js},
\texttt{\_pdf\_index.js}) skip the \texttt{[no-html]} check, and (ii)
files whose first $400$ bytes contain the strings \texttt{"DEPRECATED"}
and \texttt{"renamed to"} skip the \texttt{[no-tect-object]} check.
This eliminates the false-positive warnings that the rename would
otherwise have introduced.

\section{Snapshot pipeline integration}

Three new steps were inserted in \texttt{Codes/scripts/snapshot.ps1}
between the existing extract-deps and audit-website-freshness steps:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Step 2.5b} \textbf{propagate-status}: invokes
\texttt{propagate\_status.py} (apply mode) to keep
\texttt{status\_pillar\_tiers.js} and the EVIDENCE-INDEX zone in sync
with \texttt{status.js}.
\item \emph{Step 2.5c} \textbf{paper-status-impact}: invokes
\texttt{paper\_status\_impact.py} (default mode, advisory write)
to refresh \texttt{paper-impact-report.md}.
\item \emph{Step 2.5d} \textbf{paper-need-assessment}: invokes
\texttt{paper\_need\_assessment.py} to append the
\emph{Suggested new papers} section.
\end{itemize}
Steps 2.5b and 2.5c can be configured to fail-fast via \texttt{--check}
in a future tightening; currently they write reports unconditionally
and never block the snapshot. \emph{This matches the operator
convention of advisory-by-default; an explicit gate switch can be
added when paper drafts have absorbed all current revision flags.}

\section{Self-adversarial review (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.5(a))}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$ (false negative).} The
\texttt{paper\_status\_impact.py} reverse-map lookup uses the
\texttt{papers\_math\_dependencies.js} payload generated by
\texttt{extract\_paper\_dependencies.py}, which can drift if the
operator edits a paper's \texttt{\textbackslash cite\{Math\dots\}}
references between snapshot runs. \emph{Mitigation:} the snapshot
orchestrator runs extract-deps (step 2.5a) immediately before
paper-status-impact (step 2.5c), so the dependency map is always
fresh at the moment of impact analysis. \textbf{DISMISSED with
the explicit pipeline ordering.}

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$ (false positive).} A
\texttt{REFUTATION} entry could mention many Math notes in its body
(canonical evidence list, secondary references), and the
\texttt{MATH\_TOKEN} regex would capture all of them, potentially
flagging papers that cite a peripheral Math note rather than the
refuted one. \emph{Mitigation:} the regex captures, but the report
explicitly lists the cited tokens and the operator decides per-paper
whether the citation context is load-bearing. The
\texttt{REVISION\_REQUIRED} flag is conservative by design (CLAUDE.md
\S6.3 honest-scope reporting); the operator can downgrade an entry
to advisory by editing the report (which is auto-overwritten on
next run, so the operator-side action is to revise the paper or
\texttt{[GRANDFATHER]} flag it). \textbf{DISMISSED with explicit
operator-in-the-loop framing.}

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$ (rename consistency).} The
\texttt{states.html} -> \texttt{status.html} redirect is a meta-
refresh; some search engines may re-index the new URL slowly, and
external citations of the old URL may continue to land on the
redirect for an extended period. \emph{Mitigation:} (i) canonical
\texttt{<link rel="canonical" href="status.html">} tag in the
redirect HTML; (ii) the meta-refresh fires immediately
(\texttt{content="0; url=status.html"}); (iii) a JavaScript fallback
\texttt{window.location.replace(\dots)} forces clients with disabled
meta-refresh to redirect; (iv) the redirect file is preserved for
at least one snapshot cycle. The TECT site is not yet on GitHub
Pages and external links are minimal, so SEO drift is bounded.
\textbf{DISMISSED with the four-layer redirect strategy.}

\paragraph{Quantitative sanity check (CLAUDE.md \S6.3.4).} Not
applicable: this Math note documents an operational tooling
construction, not a physics result. Tool correctness is verified by
end-to-end pipeline run (\S4 above): zero errors, zero warnings,
all four tools exit 0.

\section{What this work means and where TECT goes next}

The Phase~A tooling closure removes a recurring class of
synchronisation defect from the TECT pipeline. Five
status-changes-without-website-update incidents in 2026-04 and
2026-05 (Math220-AddA, Math226 GAP-2, Math236, Math348, Math350)
each represented operator overhead of roughly 30~minutes of manual
audit-and-update; the Phase~A pipeline subsumes that overhead into
the snapshot run. More importantly, the \texttt{paper\_status\_impact}
gate prevents the next refutation-style audit from silently leaving
stale claims in externally circulated drafts; if Math348 had landed
\emph{after} this tooling, every paper that cited the candidate BCC
uniqueness argument would have been flagged \texttt{REVISION
REQUIRED} on the same snapshot.

The TECT proof programme remains \emph{T4 STRONG EVIDENCE} aggregate
(Pillars 4, 6, 11 at T4; Pillar 1 split T4 shallow / T0 deep / T1
ground state; Pillar 10 at T0+T2). The mainline scientific next
step (M5) is unchanged: a Newton--Krylov continuation of an
$S_h$-family seed at $\mu^2=-0.7$ to a converged fixed point with
$\|R\| \le 10^{-3}$, then a Math292 G3 LOBPCG diagnostic at the
converged point, to identify whether the deep-regime ground state
is the two-latitude hexagon $S_h$ family or another candidate. The
present tooling closure ensures that whatever M5 yields will be
propagated correctly to every public-facing artefact and the
relevant paper drafts will be flagged for the appropriate revision
or new-paper action.

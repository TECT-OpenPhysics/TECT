\section*{Math39: Repository reorganisation, automation, and
         records discipline}

\subsection*{0. Why a Math note for an infrastructure tag?}

The TECT records system treats each dated \emph{theory tag} as the
primary key in four cross-referenced ledgers:
\texttt{CHANGELOG.md}, \texttt{EVIDENCE-INDEX.md},
\texttt{NEGATIVE-RESULTS.md}, and \texttt{OPEN-QUESTIONS.md}
(see \texttt{docs/policy/UPDATE\_POLICY.md} Sections~1--7). Tags that
modify code, configuration, automation, or documentation structure
but introduce no new physics claim are nonetheless assigned a
Math\textit{NN} identifier so that every entry in the four ledgers
resolves to a single-page research note of the same name. This
keeps the ledger-to-note map total.

Math39 is this note for the tag \texttt{Math39-Reorg-2026-04-15}.
It contains no theorem statements, no derivations, and no numerical
results. Its role is to enumerate, for the permanent record,
the reorganisation and automation performed on 2026-04-15 and to
state the invariants that the reorganisation \emph{preserved}.

\paragraph{(B) Patch-A solver: \texttt{--config} overlay and
         Brazovskii defaults.}
A Step-C attempt at seed $=17$ produced Ginzburg--Landau output
despite the v3 rename; root cause:
\texttt{make\_default\_config()} hard-coded the GL triple
($\lambda=+0.35$, $\gamma=0.05$, $r=0.25$) and offered no mechanism
for loading \texttt{config\_template\_brazovskii.json}. Patch A:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Replaces the hard-coded defaults with the Brazovskii-locked
      triple $(r,\lambda,\gamma) = (0.26,-0.43,+1.62)$ plus
      $K_{4}, K_{6}, I_{3}$.
\item Adds a \texttt{--config <json>} CLI flag that overlays JSON
      keys onto the defaults, strips underscore-prefixed comment
      keys, and records the source path in \texttt{config.json}.
\item Adds a regime banner: the solver now prints at start-up
      \texttt{regime : Brazovskii / Ginzburg-Landau / Unknown
      (r=..., lambda=..., gamma=...)}. A future run under the wrong
      regime cannot be missed.
\item Bumps
      \verb|MODULE_VERSIONS["tect_solver_pt_v3.py"] = "v3.1"|
      and re-stamps. Regenerates \texttt{version\_index.json}.
\end{enumerate}
Historical \texttt{emerge\_N64\_mixed\_s*} runs (including the
2026-04-15 seed$=17$ run) are flagged as GL-regime archival
baselines, not Brazovskii validations.

\paragraph{(E) Hot-path optimisation pass and theory-code audit.}
Three physically-equivalent optimisations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Opt A.} \verb|make_xyz_grid| in
      \texttt{PDE/tect\_solver\_pt\_v3.py} (v3.1 $\rightarrow$ v3.2)
      returns sparse NumPy views with shapes $(N,1,1)$, $(1,N,1)$,
      $(1,1,N)$ instead of three dense $(N,N,N)$ meshes.
      \verb|make_xyz_grid_dense| retained for the sites that
      genuinely require a materialised tensor. Bit-exact on the
      $32^{3}$ test grid.
\item \emph{Opt B.} \verb|make_mock_branch_data| and
      \verb|reconstruct_orbit_tangents| refactored:
      the $R=\mathrm{stack}(X,Y,Z)$ tensor is eliminated, phase
      fields are formed by three broadcast multiplications
      $\hat{n}_{x}X+\hat{n}_{y}Y+\hat{n}_{z}Z$, and the six complex
      exponentials $e^{\pm i\varphi_{n,e_{1},e_{2}}}$ are cached
      once and reused across all three branches. Memory footprint
      of the mock-branch assembly drops by a factor $\approx 6$ at
      $N=64$; output tensor shape and norm preserved
      ($\lVert\psi_{\mathrm{BCC}}\rVert = 60.567256$ at $N=32$).
\item \emph{Opt C.} \texttt{PDE/real\_backend\_pt\_bcc\_mixed\_v3.py}
      (v3.0 $\rightarrow$ v3.1). Adds \verb|_kmesh_cached|
      (\verb|functools.lru_cache|, size 32) and accessors
      \verb|_get_kmesh|, \verb|_get_kmesh_scalar| returning the
      full 3D meshgrid $(K_{X}, K_{Y}, K_{Z})$ as cached tensors.
      Hot-loop callers \verb|_grad_components_t| and
      \verb|_spectral_derivative_scalar_t| fetch the cached
      meshgrid rather than rebuilding it on every invocation.
      \verb|_bcc_positive_symbol_from_axes| rewritten to use
      explicit 1-D broadcast products
      $c_{x}(k_{x})\,c_{y}(k_{y})\,c_{z}(k_{z})$, eliminating the
      internal \texttt{torch.meshgrid}.
\end{enumerate}
All three optimisations are algebraic identities. Spectral
Laplacian negative-semidefiniteness, translation symmetry, and BCC
isotropy (G-list off-diagonal $\leq 2.23\times 10^{-18}$) are
preserved.

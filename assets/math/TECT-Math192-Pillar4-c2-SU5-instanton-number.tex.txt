\section*{1. Setup: SU(5) Instanton Classification}

\subsection*{§1.1 Principal SU(5) bundles on CP²}

Principal SU(5) bundles over $\mathbb{CP}^2$ are classified topologically by their **second Chern class**:
$$c_2(P_{\mathrm{SU}(5)}) = \frac{1}{8\pi^2} \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} \mathrm{Tr}(F \wedge F),$$
where $F$ is the SU(5) curvature 2-form on the principal bundle. This integral is an integer, called the **instanton number**:
$$n = c_2(P_{\mathrm{SU}(5)}) \in \mathbb{Z},$$
and is classified by the homotopy group:
$$\pi_3(\mathrm{SU}(5)) = \mathbb{Z}.$$

**Key fact**: The first Chern class $c_1(\mathrm{SU}(5)) = 0$ **always**, since SU(5) generators are traceless: $\sum_{a=1}^{24} \mathrm{tr}(T_a) = 0$. Therefore, the **only non-Abelian topological invariant** is $c_2$.

\subsection*{§1.2 Reduction: what determines $c_2$?}

For the **associated vector bundle** $E = P_{\mathrm{SU}(5)} \times_\rho V$ (where $\rho$ is a representation of SU(5) and $V$ is the representation space), the second Chern class of the associated bundle is:
$$c_2(E) \equiv n \cdot c_2(\mathrm{ad}),$$
where the proportionality depends on the representation $\rho$ and the bundle's topological structure.

For the **fundamental representation** $\mathbf{5}$ or the **adjoint representation** $\mathbf{24}$, the numerical coefficient is determined by representation theory and Dynkin index, but the **underlying topological content** is always the instanton number $n$ of the principal bundle.

\subsection*{§2.2 Why Cartan-only transitions imply zero instanton number}

The SU(5) curvature 2-form, derived from the connection 1-form $A = g^{-1}dg + g^{-1}Ag$, decomposes as:
$$F = dA + A \wedge A.$$

For a connection built from Cartan-generator transitions $g = \exp(i\phi T_3)$, the connection 1-form on a patch is:
$$A^{(i)} = \left(\exp(i\phi T_3)\right)^{-1} d\left(\exp(i\phi T_3)\right) = -i \, d\phi \, T_3,$$
where the minus sign comes from the $U(1)$ subgroup generated by $T_3$.

**Curvature**:
$$F^{(i)} = d\left(-i \, d\phi \, T_3\right) + \left(-i \, d\phi \, T_3\right) \wedge \left(-i \, d\phi \, T_3\right).$$

The first term is zero (exterior derivative of a 1-form is a 2-form, and $-i \, d\phi$ is already closed: $d(-i \, d\phi) = 0$). The second term is:
$$\left(-i \, d\phi \, T_3\right) \wedge \left(-i \, d\phi \, T_3\right) = (-i)^2 (d\phi \wedge d\phi) \, (T_3 \wedge T_3) = -(d\phi \wedge d\phi) \, (T_3 \times T_3).$$

But $T_3 \times T_3 = 0$ (since the commutator $[T_3, T_3] = 0$, and $T_3$ itself is a Lie algebra element, not an operator). Therefore:
$$F^{(i)} = 0.$$

**Instanton number**:
$$n = \frac{1}{8\pi^2} \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} \mathrm{Tr}(F \wedge F) = \frac{1}{8\pi^2} \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} \mathrm{Tr}(0 \wedge 0) = 0.$$

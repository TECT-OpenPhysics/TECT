$$\langle\Psi^*(\mathbf{k}) \Psi(-\mathbf{k}')\rangle = (2\pi)^3 \delta^3(\mathbf{k} - \mathbf{k}')

$$\langle\Psi(\mathbf{k}, t) J_{\mathbf{k}'}(0) \rangle = (2\pi)^3 \delta(\mathbf{k} - \mathbf{k}')

By the fluctuation-dissipation theorem (§2 of Math145):
$$P_\Psi(k, T) = \frac{2T}{\Gamma \omega_k} \chi''(\mathbf{k}, \omega_k),$$

linearized TDGL operator).

$$P_\Psi(k, T) \propto \begin{cases}

\text{const} & \text{(peak, } k \approx k_c \text{)} \\

\end{cases}$$
where $k_c \sim q_0 = 0.6802$ (the BCC wavenumber) and the exponent $\eta_k$ is the anomalous dimension
(from RG, Math82-I or later).

where $f(a, k)$ is a suppression factor depending on the equation-of-state during evolution.

§3. DARK MATTER INTERPRETATION AND RELIC-DENSITY CALCULATION
-------------------------------------------------------------

**Candidate mechanism**: BCC topological defects (vortices, disclinations, monopoles) *may* serve as
dark-matter candidates if:
(i) They are stable over cosmological timescales (weak-interaction condition).
(ii) Their relic abundance matches $\Omega_{\rm DM} h^2 = 0.120 \pm 0.001$ (Planck 2018).

**Relic-abundance calculation** (Boltzmann equation approach):
The comoving number density $n_{\rm defect}(t)$ evolves as:
$$\frac{dn_{\rm defect}}{dt} + 3Hn_{\rm defect} = -\langle\sigma_{\rm ann} v\rangle (n_{\rm defect}^2 - n_{\rm eq}^2),$$
where $\sigma_{\rm ann}$ is the annihilation cross-section and $n_{\rm eq}$ is the equilibrium density.

**Freeze-out condition**: If defects are never in thermal equilibrium (e.g., they are relics from the
phase transition), then $n_{\rm eq} = 0$ and they simply dilute as $a^{-3}$ (matter-like).

**Today's density**:
$$\Omega_{\rm DM} h^2 = \frac{\rho_{\rm DM, today}}{\rho_{\rm crit}} = \frac{n_{\rm defect, today} \times m_{\rm defect}}{3H_0^2 / (8\pi G)}.$$

**Estimation** (order-of-magnitude):
- Freezeout defect density (Math146): $n_{\rm defect} \sim 10^{33}$ cm$^{-3}$.
- Dilution factor (redshift from $T_c \sim 10^{27}$ K to today): $a^3 \sim (10^{27})^3 / (10^{-3})^3 \sim 10^{90}$.
- Today's density: $n_{\rm defect, today} \sim 10^{-57}$ cm$^{-3}$ (extremely sparse).
- Mass per defect (energy per length, BCC vortex): $m_{\rm defect} \sim E_{\text{vortex}} / c^2 \sim 10^{-3}$ eV (rough estimate).
- Relic density: $\Omega_{\rm DM} h^2 \sim (10^{-57}) \times (10^{-3}) \sim 10^{-60}$ (far below observed!).

**Implication**: Pure topological defects do **not** provide enough dark matter. Instead, they may
constitute a *subdominant* component or a "dark minor" (alongside WIMPs, axions, etc.).

§4. COSMOLOGICAL CONSTANT AND VACUUM ENERGY
--------------------------------------------

**Mechanism**: During the phase transition, the free energy of the system changes discontinuously
(latent heat). This energy density contributes to the effective cosmological constant:
$$\Lambda_{\text{eff}} = \frac{8\pi}{3} G (\Delta\mathcal{F}_{\text{latent}}).$$

**Calculation**:
The free energy density in the pre-transition phase (at $T = T_c^+$, just above transition) is:
$$f_{\rm pre} = \mathcal{F}[\Psi = 0] / V = 0 \quad \text{(trivial vacuum)}.$$

The free energy density in the post-transition phase (at $T = T_c^-$, just below transition) is:
$$f_{\rm post} = \mathcal{F}[\Psi = \Psi_{\rm BCC}] / V \approx -\frac{\mu^4}{4\lambda} \quad \text{(from Hartree)}
$$
(using the fact that at the BCC ground state, $|\Psi_{\rm BCC}|^2 = -\mu^2/\lambda$).

**Latent heat**:
$$\Delta\mathcal{F}_{\text{latent}} = |f_{\rm post}| = \frac{\mu^4_c}{4\lambda} \sim \frac{(0.26)^2}{4 \times 0.43} \sim 0.04 \quad \text{in lattice units}.$$

In physical units, with $\mu^2_c \sim (10^{27})^2$ K (GUT scale, dimensionful):
$$\Delta\mathcal{F}_{\text{latent}} \sim \rho_{\rm GUT}^4 / M_{\rm Pl}^4 \times (0.04) \sim \text{few} \times 10^{-120} M_{\rm Pl}^4.$$

This is **much smaller than the observed dark-energy density** ($\rho_\Lambda \sim 10^{-120} M_{\rm Pl}^4$),
suggesting that the BCC phase-transition energy is *not* the origin of today's dark energy, but rather
a subdominant contribution.

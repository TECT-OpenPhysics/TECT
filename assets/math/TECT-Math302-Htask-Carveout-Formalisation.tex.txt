\section{Definitions and motivation}

\begin{definition}[Proof-rigor vs realization-gate]
\label{def:carveout}
A hypothesis $H$ in a TECT atomic theorem is classified as:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Proof-rigor} ($H \in \mathcal H_{\rm proof}$) iff
$H$ asserts a mathematical statement whose truth value is
determined by analytical proof or rigorous computation, independent
of operational deadlines.
\item \textbf{Realization-gate} ($H = H_{\rm task}$) iff $H$
asserts that a specific numerical task has been completed by a
specific deadline, with the truth value determined by the gate
verdict (PASS/FAIL/DEFER).
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

The two categories differ in:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Closure mechanism}: proof-rigor closes via mathematical
verification (peer review, audit, proof completion); realization-gate
closes via operational execution (running code, hitting deadline).
\item \textbf{Failure consequence}: proof-rigor failure is permanent
unless the proof is repaired; realization-gate failure can be
re-tried with different parameters or different task design.
\item \textbf{Audit trail}: proof-rigor is documented in Math notes;
realization-gate is documented in OPEN-QUESTIONS / NEGATIVE-RESULTS
+ run manifests.
\end{enumerate}

The carve-out is the recognition that mixing the two in a single
min-rule conflates two incommensurable evidence types.

\section{Theorem 302.1 statement and proof}

\begin{theorem}[Proof-rigor carve-out]
\label{thm:carveout}
For an atomic theorem $\Theta = \bigwedge_{H \in \mathcal H} H$
with $\mathcal H = \mathcal H_{\rm proof} \cup \{H_{\rm task}\}$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The proof-rigor composite tier is
$\mathrm{tier}_{\rm proof}(\Theta) =
\min_{H \in \mathcal H_{\rm proof}} \mathrm{tier}(H)$.
\item The realization status of $\Theta$ is
$\mathrm{status}_{\rm real}(\Theta) =
\mathrm{verdict}(H_{\rm task})\in\{\text{PASS},\text{FAIL},\text{DEFER},\text{PENDING}\}$.
\item The atomic-theorem record reports both
$\mathrm{tier}_{\rm proof}$ and $\mathrm{status}_{\rm real}$
explicitly.
\end{enumerate}
This is well-defined because $\mathcal H_{\rm proof}$ and
$\{H_{\rm task}\}$ are disjoint by Definition \ref{def:carveout}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The disjointness is by construction (proof-rigor and realization-gate
are mutually exclusive categories). The min-rule (Math301
Proposition) applies within each category. The combination of
$(\mathrm{tier}_{\rm proof}, \mathrm{status}_{\rm real})$ is a
two-component record, not a scalar tier; this is the explicit
acknowledgment that the two evidence types do not aggregate. \qed
\end{proof}

\section{Devil's-advocate self-test}
\paragraph{(α) ``Carve-out is a disguised tier inflation:
realization-gate is genuine uncertainty, removing it makes the
tier look stronger than it is.''}
\textbf{VALID-WITH-EXPLICIT-DOCUMENTATION.} The carve-out does NOT
remove uncertainty from the record; it explicitly tracks
$\mathrm{status}_{\rm real}$ alongside the tier. External readers
seeing "T6, realization PENDING" understand the proof is sound
mathematically and the open issue is operational. This is more
honest than a flat-T2 collapse that hides the proof rigor or a
flat-T6 claim that hides the realization gate.

\paragraph{(β) ``Definition \ref{def:carveout} is too sharp; some
hypotheses are intermediate (e.g., conditional-on-numerical).''}
\textbf{VALID-WITH-CASE-BY-CASE-AUDIT.} True; intermediate cases
exist. The carve-out is a clean binary partition, but borderline
hypotheses must be classified case-by-case in the per-pillar
Math note. For Pillar 4, Math279 enumeration is unambiguous; for
other pillars, the classifier is the responsibility of the relevant
final-consolidation note.

\paragraph{(γ) ``Why is realization-gate excluded but textbook
hypotheses (e.g., Machacek-Vaughn 1983) included? Both are
'external'.''}
\textbf{DISMISSED.} Textbook hypotheses are proof-rigor in the sense
of Definition \ref{def:carveout}: their truth value is determined
by mathematical verification (peer review of the cited reference),
not by operational deadlines. Realization-gates have a deadline +
verdict structure that textbook citations lack. The categories are
distinct.

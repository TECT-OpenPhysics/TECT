\subsection*{3.\ Hypothesis $H_{\rm compression-invariance}$ (binding)}

\[

\paragraph*{Justification of $H_{\rm compression-invariance}$.}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Brazovskii lock equations $\phi_0^2 = -4\lambda/(15\gamma)$ and $\mu^2_{\rm sp} = 4\lambda^2/(30\gamma)$ depend only on $(\lambda, \gamma)$, NOT on which specific equilibrium $\Psi^{*,(i)}$ is selected. Lock equations are branch-agnostic.
\item BCC reciprocal-lattice geometry ($q_0 = 0.6802$, BCC primitive vectors) is fixed by the lattice convention, NOT by $\mathcal{I}$.
\item $Y$ (Young modulus / elastic stiffness) and $a_{\rm BCC}$ (lattice spacing) are intrinsic material constants, NOT branch-dependent.
\item $\hbar$ enters via canonical quantisation post-classical (Math79-AddB Cor. + Math60-C-AddA QO1); the introduction of $\hbar$ does not depend on which branch is the gauge-emergent one.
\end{enumerate}
Therefore $H_{\rm compression-invariance}$ holds as a structural property of the Brazovskii free energy + BCC lattice + canonical quantisation framework. It is NOT contingent on Math75-Q2-AddA RGE numerical execution; the parameter count is theoretical, not empirical.

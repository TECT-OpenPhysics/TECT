\section{Devil's-advocate self-test (CLAUDE.md §6.3.1)}
% ===========================================================================

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$.}
\emph{The Wave 1/3/4/5 audit produced TWO ``external use
FORBIDDEN'' flags (Paper-05 and Paper-07) within a single commit ---
this is a high-impact correction rate that may indicate the audit
itself is too aggressive.}

\textbf{DISMISSED.} Paper-05 contains a mathematically incorrect
``$\mathrm{Index}(D) = \mathrm{wind}(m)$'' identification that
fails on the 3D Brillouin torus without a Callias-type framework
(textbook fact). Paper-07 attempts to force a three-generation
uniqueness theorem from a non-standard $A_{\rm grav} = \sum_f Q_f^3$
combination plus Witten + mod-2 arguments that do not close at the
draft level. Both are genuine theorem-level defects that the audit
correctly catches; the ``FORBIDDEN'' flag is the appropriate
mitigation pending full rewrite. The aggressiveness of the audit is
a strength, not a weakness, in the post-Math310-AddA
self-confirmation-bias-mitigation environment.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$.}
\emph{The Wave 1/3/4/5 corrections rely on the same hostile-referee
methodology as Math314 + AddA + AddB --- this concentrates the audit
authority in a single source. The same concern as Math314-AddA
Objection $\gamma$ applies: independent peer review remains
structurally needed.}

\textbf{VALID-with-mitigation.} The mitigation is the existing
\texttt{Q-2026-05-02-Wave7-Independent-Audit} (Math314-AddA Objection
$\gamma$ follow-up, in OPEN-QUESTIONS.md). The Wave-1/3/4/5 papers
are now added to the scope of that independent peer review request:
the request now covers all 30 papers across Wave 1/2/3/4/5/6/7
flagged \texttt{[NEEDS\_UPDATE]} or \texttt{[INTERNAL\_*]} after
Math314 + AddA + AddB + AddC.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$.}
\emph{The CLAUDE.md \S15.6 rule \#7 added in this commit is broad
(four sub-rules: analytical-class, theorem-vs-estimate, rank-dependence,
single-shell scope, window-verdict-vs-precision). Some of these
sub-rules are very specific to single audits (rank-dependence
mirrors TI-1 only; window-verdict-vs-precision mirrors Paper-04 only)
--- isn't this over-fitting the rule to the failure cases?}

\textbf{VALID-with-mitigation.} The four sub-rules are intentionally
specific because each addresses a structural pattern the
autonomous-agent dispatch produced repeatedly. Generalising the rule
further would dilute its preventive value. The lesson is: each
identified failure mode deserves an explicit dispatch-prompt
checklist item, even at the cost of rule length. The Math310-AddA /
Math314 / AddA / AddB / AddC chain is the canonical example
collection cited at the end of \S15.6 rule \#7.

\section{Quantitative sanity check (CLAUDE.md §6.3.4)}
% ===========================================================================

\textbf{Magnitude check on Paper-04 F-GAP4 wording defect.} The
original wording ``$M_{\Sigma_0} = 1.26 \times 10^{16}$ GeV \dots\
$\pm 5\%$ binding condition'' implies a $\pm 6 \times 10^{14}$ GeV
window. The canonical Math311 window
$\mu_{\rm defect} \in [10^{13}, 10^{17}]\,\mathrm{GeV}$ has total
width $\approx 10^{17} - 10^{13} \approx 10^{17}$ GeV (4 orders of
magnitude). Ratio: $5\% \cdot 10^{16} / 10^{17} = 5 \times 10^{-3}$
of the canonical window. The original $\pm 5\%$ wording was
therefore claiming precision $\sim 200 \times$ tighter than the
canonical verdict shell --- a structural mis-classification per
CLAUDE.md \S15.6 rule \#7 (window-verdict vs precision-prediction).

\textbf{Sign-check on Paper-08 ``invariance vs violation'' confusion.}
The reported interval $J_1 \in [5.99 \times 10^{-2}, 1.51 \times
10^{-1}]$ has lower bound $5.99 \times 10^{-2} > 0$. The interval
strictly excludes $0$. ``Lorentz invariance certification'' would
require the interval to CONTAIN $0$. The original title was therefore
a sign-error in the physical interpretation: violation $J_1 \ne 0$ is
established, NOT invariance.

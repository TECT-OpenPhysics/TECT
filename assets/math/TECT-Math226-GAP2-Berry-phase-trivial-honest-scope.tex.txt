\subsection*{1.2 Berry Phase on Simply-Connected Spaces}

**Standard topological fact** (Berry 1984, Simon 1983):

Let $\mathcal{H}(\lambda)$ be a family of Hamiltonians parametrised by
$\lambda(t) \in M$ (a parameter manifold). For an eigenstate $|n(t)\rangle$,
the Berry phase is:
$$\Gamma[C] = \oint_C \langle n(\lambda) | i \nabla_\lambda | n(\lambda) \rangle \, d\lambda.$$

**Key theorem**: If $M$ is **simply connected** ($\pi_1(M) = \{e\}$), then
**every loop $C$ is contractible**. For a contractible loop,
$$\oint_C d\lambda \, (\text{connection form}) = \int_{\text{disk bounded by } C} \, d(\text{connection form}) = 0$$
by Stokes' theorem (the disk is entirely contained in $M$, so no monodromies).

Therefore: **If $M$ is simply connected, the Berry phase vanishes on all
loops.**

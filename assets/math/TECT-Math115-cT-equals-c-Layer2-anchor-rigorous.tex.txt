\paragraph{Claim.}

\paragraph{Counter-argument.}

\textbf{VALID with mitigation}. The coupling of matter to TECT's
metric is \textit{not} yet fully derived from first principles
within TECT. However:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The Equivalence Principle (weak form) is a direct consequence
  of the symmetry of the Brazovskii functional under translations
  and rotations (Noether theorem).

\item Once the metric is identified, the minimal coupling
  $S_{\rm matter} = \int d^4x \sqrt{-g} \mathcal{L}_{\rm matter}(g)$
  follows from diffeomorphism invariance (a symmetry of TECT at
  large scales).

\item The strong Equivalence Principle (also tested experimentally)
  requires checking that gravitational self-energy couples the same
  way as mechanical energy, which is beyond the scope of this note
  but is addressed in Math98 (phase-transition origin).
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Mitigation}: explicit documentation in Math115 \S2.1 that
matter coupling is \textit{inferred} (not fully derived), with
reference to future work on the strong EP.

§ 1. STATEMENT
=============================================================

Let CP^2 = (C^3 \ {0}) / C^* be complex projective two-space with its
standard Fubini-Study Kähler structure. Let O = O_{CP^2} denote the
holomorphic structure sheaf. Let O(k), k ∈ Z, denote the canonical
family of holomorphic line bundles (O(1) = hyperplane bundle).

Theorem (Math202).
-----------------

  (A) H^1(CP^2, O) = 0  and  H^2(CP^2, O) = 0
      (Hodge / Dolbeault sheaf-cohomology vanishing).

  (B) The set of isomorphism classes of holomorphic line bundles on CP^2
      is

           Pic(CP^2)  ≅  H^2(CP^2, Z)  ≅  Z,

      where the isomorphism is given by the first Chern class
      c_1 : Pic(CP^2) → Z. Equivalently, every holomorphic line bundle
      on CP^2 is isomorphic to O(k) for a unique k ∈ Z.

  (C) Application to the U(1)_χ bundle of the Pillar-4 base. The
      U(1)_χ bundle on CP^2 (constructed in Math162 / Math167 as the
      reducible factor of the SO(10)/SU(5) coset bundle) is, at the
      smooth level, a principal U(1) bundle whose smooth-bundle
      isomorphism class is determined by

           c_1(U(1)_χ)  ∈  H^2(CP^2, Z)  ≅  Z

      via the standard topological classification of principal U(1)
      bundles on a compact CW complex. If, in addition, the U(1)_χ
      bundle is endowed with the holomorphic structure induced by the
      Math162 / Math167 complex atlas — which we adopt by construction
      at the operating point of Math80-AddA — then its associated
      complex line bundle is biholomorphic to O(k_χ) for a unique
      integer k_χ = c_1(U(1)_χ). The two classifications (smooth and
      holomorphic) yield the same integer because Pic^0(CP^2) = 0
      (Hodge vanishing in (A) above): the holomorphic refinement
      adds no continuous moduli on top of the topological data.

      The two layers are kept separate in the proofs below so that any
      downstream argument that uses ONLY the smooth classification can
      do so without inheriting an implicit Kähler-metric assumption.

§ 2. PROOF (TWO ROUTES)
=============================================================

  Route A (Hodge-theoretic).
  ------------------------

  CP^2 is a smooth Kähler manifold of complex dimension 2. Its Hodge
  diamond is (Griffiths-Harris, "Principles of Algebraic Geometry",
  Chap. 0):

         h^{0,0} = 1
       h^{0,1} = 0    h^{1,0} = 0
     h^{0,2} = 0    h^{1,1} = 1    h^{2,0} = 0
       h^{1,2} = 0    h^{2,1} = 0
         h^{2,2} = 1

  By Dolbeault's theorem H^q(X, Ω^p) ≅ H^{p,q}(X) for X compact Kähler.
  For p = 0, Ω^0 = O, so

           H^q(CP^2, O) = H^{0,q}(CP^2) = h^{0,q}(CP^2).

  Reading off the diamond:

           H^0(CP^2, O) = C   (constant functions),
           H^1(CP^2, O) = 0,
           H^2(CP^2, O) = 0.

  This proves (A).

  For (B), the exponential sheaf sequence

         0 → Z → O → O^* → 0

  gives the long exact sequence in cohomology

         H^1(CP^2, O) → H^1(CP^2, O^*) → H^2(CP^2, Z)
                                                        ↓
                                             H^2(CP^2, O) → ...

  The left term is 0 by (A). The right term is 0 by (A). Therefore the
  central connecting map is an isomorphism:

           H^1(CP^2, O^*) ≅ H^2(CP^2, Z).

  But H^1(CP^2, O^*) = Pic(CP^2) (by the standard definition of the
  Picard group as isomorphism classes of holomorphic line bundles), and
  H^2(CP^2, Z) ≅ Z (singular cohomology of CP^2). The composite map
  is the first Chern class c_1. This proves (B).

  Route B (explicit Čech via the standard affine cover).
  ------------------------------------------------------

  Take the standard affine cover U_i = D_+(z_i) ⊂ CP^2, i = 0, 1, 2.
  Each U_i is affine (≅ Spec C[u, v] for affine coordinates), and so
  is every finite intersection U_σ = ∩_{i ∈ σ} U_i. On an affine
  scheme, every quasi-coherent sheaf has vanishing higher cohomology
  (Serre's vanishing theorem; equivalently, in the analytic category,
  Cartan's Theorem B applies because each U_σ is Stein). Therefore
  the cover {U_i} is acyclic for the structure sheaf O = O_{CP^2},
  and Čech cohomology computes sheaf cohomology:

           H^q(CP^2, O)  =  Ȟ^q({U_i}, O).

  The standard computation (Hartshorne, "Algebraic Geometry",
  Chap. III §5, applied to O = O_{CP^2}(0); equivalently Griffiths-
  Harris, "Principles of Algebraic Geometry", Chap. 0) gives

           H^q(CP^2, O)  =  0      for all q > 0,

  in particular H^1(CP^2, O) = H^2(CP^2, O) = 0, recovering (A).

§ 3. CONSEQUENCE FOR THE PILLAR-4 U(1)_χ BUNDLE
=============================================================

The U(1)_χ bundle on CP^2 enters the TECT framework as the reducible
U(1) factor in the structure-group reduction

           SO(10) / SU(5)   ⊃   SU(5) × U(1)_χ

restricted to the CP^2 base of Math162 / Math167. By the smooth-bundle
classification c_1(U(1)_χ) ∈ H^2(CP^2, Z) = Z, and by part (B) the
holomorphic refinement does not introduce extra moduli (since
H^1(CP^2, O) = 0 ⇒ Pic^0(CP^2) = 0).

Therefore the U(1)_χ bundle is uniquely characterised, up to bundle
isomorphism, by a single integer c_1(U(1)_χ) =: k_χ ∈ Z. No additional
continuous moduli are present.

This is the gateway lemma for the forcing argument. ANY further
microscopic constraint that pins down k_χ to a specific integer (e.g.
BCC inversion equivariance — deferred to Math203) immediately fixes the
U(1)_χ bundle up to isomorphism. Conversely, the existence of multiple
inequivalent BCC-equivariant choices for k_χ would constitute a
genuine atlas non-uniqueness and would force a downgrade of Math191's
"c_1(U(1)_χ) = 0" claim from "PROVED CONDITIONAL on canonical realisation"
to "PROVED CONDITIONAL on a representation choice".

§ 4. (CAUSE, EVIDENCE, FALSIFICATION) TRIPLE — CLAUDE.md §6.3.3
=============================================================

  cause       : Standard Hodge theory of complex projective 2-space;
                applies whenever the base of the Pillar-4 bundle is
                (biholomorphic to) CP^2 with its Fubini-Study Kähler
                structure, as established in Math162 / Math80-AddA.

  evidence    : Hodge numbers h^{p,q}(CP^2) tabulated above; consistent
                with multiple independent sources (Griffiths-Harris,
                Hartshorne, Voisin). Čech computation in §2 Route B
                provides an independent verification.

  falsification : Any explicit construction of a non-trivial element of
                H^1(CP^2, O) — equivalently, a non-trivial holomorphic
                line bundle on CP^2 with c_1 = 0 — would falsify (A)
                and (B). Such an object cannot exist by Hodge theory;
                accordingly Math202 is unconditionally PROVED.

§ 5. DEVIL'S-ADVOCATE SELF-TEST — CLAUDE.md §6.3.1
=============================================================

  Objection α : "What if the U(1)_χ bundle is endowed with a non-
                standard topology that breaks the holomorphic
                identification?"
  Counter      : The Math162 atlas is by construction holomorphic. The
                BCC reciprocal-lattice embedding into the Grassmannian
                Gr(2,5) (Math80-AddA) and the subsequent restriction
                to CP^2 ⊂ Gr(2,5) preserves the complex structure. The
                topological U(1) classification H^2(CP^2, Z) = Z and
                the holomorphic Picard classification Pic(CP^2) = Z
                agree because the connecting Pic^0(CP^2) is zero
                (H^1(O) = 0). DISMISSED.

  Objection β : "Path-connectedness of Pic does not imply cohomology
                vanishing of the structure sheaf."
  Counter      : This conflates Pic^0 (continuous moduli of bundles
                with c_1 = 0) with H^1(O) (Dolbeault cohomology of the
                trivial sheaf). The lemma uses h^{0,1}(CP^2) = 0 from
                the Hodge diamond, not a Pic^0 argument. The two
                are separate; the first is what we need. DISMISSED.

  Objection γ : "Hodge numbers depend on the Kähler structure; what if
                the BCC-induced metric on CP^2 is non-Kähler?"
  Counter      : For a fixed compact complex manifold, the Hodge
                numbers h^{p,q} are determined by the complex
                structure: the Hodge decomposition uses a Kähler
                metric to define the ∂-Laplacian, but the resulting
                dimensions h^{p,q} are independent of the particular
                Kähler-metric choice (Voisin, "Hodge Theory and
                Complex Algebraic Geometry I", §6). For CP^2
                specifically, h^{0,q} are fixed by its standard
                complex structure and do not depend on the metric
                deformation. Since the Math162 / Math167 base is
                biholomorphic to CP^2 (Math80-AddA), and the
                BCC-induced metric is Kähler at the Math80-AddA
                operating point and a smooth Kähler-class
                deformation of Fubini-Study elsewhere, the
                vanishing h^{0,1}(CP^2) = h^{0,2}(CP^2) = 0 is
                unaffected. We do NOT claim Hodge numbers are
                topological invariants in the sense of homotopy-
                invariant of the underlying smooth manifold; the
                claim is more limited: they are invariants of the
                fixed complex structure on CP^2. DISMISSED.

  All three objections DISMISSED with explicit counter-arguments.

\subsection*{1. Why naive 1-loop is broken}

Brazovskii 1-loop self-energy:
$$
\Sigma^{(1)}(k)\;=\;\tfrac{\lambda}{2}\int\!\frac{d^{3}p}{(2\pi)^{3}}\,G(p),
\qquad
G(p)=[Y(p^{2}-q_{0}^{2})^{2}/q_{0}^{2}+\mu^{2}]^{-1}.
$$
Using shell concentration $|p|\approx q_{0}$ with shell-width
$\delta p\sim q_{0}\sqrt{\mu^{2}/Y}$:
$$
I_{\rm shell}\;\sim\;\frac{4\pi q_{0}^{2}\delta p}{\mu^{2}}
\;=\;\frac{4\pi q_{0}^{3}}{\mu^{2}}\sqrt{\mu^{2}/Y}
\;=\;\frac{4\pi q_{0}^{3}}{\sqrt{Y\mu^{2}}}.
$$

\paragraph{Numerical at TECT v2.4 op-pt.}
$q_{0}=0.6802$, $Y=1$, $\mu^{2}=5\times 10^{-3}$.
$$
\frac{\lambda I_{\rm shell}}{\mu^{2}}\;\sim\;\frac{(-0.43)\cdot 4\pi\,(0.6802)^{3}/\sqrt{5\times 10^{-3}}}{5\times 10^{-3}}\;\approx\;-220.
$$
\textbf{The 1-loop correction to $\mu^{2}$ exceeds $\mu^{2}$ itself by factor $\sim 220$.} Naive perturbation theory is invalid.

This is precisely the Brazovskii fluctuation-induced first-order
mechanism: the shell-integrated divergence as $\mu^{2}\to 0$ means
the IR behaviour cannot be analytically continued from the disordered
phase via 1-loop. Self-consistent (Hartree) treatment required.

\subsection*{2. Hartree-resummed framework}

Brazovskii Hartree equation: physical mass-squared $\mu^{2}_{H}$ solves
$$
\mu^{2}_{H}\;=\;\mu^{2}_{0}+\lambda I_{\rm shell}(\mu^{2}_{H}),
$$
with self-consistent $I_{\rm shell}(\mu^{2})\propto q_{0}^{3}/\sqrt{Y\mu^{2}}$.
Solution scales as $\mu^{2}_{H}\propto \lambda^{2/3}q_{0}^{2}/Y^{1/3}$
in the Brazovskii regime (cf.\ Brazovskii 1975 \S 4).

\paragraph{Implication for TECT operating point.} The reported
``$\mu^{2}_{\rm op}=5\times 10^{-3}$'' is the \emph{physical (Hartree)}
mass at the operating point, not a bare mass. All matchings in
Math110-AddG used this Hartree-resummed value implicitly.

\paragraph{Therefore $G^{\rm TECT}$ extracted via Math110-AddG matching is the Hartree-physical $G$, not bare $G$.} This is consistent with the Math115 identification $c_{T}=c$, since $c_{T}=\sqrt{\mu/\rho_{\rm cond}}$ is also Hartree-physical.

\subsection*{4. Four independent discrimination tests}

\paragraph{Test 1 -- Multi-scale length consistency.}

Compute three distinct TECT length scales:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $a_{\rm BCC}^{\rm wave}=2\pi/q_{0}=9.24$ (wavelength).
\item $\xi_{\rm corr}(\mu^{2})=1/(\varepsilon q_{0})\approx 22.6$ at op-pt (correlation length).
\item $h_{\rm latt}=L/N=62.20/32\approx 1.94$ (numerical grid).
\end{enumerate}
Each in TECT natural units. Compare to its Planck-unit prediction.
Test:
\begin{itemize}
\item All three shift by SAME factor (e.g., 1.303): $\Rightarrow (\alpha)$.
\item Different factors: $\Rightarrow (\gamma)$ Hartree-physical scale-dependent renormalization.
\item Only one scale shifts: $\Rightarrow (\beta)$ matching artefact.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Test 2 -- Multi-$\mu^{2}$ scan (definitive).}

\item $R_{F5}(\mu^{2})\to 1$ as $\mu^{2}\to\mu^{2}_{c}^{-}$: $\Rightarrow (\gamma)$.
\item $R_{F5}(\mu^{2})$ diverges as $\mu^{2}\to-\infty$: confirms Hartree breakdown deep-quench regime.

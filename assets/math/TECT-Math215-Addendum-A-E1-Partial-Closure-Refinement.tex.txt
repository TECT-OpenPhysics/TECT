§ 6. DEVIL'S-ADVOCATE SELF-TEST — CLAUDE.md §6.3.1 + EXTENSIONS
=============================================================

  Objection α^215A: "If the new conditional set is {E1_{χ,5}, E2,
                E2', E3, E3'}, this is FIVE conditions. How does
                this compare to the 'canonical realisation'
                conditional?"

  Counter (HONEST SCOPE): The five conditions are PHYSICAL
                principles (positive stiffness, no flux, no
                offset, global minimum, ground-state-realised),
                not REPRESENTATION-THEORETIC choices. The
                "canonical realisation" conditional was opaque
                ("there exists a Math162 atlas with specific
                T_3 ∈ 𝔰𝔲(5) choice that gives c_1 = 0"). The
                new five-item set is SPECIFIC and TESTABLE.
                Substantive improvement, not regression.

  Objection β^215A: "The selection-vs-forcing distinction (CLAUDE.md
                §6.3 (m) MANDATORY): is the refined Math214 + Math215
                + Math215-AddA chain still a forcing theorem?"

  Counter (ADDRESSED): Yes, conditional on the five items. None
                of E1_{χ,5}, E2, E2', E3, E3' is an empirical
                input (like "SM has 16 fermions") or a
                representation-theoretic choice (like "Math162
                atlas T_3 ∈ 𝔰𝔲(5)"). All five are physics
                principles. Therefore the chain is a CONDITIONAL
                FORCING theorem, where each conditional item
                has independent physics content.

  Objection γ^215A: "Adding E2' and E3' as new conditionals — does
                this open the door to indefinite expansion of the
                conditional set as new audit findings emerge?"

  Counter (DOCUMENTED): The reviewer explicitly identified E2' and
                E3' as physical-principle additions, not
                representation-theoretic choices. Future audits
                might identify additional conditionals at the
                physics-principle level (not at the
                representation-theoretic level), but the structural
                point — that this is a forcing theorem with a
                specific finite set of physical-principle
                conditionals — is preserved. DOCUMENTED.

  Objection δ^215A: "The §6.3 (m) check (selection vs forcing,
                MANDATORY) was applied to Math215 (ADDRESSED), but
                the partial-closure verdict suggests the §6.3 (m)
                application was insufficient. What additional
                rigor is needed?"

  Counter (METHODOLOGY): The §6.3 (m) check correctly identified
                Math215 as forcing (vs selection); the issue was
                NOT selection-vs-forcing but rather the SCOPE of
                the forcing argument's coverage (SU(2) × U(1)_Y
                derived; SO(10)/SU(5) extrapolated). A new §6.3
                extension may be needed: (p) "Distinguish
                canonical-source-derived sector vs structurally-
                extrapolated sector". Math215 derived SU(2) × U(1)_Y
                rigorously; the SO(10)/SU(5) extension was
                structurally-extrapolated, which the §2 Step 5
                comment acknowledged but the overall closure
                claim did not properly weight.

\section{Setting and conditioning hierarchy}
\label{sec:setting}

The GAP-1 closure pathway has three nested precision tiers:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Structural tier} (Math291): given exact
$\{c, G, a_{\rm BCC}\}$, the formula
$\hbar_{\rm TECT}=c^{3}a_{\rm BCC}^{2}/(16\pi G)$ is algebraic
and exact. Uncertainty enters only through finite-precision input
of $a_{\rm BCC}$ (continuum-limit extrapolation residual).
\item \textbf{Physical tier 1-loop} (Math296): given
$\hbar_{\rm TECT}$ and SM running, the matching functional
$\gamma_\hbar^{(1)}(\mu)=\alpha_\hbar^{(1)}\ln(\mu/M_Z)$ absorbs
the leading log corrections at scale $\mu\in[M_Z, M_X]$.
\item \textbf{Physical tier 2-loop+} (Tasks \#147/\#148): residual
SM-loop matching at higher orders, absorbing whatever the 1-loop
ansatz misses.
\end{enumerate}

The structural tier propagation is the cleanest place to establish
a precision budget because it has no perturbative ambiguity. The
1-loop and 2-loop tiers introduce additional uncertainties that we
do not address here.

\section{Theorem statement and propagation}
\label{sec:theorem}

\begin{theorem}[F-GAP1 structural-tier precision budget]
\label{thm:budget}
Let $\hbar_{\rm TECT} = c^{3}a_{\rm BCC}^{2}/(16\pi G)$ be the
canonical Math291 structural formula, with $\{c,G\}$ exact textbook
constants and $a_{\rm BCC}$ extracted by Math82-H continuum
extrapolation with relative uncertainty
$\epsilon_a \equiv |\delta a_{\rm BCC}/a_{\rm BCC}|$. Define the
F-GAP1 budget $\epsilon_{F-GAP1} = 10^{-3}$. The F-GAP1 gate at the
structural tier is satisfied iff
\[
\epsilon_a \;<\; \frac{1}{2}\epsilon_{F-GAP1} \;=\; 5\times 10^{-4}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Standard error propagation for $\hbar = K\cdot a^{2}$ (with $K = c^{3}/(16\pi G)$ exact):
\begin{equation}
\delta\hbar = 2 K a\,\delta a
\;\Longrightarrow\;
\frac{|\delta\hbar|}{\hbar} = 2\frac{|\delta a|}{a} = 2\epsilon_a.
\end{equation}
The F-GAP1 gate $|\delta\hbar|/\hbar < \epsilon_{F-GAP1} = 10^{-3}$
becomes $2\epsilon_a < 10^{-3}$, i.e., $\epsilon_a < 5\times 10^{-4}$.
This is necessary; it is sufficient at the structural tier because
the formula has no other inputs with uncertainty. \qed
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The factor 2 is the polynomial degree of $a$ in the formula. Had
Math291 yielded a different scaling (e.g., $a^{3}$, $a^{1}$), the
budget multiplier would be different. Theorem \ref{thm:budget} is
schema-specific to Formula B.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Numerical magnitude}
With $a_{\rm BCC} = 4\sqrt{\pi}\,\ell_P = 1.146\times 10^{-34}$ m
(Math291 Eq.~10), the absolute uncertainty target is
\begin{equation}
|\delta a_{\rm BCC}| \;<\; 5\times 10^{-4} \cdot 1.146\times 10^{-34}\,\mathrm{m}
\;\approx\; 5.7\times 10^{-38}\,\mathrm{m}.
\end{equation}
This is below the Planck-scale resolution available numerically;
in practice the Richardson extrapolation works in dimensionless
lattice units where $a_{\rm BCC}^{\rm dim} \sim 1$ and the precision
target is the same fractional bound $5\times 10^{-4}$.

\section{Math82-H continuum extrapolation precision audit}
\label{sec:m82h-audit}

The Math82-H continuum-limit ladder runs Brazovskii BCC at
$N\in\{N_1, N_2, N_3, \ldots\}$ with $a_k = L/N_k$, then fits the
amplitude $f(a) = f_\infty + A_1 a + A_2 a^{2} + O(a^{3})$ via
Richardson extrapolation. The lattice spacing $a_{\rm BCC}$ is
extracted from the converged shell-mode pattern.

\subsection{Per-$N$ statistical precision}
On a converged BCC condensate at lattice $N$, the lattice-spacing
estimate is determined by the position of the first BCC shell peak
in Fourier space, with peak-position uncertainty
\begin{equation}
\delta a^{(N)} \sim \frac{1}{q_0\,N},
\end{equation}
i.e., one lattice momentum step. For $N=16$, $q_0 \sim 0.68$, this
gives $\delta a^{(16)}/a \sim 1/(0.68 \cdot 16) \sim 9\times 10^{-2}$
— two orders of magnitude above the budget.

For $N=32$: $\delta a/a \sim 4.6\times 10^{-2}$. Still above budget.
For $N=64$: $\delta a/a \sim 2.3\times 10^{-2}$. Still above.

\subsection{Richardson extrapolation gain}
The Richardson fit reduces the uncertainty by a factor proportional
to the number of $N$ points and the convergence order. With three
$N$ points and quadratic-in-$a$ leading correction, the extrapolation
reduces the uncertainty to
\begin{equation}
\delta a_{\rm BCC}^{\rm cont} \sim \delta a^{(N_{\max})} \cdot
\bigl(a_{\min}/a_{\max}\bigr)^{p}
\quad\text{with } p\sim 2.
\end{equation}
For the ladder $N\in\{16,32,64\}$ with $a\in\{1.0, 0.5, 0.25\}$:
$\delta a_{\rm BCC}^{\rm cont}/a_{\rm BCC} \sim
2.3\times 10^{-2} \cdot (0.25/1.0)^{2} \sim 1.4\times 10^{-3}$.

\noindent\boxed{\text{Three-point ladder budget projection: }\sim 1.4\times 10^{-3} \;>\; \mathbf{5\times 10^{-4}\text{ (required)}}.}

The three-point ladder \emph{misses} the F-GAP1 structural budget by
a factor $\sim 3$. This is a quantitative falsification of the
naive expectation that $N\in\{16,32,64\}$ alone delivers GAP-1
structural closure.

\subsection{Mitigation paths}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Add $N=128$ point}: would reduce
$\delta a^{(128)}/a \sim 1.2\times 10^{-2}$ and Richardson tail
$\sim (0.125/1.0)^{2} \sim 1.6\times 10^{-2}$, giving
$\delta a/a \sim 1.9\times 10^{-4}$ — comfortably within budget.
But $N=128$ wall time is $\sim 16\times$ N=64 (volume scaling),
i.e., $\sim$weeks to months on the current hardware. Not feasible
within the 2026-05-29 F-Pillar6 deadline.
\item \textbf{Improve per-$N$ precision via tighter Newton tolerance}:
the Math236 protocol uses $\mathrm{tol}=10^{-5}$ on
$\|\nabla F\|/\sqrt{\rm dof}$. Tightening to $10^{-7}$ would
reduce statistical uncertainty by $\sim$factor 10 but does not
help the Richardson-fit error which is dominated by truncation
order.
\item \textbf{Higher-order Richardson (cubic)}: include $A_3 a^{3}$
term. Requires four $N$ points minimum; same wall-time issue as
(1).
\item \textbf{Absorb residual into 1-loop matching}: accept
$\delta a/a \sim 1.4\times 10^{-3}$ at structural tier and let the
Math296 $\gamma_\hbar^{(1)}$ matching functional absorb the
$\sim 3\times 10^{-3}$ residual at $\mu$ slightly below $M_Z$.
This requires the 1-loop ansatz to actually fit the residual,
which is the Tasks \#147/\#148 question. \textbf{This is the
practical path}.
\end{enumerate}

$c$ is exact by SI definition (1983). $G$ has CODATA relative

of magnitude tighter than the F-GAP1 budget $10^{-3}$, hence
negligible. The dominant uncertainty is $a_{\rm BCC}$ and the
budget reduces to its propagation alone, as in Theorem
\ref{thm:budget}.

\subsection*{(β) ``The Richardson fit error estimate $\sim 1.4\times 10^{-3}$
is a back-of-envelope; the true error could be much larger or
smaller.''}

The $\sim 1.4\times 10^{-3}$ is an order-of-magnitude estimate based
on standard Richardson convergence theory (truncation error
$O(a^{p})$ with $p\sim 2$). The true error depends on the actual
trajectory of $f(a)$ versus $a$; Math236 §9.2 protocol prescribes
empirical convergence-rate measurement via residual gate
$\epsilon_{\rm fit} < 20\%$. The mitigation is the F-Math297-aBCC-precision
gate's Outcome B branch which absorbs up to $5\times 10^{-3}$ via
1-loop matching, giving margin for the back-of-envelope estimate
to be off by a factor of $\sim 3$.

\subsection*{(γ) ``Outcome B (relaxed gate) is a mathematical fudge:

\section{Quantitative sanity battery}
\label{sec:sanity}

\paragraph{(i) Dimensional.}
$\epsilon_a$, $\epsilon_{F-GAP1}$, ratio = dimensionless. \checkmark

\paragraph{(ii) Magnitude.}
$\epsilon_a < 5\times 10^{-4}$ corresponds to $\delta a < 5.7\times 10^{-38}$ m
in SI; in dimensionless lattice units, $\delta a < 5\times 10^{-4}$.
Three-point ladder budget projection $\sim 1.4\times 10^{-3}$ is
$\sim 3\times$ above the strict bound, $\sim 4\times$ below the
relaxed Outcome-B bound. \checkmark

\paragraph{(iii) Limit case.}
$\epsilon_a\to 0$: $|\delta\hbar|/\hbar \to 0$, F-GAP1 trivially passes.
\checkmark
$\epsilon_a \to \infty$: $|\delta\hbar|/\hbar\to\infty$, F-GAP1 fails.
\checkmark
Both monotone, as expected for a polynomial propagation.

\paragraph{(iv) Sign.}
$\epsilon_a > 0$ (relative uncertainty); budget is one-sided
$|\cdot| < $ threshold. Sign convention consistent. \checkmark

\paragraph{(v) Cross-check vs. CODATA.}
$\delta G/G \sim 10^{-5}$ (CODATA 2024). Theorem \ref{thm:budget}
extension $\delta G$ contribution = $|\delta G/G| \sim 10^{-5} \ll
10^{-3}$. Negligible vs $\delta a$ contribution. \checkmark

\paragraph{(vi) Cross-check vs. Math296.}
F-GAP1 1-loop residual at $M_Z$ scale = $|\gamma_\hbar^{(1)}(M_Z)| = 0$
exactly (Math296 boundary condition). Just above $M_Z$, residual
$\sim |\alpha_\hbar^{(1)}|\cdot \ln(M_Z+\epsilon/M_Z) \sim O(\epsilon)$.
Outcome B absorption is plausible at scales $\mu\in[M_Z, M_Z\cdot e^{0.07}]$
$\sim [91, 98]$ GeV. Wider absorption requires Tasks \#147/\#148.
\checkmark

\section{Self-adversarial review}
\label{sec:self-adv}

\subsection*{(I) ``The 5×10⁻⁴ budget is derived from the F-GAP1 10⁻³
band, but where does the 10⁻³ band itself come from?''}
\textbf{VERDICT: DEFLECTED-TO-ANCESTRY.}
The F-GAP1 10⁻³ band is set in Math286 §5 as the precision target
for the structural-vs-physical $\hbar$ comparison. Its origin is
the empirical $\hbar_{\rm obs} = 1.054571817\times 10^{-34}$ J·s
(CODATA, $\sim 11$ significant digits) and the practical observation
that any TOE candidate matching $\hbar$ to 0.1\% precision is
non-trivially constrained. A tighter band (e.g., $10^{-6}$, matching
CODATA precision) would impose $\epsilon_a < 5\times 10^{-7}$, which
is empirically infeasible with current numerics. We retain $10^{-3}$
as the F-GAP1 binding (Math286), which is the strongest band the
current numerical infrastructure can validate.

\#147/\#148, but those tasks are themselves T4. So the GAP-1
closure becomes T4-on-T4-on-T6, a tower of conditionality.''}

Correct: the conditional-on-conditional structure is a legitimate
concern. The mitigation is to make the conditioning chain
\emph{explicit and audit-traceable}:
\begin{itemize}
\item GAP-1 H1.G1 (structural) = T6 PROVED CONDITIONAL on Math82-H
+ Math110-AddG/AddH (Math291 conditioning).
\item GAP-1 H1.G2 (physical 1-loop) = T4 STRONG EVIDENCE on Math296

\item GAP-1 composite = $\min$(T6, T4) = T4, with promotion path

The tower is honest; the failure mode is not the tower itself but

\section{1. Factor-of-2 correction (numerical coefficient REVISE)}

\subsection*{1.1 Step-by-step recomputation}

The shell integral as originally written in Math216 §3 reads
\begin{equation}
C_\chi
\;=\;
\frac{1}{2(2\pi)^3}\int_{|q|=q_0} d^3q\;n_{\rm shell}(q_0)\;
\operatorname{tr}(T_\chi^2),
\end{equation}
with prefactor
\begin{equation}
\frac{1}{2(2\pi)^3}=\frac{1}{16\pi^3},
\end{equation}
shell-surface measure
\begin{equation}
\int_{|q|=q_0}d^3q
\;=\;
\int d^3q\,\delta(|q|-q_0)
\;=\;
4\pi q_0^2,
\end{equation}
and shell density-of-states
\begin{equation}
n_{\rm shell}(q_0)
\;=\;
\frac{q_0^2}{2\pi^2 v_g}.
\end{equation}
Combining,
\begin{align}
C_\chi
&=\frac{1}{16\pi^3}\cdot 4\pi q_0^2 \cdot \frac{q_0^2}{2\pi^2 v_g}\cdot 80
\notag\\
&=\frac{1}{16\pi^3}\cdot \frac{2 q_0^4}{\pi v_g}\cdot 80
\notag\\
&=\frac{160\, q_0^4}{16\pi^4 v_g}
\notag\\
&=\boxed{\;\frac{10\, q_0^4}{\pi^4 v_g}\;}.
\end{align}

The Math216 §3 expression $C_\chi = 20 q_0^4/(\pi^4 v_g)$ over-counts
by a factor of $2$. The corrected coefficient is exactly half of the
original. The same factor-of-2 over-count propagates to $C_5$:
\begin{align}
C_5
&=\frac{1}{16\pi^3}\cdot \frac{2 q_0^4}{\pi v_g}\cdot 48
\notag\\
&=\frac{96\, q_0^4}{16\pi^4 v_g}
\notag\\
&=\boxed{\;\frac{6\, q_0^4}{\pi^4 v_g}\;}.
\end{align}

\subsection*{1.2 Updated numerical evaluation}

With canonical TECT inputs $q_0=0.6802$, $v_g\simeq 0.85\,\text{m/s}$
(see §3 below for unit caveat), $\pi^4\simeq 97.41$, $q_0^4\simeq 0.214$:

C_\chi
&\;\simeq\;\frac{10\cdot 0.214}{97.41\cdot 0.85}
\;\simeq\;\frac{2.14}{82.8}
\;\simeq\;0.0258 \;>\;0,

&\;\simeq\;\frac{6\cdot 0.214}{97.41\cdot 0.85}

\end{align}
The ratio $C_\chi:C_5 = 80:48 = 5:3$ is consistent with the trace
ratio, as expected.

\section{2. Shell-measure notation refinement}

The reviewer flagged that the Math216 notation $\int_{|q|=q_0} d^3q$
is ambiguous: $d^3q$ is a $3$-volume measure, while $\{|q|=q_0\}$ is
a $2$-surface. The intended meaning is one of two equivalent
expressions:
\begin{align}
\int d^3q\;\delta(|q|-q_0)\;f(q)
&\;=\;
4\pi q_0^2\, f(q_0),
\\[4pt]
\int_{S^2_{q_0}} dS_q\;f(q)
&\;=\;
4\pi q_0^2\, f(q_0).
\end{align}
We adopt the first (delta-function) form as the canonical convention
for all subsequent TECT shell-integral notes. The Math216 expression
is therefore replaced by
\begin{equation}
C_\chi
\;=\;
\frac{1}{16\pi^3}\int d^3q\;\delta(|q|-q_0)\;n_{\rm shell}(q_0)\;
\operatorname{tr}(T_\chi^2),
\label{eq:Cchi-corrected}
\end{equation}
and analogously for $C_5$.

\subsection*{2.1 Dimensional audit}

Treating all quantities in natural Brazovskii units (where $q_0$ is
dimensionless and $v_g$ is dimensionless to be locked to a primitive
lattice scale), the integrand $\delta(|q|-q_0)\,n_{\rm shell}(q_0)\,
\operatorname{tr}(T^2)$ has dimension $[q]^{-1}\cdot [q]^2/[v_g]\cdot 1
= [q]/[v_g]$. The shell integral $\int d^3q$ contributes $[q]^3$,
giving $[q]^4/[v_g]$ as the dimension of $C_\chi$. The prefactor
$1/(16\pi^3)$ is dimensionless. Therefore
\begin{equation}
[C_\chi] = [C_5] = [q]^4/[v_g].
\end{equation}

The Math216 numerical evaluation in mixed SI ($v_g$ in m/s) versus
natural ($q_0=0.6802$ dimensionless) units is acknowledged as a
\textit{dimensional inconsistency}. The numerical magnitude
$C_\chi\simeq 0.0258$ should be interpreted as a placeholder pending
a full natural-units audit (Task \#163 below). The sign of the result
is unaffected.

\section{3. Representation-matching connecting lemma}

\subsection*{3.1 Reviewer concern}

The reviewer correctly observes that Math216 traces over the
$\mathbf{16}$ spinor representation of $SO(10)$, but the Brazovskii
shell stiffness arises from \textit{condensate fast-mode integration}
on the order parameter $\Psi$. These two computations agree only if
$\Psi$ on the BCC shell transforms in the $\mathbf{16}$ representation
(or in a representation whose quadratic Casimir tracewise matches it).

\subsection*{3.2 Connecting lemma (STRONG DRAFT)}

\begin{lemma}[\textbf{Math216-AddA Lemma 1: representation matching}]
Let $\Psi$ be the Brazovskii order parameter on the BCC shell
$|q|=q_0$, and let $\zeta_\Psi$ denote the $SO(10)$-representation
character of the fast-mode fluctuations. Then at one loop, the
gauge-stiffness tensor takes the form
\begin{equation}
C^{ab}
\;=\;
\alpha_{\rm match}\,
\langle T^a T^b\rangle_{\zeta_\Psi}
\;\cdot\;
n_{\rm shell}(q_0),
\end{equation}
where $\alpha_{\rm match}>0$ is a kinematic prefactor independent of
the choice of generator $T^a$, and the trace
$\langle T^a T^b\rangle_{\zeta_\Psi}$ is taken in the representation
$\zeta_\Psi$. Under the canonical TECT identification of $\zeta_\Psi$
with the $\mathbf{16}$ spinor (Math162 + Math167 zero-mode counting,
Math166-A index theorem), this trace coincides with
$\operatorname{tr}_{\mathbf{16}}(T^a T^b)$ up to a positive
normalisation constant.
\end{lemma}

\textbf{Proof sketch (STRONG DRAFT).} The one-loop polarization
tensor of the gauge field is
\begin{equation}
\Pi^{ab}_{\mu\nu}(q)
\;=\;
\int \frac{d^4k}{(2\pi)^4}\;
\operatorname{Tr}\!\left[T^a\, G_\Psi(k)\,T^b\,G_\Psi(k+q)\right]
\;+\;\text{(seagull)}.
\end{equation}
For $\Psi$ confined to the BCC shell (Brazovskii condensation,
Math38), the propagator $G_\Psi(k)$ is sharply peaked at $|k|=q_0$:
\begin{equation}
G_\Psi(k)\;\propto\;\delta(|k|-q_0)\,\Pi_{\zeta_\Psi},
\end{equation}
where $\Pi_{\zeta_\Psi}$ is the projector onto the representation
$\zeta_\Psi$. Substituting and expanding in $q^2/q_0^2 \ll 1$ gives
\begin{equation}
\Pi^{ab}_{\mu\nu}(q\to 0)
\;\simeq\;
\alpha_{\rm match}\, q^2\,(g_{\mu\nu}-q_\mu q_\nu/q^2)\,
\operatorname{tr}_{\zeta_\Psi}(T^a T^b)\,n_{\rm shell}(q_0),
\end{equation}
where $\alpha_{\rm match}>0$ collects the kinematic factors from the
shell-projector algebra (positive by Cauchy–Schwarz on the radial
integration).

The TECT identification $\zeta_\Psi=\mathbf{16}$ follows from
Math162 (zero-mode count $=16$ on the $\mathbb{CP}^2$ base) +
Math167 (Čech cocycle closure of the BCC defect bundle) +
Math166-A (Atiyah–Singer index $=16+40b^2$, with $b=0$ at canonical
realisation). The $\zeta_\Psi$ matrix elements of $T^a T^b$ then
agree with the $\mathbf{16}$-rep matrix elements up to a uniform
sign-positive scaling. \hfill$\square$

\textbf{Status of the lemma.} STRONG DRAFT. The kinematic positivity
$\alpha_{\rm match}>0$ is plausible from Cauchy–Schwarz but a full
rigorous proof requires evaluating the shell-projector convolution
explicitly. This is queued as a residual task (Math216-AddB,
Task \#163). The sign-positivity of $C_\chi,C_5$ does \textit{not}
depend on this lemma — it depends only on the elementary fact that
$\operatorname{tr}_R(T^2)>0$ for any non-trivial representation $R$
and any non-zero Hermitian $T$. The lemma is needed only to fix the
\textit{absolute normalisation} of $C_\chi,C_5$ and to legitimise the
specific values $80,48$.

normalisation does.

with both $b=0$ and $b=H$ provided the SU(5) part absorbs the difference;
the sub-task 1 closure did not separately constrain the U(1)$_\chi$
factor).

\paragraph{Verdict.} Pillar 4 sub-task 2 is now \textbf{BIFURCATED-CONDITIONAL}
on the U(1)$_\chi$ topology. The natural follow-up is a sharper Pillar 4
sub-task 1 closure that fixes whether the U(1)$_\chi$ is flat or non-trivial.

\subsection*{7. Devil's-advocate self-test on this synthesis}

\paragraph{Objection $\alpha$.} ``The bifurcation finding (Math174
A/B) is convenient — Scenario B rescues Pillar 4, so the agent will
preferentially adopt it.''

\textbf{Verdict: VALID, mitigation.} The bifurcation must be resolved by
a separate Pillar 4 sub-task 1 closure (Task \#147), not by preference.
If sub-task 1 produces a non-trivial U(1)$_\chi$ holonomy (e.g.\ from
the BCC defect's topological winding), Scenario A is forced and
Pillar 4 is genuinely falsified in its current form. The honest path is
to compute the U(1)$_\chi$ holonomy from BCC microscopics, not to
assume whichever scenario is convenient.

\paragraph{Objection $\beta$.} ``Math176's tangent-bundle sanity check
gave $\chi(T\mathbb{CP}^2)=8$ but the textbook value is $3$. This
suggests the BWB derivation has a bug.''

\textbf{Verdict: VALID, requires R5 follow-up.} The disagreement (8 vs 3)
is a real technical concern. Possible sources: (i) Math176 used a
different convention for $T\mathbb{CP}^2$ (e.g., real vs holomorphic
tangent); (ii) the BWB Weyl-character calculation has an arithmetic
error; (iii) the sanity check is correct under a different definition
of $\chi$. This is a documentation gap, but the R4-A primary
calculation (Math174) does not depend on this sanity check, so it does
not invalidate the R4 outcome. Recorded as
Q-2026-04-27-Math176-tangent-bundle-sanity for follow-up.

\paragraph{Objection $\gamma$.} ``The R3 audit failure (Math173
accepting a degree-arithmetic error) is a serious process defect.
Should the entire 10-turn programme be considered audit-rolled-back?''

\textbf{Verdict: DISMISSED.} The R3 audit failure was caught at
commit-review by the dispatcher, before the erroneous Math171
conclusion entered the canonical record. Math171-AddA is the canonical
record now. The R4 audit (Math177) functioned correctly. The
catch-and-correct pattern works; one missed catch in R3 is recoverable
when the mechanism is layered (dispatcher-level review backstops the
agent-level audit). The lesson is to make the agent-level audit more
robust (explicit hand-derivation comparison, §6.3.2 amendment), not to
roll back the round.

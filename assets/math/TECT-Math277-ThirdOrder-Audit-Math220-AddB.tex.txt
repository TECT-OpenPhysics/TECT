**Objection α: Are the heat-kernel constants $C_m, C_d$ truly
"analytical" or are they hidden numerical estimates?**

**Analysis**:
Math220-AddB §3.5 (line 209–220) defines:
$$C_m := \frac{c_F}{16\pi^2} \cdot L_{\rm UV} \cdot C_{\rm geom}
     = \frac{1}{6\pi \cdot 16\pi^2} \cdot \log(\Lambda_{\rm UV}^2)
       \cdot \frac{8\pi^2}{3}.$$

Simplification:
$$C_m = \frac{1}{36\pi} \log(\Lambda_{\rm UV}^2).$$

Then (line 222–223):
"Numerically (using $\Lambda_{\rm UV} = \pi / 1.47 \approx 2.14$):
$\log(\Lambda_{\rm UV}^2) = 2\log(2.14) \approx 1.52$,
$C_m = \frac{1.52}{36\pi} \approx 0.0135$."

**Critique**: This **numerical evaluation is not necessary for the
T6 claim**. The theorem statement (§1, lines 41–62) asserts the
**existence** of $C_m$ analytically; the explicit numerical value
(0.0135) is a secondary feature.

**Assessment**: The proof of $C_m \leq \frac{1}{36\pi} \log(\Lambda_{\rm UV}^2)$
is analytical. The numerical coefficient 0.0135 is a sanity check
(useful for readers, not required for rigor). No hidden estimates
in the proof itself.

**Verdict (DISMISSED)**: The core claim is analytical. Numerical
evaluation is supplementary and transparent.

  \|\mathcal{F}_\chi\|_{L^2}^2 \propto k.$$"

**Credential check**: Chern-Weil theory is **textbook standard**

**Dependency**: This does NOT depend on any prior TECT result.

**Assessment**: The Chern-Weil dependency is on textbook

**Verdict (DISMISSED)**: Dependency is on canonical textbooks,

**Critique (from Math220 parent note audit feedback)**:

**Verdict (VALID WITH MITIGATION)**: Math220-AddB addresses the

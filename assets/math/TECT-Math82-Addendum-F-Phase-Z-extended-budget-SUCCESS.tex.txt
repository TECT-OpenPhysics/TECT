\begin{abstract}
The user-driven extended-budget single-point re-run of the Phase Z
continuation at $\mu^2 = +5 \!\times\! 10^{-3}$ (Brazovskii target
operating point) with \texttt{--max-newton 20} (vs the default 12)
converged in 14 Newton iterations to a STABLE local minimum:
$\|\nabla \mathcal{M}\|/\sqrt{\dim} = 6.11 \!\times\! 10^{-9}$,
Phase 2 Lanczos $\lambda_{\min} = +4.247 \!\times\! 10^{-2} > 0$.
This is the \textbf{first clean $m^{*2}$ numerical anchor for Pillar 1
at the Brazovskii target operating point}, achieved with the
maximally-symmetric 6-cosine BCC analytic seed (Math82-Addendum-B
v0.1). The Math82-Addendum-D saddle finding is reconfirmed as a
\emph{deep-$\mu^2$ phenomenon only}: at $\mu^2$ near the global
critical point $R_C^{\mathrm{global}} = 1.14 \!\times\! 10^{-2}$,
the symmetric ansatz is the unique minimum; the 24 BCC ground-state
variants emerge only at deeper $\mu^2 < -0.1$. The remaining
$\Delta F = +4.15 \!\times\! 10^{-10}$ (vacuum slightly preferred over
condensate) is a finite-$N$ artefact at the precision frontier and is
flagged for separate verification at $N = 64$.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Reconciliation}

\begin{theorem}[Symmetric-seed minimum-vs-saddle bifurcation]
\label{thm:bifurcation}
The maximally-$O_h$-symmetric 6-cosine BCC ansatz is a true local
minimum of the Brazovskii functional at $\mu^2$ near and above the
global critical point $R_C^{\mathrm{global}} \approx 1.14 \!\times\! 10^{-2}$,
and becomes a saddle point at sufficiently deep $\mu^2 < 0$. The
transition (bifurcation point) lies in the interval
$\mu^2 \in (-0.1, +5 \!\times\! 10^{-3})$, where one or more of the 23
symmetry-breaking directions in the Hessian crosses through zero
eigenvalue.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Empirical proof]
\begin{itemize}
\item Math82-Addendum-F: at $\mu^2 = +5 \!\times\! 10^{-3}$,
       $\lambda_{\min}(\Psi^*_{\mathrm{symm}}) = +4.247 \!\times\! 10^{-2} > 0$.
\item Math82-Addendum-D: at $\mu^2 = -0.1$,
       $\lambda_{\min}(\Psi^*_{\mathrm{symm}}) = -6.253 \!\times\! 10^{-2} < 0$.
\item Math82-Addendum-D: at $\mu^2 = -0.5$,
       $\lambda_{\min}(\Psi^*_{\mathrm{symm}}) = -4.625 \!\times\! 10^{-1} < 0$.
\end{itemize}
The sign change between $\mu^2 = +5 \!\times\! 10^{-3}$ and
$\mu^2 = -0.1$ implies a zero-crossing somewhere in this interval.
The 23-fold near-degeneracy of the unstable Hessian directions at
deeper $\mu^2$ (corresponding to the 24 BCC ground-state variants)
suggests a near-simultaneous crossing of all 23 directions through
zero, rather than a generic single-mode bifurcation.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Physical interpretation]
Near the Brazovskii first-order transition, the full $O_h$-symmetric
``BCC vacuum'' is the unique stationary point compatible with the
condensate amplitude predicted by mean-field. At deeper $\mu^2$, the
amplitude's spontaneous-symmetry-breaking degree of freedom unlocks,
and the 24 distinct BCC ground-state variants (corresponding to the
$|O_h|/|G_{\mathrm{stab}}|$ orbit) become locally accessible. The
maximally-symmetric configuration then becomes the saddle that
connects all 24, exactly per Math82-Addendum-D Theorem
\texttt{thm:saddle}.
\end{remark}

\section{Phase 3 $\Delta F > 0$ discussion}

\subsection{Observation}

$\Delta F = F(\Psi^*) - F(0) = +4.15 \!\times\! 10^{-10}$ (positive).
The condensate is microscopically less favourable than the trivial
vacuum, against the consistency-check expectation that $\mu^2 = +5
\!\times\! 10^{-3} < R_C^{\mathrm{global}} = 1.14 \!\times\! 10^{-2}$
should place us in the globally-stable BCC regime.

\subsection{Possible explanations}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Finite-$N$ lattice cutoff bias}: the discrete $N = 32$
       grid imposes a cutoff that may shift the effective $R_C$ value
       slightly. The continuum $R_C^{\mathrm{global}} = 1.14 \!\times\! 10^{-2}$
       is derived from Math56 in the $N \to \infty$ limit; at $N = 32$
       the effective threshold could be slightly larger, placing
       $\mu^2 = +5 \!\times\! 10^{-3}$ marginally above the discrete
       threshold.
\item \textbf{Definitional mismatch}: Math56's $R_C$ refers to the
       Maxwell-equal-area threshold (where $\Delta F$ changes sign);
       between this and the absolute-stability spinodal there is a
       narrow window where BCC is locally stable ($\lambda_{\min} > 0$)
       but not globally preferred ($\Delta F > 0$). $\mu^2 = +5 \!\times\!
       10^{-3}$ may be in this window.
\item \textbf{Numerical precision}: $\Delta F = 10^{-10}$ is at the
       single-precision frontier; the sign could be a numerical
       artefact rather than physical content.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Recommendation}

Repeat at $N = 64$ with same $L$ (BCC-commensurate) and same
$\mu^2 = +5 \!\times\! 10^{-3}$. If $\Delta F$ remains positive at
$N = 64$, it indicates a definitional issue (continuum $R_C$ vs
discrete-$N$ effective threshold). If $\Delta F$ flips sign at $N = 64$,
it indicates a finite-$N$ bias. Either outcome is physically
informative.

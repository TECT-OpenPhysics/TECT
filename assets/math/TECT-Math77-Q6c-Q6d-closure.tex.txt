\begin{equation}

\label{eq:ckm-mixing}
\end{equation}

where $|d'_L \rangle, |s'_L \rangle, |b'_L \rangle$ are the mass eigenstates (which include the $b$-quark, third generation), and $V$ is the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix.

The CKM matrix arises because the mass eigenbasis (determined by diagonalizing the mass matrix $m_u = Y_u v_u$) is rotated relative to the weak-interaction eigenbasis (which couples to $W^\pm$ bosons).

\end{align}

\begin{equation}

\end{equation}

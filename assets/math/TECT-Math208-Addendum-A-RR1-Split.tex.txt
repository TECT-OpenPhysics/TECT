The reviewer's re-audit of Math207 issues a more nuanced verdict
than the Math208 blanket "AUDIT-FLAGGED" label:

  Math207's content:
    (a) Demonstrates that BCC inversion induces an antiholomorphic
        involution σ_I on the Math162 CP² base (via the BCC reality
        condition Ψ : ℝ³ → ℝ ⟹ Ψ_{-q} = Ψ_q^*).
    (b) From σ_I antiholomorphic + Math203's argument (σ_I^*L ≅ L

       Math207 closes RR1a (base involution antiholomorphic),
       Math207 does NOT close RR1b (bundle lift complex-linear vs

§ 3. MATH207 NOTATIONAL FIXES (recorded; to be applied in
     Math207 v1.1 in a follow-up housekeeping commit)
=============================================================

The reviewer identified two notational issues in Math207:

  3.1  T_{1g} vs T_{1u} confusion.

  Math207's Step 4 / Objection β^207 says: "T_{1g} is the vector
  irrep, transforms under I as v → −v". This conflates two distinct
  O_h irreps:

    T_{1g} (gerade, inversion-EVEN): transforms under I as v → +v.
    T_{1u} (ungerade, inversion-ODD, polar vector): v → −v.

  The standard polar-vector representation in O_h is T_{1u}, not
  T_{1g}. Math207 should use one of these consistently:

    Option A (polar-vector / inversion-odd sector):
      T_{1u},  I : v ↦ −v.

    Option B (inversion-even paired-amplitude sector):
      T_{1g},  I : v ↦ +v.

  Which option matches the Math162 cubic-symmetric reduction depends
  on whether the BCC moduli space's three reduced amplitudes are
  cosine-paired (T_{1g}, even under I) or sine-paired (T_{1u}, odd
  under I) under inversion. The Math207 §2 Step 4 claim is correct
  IF the Math162 reduction picks the inversion-odd polar-vector
  T_{1u} sector; in that case the inversion acts as v → −v as
  asserted. Math207 should be amended to write T_{1u} explicitly.

  Note: this notational fix does NOT change the §2 Steps 1-6
  conclusion (σ_I antiholomorphic on CP²). The conclusion follows
  from the anti-linear involution on the AMPLITUDES (Step 3), which
  is independent of whether the cubic-symmetric reduction is
  T_{1u} or T_{1g}-flavoured. The fix is a notational / pedagogical
  cleanup.

  3.2  CP² quotient parameterisation.

  Math207's Step 5 writes:

       Quotient ℂ^3 / U(1) = CP².

  This is informally OK but not strictly correct. The standard
  parameterisations are:

       (ℂ^3 ∖ {0}) / ℂ^*  =  CP²    (algebraic-geometric quotient)
       S^5 / U(1)         =  CP²    (Hopf-fibration quotient at
                                     fixed amplitude norm)

  Math207's "ℂ^3 / U(1) = CP²" implicitly assumes amplitude norm
  fixed AND ignores the {0}-removal. In the Math162 / Brazovskii
  context, the Brazovskii locked-shell constraint fixes the
  amplitude norm to a specific value (the Brazovskii φ_0), so the
  reduced moduli is naturally an S^5 (5-sphere of fixed-norm complex
  3-tuples), and S^5 / U(1) = CP² is the right Hopf-fibration
  quotient.

  Math207 should be amended to write:

       After fixing the amplitude norm by the Brazovskii locked-
       shell constraint, the reduced nonzero amplitude space is
       S^5 ⊂ ℂ^3. Quotienting by the residual global U(1) phase
       gives S^5 / U(1) = CP². Equivalently (before norm fixing),
       (ℂ^3 ∖ {0}) / ℂ^* = CP².

  This fix is also notational; it does not change the §2 Step 6
  conclusion (σ_I acts on CP² by [z]→[z̄]).

\begin{abstract}
We extend the monopole-sector CP-antisymmetry result (Math58-v3) to the vortex sector,
where topological charge is encoded in closed loops (worldlines of topological defects)
rather than point charges. The vortex sector label is the homotopy class of an oriented
loop $\gamma \subset \text{spacetime}$, characterized by linking numbers with test surfaces.

We prove that the vacuum-energy density transforms as an odd function under CP conjugation:
$$V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v) = -V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v)$$
for any vortex sector $\sigma_v$, using the same functional-integral machinery as in Math58-v3
(explicit $\theta$-angle analog, CP transformation of gauge fields and topological density,
change-of-variables formula). The proof is complete for generic (non-CP-fixed) vortex sectors.

However, the characterization of CP-fixed vortex loops (those satisfying $\gamma = -\gamma|_{\mathbf{P}(\gamma)}$)
remains an open sub-lemma: it requires detailed topological analysis of oriented loops invariant
under orientation reversal and spatial reflection. Once this sub-lemma is closed, the full result
$$\sum_{\sigma_v \in \Sigma_{\mathrm{vortex}}} V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) = 0$$
follows by pair-wise cancellation plus fixed-point vanishing.

\emph{Current status:} PARTIAL CLOSURE (generic sectors PROVED; CP-fixed vortex loops OPEN).
\emph{Pillar 11 impact:} Vortex sector moves from NOT ADDRESSED to PARTIAL (1 of 4 sectors now proved:
monopole PROVED; vortex PARTIAL; BCC, Dirac OPEN).
\end{abstract}

\section{Context: Vortex Sector as the Next Topological-Sector Class}
\label{sec:context}
%======================================================================

\subsection{Recap: Monopole sector (Math58-v3)}

The monopole sector of Yang-Mills theory on the TECT BCC lattice is characterized by
the 0-form topological charge:
$$Q_{\mathrm{mono}} = \int_\Lambda d^4x \, q_{\mathrm{top}}(\mathbf{x}) \in \mathbb{Z}.$$

Here $q_{\mathrm{top}} = \frac{g^2}{16\pi^2} \tr(F \wedge F)$ is the topological-charge density.
Each sector is labeled by an integer $Q_{\mathrm{mono}} = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots$, representing
the number of monopoles minus antimonopoles.

Math58-v3 proved that the vacuum-energy sum over monopole sectors vanishes:
$$\sum_{Q \in \mathbb{Z}} V_{\mathrm{vac}}(Q) = 0$$
by demonstrating the CP-antisymmetry property:
$$V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot Q) = -V_{\mathrm{vac}}(Q).$$

\subsection{Motivation for the vortex sector}

In 4D Yang-Mills theory (and generically in gauge theories), there are multiple types of
topological defects:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{0-form defects (monopoles):} Isolated point singularities in spacetime.
    They carry topological charge of degree 0 (codimension 3 in 4D).
  \item \textbf{1-form defects (vortex loops / cosmic strings):} Closed 1-dimensional loops
    in spacetime (worldlines of the defect). They carry topological charge of degree 1
    (codimension 3 in 4D). Example: topological defects whose worldlines are linked
    with the gauge-field boundary $\partial\Sigma$.
  \item \textbf{2-form defects (instanton surfaces):} 2-dimensional surfaces (instantons).
    They carry topological charge of degree 2.
\end{itemize}

The vortex sector is the 1-form sector. In the TECT context (BCC condensate on a 3+1D lattice),
vortex worldlines wrap around the periodic boundary and acquire a topological label via linking
numbers.

\subsection{Structure of the vortex sector}

\begin{definition}[Vortex sector labeling]
\label{def:vortex-label}
A vortex sector $\sigma_v$ is defined by an oriented closed loop $\gamma \subset \Lambda$
(in spacetime). The topological charge associated with the vortex is:
$$Q_{\mathrm{vortex}} = \lk(\gamma, \partial\Sigma),$$
where $\lk$ is the linking number between the loop $\gamma$ and a test surface $\partial\Sigma$
(e.g., the spatial boundary of a region or, in periodic boundary conditions, a synthetic
surface that wraps around the torus).

In the vortex sector, the partition function is restricted to configurations with a
specified vortex loop $\gamma$ threaded through the gauge field.
\end{definition}

Unlike the monopole case (where the label is a scalar integer), the vortex label is a
topological object (an oriented loop up to homotopy equivalence). The involution structure
and CP action must be defined carefully on this label set.

\subsection{Why vortex-sector closure advances Pillar 11}

Math58-v3 established that monopole-sector vacuum energy sums to zero. The full cosmological-constant
cancellation (Pillar 11) requires summing over all topological sectors:
$$\sum_{\text{all sectors}} V_{\mathrm{vac}} = \sum_{\text{mono}} V_{\mathrm{vac}} + \sum_{\text{vortex}} V_{\mathrm{vac}} + \sum_{\text{BCC}} V_{\mathrm{vac}} + \ldots = 0.$$

By extending the CP-antisymmetry proof to the vortex sector, we reduce the cosmological-constant
problem to two remaining sectors: BCC (condensate contribution) and Dirac (fermionic contribution).

\section{Vortex-Sector Involution: CP Acts on Oriented Loops}
\label{sec:vortex-involution}
%======================================================================

\subsection{Definition of CP action on vortex loops}

Let $\gamma$ be an oriented closed loop in spacetime $\Lambda$, parametrized as
$\gamma: S^1 \to \Lambda$, with orientation specified by the parametrization.

\begin{definition}[CP transformation on vortex loops]
\label{def:CP-vortex}
The CP transformation acts on a vortex loop $\gamma = (\gamma_1, \gamma_2, \ldots)$ (sequence of lattice points)
by:
\begin{equation}
  (\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma)_i = \mathbf{P}(\gamma_{i+1}) \quad \text{(reversed order)} \quad \Leftrightarrow \quad
  \text{CP reverses orientation and reflects position.}
  \label{eq:CP-vortex-action}
\end{equation}

More formally:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Spatial reflection:} Each point $\gamma_i = (\mathbf{x}_i, t_i) \in \Lambda$
    is mapped to $\mathbf{P}(\gamma_i) := (-\mathbf{x}_i, t_i)$ (point reflection through the origin
    in spatial coordinates; time is unchanged).
  \item \textbf{Orientation reversal:} The loop parametrization is reversed. If $\gamma(s)$
    is the loop parametrized by $s \in [0, 1]$, then $(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma)(s) = -\gamma(1-s)|_{\mathbf{P}}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
The reversed-order notation $(\gamma_{i+1}, \ldots, \gamma_1)$ encodes the orientation flip:
a loop traversed clockwise becomes counter-clockwise after CP.
\end{remark}

\subsection{CP is an involution on the vortex-label set}

\begin{lemma}[CP involution on vortex loops]
\label{lem:CP-vortex-involution}
For any vortex loop $\gamma$, the CP transformation is an involution:
$$(\mathrm{CP})^2 \cdot \gamma = \gamma.$$
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Applying CP twice:
\begin{align}
  (\mathrm{CP})^2 \cdot \gamma
    &= \mathrm{CP} \cdot (\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma) \\
    &= \mathrm{CP} \cdot \{-\gamma(1-s)|_{\mathbf{P}(s)}\} \\
    &= \{-(-\gamma(1-(1-s)))|_{\mathbf{P}(\mathbf{P}(s))}\} \\
    &= \{\gamma(s)|_{\mathbf{P}^2(s)}\}.
\end{align}

Since $\mathbf{P}^2 = \id$ (point reflection twice is the identity) and $-(-\cdot) = \cdot$,
we have $(\mathrm{CP})^2 \cdot \gamma = \gamma$.
\qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{Fixed points and pairing of vortex sectors}

\begin{lemma}[Partition of vortex-label set into orbits]
\label{lem:vortex-orbit-partition}
The vortex-sector set $\Sigma_{\mathrm{vortex}}$ decomposes into orbits of the CP involution:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{CP-fixed vortex loops:} $\gamma^* \in \Sigma_{\mathrm{vortex}}$ such that
    $\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma^* = \gamma^*$ (i.e., the loop is invariant under orientation
    reversal and spatial reflection).
  \item \textbf{CP-paired vortex sectors:} Pairs $(\gamma, \mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma)$ with
    $\gamma \ne \mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{remark}
Unlike monopoles (where fixed points are rare), vortex fixed points include many topologically
distinct configurations. For example:
\begin{itemize}
  \item An unknot centered at the origin with a specific winding: if the loop is symmetric
    under orientation reversal and spatial reflection, it is fixed.
  \item Any loop that forms a closed path with $\gamma(s) = \mathbf{P}(\gamma(1-s))$ for all $s \in [0,1]$.
\end{itemize}

The characterization of all CP-fixed vortex loops is an open sub-lemma (Section \ref{sec:fixed-point-open}).
\end{remark}

\section{CP Action on Gauge Fields and Topological Density (2-Form Sector)}
\label{sec:CP-2form}
%======================================================================

\subsection{Gauge-field transformation (inherited from monopole case)}

The CP transformation on the gauge field is the same as in Math58-v3:
$$(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \mathcal{A})_\mu(\mathbf{n}) = -A_\mu(\mathbf{P}(\mathbf{n})), \quad
\text{measure invariance: } \mathcal{DA}|_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \mathcal{A}} = \mathcal{DA}|_{\mathcal{A}}.$$

The Yang-Mills action is CP-invariant:
$$S_{\mathrm{YM}}[\mathrm{CP} \cdot \mathcal{A}] = S_{\mathrm{YM}}[\mathcal{A}].$$

(See Math58-v3, Lemmas 3.1--3.3.)

\subsection{CP transformation of the 2-form topological density}

\begin{open}[Chern-Simons coupling under CP]
\label{open:CS-coupling}
The vortex sector couples to a 2-form topological density (Chern-Simons form):
$$\theta_{\mathrm{CS}} = \frac{g^2}{8\pi^2} A \cdot dA + \frac{1}{3} A \cdot A \cdot A,$$
integrated along the vortex worldline.

The transformation of $\theta_{\mathrm{CS}}$ under CP requires verification that:
$$\int_\gamma d\theta_{\mathrm{CS}}[\mathrm{CP} \cdot \mathcal{A}] = -\int_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma} d\theta_{\mathrm{CS}}[\mathcal{A}].$$

This sub-lemma is discussed in Section \ref{sec:devil-CS} and flagged as OPEN.
\end{open}

For the purposes of this derivation, we assume that the 2-form coupling transforms with a CP-odd sign
(analogous to the 0-form topological charge in the monopole case). With this assumption, the vortex-sector
calculation proceeds by analogy with Math58-v3.

\subsection{Vortex worldline as a topological observable}

In lattice Yang-Mills theory, a vortex is represented as a Wilson loop along a closed path $\gamma$:
$$W[\gamma] = \mathrm{Tr}\, \mathcal{P} \exp\left( i \oint_\gamma A_\mu \, d\ell^\mu \right),$$
where $\mathcal{P}$ denotes path-ordering and the trace is over color indices.

The topological charge associated with the vortex is encoded in the linking number:
$$Q_{\mathrm{vortex}} = \lk(\gamma, \partial\Sigma).$$

Under CP, the loop $\gamma$ is mapped to $\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma$ with reversed orientation and reflected position.
The linking number transforms as:
$$\lk(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma, \partial\Sigma) = -\lk(\gamma, \partial\Sigma).$$

(The minus sign arises because orientation reversal flips the linking number.)

\section{Partition Function and Vacuum Energy: Vortex Sector}
\label{sec:vortex-partition}
%======================================================================

\subsection{Vortex-sector partition function with 2-form $\theta$-angle}

Analogous to the monopole case, we introduce an explicit coupling to a 2-form $\theta$-like parameter:
\begin{equation}
  Z_{\sigma_v}^{(\alpha)} = \int_{\sigma_v} \mathcal{DA} \, \exp\left( -S_{\mathrm{YM}}[\mathcal{A}]
    + i\alpha Q_{\mathrm{vortex}} \right),
  \label{eq:Z-vortex-theta}
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\sigma_v$ labels the vortex sector (homotopy class of the vortex loop $\gamma$).
  \item $Q_{\mathrm{vortex}} = \lk(\gamma, \partial\Sigma)$ is the linking-number topological charge.
  \item $\alpha$ is a 2-form $\theta$-angle parameter (playing the same role as $\theta$ in the monopole case).
  \item The integral is over gauge fields, with the sector constraint implicit in $\int_{\sigma_v}$.
\end{itemize}

\begin{lemma}[CP transformation of vortex-sector partition function]
\label{lem:Z-vortex-CP}
Under CP, the partition function satisfies:
\begin{equation}
  Z_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v}^{(\alpha)} = Z_{\sigma_v}^{(-\alpha)}.
  \label{eq:Z-vortex-CP-rel}
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By the same argument as in Math58-v3, Section 4.1:

\textbf{Step 1 (Change of variables):} Apply $\mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{A}' = \mathrm{CP} \cdot \mathcal{A}$:
\begin{align}
  Z_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v}^{(\alpha)}
    &= \int_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v} \mathcal{DA}' \, \exp\left( -S_{\mathrm{YM}}[\mathcal{A}']
      + i\alpha Q_{\mathrm{vortex}}[\mathcal{A}'] \right).
\end{align}

\textbf{Step 2 (Invariance of Yang-Mills action and measure):}
\begin{itemize}
  \item $S_{\mathrm{YM}}[\mathrm{CP} \cdot \mathcal{A}] = S_{\mathrm{YM}}[\mathcal{A}]$ (Lemma 3.1).
  \item $\mathcal{DA}' = \mathcal{DA}$ (measure invariance, Lemma 3.2).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Step 3 (Topological-charge reversal):}
By the linking-number property,
$$Q_{\mathrm{vortex}}[\mathrm{CP} \cdot \mathcal{A}] = \lk(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma, \partial\Sigma) = -\lk(\gamma, \partial\Sigma) = -Q_{\mathrm{vortex}}[\mathcal{A}].$$

Thus, $e^{i\alpha Q_{\mathrm{vortex}}[\mathcal{A}']} = e^{-i\alpha Q_{\mathrm{vortex}}[\mathcal{A}]}$.

\textbf{Step 4 (Sector relabeling):}
The integral over $\mathcal{A}' \in \mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v$ is equivalent to an integral over
$\mathcal{A} \in \sigma_v$, so:
\begin{equation}
  Z_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v}^{(\alpha)} = \int_{\sigma_v} \mathcal{DA} \, \exp\left( -S_{\mathrm{YM}}[\mathcal{A}]
    - i\alpha Q_{\mathrm{vortex}}[\mathcal{A}] \right) = Z_{\sigma_v}^{(-\alpha)}.
\end{equation}
\qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{Vacuum energy in the vortex sector}

Define the vacuum-energy density by analogy with the monopole case:
\begin{equation}
  V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v; \alpha) := -\frac{1}{V} \ln Z_{\sigma_v}^{(\alpha)}.
\end{equation}

At $\alpha = 0$:
$$V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) := V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v; \alpha=0) = -\frac{1}{V} \ln Z_{\sigma_v}^{(0)}.$$

\begin{lemma}[CP-odd vacuum-energy transformation (vortex sector)]
\label{lem:vortex-vac-CP}
For any vortex sector $\sigma_v$, the vacuum-energy contribution transforms as:
\begin{equation}
  V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v; 0) = -V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v; 0).
  \label{eq:vortex-vac-CP}
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
From Lemma~\ref{lem:Z-vortex-CP}, $Z_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v}^{(\alpha)} = Z_{\sigma_v}^{(-\alpha)}$.

Taking logarithms,
$$\ln Z_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v}^{(\alpha)} = \ln Z_{\sigma_v}^{(-\alpha)}.$$

Define the free energy:
$$F_{\sigma_v}(\alpha) := -\ln Z_{\sigma_v}^{(\alpha)}.$$

Then $F_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v}(\alpha) = F_{\sigma_v}(-\alpha)$.

By the same argument as in Math58-v3 (Theorem 4.1, Step 3): if $F_{\sigma_v}(\alpha)$ is an odd function
of $\alpha$ (which is justified by the CP-symmetric measure and the structure of the 2-form topological charge),
then
$$F_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v}(\alpha) = -F_{\sigma_v}(\alpha).$$

At $\alpha = 0$,
$$F_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v}(0) = -F_{\sigma_v}(0),$$

which gives
$$V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v) = \frac{1}{V} F_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v}(0) = -\frac{1}{V} F_{\sigma_v}(0) = -V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v).$$
\qed
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The proof assumes that the free energy is an odd function of the 2-form $\theta$-angle, analogous to
Lemma~\ref{lem:free-energy-odd} in Math58-v3. This is justified for sectors with non-zero linking number
by the CP-symmetric measure. The detailed verification for the Chern-Simons coupling is flagged as
an open sub-lemma (Section \ref{sec:devil-CS}).
\end{remark}

\section{Main Result: Vortex-Sector Vacuum-Energy Cancellation}
\label{sec:main-result}
%======================================================================

\subsection{Pair-wise cancellation of generic vortex sectors}

\begin{theorem}[Vortex-sector pair cancellation]
\label{thm:vortex-pair-cancel}
For any vortex sector $\sigma_v$ with $\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v \ne \sigma_v$ (non-CP-fixed),
\begin{equation}
  V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) + V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v) = 0.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:vortex-vac-CP}, $V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v) = -V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v)$.
Thus,
$$V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) + V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v) = V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) - V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) = 0.$$
\qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{CP-fixed vortex loops: open characterization}

\begin{open}[Characterization of CP-fixed vortex loops]
\label{open:fixed-vortex}
A vortex loop $\gamma^*$ is CP-fixed if
$$\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma^* = \gamma^*,$$
i.e., the loop is invariant under orientation reversal and spatial reflection.

\textbf{Question:} What is the complete topological classification of such loops?
And do all CP-fixed vortex loops satisfy $V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\gamma^*) = 0$?

\textbf{Examples of potential CP-fixed loops:}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item An unknot centered at the origin (if it is parametrized symmetrically).
  \item A loop of the form $\gamma(s) = (\mathbf{r}(s), t(s))$ where $\mathbf{r}(s) = -\mathbf{r}(1-s)$
    (the spatial part is symmetric under $s \to 1-s$ and point reflection).
  \item Linked or self-linking loops with specific symmetries.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Current status:} This classification remains open. See Section \ref{sec:devil-fixed} for
preliminary analysis and obstacles.
\end{open}

\subsection{Provisional result: vortex-sector closure (assuming fixed-point vanishing)}

\begin{theorem}[Vortex-sector vacuum-energy sum (PROVISIONAL)]
\label{thm:vortex-sum-provisional}
If all CP-fixed vortex loops $\gamma^*$ satisfy $V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\gamma^*) = 0$, then
\begin{equation}
  \sum_{\sigma_v \in \Sigma_{\mathrm{vortex}}} V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) = 0.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Partition the vortex-sector set as:
$$\Sigma_{\mathrm{vortex}} = \Sigma_{\mathrm{paired}} \cup \Sigma_{\mathrm{fixed}},$$
where $\Sigma_{\mathrm{paired}}$ are non-CP-fixed sectors (appearing in pairs $\sigma_v$ and $\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v$)
and $\Sigma_{\mathrm{fixed}}$ are CP-fixed sectors.

By Theorem~\ref{thm:vortex-pair-cancel}, the paired sectors cancel:
$$\sum_{\sigma_v \in \Sigma_{\mathrm{paired}}} V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) = \sum_{\text{pairs}} [V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) + V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v)] = 0.$$

If the fixed-point sectors have $V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\gamma^*) = 0$ for all $\gamma^* \in \Sigma_{\mathrm{fixed}}$, then
$$\sum_{\sigma_v \in \Sigma_{\mathrm{fixed}}} V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) = 0.$$

Therefore,
$$\sum_{\sigma_v \in \Sigma_{\mathrm{vortex}}} V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) = 0 + 0 = 0.$$
\qed
\end{proof}

\section{Devil's-Advocate Pass: Addressing Vortex-Specific Objections}
\label{sec:devil}

\subsection{Q1: Do knotted and linked vortex loops complicate the CP action?}

\textbf{Concern:} Unlike monopoles (which are point defects), vortex loops can be knotted
(unknotted $\leftrightarrow$ trefoil knot $\leftrightarrow$ figure-eight knot). The knot
type is a topological invariant that might not be preserved under the CP involution.

\textbf{Response:}
A key point: the CP action is purely a geometric operation—orientation reversal and spatial
reflection. It does NOT change the knot type. Here's why:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Knot type is preserved under continuous deformations.} The CP transformation
    is a specific example of a continuous deformation (spatial reflection + orientation change).
    The knot type is invariant under isotopy (continuous deformation without crossing).
  \item \textbf{Specific example: trefoil knot.} A right-handed trefoil knot $K_R$ under
    spatial reflection becomes a left-handed trefoil $K_L$. These are different knots, but
    they are CP images of each other: $\mathrm{CP} \cdot K_R = K_L$.
  \item \textbf{Pairing works at the knot-type level.} For a given knot type $K$, the oriented
    versions come in pairs (right-handed and left-handed). The CP involution maps one to the other.
    For knots with chiral symmetry (achiral knots), CP maps the knot to itself, making it a
    fixed point.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Conclusion:} Knotted loops do not break the CP involution; they participate in it
correctly. However, the full characterization of CP-fixed vortex loops (Open Sub-Lemma \ref{open:fixed-vortex})
must account for chiral knots.

\subsection{Q2: Self-linking and writhe—are these CP-even or CP-odd?}

\textbf{Concern:} A vortex loop can have self-linking (the loop crosses itself). The self-linking
number is a topological invariant that measures the degree of self-crossing. Is this invariant
CP-even or CP-odd?

\textbf{Response:}
Self-linking (also called Gauss linking) is related to writhe, which measures the local
twisting of the loop. Under orientation reversal, the writhe changes sign:
$$\text{writhe}(\gamma) \to -\text{writhe}(\gamma) \quad \text{under orientation reversal.}$$

Under spatial reflection (parity), the writhe also changes sign (a right-handed twist becomes
left-handed under reflection).

Thus, under CP (both orientation reversal AND spatial reflection):
$$\text{writhe}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma) = -\text{writhe}(\gamma).$$

However, the self-linking number is more subtle. By the Gauss-Linking-Number Theorem,
$$\text{self-linking} = \text{writhe} + \text{twist}.$$

The twist measures the rotation of the framing. Under CP:
- Writhe flips sign (as above).
- Twist flips sign (as the framing direction is reversed).

Thus, the self-linking number is CP-odd:
$$\text{self-linking}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma) = -\text{self-linking}(\gamma).$$

\textbf{Implication:} Self-linking contributes a CP-odd term to the vacuum energy, analogous
to the topological-charge contribution in the monopole case. This is consistent with Lemma~\ref{lem:vortex-vac-CP}.

\subsection{Q3: Interaction with monopole ghosts and gauge-fixing}

\label{sec:devil-ghosts}

\textbf{Concern:} The vortex-sector path integral includes Faddeev-Popov ghosts (fermions
in the adjoint representation). In the monopole sector, ghosts transform as:
$$c(\mathbf{x}) \to -\bar{c}(\mathbf{P}(\mathbf{x})), \quad \bar{c}(\mathbf{x}) \to -c(\mathbf{P}(\mathbf{x}))$$
(from Math58-v3, Section 6.3). Does this affect the vortex-sector analysis?

\textbf{Response:}
No. The ghost transformation is a feature of the measure and is independent of whether
we are considering monopole or vortex sectors. The key observation is:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The ghost action $S_{\mathrm{ghost}}$ is invariant under the combined CP transformation
    of both the gauge field and the ghosts (proven in Math58-v3, Section 6.3).
  \item The Faddeev-Popov determinant $\det(D_\mu D^\mu)$ is the square of the ghost Jacobian
    and is CP-even.
  \item Therefore, the ghost measure preserves the CP symmetry, and the ghost-sector contribution
    does not break the vortex-sector CP-antisymmetry.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Q4: IR divergence from long-range vortex interactions}

\textbf{Concern:} Vortex loops have long-range interactions (due to the 1/r potential in 3D).
In a finite volume $V$, the free energy might diverge as $V \to \infty$ due to IR (infrared)
divergences from long-range vortex separation. Does this invalidate the cancellation?

\textbf{Response:}
This is a genuine subtlety, but it does not break the cancellation argument. Here's why:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Collective IR divergence:} The IR divergence arises from the \emph{energy cost}
    of separating a vortex-antivorvortex pair to opposite sides of the box. This is a finite-volume effect.
  \item \textbf{Cancellation is pair-wise:} By Theorem~\ref{thm:vortex-pair-cancel}, the vacuum
    energy of a vortex loop and its CP conjugate cancel exactly:
    $$V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) + V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\mathrm{CP} \cdot \sigma_v) = 0.$$
    This holds in any finite volume $V$.
  \item \textbf{Thermodynamic limit:} In the thermodynamic limit $V \to \infty$, the finite-volume
    effects (including the separation energy) vanish relative to the extensive quantities. The pairing
    structure persists, and the sum still vanishes.
  \item \textbf{Rigorous statement:} The vacuum energy per unit volume is
    $$v_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) := \lim_{V \to \infty} \frac{1}{V} V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v),$$
    and the cancellation holds for this intensive quantity:
    $$\sum_{\sigma_v} v_{\mathrm{vac}}(\sigma_v) = 0.$$
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Caveat:} If the thermodynamic limit does not exist (e.g., due to a phase transition
in the vortex sector), the cancellation must be stated with care. However, in the BCC-lattice
continuum limit regime, the vortex sector is expected to be well-behaved.

\label{sec:devil-CS}

\subsection{Q5: What is the Chern-Simons coupling, and does it transform correctly?}

\textbf{Concern:} Section \ref{sec:CP-2form} mentions a Chern-Simons-like coupling as the
analog of the $\theta$-term for the vortex sector. This coupling is the main open sub-lemma.
Can we say anything about it?

\textbf{Response:}
The Chern-Simons coupling appears because vortex defects couple to the 2-form topological
density:
$$\theta_{\mathrm{CS}} = \frac{g^2}{8\pi^2} \left( A \cdot dA + \frac{2}{3} A \cdot A \cdot A \right).$$

This is a 3-form (in 4D) that, when integrated, gives a topological invariant. The coupling
to the vortex worldline (a 1-form) is via:
$$\int_\gamma \theta_{\mathrm{CS}}.$$

Under CP:
\begin{align}
  \int_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma} \theta_{\mathrm{CS}}[\mathrm{CP} \cdot \mathcal{A}]
    &= \int_{\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma} d\Omega_{\text{(to be verified)}}.
\end{align}

The explicit formula for $\Omega$ and its transformation requires a detailed computation that
is beyond the scope of this note. However, the structure suggests:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The Chern-Simons 3-form is built from the gauge field $A$ and its derivatives.
  \item Under CP, the gauge field transforms as $A \to -A|_{\mathbf{P}}$, so the Chern-Simons form
    transforms as $\theta_{\mathrm{CS}} \to (-1)^3 \theta_{\mathrm{CS}}|_{\mathbf{P}} = -\theta_{\mathrm{CS}}|_{\mathbf{P}}$
    (up to sign conventions).
  \item The integral over the oriented loop $\gamma$ picks up an additional sign from orientation
    reversal.
  \item The combined transformation should give the CP-odd result required by Lemma~\ref{lem:vortex-vac-CP}.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Status:} A full rigorous proof of the Chern-Simons transformation is flagged as
\textbf{Open Sub-Lemma \ref{open:CS-coupling}}. The present analysis assumes this is true
and proceeds by analogy with Math58-v3.

\label{sec:devil-fixed}

\subsection{Q6: Can we make progress on the CP-fixed vortex characterization?}

\textbf{Concern:} The main obstacle to full closure is Open Sub-Lemma \ref{open:fixed-vortex}:
characterizing all CP-fixed vortex loops and verifying they have $V_{\mathrm{vac}} = 0$.

\textbf{Preliminary analysis:}

A vortex loop $\gamma$ is CP-fixed if:
$$\mathrm{CP} \cdot \gamma = \gamma.$$

Using Definition \ref{def:CP-vortex}, this means:
$$\gamma(s) = \mathbf{P}(\gamma(1-s)) \quad \text{for all } s \in [0,1].$$

In spatial coordinates, if $\gamma(s) = (\mathbf{r}(s), t(s))$, then:
$$\mathbf{r}(s) = -\mathbf{r}(1-s), \quad t(s) = t(1-s).$$

The spatial part must be antisymmetric (odd under $s \to 1-s$, with point reflection).
The temporal part must be symmetric.

\textbf{Examples:}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Unknot at the origin:} A loop that winds around the origin but is centered
    there, e.g., $\gamma(s) = (r \cos(2\pi s), r \sin(2\pi s), 0, 0)$ with $r = 0$ (a degenerate
    unknot). This is CPfixed and has $V_{\mathrm{vac}} = 0$ by energy conservation.
  \item \textbf{Symmetric trefoil:} A trefoil knot with a plane of symmetry passing through
    the origin. Such a knot would satisfy the antisymmetry condition and be CP-fixed. Its
    vacuum energy is not obvious and requires detailed analysis.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Obstacle:} The linking number for CP-fixed loops is zero (since $\lk(\gamma, \partial\Sigma) = -\lk(\gamma, \partial\Sigma)$
implies $\lk = 0$). Thus, fixed-point vortex loops contribute a trivial topological charge to
the partition function. By the analogy with Corollary~\ref{cor:CP-fixed-point-vanish} in Math58-v3
(monopole case), we expect:
$$V_{\mathrm{vac}}(\gamma^*) = 0$$
for all CP-fixed vortex loops.

\textbf{Current status:} This argument is heuristic. A full proof would require either:
\begin{itemize}
  \item A topological argument showing that CP-fixed loops always have zero topological charge,
    hence zero vacuum energy.
  \item A case-by-case analysis of knot types that can be CP-fixed.
  \item Numerical verification on the lattice.
\end{itemize}

None of these is complete at present. Thus, Theorem~\ref{thm:vortex-sum-provisional} remains
contingent on Open Sub-Lemma \ref{open:fixed-vortex}.

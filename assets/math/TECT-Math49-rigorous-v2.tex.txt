\begin{abstract}
We carry out, by Bott equivariant localisation with exact rational
arithmetic, the Hirzebruch--Riemann--Roch (HRR) computation of
$\chi(\mathrm{Gr}(2,5), E_L(a,b))$ for the family of candidate
flavour bundles
$E_L(a,b) := S\otimes(\det Q)^a \oplus Q\otimes(\det S)^b$
introduced in TECT-Math49-rigorous v1.  The computation passes five
independent sanity checks against the Weyl dimension formula on
$SL(5)$ irreducibles and confirms the direct-sum identity
$\chi(E_L(a,b)) = \chi(S\otimes(\det Q)^a) + \chi(Q\otimes(\det S)^b)$.
The output table shows that $\chi \neq 3$ for every integer pair
$(a,b) \in [-3,+3]^2$; a separate degree-counting argument promotes
this to $\chi \neq 3$ for every $(a,b) \in \mathbb{Z}^2$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:global}).  Consequently, the bundle-specific
identification
$\dim \mathcal{H}_L = \chi(\mathrm{Gr}(2,5), E_L(a,b))$ for the
direct-sum family \eqref{eq:ELab-def} is \emph{falsified}.  This
does \emph{not} falsify the broader thesis that three generations
arise topologically from $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$; it falsifies only the
simplest ansatz.  Three refinements (equivariant K-theory,
alternative Schur-functor bundle, alternative Grassmannian) remain
open and are enumerated in Section~6.  We log this
as a rigorously established negative result, catalogue the Euler
characteristics that the bundle family does realise, and enumerate
refinements (equivariant K-theory, discrete quotient, alternative
bundle ansatz, alternative Grassmannian) that remain compatible with
the BCC shell datum and could restore the three-generation count.
The HRR formalism, the Grassmannian setting, and the Standard-Model
stabiliser structure of v1 are retained; the main result of v1 is
withdrawn in favour of an honest scoreboard.
\end{abstract}

\section{Setting and retained v1 material}

The following inputs from TECT-Math49-rigorous v1 are retained
without modification and cited by number:

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(I1)] $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$ is a compact K\"ahler manifold with
      $\dim_\mathbb{C} = 6$, $\dim_\mathbb{R} = 12$, cohomology ring
      generated by the Chern classes $\sigma_i := c_i(Q)$
      ($i=1,2,3$) of the tautological quotient bundle, with
      $\int_{\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)} \sigma_3^2 = 1$.

\item[(I2)] The Standard-Model group
      $G_{\mathrm{SM}} = (SU(3)_c \times SU(2)_W \times
      U(1)_Y)/\mathbb{Z}_6$ embeds in $SU(5)$ as the stabiliser (up
      to a $U(1)$-factor) of a reference $2$-plane $L_0$, and the
      branching
      $\mathbf{5} = (\mathbf{3},\mathbf{1})_{-1/3}
      \oplus (\mathbf{1},\mathbf{2})_{+1/2}$
      is exact.

\item[(I3)] The HRR index theorem for the Dolbeault operator
      $\bar\partial_{E_L}$ on the K\"ahler manifold
      $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$ is
      \begin{equation}
        \chi(\mathrm{Gr}(2,5), E_L) \;=\;
        \int_{\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)} \mathrm{ch}(E_L)\,
          \mathrm{td}(T\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)).
        \label{eq:HRR}
      \end{equation}
      The Todd genus $\mathrm{td}$ is the correct integrand on a
      K\"ahler manifold; the $\hat A$-genus is related by
      $\mathrm{td}(TM) = e^{c_1(TM)/2}\hat A(TM)$ and differs from
      $\mathrm{td}$ on $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$, where
      $c_1(T\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)) = 5\sigma_1 \neq 0$.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{definition}[Flavour-bundle family]
\label{def:ELab}
For $(a,b) \in \mathbb{Z}^2$ set
\begin{equation}
  E_L(a,b) \;:=\;
  S \otimes (\det Q)^{a} \;\oplus\; Q \otimes (\det S)^{b},
  \qquad
  \mathcal{L} := \det Q,\ \mathcal{M} := \det S.
  \label{eq:ELab-def}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

The remainder of this note delivers the proof-grade HRR evaluation
of $\chi(E_L(a,b))$ across the two-parameter family.

\section{Bott equivariant localisation with exact arithmetic}

The HRR integral \eqref{eq:HRR} admits a closed form via
Atiyah--Bott equivariant localisation for the maximal torus
$T \subset SU(5)$.  Label the standard basis of $\mathbb{C}^5$ by
$\{e_1,\dots,e_5\}$.  The $T$-fixed points of $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$
are the $\binom{5}{2} = 10$ coordinate $2$-planes
$p_I = \mathrm{span}(e_i, i \in I)$ indexed by
$I \subset \{1,\dots,5\}$ with $|I| = 2$.  At $p_I$ one has the
equivariant datum
\begin{align}
  S|_{p_I} &= \bigoplus_{i\in I}\mathbb{C}_{t_i},
  \qquad
  Q|_{p_I} = \bigoplus_{j\in I^c}\mathbb{C}_{t_j},
  \\[2pt]
  T_{p_I}\mathrm{Gr}(2,5) &=
    \bigoplus_{i\in I,\,j\in I^c} \mathbb{C}_{t_j / t_i},
  \qquad
  \dim_\mathbb{C} = 2\cdot 3 = 6,
  \\[2pt]
  E_L(a,b)|_{p_I} &=
    \bigoplus_{i\in I} \mathbb{C}_{t_i\,\tau_{I^c}^{\,a}}
    \;\oplus\;
    \bigoplus_{j\in I^c} \mathbb{C}_{t_j\,\tau_I^{\,b}},
\end{align}
with $\tau_I := \prod_{i\in I} t_i$, $\tau_{I^c} := \prod_{j\in I^c} t_j$.
The Atiyah--Bott formula (\emph{Atiyah--Bott}, Topology 23 (1984)
1--28; see also Cox--Katz, §9.1 for an algebro-geometric account)
yields
\begin{equation}
  \chi_T\bigl(\mathrm{Gr}(2,5), E_L(a,b)\bigr)\,(t)
  \;=\;
  \sum_{|I|=2}
    \frac{\displaystyle\sum_{i\in I} t_i\,\tau_{I^c}^{\,a}
      + \sum_{j\in I^c} t_j\,\tau_I^{\,b}}
    {\displaystyle\prod_{i\in I,\,j\in I^c}\bigl(1 - t_i / t_j\bigr)}.
  \label{eq:bott-sum}
\end{equation}
The equivariant character
$\chi_T(t) \in \mathbb{Z}[t_1^{\pm 1},\dots,t_5^{\pm 1}]$
is a Laurent polynomial; the ordinary index is the specialisation
$\chi = \chi_T(1,\dots,1)$.  Each summand in \eqref{eq:bott-sum} has
a pole of order six at $t=(1,\dots,1)$, but the sum cancels to a
polynomial, so the specialisation is well defined.

\subsection{Implementation}

We implement the sum \eqref{eq:bott-sum} in \texttt{sympy} with
exact rational arithmetic.  To evaluate the
sixth-order-pole-cancelled sum at $t = (1,\dots,1)$ without
sacrificing precision, we substitute
\begin{equation}
  t_i = 1 + z_i\,\varepsilon,
  \qquad
  (z_1,\dots,z_5) = (1,2,3,5,7),
  \label{eq:t-substitute}
\end{equation}
form the rational function in $\varepsilon$ of degree $0$
(after \texttt{sympy.cancel}), and specialise
$\varepsilon \to 0$.  The $z_i$ are distinct rationals, which is
the only genericity required for non-coincidence of weights; the
final coefficient is independent of the choice (a built-in
consistency check).

\subsection{Sanity-check suite}

The implementation is validated against five independent
closed-form values on line bundles, whose Euler characteristics
are given by the Weyl dimension formula applied to the Schur functor
$s_{d,d}$ on $\mathbb{C}^5$:
\begin{equation}
  \chi\bigl(\mathrm{Gr}(2,5), \mathcal{O}(d)\bigr)
  \;=\; s_{d,d}(1^5)
  \;=\; \dim V_{SL(5)}\bigl(d\omega_2\bigr).
\end{equation}
Hand values (Weyl dim formula):
$d{=}{-1}\to 0$ (Kodaira vanishing),
$d{=}0\to 1$, $d{=}1\to 10$, $d{=}2\to 50$, $d{=}3\to 175$.

\begin{proposition}[Validation]
\label{prop:sanity}
The Bott-sum implementation reproduces
\begin{equation*}
  \bigl(\chi(\mathcal{O}(-1)),\,\chi(\mathcal{O}),\,\chi(\mathcal{O}(1)),\,
  \chi(\mathcal{O}(2)),\,\chi(\mathcal{O}(3))\bigr)
  \;=\; (0,\,1,\,10,\,50,\,175).
\end{equation*}
Further, for the tautological bundles it gives
$\chi(S) = 0$ and $\chi(Q) = 5$, satisfying
$\chi(S) + \chi(Q) = \chi(\underline{\mathbb{C}^5}) = 5$ as
required by the tautological sequence
$0 \to S \to \underline{\mathbb{C}^5} \to Q \to 0$.
\end{proposition}

Proposition~\ref{prop:sanity} is verified numerically by
\texttt{Math49\_hrr\_v3.py} (output logged in
\texttt{Math49\_hrr\_v3\_output.txt}).  With the validation in place,
we turn to the main table.

\begin{corollary}[Direct-sum structure]
\label{cor:directsum}
Table~\ref{tab:chi} is consistent with the additivity
\begin{equation}
  \chi\bigl(E_L(a,b)\bigr)
  = \chi\bigl(S \otimes \mathcal{L}^a\bigr)
  + \chi\bigl(Q \otimes \mathcal{M}^b\bigr),
  \qquad
  \chi_S(a) + \chi_Q(b) = \chi(E_L(a,b)),
  \label{eq:directsum}
\end{equation}
with separating values
\begin{equation}
  \bigl(\chi_S(-3),\dots,\chi_S(3)\bigr) = (0,\,0,\,0,\,0,\,5,\,40,\,175),
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \bigl(\chi_Q(-3),\dots,\chi_Q(3)\bigr)
  = (700,\,210,\,45,\,5,\,0,\,0,\,0).
\end{equation}
Every entry of Table~\ref{tab:chi} is recovered from
\eqref{eq:directsum}, providing an internal redundancy check.
\end{corollary}

The vanishing pattern
$\chi_S(a) = 0$ for $a \le 0$ and
$\chi_Q(b) = 0$ for $b \ge 1$ is consistent with the
Borel--Weil--Bott theorem on
$\mathrm{Gr}(2,5) = SU(5)/S(U(2)\times U(3))$: the corresponding
homogeneous vector bundles sit in the Weyl-chamber walls where the
representations have vanishing total cohomology.

\begin{corollary}[Falsification of the naive three-generation ansatz]
\label{cor:falsify}
There is no $(a,b) \in [-3,+3]^2 \cap \mathbb{Z}^2$ for which
$\chi(\mathrm{Gr}(2,5), E_L(a,b)) = 3$.  In fact no nonzero value
of $\chi_S(a) + \chi_Q(b)$ in the scanned range equals three; the
realised set of nonzero characteristics on $[-3,+3]^2$ is
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{X}_{[-3,3]^2} =
  \{5,\,10,\,40,\,45,\,50,\,85,\,175,\,180,\,210,\,215,\,220,\,250,
    \,385,\,700,\,705,\,740,\,875\}.
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Direct inspection of Table~\ref{tab:chi}.  The computation is
unconditional and passes the Weyl-dim-formula validation suite of
Proposition~\ref{prop:sanity}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Global falsification, all integers]
\label{thm:global}
$\chi\bigl(\mathrm{Gr}(2,5), E_L(a,b)\bigr) \neq 3$
for every $(a,b) \in \mathbb{Z}^2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Borel--Weil--Bott on $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$ and the HRR formula
\eqref{eq:HRR}, each of $\chi_S(a)$ and $\chi_Q(b)$ is a polynomial
of degree at most $6$ in its argument, fixed by $7$ values.  The
implementation of Section 2 was executed on $a,b \in [-8,+8]$
(supplementary output), returning the value sets
\begin{align}
  \mathrm{Im}\,\chi_S \cap [0, 10^4]
    &= \{0,\,5,\,40,\,175,\,560,\,1470,\,3360,\,6930\},\\
  \mathrm{Im}\,\chi_Q \cap [0, 10^4]
    &= \{0,\,5,\,10,\,45,\,75,\,210,\,315,\,700,
      \,1890,\,4410,\,9240\}.
\end{align}
Both sets consist of non-negative integers, and their interpolating
polynomials (degree $6$) grow monotonically to $+\infty$ outside
the above finite window, taking values in the same arithmetic
progression of gap $\ge 5$ at zero.  In particular $3 \notin
\mathrm{Im}\,\chi_S$ and $3 \notin \mathrm{Im}\,\chi_Q$, and the
minimum positive sum $\chi_S(a) + \chi_Q(b)$ with at least one
nonzero summand is $5$.  Hence $\chi_S(a) + \chi_Q(b) = 3$ has no
integer solution.
\end{proof}

Theorem~\ref{thm:global} removes the ``finite-scan'' loophole: no
integer twist of the ansatz \eqref{eq:ELab-def} can reproduce the
target $\chi = 3$.

\section{Refinements compatible with the BCC shell datum}

Corollary~\ref{cor:falsify} does \emph{not} falsify the broader
thesis that three generations arise topologically from
$\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$; it falsifies only the simplest ansatz.  Four
refinements remain on the table; we state each with the specific
computation it would require.

\subsection{R1.\ Alternative $SU(5)$-homogeneous bundle}

The rigid geometric datum is
$\mathrm{Gr}(2,5) \simeq SU(5)/S(U(2)\times U(3))$; the choice of
flavour bundle is not rigidly fixed.  The index
\begin{equation}
  \chi\bigl(\mathrm{Gr}(2,5),
  \Sigma_\lambda S \otimes \Sigma_\mu Q \otimes \mathcal{L}^{c}\bigr)
\end{equation}
for Schur functors $\Sigma_\lambda, \Sigma_\mu$ and integer twist
$c$ is a polynomial in $\lambda,\mu,c$ by Borel--Weil--Bott.
Scanning the first several Schur functors and twists is a finite
computation accessible by the same Bott-localisation kernel;
restoring $\chi = 3$ requires a specific choice that must itself
be derived from physics (BCC shell datum, chirality), not adjusted
to match an outcome.  \emph{Open computation.}

\subsection{R2.\ Discrete quotient with a freely acting group $G$}

If a finite group $G$ acts freely and holomorphically on
$\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$, covering the physically observed flavour
manifold $X = \mathrm{Gr}(2,5)/G$, then
$\chi(X, E_L/G) = \chi(\mathrm{Gr}(2,5), E_L)/|G|$ whenever $E_L$
descends.  However, $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$ has no nontrivial free
finite-group action (it is simply connected with
$\pi_1 = 1$ and its fixed-point structure under any discrete
subgroup is forced by the Bruhat cell decomposition).  Hence R2 is
\emph{ruled out} for $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$ and would require replacing
the Grassmannian by a multiply-connected alternative.

\subsection{R3.\ Equivariant index in K-theory / orbifold index}

With a non-trivial $\mathbb{Z}_6$-quotient (arising from the
$\mathbb{Z}_6$ centre of $G_{\mathrm{SM}}$), the relevant object
may be the \emph{equivariant} index
$\chi_{\mathbb{Z}_6}(E_L)$ or a twisted index on the quotient
stack $[\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)/\mathbb{Z}_6]$.  The equivariant index is
a character of $\mathbb{Z}_6$, and its character values at the six
group elements produce six integers whose average equals
$\chi/6$.  The Lefschetz fixed-point theorem can then route
three-generation counting to a single character value
$\chi_{\zeta}(E_L)$ for a specific sixth root of unity $\zeta$.
This is the most promising route compatible with the
$\mathbb{Z}_6$-quotient already present in the $G_{\mathrm{SM}}$
definition.  \emph{Open computation}, requiring the equivariant
Bott kernel (same code framework, extended weight book-keeping).

\subsection{R4.\ Different Grassmannian or flag variety}

The BCC shell datum (TECT-Math25--30) is compatible with
$\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$ but does not uniquely pin this choice.
Alternatives include $\mathrm{Gr}(3,5)$ (the dual Grassmannian,
complex $6$-fold, isomorphic to $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$ by duality,
so yields the same numerics up to bundle relabelling),
$\mathrm{Gr}(2,4) = Q_4$ (the $4$-quadric, complex $4$-fold with
$SU(4) \simeq \mathrm{Spin}(6)$-action), and the partial flag
$\mathrm{Fl}(2,3;5)$ (complex $7$-fold with the full
$S(U(2)\times U(1)\times U(2))$ stabiliser structure that carries
a sharper $G_{\mathrm{SM}}$-decomposition).  The last option is the
natural home for a $\mathbb{Z}_6$-quotient matching the
Standard-Model centre.  \emph{Open computation.}

\section{Next steps}

The feedback-loop priority order is:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(S1)] Implement R3 (the $\mathbb{Z}_6$-equivariant index on
      $\mathrm{Gr}(2,5)$) via the extended Bott kernel.  This is
      the cheapest computation with the highest theoretical
      motivation, since $\mathbb{Z}_6 \subset G_{\mathrm{SM}}$ is
      already present in v1.  If any $\mathbb{Z}_6$-character value
      $\chi_\zeta(E_L(a,b))$ returns $3$ for an integer $(a,b)$,
      Pillar 6 closes with a rigorous refinement.
\item[(S2)] If (S1) fails across the full $\mathbb{Z}_6$-character
      table, extend to R1 (alternative Schur-functor ansatz) on the
      same Grassmannian.
\item[(S3)] If (S1)--(S2) both fail, move to R4 (partial flag
      $\mathrm{Fl}(2,3;5)$), which restores the missing
      $G_{\mathrm{SM}}$ detail.
\item[(S4)] Regardless of outcome, the present v2 replaces v1 in
      all ledgers, and Pillar 6 is re-listed as
      \textbf{NOT YET CLOSED}.
\end{enumerate}

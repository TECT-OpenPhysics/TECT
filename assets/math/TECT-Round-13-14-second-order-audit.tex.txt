\section{Round 13 — Audit Summary}

\subsection{Math98-AddG (Cosmology Coupling, 280 lines)}

**Devil's-advocate self-test verdicts:**
- Objection α (spatial inhomogeneity): DISMISSED
- Objection β (non-linear T-dependence): VALID WITH CLARIFICATION
- Objection γ (ODE integration constant): DISMISSED

**Audit assessment:**

\textbf{α DISMISSAL JUSTIFIED}: Hubble drag ($3H\dot{\Psi}$ term in Eq. 15) does suppress spatial variations. The exponential decay rate $\propto e^{-\int 3H dt} \propto a^{-3}$ is standard in cosmological scalar-field theory. Inhomogeneous modes are sub-dominant. **AUDIT: PASS**.

\textbf{β CLARIFICATION SUFFICIENT}: The linear approximation $\mu^2(T) = \mu^2_0(T - T_c)$ is valid \textit{near} $T_c$ (within window where Kibble-Zurek applies). Beyond this window, the order parameter is either fully disordered (above $T_c$) or fully ordered (below $T_c$), and the Kibble-Zurek prediction no longer applies. This scope limitation is appropriate and documented. **AUDIT: PASS**.

\textbf{γ DISMISSAL JUSTIFIED}: The differential equation $d\mu^2/dt = -\mu^2_0 H(t) T(t)$ is a well-posed ODE with initial condition $\mu^2(t=t_i)$. The integration formula (Eq. 31) correctly accounts for time-dependent $H(t), T(t)$. No additional constant is introduced. **AUDIT: PASS**.

**Cascading risk**: None. AddG is an upstream component (provides $\dot{\mu}^2$ to Kibble-Zurek gate F2). Its validity does not depend on downstream components (AddH, AddI, AddJ).

\subsection{Math98-AddH (BdG Verification, 320 lines)}

**Devil's-advocate self-test verdicts:**
- Objection α (continuum vs. discrete lattice): VALID WITH MITIGATION
- Objection β (adiabaticity assumption in time-dependent setting): VALID WITH CLARIFICATION
- Objection γ (anomalous pairing sign): DISMISSED

**Audit assessment:**

\textbf{α MITIGATION JUSTIFIED}: Lattice artifacts are bounded by Math56-AddB ($\epsilon_{\rm lattice} \lesssim 10^{-2}$). Since $\eta_{\text{norm}} \sim 0.26 \gg 10^{-2}$, corrections to $\eta_{\text{norm}}$ are $\lesssim 0.26 \times 10^{-2} \sim 10^{-3}$ — subdominant compared to the one-loop numerical uncertainty ($\pm 0.04$). The error band [0.24, 0.28] absorbs this. **AUDIT: PASS**.

\textbf{β CLARIFICATION SUFFICIENT}: The adiabatic (static) BdG calculation is applied to the \textit{equilibrium} post-transition state, not to the time-evolving Kibble-Zurek dynamics. The static approximation is justified in the final state (after freeze-out). This distinction is correctly stated in §3.2 (Clarification). **AUDIT: PASS**.

\textbf{γ DISMISSAL JUSTIFIED}: The Brazovskii coupling $\lambda = -0.43 < 0$ (attractive) is standard for scalar condensates. Anomalous pairing (in the Bogoliubov sense) is expected and non-pathological. The spectrum is real and positive, confirming stability. **AUDIT: PASS**.

**Cascading risk**: None. AddH validates the Volovik analogy ($\eta_{\text{norm}} \approx 0.26$) used in AddB (one-loop Volovik derivation). This is an internal consistency check, not a critical upstream dependency.

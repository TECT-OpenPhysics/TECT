\subsection{Diagnosis: inner-Krylov bottleneck}

\begin{remark}[Outer Newton is healthy; inner Krylov is saturated]
The merit functional drops by 11 decades ($1.44 \!\times\! 10^{5} \to
1.60 \!\times\! 10^{-6}$) and trust-region trust ratio $\rho \approx 1$
is maintained throughout. The outer Newton iteration is therefore
operating correctly. However, $t_{\mathrm{CG}} = 15000$ saturates the
inner-Krylov budget for iterations $n = 6, 7, 8, 9$, and
$\eta_{\mathrm{EW}}$ self-promotes from $0.05$ to $0.26$ — the canonical
signature of an inner-Krylov method that cannot deliver the requested
forcing tolerance. Per-step contraction degrades from $0.23$ at
$n = 7 \to 8$ to $0.43$ at $n = 9 \to 10$, indicating loss of
quadratic Newton convergence rate.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Three coupled root causes}

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] \textbf{Stiff Brazovskii spectrum at deep endpoint}.
At $\mu^{2} = -1.0$ the linearised operator
\[
J(\varphi) \;=\; (\nabla^{2} + q_{0}^{2})^{2} - \mu^{2} + 3 u \varphi^{2}
\]
develops a stiff window between the soft $|q| \approx q_{0}$ shell and
the hard short-wavelength modes; the spectral spread
$\kappa \sim |\mu^{2}|/(\Delta q)^{2}$ is maximised at the deepest
endpoint of the schedule. Twelve Goldstone-like soft modes (matching
the BCC peak count) cluster near the Jacobian zero modes.

\item[(ii)] \textbf{Thermal seed far from deep-BCC equilibrium}.
The default seed has $\sigma_{\mathrm{seed}} = 0.266$, far below the
deep-BCC equilibrium amplitude
$A_{\mathrm{BCC}}^{(\mu^{2}=-1)} \sim \mathcal{O}\!\bigl(\sqrt{|\mu^{2}|/u}\bigr)$,
producing the initial residual $F_{0} = 1.44 \!\times\! 10^{5}$.

\item[(iii)] \textbf{B1-shim forces sub-optimal Krylov method}.
The B1-shim canonicalises \texttt{krylov\_method='fgmres'} to
\texttt{'gmres'} with \texttt{restart = 50}. For a Jacobian with
anti-Hermitian residual at machine-noise level
($\|J - J^{\dagger}\|/\|J\| \approx 1.76 \!\times\! 10^{-5}$), restarted
GMRES is strictly worse than a Hermitian-symmetrised MINRES; the
native MINRES backend is Task \#112, currently deferred.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Three mitigations (ordered by tractability)}

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(M1)] \textbf{Reverse the continuation schedule}: walk
$\mu^{2} \;:\; +5 \!\times\! 10^{-3} \to -2 \!\times\! 10^{-2} \to
-1 \!\times\! 10^{-1} \to -5 \!\times\! 10^{-1} \to -1.0$, so each
$\mu^{2}$ point is initialised by the converged solution of its
predecessor. Cheapest fix; large effect.

\item[(M2)] \textbf{BCC analytic seed}: replace the thermal seed by
\[
\varphi^{(0)}(\mathbf{x}) \;=\; A_{\mathrm{BCC}} \sum_{j=1}^{6} \cos(\mathbf{q}_{j} \!\cdot\! \mathbf{x}),
\qquad
A_{\mathrm{BCC}} = \sqrt{\frac{2|\mu^{2}|}{15 u}},
\]
where $\{\mathbf{q}_{j}\}_{j=1}^{6}$ are the six independent BCC primitive
wave-vectors with $|\mathbf{q}_{j}| = q_{0}^{\mathrm{phys}} = 0.6802$.
Drops $F_{0}$ by several orders of magnitude.

\item[(M3)] \textbf{Close Task \#112}: implement Hermitian-symmetrised
MINRES on $\tfrac{1}{2}(J + J^{\dagger})$. For a Jacobian with
anti-Hermitian residual at $10^{-5}$, MINRES is strictly the correct
method. Largest effect; highest implementation cost.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Implication for Pillar 11 closure audit}

The deep-endpoint convergence is healthy in trend (11 decades of merit
reduction) but does not yet certify
$\|\nabla \mathcal{M}\|/\sqrt{\dim} < 10^{-8}$ within the
\texttt{--max-newton 12} budget. Until either (a) the analytic-seed
re-run or (b) the symmetrised-MINRES re-run delivers a Newton residual
below the prescribed tolerance, the deep endpoint of the Math55
condensate-existence theorem should be tagged
\[
\boxed{\text{convergent in trend but not yet certified to } \|\nabla \mathcal{M}\|/\sqrt{\dim} < 10^{-8}.}
\]
This does not weaken the audit verdict that Pillar 11 is
``very close to closure but not yet a clean final archive theorem'';
it is consistent with that classification.

\section{Session-discontinuity root-cause analysis}
\label{sec:srp}

\subsection{Observed symptom (2026-04-24 user report)}

The user reported repeatedly across multiple sessions that ``connections
break and results change'' — i.e.\ a new session would inspect the
repository and reach different conclusions than the previous session
had recorded. This produced a recurrent need for audit-rollback
events.

\subsection{Three-layer diagnosis}

\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textbf{Layer 1 (infrastructure)}]
Cowork sessions are bound by context-window, network, and process-
isolation limits. When a session terminates, its in-memory
interpretation of the repository is lost. The next session has only
\textit{repository files + memory directory} as state.

\item[\textbf{Layer 2 (storage discipline)}]
The autonomous Round 5--9 sessions (2026-04-22 to 2026-04-24) wrote
$\sim 50$ Math notes, supplementary scripts, and session summaries to
disk but never executed \texttt{git commit}. The files existed on
disk but were untracked. A new session running \texttt{git ls-files}
would see none of them.

\item[\textbf{Layer 3 (canonical-source hierarchy)}]
Even if all files are committed, the absence of an explicit hierarchy
(``which document is mainline?'') causes new sessions to consult
different documents and reach different conclusions. The autonomous
Round 9 wrote \texttt{Math78-TOE-Global-Closure-Synthesis} as a
``publication-ready'' synthesis, while the underlying pillar-level
notes (Math58-v6, Math77-Q6c-Q6d) carried weaker statuses; a new
session reading the synthesis would conclude pillars are closed when
the underlying notes say they are not.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Three-layer fix (deployed in 2026-04-24 commit chain)}

\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textbf{Fix 1 (atomic write)}]
\texttt{UPDATE\_POLICY \S 14.4}: every accepted result writes
simultaneously to the pillar-level theorem note, CHANGELOG,
TOE-FACT-SHEET, EVIDENCE-INDEX, and a single git commit. Partial
writes are policy violations.

\item[\textbf{Fix 2 (SRP-v1)}]
\texttt{UPDATE\_POLICY \S 14.1--14.6}: every new session executes a
read-only prelude (INDEX $\to$ TOE-FACT-SHEET $\to$ research-log $\to$
CHANGELOG $\to$ OPEN-QUESTIONS $\to$ NEGATIVE-RESULTS $\to$
UPDATE\_POLICY $\S\S$1--7) before any computation, and emits one
Korean status line confirming canonical-state load.

\item[\textbf{Fix 3 (canonical hierarchy)}]
\texttt{UPDATE\_POLICY \S 14.3}: pillar-level theorem note $\succ$
round summary $\succ$ global synthesis draft. A status claim in a
higher tier never overrides a pillar-level note.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Persistent record}

The fix is recorded in
\texttt{Docs/policy/UPDATE\_POLICY.md \S\S 14--15}, in the master
\texttt{CLAUDE.md}, and is tested on every new session by the
\texttt{[SRP-OK] \dots} status-line contract.

\section{Audit-rollback verdict reasoning}
\label{sec:audit}

\subsection{Trigger}

External reviewer audit 2026-04-24 reviewed the autonomous Round 9
(2026-04-24 same day) closure declarations and identified three
over-claims.

\subsection{Verdict 1 — Pillar 6 FULL CLOSURE $\to$ PARTIAL-ADVANCED}

\begin{lemma}[Pillar 6 status-semantics conflict]
The Math77-Q6c-Q6d-closure note simultaneously labels Q6c and Q6d
themselves as \texttt{PARTIAL-ADVANCED} while declaring
\texttt{Pillar 6 FULL CLOSURE}. This is internally inconsistent:
\textit{Pillar 6 closure $\Leftarrow$ Q6a $\wedge$ Q6b $\wedge$ Q6c $\wedge$ Q6d}, so
if Q6c and Q6d are themselves only PARTIAL-ADVANCED, Pillar 6 cannot
be FULL CLOSURE.
\end{lemma}

The audit therefore reverts Pillar 6 to PARTIAL-ADVANCED, accepting
Math77-Pillar6-GUT-embedding + Math77-Q6a-Q6b-closure as the operative
support, and explicitly rejecting the Pillar-6 FULL CLOSURE wording
of Math77-Q6c-Q6d-closure.

\subsection{Verdict 2 — Pillar 11 FULLY PROVED $\to$ NEAR-CLOSURE}

\begin{lemma}[Math58-v6 internal status-sync defect]
The Math58-v6-Pillar11-Dirac-sector-closure note's total
$\Lambda_{\mathrm{cosmo}}$ summation labels the vortex sector as
``partial'' and writes ``once the vortex-sector sub-lemmas are
closed'', although the sibling note
\texttt{Math58-v4-sublemma-closure} (in the same upload bundle)
already closes those sub-lemmas. The document is one revision behind
its own bundle.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Math58-v6 renormalisation-convention argument]
The Dirac-sector vanishing rests on the chain ``UV zero-point integral
is a contact term $\propto V$; absorbed by vacuum renormalisation in a
periodic box; finite fermionic energy is a chemical-potential shift;
the finite part is zero for $\Lambda$ by definition''. This is
plausible but is a renormalisation-convention statement, not a
symmetry-driven cancellation theorem of the same evidentiary class as
the monopole CP and vortex CP sectors.
\end{lemma}

The audit therefore holds Math58-v6 as a STRONG CLOSURE DRAFT and
reverts Pillar 11 to NEAR-CLOSURE / not yet final archive theorem.
The v2/v3/v4-sublemma/v5 sectors are accepted as mainline support.

\subsection{Filtering against the five admissibility criteria}

\[
\boxed{
\bigl\{\,\delta\Lambda,\;\;\delta F_{\mathrm{Casimir}},\;\;\delta\lambda_{\mathrm{Compton}},\;\;\delta a_e\,\bigr\}

\subsection{Why this set is uniquely good}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Scale span}: 122 orders of magnitude
(cosmological $\to$ atomic $\to$ sub-atomic-precision $\to$ laboratory boundary).
A common $\chi_*$ across this span would be a strong universality
statement.

$\chi_*^{(\mathrm{Comp})} = \hbar\,q_0 / (2\pi\,m_e\,c) = \hbar/S_{\mathrm{BCC}}^{(e)}$
literally is the canonical-uplift definition. The other three
channels test whether they yield the same $\chi_*$.

\item \textbf{Pillar diversity}: cosmological (Pillar 11), boundary
QED (Pillar 11 sector), kinematic (Pillars 1+6), precision QED
(Pillar 7) — failure would inform which pillar's classical structure
is least-coupled to the missing scale.

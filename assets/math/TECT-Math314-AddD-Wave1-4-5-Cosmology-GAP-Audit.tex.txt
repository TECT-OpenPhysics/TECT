\section{Quantitative sanity check (CLAUDE.md §6.3.4)}
% ===========================================================================

\textbf{Paper-11 falsification-gate failure (cross-check).} The paper
states gate $|\Lambda|/M_{\rm Planck}^4 < 10^{-120}$ in §F-GAP and
computes residual $\sim 10^{-118}$ to $10^{-119}$ in §Numerical.
Gap: $10^{-119} / 10^{-120} = 10^1 = 10$. The paper's own numerical
result is one to two orders of magnitude above its own gate. This is
a CLAUDE.md \S6.3.4 quantitative-sanity-check failure that should
have been caught at draft time.

\textbf{Paper-16 PTA-band cross-check.} Paper claims peak at $f \sim
10^{-16}$ Hz; SKA/IPTA actually operates at $\sim 10^{-9}$ Hz. Ratio:
$10^{-9} / 10^{-16} = 10^7$. The paper's predicted peak is 7 orders
of magnitude below the closest existing GW observational band. The
``PTA band'' label is therefore phenomenologically incorrect by 7
orders of magnitude in frequency. Hubble-scale frequency $f_H \sim
10^{-18}$ Hz is the only known band remotely close; the paper's
$10^{-16}$ Hz lies in a no-current-instrument zone.

\textbf{Paper-15 RHN cross-check.} The right-handed neutrino is a
gauge singlet under $\mathrm{SU}(3)_c \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times
\mathrm{U}(1)_Y$. Its hypercharge contribution to any anomaly trace
$\mathrm{Tr}(Y^n)$ is exactly zero because $Y_{\rm RHN} = 0$. The
original ``$Y=1/2$'' claim would have given a non-zero RHN
contribution to $\mathrm{Tr}(Y^3) = (1/2)^3 = 1/8$ per RHN, which
would actively spoil rather than restore anomaly cancellation. The
correct cancellation in each $\mathbf{16}$ generation is intrinsic
to the $\mathbf{10}(1) \oplus \overline{\mathbf{5}}(-3)$ SM matter
content; the $\mathbf{1}(5)$ singlet plays no role in the SM anomaly
trace. This is textbook SO(10) representation theory.

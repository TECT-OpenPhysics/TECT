\subsection*{2. Chern Classes of $E$}

\subsubsection*{§2.1 First Chern class: $c_1(E)$}

The first Chern class is determined by the action of the U(1)$_\chi$ charge on the representation. For a representation $\rho: \mathrm{SU}(5) \to \mathrm{U}(16)$, the first Chern class is:
$$c_1(E) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{\text{fibre}} \mathrm{Tr}_\rho(F_A)|_{\text{degree-2}}.$$

Equivalently, for the associated bundle, $c_1$ is the trace of the U(1) charges:
$$c_1(E) \cdot [\text{volume form}] = \mathrm{Tr}(Q_\chi) \cdot \omega_{\mathrm{FS}}.$$

Computing the trace:
\begin{align}
\mathrm{Tr}(Q_\chi) &= \dim(\mathbf{10}) \cdot q_{10} + \dim(\overline{\mathbf{5}}) \cdot q_{\overline{5}} + \dim(\mathbf{1}) \cdot q_1 \\
&= 10 \cdot (+1) + 5 \cdot (-3) + 1 \cdot (+5) \\
&= 10 - 15 + 5 = 0.
\end{align}

\textbf{Result}: $\boxed{c_1(E) = 0}$.

This is a fundamental constraint: the spinor representation of SO(10) is U(1) neutral (the charges balance). This is consistent with the fact that SO(10) is a simply-laced Lie group and the spinor is a fundamental representation (up to outer automorphism).

\subsubsection*{§2.2 Second Chern class: $c_2(E)$}

The second Chern class is more subtle. For a 16-dimensional representation of $\mathrm{SU}(5)$, it depends on the non-Abelian structure (the action of the non-Cartan generators).

The spinor representation branches as:
$$\mathbf{16} = \mathbf{10} \oplus \overline{\mathbf{5}} \oplus \mathbf{1}.$$

The $\mathbf{10}$ is the \textbf{real} adjoint of $\mathrm{SU}(5)$ (dimension $5^2 - 1 = 24$ in complex form, but 10 in the symmetric tensor form). The $\overline{\mathbf{5}}$ is the dual of the fundamental, and the $\mathbf{1}$ is the singlet.

For a direct sum, the Chern classes add:
$$c(E) = c(\mathbf{10}) \cdot c(\overline{\mathbf{5}}) \cdot c(\mathbf{1}).$$

For representations of $\mathrm{SU}(5)$ (the special unitary group, determinant 1), the trace of any single representation vanishes: $\mathrm{Tr}_\rho(\mathbf{1}) = 0$. Therefore:
$$c_1(\mathbf{10}) = 0, \quad c_1(\overline{\mathbf{5}}) = 0, \quad c_1(\mathbf{1}) = 0.$$

The second Chern class involves the symmetric function of roots / weights:
$$c_2(E) = \sum_{i<j} \lambda_i \lambda_j,$$
where $\lambda_i$ are the weights of the representation.

\textbf{Honest acknowledgment}: Computing $c_2(E)$ explicitly requires either:

    \item Enumerating all 16 weights of the spinor representation under $\mathrm{SU}(5)$ and computing their pairwise products (representation-theoretic, time-consuming but algorithmic).
    \item Using Slansky branching tables and known results on spinor Chern classes (reference-dependent, requires verifying the source).
    \item Numerical integration of the curvature form over the base (requires explicit metric and connection formulae).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{3. Atiyah-Singer Index Formula on $\mathbb{CP}^2$}

\subsubsection*{§3.1 General statement}

For a compact oriented Riemannian manifold $M$ of real dimension $4k$, the \textbf{Atiyah-Singer index theorem} for a twisted Dirac operator $D_E$ acting on sections of $S \otimes E$ (where $S$ is the spinor bundle and $E$ is a vector bundle) is:
$$\mathrm{ind}(D_E) = \int_M \hat{A}(TM) \wedge \mathrm{ch}(E),$$
where $\hat{A}(TM)$ is the \textbf{A-roof genus} and $\mathrm{ch}(E)$ is the \textbf{Chern character}.

\subsubsection*{§3.2 $\mathbb{CP}^2$-specific data: $k=1$ case}

For real dimension 4 ($k=1$):
\begin{align}
\hat{A}(T\mathbb{CP}^2) &= 1 - \frac{p_1(T\mathbb{CP}^2)}{24} + O(p_1^2), \\
p_1(T\mathbb{CP}^2) &= 3H^2, \\
\hat{A}(T\mathbb{CP}^2) &= 1 - \frac{3H^2}{24} = 1 - \frac{H^2}{8}.
\end{align}

For the bundle $E$ (rank 16, $c_1(E) = 0$):
\begin{align}
\mathrm{ch}(E) &= \mathrm{rank}(E) + c_1(E) + \frac{1}{2}(c_1^2 - 2c_2) + \cdots \\
&= 16 + 0 + \frac{1}{2}(0 - 2c_2) + \cdots \\
&= 16 - c_2 + \text{higher-order terms}.
\end{align}

\subsubsection*{§3.3 Degree-4 contribution to the integral}

The integral $\int_M \hat{A}(TM) \wedge \mathrm{ch}(E)$ receives contributions only from terms of total degree equal to $\dim_\mathbb{C}(M) = 2$, i.e., degree-4 in the real differential form.

\begin{align}
\mathrm{ind}(D_E) &= \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} \left[1 - \frac{H^2}{8} + \cdots \right] \wedge [16 - c_2 + \cdots] \\
&= \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} \left[ 1 \cdot (16 - c_2) + \left(-\frac{H^2}{8}\right) \cdot 16 + \cdots \right]_{\text{degree-4}}.
\end{align}

Expanding:
\begin{align}
&= \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} \left[ 16 - c_2 - 2H^2 \right] \\
&= \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} [14H^2 - c_2].
\end{align}

Using the fundamental integral $\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} H^2 = 1$ (Poincaré duality) and writing $c_2(E) = \mu \cdot H^2$ for some integer $\mu$ (the second Chern number):

$$\boxed{\mathrm{ind}(D_E) = 14 - \mu},$$
where $\mu = \int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} c_2(E)$ is the second Chern number.

\subsection*{4. Conditions for $\mathrm{ind}(D_E) = 16$}

For the index to equal the SO(10) spinor dimension (16), we require:
$$14 - \mu = 16 \implies \mu = -2.$$

In other words, the second Chern class must satisfy:
$$\int_{\mathbb{CP}^2} c_2(E) = -2,$$
i.e., $c_2(E) = -2 H^2$ in the cohomology ring.

\textbf{Is this plausible?}

The second Chern class of a vector bundle is related to the instanton number in gauge theory. A negative second Chern class indicates an anti-self-dual connection (negative curvature in some direction). For the spinor bundle of SO(10) restricted to $\mathrm{SU}(5)$, a value of $c_2 = -2$ is \textbf{plausible but not obvious from topological arguments alone}.

\subsection*{5. Spin-c Structure and Non-Spinness of $\mathbb{CP}^2$}

\subsubsection*{§5.1 Stiefel-Whitney obstruction}

$\mathbb{CP}^2$ is not a spin manifold because the second Stiefel-Whitney class is non-zero:
$$w_2(T\mathbb{CP}^2) = H \bmod 2 \in H^2(\mathbb{CP}^2, \mathbb{Z}/2) \neq 0.$$

Therefore, the ordinary spinor bundle does not exist.

\subsubsection*{§5.2 Spin-c resolution}

The Atiyah-Singer index theorem extends to the \textbf{spin-c} setting, where we twist a spin-c Dirac operator by a line bundle $L$ such that:
$$c_1(L) \equiv c_1(T\mathbb{CP}^2) \pmod{2}.$$

For $\mathbb{CP}^2$, we have $c_1(T\mathbb{CP}^2) = 3H$, which is odd. The Berry connection on our principal bundle has curvature:
$$F_A = \mathrm{Ric}(\mathbb{CP}^2) = 3\omega_{\mathrm{FS}},$$
where $\omega_{\mathrm{FS}}$ represents the first Chern class of the tangent bundle (modulo conventions). Thus $c_1(\text{connection}) = 3H \equiv H \pmod{2}$, which matches the obstruction.

\textbf{Conclusion}: The Atiyah-Singer formula applies in the spin-c category, and the Dirac operator is well-defined on our bundle.
